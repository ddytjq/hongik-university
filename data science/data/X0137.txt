to spinal cord injury-related sites:</font></font></font></b></td>

<td><font face="Bell MT">Professional hockey is finally taking notice of
a fact the medical community has known for a long time--the alarming incidence
of spinal injuries in the sport. These injuries are primarily caused by
collisions with the boards, either by accident or as the result of a legal
or illegal check.&nbsp;</font>
<p><font face="Bell MT">If this level of spinal cord injury were in the
workplace, it would be considered unacceptable. Why then are we tolerating
it in sport, especially when too many incidents occur in our youth and
high school hockey programs?</font>
<p><font face="Bell MT">Hockey must police itself on this issue. If it
does not, lawyers and federal governments may decide the future of the
sport.</font>
<p><font face="Bell MT">Links to sites dealing with spinal cord injury
are located on many of our pages. To make your search easier, I have listed
most of the links below.</font>

<h2>
<a href="http://www.rsmpsychology.com/what2know.htm"><img SRC="brain.gif" ALT="Brain" HSPACE=10 VSPACE=10 BORDER=0 height=87 width=86 align=LEFT></a><font face="Arial"><a href="http://www.rsmpsychology.com/what2know.htm">What
Every Parent and Athlete Should Know About Sports Concussion</a></font></h2>

<h2>
<font face="Arial"><font color="#800000"><font size=+0>What is Concussion?</font></font></font></h2>

<dl><font face="Arial"><font size=-1>Concussion is the most common form
of head injury for athletes. It is associated with disorientation, and
sometimes with loss of consciousness (LOC) followed by amnesia (forgetting)
of what happened both immediately before and after the injury.</font></font>
<p><b><font face="Arial"><font size=+1><a href="http://www.rsmpsychology.com/sportsconcussion.htm">A
Message to Parents and Young Athletes</a></font></font></b>
<p><font face="Arial"><font color="#000000"><font size=-1>The NHL is now
routinely assessing for concussion and providing <b><u>baseline neuropsychological
screening </u></b>for their players. Now, there is a national trend to
provide baselines for our high school and young athletes who are also at
risk for concussion. Baselines that are performed prior to injury provide
valuable information to help later determine when the player is ready to
return to play, as well as if he/she is in need of treatment. Baseline
screening includes noninvasive tests that measure attention, memory and
other cognitive skills. However, it is not intelligence or achievement
testing. Neuropsychological testing is often more sensitive to the effects
of mild head injury than MRI, CT, or neurological exam.&nbsp;</font></font></font></dl>

<a NAME="Spinal injuries in Finland & Sweden"></a>
<table BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=4 CELLPADDING=4 WIDTH="100%" >
<tr ALIGN=LEFT VALIGN=TOP>
<td><a href="http://www.thieme.com/cgi-win/thieme31.exe/onGJFGDAKFEMGJ/display/762"><img SRC="entrez_pubmed.gif" ALT="National Library of Medicine" BORDER=0 height=45 width=342 align=CENTER></a>
<h3>
<sub><font size=+2><a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov:80/entrez/query.fcgi?cmd=Retrieve&db=PubMed&list_uids=10090466&dopt=Abstract">Spinal
cord injuries in ice hockey in Finland and Sweden from 1980 to 1996</a></font></sub></h3>
Molsa JJ, Tegner Y, Alaranta H, Myllynen P, Kujala UM
<p>LIKES Research Center for Sports and Health Sciences, Jyvaskyla, Finland.
<a href="mailto:jmolsa@maila.jyu.fi">jmolsa@maila.jyu.fi</a>
<p>Traumatic spinal cord injury (SCI) in the cervical or thoracic region
is one of the most catastrophic types of sport injuries. This study was
designed to determine incidence and mechanisms of major SCI in ice hockey
in Finland and Sweden from 1980 to 1996 in order to find possibilities
for prevention....
<p>Ice hockey is one of the most popular sports in Europe, and the number
of participants is still increasing. The typical mechanism in SCI is body
checking from behind, falling down and a head-first blow from the boards.
These serious injuries may be prevented by changing the rules (banning
body checking near the boards) with strict refereeing and education of
trainers and players.
