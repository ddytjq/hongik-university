<h3><!--mstheme--><font color="#330099">Wembley To Be Kept For Football Only<!--mstheme--></font></h3>
<p><img border="0" src="new_wembley.jpg" width="300" height="180"></p>
<p>Wembley, recognised the world over as the home of football, will remain just
that, the Culture Secretary, Chris Smith confirmed yesterday.</p>
<p>There is to be no running track around the hallowed turf, and the redeveloped
stadium will be earmarked for football only. This will please the thousands of
football fans who felt that the atmosphere of the ground would be ruined by
having an athletics track between the fans and the pitch.</p>
<p>The decision means that Wembley will retain its intimidating atmosphere, in
keeping with so many other famous football stadia such as the San Siro and the
Nou Camp.</p>
<p>The plans mean that the FA will have to repay £20m of the lottery funds granted
for the redevelopment of the ground.</p>
<p>The Tory party critisised the decision saying that it scuppered any chance of
a British Olympic bid with Wembley as its centre-piece. This argument is fundamentally
flawed, however, as due to Wembley's existing location and infrastructure, a
Wembley Olympic bid would have been impossible.</p>
