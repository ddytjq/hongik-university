Carlsberg Stand 
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>The Carlsberg Stand 
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Valley Parade is built on the side of a large hill in the Manningham area of Bradford. 
</P><P>Away fans are given the entire Symphony Stand (aka The Bradford End) a small (1800 seats),
two tier, covered, seated area behind the goal and facing the Carlsberg 
Kop. Some seats have obscured views and stand doesn't have a bar, but does serve food (albeit from a single kiosk)
and allow smoking.
</P><P>For most games additional seats (1200 seats) are available in the Ciba Stand which has the added advantage of having a bar !
It is also somewhat unusual in the you have to ascend several
flights of steps from the turnstiles before you reach pitch level.
</P><P>Pre and Post match banter (along with match coverage) can be heard 
on Bradford Community Broadcasting (1566 AM).
</P><P>The nearest bookmakers to the ground (that visiting fans can get access to) 
is a Coral located on Manningham Lane
between the business park at the bottom of Manningham Lane near the
city centre and The Bradford Arms pub near the ground. Fans approaching
from the town centre will undoubtedly walk past it.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=BD87DY&Title=The+Bradford+&+Bingley+Stadium+(aka+Valley+Parade)+(Bradford+City)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>The club lists their disabled facilities are 'numerous', so disabled fans are 
best contacting the club for their definition of 'numerous'.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>Most pubs in the Manningham area are full of home fans, but do allow
well behaved away fans. Club colours are not recommended in the town
centre before or after the game.
</P><P>Other than the Bradford Arms, other possible pubs include The Merry Mason,
The Corn Dolly, The Carleton Hotel and The Oakleigh. No pubs around the
ground have been singled out for hostility towards away fans.
</P></p><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Bradford-Carleton-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Bradford-Carleton-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Carleton Hotel
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Carleton Hotel
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Bradford Arms
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Bradford-Bradford-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Bradford-Bradford-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Bradford Arms
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Bradford Arms
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>Located on Manningham Lane, the Bradford Arms is normally full with both
home and away fans. Just 5 minutes walk from the ground it is an unofficial away pub and whilst home
fans are present, they are normally out numbered. The games room at the back has a pool table and opens out on
to a small gravel area that doubles as an impromptu beer garden.
</P><P>No real ale was available, but there was a selection of bitters was available. The only food available was some
shrink wrapped sandwiches.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>Bradford is probably the best place in the country to sample Indian cuisine, 
but unless you fancy a pre-match curry then you are probably better off
sticking to the burger vans situated outside the ground.
</P><P>Most of the big fast food outlets are represented in the town centre, 
sadly the once excellent 'Wendys' is now a KFC.
</P><P>The Symphony Stand has a single food kiosk serving and several people
have reported the pies as below par.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>Plenty in the town centre, but nothing close to the ground.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is BD87DY</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Whilst Valley Parade isn't the easiest ground in the league to find it 
is thankfully very well signposted and visible from several miles away.
If you get lost follow signs for Manningham.
</P><P>Leave the M62 at Junction 26 onto the M606. The motorway ends at The
Odsal Top roundabout. Take the last exit (past McDonalds on the left)
into
Rooley Lane. At the second roundabout turn left onto A650 Wakefield
Rd, stay in the middle of three lanes across two more roundabouts as Wakefield
Rd becomes Shipley Airedale Rd and then Canal Rd. As the road drops down 
the hill you can see Valley Parade on the left. About 2.3 miles after
first joining the A650, after passing the Staples retail store turn left
into Station Rd (A6177) and then left into Queens Rd. Head up the hill and
at the third set of lights turn left into Manningham Lane.
After the SAVE petrol station on the left take the first left into Valley
Parade.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>Parking can be a problem as the club car park is officials only and most of the
surrounding streets are residents parking only.
</P><P>Try the Midland Road side of the ground and the retail park just below
midland road. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>Bradford has two railway stations, Forster Square and Bradford Interchange.
Forster Square is situated close to the Bradford Town centre and is a 10-15
minute walk from the ground. The Interchange is the other side of the city
centre and fans are better of getting a bus (to Manningham Lane) or taxi
to the ground. 
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Bradford Forster Square
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Forster Square is not only the location of one of Bradford's railway station, 
but it also plays home to Bradford's best hotel 'The Midland'. Not too
cheap, but you'll not find better without heading towards Leeds.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/bradfrd.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Bradford City used to have a real reputation for trouble, but this has long
since died down and football related trouble is now rare (normally only local derbies).
Obviously recent none football related incident in Bradford have recieved much publicity.
</P><P>Bradford City fans are very proud of their clubs heritage and take exception to the club
being refered to as "Bradford" (not Braford City) as this was the name of the Bradford Park Avenue
Side (which folded in 1974, although have now returned as an amatuer side).
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>One Bradford City fan reports  "The stewards are rent-a-thug, but the Police
tend to be ok."
</P><P>On two recent visits we found they couldn't be friendlier.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Two songs from the 1999/2000 season stand out,  "The Premier League, Is
Upside Down" (We're in Europe with the Wednesday, and The Leeds are going
down) and Bradford City fans taunting of Sky TV pundit Rodney Marsh at the end of
the season (Marsh agreed to have his head shaved if Bradford City stayed in the
Premiership, which they did). 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Bradford has a pretty good nightlife, plenty of city centre pubs and more
Indian restaurants than you can imagine. Serious party goers may
wish to get a train or taxi to Leeds. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>Bradford has the normal facilities you'd expect for small city (cinemas,
ice-skating, shopping etc), but one tourist attraction of note is the TV
and Film Museum.
</P><P>For the shoppers, nearby Leeds may be a slightly better option or the
White Rose Centre ( off junction 28 of the M62) which is easier parking
than Leeds.
