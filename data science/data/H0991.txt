| <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4>Facts</h4>		<p>		<table border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0">			<tr>				<td>					<ul>						<li>During amphibian oocyte development, the amount of rDNA is 100 to 1000 times that of somatic cells.						<li>The amplified rDNA is on circular DNAs with several tandem <a href="../MGW1/MG112285.html">rDNA repeats</a>.						<li>The circles replicate by rolling circle DNA replication.					</ul>				</td>				<td><img src="i3411.gif" width="146" height="44" naturalsizeflag="3"></td>			</tr>		</table>		</p>		<p>		<table border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0">			<tr>				<td width="172"></td>				<td width="343">					<ul>						<li><i>Tetrahymena</i> macronuclei contain <a href="../MGW1/MG1351.html">linear rDNA plasmids</a> that replicate to higher copy number than they do in micronuclear DNA.					</ul>				</td>			</tr>		</table>		</p>		<p>		<table border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0">			<tr>				<td>					<ul>						<li>A section of the Drosophila X chromosome, containing chorion genes, is amplified 16 fold at stage 9 of egg chamber development.						<li>A similar region on chromosome 3 is amplified 60 fold.						<li>The amplification resembles that seen in <a href="../MGW1/MG1133.html">polytene chromosomes</a> of salivary glands.					</ul>				</td>				<td><b><img src="i3412.gif" width="208" height="67" naturalsizeflag="3"></b></td>			</tr>		</table>		</p>		<p>		<table border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0">			<tr>				<td colspan="2"><img src="i3413.gif" width="192" height="84" naturalsizeflag="3"></td>				<td>					<ul>						<li>Methotrexate inhbits dihydrofolate reductase, an enzyme important in DNA precursor synthesis. Cells grown in the presence of gradually increasing concentrations of methotrexate can adapt. Adapted cells survive MTR doses 3000 X those that kill normal cells.						<li>Adapted cells contain 200 X more DHFR, 200 X more DHFR RNA, and 200 X more DHFR DNA than non-adapted cells.					</ul>				</td>			</tr>		</table>		</p>		<p>		<table border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="0">			<tr>				<td colspan="2">					<ul>						<li>Growth of adapted cells in the absence of methotrexate can result in gradual loss of resistance (lineA) or the stable maintenance of part of the resistance (line B).						<li>Stable maintenance is associated with altered chromosome regions that stain uniformly (<strong>homogeneously staining regions</strong>, HSR's).						<li>Instability is correlated with the presence of very small chromosomes, <strong>double-minute chromosomes</strong>.					</ul>				</td>				<td><b><img src="i3414.gif" width="184" height="93" naturalsizeflag="3"></b></td>			</tr>		</table>		</p>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Interp"></a>Interpretations</h4>		<ul type="circle">			<li>Some organisms have evolved mechanisms of gene amplification to provide gene products needed in large quantities.			<li>Different mechanisms are used in different organisms, sometimes (as with double-minute chromosomes and HSR's) within the same organism.			<li>Amplification can be developmentally, temporally, or environmentally regulated.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a
