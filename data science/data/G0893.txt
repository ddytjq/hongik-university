            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"> 
              <!-- #BeginEditable "title" --><b>Astronomy Education: A Selective 
              Bibliography</b><!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>
          </td>
          <td>&nbsp;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" --> 
            <p><a href="educ_bibprint.html"><img src="../../images_main/print.gif" width="20" height="26" border="0" alt="printer-friendly version"></a> 
              <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="educ_bibprint.html">printer-friendly 
              version</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"><img src="../../images_main/6666cc.gif" width="500" height="2"></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1">Version 
              2.2; June 1998 <br>
              by Andrew Fraknoi <br>
              (Foothill College & the Astronomical Society of the Pacific) <br>
              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1">&copy;1998, 
              Andrew Fraknoi, Astronomical Society of the Pacific, 390 Ashton 
              Ave., San Francisco, CA 94112. E-mail: <a href="mailto:fraknoi@fhda.edu.">fraknoiandrew@fhda.edu.</a> 
              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>This 
              is a brief listing of some books and articles about astronomy and 
              space science education at various levels from graduate school to 
              grade school and at various types of institutions. It is by no means 
              comprehensive, but merely designed to help those approaching the 
              field become acquainted with the rich literature that already exists. 
              (Suggestions are most welcome.) </i></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="toc">Table 
              of Contents:</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">1.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="#1">General Reports on Astronomy Education</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">2.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="#2">Review Articles</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">3.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="#3">Government & Organizational Reports</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">4.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib02.html#4">Graduate-level Astronomy Education</a> 
              <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">5.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib02.html#5">Teaching College Non-science Majors</a> 
              <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">6.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib03.html#6">K-12 Astronomy Education</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">7.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib03.html#7">Articles on K-12 Education Reform</a> 
              <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">8.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib03.html#8">Astronomy Education at Planetaria & 
              Museums</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">9.&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              <a href="educ_bib04.html#9">Astron</a><a href="educ_bib03.html#8">omy</a><a href="educ_bib04.html#9"> 
              Education through the Media</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">10. 
              <a href="educ_bib04.html#10">Astron</a><a href="educ_bib03.html#8">omy</a><a href="educ_bib04.html#10"> 
              Education with Computers</a> <br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">11. 
              <a href="educ_bib04.html#11">Teaching Resources for Astronomy</a> 
              <br>
              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>1. 
              <a name="1">General Reports on Astronomy Education</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Berendzen, R., ed. <b>Education in and History of Modern Astronomy</b>. 
                1972, Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, Vol. 198. <br>
                One of the first attempts to systematize and collect information 
                on astronomy education. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A., et al., eds. <a href="../astro/astropubs/universe.html">The 
                Universe at Your Fingertips: An Astronomy Activity and Resource 
                Notebook.</a> 1995, Astronomical Society of the Pacific. <br>
                An 813-page compilation of hands-on activities, resource lists, 
                and teaching ideas; designed for grades 4-12, but usable at many 
                levels and settings. A product of the Society's Project ASTRO. 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Hunt, J., ed. <b>COSMOS: An Educational Challenge: Proceedings 
                of a 1986 GIREP Conference</b>. 1986, European Space Agency Publications 
                SP-253. <br>
                Brings together a wide range of mostly European contributions 
                on astronomy education at all levels. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Pasachoff, J. & Percy, J., eds. <b>The Teaching of Astronomy</b>. 
                1990, Cambridge U. Press. <br>
                Proceedings of an international colloquium on astronomy education 
                in the US and elsewhere. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Pennypacker, C., ed. <b>Hands-on Astronomy for Education</b>. 
                1992, World Publishing. <br>
                Proceedings of a 1990 conference emphasizing computers and small 
                telescopes, especially in high school settings. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Percy, J., ed. <b>Astronomy Education: Current Developments, Future 
                Coordination</b>. 1996, Astronomical Society of the Pacific Conference 
                Series, Vol. 89. <br>
                The most up-to-date and comprehensive introduction to current 
                issues and projects in astronomy education; with good reviews 
                and resources. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sletteback, A. & Jettner, F. <b>"Proceedings of the Conference 
                on Education in Astronomy, August 1969"</b> in Bulletin of the 
                American Astronomical Society, vol. 2, p. 254 (1970). <br>
                Interesting papers showing the concerns and projects of the 1960's, 
                some of which are still very much with us today. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p> </p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>2. 
              <a name="2">Review Articles</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bishop, J. <b>"Astronomy Education in the U.S.: Out from Under 
                a Black Cloud"</b> in The Griffith Observer, Mar. 1980, p. 2. 
                <br>
                Good historical overview of the rise and fall of astronomy in 
                the school curriculum and some educational projects in the 1970's. 
                <i>(See also her "U.S. Astronomy Education: Past, Present and 
                Future" in Science Education, vol. 61, no. 3, p. 295; 1977.) </i></font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A. <b>"The State of Astronomy Education in the U.S."</b> 
                in Percy, J., ed. Astronomy Education: Current Developments, Future 
                Coordination. 1996, Astronomical Society of the Pacific Conference 
                Series, Vol. 89. <br>
                Sixteen-page overview of astronomy education in many settings. 
                (Accompanied, later in the same book, by a "Catalog of National 
                Astronomy Education Projects".) </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sadler, P. <b>"Astronomy's Conceptual Hierarchy"</b> in Percy, 
                J., ed. Astronomy Education: Current Developments, Future Coordination. 
                1996, Astronomical Society of the Pacific Conference Series, Vol. 
                89. <br>
                How students learn (and don't learn) astronomical ideas. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>3. 
              <a name="3">Relevant Government and Organizational Reports and Documents</a> 
              </b> </font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                American Association for the Advancement of Science: <i><b>Project 
                2061: Science for All Americans.</b></i> 1990, Oxford U. Press. 
                <i><b>Project 2061: Benchmarks for Science Literacy. </b></i>1994, 
                Oxford U. Press. <br>
                Two reports from the pioneering study of how to reform science 
                education. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                National Academy of Sciences: <i><b>Reshaping the Graduate Education 
                of Scientists and Engineers.</b></i> 1995, National Academy Press, 
                2101 Constitution Ave., NW, Washington, DC 20418. <br>
                A discussion of the issues across disciplines. (Available on the 
                Web at <a href="http://www.nas.edu" target="_blank">http://www.nas.edu</a>) 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                NASA: <i><b>Implementing the Office of Space Science Education/Public 
                Outreach Strategy.</b></i> 1996, NASA report. <br>
                A strategy for leveraged ways NASA and its scientists can contribute 
                to education. Contact Jeff Rosendhal (<a href="mailto:jrosendh@mail.hq.nasa.gov">jrosendh@mail.hq.nasa.gov</a>) 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                NASA: <i><b>Partners in Education: A Strategy for Integrating 
                Education and Public Outreach into NASA's Space Science Programs.</b></i> 
                1995, NASA OSS. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                National Research Council: <i><b>National Science Education Standards.</b></i> 
                1996, National Academy Press. <br>
                The result of a national effort to define how science should be 
                taught and what is needed to bring about reform. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                National Research Council: <i><b>The Role of Scientists in the 
                Professional Development of Teachers.</b></i> (1996, Nat'l Academy 
                Press). <br>
                Done for biologists but with much to say to all of us. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                National Research Council: <i><b>Science Teaching Reconsidered: 
                A Handbook.</b></i> 1997, National Academy Press. <br>
                A summary of ideas and resources for college instructors. Nothing 
                on astronomy, but some good ideas and references in general. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sigma Xi: <i><b>Scientists, Educators, and National Standards: 
                Action at the Local Level.</b></i> 1994, Sigma Xi, Forum Coordinator, 
                P.O. Box 13975, Research Triangle Park, NC 27709. <br>
                Proceedings of a 1994 forum, bringing together educators and scientists. 
                Has a good catalog of projects in many disciplines. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
