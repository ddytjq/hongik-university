      <blockquote>
  <p><i>The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and the National Institutes of Health 
    (NIH) have devoted 3% to 5% of their annual Human Genome Project (HGP) budgets 
    toward studying the ethical, legal, and social issues (ELSI) surrounding availability 
    of genetic information. This represents the world's largest bioethics program, 
    which has become a model for ELSI programs around the world.</i></p>
</blockquote>  
      <p><b><font size=+1>Societal Concerns Arising from the New Genetics</font></b> 
      </p>
      <p><b>Fairness in the use of genetic information</b> by insurers, employers, courts, 
  schools, adoption agencies, and the military, among others. <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Who should have access to personal 
  genetic information, and how will it be used?</i> 
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="legislat.html">Privacy and Legislation</a> 
  page in this ELSI suite.</font> 
<p><b>Privacy and confidentiality</b> of genetic information. <br>
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <i>Who owns and controls genetic 
  information?</i> 
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="legislat.html">Privacy and Legislation</a> 
  page in this ELSI suite.</font> 
      <p><b>Psychological impact and stigmatization</b> due to an individual's 
        genetic differences. <br>
        &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <i>How does personal genetic 
        information affect an individual and society's perceptions of that individual?</i> 
        <br>
        &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <i>How does genomic information 
        affect members of minority communities?</i> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
        </i></font>
      <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
        </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="minorities.html">Minorities, 
        Race, and Genetics</a> page in this ELSI suite.</font><b><br>
        <br>
        Reproductive issues</b> including adequate informed consent for complex 
        and potentially controversial procedures, use of genetic information in 
        reproductive decision making, and reproductive rights. <br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Do healthcare personnel 
        properly counsel parents about the risks and limitations of genetic technology?</i> 
        <br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How reliable and useful 
        is fetal genetic testing?</i><br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; What are the larger societal 
        issues raised by new reproductive technologies?</i> 
      <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/medicine/genetest.html">Gene 
  Testing</a> page in this ELSI suite.</font> 
<p><b>Clinical issues</b> including the education of doctors and other
health service providers, patients, and the general public in genetic capabilities,
scientific limitations, and social risks; and implementation of standards
and quality-control measures in testing procedures.
      <p><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How will genetic tests 
        be evaluated and regulated for accuracy, reliability, and utility? (Currently, 
        there is little regulation at the federal level.</i>) <BR>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How do we prepare healthcare 
        professionals for the new genetics?<br>
        </i> <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How do we prepare the 
        public to make informed choices?</i><br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How do we as a society balance 
        current scientific limitations and social risk with long-term benefits?</i> 
      <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/medicine/genetest.html">Gene 
  Testing</a> and <a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/medicine/genetherapy.html">Gene 
  Therapy</a> pages in this ELSI suite.</font> 
<p><b>Uncertainties</b> associated with gene tests for susceptibilities and complex 
  conditions (e.g., heart disease) linked to multiple genes and gene-environment 
  interactions. <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Should testing be performed when 
  no treatment is available?</i> <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Should parents have the right 
  to have their minor children tested for adult-onset diseases?</i> <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Are genetic tests reliable and 
  interpretable by the medical community?</i> 
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/medicine/genetest.html">Gene 
  Testing</a> and <a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/medicine/genetherapy.html">Gene 
  Therapy</a> pages in this ELSI suite.</font> 
<p><b>Conceptual and philosophical implications</b> regarding human responsibility, 
  free will vs genetic determinism, and concepts of health and disease. <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Do people's genes make them behave 
  in a particular way?</i> <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Can people always control their 
  behavior?</i> <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; What is considered acceptable 
  diversity?</i> <br>
  <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Where is the line between medical treatment and enhancement?</i>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
  </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="behavior.html">Behavioral Genetics</a> 
  page in this ELSI suite.</font> 
      <p><b>Health and environmental issues </b>concerning genetically modified 
        foods (GM) and microbes. <br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Are GM foods and other products 
        safe to humans and the environment?</i> <br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How will these technologies 
        affect developing nations' dependence on the West?</i>
      <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
        </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="gmfood.html">Genetically 
        Modified Foods</a> page in this ELSI suite.</font>

          <p><b>Commercialization of products </b>including property rights (patents, 
        copyrights, and trade secrets) and accessibility of data and materials. 
        <br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Who owns genes and other 
        pieces of DNA?</i><br>
        <i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Will patenting DNA sequences 
        limit their accessibility and development into useful products?</i> 
      <p>
        <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1"><i>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 
        </i>For more on this topic, see the <a href="patents.html">Patenting</a> 
        page in this ELSI suite.</font><br>
