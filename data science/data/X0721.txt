<h2>TIMELINE EARLY HISTORY OF FLYING DISC PLAY</h2>

<p><font size="3">(rev. 12/8/01)</font><font size="4"><br>
</font><font size="3"></font></p>

<p><font size="3">This page was first built by Johan Lindgren of
Sweden, long-time Secretary of WFDF. We have recently made a few
additions and corrections, but there are obviously very many
significant events in disc history that have not yet been
included. If you have authortative information about events that
you feel should be added or corrections to suggest, please
contact </font><a href="mailto:cyberstork@earthlink.net"><font
size="3">cyberstork@earthlink.net</font></a><font size="3">.</font></p>

<h3>1871</h3>

<p><b>William Russel Frisbie </b>moves to Bridgeport, CT to
manage a new pie company. Shortly afterward he buys it, renaming
it the <b>Frisbie Pie Company.</b></p>

<p><a href="discearl.htm">More on the early history</a></p>

<h3>1947</h3>

<p><b>Fred Morrison</b> carves first plastic disc from a block of
solid tenite.</p>

<h3>1948</h3>

<p><b>Fred Morrison</b> manages to make his first disc from
buthyl-stearate.</p>

<p>First Premium Flying Disc, the Arcuate Vane with Lil'Abner
label appears.</p>

<h3>1951</h3>

<p>The Pluto Platter is the first mass-produced disc.</p>

<h3>1954</h3>

<p>Dartmouth University hold tournaments of two-a-side Guts.</p>

<h3>1955</h3>

<p>The founders of Wham-O, <b>Rick Knerr</b> and <b>&quot;Spud&quot;
Meline</b> first see Morrison's discs.</p>

<h3>1957</h3>

<p>Wham-O buys the rights to the Frisbee disc from Morrison. </p>

<h3>1958</h3>

<p><b>Fred Morrison </b>was awarded a design patent #183626
(30/9) for his &quot;Flying Saucer.&quot;</p>

<p>Wham-O produced the &quot;Toy Flying Saucer&quot;.</p>

<p>The Healy family organised the &quot;Invitational Frisbee
Tournament with Guts as the event. It was held in Escabana,
Michigan, USA.</p>

<p>The Frisbie Pie Bakery is closed.</p>

<h3>1959</h3>

<p>The name &quot;Frisbee&quot; is registered by Wham-O on May
26th. Trademark #679186</p>

<h3>1964</h3>

<p>In October, the Professional Model Frisbee, designed by Ed
Headrick is introduced by Wham-O.</p>

<p>The International Frisbee Association (IFA) is formed in Los
Angeles by Ed Headrick.</p>

<h3>1966</h3>

<p>Ed Headrick is awarded the first mechanical patent on a flying
disc #3359678 on Nov 1. Headrick assigned the patent, which
introduced the &quot;Lines of Headrick,&quot; to Wham-O for $10.</p>

<h3>1967</h3>

<p><b>Joel Silver</b> and others at the Columbia high school in
Maplewood, NJ, USA invent <a href="disculti.htm">Ultimate</a>
Frisbee.</p>

<p>The International Frisbee Association (IFA), is formed in Los
Angeles by <b>&quot;Steady&quot; Ed Headrick.</b></p>

<p>A <b>Mr Malu </b>in Belgium starts his own disc manufacturing.</p>

<h3>1968</h3>

<p>IFA publishes its grading system and a newsletter.</p>

<p>The IFA Masters Qualifying Meet and Tournament is held on
April 27 at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, CA., won by Jay Sheldon.</p>

<p>The first Ultimate game is played in Columbia High School.</p>

<p>The Master Model, designed by Ed Headrick is introduced by
Wham-O.</p>

<h3>1969</h3>

<p>US Army invest $400,000 to see if Frisbees can be used as a
parachute flare carrier. The project is closed without success.</p>

<p>IFA organises &quot;Berkely VS Southern California Meet&quot;.
The events were Distance, Accuracy Guts and Golf. The meet was
held in Brookside Park, in Pasadena, CA. It is the first known
disc golf tournament. <b>Jay Shelton</b> won. The temporary
targets were Hula Hoops. Berkely had a pole course at about the
same time.</p>

<p>Columbia High School play Ultimate on a parking lot.</p>

<h3>1970</h3>

<p>The first and second editions of the <a href="disculti.htm">Ultimate</a>
rules are written by <b>Buzzy Hellring, Joel Silver and Jon
Hines.</b></p>

<p>Columbia High beats Milburn High 43-10 in the very first
interscholastic game.</p>

<h3>1971</h3>

<p>New Jersey Frisbee Conference formed with five teams.</p>

<h3>1972</h3>

<p>On November 6, Rutgers beats Princeton, 29-27 in the first
intercollegiate game of ultimate. This was exactly 103 years
after the first intercollegiate fotball game, and on the same
site (which had been turned into a parking lot), and the same
team won by the same margin victory.</p>

<h3>1974</h3>

<p>On september 1st the Swedish Frisbee Federation (SFF) is
formed by <b>Johny Gustafson</b> and <b>Mikael Hj”rtsj–</b>.</p>

<p>In May the first OCTAD is organised on Rutgers University in
New Brunswick, NJ. The tournament has 8 events. <a
href="discgolf.htm">Golf </a>is won by <b>Keith White</b>.</p>

<p>In Toronto a tournament include &quot;Free-Form Frisbee&quot;.
This first <a href="discfree.htm">freestyle</a> is won by <b>Jim
Kenner and Ken Westerfield</b>.</p>

<p>On August 2nd the American Flying Disc Open is organised in
Rochester, USA. <b>Kerry Kollmar</b> did the first air-brush. <a
href="wfdfbord.htm#danroddick"><b>Dan Roddick</b></a> won golf
and got a car as first prize.</p>

<p>In June in Evanston, ILL, the North Western Frisbee Tournament
is held. <b>Bruce Konger</b> won Golf.</p>

<p>The first World Frisbee Championships are held by Wham-O in
the Rose Bowl. <b>Victor Malafronte</b> and <b>Jo Cahow</b> won
overall.</p>

<h3>1975</h3>

<p>The Japan Frisbee Disc Association is formed.</p>

<p>The first permanent <a href="discgolf.htm">Disc Golf</a>
course is made in Oak Grove, Pasadena by Ed and KenHeadrick. The
targets were made of pipe.</p>

<p>On April 25th the first organised <a href="disculti.htm">Ultimate</a>
tournament is played. Eight teams participated in Yale in the
Inter-Collegiate Ultimate Frisbee Championships. Rutgers won.</p>

<p>Ultimate is included in the WFC in Rose Bowl, where <b>Tom
Kennedy</b> won Golf and <b>Bruce Koger/Jo Cahow</b> <a
href="discovrl.htm">overall</a>.</p>

<p>The IFA newsletter and <em>Flying Disc World</em> are merged
into <em>Frisbee World</em>.</p>

<h3>1976</h3>

<p>The Australian FDA is formed.</p>

<p>The first Disc Pole Holes with chains are installed in Oak
Grove and La Mirada by Ken and Ed Headrick..</p>

<p>The Yale Ultimate tournament is renamed into the National
Ultimate Frisbee Chapionships. Rutgers won again.</p>

<p><b>Freddie Haft</b> did a nail delay during the AFDO in
Rochester.</p>

<h3>1977</h3>

<p>The Austrian FDA is formed.</p>

<p>The Belgian FDA is formed.</p>

<p>USA is split into the east and west for Ultimate play. Santa
Barbara won over Penn State in the final in the Nationals.</p>

<p>The first patent (#4,039,189) is issued on a chain catching
device to Ken and Ed Headrick on August 12th. They form the PDGA</p>

<h3>1978</h3>

<p>The Finnish FDA is formed.</p>

<p>The Guts Players Association is formed.</p>

<p>The Danish Frisbee Sport Union is formed.</p>

<p><a href="discddc.htm">DDC</a> is included in the WFC for the
first time.</p>

<p>In Sweden an indoor <a href="disculti.htm">Ultimate</a> game
is played for the first time. Skogshyddan played against
Halmstad.</p>

<h3>1979</h3>

<p>Ultimate Players Association is formed i december. <b>Tom
Kennedy</b> is elected the first director.</p>

<p>The Nederlandse Frisbie Bond is formed.</p>

<p>The Norwegian Frisbee Forbund is formed on April 17th.</p>

<p>The Federation Francaise de Frisbee is formed.</p>

<p>Frisbee Sport Italia is formed.</p>

<p>First UPA Nationals, held in Stage College, PA, won by
Glassboro over the Santa Barbara Condors.</p>

<h3>1980</h3>

<p>The Swiss Disc Sports is formed.</p>

<p>The first Ultimate European Championship is played in Paris.
Finland, England, Sweden finnished first,second and third.</p>

<p>The Individual European Championships are held in Belgium.</p>

<p>United Kingdom FDA is formed.</p>

<h3>1981</h3>

<p>The British Ultimate Federation is formed.</p>

<p>The <b>European Flying Disc Federation</b> (EFDF) is formed. <b>Mikael
Hj”rtsj–</b> is elected the first president.</p>

<p>Sweden won both Ultimate and Guts in the European
championships in Milano, Italy.</p>

<p>The Individual European Championships in Gothenburg, Sweden,
was won by <b>Klaus Arpia</b>, Finland and <b>Marianne
Spiegelhofer</b>, Austria.</p>

<p><b>Peter Jansson,</b> Sweden won the first European Disc Golf
championships in Essen, West Germany.</p>

<h3>1982</h3>

<p>The United States Disc Sports is formed.</p>

<p><b>Peter Jansson</b>, SWE won the Individual european
championships in Belgium.</p>

<p><a href="wfdfbord.htm#charliemead"><b>Charlie Mead</b></a>,
UK, won the european disc golf championships in England.</p>

<p>The first World Disc Golf Championship was held in Southern
California and directed by Ed Headrick. Harold Duvall won.</p>

<p>In the European Ultimate and Guts Championships in Austria,
Sweden won all titles except womens ultimate which Finland won.</p>

<p><b>Peter Spiegelhofer</b> is elected president for EFDF.</p>

<h3>1983</h3>

<p>The Deutscher Frisbee Sport Verband is formed.</p>

<p>The European Individual Championships in England is won by <b>Christophe
Sch–tte</b>, SWE, (Open), <b>Marianne Spiegelhofer</b>, Austria,
(Women) and <b>Jacob Wichman</b>, Denmark, (Juniors).</p>

<p>The first World Ultimate Championships are held in Gothenburg,
Sweden. Rude Boys, USA won open and Melting Pot, USA, women.
Sweden won Guts and juniors ultimate.</p>

<p>The Swedish Frisbee Forbundet gains membership in the national
Swedish sports confederation and flying disc play is officially
accepted as a sport in Sweden.</p>

<p>Kransco bought Wham-O. Both IFA and WFC are closed. At that
time IFA had 100,000 members in 30 countries.</p>

<p>The the first US Open is organised.</p>

<p>Windy City won the UPA nationals.</p>

<p>Ed Headrick turns PDGA control over to the players.</p>

<h3>1984</h3>

<p>First UPA College Nationals, held at Tufts, won by Stanford
over Glassboro.</p>

<p>The individual european championships is organised in ÷rebro,
Sweden. Overall winners are <b>Torsten G–rke</b>, WG (Open), <b>Karin
Karlsson</b>, SWE (Women), and <b>Martin Sievers</b>, SWE
(Juniors).</p>

<p>During the championships (On July 12th) the World Flying Disc
Federation is formed by EFDF. The Swedish association is given
the task of getting the organisation going. <a
href="wfdfbord.htm#charliemead"><b>Charlie Mead</b></a> is
elected president of <b>EFDF</b>.</p>

<p>The ultimate and guts world championships are held in Lucern,
Switzerland with Windy City winning ultimate and guts open,
Finland, women ultimate (USA participated but outside the actual
championship). Sweden won juniors ultimate. The decision to start
WFDF is confirmed by the other countries.</p>

<p>Finland changes its name to Finnish Flying Disc Association.</p>

<h3>1985</h3>

<p>The Jugoslavian FDA is formed.</p>

<p>The first WFDF Disc Golf World championship is held in
Helsingborg, Sweden. <b>Sam Ferrans</b> and <a
href="wfdfbord.htm#tamipellicane"><b>Tami Pellicane</b></a> won.</p>

<p>The first WFDF Congress is held and <a
href="wfdfbord.htm#charliemead"><b>Charlie Mead,</b></a> England,
is elected president, <b>Johan Lindgren</b>, Sweden, secretary
and <b>Brendan Nolan</b>, Ireland, treasurer.</p>

<p>The individual European championships are won by Dieter
Johansson, Sweden and Barbro LÂngjuth, Sweden. Tami
Pellicane,USA, participated and finished first in the women's
overall.</p>

<p>In the Ultimate and Guts European championships in Austria,
Sweden won three titles and Austria one.</p>

<h3>1986</h3>

<p>In August the third World Ultimate and Guts championship is
held. (Colchester England.) San Fransisco won open and Lady
Condors womens. Sweden won juniors and guts.</p>

<p>During the second WFDF Congress, <a
href="wfdfbord.htm#danroddick"><b>Dan Roddick, USA</b></a>, is
elected new president for WFDF, with <b>Johan Lindgren</b>
reelected as secretary and <b>Jacques Doetsch</b>, Belgium
elected treasurer.</p>

<h3>1987</h3>

<p>The first WFDF Overall World Championship is held in Ft
Collins, USA.<b>Peter Bowie</b>, New Zealand, won.</p>

<p>The Ultimate Affair tournament, Amherst,MA. With about 80
teams in attendance.</p>

<h3>1988</h3>

<p>The second WFDF overall is held in San Fransisco and the
fourth World Ultimate and Guts championship is held in Leuven,
Belgium.</p>

<h3>1989</h3>

<p>New Zealand FDA is formed.</p>

<p>In Colchester, England the WFDF Overall Championship is held
between July 24-30.</p>

<p>In Cologne, West Germany the first World Club Ultimate
championship is held.</p>

<p>Flying disc is demonstrated during the World Games in
Karlsruhe, West Germany.</p>

<h3>1990</h3>

<p>The Asociation Venezolana de Ultimate is formed.</p>

<p>In Oslo, Norway the fifth WFDF World Ultimate and Guts
championships are held, including masters for the first time..</p>

<h3>1991</h3>

<p>The second WFDF World Club Ultimate Championships are held in
Toronto, Canada.</p>

<p>The WFDF Overall Championships and the WFDF Congress is held
in Santa Cruz, CA, USA.</p>

<h3>1992</h3>

<p>The sixth WFDF World Ultimate and Guts Championships are held
in Utsunimoya, Japan.</p>

<p>The WFDF Congress is held at the championships in Utsunimoya
on Aug 16th. <a href="wfdfbord.htm#nobrauch"><b>Robert L Rauch</b></a>,
USA, elected new president for WFDF.</p>

<h3>1993</h3>

<p>The first WFDF Ultimate championships in the US. Madison
organize the third World Club championships between July 24th and
31st.</p>

<h3>1994</h3>

<p>The seventh WFDF World Ultimate and Guts championships are
organized in Colchester England. At the WFDF Congress during the
championships <a href="wfdfbord.htm#billwright"><b>Bill Wright</b></a>
is elected new President of WFDF. The Czech Flying Disc
Association and the Mexico Flying Disc Association are elected
full members of WFDF.</p>

<h3>1995</h3>

<p>The fourth WFDF Ultimate Club championships are held in
Millfield, England. At the WFDF Congress <b>Kathryn Middis</b>,
England, was elected Secretary for WFDF, <b>Johan Lindgren</b>
retaining his position as Treasurer.</p>

<p>In August the European Ultimate Championships are held in
Fontenay-le-Comte, France. Spain participating for the first
time. Sweden won all divisions.</p>

<p>In October WFDF was elected full member of both <b>GAISF</b>
(General Assembly of International Sports Fedreations) and <b>IWGA</b>
(International World Games Association).</p>
