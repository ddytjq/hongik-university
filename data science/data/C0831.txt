                                    <p class="MsoBodyText2"><span style="mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman" lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2"><span style="mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Tuesday,
                                    April 9, 2002<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></font></span><b><font face="Arial" color="#003399" size="5"><span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB">Manchester
                                    set to scale the heights of insurance</span></font></p>
                                    </b>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2"><b>Manchester
                                    is to play host to the UK’s largest
                                    insurance broker conference between the 18
                                    – 20 April. The conference comes at a time
                                    of immense change within the industry and is
                                    set to welcome senior figures from across
                                    the country who will participate in shaping
                                    a positive future within the insurance
                                    broking world.</b></font></span></p>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
                                    24th annual BIBA conference, ‘Scaling the
                                    Heights’, will centre around the need for
                                    trust in the industry and address the
                                    changing state of insurance broking and
                                    debate future concerns and regulation.</font></span></p>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">BIBA
                                    Chairman, George Nixon, said, &quot;We have
                                    a tough agenda at our Manchester conference,
                                    but I am confident that we will be
                                    delivering answers to some probing questions
                                    that will provide inspiration to those
                                    attending. Scaling the Heights will provide
                                    the opportunity for some straight talking
                                    and a platform from which all sectors of the
                                    industry can go forward to a positive and
                                    constructive future.&quot;</font></span></p>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">Major
                                    UK insurers have already pledged significant
                                    sponsorship for this event, including deals
                                    with principle sponsors like AXA Insurance,
                                    Norwich Union, Royal &amp; Sun Alliance and
                                    Alexander Forbes.</font></span></p>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">In
                                    addition to the main plenary sessions, the
                                    conference also offers delegates:</font></span></p>
                                    <ul>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">An
                                        open forum, chaired by the BBC’s
                                        Nicholas Witchell, about the
                                        industry’s future</font></span></li>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">Seminars
                                        on personal development and dealing with
                                        customer relationship management</font></span></li>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">‘Surviving
                                        the Corporate Jungle’, with Ken Hames,
                                        former elite British Army officer</font></span></li>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">Gala
                                        Dinner at Old Trafford with comedian
                                        Dominic Holland</font></span></li>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">Over
                                        50 exhibitors stands</font></span></li>
                                      <li><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">Direct
                                        access to in excess of 650 insurance
                                        industry delegates</font></span></li>
                                    </ul>
                                    <p><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">For
                                    more information on the conference, contact
                                    Janet Pitchers at BIBA on 020 7397 0221.</font></span></p>
                                    <p align="center"><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">….ends…</font></span><font FACE="Arial" SIZE="3">.</p>

                                    <p><font face="Arial" size="2">Notes to
                                    Editors</font></p>
                                    </b>
                                    <p align="left"><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" size="2">BIBA
                                    is the leading independent insurance
                                    organisation, representing both professional
                                    insurance brokers and their customers. The
                                    organisation has a thriving membership of
                                    2,000 firms and more than 37,000 individual
                                    brokers and intermediaries. BIBA brokers
                                    arrange over three quarters of all home,
                                    contents, motor, travel, commercial and
                                    industrial insurance policies in the UK.</font></span></p>

font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">&nbsp;<font color="#003399">_________________________________________________</font></span>

                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Monday

                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB">&nbsp;</span><b><font color="#003399" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="6">BIBA
                                    will welcome Aiib into membership<br>
                                    </font></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:
&quot;Times New Roman&quot;"><br>
                                    <b>400 firms could become part of largest
                                    organisation of insurance intermediaries</b><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Following
                                    the outcome of successful talks, the British
                                    Insurance Brokers’ Association (BIBA) is
                                    expecting 400 members of the Association of
                                    intermediaries and insurance Brokers (Aiib)
                                    to join its ranks from April this year.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Information packs about the proposed
                                    merger have been sent to Aiib members
                                    together with a consultation document . The
                                    consultation period ends 31 January. The
                                    Aiib anticipates holding an Extra Ordinary
                                    General Meeting (EGM) in February/March to
                                    enable its members to vote on the move.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Initial indications show that Aiib
                                    members favour the merger.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Said
                                    Mike Slack, Chairman of the Aiib:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
                                    </span>“A substantial number of AiiB
                                    members welcome the move. The merger
                                    approach follows the Aiib and BIBA working
                                    successfully together on various issues
                                    during which it became clear that we share a
                                    common vision and many common objectives.
                                    Our members will gain additional benefits by
                                    being part of what will become by far the
                                    largest and pre-eminent Trade organisation
                                    for intermediaries. BIBA already has great
                                    strengths, influence and membership
                                    benefits. I am confident that together we
                                    could more strongly represent and support
                                    the Broker and Intermediary sector in what
                                    will continue to be difficult and
                                    contracting market conditions.“<br>
                                    <br>
                                    Said Mike Williams, Chief Executive of BIBA:
                                    “This is good news for brokers and good
                                    news for the general insurance broking
                                    industry.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>The Government has said that trade
                                    associations have an important part to play
                                    in formulating practical public policy for
                                    the industry and also have a vital role in
                                    helping members become more competitive and
                                    effective while raising standards by
                                    encouraging innovation and devising ‘best
                                    practice.’<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Our combined forces will enable a
                                    strong united voice to promote our industry
                                    sector and give us all credible input to
                                    influence the shape and scope of future, and
                                    continuing, regulation with the Financial
                                    Services Authority (FSA).”<br>
                                    <br>
                                    BIBA will make changes internally to ensure
                                    that the new membership is fully represented
                                    at the highest level.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Mike Slack will join the main BIBA
                                    board and also the BIBA General Insurance
                                    Brokers’ Committee (GIBC) with
                                    responsibility for the smaller members (SME’s.)
                                    He will be responsible for leading a
                                    structure that will reflect the special
                                    needs and benefits development for all
                                    members up to 15 employees.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
                                    </span>The existing Aiib board together with
                                    the Secretary General Chris Arter will form
                                    the nucleus of the initial team to drive
                                    this project forward.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>They will also be offered positions
                                    within the BIBA Regional Structure.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span>These proposals coincide with BIBA
                                    refocusing its activities by making
                                    individual members of the BIBA board
                                    responsible for one of nine business areas
                                    into which the membership is segmented.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;<br>
                                    </span><span style="mso-tab-count:
2"><br>
                                    </span>Provision has been made for Aiib
                                    members to continue to pay membership fees
                                    at their existing rate until 1st January
                                    2003, following which fees will then be
                                    brought into line over the following two
                                    years in equal instalments.&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    On completion of a merger of the two
                                    organisations, the Aiib will be dissolved
                                    and its remaining assets transferred to
                                    BIBA.&nbsp;<br>
