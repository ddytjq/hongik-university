                              <p><b><font color="#3399CC">Maxis and Minis back in vogue</font></b> </p>
                              <p>The days of the Maxi and Mini are back - - but 
                                this time round they are investment choices instead 
                                of fashion styles. Each tax year you can select 
                                the Maxi ISA and enjoy more flexibility on stocks 
                                and shares or the Mini ISA and have a broader 
                                choice of managers for your cash, insurance and 
                                equity investments. </p>
                              <p><b>Article Overview:</b></p>
                              <ul>
                                <li> <a href="maxi.htm#1">The major differences</a> 
                                  <ul>
                                    <li><a href="maxi.htm#1a">The Maxi ISA</a></li>

                              <p><b><font color="#3399CC">The major differences</font><font color="#003399"> 
                                <a name="1a"></a></font> </b></p>
                              <p><b>THE MAXI ISA</b> (limit up to £7,000 per tax 
                                year) allows you to invest in all the three investment 
                                elements of an ISA. There are limits of £3,000 
                                for the cash element and £1,000 for the life insurance 
                                element but the stocks and shares element can 
                                make up the remainder of the allowance or it can 
                                take up the entire £7,000 allowance. This makes 
                                the Maxi ISA particularly attractive to investors 
                                who want to put emphasis on growth. However, if 
                                you choose to use the allowance up on equities 
                                you can't invest in the other two elements. And 
                                you are only allowed to choose one company to 
                                provide your Maxi ISA in any tax year. <a name="1b"></a></p>
                              <p><b>THE MINI ISA</b> allows you to invest in all 
                                three categories of investment - and with more 
                                than one manager. In theory you can have a cash 
                                Mini ISA with your Building Society (maximum £3,000), 
                                your life assurance Mini ISA with a life company 
                                (maximum £1,000) and a stocks and shares Mini 
                                ISA with a fund provider (maximum £3,000). </p>
                              <div align="right"><a name="2"></a><a href="maxi.htm#top" class="top">Back to top</a></div>

                              <p><b><font color="#3399CC">Limits on transfers</font></b> 
                                <br><br>
                                Investors should realise that they cannot transfer 
                                between one investment category and another. Investments 
                                in a life insurance component cannot be moved 
                                into stocks and shares, even within the same Maxi 
                                ISA. And those investors that have invested in 
                                a Maxi ISA in one tax year cannot then invest 
                                in a Mini ISA in the same tax year. Investors 
                                who want to invest the current maximum £7,000 
                                allowed in stocks and shares must choose the Maxi 
                                ISA. In a Maxi ISA, you can choose not to invest 
                                in the cash or life insurance elements and, instead, 
                                add those allowances on to the stocks and shares 
                                quota. It is not possible to achieve a £7,000 
                                investment in equities through choosing a stocks 
                                and shares Mini ISA because the maximum investment 
                                in that category is £3,000. </p>
                              <p>Maxis ISAs and Mini ISAs: a comparison Maxis 
                                Minis Annual £7,000 in shares £3,000 in cash Limits 
                                Or £3,000 in shares £3,000 in shares & £1,000 
                                in insurance £3,000 in cash & £1,000 in insurance
