            REPORT</b></td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td valign="top" width="100%" colspan="3" class="engfanz"> 
            <p>An abject England performance in their opening World Cup game against 
              Sweden saw Sven Goran Erikssons side having to hold on for a point 
              in Japan. England started ok, Sweden were poor early on and Sol 
              Campbell's first goal for England on 24 minutes gave the England 
              side the lead in what was a scrappy first half. Campbell out jumped 
              the Swedish defence to power home a David Beckham corner. Sweden 
              were poor in the opening fourty five minutes and did little to worry 
              the English defence but England's reliance on the long ball gave 
              away possession too many times. England were dreadful second half 
              and resembled a pub side giving the ball away and allowing Sweden 
              to come back into the game. It was not a surprise that Sweden pulled 
              level on 59 minutes Alexandersson shooting home after a mistake 
              by Danny Mills. Sweden looked the more likely to win as England 
              fell apart but held on for a draw. A very poor start, it can only 
              get better, hopefully!</p>

              after his teams poor performance against his country of birth Sweden.</p>
            <p class="engfanz">He said: &quot;It was a fair result but we could 
              have scored a second goal in the first half and won the game in 
              that way, but Sweden played well in the second half. We had the 
              chance to do it but we didn't play well in the second half. In the 
              first 35 minutes we did very well and controlled it and with a bit 
              of luck we might have scored a second goal.&quot;</p>
            <p class="engfanz">&quot;But the game changed in the second half. 
              They played long balls with good quality and were very strong at 
              winning the second ball. We suffered in the second half. It's the 
              first game we didn't win, we didn't lose and now we have to look 
              forward with great faith and hopefully we will play for more than 
              45 minutes against Argentina.&quot;</p>
            <p class="engfanz">&quot;We've got Argentina on Friday and now we've 
              got two finals (Argentina and Nigeria) if we want to go on playing 
              in the World Cup. It will be difficult for us but difficult for 
              Argentina as well.&quot;</p>
            <p class="engfanz"><b>Asked about Beckham's substitution</b>, the 
              Swede said: &quot;I didn't plan it before the match but you cannot 
              expect him to play 90 minutes when he has not played for a long 
              time. I thought he did well in the first half but in the second 
              half he got very tired. But the injured players we've had will become 
              fitter with every day.&quot;</p>
            <p class="engfanz"><b>Sweden coach Lars Lagerback</b> said: &quot;One 
              reason is that we have a lot of players playing in Britain and they 
              know the character of the players they are up against and that's 
              an advantage for us. I have mixed feelings about the result because 
              we created some good chances in the second half and with a bit of 
              luck we could have won it. They didn't create many chances and we 
              did very well in the second half.&quot;</p>
            <p class="engfanz"><b>Skipper David Beckham</b> said: &quot;A lot 
              of the lads are down in there but there's no need to be at end of 
              the day we didn't lose the game. In the first half we played some 
              good football and the lads are a bit disappointed (not to have won) 
              after being 1-0 up. In the second half we went out a bit tired and 
              we didn't start it as well as we should have done. But all credit 
              to Sweden. They did well in the second half and David Seaman kept 
              us in it. We could have ended up losing the game and it could end 
              up being a good point for us.&quot;</p>
