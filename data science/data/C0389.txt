<br><SPAN CLASS="body">
This provides a way for you to take care of all your premiums at once and is especially popular with grandparents who like the reassurance of knowing all premiums for the full term of the plan are taken care of.<P>
It works by using your lump sum to set up a funding plan which invests in Scottish Friendly's taxable fund and guarantees to pay the annual premium into your tax exempt Child Bond.<P>
If you invest the maximum lump sum of <B>&#163;2340</b> for a 10 year period this actually invests <b>&#163;270</b> a year into the Child Bond - a total of <b>&#163;2700</b>. The minimum lump sum of <b>&#163;1040</b> provides <b>&#163;120</b> a year for 10 years - a total of <b>&#163;1200</b>.<P>
If you want the payout to mature on an 18th or 21st birthday you may need the Bond to run for longer than 10 years.  For example if your child is 3 on their next birthday and you would like the Bond to mature on their 18th birthday you would need a 15 year term - the maximum lump sum you could invest would be <b>&#163;3190</b>.  If you would like to invest a lump sum to fund a Child Bond for more than 10 years please refer to the table below for the limits.</SPAN><br><br></td>
