<h2><a name="Regions">Regions & Areas & Divisions (oh my!)</a></h2>
<p>
This is a listing of Divisions, Areas, and Regions.
Some Region names are hypertext links to Regional Web pages.
For some regions, email contacts may be provided; if they are official,
or if they are only for specific SCCA activities (e.g., Solo II only),
then the entry will be so noted.  Regions desiring to have email addresses
listed or Regional web pages listed should contact
<a href="/~rwelty/">Richard Welty</a>,
<a href="mailto:rwelty@wizvax.net">rwelty@wizvax.net</a>.
<em>Regions who would like to have web pages, but are having trouble
figuring out how to manage it, should also feel free to contact
Richard at the email address listed above.</em>
</p>
<p>
In the SCCA, Divisions are administrative organizations for
the competition programs.  Areas are represented by elected Directors;
sometimes Divisions correspond directly to Areas, and sometimes
Divisions are subdivided into 2 or 3 Areas (for example, NEDiv
consists of Areas 1, 2, and 10.)  Areas are made up of Regions, which
organize local events (Club Races, Rallies, and Autocrosses.)  All
SCCA events are hosted by one or more SCCA regions, even Pro events.
</p>
<p>
Note that sometimes the boundaries may not be very obvious; for
example, the Iowa and Northeast Iowa Regions are in Central Division
(Area 5), but the Des Moines Region is in the Midwest Division (Area 6.)
</p>
<p>
In order to assist in locating Divisions and areas, the following
maps are provided.
My apologies in advance for the roughness of these scans; cleaner versions
will be along shortly.
</p>
<p>
<img src="/images/Balls/yellow.gif" width=14 height=14><a

</p>
<p>
SCCA members will, in general, be dealing with their local Regions, or
with nearby Regions.  The number in square brackets [] after the region
name is the region number; these numbers are often used as id numbers,
such as appear at the beginning of a vehicle logbook (e.g., 65-516 is
the id number for the 516th race car assigned a logbook by region 65, the
Mohawk Hudson Region -- which also happens to be the Alfetta Sedan
that belongs to the editor/author/maintainer of these pages.)
</p>
