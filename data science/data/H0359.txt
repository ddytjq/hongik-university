<p><FONT FACE="Verdana" SIZE="+2" COLOR="#FFCC00">Giardia lamblia</font></p>
<table width="100%" border="0">
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td width="90%" valign="middle"> 
      <p align="center"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Giardia 
        trophozoite </font></b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><br>
        <font size="-1">Phase contrast x 400</font></font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Giardia rarely poses 
        diagnostic difficulties when either the cyst or trophozoite stage is found. 
        <br>
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> Difficulty may stem 
        from the following: </font></p>
      <ul>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">the organism may 
          be difficult to detect in some clinical cases</font></li>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">the reader may 
          be inexperienced</font></li>
      </ul>
    </td>
    <td width=20%><img src="image/23giardia.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="23 - Giardia lamblia"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
</font>
<hr>
<font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
<table width="100%" border="0">
  <tr> 
    <td width=20%><img src="image/24giardia.jpg" width="225" height="225" align="right" alt="24 - Giardia lamblia"> 
    <td width=80%> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">In wet mounts, cysts 
        show the typical ovoid ellipsoid shape measuring from 8-19 <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"></font><font face="Symbol">&#109;</font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">m</font>, 
        the usual range being 11-14 <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"></font><font face="Symbol">&#109;</font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">m</font>. 
        </font></p>
      <p>&nbsp;</p>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>
</font>
<hr>
<font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
<table width="100%" border="0">
  <tr> 
    <td width=80%><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Mature cysts 
      have four nuclei while the immature have two. Note the varying degree of 
      clarity shown in these figures. It is possible to identify 2 nuclei, axonemes, 
      and the median bodies in these wet mounts.</font></td>
    <td width=20%><IMG SRC="image/25giardia.jpg" WIDTH="225" HEIGHT="225" BORDER="0" ALT="25 -  Giardia lamblia">

</table>
</font>

<table width="100%" border="0">
  <tr>
    <td><img src="image/43glamblia.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="43 - Giardia lamblia trophozoite"></td>
    <td> 
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Giardia 
        trophozoite</b></font><br>
        <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1">Trichrome 
        stain x 1000</font></p>
      <p>I<font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">n the trichrome stain, 
        the trophozoites exhibit the typical pyriform shape and contain two anteriorly 
        placed nuclei, one on either side of the longitudinally axonemes. <br>
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The nuclei in the 
        organisms contain centrally located karyosomes. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The dark-staining 
        median bodies can also be seen below the nuclei. </font></p>
      <p>&nbsp;</p>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>
<hr>
<table width="100%" border="0">
  <tr>
    <td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The relative position 
      of the nuclei and the median body gives the organism the typical "monkey 
      face". Flagella are rarely seen in stain preparation because they stain 
      poorly, although they are quite evident in this figure.</font></td>
    <td><img src="image/44glamblia.jpg" width="225" height="225"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
