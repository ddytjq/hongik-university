        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Beaulieu's Make 
          It A Family Affair At Saratoga </b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">No matter how it 
          gets reported, Saturday night's racing program at Saratoga Speedway 
          was a Beaulieu family affair after Jason, son Chris and brother Martin 
          all won their respective main events. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Earlier Saturday 
          it looked doubtfull that the racing program would run due the wet weather, 
          however the skies brightened up to allow a full evening of IMCA Modifieds, 
          Stock Cars and Bombers sponsonored by The OK Tire Store in Willow Point. 
          It was the first time all three Beaulieu's raced the same night at Saratoga, 
          and it turned out to be a night they'll always remember.Fourteen year 
          old <b>Chris</b>, the pupil, went out and led 18 laps to win his 2nd 
          Bomber main event of the year, followed by dad <b>Jason</b>, the teacher, 
          leading 20 laps to win the IMCA main event, then have it topped off 
          by another masterfull late race victory for uncle and brother <b>Martin</b>. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 12 car 25 lap 
          Bomber A main saw Courtenay's <b>Cody Gorosh</b> take the early race 
          lead which was quickly challenged by Beaulieu, who would take over the 
          point on the 7th lap which he would build into a sizeable lead until 
          the 14th lap when he choose to slow down behind a lapped car instead 
          of going around, which put second place driver <b>Jason Sorenson</b> 
          right on his bumper.Once around the slower car of <b>Brian Baltis,</b> 
          Beaulieu and Sorenson, subbing for <b>BJ Bradley</b>, engaged in a 10 
          lap side by side battle with Beaulieu on the inside and determined not 
          to give up the lead. He went inch for inch every lap keeping Sorenson 
          on the outside and in second until the checkered flag, giving Beaulieu 
          his 2nd straight Bomber A main victory ahead of Sorenson and Gorosh. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Bomber race 
          night began with <b>Larry Oviatt</b> winning the C dash followed by 
          dash wins for <b>Jason Waters</b>, who would have a big night with three 
          wins - in the B heat and B main, while rookie Baltis would hold his 
          own to take the win in the A dash. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Three heat races 
          prepared drivers for the two main events and they saw <b>Troy Carnie</b> 
          take the C heat , Baltis the B and point leader <b>Mike Dionne</b> took 
          top points in the A heat before dropping out of the A main early. </font></p>
