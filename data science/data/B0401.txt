        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Instant access with

          - see our current <a href="invest.htm"><b>Investment Conditions</b></a>.</font> 
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The maximum deposit 
          in the first year is &pound;3,000#. In subsequent years a maximum of 
          &pound;1,000 per year can be invested. Each year runs from 6 April to 
          5 April the following year, mirroring a 'tax year'. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Withdrawals have 
          no effect on the amount you can deposit. Once you have invested the 
          full amount for the tax year, you cannot make additional investments, 
          regardless of withdrawals made during the tax year or the balance of 
          the account. For example, if you invest &pound;3,000 in an EMA on 10 
          April 2002 and then withdraw &pound;2,000 on 6 June 2002, you will need 
          to wait until the following tax year (6 April 2003) before you can add 
          to your investment</font>.<br>
        </p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In the launch year 
          of EMA, the 'first year' runs from 25 February 2002 to 5 April 2003.

          <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The minimum opening 
          balance of this account is &pound;50. An EMA may only be held by an 
          investor in his or her sole name. Joint accounts are not permitted. 
          </font>
        <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">To be eligible for 
          the Existing Members Account (EMA) you must be an investing and/or borrowing 
          customer of Newbury Building Society. You must also have had continuous 
          membership of the Society for at least two years.</font></p>
        <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Interest rates are 
          variable. Interest is calculated daily and paid annually on 31 October. 
          Interest is normally paid or credited after deduction of income tax, 
          at the lower rate of 20% or, subject to the required certification, 
          gross.</font></p>
        <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Interest is normally 
          paid into your EMA savings account and starts to earn interest straight 
          away. We can arrange for interest to be paid into another Newbury Building 
          Society account or your bank account, if you prefer.</font></p>
        <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">An application form 
          must be completed to open an account. Applications can be made by post 
          but we would prefer you to open your account at your local branch, to 
          minimise the identification you need to provide. If you are an existing 
          investor, you will need to provide your passbook and one piece of identification 
          (ideally a passport or driving licence that confirms your current name 
          and address). If you are an existing borrower (and not an investor) 
          you need to provide two forms of identification (one to confirm name 
          and signature, one to confirm current address). If you do make an application 
          by post, you will need to provide four forms of identification (two 
          from each group above).</font></p>
        <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">This account is 
          a share account. If you have already assigned windfall benefits to the 
          Charities Aid Foundation, this assignment will continue for the full 
          5 year period. Borrowers who currently do not have a savings account 
          with the Society are exempt from Charitable Assignment on opening an 
          Existing Members' Account (EMA). If you want to know more about Charitable 
          Assignment and membership of the Society, please ask for a copy of the 
          Society's Rules and Charitable Assignment leaflet. Please read our <b><a href="invest.htm">Investment

          to our savings accounts. Please also note our <b><a href="../admin_fees.htm" target="_top">Administration
