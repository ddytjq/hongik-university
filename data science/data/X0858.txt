				<font class="news"><center><b>MATCH REPORT</b><br>Zimbabwe won by one wicket<br><br><br><b>INDIA innings </b> <br>274 for 6 off 50 overs </center><br>The hosts won the toss and led by captain Ganguly made full use of the fielding restrictions to reach 83 for 1 after 15 overs. The only wicket to fall was stand-in opener Mongia for 25. <br>Ganguly continued to plunder the Zimbabwe bowling supported by a back to form Laxman. The Indian captain was neatly stumped down the leg side by Taibu off Marillier for 57 (off 70 balls). Dravid came and went for a solid 23 - lbw sweeping Grant Flower - as India looked handily set on 171 for 3 off 33.5 overs.<br>Looking back to his best, Laxman was stupidly run out for a fine 75 (off 99 balls) and his departure was soon followed by Bangar - brilliantly caught by Friend at mid off for a duck.<br>From 171 for 5, India made slow progress with Ratra and Kaif at the crease, the latter being run out for 6 in another midwicket mix up. <br>This brought Agarkar to the middle and he wasted no time in setting about the attack with able support from Kaif. The pair added 63 from the last 6.2 overs in most exciting fashion. With mighty blows all round the ground, Agarkar raced onto 40 from just 19 balls, while Kaif finished with 39 from 45 deliveries. <br>With 39 runs from the last three overs, India&#8217;s innings closed on 274 for 6 from 50 overs. The pick of the Zimbabwe bowling was Grant Flower, who took 1 for 31 off 10 overs, as the other four bowlers used all went for more than 50 runs off their 10 overs. <br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b><br>46-1 Mongia ct Taibu b Streak 25<br>123-2 Ganguly st Taibu b Marillier 57 <br>171-3 Dravid lbw G Flower 23 <br>193-4 Laxman run out 75  <br>193-5 Bangar ct Friend b Streak 0 <br>211-6 Ratra run out 6<br>  Kaif not out 39 <br>Agarkar not out 40 <br><br> <b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 21 for 0 <br>10 overs = 55 for 1<br> 15 overs = 83 for 1 <br>20 overs = 106 for 1<br>25 overs = 136 for 2<br>30 overs = 155 for 2 <br>35 overs = 176 for 3<br>40 overs = 193 for 4<br>45 overs = 223 for 6<br>50 overs = 274 for 6<br><br> <br><br><center><b>ZIMBABWE innings</b><br> 276 for 9 off 49.4 overs </center><br>Two early strikes by a fired up Zaheer Khan rocked Zimbabwe at the start of their reply. First Khan knocked back Wishart's off stump in the first over, then removed pinch-hitter Friend, who played on to a wide delivery. <br>Campbell and Andy Flower attempted to rectify these two early losses as they kept the run rate around 4 an over, but Khan produced two consecutive maidens to force the required run rate up to 6 an over. <br>At 47 for 2 after 13 overs, Singh entered the attack and was immediately hit for a 6 and 4 by Flower in his first over. After 15 overs Zimbabwe could only reach 59 for 2, as the field was able to spread out.<br>Campbell and Flower both pushed confidently into the 30s as they upped the tempo against Singh and Bangar. The crowd awaited the introduction of Kumble to reproduce his wicket taking exploits from the recent Test series.<br>Flower reached his 50 off 59 balls with 6 fours and a six - and he looked well set for a lot more.<br>In tandem with Singh, Kumble finally entered the fray at 97 for 2 off 22 overs - and gave up 7 runs off his first over.<br>Campbell survived a run out appeal as Singh hit the sticks with a direct throw from the deep. Flower deposited Ganguly over the ropes to bring up the 100-run partnership (128 balls). Flower (71 off 72 balls) then dragged a well wide delivery from Kumble back onto his stumps. <br>Campbell and Carlisle picked off the singles as they kept the run rate at around 5 an over. After 34 overs, Zimbabwe exactly matched India's total of 171 for 3 at the same stage of its innings.<br>The 50-run partnership came up for Campbell and Carlisle in 55 balls. To stem the flow of easy runs, Khan was brought back into the attack and immediately had Carlisle (23 off 27 balls) brilliantly caught behind by a diving Ratra - 186 for 4.<br>Khan struck again by trapping Campbell (84 runs in 113 balls) right in front to reduce Zimbabwe to 193 for 5. Khan's figures read 4 for 15 off 8 overs. <br>With the wheels fast coming off Zimbabwe's reply, Singh accepted a simple return catch from Flower to leave the visitors on 198 for 6. In his next over, Singh fooled Streak with a cunning top-spinner and a leading edge looped up to Ganguly in the covers - 200 for 7. <br>Needing 12 an over, Ebrahim risked all and skied Bangar to Ganguly at deep midwicket - 210 for 8. Marillier came in at number ten and improvised four boundaries in his first 8 deliveries to give Zimbabwe a glimmer of hope - including a sweep to the off-side off Khan, which was followed by another sweep - for six over square leg, then a cheekily lobbed 4 over short fine leg's head. Khan - having giving up 15 runs in his first 8 overs - gave up 20 in his ninth. <br>Zimbabwe needed 27 off the final 3 overs.<br>Following another boundary from Marillier, Taibu was dubiously given out caught behind off what would have been a wide - 252 for 9.<br>Marillier somehow flipped a fullish delivery from Khan over the keeper's head for 4 and then repeated the act - 10 runs needed off the last over.  Marillier (52 off 21 balls) hit Kumble for four off the first ball of the last over. Followed by a single to point and a single from Brent, Marillier guided a no ball from Kumble down to third man for 4 to win the match with 2 balls remaining. Having produced one of the most remarkable one-day innings of all time, Man of the Match Marillier ended with 56 runs off 24 balls with 10 fours and a six.<br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b><br>5-1 Wishart bowled Khan 1<br>21-2 Friend bowled Khan 7<br>132-3 A Flower bowled Kumble 71<br>186-4 Carlisle ct Ratra b Khan 23 (off 27 balls)<br> 193-5 Campbell lbw Khan 84 (off 113 balls)<br>198-6 G Flower ct & b Singh 2 (off 8 balls) <br>200-7 Streak ct Ganguly b Singh 1 (of 8 balls)<br>210-8 Ebrahim ct Ganguly b Bangar 10 (off 15 balls)<br>252-9 Taibu ct Ratra b Bangar 8 (off 16 balls) <br>Marillier not out 56 <br> Brent not out 1<br><br><b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 24 for 2<br>10 overs = 39 for 2<br>15 overs = 59 for 2<br>20 overs = 86 for 2<br>25 overs = 117 for 2<br>30 overs = 151 for 3<br>35 overs = 177 for 3<br>40 overs = 198 for 6<br>45 overs = 217 for 8<br><br><br><b>TEAMS</b> <br> India: Ganguly, Mongia, Bangar, Dravid, Laxman, Kaif, Ratra, Agarkar, Kumble, H Singh, Khan.<br>Zimbabwe: Campbell, Wishart, Carlisle, A Flower, Ebrahim, G Flower, Friend, Streak, Marillier, Taibu, Brent. <br><br> <b>PITCH REPORT</b><br>"Plenty of green grass and some moisture. A very even pitch, very few cracks. It may get slower as the day wears on. An absolute belter!" from Chris Cowdrey<br><br><b>MATCH OFFICIALS</b><br><b>Umpires:</b> I Shivram & SK Porel <br><b>Third Umpire:</b> SJ Phadkar<br><b>Match Referee:</b> JR Reid<br><br>For further reports on this match go to <a href="http://www.wisden.com/static/ms/seriesODIBulletin44116230.htm" title="Wisden.com" target="_blank"><i>Wisden.com.</i></a></font><br>
