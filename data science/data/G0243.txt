            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="6">6. 
              Planetarium Education Activities</a></b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomy 
                Link: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                is a listing of astronomy research and education experts available 
                to assist planetarium educators in devising their programs and 
                outreach activities. Contact: Jim Manning, Taylor Planetarium, 
                Museum of the Rockies, Bozeman, MT 59717 (406-994-6874); [e-mail: 
                <a href="mailto:ammjm@gemini.oscs.montana.edu">ammjm@gemini.oscs.montana.edu</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>International 
                Planetarium Society: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Holds conferences, publishes <i>The Planetarian</i> magazine, 
                offers special publications and a directory. Contact them c/o 
                Hansen Planetarium, 15 S. State St., Salt Lake City, UT 84111. 
                Their web-site is <a href="http://sunsite.unc.edu/ips" target="_blank">http://sunsite.unc.edu/ips</a>. 
                A job information service is available c/o S. Fentress, Director, 
                Strasenburgh Planetarium, P.O. Box 1480, Rochester, NY 14603 (send 
                a stamped, self-addressed envelope). There are also active regional 
                organizations of planetarium staff, whose work is described in 
                <i>The Planetarian.</i> </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Lawrence 
                Hall of Science (LHS) </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                has offered an excellent series of training workshops for those 
                working with portable planetaria and has a series of activity 
                books for such planetaria (see section 2) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Learning 
                Technologies, Inc.: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Small company that makes the Starlab inflatable/portable planetaria 
                and trains teachers on how to use them. Also distributes excellent 
                kits of material for high school and college astronomy activities. 
                Contact at: 40 Cameron Ave., Somerville, MA 02144 (800-537-8703); 
                <a href="http://www.starlab.com/" target="_blank">http://www.starlab.com/</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> A 
              number of planetaria sell pre-packaged planetarium shows, including 
              the Hayden Planetarium, New York City; the Strasenburgh Planetarium, 
              Rochester, NY; the Davis Planetarium in Baltimore; and the Hansen 
              Planetarium, Salt Lake City, Utah; and many others. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="7"></a>7. 
              Programs Involving Amateur Astronomers</b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Association of Variable Star Observers: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Hands-on Astrophysics Project develops activities and materials 
                for students involving real variable star data. Contact: Janet 
                Mattei, AAVSO, 25 Birch St., Cambridge, MA 02138 (617-354-0484) 
                Further information available at www.aavso.org. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                League: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                This is the umbrella group of all the amateur astronomy clubs 
                in the U.S. They sponsor a range of national and local educational 
                programs; see their newsletter <i>The Reflector,</i> or their 
                web-site at <a href="http://www.astroleague.org" target="_blank">http://www.astroleague.org</a>. 
                (Permanent address: Astronomical League, Science Service Building, 
                1719 N. St., NW, Washington, DC 20036) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project ASTRO: forms and trains ongoing partnerships between astronomers 
                (professional & amateur) and local 4th-9th grade school teachers 
                for class visits. Astronomers turn out to make excellent partners 
                for teachers and can relate well to students. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomy 
                Day: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Annual day when amateurs around the country bring telescopes to 
                shopping centers, schools, and other sites to let the public view 
                the sky. Co-sponsored by many organizations. Contact: Gary Tomlinson, 
                Public Museum of Grand Rapids, 272 Pearl NW, Grand Rapids, MI 
                49504 (616-456-3532) <a href="http://www.mcs.net/%7Ebstevens/al/astroday.html" target="_blank">www.mcs.net/~bstevens/al/astroday.html</a>. 
                (For a copy of the Astronomy Day Handbook, see the web-site at 
                <a href="http://www.skypub.com/astroday/adayhbk.html" target="_blank">http://www.skypub.com/astroday/adayhbk.html</a>) 
                </font> 
            </dl>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="8">8. 
              Newsletters</a></b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Abrams 
                Planetarium: </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>Sky 
                Calendar</i> is a well designed monthly sheet that includes a 
                daily calendar of sky events and suggests a variety of night-sky 
                observing activities. Contact them at: Michigan State University, 
                East Lansing, MI 48824 (517-335-4676); <a href="http://www.pa.msu.edu/abrams/" target="_blank">http://www.pa.msu.edu/abrams/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Astronomical Society Working Group on Astronomy Education: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Provides an electronic newsletter on announcements and discussion. 
                Contact: Steve Shawl at <a href="mailto:shawl@ukans.edu">shawl@ukans.edu</a>; 
                or see the web site at www.aas.org. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                League: </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>The 
                Reflector</i> newsletter frequently covers educational activities 
                by or for amateurs (see section 7) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Publishes free quarterly <i>Universe in the Classroom</i> newsletter 
                for grade 3-12 teachers; co-sponsored by several other organizations. 
                Requests must be sent on school or institutional stationery; the 
                ASP is happy to provide a free subscription to any astronomer 
                actively engaged in K-12 outreach. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Association 
                of Astronomy Educators (AAE): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has a newsletter on teaching astronomy for its members [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA: 
                </b> </font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>Educational 
                Horizons</i> newsletter brings news of NASA science and education 
                activities to teachers. Contact: NASA Headquarters, Education 
                Division, Code FE, 300 E St., SW, Washington, DC 20546 </font> 
            </dl>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Note:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
              Many of the projects listed in the previous sections have newsletters 
              for their participants and supporters. </font>

            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="9"></a>9. 
              Programs for Students (K-12)</b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                League: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                National Young Astronomer Award recognizes outstanding achievment 
                in astronomy by young people 14-19 years of age. First prize is 
                a $3000 telescope. Contact: Charles Allen, NYAA, 1007 Rollingwood 
                Ln., Goshen, KY 40026 (502-589-5400); e-mail: <a href="mailto:74023.2331@compuserve.com">74023.2331@compuserve.com</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Bart 
                J. Bok Prize in Astronomy, </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                sponsored jointly by the American Astronomical Society and the 
                Astronomical Society of the Pacific, each year to the top astronomy 
                project at the International Science and Engineering Fair. Material 
                for participating in the fair is available from Science Service, 
                1719 N St., NW, Washington, DC 20036 </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NSTA/NASA 
                Space Science Involvement Program: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                A series of contests for K-12 students with space-related themes, 
                involving writing, art, and design. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>University 
                of Arizona Astronomy Camps</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                [see listing in <a href="naep05.html#12">section 12</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>U.S. 
                Space Camp: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                A commercial venture, now in three locations in Alabama, Florida, 
                and Norhtern California, has a camp for younger kids, an Academy 
                for older ones, and educator programs. U.S. Space and Rocket Center, 
                1 Tranquility Base, Huntsville, AL 35805 (1-800-63SPACE); <a href="http://www.spacecamp.com" target="_blank">http://www.spacecamp.com</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Young 
                Astronaut Council: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                This nationwide program has school or community-based chapters 
                and produces activities, contests, and materials to encourage 
                student interest in space. Contact them at: 1308 19th St NW, Washington, 
                DC 20036 (202-682-1984); <a href="http://www.yac.org" target="_blank">http://www.yac.org</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table

            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="10">10. 
              Programs for Students (College)</a></b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Astronomical Society (AAS): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Harlow Shapley Visiting Lecturer Program, a lecture series designed 
                to bring astronomers to campuses without astronomy programs in 
                the U.S.and Canada. Contact A.G. Davis Phillip, 1125 Oxford Place, 
                Schenectady, NY 12308 (518-374-5636) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Haystack 
                Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                is developing a center for undergraduate research at MIT's Haystack 
                Observatory. The education program includes materials, tutorials, 
                and tools to provide students the necessary background in radio 
                astronomy and astrophysics. Contact: Dr. Preethi Pratap, Northeast 
                Radio Observatory Corporation, MIT Haystack Observatory, Westford, 
                MA 01886 (978-692-4764); <a href="http://www.haystack.edu/haystack" target="_blank">http://www.haystack.edu/haystack</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Spacegrant College and Fellowship: </b></font>
