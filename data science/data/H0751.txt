					<h3>1 - 10 of 91</h3><p><strong><a href="http://www.agbioforum.missouri.edu/">AgBioForum</a></strong><br />AgBioForum is an online US-magazine devoted to the economics and the management of agro-biotechnology.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agbiotechnet.com/">AgBiotechNet</a></strong><br />AgBiotechNet, an online service for agricultural biotechnology, offers general information on genetic engineering, in vitro culture and ethical considerations with a list of events and news.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agcare.org/">AgCare</a></strong><br />Agricultural Groups (AgCare) concerned about recources and the environment give general information about crop production issues, news in biotechnology and activities of their members.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.iia.msu.edu/absp/">Agricultural Biotechnology Support Project (ABSP), Michigan</a></strong><br />Presentation of the Agricultural Biotechnology Support Project (ABSP) of the Institute of International Agriculture at the Michigan State University. The project aims to help developing countries to use and manage biotechnology.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agbios.com/default.asp">Agriculture and Biotechnology Strategies Inc. (AgBioS)</a></strong><br />Agriculture & Biotechnology Strategies Inc. (AgBioS), a Canadian company, consultes the agricultural biotechnology industry, government departments and producer groups. Their website offers general agricultural biotechnology related information and a huge database of articles related to biosafety, environmental impact and ethics.

</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.aebc.gov.uk/">Agriculture and Environment Biotechnology Commission (AEBC)</a></strong><br />The new Agriculture and Environment Biotechnology Commission (AEBC) offers the UK government independent strategic advice on biotechnology issues. It focuses primarily the impact of developments in biotechnology on agriculture, environment and health care.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.asa-europe.org/">American Soybean Association</a></strong><br />The American Soybean Association (ASA) is a non-profit organisation that works to strengthen soybeans as a viable crop. It fosters research that will improve genetic material available for planting. Calendar and links to nutrition and genetics.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.pi.csiro.au/">Australian research centre in the plant sciences (CSIRO)</a></strong><br />CSIRO is an Australian research centre in the plant sciences to promote profitable and sustainable agri-food and to improve natural resource management. Its main goal is genetic engineering for plant improvement.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://biosafety.ihe.be/">Belgian Biosafety Server</a></strong><br />The Belgian Biosafety Server informs about the different European regulations of the release and marketing of GMO's.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.biogene.org/">Biogene</a></strong><br />Biogene.org promotes production without genetic manipulation (GM) and focuses alternatives to GM such as organic farming or GM-free food. It is managed by the group "arche noah BioGene" of the Research Institute of Organic Agriculture (Fibl) in Switzerland and the Institute for Applied Ecology in Germany. </p>
