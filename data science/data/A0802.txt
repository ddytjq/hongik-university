      <p>Here are some details about the two main types of mortgage you can take 
        out. </p>
      <p class="bodyBold">repayment, or capital and interest mortgages</p>
      <p> These payments consist of two elements. The capital, which is the balance 
        of your original loan amount, and the interest, which is calculated on 
        the reducing balance of your mortgage. </p>
      <p>We calculate payments to make sure that as long as you always pay on 
        time, your mortgage will be paid in full at the end of the agreed period. 
        Payments are affected by interest rate changes, and will decrease or increase 
        with those changes. If the rate changes, we will always send you a letter 
        to let you know what your payments will be. </p>
      <p class="bodyBold">interest only mortgages </p>
      <p>Payments are used to pay just the interest. Payments are affected by 
        interest rate changes and will decrease or increase in line with those. 
        If the rate changes, we'll always send you a letter to tell you what your 
        payments will be. </p>
      <p>Because payments only cover the interest, the balance is not reduced, 
        and you'll have to pay the full capital amount at the end of the agreed 
        period. </p>
      <p>You must make sure that arrangements have been made to repay the loan 
        amount outstanding by the end of the agreed period. You'll have to contribute 
        to an approved investment plan, or pension policy, which will repay your 
        mortgage at the end of this period.</p>
      <p> If you take out a joint mortgage, you should be aware that the death 
        or bankruptcy of the other party could have consequences for you. You 
        don't have to take out life policies with us, but you should make arrangements 
        to safeguard your interests. Take advice from your solicitor. </p>
      <p>There are many investment plans or pension policies which can be used 
        to repay an interest only mortgage. They all have benefits and drawbacks. 
        With all interest only loans, repaying the loan early can be a problem, 
        since the investment plan or pension policy may not be enough to repay 
        the loan amount. </p>
