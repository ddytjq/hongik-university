      <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva"><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">Saturday, June 30, 1 p.m.&nbsp;<br>
          Lifelong Watkins Glen resident <b>James Scaptura</b> will talk about
          the fascination racing has always held for him. &quot;A View from
          Behind the Pits, A Personal Observation&quot; will cover the many
          changes in racing at Watkins Glen over the years. Scaptura, a retired
          high school teacher, is an avid race car model collector and a
          frequent exhibit organizer at the Racing Research Center.&nbsp;<br>
          </font><!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">Saturday, July 28, 1 p.m.<br>
          Motorsports artist <b>Robert Gillespie </b>will speak at the Center..<b><br>
          </b>Gillespie’s work is renowned for its accurate depiction of
          scenes from Watkins Glen’s own racing history. “I would describe
          my paintings as both cars within landscapes and landscapes with cars
          because at Watkins Glen these elements are equally exciting,”
          Gillespie says on his Web site, <a href="http://www.glenspeed.com" target="main">www.glenspeed.com</a>.&nbsp;<br>
          <br>
          The Racing Research Center has several of Gillespie’s pieces in its
          collection, and everyone will share the beauty of his work through a
          mural he has just begun at Salt of the Earth Gallery, North Franklin
          Street, Watkins Glen. The outdoor mural will depict Bill Milliken in
          the first race in 1948, going around the downtown curve that would
          become known as Milliken ’s Corner for the tumble the driver would
          take there later in the race.&nbsp;<br>
          <br>
          In his talk, Gillespie, a high school art teacher, will discuss how he
          became involved with motorsports art and how he researches his work,
          sometimes including interviews with the men who drove the cars.&nbsp;<br>
        <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">Saturday, Aug. 18, 1 p.m.&nbsp;<br>
          Racing Research Center Historian <b>Bill Green</b>  will talk about
          the history of NASCAR racing at Watkins Glen, with a special tribute
          to the late Dale Earnhardt. This talk comes just days after the
          Winston Cup series stop at Watkins Glen International.<br>
        <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">Saturday, Sept. 22, 1 p.m.&nbsp;<br>
          J.C. Argetsinger, a member of the Racing Research Center's Council and
          son of Watkins Glen racing founder Cameron Argetsinger, will talk
          about the MG. His talk about this much-loved car comes just days
          before the end of the visit to the Center by the historic MG TC on
          loan for the past several months.<br>
        <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva"><b>Saturday, Oct. 13, 1 p.m.</b>&nbsp;<br>
          A tribute to Lime Rock Park in Lime Rock, Conn. Racing legend John
          Fitch, who helped design Lime Rock, will join with racer Butch
          Leitzinger and filmmaker Chris Szwedo to discuss this well-known
          racetrack. Szwedo will show his recently completed PBS tribute to Lime
          Rock, &quot;Lime Rock Park, The Secret Valley of Racing.&quot;<br>
        <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva"><b>Saturday, Nov. 3, 1-5 p.m.&nbsp;</b><br>
          &quot;An Afternoon with Brock Yates.&quot;&nbsp;<br>
          This special event will offer a silent auction from 1-4:30 p.m. and a
          talk by writer Brock Yates on motorsports journalism at 3 p.m.
          Refreshments. Free, open to the public.<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>

      <h2><!--mstheme--><font face="Arial, helvetica, geneva" color="#000000">2002 events<!--mstheme--></font></h2>
      <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">This second year of the popular series starts <b>January 26</b> with
          a talk by <b>Kevin Edgley</b> a former mechanic and shock absorber
          specialist for Dan Gurney in sports car racing and Chip Ganassi in
          CART. Edgley, who now lives in Watkins Glen, will discuss his
          experiences in this grueling - and sometimes dangerous - arena of
          racing.&nbsp;<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>

      <!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
        <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/racing9/bulletblue.gif" width="10" height="10" hspace="16" alt="bullet"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="arial, helvetica, geneva">In <b>February 2002</b>, the Center welcomes <b>Corinne Stapleton</b>,
          owner with her husband, Bob, of the Chemung Speedrome in Chemung, N.Y.
          The Stapletons overcame skepticism and bureaucracy to re-open the
          historic Chemung Speedrome last year. Corinne Stapleton will talk
          about the commitment and perseverance needed for this project and will
          discuss the first year's season as well the upcoming season. The date
          for her talk has not yet been set.&nbsp;<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>

          about his beloved Ardent Alligator, the historic race car now on
          display at the Center. The bright red car is a 1929 Brooklands Riley
          modified by the Collier brothers and fitted with a 3.9-liter Mercury
          Flathead engine and drivetrain. The Alligator is of significant local
          interest as the winner of the 1949 race in Watkins Glen.&nbsp;<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>

          photographers <b>Bill Bauman and Adrian Ketchum.</b> They will talk
          about their own work at many different tracks and will discuss how the
          equipment has changed. Bauman and Ketchum also will advise on how the
          average race fan can get a winning shot. Tales from racing past return
          to the Center in May when Jim Forno of Vestal discusses his
          involvement with sports cars, which goes back to World War II and
          being stationed in England. Forno, a skillful storyteller, started
          selling imported cars in 1955 - when he wasn't racing. His racing
          experience includes Watkins Glen and Sebring. Center Conversations
          will continue through the year.&nbsp;<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
