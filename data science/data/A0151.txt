<span class="bodytextbold">First things first</span>
<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How easy is it to arrange a Buy to Let Mortgage?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">Just call us on <b>0845 606 6710</b> to apply. Alternatively, you can speak to your financial adviser who will be able to arrange a Buy to Let Mortgage on your behalf. 
<p>
Whichever way you choose to apply, you will be asked some straightforward questions about your borrowing requirements, your earnings and the property you’re looking to buy. 
<p>
Once we have your details, we’ll send out completed forms for you to check, sign and return to us, with a number of documents which are necessary to process your mortgage application.
</span>
<BR><BR>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-2"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Who can apply for a Buy to Let Mortgage?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">UK residents aged between 25 and 70 (subject to income). Joint applicants, the employed and the self-employed. Professional landlords cannot apply. Minimum employment income £20,000 for single applicants or £30,000 joint (excluding rental income).
<br><br>
If your LTV is less than 50%, you will not need to provide us with employment income verification.
</span>
<BR><BR>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-3"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">What circumstances are appropriate for a Buy to Let Mortgage?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">If you want to build a property portfolio that can provide you with an income in retirement it’s a cost-effective way to buy up to five properties. It’s also the perfect choice if you’d like to buy a property for your children when they are studying away from home or for purchasing a holiday home. In fact, it’s the ideal way to buy any property for investment purposes. 
</span>
<BR><BR>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-4"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How much can I borrow?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">We will lend up to 80% of your property’s value or purchase price, whichever is the lower. You can borrow between £40,000 and £500,000 and can purchase up to five rental properties (subject to lending terms). The maximum loan on one property must not exceed £400,000. Remember that at least 8% of the loan amount must be recouped by your rental income every year.
</span>
<BR><BR>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-5"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How do I repay?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">You can pay interest only – and if you do we strongly recommend you run a suitable investment plan alongside your mortgage, although this is not a condition of the mortgage. <p>
Alternatively, you can pay capital and interest, with each monthly payment covering part of the capital and part of the interest, reducing your outstanding mortgage balance each month. 
</span>
<BR><BR>

<a name="firstthingsfirst-6"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How long can I take to repay?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">You decide on the term of your mortgage. The minimum period is five years and it must be repaid by the time you’re 75 (subject to income). Remember, on your variable rate mortgage you also have the option to make overpayments at any time.</span>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-7"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How does the variable interest rate tiering system work?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">One of the most attractive features of our variable rate Buy to Let Mortgage is a tiered interest rate system. There are three tiers – and the more you can deposit at the start of your mortgage the better your rate of interest will be over the term of your mortgage.
</span>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-7.1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Can I mix fixed and variable rates? </span><br>
<span class="bodytext">You can choose fixed rates when they are available, but fixed and variable rates cannot be mixed at present on our Buy to Let mortgages.</span>

<a name="firstthingsfirst-7.2"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">What happens when a fixed rate or a discounted period finishes?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">The rate will automatically revert to the appropriate Buy to Let variable rate and we'll write to tell you how much your payments will be. At this point you could opt for a fixed rate, subject to availability of funds and payment of an arrangement fee.</span>

<p>
<a name="firstthingsfirst-8"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">What about security?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">Standard Life Bank will take first legal charge over the mortgaged property which must be located in the UK. All properties must be surveyed by arrangement with Standard Life Bank approved valuers. At a minimum, we require to see a standard valuation report.
</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Running your mortgage</span>
<p>
<a name="running-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How are repayments made?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">You can choose the day of the month you want to make your payments – and you can change it whenever you want by simply giving us a call. Monthly payments are made by Direct Debit and regular overpayments can be made at any time on variable rate mortgages. If you make additional payments (either as regular overpayments or lump sum) you can reduce the term of your mortgage and the interest paid on it. Lump sum overpayments are subject to a minimum of £1,000.
</span>

<p>
<a name="running-3"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">What about reviews?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">
At the initial application stage, you can decide when you want to have your account reviewed. This can be annually or when interest rates change – you can choose to alter this whenever you wish. At reviews, we will add up the total amount of all payment on your Buy to Let Mortgage and recalculate your monthly payments in line with your original term. After these adjustments you are still free to make overpayments or take payment holidays in the normal manner.
</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Flexible options</span>
<p>
<a name="flexible-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Can I make overpayments on my Buy to Let Mortgage?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">Yes.  On variable rate mortgages you can make overpayments either regularly or as a lump sum.  On fixed rated mortgages, overpayments are subject to charges during the fixed rate period.  
<P>
The effect of overpayments applies immediately because we calculate interest daily.  This can reduce the term of your mortgage and save you money in interest.
<P>
Any overpayments on fixed or variable rates you make, can be accessed at a later date. For example, if you are considering a major outlay (home improvements, perhaps) you can borrow back funds you have already overpaid and repay them at Buy to Let variable rates. What's more, you could also use your overpayment reserve to fund the deposit on another property, subject to lending terms.</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Options for the future</span>
<p>
<a name="options-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Can I buy more properties?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">You can purchase up to five rental properties in addition to your residential property with our Buy to Let Mortgage subject to lending terms. Please note that the combined balance of your mortgages with Standard Life Bank must not exceed £750,000 and no loan on a single property in your portfolio should exceed £400,000 in value. If you wish to purchase a new buy to let rental property, you must take out a new Buy to Let Mortgage. Remember, you can use your existing Buy to Let Mortgage overpayment reserve to fund the deposit for a further rental property.
<p>
Of course, if you require additional funding, we will be happy to consider your application. Call us direct on <b>0845 606 6710</b> for further information on credit re-assessment or contact your financial adviser.
</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Can I have a credit reassessment in the future?</span><br>
<span class="bodytext">
Our Buy to Let Mortgage is very flexible and can cope with your changing circumstances. If the value of your property increases for example, you could find that you qualify for a credit reassessment – which may mean you could borrow more money. If credit reassessment is carried out and you borrow additional funds on your variable rate mortgage, your loan-to-value tier may increase. </span>
