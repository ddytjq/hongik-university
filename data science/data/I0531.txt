  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 2" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  In 1946 England, Scotland, Wales and N Ireland
  rejoined FIFA and it was decided that qualification would depend on the home
  championship final table.&nbsp; We won the group winning all three matches and Scotland
  also qualified in second but decided that if they weren't going as champions,
  they wouldn't go at all and consequently withdrew,&nbsp; (a policy they have
  since changed upon realising how shit they are).&nbsp; In Brazil, England were
  drawn in the same group as Spain, Chile and the USA.&nbsp;&nbsp;</b></font>
  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 2" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  We beat Chile 2-0 but with a team consisting of
  Alf Ramsey, Billy Wright, Tom Finney, Wilf Mannion and Stan Mortenson, England
  managed to lose 1-0 to the USA.&nbsp; Jack Millburn and Stanley Mathews
  appeared for the last game against Spain but we still went down 1-0, Millburn had a perfectly good goal disallowed and England went
  home.&nbsp; Uruguay had a much easier group, just them and Bolivia who they
  managed to beat 8-0 to qualify for the final group and from there, they went on to win the
  trophy.</b></font>
