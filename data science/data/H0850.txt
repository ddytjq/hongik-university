It grows as a scyntium with haploid nuclei. A very brief diploid			phase occurs. After mating, <A HREF="../MGW1/MG1361.html">meiosis</A> follows immediately after fusion of the two haploid nuclei and			leads to the formation of eight <A HREF="MG3511.html">ascospores</A>. 			<LI><I>N. crassa</I> lacking the <I>Am</I> gene for NADP glutamate dehydrogenase was <A HREF="../MGW4/MG43.html">transformed</A> with cloned DNA containing a functional <I>Am</I>gene. Transformants were selected nutritionally and characterized			by <A HREF="../MGW4/MG423.html">Southern blotting</A> as to whether the strains contained single or multiple copies			of Am genes. 		</UL>		<P><TABLE>			<TR>				<TD WIDTH="275"><B><IMG SRC="i3321.gif"></B></TD>				<TD WIDTH="239">				<UL>					<LI>Formation of ascospores was induced by mating <I>Am</I> transformant strains with an <I>Am</I> deletion strain of opposite mating type. Strains with multiple					copies of <I>Am</I> produced only spores whose Am genes had <A HREF="../MGW4/MG421.html">restriction patterns</A> widely different from those of the <I>Am</I> transformant  parent. 					<LI><I>Am </I>transformant strains with single  copies of <I>Am</I> , when mated as above, did not produce spores with altered <I>Am</I> restriction patterns. However, crosses of two sisngle <I>Am</I> gene strains resulted in altered patterns after a sceond round					of mating. 				</UL></TD>			</TR>		</TABLE>		</P>		<UL>			<LI>Altered <I>Am</I> genes had  numeorus <A HREF="MG311.html">point mutations</A>.  These were asymmetric with respect to DNA strand.  For example,			in 2.6 kbp of DNA, 158 G's on one strand were converted to A's.			No C's were converted to T's on this strand. The mutated regions			had most of their C's methylated, even though <I>N. crassa</I> generally has very low levels of <A HREF="../MGW1/MG138.html">5-meC</A>. 			<LI>Cloned (and thus demethylated) altered genes reacquired <A HREF="../MGW1/MG138.html">methylation</A> when transformed into <I>N.crassa</I>. 		</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A HREF="#Facts">Facts</A> | <A HREF="#Interp">Interpretations</A> | <A HREF="#Info">Further Info.</A> | <A HREF="#bottom">Other Pages</A> </P>		</CENTER>		<H4><A NAME="Interp"></A>Interpretations </H4>		<UL TYPE="circle">			<LI>RIP is induced by the presence of two or more copies of the same			sequence in haploid nuclei in stages just preceding nuclear fusion			and meiosis. 			<LI>Though <A HREF="../MGW1/MG138.html">cytosine methylation</A> could result in the exclusive G to A (or C to T) <A HREF="MG311.html">transversion</A> pattern, methylation may be a result rather than a cause of RIP.						<LI>RIP severely limits the amount of repetitive DNA in the <I>N. crassa</I> genome. 			<LI>RIP accelerates the evolution of duplicated genes.<IMG SRC="nav4.gif" WIDTH="69" HEIGHT="71" ALIGN="right" USEMAP="#nav4B1F889C9">			<MAP NAME="nav4B1F889C9">				<AREA COORDS="4,53,26,69" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG331.html">				<AREA COORDS="41,56,64,72" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG333.html">				<AREA COORDS="4,29,26,50" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG39.html">				<AREA COORDS="24,3,44,27" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG3.html">				<AREA COORDS="43,29,58,56" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG34.html">				<AREA COORDS="26,30,40,56" SHAPE="rect" HREF="MG33.html">			</MAP>				</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A HREF="#Facts">Facts</A> | <A HREF="#Interp">Interpretations</A> | <A HREF="#Info">Further Info.</A> | <A HREF="#bottom">Other Pages</A> <BR>			<BR>			</P>		</CENTER>		<H4><A NAME="Info"></A>Further information</H4>		<UL TYPE="square">			<LI>The alteration occurs premeiotically. It was originally designated			&quot;rearrangement induced premeiotically&quot; and abbreviated <STRONG>RIP</STRONG>. 			<LI>The alterations in restriction patterns reflect extensive point			mutations, not rearrangements. The term &quot;RIP&quot; has stuck but now			stands for &quot;repeat-induced point mutation&quot;. 			<LI> RIP may be related to <A HREF="../MGW1/MG1383.html">paramutation</A>. 		</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A NAME="bottom"></A><A HREF="MG331.html">Last</A> | <A HREF="MG39.html">Vocabulary</A> | <A HREF="MG33.html">Overview</A> | <A HREF="#Facts">Top</A> | <A HREF="MG334.html">Next</A><BR>			<BR>			</P>		</CENTER>		<P>		<HR>		</P>		<P>This is page 332 of <A HREF="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</A> by <A HREF="http://biochem.okstate.edu/faculty/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</A>, &copy; 1997, 1998<BR>		<BR>		<HR>		</P>		<ADDRESS>E-mail inquiries to <A HREF="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu" TO/ UMELCHER@BIOCHEM.OKSTATE.EDU">U.
