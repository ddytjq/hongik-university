		<td><h2>Saving for the future<br></h2></td>
	</tr>
	<tr> 
		<td>
			<img src="/images/spacer.gif" height="1" width="10" alt="spacer">
		</td>
		<td> 
			<p class="biggreentext">What are your long-term aims?</p>
            <ul class="plaintext">
	            <li>Capital growth for future security - longer term savings with interest added to build up your savings?<br>OR<br></li>
	            <li>An income from your savings - interest paid regularly for you to use?<br><br></li>
		    </ul>
			<p class="plaintext">
			Having decided your aims, there are a number of things to consider when choosing a savings account.  Our <a href="#tree">Decision tree</a> may help.</p><br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Can you save over several years or will you need access to your money?</p>	
			<p class="plaintext">
			You will get the best return on your savings if you can afford to put your money away for several years, into an account where access is limited.  If you are going to need access to your savings sooner then you may need an account which does not penalise you for withdrawing funds.
			</p><br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Do you want to deposit a lump sum or to save on a regular basis?</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			In choosing an account for a lump sum you will need to consider, for example, the minimum deposit requirements, whether you can add to your initial lump sum and if there is a &quot;maturity&quot; date.<br>
			<br>
			When choosing an account for regular payments, look out for any restrictions or limits.  If you choose the tax advantages of an ISA, make sure that you don't exceed the maximum amount for that tax year, to avoid being taxed on your interest.
			</p><br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Do you want risk-free savings or will you take a risk for a higher return?</p>
			<table border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="4" width="100%" bordercolor="#FFD900">
                	<tr>
						<td height="30" align="center" bgcolor="#FFFFCC">
							<p class="boldtext">Higher risk investments</p>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="left">
							<ul class="plaintext">
								<li>These are generally &quot;equity&quot; based investments, i.e. those based on investing on the stock market, although there is a wide variety of high risk investments.<br><br>
								<li>Examples are shares in particular companies, Unit Trusts or equity based ISAs.<br><br>
								<li>The potential returns are higher but so are the risks in that the value of your savings could go down as well as up.<br><br>
								<li>If you are interested in high risk investments, make sure you get sound financial advice first.<br><br>
								<li>We can arrange an interview with a Legal & General Financial Adviser, which can be held at one of <a href="/contact_us/branch_finder.jsp">our branches</a> or in your own home.
							</ul>
						</td>
					</tr>
					<tr>
						<td height="30" align="center" bgcolor="#FFFFCC">
							<p class="boldtext">Low risk savings</p>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="left">
							<ul class="plaintext">
								<li>A Building Society account offers a great risk-free way of saving.<br><br>
								<li>If you do not need regular access to your money or can leave your money untouched for a longer period, you can get higher rates of interest.
							</ul>
						</td>		
					</tr>
					<tr>
						<td height="30" align="center" bgcolor="#FFFFCC">
							<p class="boldtext">Monthly income</p>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="left">
							<ul class="plaintext">
								<li>You can generally get an investment that pays a regular income whatever type of savings you select.<br><br>
								<li>With this option, the interest your savings earn is paid into an account of your choice, generally monthly.
							</ul>
						</td>
					</tr>
				</table>
			<br><br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Offshore Accounts</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			Depending on your circumstances, you could consider an offshore account.  Interest from offshore accounts is paid gross, i.e. there is no deduction of tax, but it is your responsibility to disclose to the relevant tax authority the amount of interest credited.  For more information about offshore accounts, visit the <a href="http://www.yorkshireguernsey.gg/" target="_new">Yorkshire Guernsey</a> website.  Yorkshire Guernsey is a wholly owned subsidiary of the Yorkshire Building Society.
			</p>
			<br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Online Research</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			There are a number of financial sites offering free advice.<a href="http://www.thisismoney.com" target="_new">This is money</a> is a good place to start.
			</p><br>
