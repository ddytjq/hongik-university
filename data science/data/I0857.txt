Duncan signs new U's deal
9 Jul<br>
			<SPAN Class='gentext' ><P>Cambridge United defender Andy Duncan has agreed a new contract with the Nationwide Division Three club despite late interest from another side.</P><P>The 24-year-old former Manchester United trainee is a key figure in manager John Taylor's future plans and the U's boss is delighted to have got his man.</P><P>"There was some late interest from another football club but we've tried to be as honest with each other as possible and Andy gave me his word he would sign, and he has," Taylor told the club's official website.</P><P>"The enquiry came out of the blue over the weekend but he didn't even speak to the club concerned and signed last night.</P><P>"He's delighted to be on board, as we are to have him."</P><P>Duncan has played almost 150 League games for Cambridge in almost five year with the club after starring in just over half the club's fixtures last season as the U's were relegated from the second division.</P>
