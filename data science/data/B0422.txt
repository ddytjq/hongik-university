		<td> 
			<h2>Buying your first home</h2>
		</td>
	</tr>
	<tr> 
		<td>
			<img src="/images/spacer.gif" height="1" width="10" alt="spacer">
		</td>
		<td> 
			  <p class="plaintext">Buying a home and getting a mortgage for the first time can be a little confusing. 
			  This section brings together the help and advice you need to get started.<br><br>
			  And don't forget our <a href="/mortgages/jargon_master.jsp">Jargon Master</a> if there's any word or term 
			  you don't understand.</p>
			  <ul class="plaintext">
			  <li><a href="#choosing">Choosing a suitable mortgage</a></li>
			  <li><a href="#cost">How much can I borrow and what's the cost?</a></li>
			  <li><a href="#othercosts">Other costs</a></li>
			  <li><a href="#next">Next steps</a></li>
			  <li><a href="#linksbuying">Useful links for buying your home</a></li>
			  <li><a href="#linksplanning">Useful links for planning your move</a></li>
			  <li><a href="/mortgages/mortgage_needs/decision.jsp">Decision Tree</a></li>
			  </ul>
			  <br>
			  <p class="biggreentext"><a name="choosing"></a>Choosing a suitable mortgage</p>
			  <p class="plaintext">Most lenders have several types of mortgage available, so firstly you'll need to be clear about what you really want.  For example, you may want a mortgage that discounts your early payments to help with cash flow.  Or you may prefer the security of a fixed rate mortgage, so if interest rates rise, 
			  your mortgage stays at the level you've budgeted for.<br><br>
			  For an overview, our section <a href="/mortgages/arranging/mortgage_types.jsp">Types of mortgages</a> explains all the different loans, 
			  and for details of our latest offers, visit our <a href="/mortgages/current_products/index.jsp">Current products</a> section. You can also use our mortgage 
			<a href="/mortgages/mortgage_needs/decision.jsp">Decision tree</a> to help you choose which mortgage could be right for you.</p>
				<br>
				<a name="cost"></a>
			  <p class="biggreentext">How much can I borrow and what's the cost?</p>
			  <p class="plaintext">Working out how much deposit you can afford and how much you are able to borrow is 
			  always a priority.  Without knowing how much you can borrow, you won't be able to know the price bracket 
			  of the homes you can look for.<br><br>
			  The bigger your deposit the better, but usually you'll be asked for a minimum of 5% of the purchase price. 
			  Sometimes it is possible to get a mortgage without a deposit (a 100% mortgage) depending on your 
			  circumstances and the amount you want to borrow.<br><br>
			  To start working out a few details you can use our two calculators. Our <a href="https://online.ybs.co.uk/public/mortgages/enquiry/quick_enquiry.jsp">Borrowing Calculator</a> will let you 
			  know how much you can borrow, and our <a href="https://online.ybs.co.uk/public/mortgages/calculator/quick_calc.jsp">Cost Calculator</a> will show you the monthly payment of a mortgage 
			  based on the loan amount and type you've chosen.<br><br>
			  For some extra help, <a href="/mortgages/arranging/lending_rules/income.jsp">Income requirements</a> 
			  explains how we work out what we can lend to all applicants, including joint applications, and also how to 
			  include overtime, bonuses and other income.<br><br>
			  When you're working out how much you can afford, don't forget to allow for all your monthly outgoings so 
			  that you can comfortably meet your monthly payments.</p>
			  <br>
			  <a name="othercosts"></a>
			  <p class="biggreentext">Other costs</p>
			  <p class="plaintext">When you buy a house there are several other 
                costs you are likely to incur:</p>
              <table width="100%" border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="6" bordercolor="#000066">
                <tr> 
                  <td height="40" bgcolor="#99ccff"> 
                    <p class="boldtext">Description</p>
                  </td>
                  <td height="40" bgcolor="#99ccff"> 
                    <p class="boldtext">Cost</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Valuation</b><br>
                      Typically, a lender will send a valuer to assess your prospective 
                      home before agreeing a mortgage. The cost of this is met 
                      by you.</p>
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Survey</b><br>
                      You may also want to have either a Home Buyer Survey or 
                      a full Building Survey carried out by a specialist surveyor. 
                      This is optional and, again, the cost will be met by you.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Both depend on your property value. See 
                      <a href="/mortgages/arranging/costs.jsp">The costs involved</a> 
                      for our charges.</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Application fees</b><br>
                      May be required depending on the mortgage you choose.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Check the terms of your mortgage</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Insurance</b><br>
                      Taking out buildings <a href="/mortgages/insurance/index.jsp">Insurance</a> 
                      will be a condition of any mortgage you have. You may also 
                      want to consider contents cover and mortgage payment protection.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Depends on value of your home and level 
                      of cover. See our <a href="https://online.ybs.co.uk/public/mortgages/pmi/pmi_intro_page.jsp"> 
                      Mortgage Illustrator</a> for an estimate</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Conveyancing fees</b><br>
                      You will need legal advice when buying or selling your home, 
                      and someone to do all the necessary legal work. This could 
                      be a solicitor or a licensed conveyancer.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Costs vary significantly and are often 
                      related to the value of the home you are buying. It's best 
                      to get several estimates.</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Land registry</b><br>
                      Your solicitor or licensed conveyancer should register you 
                      as the new owner of the property.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">There is a sliding scale depending on 
                      the value of the property. For example, the cost is:<br>
                      &pound;60 for property worth between &pound;50 - &pound;60,000.<br>
                      &pound;100 for property worth between &pound;90 - &pound;100,000.</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Mortgage Repayment Protection </b><br>
                      Mortgage Repayment Protection pays either a lump sum equal 
                      to the outstanding balance on a mortgage agreement or pays 
                      the monthly mortgage repayment. Most plans provide one or 
                      more of the following benefits: life cover, disability cover 
                      resulting from accident or sickness, hospitalisation cover, 
                      critical illness cover or redundancy.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">The cost of Mortgage Repayment Protection 
                      depends on the amount of benefit you wish to take out, the 
                      type of cover, and the excess period. </p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Mortgage Risk Payment (MRP)</b><br>
                      Lending a higher portion of the purchase price of a property 
                      brings with it a higher risk to the lender. This risk is 
                      alleviated by a Mortgage Risk Payment (MRP) which provides 
                      the lender with a form of insurance. If you cannot repay 
                      your mortgage, and the lender is forced to sell your property 
                      for less than your outstanding loan, the shortfall may be 
                      claimed back through this insurance.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Whether you pay on MRP, and how much 
                      you pay, will depend on the amount of loan compared to the 
                      value or purchase price of the property (whichever is the 
                      lower). This amount is shown as a percentage and is described 
                      as the Loan to Value (LTV). </p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Search Fees</b><br>
                      There are a variety of searches that are typically carried 
                      out by your solicitor or licensed conveyancer when buying 
                      a home, depending on the area the property is in. These 
                      find out, for example, about any developments which may 
                      affect your home, any known mineral deposits or coal mining 
                      in the area, etc. Any fees for these searches will be paid 
                      by you.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">The costs vary depending on the type 
                      of searches carried out and where the property is situated. 
                      A local authority search, for example, could be &pound;100 
                      or more, whilst water authority searches are typically &pound;30. 
                      Your solicitor or licensed conveyancer will let you know 
                      what searches are needed and how much they cost.</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Stamp duty</b><br>
                      If the house you intend to buy is over a certain price, 
                      you'll need to pay the government stamp duty. Your legal 
                      adviser will arrange this and charge you accordingly.<br>
                      <br>
                      For more information visit <a href="http://www.inlandrevenue.gov.uk/so/so1.htm">www.inlandrevenue.gov.uk</a></p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Stamp Duty is charged as a percentage 
                      of the purchase price as follows:<br>
                      Properties under &pound;60,000 - exempt<br>
                      Properties over &pound;60,000 - 1%<br>
                      Properties over &pound;250,000 - 3%<br>
                      Properties over &pound;500,000 - 4%</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Home improvements</b><br>
                      Depending on the property you are buying, you may want to 
                      keep some money in hand for essential improvements.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Remember, they always cost more than 
                      you expect!</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
                <tr> 
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext"><b>Removals</b><br>
                      You can either use a professional removal company or do 
                      it yourself. Whatever you do, it is wise to have insurance 
                      to cover breakages en-route.</p>
                  </td>
                  <td valign="top"> 
                    <p class="plaintext">Prices vary, depending on the company 
                      and the distance involved. Make sure you shop around. If 
                      you're using a professional removal company you need to 
                      allow several hundred pounds.</p>
                  </td>
                </tr>
              </table>
			  <br><br>
			  <a name="next"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Next steps</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Hopefully we've given you a taste of all the things you need to think about. 
			Our <a href="/mortgages/arranging/index.jsp">Arranging a mortgage</a> section has lots of helpful information for you, and don't forget to use our 
			<a href="/mortgages/jargon_master.jsp">Jargon Master</a> to look up any unfamiliar words or terms.<br><br>
			Once you have worked out the mortgage you'd like and how much you can borrow, it's easy to make an 
			<a href="https://online.ybs.co.uk/public/mortgages/aip/index.jsp">Application online</a> or receive an application form in the post. See 
			<a href="/contact_us/index.jsp">Contact us.</a><br><br>
			The next section is the fun part. Why not click on some of our useful links to start looking for your 
			home and planning your move.</p><br>
			<a name="linksbuying"></a>
			<table width="67%" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
  <tr> 
    <td> 
      <p class="biggreentext">Useful links for buying your home</p>
	  <p class="boldtext">Find an estate agent</p>
	  <p class="plaintext">To find an estate agent try <a href="http://handbag.ukpropertyshop.com" target="_new">handbag.ukpropertyshop.com</a></p>
	  <p class="boldtext">Find a home online</p>
	  <p class="plaintext">
	  If you'd like to look for a home online, both <a href="http://www.home.co.uk" target="_new">www.home.co.uk</a> and <a href="http://www.findaproperty.com" target="_new">www.findaproperty.com</a> are useful sites.</p>
	  <p class="boldtext">Check out prices, local services, facts and figures</p>
	  <p class="plaintext">
	  It's also good to get to know the area before you make a commitment to buy.  By entering a postcode on <a href="http://www.upmystreet.co.uk" target="_new">www.upmystreet.co.uk</a> you can discover local services and shops, examine property prices, nearby schools and also the record of the local council.<br>
<br>
Another useful site, sponsored by the government, is <a href="http://www.annualreport.gov.uk/inyourarea" target="_new">www.annualreport.gov.uk/inyourarea</a>. This provides key government facts and figures - good and bad - for an area, together with other interesting information.
