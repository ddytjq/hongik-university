			<p class="biggreentext">Which inspection is right for me?</p>
			<p class="boldtext">The mortgage valuation</p>
			<p class="plaintext">The mortgage valuation is solely for our purposes so that we can be satisfied that 
			the property provides sufficient security for us to lend. The mortgage valuation is not a survey and does not give any 
			indication as to whether the property is worth what you are paying for it, nor does it provide a list 
			of any repairs that may be needed.</p>
			<p class="boldtext">Home Buyer Survey and Valuation</p>
			<p class="plaintext">We can arrange to carry out the Home Buyer Survey and Valuation (HSBV) for you at 
			the same time as the mortgage valuation.  Presented in a standard format, it is designed to focus on 
			defects and problems which are considered urgent or significant. An HSBV is suitable for properties which 
			are of standard type and construction and appear to be in reasonable condition.</p>
			<p class="boldtext">Building Survey</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This type of survey is suitable for all types of residential properties and, in particular, for period properties, 
			those with extensive accommodation or those in a particularly poor state of repair although it can be done for any 
			type of residential property.  It is tailored to your requirements but will be very detailed, covering 
			all defects whether major or minor.  There is no standard or scale fee for a Building Survey but the cost 
			will be significantly higher than for the HSBV.  In most cases, the cost does not include a valuation so 
			a mortgage valuation will need to be done as well.</p>

              <p class="biggreentext">Application fees</p>
			  <p class="plaintext">With some special mortgage offers, you may have 
                to pay what's known as an application fee, the first part of which 
                is non-refundable should your mortgage not proceed. The main reason 
                for this is that with some special products, we actually &quot;commit&quot; 
                part of our available funds, so that these are not lent to anyone 
                else. If your mortgage does not complete, we suffer a loss. Normally, 
                you will be asked to pay part of the fee when you apply. You can 
                then pay the remainder of the fee when your mortgage funds are 
                released, or it can be collected as part of the monthly mortgage 
                payment following the next annual review of your account.</p>
