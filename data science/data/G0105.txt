<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i>What are the Level 1 and Level

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'>See charts in the attached

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><i>How do these motivate the candidate
technologies which will be investigated? Explain the connections between the
science requirements and the engineering requirements on those technologies.<o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>Photometric System:</p>

<p class=MsoBodyText>The requirements for a very large instrumented field of
view with good photometric sampling over a wide wavelength range have led to a
mission concept with a single focal plane with both visible and infrared
detectors.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The development of
multiple large area focal planes by the optical system is cost
prohibitive.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>To satisfy the
sampling requirements on a single focal plane the pixel size of the visible and
infrared detectors must be approximately in proportion to the wavelengths
observed.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>For the infrared system,
the requirements are very similar to those developed for WFC3. <span
style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span>The operating temperature, wavelength
coverage, and electrical performance are all similar.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>For that reason, we are considering the 18 micron pixel,
wavelength limited devices, developed by Rockwell for WFC3 to be an excellent
candidate technology for our IR system (1 micron to 1.7 micron).<span
style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>By similar reasoning, we are looking at
radiation tolerant visible light detectors (0.4 to 1 micron) with 10.5 micron
pixel sizes that can be operated on the same focal plane and at the same
temperature (140 K) as the IR detectors.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span>Given the large number of devices needed (36 in each technology) both
production manufacture and the potential of miniaturized electronics are key
components of our R&amp;D strategy.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class=MsoBodyText>From the Level 2 Science Requirements, the 3-year mission
lifetime, the observation strategy, and supernova fluxes as a function of
redshift, it is possible to calculate the required engineering parameters of
the imaging system.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The details of
this computation and optimization are beyond the scope of this note. The
results are as follows:</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal>There are six key technical issue/challenges for SNAP:</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>1)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>The
optical sensor technology: a) radiation hardness, b) the long wavelength QE
(700-1000 nm), c) small pixel size, d) production volume.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>2)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>The
IR sensors: a) detector characteristics: i.e. QE, readnoise, dark current, b)
production volume</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>3)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Electronic
readout technology for each of the sensor technologies</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>4)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>IFU
spectrograph </p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>5)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Achieving
&lt;2% errors in spectrum slope from visible through NIR</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.5in;text-align:justify;text-indent:-.25in;
mso-list:l1 level1 lfo1;tab-stops:list .5in'><![if !supportLists]>6)<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Mission
related issues: pointing (jitter and stability), telemetry, data storage</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i>(3) For each technological challenge, what are the
metrics by which success --- i.e., technical readiness --- is to be measured?<o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoBodyText>The detector technologies will require specific
demonstrations to ensure that a mission of the scope of SNAP can be achieved
with a minimum of risk.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>A
technology readiness level of 5 would be expected by the end of phase A and a
readiness level of 6 by the end of phase B would be a compromise between
minimization of risk and ensuring adequate time to refine the
technologies.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Miniaturized
electronic readout of imagers has been used for years in consumer devices;
however, the large dynamic range and low noise requirements for astronomical
observations place new demands on the electronics.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>A demonstration that there are no insurmountable problems
with the electronics concept will be required.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>A TRL of 5 for the electronics needs to be achieved by the
end of phase B.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Both detector and
electronics technologies will require some degree of deployment to ensure that
specifications can be met for astronomical observations.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'>A 2 x 2 arcsec image slicer
appears to be required to permit semi-autonomous pointing of the spacecraft at
the supernova and at to obtain the host galaxy spectrum simultaneously with the
supernova spectrum.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The integral
field spectrograph is of a new design using a glass image slicer; although
theoretically this concept is low risk, the concept has yet to be demonstrated
and technology development is required.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='text-align:justify'>Calibration techniques need to be
developed that assure that overall spectrum slope errors can be kept below
about 2%.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>This work will include
some combination of optical/NIR observations using ground-based and/or SOFIA,
balloon, or spaceborne instruments. </p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i>(4) What kinds of demonstrations are required to validate
technical readiness?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Will ground
testing be sufficient, or are there technical capabilities which can only be
demonstrated in space?<o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoBodyText>We would expect to deploy science grade device visible and
IR devices to ground-based observatories by the end of phase A as a necessary
demonstration of the intended detector technologies.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Radiation hardness, thermal cycling, etc., can be adequately
addressed by ground based techniques.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i>(6) Is the required capability a reasonable extension of
the current capability or does it require a significant advancement or new
approach?<o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal><i><![if !supportEmptyParas]>&nbsp;<![endif]><o:p></o:p></i></p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo2;tab-stops:list .75in'><![if !supportLists]>1.<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Optical
sensor technology: The technology is mature with the remaining challenges being
industrial-like production and operation with either thinner devices or larger
depletion voltage (100V) in a space-like environment.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo2;tab-stops:list .75in'><![if !supportLists]>2.<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>IR
sensors: If WFC3 requirements are achieved, these will be adequate for SNAP. To
date, the dark current is fine, the readnoise needs to achieve the Rockwell
spec of 10 e versus the present ~30 e, and the QE needs to be corrected.
Actions to adjust the QE are underway at Rockwell as we write. If the Rockwell
readout ASIC does not materialize we will have to design and construct our own
equivalent.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo2;tab-stops:list .75in'><![if !supportLists]>3.<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Electronic
readout: Our preliminary analysis shows that 0.25 <span style='font-family:
Symbol'>m</span>m CMOS can meet the low noise requirements of the analog
processing and digitization chain. Single transistor measurements performed at
150K indicate that low temperature operation is possible and that the
technology simulation models are valid to this temperature. Clock pattern
generation and amplitude control should be easy to integrate into an additional
IC. How best to perform the large voltages for clocks and biases remains to be
analyzed.</p>

<p class=MsoNormal style='margin-left:.75in;text-align:justify;text-indent:
-.25in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo2;tab-stops:list .75in'><![if !supportLists]>4.<span
style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><![endif]>Mission
related issues: Telemetry and data storage requirements for SNAP appear to be
compatible with the natural evolution of these technologies and we believe the
required K-band telemetry bandwidth and SSR buffer memory size will be
available in a timely fashion.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
</span>Cross-wavelength relative calibration from 0.4 to 1.7 microns will push
the state-of-the-art.</p>
