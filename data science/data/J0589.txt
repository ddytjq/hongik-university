                    <td colspan="2" height="14" class="titrearticle" valign="top" width="532">Cautious Ukawa beats risky Rossi in Welkom thriller</td>

                  <tr>
                    <td height="5"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="5" height="5"></td>

                      <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
                        <tr>
                          <td class="texte10">South African Grand Prix / Final report / 21 April</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                          <td>
                              <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" width="536">
                                <tr valign="top">
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                    <hr><font  color='000000' ><b>Ukawa's conservative tyre choice proves perfect in sweltering South African race.</b></font>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="67" height="36"></td>
                                </tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                    <hr><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td>
                                </tr>
                                
                                
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Podium" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/pod10.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >A delighted </font><font  color='0000cc' >Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > scored his first win in bike racing's premier class at Welkom today, defeating team-mate </font><font  color='0000cc' >Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > in a thrilling end-of-race showdown. The four-stroke RCVs dominated the race from the first lap, chased hard by</font><font  color='0000cc' > Loris Capirossi (West Honda Pons NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > who was by far the best two-stroke performer in today's sweltering 32-degree heat. Michelin riders dominated the event, the first dry race of MotoGP's four-stroke era, taking the first nine places. Michelin riders also hold nine of the first ten places in the current World Championship standings.</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          
                                          <img align="left" alt="Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/uka3d3.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='000000' >	Ukawa was able to defeat Rossi, winner of the season-opening Japanese GP, because he'd made a more cautious rear-tyre choice. Welkom is the toughest of all GP tracks for tyre endurance and the Japanese chose a harder 16.5in rear while Rossi went for a medium-compound rear.<br>	</font><font  color='0000cc' >"This morning we convinced Ukawa and his crew that the harder tyre was the best for this race,"</font><font  color='000000' > explained </font><font  color='0000cc' >Michelin Grand Prix manager Emmanuel Fournier</font><font  color='000000' >.</font><font  color='0000cc' >" We recommended the same tyre to Rossi but he was determined to run a medium compound, and, of course, tyre choice is ultimately the rider's. We knew that his choice would be risky for the last few laps and the tyre did prove a bit too soft for the right handers. But our congratulations to both of them, they rode a great race, riding hard all the way to the flag. Loris chose the same tyre as Rossi up, which worked well for him, and he too rode a brilliant race."</font><font  color='000000' ></font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/uka2d4.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >	Rossi led much of the race, leading from pole position and staying there for the first 19 laps, shadowed all the way by Ukawa, in determined mood after crashing out at Suzuka two weeks ago. On lap 20 Rossi slowed momentarily to force Ukawa into the lead, so he could study his rival. He repassed him with three laps left but out-braked himself halfway through the final lap, running wide into The Boot' hairpin where Ukawa went back ahead. The Italian came back for one last go at the penultimate turn where he regained the lead for a second before running wide again.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >	"Today was just like when we were racing 250s in 1999, but the result was not!"</font><font  color='000000' > grinned Ukawa, who was beaten to the '99 250 title by Rossi. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"My tyres were fantastic; I chose a hard rear and this was the best choice because I could see Valentino was having a lot of slides through the rights during the final laps. Then he made a mistake and I passed him okay. I'm very happy!"</font><font  color='000000' ></font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          
                                          <img align="left" alt="Start" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/startd24.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='000000' >	Rossi, who extended his series points lead with his runner-up finish, was smiling despite the defeat. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"This is good for the championship,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the 125, 250 and 500 king who has always liked to entertain the fans. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"My rear tyre was a little too soft for the rights. My plan was to get a good start and run a very fast rhythm but Ukawa came with me. After those few laps behind him I pushed harder to break away but still he came with me. I made my first mistake braking for a hairpin and though I recovered from that to make another attack, I arrived too fast and I couldn't make the corner. Next time..."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>

                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">

                                          <font  color='000000' >	Capirossi ran close to the RCV until slipping behind in the second half of the race. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"On new tyres I could just stay with them,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the Italian. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I'm happy to be first 500 but the gap between the two-strokes and four-strokes is already big and it will get bigger as the season goes on."</font><font  color='000000' ><br>	</font><font  color='0000cc' >Daijiro Katoh (Fortuna Honda Gresini NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > had an inspired first dry race on a 500, taking fourth place on the last lap from </font><font  color='0000cc' >Carlos Checa (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > who now lies second on points. </font><font  color='0000cc' >Olivier Jacque (Gauloises Yamaha Tech 3 YZR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > was less than a second behind Checa at the flag and two seconds ahead of </font><font  color='0000cc' >Norick Abe (Antena 3 Yamaha-d'Antin YZR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > and</font><font  color='0000cc' > Shinya Nakano (Gauloises Yamaha Tech 3 YZR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >. </font><font  color='0000cc' >Max Biaggi (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin) </font><font  color='000000' >had a lonely ride to ninth after a slow getaway from the front row of the grid.</font>
