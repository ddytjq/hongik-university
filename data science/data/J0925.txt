    <td class="texte10">One year after making its Formula 1 comeback, <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font> 
      reflects on the 2001 season and outlines its targets for the campaign ahead. 
      <p>In 2001 <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font> Group president <font color="#0000cc">Edouard 
        Michelin</font> said: <font color="#0000cc"><i>&quot;This sport has evolved 
        considerably during the 17 years since we last competed in F1. Car technology 
        has changed and the same is true of tyres. It is going to be a major challenge 
        for us, but that is just the kind of thing Michelin enjoys.&quot;</i></font></p>
      <p>Spurred on by his words, <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font>'s F1 partners 
        rose to the task and the first season back fulfilled even the most optimistic 
        expectations. Between them, <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font>'s five 
        teams performed with great brio to notch 4 victories, 4 pole positions, 
        4 second places and 3 thirds. </p>
      <p>In 2001 <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font> had the daunting task of 
        competing in the highest-profile form of all motor sports while discovering 
        the trade secrets of tracks it didn't know, learning about new grooved 
        tyres and working with a particularly determined set of partners - not 
        the most straightforward of challenges.</p>
      <p>Nevertheless, <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font> can take great satisfaction 
        from the fact that <font color="#0000cc">BMW WilliamsF1 Team</font> drivers 
        <font color="#0000cc">Ralf Schumacher</font> and <font color="#0000cc">Juan 
        Pablo Montoya</font> set a number of fastest race laps and also contributed 
        to some great lead battles against illustrious rivals. </p>

      <p class="texte12bold">You won four races last season. Will you know how 
        to do even better in 2002?</p>
      <p><font color="#0000cc"><i>&quot;One can't be certain,&quot;</i></font> 
        says <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font>'s competition director <font color="#0000cc">Pierre 
        Dupasquier</font>. <font color="#0000cc"><i>&quot;Seriously, we ought 
        to do better, much better. We can no longer make the excuse that we don't 
        know the tracks and the effectiveness of some of our partner teams is 
        already proven. From now on, the challenge will be to show that Michelin 
        is as competitive as people assume we are following our victories last 
        season.&quot; </i></font></p>
      <p>The tyre regulations have not changed for 2002 and so drivers retain 
        an allocation of 10 sets of grooved tyres and 7 sets of wets per grand 
        prix.</p>
      <p>The seasonal schedule begins as usual with three races outside Europe. 
        The only real change to customary procedure comes in July, when the French 
        Grand Prix at Magny-Cours moves back to the 21st and the British GP at 
        Silverstone has been brought forward to the 7th. This wasn't a universally 
        popular swap, because it might be even wetter than usual in England&#133;</p>
      <p>In 2002 <font color="#0000cc">Michelin</font>'s F1 partners have even 
        higher expectations than before. The Clermont-Ferrand firm will spare 
        no effort in order to make progress and to become recognised as the F1 
        tyre supplier by whom others should be judged.</p>
