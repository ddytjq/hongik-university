          Race Series Western Speedway, Victoria, B.C. May 4, 2002 <br>
          by F. Ross Rockett</font> </i></font><br>
          The initial 2002 race for the WILROC Sprint Car & Super Modified Race 
          Series was held under very threatening skies and cold temperatures at 
          Victoria's Western Speedway. The winged warriors shared the bill with 
          the Sportsman, Dwarf Car and Road Hog Classes. As well as keeping the 
          race spectators much lower than usual, the inclement weather seemed 
          to affect the car counts. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The WILROC portion 
          of the quickly-run program saw Victoria's <b>Randy Vincent</b> qualify 
          fastest at 14:44, only 2/100ths off the existing class track record. 
          The only car unable to qualify was that of 2001 WILROC points champion 
          <b>Darren Yates</b> from Duncan, B.C. Darren lost his engine during 
          the last hot lap session. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The "A" Dash saw 
          Victoria's <b>Jeff Montgomery</b> start on the pole and cruise to an 
          easy victory over his speedy competitors. Jeff is carrying on the family 
          tradition of winning as he is the younger brother of <b>Trevor "The 
          Tornado" Montgomery</b> of N.S.R.A. fame. Father <b>Neil</b> is also 
          involved with WILROC in 2002 with some much appreciated class sponsorship. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 10-car fast 
          heat saw Nanaimo's <b>Mark Fisher</b> jump into an early lead over pole 
          sitter <b>Dave Conway</b>. A little bit of wheel banging took place 
          in the early laps, but nobody got spun out. On lap twelve, Sidney's 
          <b>Rob Scott</b> left the fray with a sour engine, followed closely 
          to the pits by <b>Dave Conway</b> with steering problems. The fast guys 
          worked their way to the front and <b>Jeff Montgomery</b> emerged from 
          the pack and charged to the checkered flag for his second win of the 
          evening. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">When it was time 
          for the Main Event, track officials held up the start to try and get 
          <b>Darren Yates'</b> car back into competition. After losing his engine 
          in hot laps, his crew "raced" back to Duncan, picked up their spare 
          engine and were busy buttoning it up. Victoria's <b>Duane Zeinstra</b> 
          had also made some extensive engine repairs and was ready to run the 
          Main. Darren made three valiant attempts to get the car running correctly, 
          but his luck just wasn't there on this evening as he had to park it 
          and become a spectator. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Main saw <b>Dave 
          Conway</b> in the Wilcox roadster take the early lead, followed closely 
          by <b>Mark Fisher</b>. <b>Rob Scott</b>, driving the beautifully-prepared 
          Tipke roadster owned by <b>Ron Douglas</b>, broke free of the field 
          and set sail. <b>Jeff Bird</b> was the second car to clear and went 
          after Rob. As these two cars led the way, a spirited battle between 
          <b>Randy Vincent</b>, <b>Jeff Montgomery</b> and <b>Britt Gillett</b> 
          was occurring.. Rob's roadster, the heaviest car in a field of the modern 
          sprinters, was handling superbly through the turns and was not going 
          to be overtaken. It was a very special win for the owner and driver 
          as their upgraded 1970's technology is still super competitive with 
          the "new stuff". </font></p>

        <p> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Hallgarth and 
          Beaulieu Capture Big 1st Time Wins<br>
          </b><br>
          The invisible umbrella that hovers over Saratoga Speedway was back at 
          work Saturday night, keeping the rain away and allowing for an exciting 
          night of Stock Cars, Bombers, and the Island Mini Stocks with over 50 
          cars on hand.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The small but dedicated 
          crowd that braved the cold weather saw Port Alberni's <b>Don Hallgarth</b> 
          sweep all three of his Stock car races, including the 35 lap main event, 
          and sawl 14 year old <b>Chris Beaulieu</b> pull off a trademark family 
          move in winning his first Bomber A main. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Stock cars opened 
          their night with a very competitive qualifying session that saw <b>Martin 
          Beaulieu's</b> track record of 16.44 challenged by <b>Rob Leighton</b> 
          with a quick lap time of 16.46 followed by a quick 16.56 for <b>David 
          Mainse</b> and a 16.65 for Beaulieu.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">For the 3rd straight 
          week Black Creeks <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> won the night's first trophy 
          dash with a win in the C Dash followed by wins for <b>Dave Hargraves</b> 
          in the B dash (his first ever stock car race win) and <b>Hallgarth</b> 
          in the A dash. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The first of two 
          12 lap heat races saw 2001 Bomber point champion <b>Brad McLean</b> 
          win his first stock car race in the B heat while the A heat saw <b>Hallgarth</b> 
          take his second win of the night, setting the stage for a competitive 
          35 lap main event.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 12 car race 
          saw Port Alberni's <b>Rob Gaudrealt</b> in his #75 car take an early 
          lead and he would hold that position untill the 13th lap when Comox's 
          <b>Rob Ryan</b> got under him for the lead, which he would hold untill 
          the 25th lap, with a strong run on his return to Stock car racing. Ryan's 
          race lead was lost when Hallgarth went on the inside and <b>David Mainse</b> 
          on the outside through the dogleg, with Hallgarth coming out in the 
          lead. Hallgarth, in his 3rd season in the stock car class, is off to 
          his strongest start of the three and it showed over the final 10 laps 
          when he held off a strong challenge from Mainse for his biggest win 
          in the three years. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Following Hallgarth 
          and Mainse across the line were Ryan and James Dawson. A mid race mishap 
          involing Hargraves and Leighton resulted in heavy race-ending damage 
          to Leighton's car and saw Beaulieu tagged with a one lap stop and go 
          penalty that had him finish 8th. He now sits 7 points behind Mainse 
          for the point lead. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 15 car Bomber 
          main event saw Courtenay's <b>Cody Gorosh</b> take an early race lead 
          that he would hold onto untill the last lap, when Beaulieu ended a 9 
          lap side-by-side battle with a last lap pass to capture his first ever 
          main event ahead of Gorosh and point leader <b>Mike Dionne</b>. Beaulieu, 
          son of two time Canadain IMCA champion Jason Beaulieu, took to the outside 
          groove, reminisecent of his uncle Martin who has won countless races 
          with passes on the outside, which is just what the youngest Beaulieu 
          did in passing Gorosh for the lead on the final lap to grab the win. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Rookie <b>Doug Lutz</b> 
          captured his first win in his first race by grabbing the D Dash followed 
          by trophy dash wins for <b>Jeff von Kampen</b> in the C, <b>Jason Waters</b> 
          the B and <b>Big Rick Martin</b> the A. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Heat race wins went 
          to <b>Verona Tannock</b> who would later win the B main and Dionne in 
          the fast A heat. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Island Mini 
          Stocks raced on the Black Creek oval for the first time in four years 
          and they put on a fast paced exciting program which saw #11 <b>Matt 
          Mansel</b> come away with three big wins including the 35 lap main event, 
          fast heat and trophy dash, while <b>Sharon Constantine</b> won the 1st 
          heat. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This Saturday night 
          the high powered <b>WILROC Sprint Cars</b> make their first visit to 
          Saratoga with a expected field of 15 cars and they will be joined by 
          the <b>Motorcycle Road Racers</b> and the <b>Stock Cars</b> with all 
          the action set for 7pm. Guest talk...........A schedule misprint had 
          <b>Jason Beaulieu</b> opening defence of his Canadain Championship last 
          Saturday night however he doesn't open his season untill May 18th...........Rough 
          return for Bomber driver <b>Larry Oviatt</b> as the former lightning 
          car driver hit the wall during the B heat ending his 1st night back 
          early........Tickets for the May 18th& 19th Monster truck shows are 
          still on sale for $10 at the Sid Williams and Tidemark Theatre's or 
          local auto parts stores including Twin City Auto Parts.......Guest driver 
          of the week is a first as <b>Don Hallgarth</b> becomes the first ever 
          back-to-back Guest pick after his three win night.</font></p>
