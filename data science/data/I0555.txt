				<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>National Competitions</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">Above all else, Wembley's place in history has been secured by its role as venue of the FA Cup Final.<br>
				<br>
				That first final, between Bolton Wanderers and West Ham United, has gone down in the history books as the 'White Horse Final'.<br>
				<br>
				The official attendance was quoted as 126,947, but an estimated 200,000 fans packed into the ground and for a while, it seemed that the match wouldn't go ahead.<br>
				<br>
				It took the appearance of PC George Scorey and his white horse, Billie, to slowly push the masses back to the sides of the pitch so the game could begin. Bolton won 2-0.<br>
				<br>
				There have been many famous finals since then - from the one in which Stanley Matthews appeared in 1953, to the north-London showdown in 1991 where Paul Gascoigne was taken off with a crippling injury after just 15 minutes.<br>
				<br>
				The fastest ever goal in a final was scored by Chelsea's Roberto di Matteo in 1997, where he took just 43 seconds to hit the back of the net against Middlesborough.<br>
				</font></div>
			<center>
				<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><img height="93" width="90" src="../images/main/history/facup02.jpg"><br>
				</font><a href="facup.asp"><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular">Click for full list of Wembley FA Cup Finals<br>
				</font></a></p>
			</center>
			<div align="left">
				<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b><br>
				European Club Competitions</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
				Wembley has also hosted many European club finals. Benfica and Milan competed for the 1963 European Cup Final, with Milan winning 2-1. West Ham United beat TSV Munich 1860 in the 1965 Cup Winners' Cup.<br>
				<br>
				Manchester United won the 1968 European Cup at Wembley and the great Ajax team of Johann Cryuff won the middle of three successive European Cups in 1971.<br>
				<br>
				Barcelona finally achieved their long-held ambition of winning the same cup at Wembley under Cryuffs management in 1992. The last European final was a Cup Winners' Cup final in 1993 when Parma beat Royal Antwerp 3-1.<br>
				<br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>International Competitions</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">England played their entire 1966 World Cup campaign at Wembley and the final, against West Germany, now has legendary status. The game is widely regarded as the most exciting World Cup Final ever, even if some still dispute the validity of Geoff Hurst's second goal!. The crossbar that featured so prominently in that goal is still on display at Wembley and can be seen on the Wembley Tour.<br>
				<br>
				The European Championship, Euro 96, was another vintage Wembley occasion. England again played all their matches in the stadium. It ended with Germany beating the Czech Republic 2-1 after a nail-biting sudden-death goal, the first time a national team had ever won a major trophy in such a way. However, the game of the tournament was undoubtedly the England v Germany semi-final. Germany eventually won a penalty shoot out after the initial 90 minutes ended with a 1-1 scoreline.<br>
				<br>
				Perhaps the most embarrassing day for England at Wembley was November 1953 when a Puskas inspired Hungary introduced England to modern football. Hungary took the lead after 90 seconds and ran out comfortable 6-3 winners in a match that shook English Football to its foundations.<br>
