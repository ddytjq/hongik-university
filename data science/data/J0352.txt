<h1>Wallace sets road course record at Save Mart 300</h1>

What works on short tracks also works on road courses for Wallace, who hadn't won on a road course since winning at Sears Point in 1990. He ended the drought with his sixth road course victory, breaking a tie with Ricky Rudd, Darrell Waltrip, Bobby Allison and Tim Richmond. The modern era race tracks include Watkins Glen and Riverside Raceway, which was replaced on the NASCAR circuit in 1989 by Sears Point. <p>
Wallace used some tactical maneuvering of his Ford Thunderbird during a pair of re-starts in the final 10 laps, first taking the lead from Jeff Gordon and then holding off a charging Mark Martin. <p>
Wallace led 36 of the 74 laps in notching his 43rd career Winston Cup victory. He averaged 77.673 miles per hour and won by almost half a second. He earned $58,395 <p>
Martin finished second and was followed by Wally Dallenbach Junior, both in Fords. The Chevrolets of Dale Earnhardt and Terry Labonte were fourth and fifth, respectively. <p>
Gordon's late slip dropped him to sixth but he has now held a lead on a lap of every track on the current Winston Cup circuit.<p>
During the first re-start on lap 69, Wallace took advantage of an apparent traction problem to take the lead away from Gordon. <p>
&quot;I just had to get ready. The kid was racing good,&quot; said Wallace, a winner for the second time this year. &quot;He (Gordon) left me about a foot, and I drove right through it. It was a real dramatic race today.&quot; <p>
The drama didn't end when Wallace blew by Gordon and quickly established a substantial lead. The field bunched back up when Ron Hornaday Junior, Ricky Craven's relief driver, slid into a tire barrier and forced another caution. With three laps to go, the battle came down to Wallace, Martin and Dallenbach. <p>
Dallenbach initially challenged his former Roush Racing teammate Martin for second, renewing a rivalry that resulted in a Martin-Dallenbach finish at Watkins Glen last year. But Martin finally freed himself from Dallenbach and concentrated his efforts on Wallace. <p>
&quot;There was nowhere for us to go, although we were a little bit quicker,&quot; said Martin, who was forced to start from the back of the field because of a pre-start pit stop. &quot;I don't know whether to be happy or disappointed. I'm disappointed because we didn't win, but I can't believe we got to second.&quot; <p>
Wallace grabbed his first lead during an under-caution pit stop on the 16th lap of the race. It was Wallace a nose ahead of Earnhardt as they crossed the start-finish line while taking a caution flag for Joe Nemechek's crash into a tire barrier on the 29th lap. <p>
Gordon grabbed the lead by beating Wallace out of the pits during a green flag pit stop on the 52nd lap and held the lead until the restart on lap 69. <p>
With all of the top Winston Cup point contenders trailing him across the finish, Earnhardt should increase his points lead over Dale Jarrett to 115 points when NASCAR releases the Winston Cup points standings and official race results Monday. <p>
The top five will remain the same with Terry Labonte, Gordon and Sterling Marlin rounding out the leaders. Rudd, who finished seventh today, will be sixth, followed by Wallace and Ricky Craven. <p>
The 187-mile Save Mart 300 is the 10th of 31 races on the Winston Cup circuit. The Winston Cup drivers aren't in action again until the Coca Cola 600 on May 26th at Charlotte Motor Speedway.<p><hr>
