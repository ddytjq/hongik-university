<h1>Wallace sure is a Goody in Martinsville</h1>

MARTINSVILLE, Va. -- The clouds lifted over Martinsville Speedway in the morning
and the veil of bad luck lifted from Rusty Wallace in the
afternoon.
<p>
Wallace overshadowed Terry Labonte's record-setting race, rolling to victory in the Goody's Headache
Powders 500 for the fourth consecutive year.
<p>
Wallace passed Jeff Gordon as the racers moved inside 15 laps remaining. Wallace and Gordon battled hard over the final 50 laps of the
race, overcoming Dale Earnhardt and Ernie Irvan at the front of
the field following a caution period pit stop. 
<p>
&quot;We earned it today,&quot; said Wallace, the
only driver to lead in all four short track events this season.
&quot;Our car wasn't right all day, but the guys just kept working on
it and working on it.
<p>
&quot;Gordon did a marvelous job out there, and I didn't think I
could get around him. I'm still surprised we caught him.&quot;
<p>
Irvan then slipped past Gordon for second with just four laps
remaining. Jeremy Mayfield notched his career best with a fourth-place finish, followed by a fading Earnhardt in fifth. 
Earnhardt, however, will assume the Winston Cup points lead when official
tallies are announced Monday.
<p>
Labonte, breaking a tie he forged a week earlier with longtime
stock car king Richard Petty, drove in his 514th consecutive
Winston Cup event, a streak dating to the opening race of the 1979
season.
<p>
Although he led as late as Lap 414 in the 500-lap event on
Martinsville Speedway's .526-mile oval, Labonte had to make a
series of pit stops because of a brake fluid leak and wound up 24th, 20
laps off the pace.
<p>
Wallace -- NASCAR's leading driver on track's
shorter than one mile -- came back from a mid-race problem of his own
and chased down defending Winston Cup champion Jeff Gordon. Wallace
passed Gordon with 12 laps remaining and raced to his 42nd career
victory, sixth at Martinsville and first of the season.
<p>
&quot;The crew did a good job; my hat's off to them,&quot; Wallace said.
&quot;I think Gordon was stronger earlier in the race than he was at
the end. I was killing him off the corner.&quot; 
<p>
Robin Pemberton, the crew chief for Wallace's Penske South team,
led the successful effort to rebuild Wallace's Ford after the
car was wrecked last Sunday in North Wilkesboro and had it
prepared for the track before Friday's qualifying session. 
<p>
It also was the first win for Ford since Dale Jarrett took the
season-opening Daytona 500.
<p>
Wallace was on his way to victory in last week's First Union 400
in North Wilkesboro, when he got involved with John Andretti's
wreck, dropping him out of first and deep into the field with
less than 35 laps to go. 
<p>
Wallace had to make an extra pit stop during a mid-race caution
period because of a broken lug nut and fell from second to
seventh. He stayed on the lead lap but didn't get back into
contention until after the last tire change.
<p>
It was an unusually clean race for a short track event, with
only six caution flags for 36 laps. Three of those yellow flags
came out in the first 95 laps. The track remained green for 161
laps before the yellow waved again on lap 261 for Jeff Burton's
spin in turn four.
<p>
That long uninterrupted stretch allowed the field to stretch out and left only eight cars on the lead lap. It also helped Wallace
set a new track record of 81.396 mph, breaking the mark of 79.336
set by now-retired Cale Yarborough in September of 1978. Wallace
won $59,245.
<p>
Irvan, in the best showing of his comeback season, passed
Gordon for second place on lap 497, and gave Ford its first 1-2
finish of the season. Irvan finished 2.526-seconds -- about 15
car-lengths -- behind Wallace's Penske Racing South Thunderbird.
<p>
Gordon, who was the top lap leader in the race with 210, wound
up third, followed by Jeremy Mayfield -- who drives for Yarborough --
in his best career finish, and Earnhardt, who took over the
series point lead.
<p>
&quot;The car was loose (at the end of the race), but we hung on and
survived,&quot; Earnhardt said. 
<p>
Earnhardt, trying for a record-breaking eighth season title,
came into the race trailing season-long leader Jarrett by two
points and came away with a 76-point lead over Gordon. Jarrett, who
lost his brakes on lap 345, wound up 29th, falling to third, 82
points behind.
<p>
Kyle Petty took the lead from first-time pole-winner Ricky
Craven on lap 29, leading a lap for the first time this season and
becoming only the second Pontiac driver to lead this year. The
other was Bobby Hamilton at Rockingham and Richmond.
<p>
Wallace took his first lead of the day on lap 96 as he beat
Petty out of the pits during a caution period. Moments later,
Petty's car spouted flames from its right front wheel well. He
pitted and lost several laps as his team took the car behind the
wall to change the brake caliper, which was engulfed in flames
after an oil seal broke.<p>
