      <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+3"><U>
        &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mortgage Glossary&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
      </U></FONT>
    </TD>
  </TR>
<IMG SRC="/icons/ecblank.gif" BORDER=0 HEIGHT=1 WIDTH=16 ALT=""><TR><TD><BR><BR><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+2"><B>A</B></FONT><BR><IMG SRC="/images/general/bluedot.gif" WIDTH="560" HEIGHT="1" BORDER="0"><BR></TD></TR>  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Authority to Inspect The Register</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Document from registered proprietor of land allowing another party, such as the purchasers' solicitor, to be given information from the register of a property.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apportionment</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>The division of liability for property tax, water charges etc between the buyer and seller of a property.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Agricultural Restriction</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A Freehold covenant restricting the occupancy of a property to those engaged in agriculture.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;APR</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Annual percentage rate. A term defined in consumer credit legislation with the intention of providing a standard basis for comparing different forms of credit. It has had limited success and can be confusing. For example, case law has established that a lender can quote a mortgage APR based on a short-term fixed rate without including in the calculation the fact that, at the end of the fixed rate period, the rate will change. The allowable assumption is that the rate will continue throughout the mortgage term. The calculation must however include the 'total charge for credit' which includes such things as arrangement fees, valuation fees etc and so does have some merit. The concept is overdue for overhaul and redefinition.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Auction</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Public sale of a property to the highest bidder. The purchaser must immediately sign a binding contract and should ensure that all valuations, searches etc are carried out prior to the sale.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Assignment</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Document transferring rights of ownership from one person to another, such as an endowment policy to the building society in connection with a mortgage. Can also be the document transferring the lease on a property.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Additional Security Fee</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>An Additional Security Fee (Mortgage Indemnity Guarantee policy) is paid to take out an insurance policy designed to indemnify the mortgagee (lender) against loss in the event of default on the mortgage repayment. It is normally taken out by the lender at the start of the mortgage and the mortgagor (borrower) is made to pay the premium! The premium is normally calculated as a percentage (5.8% is typical) of that part of the loan above a certain percentage of the property value, normally 70 - 75%. It is charged as a lump sum to the borrower and can usually be added to the mortgage advance. It should be understood that such policies are for the protection of the lender and NOT the borrower.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD><BR><BR><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+2"><B>B</B></FONT><BR><IMG SRC="/images/general/bluedot.gif" WIDTH="560" HEIGHT="1" BORDER="0"><BR></TD></TR>  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;BSA</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Building Societies Association.  Represents interests of member societies. Address 3 Savile Row, London W1X 1AF.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Building Society</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Mutual organisation specialising in lending money to individuals to purchase or remortgage residential properties. Most of this money comes from individual saving members who are paid interest. A proportion of building society funds is also raised on the commercial money markets. Since the early eighties there has been a progressive relaxation of the rules governing the allowable sources of building society funds for lending to allow societies to compete more effectively with banks and there is now no restrictions as between the allowable proportions of 'retail' and 'wholesale funding'.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Building Societies Commission</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Regulatory organisation for Building Societies. Reporting to Treasury Ministers.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bridging Loan</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Short term loan to facilitate the purchase of one property prior to the sale of another releasing funds that are required for the purchase. Professional advice should always be taken prior to considering any bridging finance as it can be a solution which is worse than the problem.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bankers Draft</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A cheque drawn on the Bank itself against deposit of cash. Normally required in property transactions.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD><BR><BR><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+2"><B>C</B></FONT><BR><IMG SRC="/images/general/bluedot.gif" WIDTH="560" HEIGHT="1" BORDER="0"><BR></TD></TR>  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Capped Rate</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>An interest rate charged on a mortgage where there is a guarantee from the mortgagee that the rate will not exceed a certain amount usually for a set period of 1 - 5 years but which will reduce if the standard variable rate falls below the capped rate.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chief Rent</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A rent payable by the owner of a freehold property similar to the ground rent payable by a leaseholder. Normally only found in the North of England. Can be bought out by freeholder.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Contract</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Legally binding agreement for sale. In two identical parts, one signed by seller and one by purchaser. When the two parts are exchanged (exchange of contracts) both parties are committed to the transaction.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CML</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Council of Mortgage Lenders</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Covenant</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A promise contained in a deed.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conveyance</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>The deed by which freehold, unregistered title changes hands. If the property is leasehold and unregistered it is called an assignment. If the title is registered the deed is called a transfer.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Centralised Lender</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Term used to describe a mortgage lender who does not rely on a branch network for distribution. Originally applied to specialist lenders who entered the mortgage market in the mid-late eighties (National Home Loans, The Mortgage Corporation, First Mortgage Securities, Mortgage Express and many others). This followed some de-regulation, which made the securitisation of mortgage loans a viable and potentially profitable option for lenders. (See SECURITISATION). Several building societies now have "centralised lending" operations which operate quite separately from their branch networks and rely exclusively on mortgages from intermediary sources.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Charge</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Any right or interest, especially a mortgage, to which a freehold or leasehold property may be held.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Chattels</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>Moveable items such as furniture or personal possessions.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Charge Certificate</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>The certificate issued by HM Land Registry to the mortgagee of a property with registered title. Contains three parts - charges register, property register and proprietorship register. Contains details of restrictions, mortgages and other interests. Where there is no mortgage it is called the Land Certificate and issued to the registered proprietor.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD><BR><BR><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+2"><B>D</B></FONT><BR><IMG SRC="/images/general/bluedot.gif" WIDTH="560" HEIGHT="1" BORDER="0"><BR></TD></TR>  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discount Rate</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>An interest rate which is set at a set margin below standard variable rate usually for a period of 1 - 5 years. Used as an incentive to attract potential new borrowers.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deed</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A legal document which is 'signed, sealed and delivered' not just signed. This has special significance in law. Title to both freehold and leasehold property can only be transferred by deed.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD><BR><BR><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="+2"><B>E</B></FONT><BR><IMG SRC="/images/general/bluedot.gif" WIDTH="560" HEIGHT="1" BORDER="0"><BR></TD></TR>  <TR>
    <TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica"><B><I>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Easement</B></I></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP">
        <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica" SIZE="-1"><BLOCKQUOTE>A right, such as a right of way, which the owner of one property has over an adjoining property.</BLOCKQUOTE></FONT></TD></TR>
