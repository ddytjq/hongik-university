<H3>Special Thanks</H3>

<P> This book's concept and first edition were created by Jan and Mike
Riter, the original Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew. Between March 1997 and
January 2000, the Riters taught trail construction and maintenance
techniques to thousands of land managers, mountain bike club leaders and
other trail users. Their pioneering work took them to 48 U.S. states,
several Canadian provinces and nine European countries. The Riters built
such an awesome trail legacy that their number, Trail Care Crew No. 1
(TCC1), has been retired by IMBA. The Riters can be reached at their
Georgia-based consulting business, Trail Design Specialists, at
<A HREF="mailto:traildesign@mindspring.com">traildesign@mindspring.com</A>. </P>

<P> In 2001, this booklet was updated and expanded by IMBA to include
new information from additional years on the trails. Major contributions
were made by Joey Klein of TCC2 and Rich and Jen Edwards of TCC3. The
guidelines that follow come from the observations, techniques and
theories of many people working on trails in many places. </P>

<P> Special thanks also to various photographers and to John Halloway at

<H3>How To Get The PDF Version</H3>

<P>Due to the increased size of the new trailbuilding manual the PDF
file is now over 10MB! In order to request the PDF file, please email <A
HREF="mailto:webmaster@imba.com">webmaster@imba.com</A>. It will sent as
an attachment via email. <I><B>DO NOT</B> request a copy unless you have
a high speed Internet connection - cable, DSL, or faster. You also need a true POP email
account. Free accounts - Hotmail, Yahoo, etc. - will probably not accept this big of a file.</I>

<H3>How To Print The PDF </H3>


<P> <B>Building Better Trails</B> is designed to be an 8.5" x 5.5" booklet,  centered stapled.. The pdf,
when printed properly, will contain two facing pages per one 8.5" x 11" sheet of
paper. </P>

<P> To make a booklet, then print it double-sided, and you should be able to get
4 pages to one piece of 8.5" x  11" paper.  <B>Hint: A printer like Kinko's can do
this for you, and usually quicker, but it will cost you.</B> </P>


<H3>Printing Directions on a Mac</H3>

<P> You have to have your printer preferences set up correctly to get the new
trail building guide pdf to print two facing pages per one 8.5 x 11 sheet of
paper. </P>


<OL TYPE="1" START="1">
	<LI> With the document open, In Acrobat choose FILE -> PRINT...

	<LI> The Print menu will pop up.

	<LI> There will be a drop-down menu that will start with GENERAL

	<LI> Drop down to the LAYOUT Menu

	<LI> Change "Pages per sheet" to "2"

	<LI> Change "Border" to "Single Hairline"

	<LI> Choose PRINT.
</OL>
