monthly payment then sit back and watch your savings grow.<br>						</span><br>						If you are aged 16 or over and resident in the UK for tax purposes, you can open a Regular Saver account by simply taking your initial investment, along with the necessary identification, into any of our branches and completing an application form.<br>						<br>						Alternatively you can apply by post by forwarding a completed application form with the necessary identification along with a cheque for your initial investment.<br>						<br>						<b>A maximum of two withdrawals are permitted in any one calendar year</b>. If you make more than two withdrawals in a calendar year we will change your balance to an Instant Access account and you will receive a lower rate of interest.<br>						<br>						Withdrawals may be made after 7 days notice has been given with a limit of £2,500 cash per day. Cash withdrawals are subject to a maximum &#163;2,500 per day. The minimum amount for a cheque is &#163;10.<br>						<br>						The minimum monthly deposit is &#163;10 and the maximum deposit is &#163;1,000 (subject to a maximum overall holding of &#163;350,000). Deposits are variable and can be increased or decreased at any time throughout the year.<br>						<br>						A minimum of one deposit must be made per calendar month. Missing a deposit will result in the account being changed to an Instant Access account and the rate of interest adjusted accordingly.<br>						<br>						Deposits must be made either by Standing Order from your bank or by Automated Monthly Transfer from another account held with the Society. Other than the initial investment, NO deposit transactions may be made at any of our branch offices.<br>						<br>						Your investment earns interest from the day after your deposit is made.<br>						<br>						Provided that you have made one deposit per calendar month and no more than two withdrawals in a calendar year, your interest will be credited to your account annually on 31 December at the Regular Saver rate.<br>						<br>						If these commitments are not met your account will be changed to an Instant Access account and the rate of interest payable will be adjusted accordingly.<br>						<br>						Regular Saver accounts may be held in single or joint names.<br>						<br>						Regular Saver accounts held in a single name must be closed on the death of the account holder. The account will still earn interest at the Regular Saver rate.<br>						<span class="bodytextbold"><br>							<span class="bodytextgreen">Download the Regular Saver application </span></span>form (35K) to print out and fill in<span class="bodytextbold">.<br>							<span class="bodytextbold">Note:</span></span>You will need <a href="http://www.adobe.co.uk" title="Download Adobe Acrobat reader - its free!">Adobe Acrobat Reader</a> to view the form.						<p><span class="bodytextbold"><a href="pdfs/InvestApp_Form.pdf" title="Click here to download the application form"><img src="images/but_downloadpdf.gif" width="112" height="27" border="0"><br>									<br>									<br>								</a></span></p>					</div>					<table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="8" width="100%">								<tr>									<td bgcolor="#001e6f" align="left" colspan="4"><span class="formheading">Interest Rates &#150; Regular Saver (from 1 March 2002)</span></td>								</tr>								<tr>									<td bgcolor="#bbbbbb" align="left" width="30%">&nbsp;</td>									<td bgcolor="#bbbbbb"><span class="formtextbold">Gross %</span></td>									<td bgcolor="#bbbbbb" align="left"><span class="formtextbold">AER* %</span></td>									<td bgcolor="#bbbbbb" align="left"><span class="formtextbold">Net**%</span></td>								</tr>								<tr>									<td bgcolor="#dcdcdc" align="left" width="30%"><span class="formtextbold">&#163;10+</span></td>									<td bgcolor="#eeeeee"><span class="formtext">4.30 <b>##</b></span></td>									<td bgcolor="#eeeeee" align="left"><span class="formtext">4.30 <b>##</b></span></td>									<td bgcolor="#eeeeee" align="left"><span class="formtext">3.66</span></td>								</tr>							</table>					<br>					<p><span class="bodytext"><b>##</b> This rate includes an Introductory Bonus of 0.50% until 1 September 2002.
