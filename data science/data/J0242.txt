        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Montgomery Wins 
          Jim Haslam Memorial</b><br>
          <br>
          After a light Saturday rain shower, the skies cleared up in time for 
          the Wilroc Sprint Cars and Bomber Cars, to put on a full night of exciting 
          racing action at Saratoga Speedway, a presented by Comox Builders Supplies.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The light rain 
          had no effect on track conditions which was evident in time trails, 
          as for the second time in two visits to the Black Creek oval, Victoria's 
          <b>Jeff "The Hurricane" Montgomery</b> set a new track record with eye-blinking 
          quick lap time of 13.45, eclipsing his previous mark of 13.66.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Saturday's Comox 
          Building Supply sponsored sprint car main event was run as their <b>Hall 
          of Fame Race</b> with the <b>Jim Haslam Memorial Trophy</b> up for grabs 
          to the winner of the 25 lap main.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 9 car race saw 
          Nanaimo's <b>Ron Lambert</b>, a main event winner at Saratoga in 2001, 
          take a first lap lead which he would hold for the first 15 circuits 
          while the pack battled behind him for positions.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Montgomery, starting 
          in the 6th spot, seemed to pace himself over the first half of the race, 
          not making much of a move until the between the 10th and 15th lap when 
          he easily powered his way form 5th to 1st. He would be uncontested at 
          the finish ahead of Lambert and Duncan's <b>Darren Yates</b>.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The winged sprint 
          car action kicked off with two trophy dashes - both taking less than 
          60 seconds to race four laps - Victoria's <b>Randy Vincent</b> took 
          the win in the B dash, followed by the A dash win for Yates. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Two 10 lap heat 
          races followed and they were won by Victoria's <b>Rob Scott</b> and 
          Vincent. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A 5 car crash on 
          the first lap of the Bomber A main eliminated point leader <b>BJ Bradley</b> 
          which opened the door for Oyster River's <b>Chris Beaulieu,</b> who 
          not only walked in and won his third straight A Main, but also moved 
          past Bradley by 34 points to take over the points lead. The first lap 
          crash saw <b>Cody Gorosh</b>, <b>Darren Larsen</b>, <b>Jeff von Von 
          Kampen</b>, <b>Troy Carnie</b> and Bradley all involved on the front 
          stretch crash. Only Carnie returned for the restart, while Bradley re-entered 
          the race for 1 lap on the 9th lap. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 40 lap race 
          saw <b>Larry Oviatt</b> hold onto the race lead for 22 laps before Beaulieu 
          was able to get past on the outside of the dogleg. Beaulieu then went 
          unchallenged until the last lap when he got tangled with slower cars 
          almost costing him the win. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Rookie <b>Brian 
          Baltis</b>, driving <b>James Dawson's </b>2000 championship car, had 
          an impressive showing as he drove strong to finish a best-ever 2nd behind 
          Beaulieu and ahead of <b>Big Rick Martin</b> and <b>Darrell Larsen</b>. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nanaimo's <b>Carl 
          Carlson</b>, a regular crash to pass racer, recently purchased <b>Dustin 
          Purden's</b> 2001 Bomber car and after some figuring out, Carlson found 
          his way to the chequered flag with the win in the 20 lap B main. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A 23 car field of 
          Bombers made for a 9-race program kicked off with four Trophy Dashes 
          which saw <b>Carol Sadler</b> return from a 3 race break to win the 
          D Dash, Oviatt took the C Dash win, Carnie the B Dash and Darren Larsen 
          the fast A Dash.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The 10 lap heat 
          races had <b>Tanya Shannon</b> win the C Heat, Oviatt took his 2nd win 
          in the B Heat and Quadra's <b>Little Richard Olson</b> took the win 
          in the A Heat. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This weekend the 
          speedway comes alive with two days off action, that will be Rain or 
          Shine, highlighted by two days of Magic 98.9 Crash to Pass action, Island 
          Mini Stocks, Road Hogs,Figure 8 racing and a Dwarf car invitational 
          along with a car jumping stunt show Saturday night, then a sky brightening
