                      <td height="24" class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                      <td colspan="2" class="submenu" height="24">Fire prevention 
                      </td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr> 
                      <td class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                      <td colspan="2" class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr> 
                      <td class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                      <td colspan="2" class="bk10pt">Check <b>your</b> electrical 
                        equipment regularly. Make sure that <b>you</b> use the 
                        correct fuses and do not overload the circuits. Ask for 
                        the help of a qualified electrician if <b>you</b> are 
                        not sure. If <b>you</b> leave the property for more than 
                        24 hours, unplug all unnecessary appliances. Fires often 
                        happen in kitchens. <b>You</b> can put out chip-pan fires 
                        by shutting out the air. Cover the pan with a lid or thick 
                        damp cloth. Do not use water or try to carry the pan out 
                        of doors.<br>
                        <span class="blue10ptIt"></span></td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>

                      <td colspan="2" class="submenu" bgcolor="6699cc">Water damage 
                      </td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>
                      <td class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                      <td colspan="2" class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr> 
                      <td class="bk10pt">&nbsp;</td>
                      <td colspan="2" class="bk10pt">Lag exposed water pipes and 
                        tanks in the roof area and taps in the garage or outbuildings. 
                        Cut off the water and drain the system if <b>you</b> leave 
                        the property without heat in winter. If pipes freeze, 
                        despite <b>your</b> precautions, thaw them out using hot-water 
                        bottles. Never use a blowlamp. </td>
