 <tr><td><p class="PageTitle">The arts
      <p class="bodytext">In the arts, we aim to promote accessibility 
    and wider enjoyment. Our new two-year &pound;1.9 million arts 
    sponsorship programme, <i>Invest and Inspire</i>, has seen 
    us sponsor major exhibitions at the British Museum, the National 
    Gallery and Tate Britain in London, as well as all productions 
    at The National Theatre's Olivier Theatre during 2002.
      <p class="bodytext">Speaking about the launch of <i>Invest and 
    Inspire</i>, Minister of State for the Arts Baroness Blackstone 
    said: 'Partnerships such as this offer exceptional opportunities 
    for businesses to achieve far-reaching business objectives, 
    to promote artistic excellence and innovation, and to collaborate 
    effectively in improving access to the arts for all.'
      <p class="bodytext">Through <i>Barclays Firsts</i>, we will 
    be involved with the Royal National Theatre's education and 
    community work, with special emphasis on families, young people 
    and first-time visitors. We were also involved in numerous 
    local arts projects during the year. These included a project 
    with Hijinx Theatre in Wales creating productions for adults 
    with learning difficulties, and <i>Access Works</i>, an initiative 
    with Unicorn Theatre introducing children from some deprived 
    London boroughs to theatre.
