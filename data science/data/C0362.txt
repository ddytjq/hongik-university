<p align="left"><u><b>Why you need income protection insurance</b></u></p>
<ul>
  <li>
    <p align="left"><b>90 families a day had their homes repossessed in 2000. The
    majority due to financial problems associated with redundancy. A suitable
    redundancy insurance policy would have helped them.</b></li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">One in three people aged between 25 and 34 have experienced
    unemployment for a period in excess of one month.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">Every adult in Britain is five times more likely to suffer a
    serious disability than die before the age of 60.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">New mortgage borrowers obtain no assistance for nine months.
    Existing (Pre October 1995) mortgage borrowers receive nothing for two
    months, only 50% for the next 4 months and then full benefit of any mortgage
    up to £100,000 <b>provided they qualify for income support </b>( The
    Government estimates 70% of mortgage borrowers will not get Income support
    due to saving, income or working partner.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">102,000 claimants have been turned down for incapacity
    benefit. Recently the Government has suggested reviewing those who do
    receive this benefit every three years.</li>
</ul>

<h4 align="left"><u><b>Are you eligible?</b></u></h4>
<ul>
  <li>
    <p align="left">You must be over 18 but under 64 and are permanently
    resident in the UK, Channel Islands or Isle of Man</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">You must be actively working and have been in continuous
    employment for more than six months before the start date. You are not
    eligible if you work in temporary or seasonal employment.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">You must be in good health and know of no impending
    unemployment or disability.</li>
</ul>
<h4 align="left"><u><b>How much cover can you have?</b></u></h4>
<ul>
  <li>
    <p align="left">You may have up to £2,500 per month or 50% of your gross
    monthly salary (average to include overtime, commission and bonuses
    over&nbsp; the last 12 months)<a href="http://www.goodmortgageprotection.co.uk/redundancy-insurance-information.htm"><img border="0" src="images/income-protection-insurance.gif" width="1" height="1"></a></li>
</ul>
<h4 align="left"><u><b>When am I covered</b></u></h4>
<ul>
  <li>
    <p align="left">If you are employed (excluding directors/ secretaries or
    relatives or contract workers) you will be insured if you are made
    redundant.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">If you own less than 10% (or a relative of someone who owns
    less than 10%) of the share capital you will be insured if you become
    unemployed.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">If you own more than 10% (or a relative of someone who owns
    more than 10%) you will be insured if the business ceases to trade.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">If you are self employed you will be insured if the business
    ceases to trade.</li>
  <li>
    <p align="left">If you are on a fixed term contract for more than 2 years
    you will be insured if made redundant. If less than 2 years you will be
    insured if you are made redundant during the contract and payment will cease
    on the original contract expiry date.</li>
</ul>
