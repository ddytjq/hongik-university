<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>SMITHFIELD, R.I.&nbsp;-- Three years ago Damion Grant lived in Jamaica. Three years ago Damion Grant did track, soccer and cricket. Three years ago Damion Grant had never played in a basketball game.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Not a one.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The closest court was a half hour, 45 minutes away," he said this weekend here at the </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=502"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3>National Prep School Invitational</FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3> on the campus of Bryant College. "But I watched a few NBA games on TV, and I had a hoop in my driveway, and it was fun."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But now basketball is business.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Caribbean 7-footer at New Hampshire's Brewster Academy is earmarked for Chapel Hill&nbsp;-- he made a commitment to coach Matt Doherty's struggling Tar Heels earlier this season&nbsp;-- and the pressure suddenly has become very real.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I know there are a lot of expectations on me," the affable big man said Sunday night. "When I sit and think about it, I sometimes say to myself, 'This is going to be a big jump for me.' A very big jump.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I'm going to have to bust my ass, and I have no problem with that. I want to be a part of the Carolina basketball tradition."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He will be. There's no doubt about that. The only question is whether he'll be part of a winning tradition.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>On a weekend during which fellow UNC pledge </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=139&amp;section_id=486"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3>Rashad McCants</FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3> excelled in the same event&nbsp;-- the New Hampton (N.H.) School superstar had 35 points Friday night and 44 Saturday morning&nbsp;-- Grant scored two.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Total.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He had no points and five rebounds Saturday in a win over the Pendleton School from Bradenton, Fla., and he managed to drop in a basket to go along with a couple rebounds and at least one monster block in Sunday's NPSI finale against the MacDuffie School out of Springfield, Mass.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>To be fair, though, the games' matchups didn't exactly mesh with Grant's style of play. Both Pendleton and MacDuffie brought quick, smallish, guard-heavy teams, and Brewster coach Jason Smith opted to use Grant sparingly during stretches.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It was tough for him," Smith explained. "Every single one of them could step out and shoot it."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And Grant isn't the kind of player who's going to go out to the perimeter and start chasing 6-5 "centers."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"That's hard to guard for a big man," he said. "It was very frustrating. Our offense is centered around our guards, too, so I don't get a lot of touches. But I want people to know I can score."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He has. Grant made the all-tournament team two weekends ago at the Maine Central Institute Invitational, tallying 17 points, 14 rebounds and nine blocks against MCI and 19 points, nine rebounds and nine blocks against Troy, N.Y., prep school Redemption.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But make no mistake: The best thing about Grant's game right now isn't his game at all. It's his body. He has a rangy and sturdy frame, long arms and big hands and the type of natural musculature that should do nothing but get better through a college weight regimen.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But his movements are somewhat herky-jerky, his hands have to be considered a major question mark, and even his general coordination&nbsp;-- different from his pure run-and-jump athleticism&nbsp;-- is so-so.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Good thing the kid likes to get after it.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"From the first day he stepped onto campus," Smith said, "he's been so coachable. He's like a sponge: He picks up things thanks to his work ethic, and he's very well-liked by his peers and the faculty on campus."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>It's easy to see why. Grant's a humble, articulate kid with a quick smile. But the folks up in Wolfeboro, N.H., see him for what he is as a person rather than what he could be as a basketball player.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>For restless Tar Heel fans, though, he's one part of a critical five-man recruiting class that'll need to hit the Smith Center next fall and make marked and immediate contributions. Chapel Hill, after all, doesn't take very well to 6-15.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"They're not the Carolina they used to be," said Grant, who grew up in Jamaica even cognizant of only three college programs in the States&nbsp;-- Duke, Kentucky and the one he's about to join.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"But it's only for a year. We'll definitely have a good team next year. I'm looking forward to getting down there to do whatever I'm asked to do to put Carolina back to where it should be."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But be warned: McCants, Latta, S.C., point guard Raymond Felton and Bloomington, Ind., post player Sean May all are instant-impact talents.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Gulfport, Miss., forward Byron Sanders is not. Nor is Grant. Even though he might have to be.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I'm getting a lot of individual attention at Brewster," Grant said. "I'm doing repetitions with post moves. I've definitely improved a lot since I got to Brewster in September."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>With more to come&nbsp;-- or else.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He knows he has a way to go," Smith said.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>For Grant's sake, here's hoping the ornery Carolina fandom does, too.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>THE SAINTS ARE MARCHING</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Demetris Nichols and Abdi Lidonde are roommates as borders at St. Andrew's. They're also the top two reasons the Barrington, R.I., school is a prep program worth tracking on the national radar&nbsp;-- the rest of the this year and certainly come next season.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"They push each other, they support each other, and they challenge each other to get better," coach Mike Hart said after two wins here at the NPSI. "They're my two best players, they're great kids, and they're awesome role models."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And the rest of the Saints are following their lead.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Hart's team&nbsp;-- clearly the deepest and maybe the most talented of his eight-year tenure at St. Andrew's&nbsp;-- is 21-6 after weekend victories over the Brunswick School from Greenwich, Conn., and the Salisbury School from Salisbury, Conn.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"We've been really getting after it defensively," Hart said.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Not to mention offensively. His top duo is combining to average nearly 32 points and almost 15 rebounds a game.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Nichols, a native of Dorchester, Mass., arrived at St. Andrew's as a 6-6, 160-pound freshman who at that point was showing some promise with Mike Stephens' Rhode Island Breakers AAU program.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Now he's a 6-7, 185-pound combo forward who travels in the summer with the high-profile Tim Thomas Playaz out of Paterson, N.J. He's interested in Providence, Syracuse, Seton Hall and Villanova, and they're all very much interested in him.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's got great touch, and he really works at it," Hart said. "He's come a long way in three years."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>As has Lidonde. The 6-2 wing guard is one tough mother who (like Nichols) doubles as an honor roll student. He'll likely end up at an Ivy or Patriot League school, and he'll be darn good.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Or maybe he'll go even higher. There is, after all, still time to get some serious exposure&nbsp;-- especially with St. Andrew's potentially sitting on something big.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>This year's roster shows only one senior. But Hart's youngsters beat a strong North Carolina pair&nbsp;-- Shavlik Randolph's Raleigh Broughton team along with Kinston High School&nbsp;-- at the post-Christmas GlaxoSmithKline Invitational and took care of Los Angeles Dorsey and Louisville Ballard in Fairdale, Ky., in the pre-Christmas King of the Bluegrass.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Saints made a brief cameo in the nation's Top 25 three years ago back when current UConn guard Tony Robertson called Barrington home. Expect more of those rankings&nbsp;-- and potentially even higher&nbsp;-- come next season.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Current sophomores Emery Outland and Nick Mattone will be juniors, Nichols and Lidonde will be seniors, Hart will be his usual jovial, good-coaching self&nbsp;... and folks better watch out.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Leadership is the key to your team," Hart said, "and Abdi and Demetris have really stepped up. I think we're a year away from possibly being a Top 10 team."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Dallas Lincoln continues to annihilate its opponents in Texas. Last week's two games were won by a combined 87 points. America's new No. 1 high school basketball team is now 32-0 and looks to have a clear path to the mythical national championship based on <STRONG>Chris Lawlor</STRONG>'s<STRONG> </STRONG>poll in USA Today.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But what if Lincoln loses? Not likely the rest of the way&nbsp;-- at least not in regulation play. Yet here's the catch: <STRONG>Chris Bosh</STRONG>, <STRONG>Bryan Hopkins </STRONG>and the rest of the Dallas crew are scheduled to face Lone Star State foe Cedar Hill&nbsp;-- No. 4 in the nation in <STRONG>Mark Tennis</STRONG>'<STRONG> </STRONG>and <STRONG>Doug Huff</STRONG>'s<STRONG> </STRONG>Student Sports rankings and eighth in USA Today&nbsp;-- in a tantalizing exhibition game Saturday at Reunion Arena.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Two things: (1) If the travel budget were limitless&nbsp;-- or if there were, like, 37 hours in a day&nbsp;-- this correspondent would be down there in a heartbeat, and (2) even though the game doesn't count in Texas standings, it almost has to count in the minds of pollsters. Right?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And what happens if Lincoln does lose? Then is one-loss Oak Hill No. 1 again? Or one-loss St. Anthony? Or would the nod have to go to undefeated (but relatively untested) Milwaukee Vincent?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Canadian UConn signee <STRONG>Denham Brown </STRONG>scored 111 points in a Toronto high school game last week. Brown's people were crowing about the performance being a North American record&nbsp;... even though it wasn't.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>According to Huff, a&nbsp;longtime high school sports historian, five kids in the U.S. have notched better than Brown's 111, starting with the 135 <STRONG>Danny Heater </STRONG>scored in Burnsville, W.Va., in 1960.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Brown's 111 were, however, the top single-game schoolboy scoring performance on this continent since <STRONG>Kenneth Johnson </STRONG>of Grandfield, Okla., went for 105 in 1979. Just to set the record straight.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** People focus on <STRONG>Rashad McCants </STRONG>when they watch the New Hampton School. And rightfully so. But coach <STRONG>Jamie Arsenault</STRONG>'s<STRONG> </STRONG>squad boasts two kids who could be superstars at the low-major Division I level.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3><STRONG>Wes Miller</STRONG>, a point guard from Charlotte, N.C., is a fifth-year senior at NHS. The 5-11 scrapper has improved considerably during his three years in New England&nbsp;-- night-and-day stuff&nbsp;-- and he's now a tough-as-nails defensive ace (even if he isn't all that athletically gifted) who flat-out makes the open three. He hit seven Friday night.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3><STRONG>Michael Clarke</STRONG>, meanwhile, committed to Albany coming out of New York City last spring. But an admissions snafu sent him to New Hampton. Now the 6-5 undersized four-man is wide open. He gets a lot of things done in the post at NHS&nbsp;-- and he'd do the same in, say, an America East or NEC or maybe even a MAAC program.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** The annual Capital Classic has a new sponsor: <STRONG>Michael Jordan</STRONG>. The Jordan Brand Cap Classic is set for April 11 at Washington's MCI Center. It's considered one of the top three post-season high school all-star games in the country&nbsp;-- along with the McDonald's All-American Game, of course, and the adidas-sponsored Roundball Classic&nbsp;-- but now the Cap's got some added oomph. Think any of the Nike circuit kids will be able to say no to Michael? No way.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Among those "Swoosh" stars who'll almost certainly be there: McCants; Indiana-bound <STRONG>Bracey Wright </STRONG>out of The Colony, Texas; UNC-bound <STRONG>Sean May </STRONG>out of Bloomington, Ind.; Duke-bound <STRONG>J.J. Redick </STRONG>out of Roanoke, Va.; Notre Dame-bound </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=151&amp;section_id=486"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3><STRONG>Torin Francis</STRONG></FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3><STRONG> </STRONG>out of Marion, Mass.; Arizona-bound <STRONG>Hassan Adams </STRONG>out of Los Angeles; Maryland-bound </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=485&amp;section_id=486"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3><STRONG>John Gilchrist</STRONG></FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3><STRONG> </STRONG>out of Virginia Beach, Va.; and Georgia Tech-bound <STRONG>Jarrett Jack </STRONG>out of Fort Washington, Md.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Sophomore <STRONG>Brandon Rush </STRONG>scored 18 points in his high school debut last Tuesday night in Kansas City. Name sound familiar? Of course: He's the younger brother of <STRONG>Kareem Rush&nbsp;</STRONG>and <STRONG>Jaron Rush</STRONG>. Brandon sat out the first year and a half of his prep career due to academic ineligibility and transfer requirements. But no fear: He's playing now.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"When you consider everything else that has gone on with hype, transfers and talk about his grades, it was an impressive debut," said Kansas-based PrepStars.com correspondent <STRONG>Eric Bossi</STRONG>, who was at the game. "He just got the ball out there and played instinctive basketball. He has the ability to alter the game on both ends of the court."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** National Prep School Invitational director <STRONG>Mike Procopio </STRONG>operated the scoreboard for all 20 games this weekend at Bryant College's Recreation Center&nbsp;-- four on Friday night, nine all day Saturday, seven more on Sunday. "I think I'm the first tournament organizer to ever do that," he beamed during the second half of Sunday's second-to-last contest. Of that there is little doubt.</FONT></P></DIV>
