 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Newcastle United</H1><H2 class="entry">St James Park</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Newcastle-Ground1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Newcastle-Ground1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B></B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Newcastle-Ground8-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Newcastle-Ground8-Thumb.jpg" ALT="" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B></B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Newcastle-Ground2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Newcastle-Ground2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B></B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Newcastle-Ground9
-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Newcastle-Ground9
-Thumb.jpg" ALT="" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B></B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Away fans are allocated 3000 seats in the Sir John Hall (North) Stand. The maximum allocation of 3000
is only usually taken by 4 or 5 teams so for most games the number of away tickets is less.
There is both food and alcohol available on the concourses.
</P><P>We have been informed that the policy regarding flags has been changed recently and
you are now allowed flags in the stadium. You are allowed hand held flags
to a maximum of one square metre and there are now railings to drape larger flags
over. You are also allowed flags with polls as long as they are not too large.
If they are thought to be a danger then they will be retained until after the
game when you can get them back. We still believe that drums are not allowed into the stadium.
</P><P></P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=NE14ST&Title=St+James+Park+(Newcastle+United)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>There are some spaces for wheelchair users in front of the John Hall
Stand.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>The pubs near to the ground let away fans in but are not recommended as 
they get too busy. It is best avoiding The Trent House, Strawberry, Rosies
Bar, Newcastle Arms and 3 Bulls Head as they get over-crowded with home
fans on matchdays. 
</P><P>The ground is close to the city centre and there are plenty of options
there for visiting fans. Recommended options are Bodega and
Tilly's (both on Westgate Road), Crown Posada and Cooperage (both on
Quayside) and Duke of Wellington (on High Bridge), Bridge
(next to High Level Bridge) and Hotspur (next to Haymarket Bus Station)   
</P><P>CIU card carriers try the Labour Club or Irish Centre, both south of the
ground.
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>There are the customary Burger vans at South end of ground. There is a Burger
King on Westgate Road and a large number of Chinese eateries on Stowell
Street. There is plenty more choice available in the city centre
which is a short walk from the ground.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>There are Halifax and Barclays cash machines at the ground.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is NE1 4ST</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>The nearest bookmakers is a Ladbrokes 5 minutes from the south-east corner
of the ground on the corner Darn Crook and Stowell Street. There are
also bookmakers on Westgate Road & Clayton Street which are between
the train station and the ground.
</P></p><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>The majority of visiting fans will approach via the A1(M). Exit the A1(M)
at Junction 64 and take the A1 towards Newcastle. After just over 4 miles
turn right onto the A184 and follow this for just over 1 mile until the
junction with the A189. Exit onto the A189 (North) towards Newcastle
which crosses the river Tyne. At the first roundabout go straight
across onto Blenheim Street and then turn left onto Bath Lane. Take
the first right onto Corporation Street (B1311) and then turn left
at the roundabout onto Gallowgate. The ground is straight ahead.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>The is limited street parking available near to the ground and also plenty
of car parks nearby.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>One option is to take the Metro from the station to the ground as St
James metro station is next to the ground. To get to St James from Newcastle Central you need to change at Monument. For a map of the Newcastle Underground click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.tyneandwearmetro.co.uk">here</a>.
</P><P>If you prefer to walk to the ground from the station its only about 15
minutes away. From the station cross over Neville Street and follow it
right. Take the first left up Grainger Street and head through square and
turn left onto Blackett Street. Go through the subway and onto
Gallowgate and follow this into Barrack Road and the ground is straight
ahead.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Newcastle Central
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>As you would expect for a large city there is plenty of accommodation to
choose from.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/newcastl.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Newcastle has some of the most fanatical fans in the football leagues and
they always take a big following when travelling away. They have been
noted
for wearing just their Newcastle Shirt (i.e. no coat) in the middle of
winter.
</P><P>If visiting St James Park you are likely to get a warm welcome but expect
to be well outnumbered. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>We have received comments that the police are not known for their good
cheer, sense of fair play or being able to resist ejecting or arresting
for the most trivial of acts. We also received the comment that
they have an in-depth knowledge of rail timetable ensuring departure
from
custody precisely timed to miss last train !
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Newcastle fans are noted for singing Toon Army incessantly and are also
the originators of Blaydon Races as a football song. They also sing plenty
of anti-Sunderland songs.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Newcastle is a fantastic night out with the two main drinking areas being 
the Quayside and Bigg Market. There is a huge choice of pubs and clubs to
choose from. Newcastle also has its own China Town around Stowell Street
as well as plenty of other restaurants to choose from. Please note that you will get turned away from most bar and eating places after
the game if wearing a football shirt.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>The most famous landmark in Newcastle is probably the Tyne Bridge.
Newcastle also has a Castle Keep which is open to the public.
</P><P>If its shopping you're after you are spoilt for choice with Newcastle
having a very good shopping centre and the Gateshead Metro Centre only a
short drive away. There are also buses and trains from the city centre to
the Metro Centre.
</P></p><P>You can also use the following to search for events in the area</p><BR><CENTER>
