    background="../../../../images/Backgrounds/paper_back.gif"><h2>Talking X's and O's- <font
    color="#8080C0">Interactive Coach</font></h2>
      <h2 align="center">Eddie Oliver's HoopsUSA&nbsp;<br>
      Tournament &amp; Events Report</h2>
      <div align="center"><center><table border="0" width="95%">
          <tr>
            <td width="100%" valign="top" style="margin-right: 5px">
              <h2 align="center"><font size="1" color="#000000"><img border="1" src="../../images/Logos/HoopsUSAlogo_sm.gif" align="center" vspace="10"></font></h2>
              <h2 align="center"><font size="1" color="#000000"><a href="http://www.visionsports.com"><img border="0" src="../../images/Logos/academy_nat_inv.gif" align="left" hspace="5"></a>Inaugural
              Academy National Invitational Tournament<br>
              Campbell Center<br>
              Houston, Texas<br>
              Dec. 26-29, 2001
              </font></h2>
              <h3 align="center"><font size="1" color="#000000"><br>
    by Eddie Oliver<br>
              HoopsUSA </font><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt"><font size="1"><o:p>
              </o:p>
              </font></span></h3>
              <p><font size="1">Sixteen of the nation's top boy's high school
              basketball teams will convene in Houston from December 26-29 to
              participate in the inaugural Academy National Invitational
              presented by Nike. The field will consist of an elite division of
              eight teams from across the country that will comprise the
              National Division and a division of eight teams from the host
              state who will make up the Texas Division. All games will be
              played at the state-of-the-art 4,700-seat Campbell Center.
              &quot;We are excited to host a tournament that will have an
              outstanding collection of high school basketball talent from
              across the country and around the state of Texas,&quot; said
              Tournament Director Hal Pastner of Vision Sports. &quot;The city
              of Houston is honored to host this event and we hope this is the
              beginning of a long relationship with Academy Sports &amp;
              Outdoors and Nike that will result in this tournament becoming the
              premiere national high school basketball tournament.&quot;</font></p>
              <p><font size="1">The National Division boasts seven teams ranked
              in the nation's Top 25 by both USA Today and Street and Smith
              magazine. The National Division includes the nation's defending
              national champion Mouth of Wilson (VA) Oak Hill Academy, which is
              led by National Player of the Year candidate 6'8&quot; Carmelo
              Anthony. From the other side of the tournament bracket and country
              is powerful Westchester High out of Los Angeles posing a possible
              1-2 national ranking final in the event. Westchester is led by
              6'4&quot; Hassan Adams and 6'3&quot; Brandon Heath - both top 50
              seniors in the class of 2002.</font></p>
              <p><font size="1">The sleeper team in this year's event may very
              well be Beaumont Ozen, which finished 36-0 in Texas last year and
              returns 6'11&quot; junior Kendrick Perkins, believed to be the
              number one junior in the country. Other National Division teams in
              this year's highly anticipated event are Midwest City High
              (Oklahoma) with 6'8&quot; Sheldon Williams and Male High
              (Louisville) led by 6'3&quot; junior Michael Bush, who may also be
              the state of Kentucky's best football player. Storied Rice High
              (New York) will be on hand with 7'3&quot; junior Shagari Alleyne
              and from across the river in New Jersey, St. Patrick's High will
              travel to Houston and open the tournament on the night of December
              26 against Midwest City. St. Patrick's is led by 6'10&quot; big
              man Grant Billmeier. Rounding out the National Division will be
              DeLaSalle High from Concord, California.</font></p>
              <p><font size="1">The Texas Division will feature the top teams
              from the state, including two private perennial powerhouses,
              Westbury Christian and Heritage Christian. Westbury is led by one
              of the nation's top juniors in 6'10&quot; Ndudi Ebi. Heritage
              Christian features five potential Division I players in their
              starting lineup, with 6'4&quot; guard Cedrick Hensley leading the
              way. Texas City comes back strong this year after a 33-3 season
              with senior point guard and District MVP Jonathan Burnett. Other
              Texas teams include Clements High led by freshman sensation David
              Devezin, Aldine Nimitz High led by junior 6'7&quot; Cartier Martin
              and Westfield High with 6'6&quot; senior forward Kenneth
              Henderson. Cy Springs with senior point guard Karon Bradley and
              Eisenhower High School with 6'7&quot; senior Dontay Jackson round
              out the Texas division.</font></p>
              <p><font size="1">&quot;Academy Sports &amp; Outdoors is proud to
              team with Nike and Vision Sports to bring the country's top high
              school basketball teams together for this tournament,&quot; said
              Casey Ramm, Executive Director of Communications for Academy
              Sports &amp; Outdoors. &quot;The competition will be exciting and
              we are thrilled to bring this caliber of basketball to
              Houston.&quot;</font></p>
