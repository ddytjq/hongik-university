<TD WIDTH="69%" VALIGN="TOP"><P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.ucmp.berkeley.edu/history/evolution.html">Enter
Evolution: Theory and History</A>. University of California at Berkeley
Museum of Paleontology. The Museum of Paleontology at Berkeley probably
has the premier web site on paleontology and evolution. This introductory
page and will lead you into the rest of this massive site. A number of essays
on the people and history of biology and evolutionary thought from the ancient
past to the present may also be reached from here. Highly recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.literature.org/Works/Charles-Darwin/">Charles
Darwin</A>. Online Literature Library. Charles Darwin, of course, is one
of the fathers of evolutionary theory. This web site contains the complete
text of three of his most important works; Voyage of the Beagle, The Origin
of Species, and The Descent of Man. What else can we say. Highly recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.ed.uiuc.edu/facstaff/g-cziko/without_miracles/">Without
Miracles</A>. Gary Dziko. This web site contains an online book dedicated
to the principle of natural selection and its production of structure in
plant and animal species. The emphasis is on humans. On the whole the book
is philosophy oriented and is strongest on the history of evolutionary thought.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://userwww.sfsu.edu/~rsauzier/Biography.html">Biography.
Glass Bead Game</A>. This particular page has a series of short biographies
of famous people, living and dead. Check under biology for biographies of
evolutionary biologists.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www3.mistral.co.uk/bradburyac/dar0.html">Charles
Darwin - The Truth?</A>. Andrew J. Bradbury. This site examines the roles
Charles Darwin, Edward Blyth, Charles Lyell, and Alfred Wallace played in
the formation of the concept of evolution. The pages are written in the
form of an expose and attribute much more of the credit for evolutionary
theory to persons other than Darwin than is usually the case. Of particular
interest are full text versions of papers written by Edward Blyth between
1835 and 1837.</B></P>
