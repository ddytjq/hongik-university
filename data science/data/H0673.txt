href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<p>Stability of mRNAs depends on complex interactions between sequences in the RNA and protein factors that bind to them.</p>		<h4>Facts</h4>		<ul>			<li>Class I (from c-fos and c-myc mRNAs) <a href="MG237.html">AU rich elements</a> destabilized <i>neo</i> <a href="../MGW4/MG429.html">reporter</a> mRNA in a monocytic tumor cell line, but <a href="MG237.html">class II elements</a> (from GM-CSF mRNA) did not (<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov:80/entrez/query.fcgi?cmd=Retrieve&db=PubMed&list_uids=3060261&dopt=Abstract">ref</a>).		</ul>		<center>			<p>			<table>				<tr>					<td lymphocytes, most mRNA is unstable. Stimulation of primary T cells stabilized class II containing mRNAs, but not those with class I elements.			<li>A class II element (from IL-3 mRNA) is required for destabilization of IL-3 mRNA in mast cells treated with cyclosporin. Class I element containing mRNAs are not affected by cyclosporin.			<p><img align="right" src="i23711.gif"></p>			<li>Transferrin is a serum protein that carries iron. Receptors for this protein, transferrin receptors (TfR), can appear on liver cell surfaces. Regulation of TfR is important to the regulation of iron uptake and metabolism.			<li>The TfR mRNA is unstable when sufficient iron is present in the cell. It is stable (and thus capable of producing more TfR) when iron is deficient (<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov:80/entrez/query.fcgi?cmd=Retrieve&db=PubMed&list_uids=8994263&dopt=Abstract">ref</a>).			<li>Iron responsiveness of TfR mRNA stability is conferred by a secondary structure element in the 3' non-translated region of TfR mRNA, the <a href="MG2441.html">iron responsive element</a> (IRE).			<li>The IRE is the binding target of the iron-poor form of the tricarboxylic acid cycle enzyme <a href="MG2442.html">aconitase</a>. Binding leads to stabilization.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Interp"></a>Interpretations</h4>		<ul type="circle">			<li><a href="MG237.html">mRNA degradation</a> through recognition of AU-rich elements is a regulated process.			<li>Each class of elements is regulated by a different pathway.			<li>mRNA stability can be regulated by binding to sequences that prevent recognition of the destabilizing sequences.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other initiation</a> of another protein involved in iron metabolism, ferritin. <img src="nav5.gif" width="69" height="71" align="right" usemap="#nav4B1F889C9"><map name=""></map>			<li>Light increase the
