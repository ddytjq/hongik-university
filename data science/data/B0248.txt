<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" COLOR="cc0066"><B>

BORROWER AND STATUS</B></FONT>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">

<BR><BR>



·	Borrower should be U.K. Residents, able to provide usual proof of accommodation addresses.





<BR>

·	The Society must be satisfied that the borrower is conversant with the lettings market and a landlord's responsibilities, having appointed a professional managing agent for the letting, proof will be required.<BR>

·	Employed, self-employed and retired borrowers will be considered, subject to any existing and previous loans in the last three years having been well maintained.<BR>

·	75% of the rental income must be sufficient to cover the mortgage repayments (not just the interest) at the full rate.  Confirmation must be received from managing agent of rental income prior to offer.<BR>

·	In addition, applicant's income must be sufficient to cover all personal financial commitments.<BR>

·	Applications cannot be considered from applicants where the sole source of income is derived from rental income.<BR><BR>

</FONT>



<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" COLOR="cc0066">

<B>

PROPERTY</B>

</FONT>

<BR><BR>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">

·	Properties must be of standard construction, situated in England and Wales and within predominantly residential areas.<BR>

·	Properties with more than 1 kitchen or more than 5 bedrooms per letting unit will not be accepted.<BR>

·	All flats/maisonettes must be purpose built, self-contained units.  Bedsits are not acceptable.  The number of floors in any one block of flats must be no more than two.<BR>

·	Leasehold Flats/maisonettes are accepted subject to a minimum period of 40 years unexpired on the lease at the end of the mortgage term.<BR>

·	The Society will not accept converted or flat roofed properties.</FONT><BR><BR>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" COLOR="cc0066"><B>

TENANCIES</B></FONT>

<BR><BR>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">

·	Diplomatic, Student, Housing Association and Council lets are not considered.<BR>

·	Except where the tenant is a limited company, assured short hold tenancies only are acceptable with the period of each tenancy for not more than 12 months.<BR>

·	Where the tenant is a limited company, the Society must be satisfied with regard to the status of the company, as evidence by the most recent Report and Accounts, with occupation restricted to an employee of the company and his/her immediate family.<BR>

·	If not arranged by the Society*, insurance cover for the building must be approved by the Society prior to completion.  The policy must provide for letting of the property.<BR>

·	The Society's appointed (panel) solicitors, will represent the Society in each case.<BR><BR>



*  Insurance re-arrangement fee of £25 charged if not arranged by Society.</FONT>

<BR><BR>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" COLOR="cc0066"><B>

FEES</B></FONT>

<BR><BR>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">

·	A £100 non refundable arrangement fee is to be paid by the applicant at the time the application is submitted.<BR>

·	Application fee of £25.00 required with submission of application, this is non-refundable.<BR>

·	Valuation fee as per the Society's "Mortgage Valuations - Fees and Conditions" leaflet is required with submission of application.<BR><BR>

</FONT>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" COLOR="cc0066"><B>

PLEASE NOTE</B></FONT>

<FONT SIZE="2" FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">

<BR>

When considering a Buy to Let application, the Society does not imply that we believe rental income will continue at present levels, that the property is a good investment or that we believe the letting market will remain at the present level of demand.  It is recommended the applicant obtains independent professional advice prior to proceeding with the transaction.

<BR>

Applicants are recommended to read the leaflets "Buying to Let" and "Thinking of Buying a Residential Property to Let" which have been produced by the Council of Mortgage Lenders.  Copies are available on request from the Society.<BR><BR>
