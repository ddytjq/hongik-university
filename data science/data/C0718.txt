                                    <p align="center">&nbsp; </td>
                                  <td background width="398" height="100%">
 <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Your 
                                                contents policy will cover everything 
                                                you would take with you if you 
                                                moved house. This will include 
                                                furniture, kitchen equipment, 
                                                food and drink, electrical appliances, 
                                                clothes, ornaments and valuables. 
                                                <br>
                                                <br>
                                                The risks covered usually include 
                                                the following:
                                                </font> 
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Fire<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Theft<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Escape 
                                                  of water from tanks, apparatus 
                                                  or pipes<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Oil 
                                                  leaks from heating systems<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Storm<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Flood<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Subsidence<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Heave 
                                                  or landslip<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Falling 
                                                  trees or aerials<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Riot 
                                                  or malicious acts<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Explosion<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Earthquake<br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Impact 
                                                  by aircraft, vehicles or animals 
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                </ul>
                                    <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
                                                  Many policies also include accidental 
                                                  breakage of mirrors, glass tops 
                                                  and fixed glass in furniture. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Some also include accidental 
                                                  damage to televisions, videos, 
                                                  home computers and audio equipment. 
                                                  Sometimes you can extend your 
                                                  policy to cover such items as 
                                                  accidental damage to all your 
                                                  contents. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  'Limited cover' applies to any 
                                                  contents you temporarily remove 
                                                  from your home, for example 
                                                  when you visit friends or go 
                                                  on holiday. With an 'all risks 
                                                  policy' you can cover accidental 
                                                  damage or loss of valuable items 
                                                  you take out of your home, such 
                                                  as jewellery, cameras and sports 
                                                  equipment. <br>
                                                  <a name="#1"></a><br>
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">EXCEPTIONS 
                                                  AND EXCLUSIONS</font> </b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Read your policy to discover 
                                                  what exceptions and exclusions 
                                                  apply, but if you are in any 
                                                  doubt, consult your broker. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  The most common exclusions are: 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                    </p>
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                                  Theft - unless there was forced 
                                                  entry - if you have let or sub-let 
                                                  your house of part of it </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Sonic 
                                                  bangs </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Contamination 
                                                  from nuclear fuel or nuclear 
                                                  waste </font> 
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">War 
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                </ul>
                                    <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
                                                  Limits are usually imposed on 
                                                  the value of any works of art, 
                                                  ornaments or jewellery, and 
                                                  occasionally on high value goods 
                                                  like video equipment. Some insurance 
                                                  companies may agree to raise 
                                                  these limits. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  You may be required to pay some 
                                                  of the claim yourself - this 
                                                  is the excess. It's wise to 
                                                  check both limits and excess 
                                                  when you buy your policy. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Some insurers allow a discount 
                                                  if you offer to accept an excess 
                                                  on all claims no matter how 
                                                  they're caused. The discount 
                                                  increases in proportion to the 
                                                  size of the excess.<br>
                                                  <a name="#2"></a><br>
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">SETTLING 
                                                  YOUR CLAIM</font> </b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  The settlement of your claim 
                                                  depends on whether your policy 
                                                  is 'replacement as new' or 'indemnity'. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  'Replacement as new' policies 
                                                  pay the full cost of repairing 
                                                  damaged items under three years' 
                                                  old, or replacing them with 
                                                  new if stolen or destroyed. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Before an indemnity policy pays 
                                                  out, a deduction is made to 
                                                  take into account wear and tear. 
                                                  <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Most contents can be insured 
                                                  on a replacement as new basis, 
                                                  but this kind of policy cannot 
                                                  apply to clothes and household 
                                                  linen. Age limits can also apply, 
                                                  so check your policy carefully.<br>
                                                  <a name="#3"></a><br>
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">WORKING 
                                                  OUT THE SUM INSURED</font> </b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  The sum insured is the total 
                                                  amount for which your contents 
                                                  are covered, and is the most 
                                                  your insurer will pay, even 
                                                  if all your possessions are 
                                                  totally destroyed. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  If you are under-insured, some 
                                                  claim payments will be reduced, 
                                                  so:
                                                  </font> 
                                    </p>
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">List 
                                                  everything in your home, and 
                                                  note what it would cost to replace 
                                                  them at current prices. </font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">If 
                                                  certain items are difficult 
                                                  to value - antiques and jewellery 
                                                  are good examples - get an expert 
                                                  valuation.</font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Make 
                                                  sure that your insured sum stays 
                                                  up to date - seek a policy that 
                                                  is index-linked.</font></li>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Increase 
                                                  the sum insured if you buy further 
                                                  possessions.</font></li>
                                                </ul>
 <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Always 
                                                  keep receipts and valuations 
                                                  - they can be extremely helpful 
                                                  if you have to make a claim. 
                                                  Photographs of valuables can 
                                                  also be very useful in the event 
                                                  of a claim. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Ask <a href="findbroker.asp"><font color="#0000FF"> your broker</font></a>
to 
                                                  check if your insurer operates 
                                                  a no-claim discount on renewal 
                                                  of the policy, and whether you 
                                                  can get any discounts - for 
                                                  example, on account of your 
                                                  age, if security systems are 
                                                  installed, or if you pay an 
                                                  increased excess.<br>
                                                  <a name="#4"></a><br>
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">PREVENTION 
                                                  IS BETTER THAN A CLAIM </font></b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  If you increase the security 
                                                  of your home, some insurers 
                                                  will offer you a discount. Your 
                                                  local police will be pleased 
                                                  to offer you any advice on this 
                                                  subject. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  Insulating your water pipes 
                                                  and tanks will help prevent 
                                                  any accidental burst pipes, 
                                                  leaks or spillages - it also 
                                                  makes sense to make sure you 
                                                  and your family know where the 
                                                  mains water stop cock is and 
                                                  to check that it is in good 
                                                  working order.<br>
                                                  <a name="#5"></a><br>
                                                  <b><font color="#333399">OTHER 
                                                  ASPECTS OF CONTENTS COVER</font></b><br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font> 
                                                <ul>
                                                <li><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Alternative 
                                                  accommodation</b> - if your 
                                                  home is so badly damaged that 
                                                  you cannot live it in, your 
                                                  policy will pay towards the 
                                                  cost of alternative accommodation 
                                                  until your own home is repaired. 
                                                  This payment can be as much 
                                                  as 20% of your sum insured.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li> <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Your 
                                                  legal liabilitie</b>s - if, 
                                                  as occupier of your home, you 
                                                  cause injury to someone else, 
                                                  or damage their property, your 
                                                  policy will pay, in most cases, 
                                                  damages of up to £1 million. 
                                                  In addition, you and your family 
                                                  are usually covered for liability 
                                                  in your day-to-day life.<br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                <li> <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>As 
                                                  tenants</b> - many policies 
                                                  include legal expenses insurance 
                                                  and provide cover for your liability 
                                                  for damage to the building you 
                                                  live in. <br>
                                                  <br>
                                                  </font></li>
                                                </ul>
