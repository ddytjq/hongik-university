				<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Music at Wembley</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">Each generation has its own defining image of Wembley: the white horse, the 1948 Olympics, the 1966 World Cup. However, before Euro 96 reclaimed Wembley for football, many people came to regard Wembley as a music venue. This is largely down to the staggering success of Live Aid.</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
				<br>
				</font>
				<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="512">
					<tr>
						<td width="60" align="left" valign="top">
							<div align="left">
								<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><img height="110" width="48" src="../images/main/history/live_aid.gif"></font></div>
						</td>
						<td width="452" align="left" valign="top">
							<div align="left">
								<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><b>The Live Aid Concert</b> began on 13 July 1985 to the strains of 'Rocking All Over the World' by Status Quo. At 5.00pm London time it was linked with the JFK Stadium in Philadelphia, USA, when the performers played alternate sets for worldwide broadcast. Billed as the Global Juke Box, the event was the brainchild of Sir Bob Geldof, whose aim was to raise money for the starving millions in Ethiopia and Sudan, and increase consciousness of their plight to people across the globe.</font></div>
						</td>
					</tr>
				</table>
				<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">In total, the concert raised in excess of $100 million, excluding government contributions to specific projects.<br>
				<br>
				Three years later, The Nelson Mandela 70th Birthday Tribute Concert on 11 June 1988 was broadcast to over 72 countries throughout the world. It was the world's largest audience for any televised music event.<br>
				<br>
				In terms of number of appearances, Michael Jackson has played more dates at Wembley Stadium than any other artist. He has been seen by well over a million fans at Wembley since his first shows there in 1988.<br>
				<br>
				Going back to the beginning of Wembley's musical heritage, the first concert in the stadium was The London Rock and Roll Show in August 1972.<br>
				<br>
				The bill featured Bill Haley and the Comets, Jerry Lee Lewis, Little Richard and Chuck Berry. It took another five years for a band to receive solo billing at Wembley - Crosby Stills and Nash in September 1977.<br>
