                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1" class="NPBS_header">Mortgage 
                              Code</font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">The 
                              Society subscribes to the Mortgage Code and is registered 
                              with the Mortgage Code Compliance Board (registration

                              standards which mortgage lenders and intermediaries 
                              have to meet. This page is designed to introduce 
                              the Code to you. </font></p>

                            <p><br>
                              <font size="-1"><b><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><font color="#000066"><span class="Text5"><a name="top"></a><a href="#thmoco" class=second  onMouseOver="MM_displayStatusMsg('The Mortgage Code');return document.MM_returnValue">The 
                              Mortgage Code</a><br>
                              </span></font></font></b></font><font size="-1"><b><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><font color="#000066"><span class="Text5"><a href="#chamo" class=second  onMouseOver="MM_displayStatusMsg('Choosing a mortgage');return document.MM_returnValue">Choosing 
                              a mortgage</a><br>
                              </span></font></font></b></font><font size="-1"><b><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><font color="#000066"><span class="Text5"><a href="#thcomaco" class=second  onMouseOver="MM_displayStatusMsg('The Codes main commitments');return document.MM_returnValue">The 
                              Code's main commitments<br>
                              </a></span></font></font></b></font><font size="-1"><b><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><font color="#000066"><span class="Text5"><a href="#ketothco" class=second  onMouseOver="MM_displayStatusMsg('Keeping to the code');return document.MM_returnValue">Keeping 
                              to the code</a></span></font></font></b></font></p>
                            <p>&nbsp;</p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"><b> 
                              <font color="#006699"><a name="thmoco"></a></font></b></font><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><b><font color="#006699"><font color="#000066">The 
                              Mortgage Code</font> </font></b></font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              The code sets out: </font></p>
                            <ul>
                              <li> <font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">how 
                                your mortgage should be arranged;</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">what 
                                information you should receive before you commit 
                                yourself; and</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">how 
                                your mortgage should be dealt with once it is 
                                in place.<br>
                                </font></li>
                            </ul>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              If a lender or intermediary fails to meet the standards 
                              of the Code, and you suffer as a result, you have 
                              the right to compensation under a compulsory independent 
                              complaints scheme. <br>
                              <br>
                              </font><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">The 
                              rest of this page concentrates on the details which 
                              are most relevant to you when you are arranging 
                              a mortgage. We then give you an outline of the Code's 
                              main commitments. You can use this page as a checklist 
                              to help you through the process of arranging a mortgage. 
                              </font></p>
                            <p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../graphics/nav_up.gif" width="21" height="21" lowsrc="#top" alt="Return to menu options" border="0"></a></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"><b> 
                              <font color="#006699"><a name="chamo"></a></font></b></font><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><b><font color="#000066">Choosing 
                              a mortgage </font></b></font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              There are three different levels of service which 
                              your lender or intermediary may provide to help 
                              you choose a suitable mortgage. The lender or intermediary 
                              will tell you, at the beginning, which of these 
                              levels of service they can provide. The levels are: 
                              </font></p>
                            <ul>
                              <li> <font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"><b><font color="#006699">advice 
                                and recommendation</font></b> on which of the 
                                mortgages they can provide is most suitable for 
                                you;</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"><b><font color="#006699">information 
                                on the different types of mortgage product</font></b> 
                                on offer so that you can make an informed choice 
                                of which to take; or</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1" color="#006699"><b>information 
                                on a single mortgage product only</b></font><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">, 
                                if only one mortgage is available or if you have 
                                already made up your mind.<br>
                                </font></li>
                            </ul>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              Check that you understand which level of service 
                              you are being offered, and what this means for you. 
                              </font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              Whichever level of service they provide, your lender 
                              or intermediary should give you information on all 
                              the following areas of the mortgage you are considering:</font></p>
                            <ul>
                              <li> <font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">the 
                                repayment method (for example, endowment, capital 
                                and interest) and the repayment period</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">the 
                                financial consequences of repaying the mortgage 
                                early</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">the 
                                type of interest rates - variable, fixed, discounted, 
                                capped and so on</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">what 
                                your future repayments after any fixed or discounted 
                                period might be</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">whether 
                                you have to take any insurance services with the 
                                mortgage, and if so whether the insurance must 
                                be arranged by the lender or the intermediary</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">the 
                                costs and fees which might be involved with the 
                                mortgage - valuation fees, arrangement fees, legal 
                                fees, early repayment charges and so on</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">whether 
                                you can continue with your selected mortgage terms 
                                if you move house</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">when 
                                your account details may be passed to credit reference 
                                agencies</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">Mortgage 
                                Interest Tax Relief (MIRAS)</font></li>
                              <li><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1">whether 
                                you need to pay a high percentage lending fee, 
                                and if so what this means to you.<br>
                                </font></li>
                            </ul>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              If you are using the services of a mortgage intermediary 
                              to arrange the loan, they must also tell you if 
                              they are receiving a fee from the lender for introducing 
                              the mortgage to the lender. They must also let you 
                              know whether they usually arrange mortgages from 
                              a number of selected lenders or from the market 
                              as a whole. </font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              Before your mortgage is completed, your lender or 
                              intermediary will confirm, in writing, the level 
                              of service they have provided, and reasons for any 
                              mortgage recommendation (if they gave you one). 
                              Check that you fully understand this written confirmation, 
                              and ask if there is anything that is still not clear 
                              to you at this stage. </font></p>
                            <p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../graphics/nav_up.gif" width="21" height="21" lowsrc="#top" alt="Return to menu options" border="0"></a></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"><b> 
                              <font color="#006699"><a name="thcomaco"></a></font></b></font><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif"><b><font color="#000066">The 
                              Code's main commitments </font></b></font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              The Code has 10 main commitments. Broadly speaking, 
                              these say that lenders and intermediaries will: 
                              </font></p>
                            <font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                            <ul>
                              <li>act fairly and reasonably with you at all times;</li>
                              <li>make sure that all services and products keep 
                                to the conditions of the Code, even if they have 
                                their own terms and conditions;</li>
                              <li>give you information on services and products 
                                in plain language, and offer help if there is 
                                any area which you do not understand;</li>
                              <li>help you to choose a mortgage to fit your needs, 
                                unless you have already decided on your mortgage;</li>
                              <li>help you to understand the financial effects 
                                of having a mortgage;</li>
                              <li>help you to understand how your mortgage account 
                                works;</li>
                              <li>make sure that the procedures staff follow reflect 
                                the commitments set out in the Code;</li>
                              <li>correct errors and handle complaints speedily;</li>
                              <li>consider cases of financial difficulty and mortgage 
                                arrears (missed payments) sympathetically and 
                                positively; and</li>
                              <li>make sure that all services and products meet 
                                the relevant laws and regulations.<br>
                              </li>
                            </ul>
                            </font>

                              to the Code </font></font></b></font></p>
                            <p><font face="Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif" size="-1"> 
                              How the lenders or intermediaries keep to the mortgage 
                              code is monitored independently. And, any organisation 
                              under the Code must be a member of a recognised 
                              complaints scheme - such as the Banking Ombudsman, 
                              the Building Society Ombudsman, or the Mortgage 
                              Code Arbitration Scheme. This gives you an extra 
                              level of protection, as each of these schemes can 
                              award compensation of up to £100,000 to you if you 
                              suffer as a result of your lender or intermediary 
                              failing to keep to the Code. Your lender or intermediary 
                              will be able to tell you which scheme applies.</font></p>
