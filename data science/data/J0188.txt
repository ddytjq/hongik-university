        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Von Kampen Claims 
          First Bomber Main Event</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Winning your first 
          ever Bomber A Main on a night your parent's business sponsored the nights 
          racing is something Courtenay's <b>Jeff von Kampen</b> will remember 
          doing for a long time as the second year driver did just that on Saturday 
          at Saratoga Speedway. Black Creek Building Supply, the race sponsor, 
          is owned by Jeff's parents <b>Ralph</b> and <b>Cindy von Kampen</b> 
          who have been dedicated speedway sponsors since it reopened 22 years 
          ago, and actually go back even further as they were even involved with 
          the speedway when it was operated as Oyster River Speedway.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This season Ralph 
          competes in the Stock Car divison, while Cindy has returned to work 
          in the tower - a position she has held many times over the years, as 
          well as taking a turn as a driver for a season. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">After running mostly 
          B Mains over his first two seasons, Jeff started 7th in the 14 car feature 
          race, and by the 8th lap found himself the race leader, with everyone 
          now chasing him for the lead. Over the next 13 laps he would ,build 
          up a big lead while lapping cars, however that lead would start to disappear 
          as <b>Chris Beaulieu</b> would move up on his rear bumper on the 21st 
          lap.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With lapped traffic 
          now ahead of him, and Beaulieu pressuring him from behind, von Kampen 
          calmly weaved his way through the traffic and kept the winner of the 
          previous two A mains behind him for the final three laps to win his 
          first A main ahead of Beaulieu and <b>BJ Bradley</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With his third place 
          main event finish Bradley continues to lead the point race 40 points 
          ahead of Beauilieu, while von Kampen's win puts him in 8th place overall. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Four trophy dashes 
          kicked off the Bomber racing and they had <b>Carl Carlsen</b> winning 
          his first Bomber race as he took the D dash win, followed by a C dash 
          win for <b>Daryl Wardrope</b>, a B dash win for von Kampen and an A 
          dash win for Bradley. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Heat race wins went 
          to rookies <b>Mike Logan</b>, <b>Austin Hsylop</b> and Beaulieu.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Nanaimo's <b>Verona 
          Tannock</b> came from the back of the 10 car field to win the B Main 
          ahead of <b>Glen Pinto</b> and Logan. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A 25 lap, 9 car 
          bumper-to-bumper Stock Car Main event saw Parksville's <b>Brad McLean</b> 
          get to the front first on the 2nd lap, then proceed to drive an error 
          free race. With every other car right behind him waiting for a slip 
          up or a break that never came, McLean claimed his 2nd main event win 
          of the season. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Once McLean was 
          in the lead, <b>Ray Anderosov</b> slipped in behind him followed by 
          <b>Don Hallgarth</b>, <b>Dave Hargrave</b>, <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> and 
          <b>David Mainse.</b> They battled side by side and bumper to bumper 
          untill the 15th lap when Hargrave and Beaulieu came together resulting 
          in a black flag for Hargrave and a flat tire for Beaulieu 7 laps latter. 
          The final laps saw McLean hold his grip on first, with Anderosov holding 
          off a strong challenge from Hallgarth to finish 2nd, while Mainse would 
          finish 4th ahead of <b>Mike Meeres</b>.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Before his main 
          event black flag, Hargrave was on his way to a big night after wins 
          in the B dash and 12 lap, 9 car heat race, while Nanaimo's <b>Morley 
          Axelson</b> took the win in the A dash.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Following his 4th 
          place finish in the main, and Beaulieu's 8th place finish, Mainse is 
          now 3 points ahead for the overall points lead. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A small turnout 
          of motorcycles made for some quick races and they had Nanaimo's <b>Glen 
          Harrison</b> winning the trophy dash and main event, while <b>Dean Thompson</b> 
          won the heat race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Racing this Saturday 
          night have the Magic 98.9 Crash to Pass cars competeing for the <b>Bob 
          Kelly Memorial Trophy</b> and they will be joined by the Stock cars 
          and the Figure 8 cars. Gates open at 5.30 and all the racing set for 
          7pm. </font></p>
