			<P><HR COLOR="#FF6F0F"></P>
		
		<P>The year 2000 was one of change and yet consolidation for Mal. He secured a modern workshop to prepare the car for racing and passenger rides and worked steadily building up the business of The Supercar Experience, which went from strength to strength as the public began to flock to the rare opportunity to take a ride on the wild side.</P>
		<P>In 2000 Mal competed in several rounds of the Konica V8 Lites Championship. Although the full season consisted of 5 rounds, due to budget limitations, Mal was only able to race at Oran Park and Lakeside, and yet still finished the Championship a creditable 9th.</P>
		<P>In October 2000, Mal bought the ex Chris Smerdon VS Commodore. It's debut was at Bathurst in November, which transpired to be one of the most eventful races ever seen on the mountain.</P>

		<P>In 2001 Mal again competed at only two rounds of the Konica V8 Supercar Championship. A strong showing at Wakefield Park in round one saw him take a 2nd placing, and a 4th, for 5th overall.</P>
		<P>Deciding to focus his efforts on promoting and developing the Supercar Experience, Mal decided to forgo the remaining rounds of the Konica series, and only to run at Bathurst late in the year, as this is the pinnacle event and provides the highest exposure for sponsors and backers.</P>
		<P>In October 2001, Mal took the ex-Pinnacle Motorsport VS Commodore around Bathurst in the Konica V8 Supercar Challenge. He qualified this 6 year old car virtually as fast as it had ever been around the circuit - in 2 minutes 16 seconds. A tyre blowout while running 4th caused some damage under the car, and Mal retired from the race just two laps from home. Nonetheless, Bathurst is Mal's favourite track and not to be missed.</P>
		<P>Mal spent the rest of the year &quot;freshening up&quot; the motors and drivetrain of his two Commodores, to ensure his customers continue to get a 100% V8 experience.</P>
