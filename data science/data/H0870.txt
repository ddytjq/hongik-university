<h1>
<a href ="http://blocks.fhcrc.org/">Protein Sequence Blocks</a>
</h1>

<A HREF = "icons/tc1.big.gif"><IMG SRC="icons/tc1.small.gif" align = right></a>

<p>
Here are some comments by <br>
 Shmuel Pietrokovski                     ! pietro@sparky.fhcrc.org  <br>
 Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center  ! fax: ##1 (206) 667 5889  <br>
 1124 Columbia St. Seattle, WA 98104 USA ! tel: ##1 (206) 667 4509  <br>
on this technique for looking at protein components:

<p>
"We implemented among other things a 
logo representation for blocks I think its great. You can check it out 
by choosing the Getblock option, finding some block family you like using 
the full indexed keyword search, returning to the getblock screen and 
entering that block family number in the window.  There you would see the 
logo options."

<p>
"Each block family has a code(BL#####, # is a digit) with no special 
significance except being derived from the prosite family number 
(PS#####)."

<p>
"Each block in a block family has logo representation.  In order to see 
a logo you first have to choose a family and then a block in it.  If you 
don't know the family code you can use the "full indexed keyword search
of Blocks Database" to find the block using keywords (globin, serine 
kinase, histone etc).  Even if you just want to see any logo you first 
have to choose a block. You can try any random number but it might happen 
that there is no such block family. BL00027, BL00622 are some family code 
I happen to remember, but you might want to see a logo of a specific 
family (BL01033 is the globin family)."

<p>
"We described the logo option in a manuscript about the server.  You can 
<a href="ftp://howard.fhcrc.org/papers/">FTP</a>
it from
<a href="ftp://howard.fhcrc.org/papers/BLOCKMAKER.ps">
ftp://howard.fhcrc.org/papers/BLOCKMAKER.ps</a>.

<p>
The paper was published at
<pre>
@article{Henikoff-Pietrokovski1995,
author = "S. Henikoff
 and J. G. Henikoff 
 and W. J. Alford 
 and S. Pietrokovski",   
title = "Automated construction and graphical presentation of protein blocks
from unaligned sequences",   
journal = "Gene",
volume = "163", 
pages = "GC17-GC26",
year = "1995"}
</pre>

<p>
To see the example of the globin family, go
<a href = "http://blocks.fhcrc.org/blocks-bin/getblock.www?BL01033">here</A>.

<h1>
<a href ="http://www.cbs.dtu.dk/gorodkin/appl/slogo.html">RNA Structure Logos</a>
</h1>
 
<p>
J. Gorodkin, L. J. Heyer, S. Brunak and G. D. Stormo. Displaying the information
contents of structural RNA alignments: the structure logos. Comput. Appl. Biosci.,
Vol. 13, no. 6 pp 583-586, 1997. 
</p>

<p>
<A HREF = "icons/rnastructurelogo.gif"><IMG
SRC="icons/rnastructurelogotiny.gif" align = right></a>
"The structure logo is an extension of the sequence logo by Schneider and
Stephens. We have extended the standard sequence logo to cope with any prior
nucleotide distribution as well as allowing for gaps in the alignments, and
indicate mutual information of basepaired positions in RNA. Thus the logo is
composed by a sequence part and a structure/basepair part."
</p>
