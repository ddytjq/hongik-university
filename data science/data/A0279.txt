<a name="50day1"></a><span class="bodytextbold">Who can open a 50 Day Notice Account?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">All UK residents aged 16 or over. Accounts can be opened on behalf of 
a minor (under 16 years old) by an adult. Accounts can be held in single or joint 
names.</span><br>
<br>
<a name="50day2"></a><span class="bodytextbold">What is a Savings Plan?</span> <br>
<span class="bodytext"> A Savings Plan can hold up to 99 different accounts owned by up to 
six different people. Each individual's account details will remain private and 
secure at all times with each person having their own PIN and password. A Savings 
Plan can include Direct Access Savings Accounts, 50 Day Notice Accounts and Individual 
Savings Accounts (ISAs).<br>
<br>

<BR><BR></span> <a name="50day4"></a><span class="bodytextbold">Are there any charges 
  for this account?</span> <br>
  <span class="bodytext">There are no charges attached to this account if the notice period 
  is adhered to. You will be charged a penalty of 50 days' notice on the amount 
  withdrawn if you do not give us 50 clear days' notice of withdrawal. This penalty 
  will be deducted from the remaining balance in your account. There is no limit 
  to the number of withdrawals that you can request. In addition, interim statements 
  can be requested at any time free of charge.</span>

<BR><BR></span> <a name="50day5"></A><span class="bodytextbold">How secure is

  Your account is easy to access and yet incredibly secure. When you open an account

  for access to your accounts on the telephone. It's important to remember that 
  you should not divulge your PIN and password to anyone. </p>
<p>When you ring us to transact on your account you will be asked for a random 
  number from your PIN and a character from your password. </p>
<p>If you wish to transact on your accounts via the internet, you can register

<p>If you transact online, you have the reassurance that we use the latest security

  your UserId or internet password to anyone, not even our staff. And in case 
  you forget to log off, the system will automatically log off for you if you 
  haven't used your mouse or keypad after a few minutes.</p>
<p>As with all Standard Life Bank products, our security functions have been stringently 
  tested. Data is scrambled to ensure privacy and we use 128 bit SSL encryption 
  to securely encode and decode the data flowing between your PC and our server.<br>
</p></span>

<a name="50day7"></a><span class="bodytextbold">What is pooling?</span>
    <br>
    <span class="bodytext">Pooling allows you to link together more than one account 
    within a Savings Plan (either on your own or with up to five members of family 
    and friends) to take extra advantage of our interest rates. Interest is paid 
    on each individual account at the rate which applies to the total balance 
    of all the accounts held in the Savings Plan. As many as 99 accounts can be 
    pooled within a Plan with up to five other people. In addition, you can have 
    five children named in your Plan.</p>
  <p>For example, six people agree that they will pool together in the same Plan. 
    Each deposits £2,000 into a 50 Day Notice Account. Despite the fact 
    that individually they only have £2,000 in their accounts, they will 
    all get paid interest at the rate which applies to the £12,000 tier. 
  </p>
  <p>Importantly each account remains completely private. Each account holder 
    still has their own PIN and password and receives their own separate statements, 
    but simply by pooling together everybody in the Plan benefits from a higher 
    rate of interest. </p>
  <p>If you want to add another person to your existing plan, you will need to 
    call us so that we can take you through your security. You will then need 
    to give us all the details of the new person to be added. This is the only 
    time you will talk on their behalf, they will be sent their own security once 
    their own new account is set up.</p>
  <p>Please note however, that you can only pool accounts with the same notice 
    period - you can't pool 50 Day Notice Accounts with Direct Access Savings 
    Accounts.<br>

<a name="50day8"></a><span class="bodytextbold">How & when is interest paid?</span>
    <br>
    <span class="bodytext">Interest can be paid monthly or yearly by either: <br></span>
  <UL>
    <LI><span class="bodytext">adding it to your account</span></LI> 
    <LI><span class="bodytext">transferring it to another account in your Plan </span></LI> 
    <LI><span class="bodytext">transferring it to your designated external account.</span></LI> 
  </UL>
  <span class="bodytext"><p>For personal savings, all the interest will be paid net for UK tax-payers 
    except for ISA accounts where interest is paid gross. If you are exempt from 
    tax we will send you the necessary documentation to complete and all your 
    interest will be paid gross. </p>
  <p>If you already hold a Standard Life Bank account and have registered with 
    us to use Online Banking facilities, then you can browse through your account 
    during online banking hours (Monday to Friday 7am-11pm, Saturday 8am-8pm and 
    Sunday 12pm-8pm) and see how much interest you have.<br></span>
