<p>
This web page is designed to give everyone an idea of what our universe
actually looks like.  There are nine main maps on this web page, each one
approximately ten times the scale of the previous one.  The first map shows
the nearest stars and then the other maps slowly expand out until we have
reached the scale of the entire visible universe.
</p>

        <u>The Nearest Stars</u></center>
The closest star to the Sun is a mere 7000 times further than
the edge of our solar system.  This map shows all of the stellar
systems that lie within 12.5 light years from us.</td></tr>

    <td><center><a href="250lys.html"><b>250 Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Solar Neighbourhood</u></center>
A large proportion of the stars visible with the naked eye are within
250 light years.  This map shows this tiny section of our galaxy
that surrounds our Sun.</td></tr>

        <u>The Orion Arm</u></center>
The local arm of our galaxy is called the Orion Arm.  It is
depicted here showing the millions of stars interspersed with
clouds of interstellar gas.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows10.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="galaxy.html"><img src="s_galaxy.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Milky Way Galaxy" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="galaxy.html"><b>50000 Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Milky Way Galaxy</u></center>
Our galaxy is a loose spiral disc of one hundred billion stars
rotating around a compact centre.  This is a diagram showing the
main features of the Galaxy.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows10.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="sattelit.html"><img src="s_satell.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Satellite Galaxies" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="sattelit.html"><b>500000 Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Satellite Galaxies</u></center>
The Milky Way is surrounded by several dwarf galaxies slowly
orbiting it in periods of billions of years.  This map shows the
nearest such satellite galaxies.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows10.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="localgr.html"><img src="s_localg.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Local Group" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="localgr.html"><b>5 million Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Local Group</u></center>
The Milky Way is gravitationally bound to two other large spiral
galaxies as well as dozens of dwarf galaxies.  This local group
of galaxies is illustrated here.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows20.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="virgo.html"><img src="s_virgoc.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Virgo Supercluster" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="virgo.html"><b>100 million Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Virgo Supercluster</u></center>
The local group of galaxies is just one of many centred around
the massive Virgo Cluster.  Collectively, all of these groups and
clusters form a unit known as the Virgo Supercluster shown here.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows10.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="superc.html"><img src="s_superc.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Neighbouring Superclusters" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="superc.html"><b>1 billion Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Neighbouring Superclusters</u></center>
The distribution of galaxies in the universe is far from regular.
They tend to clump together into huge supercluster formations.
This map shows many of the superclusters within 1 billion light
years of us.</td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td colspan="2" align="center"><img src="arrows15.gif" width="200" height="35" /></td></tr>
<tr bgcolor="#000000"><td><a href="universe.html"><img src="s_univer.gif" width="128" height="96" alt="The Visible Universe" /></a></td>
    <td><center><a href="universe.html"><b>15 billion Light Years from the Sun</b></a><br />
        <u>The Visible Universe</u></center>
Although our knowledge of the large scale structure of the
universe is incomplete, many large and small scale features are
visible right out to the very edge of the visible universe.  The
entire universe is fairly uniform, as this map shows.</td></tr>
