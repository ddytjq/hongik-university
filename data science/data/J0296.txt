        <p> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">If the first race 
          of the 2002 season is any indication of what to expect this year at 
          <b>Saratoga Speedway</b> then race fans are in for exciting spring and 
          summer of auto racing. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Forty one race cars 
          filled the pit area as the <b>Stock Cars</b> and <b>Bomber Cars</b> 
          opened their season with a double point race night, while the travelling 
          <b>Road Hogs</b> opened their season at the Black Creek oval. All three 
          classes combined to entertained a small crowd with some highly competitive 
          races.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The Stock Car class 
          had 11 cars out for their 1st race which saw Campbell River's <b>Ray 
          Anderosov</b> come away with his 1st main event win by holding off defending 
          point champion <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> and <b>David Mainse</b> in the 
          30 lap race.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The first main event 
          of the season had 2001 Bomber point champion <b>Brad McLean</b> lead 
          the field for the first 13 laps before <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> got by 
          to take a brief lead before Anderosov got by him on the next lap. Once 
          Anderosov got by von Kampen and McLean ,Mainse and Beaulieu would guickly 
          follow and move right in behind the 4th year driver with Mainse the 
          first to pressure as he almost got underneath the #3 chevelle coming 
          thru the doglegg a couple of times, but Anderosov shut the door each 
          time. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Beaulieu meanwhile 
          struggled for a couple laps on the outside groove but, with less than 
          5 laps remaining, was able to get on the outside of Mainse and put the 
          pressure on Anderosov who didn't make a mistake to win ahead of Beaulieu 
          and Mainse. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Black Creeks von 
          Kampen kicked off the stock car season with the season's first win in 
          the B dash while <b>Rob Leighton</b> broke his new car in the winning 
          way by capturing the A dash while <b>Mike Meers</b> made his return 
          to the stock car class a winning one by taking the win a fast 15 lap 
          heat race, the season's first point race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Twenty Bomber cars 
          opened their season with eight races with Campbell River's <b>Darren 
          " Bam Bam" Larsen</b> taking the first A main of the season with Courtenay's 
          <b>Jason Waters</b> coming away with the B main win.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 25 lap A main 
          which had no yellow flags for a flag-free race, saw Larsen jump out 
          to a early lead which he would build into a huge margin which <b>Mike 
          Dionne</b> cut into, but ran out of time and had to settle for second 
          ahead of Quadra's <b>Little Richard Olson</b>. The 2nd place finish 
          gives Dionne the early season point lead 14 points up on <b>BJ Bradley</b> 
          and Larsen. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Bomber season 
          kicked off with four trophy dashes begining with the D dash which was 
          won by Parksville's <b>Brad Knorr</b> followed by a C dash win by <b>Troy 
          Carnie</b>,a B dash win for Larsen and a A dash win for Dionne who would 
          also win the A heat, while <b>Jeff von Kampen</b> took the B heat win. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A group of 10 Road 
          Hogs made the tow up island for their first of six races for the two 
          person race cars at Saratoga this season and they continued the way 
          they left off last season, with some spirited racing that left most 
          of the cars with more extra paint than they showed up with.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The four road hog 
          races saw <b>Al Jeffires</b> and <b>Andy Tibideau</b> win the b dash, 
          <b>Ross Moore</b> and <b>Brad Cox</b> the A dash and heat race and the 
          25 lap main event win went to <b>Bruce Gill</b> and <b>Scott Sheets</b>. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Racing action this 
          Saturday night (April 27th) features the <b>Crash to Pass</b> cars, 
          the <b>Stock Cars</b>, <b>Figure 8 Cars</b> and the <b>Dwarf Cars</b> 
          with gates open at 5.30.time trails are at 6 with racing set to get 
          under way at this season['s new start time of 7pm. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Guest talk</b>...........Tough 
          break for 92 Hobby stock champion <b>Rob Gaudrealt</b> as the Port Alberni 
          racer, making his return to racing this season in the stock cars, as 
          his motor blew only three laps into the main event......Saturday nights 
          racing was sponsored by <b>The Winners Circle</b> in Willow Point.......Long 
          time speedway sponsors back on board this season are <b>Black Creek 
          Building Supply</b> and <b>Oyster River Florist</b> and to you and other 
          a big thanks,,,,,,,,,,,Car driver changes have Gaudrealt in <b>Chris
