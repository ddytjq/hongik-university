  <hr color="#0000FF">
  <p align="left" style="word-spacing: 0; line-height: 100%; text-indent: 0; margin: 0"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  England
  were brilliant in qualification for Italia '80.&nbsp; The competition had
  expanded to accommodate a new group stage in the host country instead of the
  old quarter finals which were originally played over two legs.&nbsp; We were drawn
  in the same group as both Northern Ireland and the Republic and in a very
  tough group we were joined by
  Denmark and Bulgaria.&nbsp; But Ron Greenwoods men made light work or our
  opponents and the only points we dropped were in Dublin where a 1-1 draw was
  played out.&nbsp; Finishing top of the group was accomplished by scoring
  twenty two goals and letting in only five.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
  <p align="left" style="word-spacing: 0; line-height: 100%; text-indent: 0; margin: 0"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  Once we reached Italy, England were paired with the host nation, Belgium and
  Spain.&nbsp; But we got off to a bad start with a 1-1 draw against Belgium
  where the match was marred by crown trouble.&nbsp; Against Italy, England
  played a hard fought game but went down 1-0 to a Tardelli goal 10 minutes from
  time, this effectively eliminated us from the tournament.&nbsp; In the final
  match, Tony Woodcock and Trevor Brooking scored in a fine 2-1 win over Spain,
  but that wasn't enough for us to qualify for the next stage and went sadly
  home.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
  <p align="left" style="word-spacing: 0; line-height: 100%; text-indent: 0; margin: 0"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
  In their third final running, West Germany beat belgium 2-1 to take the
  trophy.</font></p>
