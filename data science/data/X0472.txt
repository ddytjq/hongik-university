				<font class="news"><center><b>INDIA INNINGS</b><br>333 for 6 off 50 overs</center><br>India wins the toss and Ganguly elects to bat, despite the fact that 3 of the 4 teams batting second in the series have won the match. For India, Vijay Bhardwaj replaces Murali Kartik, and for the visitors Gary Brent comes in for the injured Pommie Mbangwa.<br>Ganguly opens with Mongia on a pitch that suits strokeplay. Ganguly collects his 7500th ODI run, as the openers find it hard going after a bright start. Ganguly breaks the tension with a lofted drive over mid on, then slams Hondo to the cover boundary in the same over. Mongia also goes deep off Hondo to take 13 runs off the six balls. The captain then takes two more fours off Streak's next over as India's total takes off with 31 runs in 3 overs (49 off 10 overs). <br>Taibu drops Mongia off an inside edge that could prove very costly, but the keeper safely pouches Ganguly's thick edge (52-1). Laxman joins Mongia and keeps the run rate at over 5 an over over to reach 83 off the first 15 overs. A mix up sees Laxman run out needlessly for 16. Dravid strokes his first ball for 4 to bring up the 100 off 19.3 overs. <br>Mongia brings up his 50 off 64 balls keeping the rate at 5 an over (116 off 23). At halfway India is well placed on 123 for 2 looking at a total of around 275. 50 partnership off 52 balls, but playing across the line Dravid is bowled by Hondo for 26.<br>Kaif fails to match his previous innings by holing out to A Flower at midwicket off Hondo for only 5 (157-4). Mongia reaches 72 (off 86 balls) to post his highest ODI score. With some sweet timing, Yuvraj Singh ominously looks to be in the same form as Hyderabad's match winning innings. <br>With Mongia well set and Singh looking to move the score along, the run rate rises to 5.28 off 38 overs (200-4). Mongia sets his sights on an ODI century, while Singh continues to flay the ball around the park reaching a 50 partnership off 52 balls. <br>Mongia reaches his 100 off 121 balls (226-4) and then goes after the bowling with his 12th boundary, as Singh also follows suit as 14 comes off Friend's over. Mongia throws caution to the wind, opens his shoulders but is dropped in the deep by Campbell, then Singh pulls a full toss for six - 252 for 4 (off 44 overs). Runs come all round the wicket off both bats as 100 partnership comes off 83 balls. <br>Singh's 50 comes off 41 balls. Zimbabwe has no answer to this torrent of runs as Friend goes for 29 off 10 balls. With 5 overs left, 300 plus is well on, as Mongia hits a massive straight six off Brent, quickly followed by a towering Singh six. <br>Both bats are on fire - fours, sixes, every shot in the book as runs pile up at an alarming rate. 132 partnership of 93 balls - 67 runs in 4 overs. 300 off 46.4 overs, the last 50 off a sensational 16 balls! <br>Zimbabwe is in shock as 83 runs come off 5 overs. 306 for 4 sets a new stadium ODI record. <br>150 partnership off 102 balls, but Singh caught off Marillier for 75.  Bhardwaj run out first ball as he sacrifices himself. <br>Mongia reaches 150 off 143 balls with one over left. Agarkar weighs in with a boundary as India brings up its highest ever total against Zimbabwe. Mongia ends in style with two boundaries and 159 not out (17x4 1x6). The innings closes on a mammoth 333 for 6 off 50 overs - India's 4th highest ODI score. <br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b> <br>52 for 1 Ganguly ct Taibu b Streak 28 (off 31 balls 4x4) <br>98 for 2 Laxman run out 16 (off 27 balls 2x4) <br>149 for 3 Dravid b Hondo 26 (off 33 balls 1x4) <br>157 for 4 Kaif ct A Flower b Hondo 5 (off 6 balls) <br>315 for 5 Y Singh ct Friend b Marillier 75 (off 52 balls) <br>316 for 6  Bhardwaj run out 0 (off 1 ball) <br>Mongia not out 159 (off 147 balls)<br>Agarkar not out 6 (off 3 balls)<br>India 333 for 6 off 50 overs<br><br><b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 16 for 0 <br>10 overs = 49 for 0<br>15 overs = 81 for 1 <br>20 overs = 102 for 2<br>25 overs = 130 for 2 <br>30 overs = 156 for 3 <br>35 overs = 182 for 4<br>40 overs = 212 for 4<br>45 overs = 269 for 4 <br>50 overs = 333 for 6<br><br> <center><b>ZIMBABWE INNINGS</b><br>232 all out off 42.1 overs </center><br> Campbell and Ebrahim open facing a daunting task made even harder by the loss of two overs due to Zimbabwe's slow over rate. Campbell shows his intent with an early drive high over mid off for 4. A steady start slowly drops Zimbabwe behind the 7-run an over rate required. Campbell cracks and lobs an easy catch to Bhardwaj at midoff (50-1). Friend brings a little more urgency to the attack, as Ebrahim struggles to find the gaps. 100 off 19.3 overs as Friend starts to cut loose. <br>88 off 17 overs gives the visitors a firm base on which to launch their reply ... but do they have the firepower to sustain an onslaught over the second half of the innings? 227 needed off 27 overs at 8.41. <br>Friend dances down to Harbhajan and is stumped for 31 (111-2). Then danger man Andy Flower is brilliantly caught by Khan over his shoulder in the same Singh over. <br>Ebrahim quickly follows a few balls later bowled behind his pads by Ganguly as the wheels come off Zimbabwe's reply - 3 wickets for 3 runs in 7 balls. Carlisle and G Flower face a Herculean task to even approach India's massive score (9 runs an over needed off the last 26) <br>Carlisle picks out Dravid on the midwicket boundary (143-5) - 10 an over needed with the top order gone. G Flower and Streak can only pick up 7 an over as the run rate races beyond 10. 200 up off 36 overs - fourth fifty in just 36 balls as Streak chances his arm more and more. A partnership of 78 off 58 balls gives Zimbabwe the most outside of chances bearing in mind Douglas Marillier is still to hit the crease. <br>Flower yorked by Khan for 48 (225-6) and Marillier follows him two balls later - also bowled by Khan. Taibu run out first ball (228-8). Brent caught off H Singh ... last wicket and 100 runs needed ... Streak last man out at 232.<br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b><br>50 for 1 Campbell ct Bhardwaj b Khan 31 (off 36 balls 4x4) <br>111 for 2 Friend st Ratra b H Singh 31 (off 34 balls 4x4) <br>113 for 3 A Flower ct Khan b H Singh 1 (off 2 balls) <br>114 for 4 Ebrahim b Ganguly 42 (off 69 balls 2x4) <br>143 for 5 Carlisle ct Dravid b Bhardwaj 17 (off 21 balls) <br>227 for 6 G Flower b Khan 48 (off 47 balls 6x4)<br>227 for 7 Marillier b Khan 0 (off 2 balls) <br>228 for 8 Taibu run out 0 (off 1 ball)<br>229 for 9 Brent ct Kaif b H Singh 0 (off 3 balls) <br>232 for 10 Streak ct Khan b H Singh 39 (off 36 balls 5x4) <br>Hondo not out 1 (off 4 balls)<br>Zimbabwe 232 all out off 42.1 overs<br><br><b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 25 for 0 <br>10 overs = 50 for 0 <br>15 overs = 76 for 1 <br>20 overs = 105 for 1<br>25 overs = 122 for 4<br>30 overs = 150 for 5<br>35 overs = 195 for 5 <br>40 overs = 227 for 7 <br><br><b>TEAMS</b><br> INDIA: Ganguly, Mongia, Dravid, Laxman, Kaif, Ratra, Agarkar, H Singh, Khan, Bhardwaj, Y Singh.<br>ZIMBABWE: Campbell, Carlisle, A Flower, Ebrahim, G Flower, Friend, Streak, Marillier, Taibu, Brent, Hondo. <br><br><b>OFFICIALS</b><br>Umpires: KG Lakshminarayanan & Samir Bhandekar <br>Third Umpire: BSB Rao <br>Match Referee: JRReid <br><br><b>PITCH REPORT</b><br>"It is pretty dry with some grass interspersed so it will not seam much.
