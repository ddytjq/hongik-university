            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This 
              is an evolving list of those projects and programs in astronomy 
              education to which anyone from around the U.S. can apply or from 
              which anyone can receive materials. It does not include the many 
              worthwhile projects that are designed to serve only one city, one 
              state, or one institution (although we recognize that such programs 
              may nevertheless serve as models for the rest of the country). We 
              very much welcome suggestions and additions for future versions 
              of this list. Please contact the first author at the above address 
              or e-mail: <a href="mailto:fraknoiandrew@fhda.edu">fraknoiandrew@fhda.edu</a>. 
              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Note:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
              Organizations that are involved with a number of projects are just 
              listed with their names; see the key at the end of the list for 
              their addresses and telephone numbers.</font></p>

            <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="1"></a>1.Workshops 
            and Training for Teachers of Astronomy (K-12)</b></font> 
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Association of Physics Teachers (AAPT): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has astronomy education sessions aimed at secondary and community 
                college teachers at meetings. Has a Physics Teacher Resource Agent 
                program to train physics teachers to help other teachers [see 
                <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Astronomical Society: A-ASTRA Program: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Intensive summer workshops for elementary and secondary teachers 
                to become astronomy teaching resource agents in their own communities; 
                ends July 1998. Resource agents are available to serve as community 
                resources; for names and addresses see: <a href="http://www.aas.org/%7Eaastra/aastra.html" target="_blank">www.aas.org/~aastra/aastra.html</a>. 
                Contact: Mary Kay Hemenway, Dept. of Astronomy, University of 
                Texas, Austin, TX 78712 (512-471-6503) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Astronomical Society (AAS): </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">"Astronomer 
                for a Day" or "From Black Holes to Blackboards" workshops for 
                teachers, in conjunction with the Society's meetings in different 
                cities [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Association 
                of Astronomy Educators (AAE):</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Puts on astronomy education sessions at meetings of the National 
                Science Teachers Association [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Arizona 
                State University, Thermal Emission Spectrometer Project (TES): 
                </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Workshops for teachers grades K-12 focused on current space missions. 
                Contact: ASU, Department of Geology, Mars K-12 Education Program, 
                PO Box 871404, Tempe, AZ 85287 (602-965-1788); <a href="http://esther.la.asu.edu/asu_tes/" target="_blank">http://esther.la.asu.edu/asu_tes/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                2-day "Universe in the Classroom", hands-on workshops for about 
                200 teachers in grades 3-12, each summer, in conjunction with 
                the Society's meetings [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project ASTRO: national project to form and train ongoing partnerships 
                between astronomers (professional & amateur) and local 4th-9th 
                grade school teachers for class visits [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Center 
                for Astrophysics (CfA): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project ARIES Summer Workshops for Elementary School Teachers 
                to help develop new curriculum [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Challenger 
                Center: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has a variety of teacher training programs , some in connection 
                with one its 29 regional Learning Centers, and some as part of 
                a traveling program called "Window on the Universe." [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Hands-on-Universe 
                Project: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                has teacher training workshops [see listing in <a href="naep03.html#5">section 
                5</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Hofstra 
                University: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Annual two-week summer astronomy workshop in Astronomy, Space 
                Science, and Aerospace for teachers of all levels. Curriculum 
                materials on aerospace education available to participants. Contact: 
                Jerome Notkin, New College, 130 Hofstra University, Hempstead, 
                NY 11549 (516-463-5849) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Image 
                Processing for Teaching</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                [see listing in <a href="naep03.html#5">section 5</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Jet 
                Propulsion Laboratories: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Ice and Fire Pre-Projects offers teacher workshops and curriculum 
                support materials focused on a series of missions to Pluto, Europa, 
                and the Sun. Contact: Jackie Giuliano, JPL, Mail Stop 301-250D, 
                4800 Oak Grove Drive, Pasadena, CA 91109 (818-354-6903) <a href="http://www.jpl.nasa.gov" target="_blank">http://www.jpl.nasa.gov</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Center Workshops:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Many of the NASA Centers around the country offer occasional workshops 
                and training for teachers. Contact the center nearest you for 
                the latest information. For a list of the Centers see: <a href="http://www.msfc.nasa.gov/education/docs/99.htm" target="_blank">http://www.msfc.nasa.gov/education/docs/99.htm</a> 
                or write: Elementary and Secondary Branch, Education Division, 
                Code FEE, NASA Headquarters, Washington, DC 20546, (202-358-1518) 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Space Grant Consortia: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                The consortia in many states have outreach programs to help K-12 
                teachers. Look for the program for your state at their web site 
                <a href="http://calspace.ucsd.edu/spacegrant/" target="_blank">http://calspace.ucsd.edu/spacegrant/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Optical Astronomy Observatories: </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">"The 
                Use of Astronomy in Research Based Science Education" Project 
                offers 180-hour summer workshops for middle and high-school teachers, 
                including image processing, observing, use of the Internet, etc. 
                Contact: Suzanne Jacoby, Education Office, NOAO, P.O. Box 26732, 
                Tucson, AZ 85726, (520-318-8230); <a href="http://www.noao.edu/outreach/" target="_blank">http://www.noao.edu/outreach/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Science Teachers' Association (NSTA): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                NEWMAST and NEWEST Workshops, offered at various NASA centers 
                around the country (astronomy is only one part of the workshops' 
                focus). NSTA also holds large national and regional conventions 
                for science teachers; most of them have astronomy lectures and 
                programs [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Project 
                ARTIST:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Workshops and curriculum development for teachers of astronomy 
                and planetary sciences grades 2-8. Contact: Larry Lebofsky, University 
                of Arizona, Lunar and Planetary Lab., Tucson, AZ 85721 (520-621-6947); 
                <a href="http://www.uarizona.edu/%7Elebofsky" target="_blank">http://www.uarizona.edu/~lebofsky</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
