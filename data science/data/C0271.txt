<br><center><h2><i>Calculating the approximate cost of rebuilding your Home</i></H2></center><hr>

<P>
<b>It is important that you calculate the <I>rebuilding cost</I>
of your home to know how much to insure the building for.</b>
<H4>NB</H4>

<UL>
<LI>Rebuilding cost are not the same as market value.
<LI>The calculation below is for broadly standard construction.
i.e. built mainly of brick, upto 3 storeys high and no special
features.
</UL>

<img align=right src="p1combo.gif" width=148 height=394>
<P>
To calculate the rebuilding cost, you need to know the building
dimensions:
<UL>
<LI>Length (n feet)
<LI>Width (in feet)
<LI>Number of storeys
</UL>

<H4>1. Calculate the ground floor area:</H4>

<P>
Ground floor area = Length x Width
<H4>2. Calculate the Total floor area:</H4>

<P>
Total floor area = Ground floor area x Number of storeys
<H4>3. Calculate the rebuilding cost:</H4>

<P>
Rebuilding cost = Total floor area x Rebuilding cost per sq. ft.
(see below)
<UL>
<LI>London/Channel Island (pre 1946) &#163;66 (post 1946) &#163;55
<LI>South East (pre 1946) &#163;60 (post 1946) &#163;50
<LI>East Anglia (pre 1946) &#163;57 (post 1946) &#163;48
<LI>Rest of UK (pre 1946) &#163;55 (post 1946) &#163;46
</UL>

<H4>For example:</H4>

<P>
Your House is 30 ft wide and 40 foot long and has 2 storeys. You
live in Scotland. Your house is 20 years old.
<OL>
<LI>Ground floor area: 30 x 40 = 1200 sq. ft
<LI>Total floor area: 1200 x 2 = 2400 sq. ft
<LI>Rebuilding cost: 2400 x 46 = &#163;110,400
</OL>
