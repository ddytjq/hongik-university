  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 4" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>Once
  more we were the sole entrants from the British Isles albeit qualifying as
  hosts.&nbsp; The team was playing good football and from the ten games prior the
  the finals, England had won 9 and drawn only 1, scoring 24 and conceding
  only 6 goals (including a 1-0 win over the Germans).&nbsp; Our group was to be
  played at White City and Wembley, England's games of course were to be played
  under the famous twin towers, the first being a dull 0-0 draw with a defence
  minded Uruguay team.&nbsp; The Mexicans were beaten 2-0 (Charlton, Hunt)
  before the final group game against France.&nbsp; Our opponents needed a clear four
  goal victory to progress, but two goals from Roger Hunt gave us another 2-0 win
  and set up the infamous quarter final with Argentina.&nbsp; From the first
  whistle, the South Americans kicked and fouled the English and the German
  referee booked player after player while Argentinean captain Rattin protested
  at every decision.&nbsp; Shortly before half time, the Referee had had enough
  and sent Rattin off.&nbsp; In the pandemonium that followed, it took a whole ten minutes and
  many FIFA officials to persuade him to leave the field.&nbsp; In the second half,
  things calmed down just enough for Hurst to score the only goal.&nbsp;
  The day after, the Germans booked their final place with a &quot;bad
  tempered&quot; 2-1 win over the nine men of Russia whilst in the other semi
  final, England faced
  Portugal.&nbsp; Bobby Charlton played one of his finest games for his country
  and scored both of our goals, as did Nobby Stiles who had marked Eusebio out
  of the game, letting him score only from the penalty spot as a late
  consolation.&nbsp; The World Cup Final on July the 30th 1966 will live in
  English footballing history for ever.&nbsp; Geoff Hurst scored the first and
  only Final hat-trick with Martin Peters scoring the other goal in a 4-2
  win.&nbsp; The Germans scored the opener after 13 minutes and equalised at 2-2
  in the dying seconds to force extra time, but the game will be remembered for
  two things:- Firstly:- our third goal which famously hit the bar before
  bouncing down on the line and away from the goal.&nbsp; Only one person was
  sure weather or not the ball had crossed the line, the Russian Linesman
  Bakhramov, and after a quick word, the referee gave the goal. Secondly:- Kenneth
  Wolstenhome's famous quote as the fourth goal went in &quot;there are some
  people on the pitch, they think its all over.....it is now.&quot;</b></font><font face="Albertus Medium">
