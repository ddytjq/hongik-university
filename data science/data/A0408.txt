 <tr><td><p class="PageTitle">Global communities
      <p class="bodytext">In the past, most of our community affairs 
    investment has been in the UK but we have begun increasing 
    our community involvement world-wide.
      <p class="bodytext">During 2001, we increased our charitable activity in Africa following the announcement by Barclays Africa in November 2000 that it would be allocating 1% of annual pre-tax profits to community support across the continent. The focus is on education, dealing with the many impacts of HIV/AIDS, and helping disabled people. As part of our commitment, we appointed a community manager with responsibility for our community activities in Africa.<p class="bodytext">One of the organisations we funded during the year was the University of Ghana Medical Students Association. The funds were used for an HIV/AIDS awareness campaign run by medical students and aimed at more than 2,000 school pupils across Ghana. The campaign was backed by film shows and the production of leaflets and posters.<p class="bodytext">Other projects we supported ranged from a maize-grinding mill at the Kikonkomene Leprosy Centre in Zambia to the provision of sports wheelchairs at Danhiko, a leading Zimbabwean sports centre for people with disabilities.
      <p class="bodytext">Many of our employees in Africa participated 
    in community projects. For example, 17 staff in Kenya trained 
    as volunteer counsellors for students through Junior Achievement 
    Kenya (JAK), a body which focuses on helping young people 
    develop their business skills and self-confidence. Part of 
    our commitment to JAK includes hosting an annual 'job shadow 
    day' when high-school students spend a day with a Barclays 
    employee. In addition, employees in Ghana, Kenya, Mauritius 
    and Tanzania organised four large-scale sponsored walks to 
    raise money for projects for orphaned children and the disabled. 
    In 2002, we are planning to start a new three-year pan-African 
    community partnership with Voluntary Service Overseas to support 
    its work on the HIV/AIDS crisis in Botswana, Ghana, Kenya, 
    Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia and Zimbabwe.</p> <br>
