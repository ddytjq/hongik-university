        <p> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Beaulieu Off 
          To Fast Start</b> </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">He ran out of laps 
          the week before and had to settle for second, but on Saturday night 
          at <b>Saratoga Speedway</b>, defending <b>Stock Car</b> points champion 
          <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> got to the front quickly, and to the race lead 
          by the 16th lap, on his way to his first main event win of the 2002 
          season.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Shooting for his 
          speedway best 6th points championship, Beaulieu settled for second behind 
          <b>Ray Anderosov</b> on April 20th after struggling to get through the 
          field. That wasn't a problem Saturday night as he and <b>David Mainse</b> 
          had their cars hooked up on the outside groove, which got both of them 
          to the front by the 16th lap. Once the two where at the front of the 
          field Mainse could only chase Beaulieu for the remaining 19 laps, hoping 
          for a slip-up by the veteran which didn't come, giving the Oyster River 
          racer the win and a 53 point advantage over Mainse in the season point 
          race.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With the top two 
          cars in a race of their own, the racing was fast and competitive behind 
          them as early race leader <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> attempted to hold 
          on to 3rd until he got stuck on the outside, allowing Port Alberni's 
          <b>Don Hallgarth</b> and Courtenay's <b>Mike Meers</b> to slip past 
          to finish 3rd and 4th. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The <b>Campbell 
          River Re Max</b> sponsored night of racing began with von Kampen winning 
          his 2nd straight Stock Car B dash, while the A dash was a big win for 
          Hallgarth who held off Beaulieu for the win, unlike the 15 lap heat 
          race where Beaulieu got around <b>Brad McLean</b> on the last lap to 
          snatch away the win. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The second point 
          race for the <b>Magic 98.9 Crash to Pass</b> cars turned out to be a 
          heavy hitting night of racing with a number of cars not even making 
          it through the heat race into the main event, which saw only four cars 
          running at the final checkered flag. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nanaimo drivers 
          dominated the crash to pass racing and as a result also sit on top of 
          the points standings with <b>Mark Haslam</b> 1 point ahead of Saturday 
          night's main event winner <b>Jimmy Joyce</b>. Short by two points to 
          Campbell River's <b>Mark Stephens</b> for the 2001 points championship, 
          Haslam has started his season off strong in a new car which survived 
          the 8 lap heat race to take the win, while Stephens, still running last 
          seasons very beat up car, had <b>Troy Sheridan</b> behind the wheel 
          who didn't make it through a big first lap crash in the heat race to 
          finish his night behind Little Richard's tow truck.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 8 car main event 
          had Joyce take out race leader <b>Brian "Beefy" Baker</b> on his way 
          to the win ahead of Campbell River's <b>David Crowhurst</b> and Baker.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The crash to pass 
          night kicked off with <b>Jeff May</b> winning his 2nd straight People's 
          Choice Award, followed by trophy dash wins for <b>Carl Carlsen</b> in 
          the B dash and Crowhurst in the A dash. Crowhurst, with his 2nd place 
          main event finish, sits only 18 points behind first place Haslam. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Four <b>Mini Figure 
          8</b> cars excited the crowd with three bump and spin races that featured 
          numerous near collisons and a few good bumps as the speedway's newest 
          class ran their first night of racing. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Saratoga Speedway 
          driving school 2002 graduate <b>Shawn Stoddard</b> captured a win in 
          his first ever race by capturing the trophy dash in his Toyota, followed 
          by wins for <b>Dustin Purden</b> in the heat race and <b>Matt May</b> 
          in the 15 lap main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Bomber cars 
          return this Saturday night for their second point race of the season 
          with Black Creeks <b>Mike Dionne</b> leading the points standings heading 
          into Saturday nights racing by 14 points over <b>BJ Bradley</b> and 
          <b>Darren Larsen</b>, the April 20th A main winner. Only 40 points seperate 
          4th place <b>Jason Waters</b>, <b>Cody Gorosh</b>, <b>Richard Olsen</b>, 
          <b>Big Rick Martin</b>, <b>Chris Beaulieu</b> and 9th plce <b>Tim Crouch</b>.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This Saturday night 
          (May 4th) joining the Bomber cars will be the Stock Cars and for the 
          first time in a number of years the Victoria based fast Island Mini 
          Stocks with all the action set for 7pm. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Guest talk.........Courtenay's 
          <b>Rob Leighton</b> had fast time for Stock Cars with a quick 16.512 
          lap just off Martin Beaulieu's track record lap of 16.448.........second 
          straight bad week for Port Alberni's <b>Rob Gaudrealt</b> as motor problems 
          didn't even allow him to time in.....tickets for the upcoming two day 
          Monster truck shows are on sale and going fast at the Sid Williams and 
          Tidemark Theatres.......Defending Two time Canadain IMCA points Champion 
          <b>Jason Beaulieu</b> opens his quest for a third championship this 
          Saturday night in Victoria.This season will see Beaulieu race a car 
          out of Port Hardy...Guest 1st driver of the week for 2002 is Port Alberni's 
          Hallgarth who drove strong to win the A dash, then came back from a 
          mishap in the heat race to post a impressive 3rd in the main. </font></p>
