                      <td valign="top" class="BodyText"> Actuarial work encompasses 
                        a wide range of financial and statistical areas, including 
                        Life Assurance, Pensions and Investments. A qualified 
                        actuary will have the skills and knowledge to work in 
                        any of these areas. Many actuaries have moved into areas 
                        outside the Financial Services industry, where their training 
                        and experience have been invaluable in helping them become 
                        successful managers. 
                        <p>Read further for information on Working at Guardian 
                          and <a href="#jg" class="UnvisitedLink">Joining Guardian</a>.</p>
                        <p class="BodyTextBold"><a name="wag"></a>Working at Guardian 
                        </p>
                        <p>Guardian offers a full training scheme to new graduates, 
                          combined with a competitive salary and attractive benefits 
                          package. </p>
                        <p><span class="BodyTextBold">1. Office Work<br>
                          </span><span class="BodyText">An actuarial trainee will 
                          work in several areas during their training, such as:</span></p>
                        <table width="500" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" align="center">
                          <tr> 
                            <td class="BodyText" width="100" valign="top">Valuation 
                            </td>
                            <td class="BodyText" width="20" valign="top">- </td>
                            <td width="380" class="BodyText">calculating the Company's 
                              liabilities and ensuring that the Company has enough 
                              assets to meet its financial obligations.</td>
                          </tr>
                          <tr> 
                            <td valign="top" class="BodyText">Pricing</td>
                            <td valign="top" class="BodyText">- </td>
                            <td class="BodyText">setting the premiums on our financial 
                              products to ensure that we remain competitive whilst 
                              still being profitable.</td>
                          </tr>
                          <tr> 
                            <td valign="top" class="BodyText">Projections</td>
                            <td valign="top" class="BodyText">- </td>
                            <td class="BodyText">looking at how the financial 
                              picture may change in the future so that we can 
                              ensure our business and investment policy is set 
                              appropriately.</td>
                          </tr>
                        </table>
                        <p> <span class="BodyText">In addition, actuarial trainees 
                          can expect to be involved in various projects, giving 
                          them the opportunity to use their skills to solve business 
                          problems. </span></p>
                        <p><span class="BodyText">By moving people into different 
                          areas regularly, we ensure that our newly qualified 
                          actuaries have lots of knowledge and experience of Life 
                          Assurance work.</span> </p>
                        <span class="BodyTextBold">2. Examinations </span><br>
                        <span class="BodyText">To qualify as an actuary, a long 
                        and difficult set of examinations must be completed. In 
                        order to pass the exams, a trainee must be prepared to 
                        put in many hours of studying. The exams test mathematical 
                        skills, the ability to apply theoretical concepts to practical 
                        problems and the ability to communicate effectively. </span> 
                        <p class="BodyText">In order to help our trainees complete 
                          the exams as quickly as possible, Guardian offer a comprehensive 
                          study package. Trainees are given a generous study time 
                          allowance away from the office. Each trainee has a supervisor 
                          who is available to help out with study or work problems. 
                          In addition, we pay for courses, tutorials and exam 
                          fees. </p>
                        <p><span class="BodyText">Examination success is rewarded 
                          with salary increases. In fact, the actuarial profession 
                          is one of the highest paid in Britain.</span> </p>

                        <p class="BodyTextBold"><a name="jg"></a>Joining Guardian 
                        </p>
                        <p class="BodyText">In order to become an actuarial trainee 
                          with Guardian Financial Services, you need to be highly 
                          numerate. We generally look for people with a Grade 
                          A in A-level Maths and at least an upper second university 
                          degree in a numeric subject. Although most of our trainees 
                          have a Mathematics/Statistics background, we will consider 
                          people with a Science, Economics or Business degree 
                          if the course had a reasonable Mathematical content. 
                        </p>
                        <p class="BodyText">In addition, we look for graduates 
                          who are enthusiastic and self-disciplined, two key qualities 
                          for getting through the exams and for producing first 
                          class work in the office.</p>
                        <p class="BodyText">If you have the academic background 
                          and the personal qualities we require, we will be delighted 
                          to consider you for an actuarial position at Guardian 
                          Financial Services. </p>
