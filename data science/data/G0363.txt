            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="11"></a>11. 
              Awards or Grants for Astronomy Education</b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Institute for Aeronautics and Astronautics: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has $200 Classroom Aid Grants for K-12 teachers, and Undergraduate 
                and Graduate Fellowships. Contact: AIAA, 1801 Alexander Bell Drive, 
                Suite 500, Reston, VA 20191 (1-800-NEW-AIAA); <a href="http://www.aiaa.org" target="_blank">http://www.aiaa.org</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                The Klumpke-Roberts Award is given each year for lifetime of contributions 
                to the public understanding of astronomy (offered since 1974). 
                Contact the Society for nomination guidelines; see addresses at 
                end. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                The Brennan Award is given each year to someone who has made outstanding 
                contributions to the high school teaching of astronomy (offered 
                since 1993). Write for guidelines. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Astrophysics Division (through the Space Telescope Science Institute): 
                </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                IDEA Grants Program funds small and medium size projects in astronomy 
                education by astronomers. Contact: IDEA Grants Program, Education 
                Division, STScI, 3700 San Martin Drive, Baltimore, MD 21218 [e-mail: 
                <a href="mailto:idea@stsci.edu">idea@stsci.edu</a>]; <a href="http://ideas.stsci.edu" target="_blank">http://ideas.stsci.edu</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Science Foundation: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Offers grants for projects in science education through various 
                education and training programs. Write for brochures about current 
                programs and grant guidelines to: NSF Directorate for Education 
                and Human Resources, 4201 Wilson Blvd., Arlington, VA 22230 (703-306-1600); 
                <a href="http://www.nsf.gov/" target="_blank">http://www.nsf.gov/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NSF 
                Informal Science Education Supplements to Research Awards: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                up to $50,000 is available to research grant recipients who want 
                to do broad dissemination of their results in an out of school 
                setting. Contact the Informal Science Education Division [see 
                <a href="#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Slipher 
                Fund of the National Academy of Sciences: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                supports small grants for innovative astronomy education projects 
                around the country. Contact: Dennis Schatz, Pacific Science Center, 
                200 Second Avenue N, Seattle, WA 98109 (206-443-2886) [e-mail: 
                <a href="mailto:schatz@pacsci.org">schatz@pacsci.org</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Tufts 
                University: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Wright Fellowship Program for secondary school science teachers 
                to develop and disseminate curriculum materials while in residence 
                at Tufts. The yearlong fellowship offers a $35,000 salary, plus 
                benefits and a relocation stipend of $2,000. Their Teacher Scholarship 
                Program is a one-week interdisciplinary fellow for middle and 
                highschool teachers and a teacher of another subject to develop 
                a unit or lesson which reflect both subject areas. Scholarship 
                includes $750.00, substitute pay, plus use of all Tufts facilities 
                (room and board not included). Contact: Educational Coordinator, 
                Wright Center for Science Education, Tufts University, 4 Colby 
                Street, Medford, MA 02155 (617-628-5000) <a href="http://www.tufts.edu/as/wright_center/index.html" target="_blank">http://www.tufts.edu/as/wright_center/index.html</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table

            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="12">12. 
              Miscellaneous Projects</a></b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Universe Expo, a weekend "festival" of astronomy talks, seminars, 
                and exhibits for the public, held in conjunction with the Society's 
                annual meeting. [see <a href="#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Boston 
                Museum of Science: </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>Touch 
                the Stars</i> is a tactile astronomy book in Braille for the visually 
                impaired. Contact Noreen Grice, Museum of Science, Science Park, 
                Boston, MA 02114 (617-589-0439) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Coalition 
                for Earth Science Education: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                An umbrella group of organizations interested in encouraging the 
                teaching of earth science, which includes astronomy. Contact: 
                Frank Ireton, AGU, 2000 Florida Ave., NW Washington, DC 20009 
                (202-462-6900); <a href="http://www.agu.org" target="_blank">http://www.agu.org</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Committee 
                for the Scientific Investigation of Claims of the Paranormal (CSICOP): 
                </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Group of scientists, educators, magicians, & skeptics that informs 
                teachers and the public about the scientific perspective on such 
                pseudosciences as astrology, psychic power, or the face on Mars. 
                They publish the excellent <i>Skeptical Inquirer</i> magazine. 
                Contact at: Box 703, Buffalo, NY 14226 (716-636-1425); <a href="http://www.csicop.org" target="_blank">http://www.csicop.org</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Earth 
                and Sky Radio Series: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                a 90 second daily radio series on astronomy and earth science. 
                Highlights tape available. A newsletter for this project, called 
                <i>Earth in the Classroom,</i> is distributed with <i>The Universe 
                in the Classroom</i> (see Section 8). Contact: P.O. Box 2203, 
                Austin, TX 78768 (512-480-8773); <a href="http://www.earthsky.com" target="_blank">http://www.earthsky.com</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>International 
                Dark-Sky Association: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Non-profit organization devoted to educating the public about 
                the danger and waste of light pollution. Good information packets 
                available. Contact: IDA, 3545 N. Stewart, Tucson, AZ 85716; <a href="http://www.darksky.org" target="_blank">http://www.darksky.org</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Mt. 
                Wilson Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Telescopes in Education Project allows classes to do remote observing 
                with a 24-inch telescope using a computer and modem in their school. 
                While the remote observing costs money for amateurs or colleges, 
                NASA is funding the program free for schools. Contact: TIE, Box 
                24, Mt. Wilson, CA 91023 (818-793-3100); <a href="http://tie.jpl.nasa.gov/tie/index.html" target="_blank">http://tie.jpl.nasa.gov/tie/index.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Undergraduate Research Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                A 31-inch telescope near Flagstaff, AZ and a consortium of colleges 
                to operate it. Contact: Kathy Eastwood, Physics & Astronomy, N. 
                Arizona Univ., P.O. Box 6010, Flagstaff, AZ 86011 (602-523-2661); 
                <a href="http://nuro.phy.nau.edu/" target="_blank">http://nuro.phy.nau.edu/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Space 
                Science Institute: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Provides traveling exhibitions for science museums related to 
                current NASA missions and space science. Currently offers Electric 
                Space and is developing a Mars Quest exhibition. [see <a href="#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Jack 
                Horkheimer: Star Gazer (formerly Star Hustler) </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                A five minute, weekly TV series on naked-eye viewing produced 
                in cooperation with Miami Museum of Science and Space Transit. 
                Teachers can obtain episodes through NASA CORE [see <a href="naep03.html#4">section 
                #4</a> for NASA CORE contact information]. Contact: J.E. Harper, 
                Miami Space Transit Planetarium, 3280 South Miami Avenue, Miami, 
                FL 33129 (305-854-4244); <a href="http://www.jackstargazer.com/" target="_blank">http://www.jackstargazer.com/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>University 
                of Arizona Astronomy Camps: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Summer programs for youngsters, adults, and teachers. Contact 
                Don McCarthy, Steward Obs., U. of Arizona, Tucson, AZ 85721 (520-621-4079); 
                <a href="http://ethel.as.arizona.edu/astro_camp/" target="_blank">http://ethel.as.arizona.edu/astro_camp/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>University 
                of Texas McDonald Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>StarDate,</i> 
                a 2-minute daily radio program, broadcast on many stations, available 
                on CD's or tape. A new Spanish-language version called Universo 
                debuted in April 1995. Contact at RLM 15.308, U. of Texas, Austin, 
                TX 78712 (512-471-5285); <a href="http://stardate.utexas.edu" target="_blank">http://stardate.utexas.edu</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Wright 
                Center for Science Education: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Offers a wide spectrum of resources including summer workshops, 
                seminars and symposia targeting women and underrepresented minorities, 
                and a variety of science education resources. Contact: Wright 
                Center for Science Education, Science and Technology Center, 4 
                Colby Street, Tufts University, Madford, MA 02155 (617-628-5000 
                x5394); <a href="http://www.tufts.edu/as/wright_center/index.html" target="_blank">http://www.tufts.edu/as/wright_center/index.html</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
