<td bgcolor="#FFFFFF" width="100%" align="left" valign="top"><h1><b><span style="font-size: 16pt">Chemical Sciences Roundtable</span></b></h1><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black"><i>Sponsors</i>:<b> </b></span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.darpa.mil/" target="_top">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.doe.gov/" target="_top">U.S. Department of Energy</a></span><u><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span></u><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.nsf.gov/" target="_top">National Science Foundation</a></span><u><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span></u><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.rescorp.org/" target="_top">Research Corporation</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.dreyfus.org/foundation.shtml" target="_top">The Camille &amp; Henry Dreyfus Foundation, Inc</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.hhs.gov/" target="_top">US Department of Health &amp; Human Services</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, </span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.nist.gov/" target="_top">National Institute of Standards and Technology</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">, and the </span><b><span style="font-size: 10pt"><a href="http://www.epa.gov/" target="_top">U.S. Environmental Protection Agency</a></span></b><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">.</span></p><h3><b><span style="font-size: 13pt">Project scope and statement of task:</span></b></h3><p><span style="font-size: 10pt; color: Black">The <i>Chemical Sciences Roundtable</i> (CSR) provides a science-oriented, apolitical forum to enhance understanding of issues in chemical sciences and technologies affecting governmental, industrial, academic, and non-profit sectors.  It was established in 1996 by the National Research Council and its Board on Chemical Sciences and Technology.  The CSR held its first meeting in February 1997, and it meets two or three times annually.  Alexis T. Bell, University of California, Berkeley is the Chair of the CSR, and Mary L. Mandich, AT&amp;T Bell Laboratory, is Vice Chair.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt">Established under the Board on Chemical Sciences and Technology, the Chemical Science Roundtable brings together in a neutral and apolitical environment the leaders in chemical science and technology.  The Roundtable provides a science-oriented, apolitical forum for discussing chemically related issues affecting government, industry, and universities. It does not serve as a source of advice for federal officials but provides an educational vehicle for exchange of information that could lead to follow-up action by private-sector participants and their parent organizations.  The Roundtable organizes workshops, for which proceedings may be published, but it does not make recommendations, nor does it offer specific advice.  This neutral and credible forum provides great potential for strengthening the chemical sciences by fostering communication among all stakeholders in the chemical enterprise (i.e., the persons and organizations spanning industry, government, universities, and professional organizations that are engaged in chemically related activities).</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt">The Chemical Sciences Roundtable identifies problems and issues in the chemical enterprise that require national attention.  Among topics that might be the subject of dialog and discussions are changes that are taking place in each sector of the chemical enterprise; the changing environment for research; new models for graduate education and training; changing employment patterns; potential consequences for the chemical enterprise caused by federal restructuring; areas in the chemical sciences where increased focus could lead to major breakthroughs; optimization of resource allocation in chemical R&amp;D; institutional constraints that inhibit scientific and technological advances; and new ways for the chemical sciences enterprise to facilitate international opportunities.</span></p><p><span style="font-size: 10pt">The Roundtable itself will not be the organization to resolve these issues and problems, but will instead facilitate interactions that can assist the participants&#8217; organizations in acting independently or collaboratively.  This approach enables each organization to take maximum advantage of its strengths; it allows groups to build on the results of others; and it minimizes competing, redundant, and conflicting efforts. </span></p><h3><b><span style="font-size: 13pt">Upcoming Workshops:</span></b></h3>
