                  <p class="introtitle">Welcome to Stakeholder Pensions for Self-employed 
                    people </p>
                  <p class="body"><span class="bodytitle">What pension options 
                    do I have?</span> <br>
                    You have several options available to you if you want to start 
                    a pension for yourself. These include:</p>

          <td colspan="3" height="343"> 
            <p><img src="../assets/dummy(1).gif" width="130" height="3239" alt="blank image" border="0" align="left"><span class="body"><span class="bodybold">Personal 
              pension plan</span> <br>
              A <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#personal-pension-plan')">personal 
              pension plan</a></span> lets you set aside money on either a regular 
              or one-off basis. All contributions you make are paid net of basic 
              rate <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#income-tax')">income 
              tax</a></span>. If you are a higher rate taxpayer you can claim 
              additional tax relief from your local tax office. The money is invested 
              in the pension fund and grows in a tax efficient environment. On 
              reaching retirement, you can normally take part of the fund (up 
              to 25%) as a tax-free lump sum. The remainder is then used to provide 
              an income, which will be subject to <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#income-tax')">income 
              tax</a></span>. You can usually have access to your pension fund 
              from the age of 50. You must take the benefits by your 75th birthday. 
              </span></p>
            <p class="body">In order to take out a personal pension plan you must: 
            </p>
            <table width="310" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
              <tr> 
                <td height="37"> 
                  <ul>
                    <li> <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#uk-resident')">Be resident and be ordinarily 
                      resident in the UK </a></span></li>
                    <li><span class="body"> Have a National Insurance number </span></li>
                  </ul>
                </td>
              </tr>
            </table>
            <span class="body">You can pay in up to £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
            per year, regardless of your earnings. It may be possible to pay more. 
            This depends on your age and earnings, as indicated in the table below. 
            If you do want to pay more than £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
            per year we will require proof of your earnings. This can be in the 
            form of a letter from your accountant, a copy of your accounts or 
            a copy of your tax self-assessment returns.</span> <br>
            <br>
            <table width="310" border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" height="119">
              <tr bgcolor="#CCCCCC"> 
                <td colspan="2" class="bodybold">Inland Revenue limits on contributions 
                  into a personal pension per tax year</td>
              </tr>

                    <br>

                    <br>

                  up to £91,800** <br>

                  up to £91,800** <br>

                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  40% of net relevant earnings <br>
                  up to £91,800**</td>
              </tr>
            </table>
            <p class="body"> *Age at beginning of the tax year. <br>
              **The earnings cap for 2000/2001 tax year. This may change in the 
              future. <br>
              The tax year begins 6 April and ends 5 April. </p>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodybold">Self-invested personal pension 
              (SIPPS) </span> <br>
              This is similar to a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#personal-pension-plan')">personal 
              pension plan</a></span>. The main differences are that the associated 
              charges with a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#sipp')">SIPP</a></span> 
              may be higher. The minimum investment in a <span class="bodybold">SIPP</span> 
              is usually higher and a <span class="bodybold">SIPP</span> offers 
              greater investment freedom. For example, it is possible to buy individual 
              stocks and shares. </p>
            <p class="body">If you would like to find out more information about 
              <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#personal-pension-plan')">personal 
              pension plans</a></span> and <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#sipp')">self- 
              invested personal pensions</a></span>, please contact an HSBC Bank 
              branch and speak to the <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#financial-planning-manager')">Financial 
              Planning Manager</a></span> who will be able to help you. <a href="../../lad/country.asp">Click 
              here</a> to find your nearest branch. <span class="bodyItalic">Please 
              note these products may incur higher charges than a Stakeholder 
              Pension.</span> </p>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodybold">Stakeholder Pension</span> 
              <br>
              This is similar to a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#personal-pension-plan')">personal 
              pension plan</a></span> except it has to meet certain strict government 
              standards, which are intended to benefit you. These are: </p>
            <table width="310" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
              <tr> 
                <td height="554"> 
                  <ul>
                    <li><span class="body"> The only charge you will pay is an <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#annual-management-charge')">annual 
                      management charge</a></span>.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> The maximum <span class="bodybold">annual 
                      management charge</span> will be 1% of the pension fund 
                      each year.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> Contributions from as little as £20 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                      can be made, whether this is a regular or single premium. 
                    </span></li>
                    <li><span class="body"> You will be able to stop and start contributions 
                      whenever it suits you without charge.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> You will be able to transfer your pension 
                      fund without charge.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> Employees who are members of <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#occupational-pension-scheme')">occupational 
                      pension schemes</a></span> can contribute up to £3,600 <span class="bodybold">gross</span> 
                      per year into a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension')">Stakeholder Pension</a></span> 
                      provided they earn less than £30,000 per year. <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#controlling-director')">Controlling 
                      directors</a></span> and individuals earning more than £30,000 
                      per year cannot.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> Contributions to a Stakeholder Pension can 
                      be made by <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#variable-direct-debit')">variable Direct Debit</a></span>. 
                    </span></li>
                    <li><span class="body"> You can pay into a Stakeholder Pension 
                      for your children and spouse. There is no minimum age or 
                      earnings level for someone to start a Stakeholder Pension. 
                      In effect you could pay up to £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                      each year into a Stakeholder Pension for someone until their 
                      75th birthday. If they are a member of an <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#occupational-pension-scheme')">occupational 
                      pension scheme</a></span> earning more than £30,000 per 
                      year or are a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#controlling-director')">controlling 
                      director</a></span> they will not be eligible for a Stakeholder 
                      Pension. If you wish to apply for a Stakeholder Pension 
                      for someone over the age of 18, you can do this via our 
                      website. However, you will need to complete a paper Direct 
                      Debit, which we will send to you. If you wish to set up 
                      a Stakeholder Pension for someone below the age of 18 you 
                      will need to contact one of our <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#financial-planning-manager')">Financial 
                      Planning Managers</a></span> at an HSBC Bank branch. <a href="../../lad/country.asp">Click 
                      here</a> to go to our branch directory.</span> </li>
                  </ul>
                </td>
              </tr>
            </table>
            <span class="body">If you are interested in Stakeholder Pensions,

                    <li><span class="body"> You can create an estimate of potential

                      calculator</a>.</span> </li>

            <span class="body">&nbsp;If you have already used the Decision 
              Trees, and are familiar with what a Stakeholder Pension could offer

            <p><span class="bodytitle">Tell me more about Stakeholder&nbsp;Pensions</span><br>
              <br>
              <span class="bodybold">Reasons for introduction</span> <br>
              <span class="body">The Government announced its intention to reform 
              pensions from April 2001. A full copy of the Government's document 
              (published on 25 May 2000) can be viewed by visiting the Department 
              of Social Security website, <a href="javascript:leavingHSBC('../leaving-hsbc.htm','http://www.dss.gov.uk')">www.dss.gov.uk</a>. 
              This document outlines the key proposals to change the face of pensions.</span> 
            </p>
            <p class="body">A new simple, flexible and low cost pension will be 
              introduced known as a Stakeholder Pension. The low charges and product 
              simplicity should make retirement planning more attractive and easier 
              for many people. Individuals will normally be able to take the pension 
              benefits built up from a Stakeholder Pension in addition to the 
              <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#state-pension')">State pension</a></span> and other pension benefits. 
            </p>
            <p class="bodybold">Regulation<br>
            </p>
            <table width="310" border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
              <tr> 
                <td height="140"> 
                  <ul>
                    <li><span class="body"> The <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">Occupational 
                      Pensions Regulatory Authority (OPRA)</a></span> will regulate 
                      the operation of all schemes and maintain a register of 
                      them. Employers responsible for offering a Stakeholder Pension 
                      will also need to ensure the Stakeholder Pension they have 
                      designated for their employees is continually registered 
                      with <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">OPRA</a></span>.</span> </li>
                    <li><span class="body"> The <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#fsa')">Financial Services 
                      Authority (FSA)</a></span> will regulate the marketing of schemes. 
                    </span></li>
                  </ul>
                </td>
              </tr>
            </table>
            <span class="body"><span class="bodybold">What if I have other pension 
            benefits?</span> <br>
            You may already have one or more existing pensions. These may be from 
            previous employers or pensions you have set up yourself. If you are 
            still paying into one or more of these pensions, this will have an 
            effect on the amount you can pay into a Stakeholder Pension. The maximum 
            amount you can pay, called the contribution 
            limit includes all pensions, not just contributions to one 
            particular pension. You will need to ensure that the total of all 
            pension contributions does not exceed the contribution 
            limits. You may want to seek advice from a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#financial-adviser')">Financial 
            Adviser</a></span> on what options you have.</span> <br>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodybold">Transferring Stakeholder Pension 
              benefits</span> <br>
              One of the benefits of a Stakeholder Pension is that you will be 
              able to move funds from one Stakeholder Pension to another, without 
              additional charge. This gives you complete flexibility to move your 
              existing Stakeholder Pension funds when it suits you. It is possible 
              to move your existing pension arrangements to a new Stakeholder 
              Pension with us. <span class="bodyItalic">Please note that there 
              may be a charge for advice relating to transfers</span>.</p>
            <p class="body">If you are considering moving your existing pension 
              arrangements to a Stakeholder Pension with us you will need to contact 
              the <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#financial-planning-manager')">Financial Planning Manager</a></span> at 
              an HSBC Bank branch. <a href="../../lad/country.asp">Click here</a> 
              to find your nearest branch of HSBC.</p>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodytitle">What if I have employees?</span> 
              <br>
              <a href="../employer/default.htm">Click here</a> to go to our employer's 
              site. </p>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodytitle">Apply for our Stakeholder&nbsp;Pension</span> 
              <br>
              You can apply for a Stakeholder Pension with HSBC over the internet. 
              However, we would recommend that you make use of the <a href="../decision-tree.htm"> 
              decision trees</a> in advance of making a decision.</p>
            <p class="bodybold">We recommend that you read our important notes.</p>

              on its own life assurance, pensions and investment funds.</i></span>
