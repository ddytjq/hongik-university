    Note that IBM used to be one of those legally paranoid companies, and they probably still have pretty strict restrictions on what their employees can and cannot do with GPL and LGPL software. That hasn't prevented them from contributing to Apache.<br/><br/>GPL and LGPL can be useful tools, but not because they increase the likelihood of contributions; rather, they ensure continued availability of a project. For encouraging contributions, you don't need to hit people over the head with a hammer to get them to cooperate and contribute. Quite to the contrary. Many people who don't want to contribute won't do so, whether you have an SCSL, a GPL, LGPL, or an Apache-style license. But less restrictive licenses increase the pool of people that can contribute. <br/><br/>I think Sun would do a lot better in terms of getting the Java platform to mature if they dropped the whole JCP and SCSL nonsense and went either with a GPL or Apache-style license for Java; they could still retain full rights to their trademark to ensure compliance with the Java platform for Java-branded releases.<BR>
