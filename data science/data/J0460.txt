<h1>Michael Waltrip stuns Select field with rally</h1>

CONCORD, N.C. -- Michael Waltrip pulled off the biggest
upset in the history of stock car racing's all-star event Saturday
night, charging from the final starting spot to win The Winston
Select.
<p>
In the three-part event, Waltrip, a non-winner in 11-plus seasons on the Winston Cup
circuit, waited until the very last
10-lap segment to show the power of his Ford Thunderbird,
finding his opening after one lap and running away with the
event and the $200,000 top prize. 
<p>
&quot;I was running scared,&quot; Waltrip said. &quot;After I got out front,
I said, 'Just don't pay any attention to anything in the whole
world. Just do your job.' &quot;
<p>
Waltrip, 33, the younger brother of three-time Winston Cup champion
Darrell Waltrip, took the lead when Dale Earnhardt and Terry Labonte were
racing side-by-side throughout the first lap, and Earnhardt's
car tapped Labonte, slowing the pair of Chevrolets.  Waltrip
went low on the track and roared into the lead, never looking
back. 
<p>
&quot;I knew something like that would happen with those guys side
by side,&quot; Waltrip said. &quot;When those guys got to bumping each
other, I just nailed it and went under them.&quot;
<p>
Michael Waltrip, in his first season driving Ford Thunderbirds
for the famed Wood Brothers Racing team, was not one of the 15
drivers who automatically qualified for the Select, which is
reserved mainly for Winston Cup winners. He became the 20th and
final starter for Saturday night's event by finishing fifth in The
Winston Select Open, a 50-lap qualifying race from which the top
five finishers made the field for the main event.
<p>
&quot;Darrell was in victory lane. He came and hugged me and said,
'I love you, man,' &quot; the younger Waltrip said, doing his best
imitation of a popular beer commercial.
<p>
&quot;This is big. This might be real big,&quot; said Darrell Waltrip,
who won the inaugural Select in 1985. &quot;He really deserves it, he
beat the best in a shootout.
<p>
&quot;I'm thrilled for my brother. This proves it ain't just one brother in
this family can drive.&quot; 
<p>
Waltrip, who became the first Winston Select Open transfer to
win the big show, was pulling away from the field at the conclusion
of the 12th annual event. His official margin of victory over Rusty
Wallace was 1.05 seconds.
<p>
&quot;This means a whole lot to our team because we haven't won in a
while,&quot; said co-owner Glen Wood, whose team has 96 Winston Cup
victories but none since March 1993 in Atlanta. &quot;After you've been
around as long as we have, the only thing that excites you is a
win, and we are definitely excited. This just proves that Michael
can do it.&quot;
<p>
Rusty Wallace followed Waltrip past Earnhardt and Labonte, and
followed him around the nine remaining laps for second.
Earnhardt was third, with Mark Martin fourth and Labonte fifth. 
<p>
&quot;I tell you what, Michael had me beat, no doubt about that,&quot;
Wallace said. &quot;I got a second and I pulled away from the
third-place car. The only thing I can say is he was awfully strong.
The thing was really pulling away. He had us beat and he deserves
it.&quot;
<p>
Dale Jarrett, this year's Daytona 500 winner, led 19 laps to win
the first 30-lap segment. Jarrett's car was a rocket the first
race, pulling away and winning by a tremendous margin over Bobby
Labonte and Ward Burton. 
<p>
The field was inverted for the second 30-lap segment, with Harry
Gant substituting for the injured Bill Elliott starting on the
pole. Gant's return from a two-year retirement was obvious as he
quickly slid to the back of the field with Labonte and
Earnhardt battling at the head of the field. Earnhardt
ultimately came out on top in the second segment, with Labonte
finishing second and Wallace third. 
<p>
First prize in each of the 30-lap segments paid $50,000, with
$15,000 for second and $7,500 for third. 
<p>
Jimmy Spencer took the 50-lap Winston Select Open, outdueling
Lake Speed for top honors and a $28,000 first prize.  Hut
Stricklin, Jeff Burton and Michael Waltrip were the top five
finishers, and moved into the Winston Select. 
<p>
The Burton brothers were the only unfortunate footnotes on the
night. Jeff Burton blew his engine on the start of the first
30-lap segment, taking his car straight to the garage area
without starting a lap.  Ward Burton crashed in Turn 4 on the
eighth lap of the second 30-lap segment, ending his day. 
<p>
Automatically eligible drivers include winning drivers and
owners of races since last year's Winston Select, active former
Winston Cup champions, and winners of the Winston Select within
the last five years. 
<p>
This event has no impact on the Winston Cup standings. 
<p>
The next Winston Cup event is the Coca-Cola 600 at Charlotte
Motor Speedway on May 26.  It will be the 11th of 31 races in
the NASCAR Winston Cup series.
<p>
