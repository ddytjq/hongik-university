                                    <br>
                                    The PPB pointed out that although some
                                    changes had been made to the provisions of
                                    the Policyholders Protection Act (PPA) by
                                    legislation in 1995, the 1975 Act still
                                    governs the position following Independent's
                                    liquidation.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>A new Financial Services Compensation
                                    Scheme (FSCS) is currently being drafted
                                    with the help of the insurance industry.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>It is likely that some of its
                                    provisions will differ from those of the PPA.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:
&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">PPB's overview<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></b><span style="font-size:11.0pt;
mso-bidi-font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Independent
                                    writes only general, i.e. non-life,
                                    business.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>The PPA's definition of &quot;long
                                    term&quot; business refers essentially to
                                    life assurance and differs from the
                                    &quot;long term&quot; - typically three or
                                    five year - policies written by Independent.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    The Act protects individuals, partnerships
                                    or other unincorporated partnerships of
                                    individuals and permits the PPB to pay 90%
                                    of claims in respect of non-compulsory
                                    classes of insurance and 100% of claims for
                                    compulsory classes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>For corporate bodies, no payments are
                                    made other than in respect of compulsory
                                    classes, where the Act allows 100% payments.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    </span>The PPB pays only indemnity claims,
                                    i.e. first or third party liabilities.<br>
                                    <br>

                                    short-term temporary website to publish
                                    announcements but will maintain contacts
                                    with the Provisional Liquidators and BIBA on
