<H2 ALIGN="CENTER"> Castlewood Systems Orb Drive vs Iomega
Jaz 2GB Drive Comparison </H2>
<HR>
<P><B>New:&nbsp; Castlewood is now shipping a 5.7GB
  drive!&nbsp; Approximately $300 (US$) for the drive; about
  $46 (US$) per 5.7 GB disk in quantities of 5 disks;
  backwards-compatible with 2.2GB disks; and the drive has
  similar specifications (similar read/write/transfer rates;
  a bit slower start/stop times).&nbsp; See the updated
  information in the comparison table below.</B> 
</P><HR>
<P> The Orb Drive stores 2.2GB of data, is about &frac34;
  as fast as a true hard drive, and costs less than most tape
  drives. Compared to an Iomega Jaz 2GB Drive, it is bigger,
  faster, and much, much cheaper.&nbsp; Here is a comparison
  of the two removable medias. 
</P> 
<P> Items marked &quot;(*)&quot; are based on PC Connection
  catalog received in early March 1999; items marked
  &quot;(**)&quot; are based on the manufacturer's web site
  (internal EIDE version for Orb Drive, unidentified version
  for Jaz Drive); items marked &quot;(***)&quot; are based
  upon actual observation of the internal EIDE Orb Drive on
  the author's Pentium Pro 200MHz, Windows 98 system, or
  purchasing experience.&nbsp; Items marked
  &quot;(****)&quot; are based upon pricing from PC
  Connection catalog/online purchase in July 2001. 
</P> <HR>
