Abstract
<p>
We present a two-dimensional UBV photometric study of three
early-type galaxies in the neighborhood of M87:
NGC 4476, NGC 4478 and NGC 4486B. It is based on
high-resolution (FWHM=0.7"-1.0") photographic material
obtained at the CFH telescope and completed by
photoelectric and spectroscopic data.
<p>
The luminosity profile of NGC 4476 follows an r1/4 law.
This galaxy should be considered as another dusty elliptical.
At variance with previous results, the profile of NGC 4486B
does not appear severely truncated, and may resonably follow either
an r1/4 law or a King (1966) model. The profile of NGC 4478 appears
truncated. It would be a new object of the compact elliptical class.
<p>
The three galaxies show no trend for isophotal elongation
outwards, but instead a trend for circularity.
We suggest that this is another characteristic of low-mass interacting 
ellipticals. There is no color gradient in these galaxies within the
uncertainty of our data.
