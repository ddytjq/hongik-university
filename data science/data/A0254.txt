<a name="firstthingsfirst-5"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Do I have options on how I can repay?</span><BR>
<span class="bodytext">You can pay interest only – which means that you will have to run a suitable investment plan alongside your mortgage (such as an endowment policy) which will then clear the capital sum borrowed at the end of the mortgage term. 
<P>
You can also pay capital and interest where each monthly payment covers both the interest due and part of the capital, reducing your outstanding loan each month. 
<P>
You can also split your mortgage so that a portion is paid interest only while the remainder is capital and interest.</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">What cover and insurance do I need?</span><BR>
<span class="bodytext">You must take out buildings insurance before we release your mortgage funds and it must remain in place throughout the term of your mortgage. You can choose the insurance company and you must show a copy of the policy document to your solicitors. Standard Life Bank can arrange your buildings insurance (and optional home contents cover). Just call <b>0845 600 1601</b>, click here for <a href="/page.php?id_link=154">Home Insurance</a> information.

We’d also strongly recommend Mortgage Payment Protection Insurance for cover against not being able to pay your mortgage due to an accident, sickness or unemployment. Again we can arrange this for you – call <b>0845 601 2357</b>, click here for <a href="/page.php?id_link=155">Mortgage Payment Protection Insurance</a>  information.
<P>
Similarly, we recommend that you have adequate life assurance cover linked to your mortgage. The <A HREF="http://www.standardlife.co.uk" target="blank">Standard Life Assurance Company</A> can organise this cover for you if you wish – call <b>0800 333 377</b>. Any advice given, or recommendation made, will relate only to the products of the Standard Life Group. Alternatively, speak to your financial adviser.</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Running your mortgage</span>
<P>
<a name="runningyourmortgage-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How does Capital Reward work?</span><BR>
<span class="bodytext">We will pay you a Capital Reward bonus of 1% every two years which will be applied to help reduce your outstanding capital balance. This will be paid directly into your Prepayment Reserve. Because the bonus reduces the capital you have borrowed, your interest payments go down too.
<P>
The bonus is calculated on the outstanding capital balance on the relevant anniversary of mortgage completion.
</span>

<a name="runningyourmortgage-3"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">How long can I take to repay?</span><BR>
<span class="bodytext">It’s up to you to decide how long you want to take to repay your mortgage. The minimum period is three years and the mortgage must be repaid by the time you are 65. The less time you take, the sooner you pay off your mortgage and own your home. You can even have different portions of the mortgage over different periods if you prefer.</span>

<span class="bodytextbold">Saving money</span>
<P>
<a name="savingmoney-1"></a>
<span class="bodytextbold">Can I make overpayments on my Capital Reward Mortgage?</span><BR>
<span class="bodytext">Yes. You can make overpayments at any time either regularly or as a lump sum. The effect of overpayments also applies immediately because we calculate interest daily. This can reduce the term of your mortgage and save you money in interest. When the time comes to receive your bonus, you might decide to continue paying the same monthly amount you’ve always paid. In effect, this means you’re making overpayments on your mortgage – but at no additional cost to you.</span>
