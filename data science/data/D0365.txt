      <h1><strong><font color="#800000"">Managerisms</font></strong></h1></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td style="font-size:125%" bordercolordark="#000080" width="34%">
      <h3>What the manager says...</h3></td>
		<td style="font-size:125%" bordercolordark="#000080" width="66%">
      <h3>What the manager means...</h3></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td width="34%"><font size="2">We have some challenges here.</font></td>
		<td width="66%"><font size="2">There are some problems here but if I call
      them "challenges" maybe I'll subliminally "inspire" you to work harder to
      fix them.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="23" width="34%"><font size="2">We're on a compressed schedule.</font></td>
		<td height="23" width="66%"><font size="2">We don't bother to gather requirements
      let alone do a design. After all, we're not even sure what we're supposed to be building. We just hack things together quickly to show the
      VCs.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="38" width="34%"><font size="2">We successfully met our deadline,
      now we're in "bug fixing" mode.</font></td>
		<td height="38" width="66%"><font size="2">Our "compressed schedule" has ended
      and we didn't make our deliverables. Last night I transformed our "<i>Feature
      List</i>" into a <i>"Bug List</i>". We're still working on the same things
      as a month ago only now that our deadline has expired we're calling them "bugs".</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="2" width="34%"><font size="2">I'll be investigating potential
      strategic advantages.</font></td>
		<td height="2" width="66%"><font size="2">I'll surf the web looking for an
      embeddable Java database or an HTML editor or something...Hey, what's this?
      &quot;Grandma's Favorite Old-Time Cookie Recipes&quot;. Hmmmm....that sounds
      neat.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="50" width="34%"><font size="2">We're working in "Internet time".</font></td>
		<td height="50" width="66%"><font size="2">This is one of my favorites. Manager's
      love this expression because it sounds chic and makes them feel like they're leading the charge in some "Internet Revolution". Usually though, this is just
      a sexy euphemism for "we're on a compressed schedule..."</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="22" width="34%"><font size="2">Don't deposit your checks until
      Tuesday.</font></td>
		<td height="22" width="66%"><font size="2">Put your resume on <a href="http://www.monster.com" target="_blank">Monster</a>.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="16" width="34%"><font size="2">We need to work smarter, not harder.</font></td>
		<td height="16" width="66%"><font size="2">If you don't meet the deadline
      it's not because <strong><em>I</em></strong> asked you to work longer hours,
      it's because <strong><em>you</em></strong> were not smart enough to figure
      out a way to get everything done.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="2" width="34%"><font size="2">Failure is not an option.</font></td>
		<td height="2" width="66%"><font size="2">I could lose my job if you guys
      don't get this thing working.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
      <td height="4" width="34%"><font size="2">We need to empower the developers
      to make strategic decisions.</font></td>
		<td height="4" width="66%"><font size="2">I'm not very technical so you guys
      decide.</font></td>
	</tr>
   <tr>
		<td height="2"><font size="2">We're a pre-IPO company with an aggressive strategy to become market leaders.</font></td>
		<td height="2"><font size="2">We're a start-up company and we want you to work 60 hours a week.</font></td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
      <td height="2" width="34%"><font size="2">I think a lot of really good things have
      come out of this project.</font></td>
		<td height="2" width="66%"><font size="2">The project has failed and is being canceled but I've got to make it look like it was at least a partial success.</font></td>
	</tr>
</table>
