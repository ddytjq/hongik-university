                    <td colspan="2" height="14" class="titrearticle" valign="top" width="532">Joker Rossi wins at home to delight his fans</td>
                  </tr>
                  <tr>
                    <td height="5"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="5" height="5"></td>
                    <td width="465">
                      <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
                        <tr>
                          <td class="texte10">Italian Grand Prix / Final report / 02 June</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                          <td>
                              <table border="0" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" width="536">
                                <tr valign="top">
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                    <hr><font  color='000000' ><b>Championship leader wins his fourth MotoGP victory, Michelin riders fill first seven spots</b></font>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="67" height="36"></td>
                                </tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                    <hr><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td>
                                </tr>
                                
                                
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi5d3.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >Fun-loving Italian hero </font><font  color='0000cc' >Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > took a finely judged gamble on tyre choice at Mugello this afternoon, choosing a softer rear Michelin than his immediate rivals to win his fourth victory of the year in fine style. Compatriot and arch-rival </font><font  color='0000cc' >Max Biaggi (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > took second, with</font><font  color='0000cc' > Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > third, just ahead of </font><font  color='0000cc' >Carlos Checa (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >. Rossi chose a medium-hard 16.5in S4 slick, while the next three men home all went for hard compound S4s. Michelin monopolised the top seven positions and currently hold the top eight places in the MotoGP World Championship.<br>	In 30-degree heat and at one of GP racing's most demanding circuits, Rossi knew that he would need the best-possible machine/tyre combination if he was to defeat team-mate Ukawa, who beat him in April's South African GP, and the ever-improving M1s of Biaggi and Checa. After tumbling during morning warm-up, his plan worked to perfection. He led the early stages from pole position, then slipped to second, followed Biaggi for several laps before moving back ahead. During the last 11 laps he upped the pace, artfully controlling some impressive wheelspin in the closing stages to win by 2.4 seconds.</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          
                                          <img align="left" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi2d25.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='0000cc' >	"I made a mistake this morning, which put me under some pressure, so I tried to ride the bike more sweetly for the race,"</font><font  color='000000' > said Rossi after his first home-race win in the premier class.</font><font  color='0000cc' >" I used my second bike which worked quite good. I chose a softer rear than the other four-stroke guys, so I knew it wouldn't be possible to push 100 per cent all race. In the later stages I had a good rhythm and enjoyed myself."</font><font  color='000000' ><br>	Ever the joker, Rossi delighted the 65,000 crowd by stopping immediately after the finish line, where two friends dressed in polizia uniforms booked him for speeding. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"It was a great race, only spoiled by the big trouble with the police,"</font><font  color='000000' > he joked. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"They told me I'd exceeded the speed limit, so I told them who I am and they let me off, but when they saw I had no registration plate, they booked me!"</font><font  color='000000' ></font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi3d16.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >	</font><font  color='0000cc' >Michelin Grand Prix manager Emmanuel Fournier</font><font  color='000000' > was as impressed as ever with Rossi's fourth win from five rides.<br> </font><font  color='0000cc' >"His tyre choice was a little more risky than the others but it worked well for him,"</font><font  color='000000' > said Fournier. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"He was still going fast at the end of the race and we've now checked the tyre; it had good wear, though a little more than the harder tyres that his rivals chose."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          
                                          <img align="left" alt="Max Biaggi (Marlboro Yamaha Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/biagd30.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='000000' >	Biaggi, who like team-mate Checa ran a new chassis this weekend, was happy enough with his best result of the season. <br></font><font  color='0000cc' >"Second isn't first but I'm totally satisfied because I did my best and because we weren't so far away from winning,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the Roman, who had qualified second quickest. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I started well, but when Rossi was behind me, I felt like he was just waiting."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/ukad8.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >	The final podium slot went to Ukawa after a lengthy battle with Checa. The Japanese rider had a straight-line speed advantage over his Spanish rival and used that to firm effect in the final laps. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"It all came down to the last two laps,"</font><font  color='000000' > he said. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I'm happy with third because I've never had luck at this track."</font><font  color='000000' ><br>	Checa was disappointed with fourth because his bike was down on speed. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"We had some problems in practice which lost us valuable time in preparing for the race,"</font><font  color='000000' > he said. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I got away well and managed to pass Max and Ukawa a few times but in the end I couldn't stay with them."</font><font  color='000000' >	</font>

He did the same here last year, winning the last-ever Italian 500 GP. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"We made a good tyre choice,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the Brazilian, who chose a soft-compound S4, just like team-mate</font><font  color='0000cc' > Loris Capirossi (West Honda Pons NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >, who he beat by 4.6 seconds.<br> </font><font  color='0000cc' >Norick Abe (Antena 3 Yamaha-d'Antin YZR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > rode a good race to seventh, six seconds down on Capirossi.</font>

Biaggi's M1 was second-fastest bike of the weekend at 322.9kmh. Rossi managed 321.1kmh.</font>
