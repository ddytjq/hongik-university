The multiple copies are maintained probably because the rDNA gene needs voluminous transcription.			<li>rDNA genes are tandemly repeated in eukaryotes. They differ from <a href="MG112284.html">satellite DNAs</a> in that the length of the repeat is longer and the DNA is not highly repeated.			<li>The eukaryotic transcribed rDNA unit is flanked by not transcribed or lightly transcribed segments.			<li>rDNA genes are <strong>dispersed</strong> at different locations on the <i>E. coli</i> DNA.			<li><a name="HistInt"></a>Like rDNA genes, the <i>D. melanogaster</i> <a href="#Hist">histone</a> gene unit is moderately repetitive DNA, is arranged in tandem repeats, and has products that are required in large amounts by all cells.			<li>Unlike rDNA genes, the histone unit consists of transcribed regions separated by non-identical spacer regions.			<li>Gene <a href="MG112286.html">families</a> are groups of genes related, but not necessarily identical, in structure. The members of the family can have functions that are identical or similar. Examples of further gene families with members with related functions are the growth hormone and the immunoglobulin gene families.			<li>Whether gene families correspond to repeated sequence families depends on the degree of relatedness of the members of the gene family and the <a href="MG112281.html">stringency of hybridization</a> used to define the sequence family.			<li>It is difficult to analyze genetically the function of multi-copy genes since mutating one copy or adding one extra copy should have only a small effect on the level of expression from the multi-copy family.			<li>Note that single locus and multi-locus <a href="MG11125.html">VNTR loci</a> are tandem moderately repeated sequences used in forensic and legal investigations. Multi-locus VNTRs are dispersed sets of tandem repeats.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a><br>			</p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Info"></a>Further information</h4>		<ul type="square">			<li>Further experiments established that the rDNA repeat unit is truly tandem, consisting of both transcribed and non-transcribed segments.			<li>Unlike sea urchin and fruit fly histone genes, mammalian histone genes are dispersed (like <i>E.coli</i> rRNA genes are) at various chromosomal locations, as are other genes whose products are needed in most cells (actin and tubulin, for example). <img src="nav4.gif" width="69" height="71" align="right" usemap="#nav4B1F889C9"><map name=""></map> Genes for <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/viewer.cgi?save=0&cmd=&cfm=on&f=1&view=graph&txt=0&query_key=3&db=nucleotide&WebEnv=Q%3DpdJ%3C%3C%3CCQEcKC%3Fe%3F%3F%3EgAg%5CVh%3EfG%3DDDabA%5Dj%40Xj%3FIc%3FIfBaBD%3D%3D%3C&WebEnvRq=1&vwe=1">histones</a> are clustered in <i>Drosophila.</i>			<li>Genes for <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/cgi-bin/Entrez/framik?gi=555853&db=Nucleotide">ribosomal RNA (rDNA)</a> are moderately repeated sequences organized in <a href="http://cellbio.utmb.edu/cellbio/nucleus3.htm#Nucleolus">tandem repeats</a>. The large intergenic spacer of the human rDNA unit has many <a href="MG112286.html">Alu</a> repeats.			<li><a href="http://www.dbbm.fiocruz.br/www-mem/926/3429re.html">Retrotransposons</a> can be a significant fraction of middle repetitive DNA.			<li>Short tandemly repeated sequences can occur in regions of genomes that are <a href="../MGW2/MG22.html">transcribed</a> and <a href="../MGW2/Mg24.html">translated</a>.			<ul>				<li>The expansion of <a href="../MGW3/MG342.html">trinucleotide repeats</a> is associated with disease.				<li>Translation of 23 nucleotide repeats of the <a href="../MGW2/MG2414.html#Info">K12</a> gene of the Karposi Sarcoma herpesvirus is reading frame independent.			</ul>		</ul>		<center>			<p><a name="bottom"></a><a href="MG112284.html">Last</a> | <a href="MG1129.html">Vocabulary</a> | <a href="MG11228.html">Overview</a> | <a href="#Facts">Top</a> | <a href="MG112286.html">Next</a><br>			<br>			</p>		</center>		<p><img height="72" width="108" src="divlogo2.gif" align="right">		<hr>		</p>		<p>This is page 112285 of <a href="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</a> by <a href="http://opbs.okstate.edu/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</a>, &copy; 1998-2000<br>		<hr>		</p>		<address>E-mail inquiries to <a href="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu">U.
