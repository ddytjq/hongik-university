			<h2>Jargon Master<br></h2>
		</td>
	</tr>
	<tr> 
		<td>
			<img src="/images/spacer.gif" height="1" width="10" alt="spacer"> 
		</td>
		<td> 
			<p class="plaintext">Confused by all the talk of valuations, products 
			and repayment vehicles? Our Jargon Master might just help.<br><br>
			Use the alphabetic groups to click to the word you are looking for</p>
				<table width="100%" border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" class="plaintext" bordercolor="#66cc66">
					<tr align="center"> 
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#a">A - C</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#d">D - F</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#g">G - I</a>
						</td>
					</tr>
					<tr align="center"> 
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#l">J - L</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#m">M - O</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#p">P - R</a>
						</td>
					</tr>
					<tr align="center"> 
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#s">S - U</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#v">V - X</a>
						</td>
						<td height="30" align="center" class="plaintext">
							<a href="#v">Y + Z</a>
						</td>
					</tr>
				</table>
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="a"></a>Annual Percentage Rate (APR)</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A figure which all lenders must quote when referring to mortgages. 
			It is designed to show the total yearly cost of a mortgage stated as a percentage of the loan. 
			It includes items such as the interest rate paid at the start of the mortgage, application fee, 
			valuation fee and other charges commonly paid at the end of a mortgage.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Arrears</p>
			<p class="plaintext">The term "in arrears" is used to describe a mortgage account if a borrower 
			has failed to keep up the monthly mortgage payments.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="b"></a>Bank of England Tracker</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A mortgage product where the interest rate is linked to the Bank of England Base Rate.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="c"></a>Cap</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A provision which may be in a variable rate mortgage which sets an upper limit 
			on the interest rate payable on the mortgage. A "capped rate" is an interest rate which is subject to a cap.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Completion</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is used in two ways: a. it means the legal transfer of a property so that 
			you own it and are able to move in, and; b. (whether you are buying or remortgaging), it is the time 
			when we actually pay over the money we are lending to you.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Conveyancing</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Another word for the legal side of transferring ownership of a property from 
			one person to another.</p>
<a name="d"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Direct customer</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A customer who does not come to us via a third party.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Discount rate</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is where the interest rate you pay is discounted by a set amount below our 
			Variable Mortgage Base Rate for the Initial Period. Your monthly payments will still alter in 
			accordance with the terms of the Loan.</p>
			
<p class="biggreentext">Early Repayment Charge / Early Repayment Charge Period</p>
<p class="plaintext">If you pay off all of your loan before the end of the mortgage term, you may have to pay an Early Repayment Charge.  This usually applies in the early years of a mortgage and details of the charge will be given in your mortgage offer.  The period during which the charge applies is known as the Early Reayment Charge Period.</p>

<p class="biggreentext">Equity</p>
<p class="plaintext">If your house is worth more than the mortgage on it, the difference is known as the 
			'equity'. So for example, if your outstanding mortgage is &pound;50,000 and your home is worth &pound;60,000 you have 
			equity of &pound;10,000.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="f"></a>Financial advice</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Advice available from financial advisers who are authorised to give advice and make 
			recommendations on products regulated under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000. We have decided to introduce 
			customers exclusively to Legal &amp; General for financial advice.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Fixed rate</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is a special product in which the interest rate is fixed for a given period of 
			time - the Initial Period. During this period the monthly payments may alter as a result of changes in 
			insurance premiums or because fees have been charged to your account.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Future reserve</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A facility available on all mortgages providing a reserve of cash which allows you 
			to increase your borrowing up to 75% of the value of your home or 50% of the original advance whichever is the lower. Such borrowing is added to your existing 
			mortgage which you can repay over the term of your mortgage or over a shorter period if you wish.</p>
<a name="g"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Gross Payment</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is where you choose to make the same monthly mortgage payment as would be payable without taking account of the benefit from any offset 
			savings account.  This allows any offset savings balances to work harder by reducing the outstanding offset mortgage balance faster.  This option only applies to
			our offset account.</p>

<a name="i"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Initial Interest</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is the interest charged from the working day before the completion date notified 
			to us by our legal adviser to the end of the calendar month in which interest 
			charging commences.</p>
			
<p class="biggreentext">Initial Period</p>
<p class="plaintext">A period of time that applies to certain special products. For example during the time a fixed rate or discount rate of interest may be payable.</p>

<p class="biggreentext">Introducer</p>
<p class="plaintext">This is somebody other than another lender or us who advises you about the choice of your mortgage.  As we subscribe to the Mortgage Code, all the mortgage introducers with whom we deal must also subscribe to it and they will also appear on the Mortgage Code Compliance Board Register of Intermediaries.  We are not responsible for their actions and advice even if they advising on our products and services.</p>

<a name="l"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">LINK card</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A LINK card will give you 24 hour access to your money, instantly - via the LINK cashpoint network.  
			With your personal identification number (PIN) you will be able to withdraw up to £250 per day from any cash machine 
			displaying the distinctive LINK logo.  You can also make withdrawals from any of our branches.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext">Loan</p>
			<p class="plaintext">The full amount of the money which we lend you.</p>
			
<p class="biggreentext">Loan to Value (LTV)</p>
<p class="plaintext">The amount of your loan compared to the valuation or purchase price of your property (whichever is the lower), shown as a percentage.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="m"></a>Mortgage</p>
			<p class="plaintext">The whole arrangement between us including the loan and all terms and conditions. 
			It is also a legal document (which can be referred to as a Legal Charge or, in Scotland, a Standard Security) 
			under which the owner of a property agrees that the property will be security for repayment of a loan which 
			a lender makes.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext">Mortgage Term</p>
			<p class="plaintext">The period over which the loan is to be repaid. This period will be in the mortgage offer.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Mortgagee</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Another name for the lender (us).</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Mortgagor</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Another name for the borrower (you)</p>
			
<a name="n"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Net Payment</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is where you choose a monthly mortgage payment that takes into account any balances in 
			your offset savings account(s) before calculating the interest charged on your offset mortgage.  This option only 
			applies to our offset account.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="o"></a>Offer</p>
			<p class="plaintext">If we approve your mortgage application, we will make you an 'offer' of a loan. This is 
			also known as a "mortgage offer". Remember that the offer is often subject to conditions and can be withdrawn 
			by us at any time up to completion of the mortgage.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext">offset savings account</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A savings account which is linked to your offset mortgage.  The amount in this savings 
			account is used to reduce the offset mortgage balance before the interest on your offset mortgage is calculated.</p>
			
<a name="p"></a>
			<p class="biggreentext">Product</p>
			<p class="plaintext">A word which we use to describe any one of the mortgages in our full range, including a 
			standard mortgage at our Variable Mortgage Base Rate and our special products such as fixed, capped or 
			discounted rates.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext">Redemption</p>
			<p class="plaintext">You redeem - or repay - a mortgage when you have paid back all of the loan and all 
			interest, costs or other charges (such as insurance premiums) which are due on it. The mortgage is brought to an end.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Regulated products</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Products regulated under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 such as life assurance policies, 
			endowments, pensions and investments. Regulated products can only be recommended by an authorised advisor. 
			We have decided to introduce customers exclusively to Legal &amp; General for advice on regulated products.</p>
			<p class="biggreentext">Remortgage</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Repaying one mortgage by taking out another with a new lender which is secured on the 
			same property.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext"><a name="s"></a>Settlement</p>
			<p class="plaintext">In Scotland, completion is known as 'settlement'. If you have been made an offer and 
			you decide to go ahead with your mortgage, the final agreement between you and us is known as 
			settlement. It's when we actually pay over the money we are lending to you and when you will usually be 
			able to move into your home.</p>
			
			<p class="biggreentext">Special features</p>
			<p class="plaintext">Features that enable you to have a mortgage with the facility to make overpayments 
			and, subject to agreement, underpayments or payment holidays in the future. These features may not be 
			available immediately if you have a mortgage with an early repayment charge period.</p>

			<p class="biggreentext">Standard security</p>
			<p class="plaintext">This is a Scottish document which sets out the terms of a mortgage.</p>

<a name="v"></a>
<p class="biggreentext">Variable Mortgage Base Rate</p>
<p class="plaintext">This is our standard rate of interest which will be charged on most mortgages at some 
time during the mortgage term. It is variable and can go up or down.</p>

<p class="biggreentext">Vendor</p>
<p class="plaintext">Another name for the person selling a property.<br><br></p>
