            <H1><!-- #BeginEditable "Heading" -->Bitwise Operators Quiz Answers<!-- #EndEditable --></H1>
            <!-- #BeginEditable "Content" --> 
            <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="4" CELLPADDING="5">
              <TR BGCOLOR="#F8FFF0"> 
                <TD COLSPAN="2"> 
                  <P CLASS="SUBHEADING">AND, OR, XOR</P>
                </TD>
              </TR>
              <TR VALIGN="TOP" BGCOLOR="#EEFFEE"> 
                <TD WIDTH="100%" COLSPAN="2"> 
                  <TABLE WIDTH="90%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="0" ALIGN="CENTER" BGCOLOR="#CCCCCC">
                    <TR> 
                      <TD> 
                        <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="1" CELLPADDING="3">
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#CCEEFF" VALIGN="TOP" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                            <TD WIDTH="10%" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Expression</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="10%"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">returns...</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="80%"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Reason</P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3 &amp; 4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>3 is 010 in binary and 4 is 100. Comparing the bits using AND, you'd get 000 which is zero. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>5 &amp; 4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>5 is 4+1, which is 101 in binary. 4 is simply 100, so <TT>5 &amp; 4</TT> returns 100 in binary, which is decimal 4. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>8 &amp; 7</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>8 is simply 1000. 7 is 0111. So <TT>8 &amp; 7</TT> returns 0000 in binary, which is zero. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>16 &amp; 6</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>16 is 10000. 6 is 00110. So <TT>16 &amp; 6</TT> returns 00000 in binary, which is zero. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>24 &amp; 16</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>16</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>24 is 16+8, which is 11000 in binary. 16 is simply 10000. So <TT>24 &amp; 16</TT> returns 10000 in binary, which is 16. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>32 &amp; 16</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Now we're comparing 2 powers of 2 using bitwise AND. In the cases when the powers are different, the result is always zero: 
                                32 is 100000, 16 is 010000 and since the 1 occurs in different places, <TT>32 &amp; 16</TT> is 0. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>17 &amp;&amp; 23</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><SPAN CLASS="RED">Trick question!!</SPAN> Remember that bitwise AND is just <TT>&amp;</TT>, where as logical AND is <TT>&amp;&amp;</TT>. 
                                Since both operands are non zero, <TT>1</TT> is returned by logical AND. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>8 ^ 12</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Now for bitwise XOR. 8 is 1000, 12 is 8+4, which is 1100. Now, 1 XOR 1 is zero because BOTH operands is one. The a XOR 
                                b returns 1 if only one of a or b are 1. So <TT>8 ^ 12</TT> returns 0100, which is 4 in decimal. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>1 ^ 17</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>16</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>1 is simply 00001, 17 is 16+1, which is 10001 in binary. Doing a XOR comparison, we see that <TT>1 ^ 17</TT> returns 10000, 
                                which is 16. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>3 ^ 8</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>11</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>3 is 2+1, which is 0011 in binary, 8 is 1000. Doing a XOR comparison, we see that <TT>3 ^ 8</TT> returns 1011, which is 
                                8+2+1 = 11. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>13 | 23</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>31</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Now for bitwise OR. 13 is 8+4+1, so it's 01101 in binary. 23 is 16+4+2+1, so it's 10111 in binary. Unlike XOR, a OR b returns 
                                1 if any of the operands are 1. So applying bitwise OR, <TT>13 | 23</TT> is 11111, which is 31. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>16 | 4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>20</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Here's another trick: if you're comparing 2 different powers of 2 using bitwise OR, you simply add them. So <TT>16 | 4</TT> 
                                returns 16 + 4 =20. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3 | 10</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>11</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>3 is 0011 in binary. 10 is 1010. So <TT>3 | 10</TT> is 1011, which is 11. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>8 | 3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>11</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>8 is 1000 in binary. 3 is 0011. So <TT>8 | 3</TT> is 1011, which is 11. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>5 | 3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>7</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>5 is 101 in binary. 3 is 011. So <TT>5 | 3</TT> is 111, which is 7. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>8 | 10</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>10</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>8 is 1000 in binary. 10 is 1010. So <TT>8 | 10</TT> is 1010, which is 10. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>32 | 64</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>96</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>We are comparing 2 different powers of 2 again, using bitwise OR. So we just add them to get 96 as the return value. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP" HEIGHT="37"><TT>3 ^ 5</TT></TD>
                            <TD HEIGHT="37"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>6</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Back to XOR, 3 is 011 in binary, 5 is 101. So <TT>3 ^ 5</TT> returns 110, which is 6 in decimal. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>6 ^ 13</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>11</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>6 is 0110 in binary, 13 is 1101. So <TT>6 ^ 13</TT> returns 1011, which is 11 in decimal. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><TT>20 ^ 20</TT></TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>You may have noticed that <TT>a &amp; a</TT> and <TT>a | a</TT> return <TT>a</TT>. But <TT>a ^ a</TT> always returns 0. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>

                      </TD>

                    <TR> 
                      <TD> 
                        <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="1" CELLPADDING="3">
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#CCEEFF" VALIGN="TOP" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                            <TD WIDTH="10%" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Expression</P>

                            <TD WIDTH="10%"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">returns...</P>

                            <TD WIDTH="80%"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Reason</P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT> 3 &lt;&lt; 0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Shifting zero places has no effect! </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4 &lt;&lt; 1</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>8</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Shifting 4 once to the left is the same as multiplying 4 by 2. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>7 &lt;&lt; 3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>56</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Shifting 7 3 times to the to the left is the same as multiplying 7 by 2<SUP>3</SUP>, so 7*8 = 56. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3 &lt;&lt; 4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>48</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Shifting 3 4 times to the to the left is the same as multiplying 3 by 2<SUP>4</SUP>, so 3*16 = 48. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>2 &lt;&lt; 5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>64</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>2*2<SUP>5</SUP> is 2*32 = 64. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>32 &gt;&gt; 2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>8</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying 32/2<SUP>2</SUP> i.e. 32/4, which is 8. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>34 &gt;&gt; 3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>2<SUP>3</SUP> is 8. But 34/8 is 4.25, which rounds down to 4. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>56 &gt;&gt; 4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
