<h1>Half Year Results 2001</h1>
<h2>HIGHLIGHTS</h2>
<ul>
<li><p>Total assets have risen to £2.7 billion, an increase of £112 million (4.3%)
in the 6 month period and  £384 million (16%) up on 30 June 2000.</p>
<li><p>Profit after tax for the half year stood at £6.9 million, an increase of 6.15
% on the 6 months to June 2000.</p>
<li><p>Other Income up 10% to £7.8m (from £7.1m in the first half of 2000).</p>
<li><p>Retail Savings Balances up by  £353m to £1.9bn compared with June 2000.</p>
</ul>
<h2>Comment</h2>
<p>Commenting on the results, Cheshire Building Society Chief Executive, Colin Whittle,
said: “The first half of 2001 has again produced excellent results for the Group.
Consistency has been key to our performance.</p>
<p>We have continued to deliver real value and tangible benefits to our customers.
Recognition for this has come from, amongst others, financial information experts
MoneyFacts.  The Cheshire has been top building society in the survey for the last
three occasions. <b>(See note 2)</b></p>
<p>We also continued to maintain competitive interest rates on our savings products
which is reflected in a 23% increase in savings balances over the last twelve months.</p>
<p>We are pressing on with the aggressive modernisation plans started in year 2000.
This investment in people, processes and technology has been reflected in a rise in
administrative costs but, despite this, Management Expenses as a percentage of mean
assets have fallen slightly from 0.59% at June 2000 to 0.58% in June 2001.</p>
<p>A key component of our approach is to ensure that customers have access to their
accounts and the services of the Society at a time and place that is convenient to
them. In this first half of 2001 we have extended the hours of the Cheshire’s Customer
Contact Centre to better serve our customers.</p>
<p>The Society’s website capability has also been significantly enhanced. Building
upon the successful launch of an online real time Mortgage Decision and Mortgage Application
our website , www.thecheshire.co.uk,<b> </b>now provides online Personal Loan Applications
and the ability to apply online for any of the Society’s Savings Products.</p>
<p>Access also means that branches continue to be a vital component of the Cheshire’s
service. We have recently introduced Personal Advisers in our top branches to deliver
a more personal ‘one to one’ service for our customers. In addition we are introducing
new technology to improve the service we can provide.</p>
<p>The Cheshire’s consistently competitive and award winning product range has not
only delivered the benefits of mutuality to our members but also generated excellent
results for the first half of the year.”</p>
<p>ENDS</p>
<p><b>Note 1:</b></p>
<p>Cheshire Building Society is one of the UK’s top regional building societies. 
It is based in Macclesfield, has 51 branches throughout the North West of England
and serves members nationwide.  The Society subscribes to both the Mortgage and Banking
codes and is committed to mutuality as being in the best interests of its members.</p>
<p><b>Note 2 - Award-winning Mortgages</b></p>
<p>Cheshire Building Society's Loyalty Mortgage remains the number one mortgage offered
by a building society, according to the latest survey by financial information experts
MoneyFacts.  The Loyalty Mortgage rewards borrowers of 5 years plus with a half per
cent discount on the Society’s standard variable rate.  This latest result means that
the Cheshire has achieved the treble, having been top building society in the survey
for the last three occasions.</p>
<p>The research looked at the total interest paid on a £50,000 standard variable rate
mortgage between 1 July 2000 and 30 June 2001.  Borrowers on the Cheshire’s Loyalty
Mortgage paid £385 less than someone with the survey’s most expensive lender.</p>
