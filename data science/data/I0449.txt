  In 1954 - as in 1950 - the home championships
  decided the qualification of the four British teams.&nbsp; We once more
  finished top and the sweaty socks once more finished as runners-up, only this
  time they decided to participate in the finals, with 2 games, 2 defeats, 0
  goals and a 7-0 defeat by Uruguay, a Scottish tradition of being rubbish had
  begun.&nbsp; The group stage had 4 groups of 4 but only 2 games were
  played.&nbsp; England drew 4-4 with Belgium after extra time (unusual for a
  group match) and then we beat the Swiss 2-0 to meet Uruguay in the quarter
  finals.&nbsp; But goals from Nat Lofthouse and Tom Finney couldn't prevent us
  from losing the game 4-2 and being eliminated from the World Cup.</b></font>
