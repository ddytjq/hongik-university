							<td><p><span class="body">HSBC is one of the first lenders to offer mortgages which comply with the government's CAT standards for mortgages. 
								CAT standards set out benchmarks for fair <span class="bodybold">C</span>harges, <span class="bodybold">A</span>ccess and <span class="bodybold">T</span>erms to help homebuyers compare offers. They provide the reassurance that loans which comply with the standards will be fair.</span></p>

										<li>All fees will be disclosed in advance so there are no surprises.

							<td><p><span class="bodybold">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></p></td>
							<td valign="top"><a name="6"><span class="bodybold">Why the lowest rate isn't always the cheapest
</span></a></td>
						</tr>
							<tr>
								<td>&nbsp;</td>
								<td><p><span class="body">Strange but true. We hope the following will help you understand the issues and prevent you making a costly mistake.</span></p>
									<p><span class="bodybold">Calculation of interest</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">With daily interest, the lender only charges interest on the balance of the account after allowing for any cleared funds received that day.</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">With annual interest, the lender only reduces the balance on which interest is charged once a year. This means you could pay interest on money already repaid!</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">It is always cheaper to have a loan on which interest is calculated on a daily basis, where the headline rates are the same.</span></p>
									<p><span class="bodybold">Lock-ins and extended lock-ins</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">Some lenders offer lower interest rates on the condition that you stay with them for a set period of time. With an extended lock-in, you are required to stay with the lender after the lower rate has finished.</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">If you try to repay the loan, perhaps by transfer to another lender, during the extended lock-in period, then the lender will charge a fee.</span></p>
									<p><span class="bodybold">Is there compulsory insurance?</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">Some lenders make it a condition of their mortgage that the customer must buy and maintain the lender's insurance, which can be expensive. If the customer does not maintain the insurance, the lender may increase the interest rate.</span></p>
									<p><span class="bodybold">In summary</span></p>
									<p><span class="body">
										<ul>
											<li>Always compare the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) and total amount of interest payable over the full term.
											<li>Check for lock-ins and make sure you understand what early repayment fees are payable.
											<li>Avoid loans where the rate is dependent on you buying the lender's insurance.
											<li>Look for a mortgage which meets the government's CAT standards for fair charges and terms.
										</ul>
									</span></p>
								</td>
							</tr>
