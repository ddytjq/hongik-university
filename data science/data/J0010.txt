<h2>
<font face="Arial,Helvetica">Introduction</font></h2></center>
<font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=+1>Many Motor Clubs are recognised
by the MSA for the organisation of motor competitions.</font></font>
<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=+1>Before <a href="racmsa/starting/" OnMouseOver="window.status='Starting MotorSport';return true">you
can start motorsport</a> of any sort you should join a motor club. There
should be one near you and The <a href="racmsa/starting/contacts.html" OnMouseOver="window.status='The Regional Associations';return true">Regional
Associations</a> (Tel: (01753) 681736) will give you this information.
However, most clubs have their speciality areas of interest and not all
are active in all areas. Also, please consult Steve Smith's <a href="http://www.btinternet.com/~smith.steve/club_corner.htm">Club
Corner Directory</a> for more clubs which do not, as yet, have their own
web site but still promote grass-roots motorsport. You could also try the
very good and brand new <a href="http://www.motorsportlife.com/">Motorsportlife.com</a></font></font>
<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=+1>The MSA has recently started
its <a href="http://www.msauk.org/club/searchdirectories.asp">own directory</a>
of clubs which is worth trying.</font></font>
<p><i><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=+1>n.b.&nbsp;<img SRC="images/ball_red.gif" ALT="Non-MSA recognised motor club" height=14 width=14>Some
of the clubs listed below are not recognised by the MSA but they are nonetheless,
clubs promoting motor sport.</font></font></i>
