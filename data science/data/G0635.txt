            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC">

              System</b><!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>

        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" -->
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="14">Books 
              or Articles about Asteroids</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Binzel, R., et al. <b>"The Origins of the Asteroids"</b> in Scientific 
                American, Oct. 1991, p. 88. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Burnham, 
                R. <b>"Here' s Looking at Ida"</b> in Astronomy, Apr. 1994, p. 
                38. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Durda, 
                D. <b>"All in the Family"</b> in Astronomy, Feb. 1993, p. 36. 
                Discusses asteroid families. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kowal, 
                Charles <b>Asteroids: Their Nature and Utilization</b>, 2nd ed. 
                1996, John Wiley/Praxis. Introductory book by the astronomer who 
                discovered Chiron. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Ostro, 
                S. <b>"Radar Reveals a Double Asteroid"</b> in Astronomy, Apr. 
                1990, p. 38. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Talcott, 
                R. <b>"Galileo Views Gaspra"</b> in Astronomy, Feb. 1992, p. 52. 
                <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Zimmerman, 
                R. <b>"Ice Cream Sundaes and Mashed Potatoes"</b> in Astronomy, 
                Feb. 1999, p. 54. On the NEAR mission. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="solarsys.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="15">Books 
              or Articles about Comets</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Aguirre, E. <b>"The Great Comet of 1997"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                July 1997, p. 50. On Comet Hale-Bopp. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Benningfield, 
                D. <b>"Where Do Comets Come From?"</b> in Astronomy, Sep. 1990, 
                p. 28. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Brandt, 
                John and Chapman, Robert <b>Rendezvous in Space</b>. 1992, W. 
                H. Freeman. Introduction by two leading comet experts. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Gore, 
                R. <b>"Halley' s Comet ' 86: Much More Than Met the Eye"</b> in 
                National Geographic, Dec. 1986, p. 758. (Also the March 1987 issue 
                of Sky & Telescope was devoted to what we learned from Halley' 
                s Comet in 1986.) <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Levy, 
                D. <b>The Quest for Comets</b>. 1994, Plenum Press. Personal story 
                of comet discovery and comet science by an amateur astronomer 
                who has found many comets. (See also his Comets: Creators and 
                Destroyers. 1998, Simon & Schuster. On what we can learn from 
                studying comets.) <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Newcott, 
                W. <b>"The Age of Comets"</b> in National Geographic, Dec. 1997, 
                p. 94. Good introduction with beautiful photos. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sagan, 
                Carl and Druyan, Anne <b>Comet</b>. 1986, Random House. Very good 
                presentation of historical material and human connections. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Spencer, 
                John and Mitton, Jacqueline, eds. <b>The Great Comet Crash: The 
                Collision of Comet Shoemaker-Levy 9 and Jupiter</b>. 1995, Cambridge 
                U. Press. Good, non-technical summary. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Weissman, 
                P. <b>"Comets at the Solar System' s Edge"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Jan. 1993, p. 26. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Weissman, 
                P. <b>"The Oort Cloud"</b> in Scientific American, Sep. 1998, 
                p. 84. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="solarsys.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="16">Books 
              or Articles about Cosmic Impacts</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Chyba, C. <b>"Death from The Sky: Tunguska"</b> in Astronomy, 
                Dec. 1993, p. 38. Excellent review article. (See also: Gallant, 
                R. <b>"Journey to Tunguska"</b> in Sky & Telescope, June 1994, 
                p. 38.) <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Lewis, 
                John <b>Rain of Fire and Ice: The Very Real Threat of Comet and 
                Asteroid Bombardment</b>. 1995, Addison-Wesley. Good overview 
                of impacts. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">McFadden, 
                L. and Chapman, C. <b>"Near-Earth Objects: Interplanetary Fugitives"</b> 
                in Astronomy, Aug. 1992, p. 30. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Morrison, 
                D. & Chapman, C. <b>"Target Earth: It Will Happen"</b> in Sky 
                & Telescope, Mar. 1990, p. 261. (See an updated version in Astronomy, 
                Oct. 1995, p. 34.) <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Morrison, 
                D. <b>"The Spaceguard Survey: Protecting the Earth from Cosmic 
                Impacts"</b> in Mercury, Sep./Oct. 1992, p. 103. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Ortega, 
                T. <b>"Comet Stalker"</b> in Astronomy, Apr. 1999, p. 58. Profile 
                of astronomer Tom Gehrels and his work in finding earth-crossing 
                asteroids and comets. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sagan, 
                C. <b>Pale Blue Dot</b>. 1994, Random House. Chapter 18 is about 
                impacts and how we might respond to the danger. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="solarsys.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="17">Books 
              and Articles about the Search for Life in the Solar System</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Achenbach, J. <b>"Life Beyond Earth"</b> in National Geographic, 
                Jan. 2000, p. 24. Discussion of many themes, from science to science 
                fiction, but with good information. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Bernstein, 
                M., et al. <b>"Life' s Far-flung Raw Materials"</b> in Scientific 
                American, July 1999, p. 42. On the discovery of organic materials 
                in the gas and dust among the stars, and in comets. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Jakosky, 
                Bruce <b>The Search for Life On Other Planets</b>. 1998, Cambridge 
                U. Press. Clear introduction by a leader in the field. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kerr, 
                R. <b>"Requiem for Life on Mars? Support for Microbes [in Meteorites] 
                Fades"</b> in Science, vol. 282, p. 1398 (20 Nov. 1998). <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Taylor, 
                Michael <b>Dark Life: Martian Nanobacteria, Rock Eating Cave Bugs, 
                and Other Extreme Organisms of Inner Earth and Outer Space</b>. 
                1999, Scribners. Good behind-the-scenes discussion of the controversy 
                surrounding the meteorites from Mars. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Treiman, 
                A. <b>"Microbes in a Martian Meteorite?"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Apr. 1999, p. 52. An update on the rock from Mars. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><br>
              </font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="solarsys.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="18">A 
              Few Especially Useful Web Sites</a> </b></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The 
              following sites contain a wealth of information, images, and links 
              about each world: </font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <ul>
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://seds.lpl.arizona.edu/nineplanets/nineplanets/nineplanets.html" target="_blank">The 
                Nine Planets</a> </font> 
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.hawastsoc.org/solar/eng/homepage.htm" target="_blank">Views 
                of the Solar System</a> </font> 
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov/planetary/planetary_home.html" target="_blank">Planetary 
                Sciences Site</a> </font> 
            </ul>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The 
              best images from recent NASA spacecraft can be found (and are well 
              organized) at <a href="http://photojournal.jpl.nasa.gov" target="_blank">Planetary 
              Photojournal</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> If 
              you want to know how objects and features got their names, try: 
              <a href="http://wwwflag.wr.usgs.gov/USGSFlag/Space/nomen/nomen.html" target="_blank">"The 
              Gazetteer of Planetary Nomenclature"</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> A 
              good review of American and Soviet moon exploration is at the <a href="http://cass.jsc.nasa.gov/pub/expmoon/lunar_missions.html" target="_blank">"Exploring 
              the Moon" site</a> from NASA' s Johnson Space Flight Center </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> For 
              a wealth of information, maps, and links about Mars, try the <a href="http://cmex-www.arc.nasa.gov/" target="_blank">Center 
              for Mars Exploration Home Page</a> at NASA' s Ames Research Center 
              </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> The 
              latest information and images about Jupiter and its moons can be 
              found at the <a href="http://www.jpl.nasa.gov/galileo" target="_blank">Galileo 
              Mission Site</a> from the Jet Propulsion Lab </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> For 
              a preview of the Cassini mission to Saturn and Titan, a good source 
              is <a href="http://www.jpl.nasa.gov/cassini/" target="_blank">Voyage 
              to Saturn</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> A 
              lot of information and links about comets and meteor showers has 
              been compiled by <a href="http://medicine.wustl.edu/%7Ekronkg/index.html" target="_blank">Gary 
              Kronk</a>. He includes lists of currently visible comets, sungrazing 
              comets, periodic comets, and interesting comets of the past. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> For 
              more on cosmic impacts and how to guard the Earth against them, 
              see the <a href="http://impact.arc.nasa.gov/" target="_blank">Asteroid 
              and Comet Impact Hazards Page</a> managed by Dr. David Morrison.</font></p>
