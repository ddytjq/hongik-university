      <p>We hope this Check List is useful. You should, of course satisfy yourself 
        that you are able to afford the payments before committing yourself to 
        any loan.<br>
        <br>
        <STRONG class="redtext">Home Checklist<br>
        </STRONG>When you look at a house for the first time it's all too easy 
        to be captivated by the excitement of buying and owning the house of your 
        choice and to ignore the potential risks such as a leaky roof or dry rot. 
        Our Home Check List gives you the major pitfalls to look out for. By using 
        this check list and your own common-sense you can avoid making mistakes 
        you may regret later.</p>
      <p><EM><STRONG><span class="redtext">General<br>
        </span></STRONG></EM>Do you like it?<br>
        Would travelling to work be easy?<br>
        Are there good transport facilities?<br>
        Are there good shops and good schools nearby?<br>
        Are the adjacent homes well maintained?<br>
        Will the house be available when needed?</p>
      <p><EM><STRONG><span class="redtext">Exterior<br>
        </span></STRONG></EM>Does the house seem to be in a good state of repair?<br>
        Can the exterior be easily maintained?<br>
        Are the windows double glazed?<br>
        Is there a garage/garage space?<br>
        Is the garden the right size?<br>
        Is the house/garden overlooked?<br>
        Is there private access to the house?<br>
        Any other good features?</p>
      <p><EM><STRONG><span class="redtext">Interior<br>
        </span></STRONG></EM>Are there any serious visible defects?<br>
        Is the house in good decorative order?<br>
        Is gas/electricity available?<br>
        Is it plumbed for washing machine/dishwasher?<br>
        Are there sufficient power points?<br>
        Is there one or more television points?<br>
        Is there one or more telephone points?</p>
