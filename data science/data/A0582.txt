<p>Introduced to standardise the identification of bank accounts, the IBAN is not a new account number.  With the addition of further characters to your existing account number the aim is to improve the quality of information exchanged during European cross-border payments to reduce errors and delays.

<p>An IBAN will only be required when invoices are sent between trading partners in Europe.  When an invoice is received containing an IBAN this should be quoted in the account number field on the payment instruction to your bank.  Should the word IBAN preface the number this should not be quoted on the payment instruction.

This is followed by your usual sort code and account number.
