<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b><u><font size="4">Report:</font></u></b></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">March
31st&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">The
Marco Polo Cycling Club travelled with two teams to Morocco; a Dutch selection
of Marco Polo Cycling Club members and in cooperation with NZMBA; Marco Polo
Cycling New Zealand.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">After
arrival in Casablana we were picked up by the race organisers and together with
a German team from Berlin we were brought to a complex of the ministry of Sports.</span></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB; mso-fareast-language: NL; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA">We
got a little nervous after we saw the other teams that would take part in the
Tour du Maroc. Trade Teams II from Italy, Colombia, Poland and France, Trade
Teams III from Denmark and Switserland and National Teams from Egypt, Tunisia,
Russia, Bulgaria, Czech Republic, Algeria and Morocco.</span>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">April
1</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">First
day in Morocco was spent on preparing the bicycles and a training to Rabat, the
capitol of Morocco. We went easy and enjoyed the landscape of the coast and the
typical buildings of Morocco; Mosques.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">April
2</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 10.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB">We
were taken by bus to the start of the Tour du Maroc in Rabbat. Because there was
a lot of traffic in the city we were running late on program. After the start
the peloton rode 500 meters until we came in a huge traffic jam. The riders all
came through but after that the peloton was racing without a course director.
Luckily some Morrocan riders knew we still had to go to a presentation in the
centre of Rabat. Amazingly they were able to stop the peloton and all riders
turned around and were welcomed by music groups and thousands of spectators at
the official start.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><img border="0" src="musicband.jpg" width="596" height="398"></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><font size="2">A
music band to entertain the spectators and cyclists at the start in Rabat. </font><font size="1">(Photographer;
Francis Cerny)</font></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span style="font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-ansi-language: EN-GB" lang="EN-GB">The
first days of the Tour du Maroc the cross-winds sometimes split the peloton. But
most of the time the stages were decided in field sprints. In the <span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial;
mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">first stage New zealander Glenn Mitchel was up front
and attacked to the last curve. Only Andrus Aug (Amore &amp; Vita) caught his
wheel and beat him in the sprint. The Dutch team tried to lead out Dennis
Hammink a couple of times but it was hard because the signs to mark the distance
were invisible behind the many spectators.</span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><font size="2">An

<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><font size="1">(Photographer;

<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:10.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
12.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">April 6</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial">Fès-
Khénifra</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial;
mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">Most riders were scared of the climb of the 2nd
category starting after 30 kilometres. An early break with Nathan Dahlberg and
Roel van Schalen went<span style="mso-spacerun: yes"> </span>immediately after
the start. The group almost was caught on top of the mountain, Roel was dropped,
but 10 riders kept the lead. Because of the warm weather, the hard circuit and
the headwind the peloton went “piano”.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-family:Arial;
mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">The Frenchman Lecrosnier and the Italian Baliani were
the best classified riders in this group. But with 20 kilometres to go Nathan
put on the turbo and went solo. He gained 1 minute on Lecrosnier and almost two
minutes on the rest of the group. The peloton finished more then 10 minutes
later.</span></p>
