		  <p class="contentsubhead">Northern Bank Annual Results Year to 30 September 2001</p>
		  <p class="contenttextbold">Highlights</p>
<ul>
<li>Pre-tax profit of £98 million, up 6.3%</li>
<li>Business lending, up 6.5%, due to highly successful Tailored Business Loan</li>
<li>Success of Base Rate Tracker Mortgage has been the main driver for 7% growth in Home Loans</li>
<li>Launch of major customer service improvement programme in all Northern Bank branches</li>
</ul>
<p>Northern Bank today released figures for its annual results and announced pre-tax profits of £98 million, an increase of 6.3% on last year.</p>
<p>Personal loans grew by 9% and mortgages by 7%.  Business lending is up by 6.5%, which can be attributed to the highly successful Tailored Business Loans. Customer deposits grew by 7%.</p>
<p>"This has been a very satisfactory performance in an increasingly uncertain economic environment", explains Don Price, Chief Executive, Northern Bank.   "Income is up by 2.3% overall, reflecting good volume growth but lower margins.  Throughout this time the bank has maintained very tight cost control with expenses increasing just 1.9%.  This has helped to produce a cost/income ratio of 51.1%."</p>
<p>He added, "The bad debt charge is down by £3 million. This is very pleasing to see as it demonstrates how our dedicated business banking team is helping to ensure that our customers do not over stretch themselves at an uncertain time."</p>
<p>During the year there was a focus on personal banking and business banking, following the establishment of 11 Business Centres throughout Northern Ireland.  Our ATM network has been modernised, resulting in 79 replacements and 101 upgrades.</p>   
<p>A number of successful new products have been introduced which included the Base Rate Tracker Mortgage contributing to a 7% growth in mortgages.  On the savings front the Guaranteed Equity Bonds and Charity Bond were equally successful, with the Guaranteed Equity Bond allowing customers to make lump sum investments with guaranteed returns linked to the stock market.</p> 
<p>Northern has also introduced a Maestro Card, which is an electronic global debit card, replacing the Eurocheque card, allowing customers added flexibility when travelling abroad.  The launch of our new on-line share dealing service offers an innovative low-cost and convenient way to access and trade shares via the internet.</p>
<p>Don Price added, "This year, we have made significant investment in our community support programme, and have launched a number of community support initiatives.  Northern was joint winner of the "Northern Ireland Business Award for Outstanding Community Involvement" for Time 2 Count, an initiative to promote numeracy skills in primary schools."</p>
<p>Looking to the future, Mr Price concludes,  "By the summer of next year, customers of Northern Bank will be able to use the new European Customer Contact Centre, located in Ayrshire.  The centre will allow customers to conduct most of their banking needs over the phone, as well as apply for new financial products.  The new centre will significantly enhance the Bank's ability to provide customers with a choice of delivery channels.  This is critical to the growth of our business as we seek to ensure customers see us as the bank of their choice. We will continue to focus on improving customer service and will continue to invest in the training of our staff."</p>
<p>Northern Bank has 95 branches throughout Northern Ireland and employs 2136 staff.</p>
