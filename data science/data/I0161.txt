<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">PLAYOFF 1st Leg -
Scotland v England</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<TABLE WIDTH="100%">
<TR>
<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">England became the 16th and final team to
qualify for next year's European Championships despite playing poorly and
crashing to a 1-0 home defeat at the hands of Scotland in the second leg of
their play-off on Wednesday. Scotland's fully-deserved win on the night was
secured by a 39th minute Don Hutchison header but England qualified 2-1 on
aggregate following their 2-0 win at Hampden Park on Saturday. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Scotland, who had not beaten England since 1985
and not scored against them in their previous five matches, were by far the
better, balanced and more enterprising team against the &quot;auld enemy&quot;
who were over-run in midfield, played like strangers and did not have one shot
on target in the entire match. Their only salvation was that they just about
defended well enough to stifle the Scottish attack. But goalkeeper David Seaman
had to made one stunning point-blank save from a diving header from Christian
Dailly with 10 minutes to play. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The only goal came when Callum Davidson broke
on the left, and sent Neil McCann away as Sol Campbell slipped. McCann crossed
high into the box and Hutchison rose above Tony Adams to head home. That sent
the 7,000 Scottish fans into raptures, but in the end they had to be content
with inflicting a first defeat on Kevin Keegan in his ninth match in charge.
</FONT></P>
</TD>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">PLAYOFF 1st Leg -

<TABLE WIDTH="100%">
<TR>
<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Paul Scholes capitalised on some woeful
defending to score two first half goals as England beat Scotland 2-0 at Hampden
Park in the first leg of their European Championship playoff on Saturday. The
victory moves England to the threshold of next year's finals and they should
duly take their place after the sides meet again in the second leg at Wembley
on Wednesday. </FONT></P>
</TD>
<TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="http://www.empics.com"><IMG

<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Scholes opened the scoring with a superbly
executed goal after 21 minutes when he controlled a long pass out of midfield
from Sol Campbell on his chest, taking it past Scottish skipper Colin Hendry,
before running on and firing it past goalkeeper Neil Sullivan. Scottish manager
Craig Brown had gambled on playing the talismanic Hendry despite his lack of
first team appearances at Rangers this season -- but another bad defensive
error allowed Scholes to make it 2-0 after 42 minutes. His Manchester United
team mate David Beckham whipped in a well-struck free-kick almost from the
touchline wide on the right, and the Scottish defence allowed the unmarked
Scholes a free header which powered past Sullivan into the net. It was his
ninth goal in his 19th appearance. </FONT></P>

second half and the once-mighty Hampden &quot;roar&quot; became barely a

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Although it was the 109th meeting of the
&quot;auld enemies&quot;, it was the first time they had met at Hampden since
1989 when England also won 2-0, and their first meeting since England beat
Scotland 2-0 in Euro'96. Scotland have now failed to score against England in
their last five meetings and have still not beaten their old enemy since a 1-0
win here in 1985. The record now reads England 45 wins, Scotland 40 with 24
matches drawn. Even if Scotland improve on those figures and win at Wembley on
Wednesday they will have to score their biggest win there since a 3-1 victory
50 years ago to advance to the finals. </FONT></P>

An electrifying 25-metre drive from Jamie Redknapp after 67 minutes gave an
experimental England side a well-deserved 2-1 victory over Belgium in a

after scoring his fourth goal in his fourth internatonal. The match, the first

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">England qualified for the European championship
play-offs after Sweden beat Poland 2-0 in their final Group Five qualifying
match in Stockholm on Saturday. The fact that Poland lost meant that England
retained second place in the group without kicking a ball and finished as
runners-up to the Swedes who emphatically won the group. Sweden finished with
22 points from their eight matches, ahead of England and Poland who both ended
with 13 points but England qualified because they had a better record than
Poland in their head-to-head matches. The two-legged play-offs will take place
on November 13 or 14 and November 17. The draw for them will be made in Germany
next Wednesday. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Oct
1</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">England coach Kevin Keegan made sweeping
changes to his squad on Friday, recalling Chelsea's Dennis Wise and
Middlesbrough's Paul Ince for the friendly international against Belgium in
Sunderland on October 10. Keegan named a 25-man squad for match but there was
no place for David Batty, sent off against Poland in England's last Euro 2000
qualifier on September 8. Eleven of the squad called up for the match in Warsaw
are not included this time although some, such as Liverpool's Robbie Fowler,
Manchester United's Gary Neville and Real Madrid's Steve McManaman, are out
because of injury. Keegan has recognised Leicester's good start to the season
by recalling striker Emile Heskey and including Steve Guppy for the first time.
He has also recalled Chelsea skipper Wise who won the last of his 12 caps
against Hungary three years ago, and Ince, who has not played for England since
their 2-0 defeat to world champions France last February. West Ham midfielder
Trevor Sinclair, who has won under-21 and England &quot;B&quot; caps, is also
included for the first time, while Ipswich Town's goalkeeper Richard Wright has
been recalled in place of Tottenham's Ian Walker. Keegan has kept faith with
his number one goalkeeper David Seaman who has recovered from a calf injury and
is expected to make his first appearance of the season for Arsenal at West Ham
on Sunday. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">England, inspired by an Alan Shearer first-half
hat-trick, beat Luxembourg 6-0 on Saturday as they tuned up perfectly for
Wednesday's all-important Euro 2000 group five qualifier in Poland. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Captain Alan Shearer struck a first half

preserve their hopes of reaching the Euro 2000 finals via the play-offs.

more in the 27th and 33rd to take his goal tally for his country to 27 in 52
internationals. Real Madrid winger Steve McManaman, who had never scored for
England in 24 previous games, weighed in with two in the 29th and 43rd minutes
of the group five match against a Luxembourg side which offered little
opposition. Substitute Michael Owen marked his England comeback with a
brilliantly curled-in right-foot shot in the 90th minute. England's 20-year-old
right back Kieron Dyer had an outstanding debut before going off injured at
halftime and had a hand in all of Shearer's goals. </FONT> </P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Sweden scored a convincing 2-0 win over Poland
on Saturday which eliminated the Poles from the European Championships and gave
England a place in the play-offs. Sweden, who had already won the group and
guaranteed themselves a place in next year's finals took the lead through
Kennet Andersson after 64 minutes and were always the more impressive side.
Henrik Larsson added the second in the last minute. The breakthrough -- for
both Sweden and England -- came midway through the second half when Fredrik
Ljungberg set up Andersson to score the opener to the delight of the
35,000-strong crowd. Larsson's late goal from close range not only delighted
the Swedes but also gave millions of English fans hopes for the play-offs --
and left the Poles utterly dejected. Poland could have scored a spectacular
opener in the 22nd minute but Andrzej Juskowiak's ambitious flick went wide.
Andersson also should have done better later in the game when, from point-blank
range and the goal at his mercy, he somehow managed to chest the ball over the
bar and onto the roof of the net when it looked easier to head it into the
empty goal. </FONT></P>

<TD COLSPAN="2"><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Oct 10
- Bulgaria 3 Luxembourg 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Former striker Hristo Stoichkov celebrated his
debut as Bulgaria assistant coach with a comfortable 3-0 victory against
Luxembourg in their final Euro 2000 qualifier on Sunday. The game had no
significance as both teams occupied the last two places in group five. Bulgaria
went ahead in the 40th minute when Daniel Borimirov headed home a corner.
Ivailo Petkov doubled the tally from a 68th minute free kick and substitute
Roumen Hristov scored on his debut 12 minutes from time. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Oct 9 - Sweden 2
Poland 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Sweden, who had already won the group and
guaranteed themselves a place in next year's finals took the lead through
Kennet Andersson after 64 minutes and were always the more impressive side.
Henrik Larsson added the second in the last minute. The teams produced a
lacklustre first half, but the Poles, who only needed a point to take second
place, rarely threatened and although they had a couple of chances in the
second half, Sweden always appeared to be in control. The breakthrough finally
came midway through the second half when Fredrik Ljungberg set up Andersson to
score the opener. Larsson's late goal from close range not only delighted the
Swedes but also gave millions of English fans hopes for the play-offs -- and
left the Poles utterly dejected. </FONT></P>
