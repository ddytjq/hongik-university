Any Club or Player dissatisfied with a decision given by the Board shall have the right of appeal as provided for in the Regulations.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>5</STRONG>&nbsp;All Members of The League from time to time in the Second and Third Divisions of The League shall take part in the Competition together with invited Clubs from the Conference.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>6</STRONG>&nbsp;All Clubs participating in the Competition shall be bound by and comply with the Competition Rules.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>7</STRONG>&nbsp;The Competition shall be played on the following basis:-</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(a)&nbsp;First Round: When the number of Clubs entered requires it, the Board shall arrange for Football League clubs to be awarded such number of byes as are necessary. </DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>The requisite number of Clubs shall be divided equally into two sections – Northern and Southern. Clubs will be drawn in couples within each section and shall compete on a knock-out basis with the first drawn club playing at home.&nbsp; </DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(b)&nbsp;Second Round: The winners from Round One and the clubs awarded byes shall be divided equally into Northern and Southern sections and matches will be played on a knock-out basis with the first drawn club playing at home.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(c)&nbsp;Area Quarter-Finals: These consist of 16 winners from the Second Round and matches will be played on a knock-out basis.&nbsp; The first drawn Club shall play at home.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(d)&nbsp;Area Semi-Finals: These shall consist of the 8 winners from the Area Quarter-Finals and matches will be played on a knock-out basis.&nbsp; The first drawn club shall play at home.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(e)&nbsp;Area Finals: These shall consist of the 4 winners from the Area Semi-Finals.&nbsp; Each tie shall be played over two legs, the first drawn Club playing at home in the first leg.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(f)&nbsp;Final: This shall consist of the two winners from the Area-Finals.&nbsp; The Final shall be played on a neutral ground to be determined by the Board.&nbsp; The Board shall have the authority to decide that the Final Tie be played on a Sunday.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>8</STRONG>&nbsp;Any Club without just cause failing to fulfil an engagement to play a fixture in the Competition on the approved day or date shall forfeit the tie and be expelled from the Competition.&nbsp; They shall also be liable to pay a compensatory payment to their opponents in the sum of £5,000.&nbsp; Such sum shall be paid to The League forthwith upon demand, for onward payment by The League to the defaulting Club’s opponents.&nbsp; Such failure without just cause to fulfil an engagement to play a fixture shall be deemed misconduct and render the Club liable to such further disciplinary proceedings as are permitted pursuant to the Regulations.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>9</STRONG>&nbsp;(a) All matches shall be played in conformity with the Laws of the Game promulgated by the International Football Association Board.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(b) Matches shall be of 90 minutes duration but any match which, for any cause whatsoever, falls short of 90 minutes duration may be ordered to count as a completed fixture, or be replayed in full or in part as the Board or any person or body to whom the Board may delegate power to organise the Competition may, in their absolute discretion determine.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(c) In all Matches, the half-time interval shall be fifteen minutes.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>10&nbsp;</STRONG>In the event of the scores being level at the end of ninety minutes’ play in all rounds apart from the Area-Finals, two periods of 15 minutes overtime shall be played.&nbsp; Should one of the teams score a goal during overtime, the match will be considered as finished and the team which scored the goal declared the winner.&nbsp; If the tie is still undecided after overtime, the winners shall be determined by the taking of kicks from the penalty mark in accordance with the Laws of the Game.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>In the Area-Final ties, if the aggregate scores are level at the end of 90 minutes in the second leg, two periods of 15 minutes overtime shall be played.&nbsp; Should one of the teams score a goal during overtime, the match will be considered as finished and the team which scored the goal declared the winner.&nbsp; If the tie is still undecided after overtime, the winners shall be determined by the taking of kicks from the penalty mark in accordance with the Laws of the Game.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>11&nbsp;</STRONG>All Clubs must provide separate areas adjacent to the pitch for the exclusive use of team officials and substitute Players (and any substituted Players) for both the Home and Visiting&nbsp;Club.&nbsp; These areas shall have direct access to the pitch, shall be under cover and shall&nbsp;contain eleven seats.&nbsp; The layout, facilities and viewing positions for the Visiting Club bench shall be the same as for the Home Club.&nbsp; The following rules apply:-</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>&nbsp;&nbsp;(a)&nbsp;only those team officials and substitutes (and any substituted Player) listed&nbsp;on the team sheet are permitted to sit on the trainers bench<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;(b)&nbsp;Clubs must clearly indicate on the team sheet the team official responsible&nbsp;for the conduct of personnel on the trainers bench<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;(c)&nbsp;with the exception of the team manager, the team coach and any&nbsp; substitutes <BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;who are warming up, all other personnel are to remain seated on the trainers bench<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) &nbsp;the team manager or the team coach is allowed to move to the edge of the&nbsp;Technical Area to issue instructions to his team, but must then return to the Trainers bench and<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;(e)&nbsp;all such personnel must comply with any instruction under this regulation from any of the Match Officials, including the Fourth Official.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>12</STRONG>&nbsp;In all Matches, each team is permitted up to five substitutes of whom not more than three may take part in the Match.&nbsp; All substitutes must be nominated and included on the official Team Sheet handed to the Referee prior to the Match.&nbsp; Substitutes not on the Team Sheet may not take part in the Match.&nbsp; Not more than three substitutes of each Club shall warm up at the same time on the perimeter of the pitch on which the Match is being played.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>13</STRONG>&nbsp;(a) The Captain, accompanied by the person in charge of the team on the day (i.e. the Manager or Assistant Manager if present), must hand copies of a list of names of Players taking part in the game (including the names of the nominated substitutes) to the Referee and a representative of their opponents,&nbsp; in the presence of the Referee in his dressing room at least 60 (sixty) minutes before the advertised time of kick-off.&nbsp; This list shall indicate the full names of Players and shall indicate the colour of the Goalkeeper’s shirt.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(b) Should any nominated player or substitute sustain an injury or become otherwise incapacitated after the submission of the team sheet to the Referee and before the kick-off, he may be replaced; the Referee shall be informed.&nbsp; Should there be any other extenuating circumstances which require a nominated player or substitute to be replaced during the aforementioned period, the Referee shall allow such replacement and report the full circumstances to the Secretary. The Secretary shall then initiate any inquiries he may deem fit so as to satisfy himself as to the bona fide nature of the replacement.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(c) The Players’ shirts and shorts must be clearly numbered in accordance with the Rules and Regulations of the Football League and Conference respectively, and a list handed to the Referee before any Competition match. </DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(d) The Captain shall wear a distinguishing armband to indicate his status.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(e) For all matches Clubs shall be required to take the field of play together preceded by the match officials not less than 5 (five) minutes before the advertised time of kick-off.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>14</STRONG>&nbsp;(a) When away from home Clubs will play in colours (shirts, shorts and stockings) which are clearly distinguishable from those of their opponents.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();>(b) No Club shall be permitted to play in shirts, the colour of which is likely to cause confusion with the outfit worn by the Match Officials.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>15</STRONG>&nbsp;The Secretary of the Competition shall be responsible for the appointment of Officials to all matches.</DIV><DIV onclick=parent.editInsertedImage();><BR><STRONG>16</STRONG>&nbsp;All provisions of The Standing Orders referred to in the Regulations of The Football League or as amended by the Board relating to Referees and Assistant Referees shall apply.<BR>&nbsp; <BR><STRONG>17</STRONG>&nbsp;A Trophy will be presented to the winners of the Competition and suitable souvenirs will be presented to the winners and runners-up.&nbsp; There shall be twenty-one souvenirs to the winning Club in the Final Tie and twenty-one souvenirs to the losing Club in the Final Tie – sixteen for the players nominated, one each for the Secretary, Manager and Physiotherapist and two for staff other than players.&nbsp;
