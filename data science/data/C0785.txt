<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><P><br><font face="Verdana" size="1">Some constabularies will only record details and carry out initial investigations in the following circumstances:<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where a person is killed or injured.<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where one or more drivers have failed to stop.<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where the police consider there is sufficient evidence<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;to support a prosecution of one or more parties.<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where a driver has obviously lost control.<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If damage is caused to any road sign or crash barrier.<br><br><IMG alt="" border="0" height="12" src="images/arrow.gif" width="8">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where a vehicle defect has significantly contributed to the<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;accident.</font></P></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><font face="Verdana" size="1"><b>What insurance details will the police require?</b></font></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><P><font face="Verdana" size="1"><br>The policy number and the address of the drivers insurance company is all that is generally required. These details are contained on every insurance certificate.<br><br>As a driver you must give your insurance details to anyone who has been injured or to anyone who wishes to make a claim against you, even though you may feel you are not to blame for the accident. Likewise, you may ask for another persons insurance if you have been injured or you wish to make a claim against them.<br><br>Failure to provide insurance details in these circumstances may constitute to an offence, which should be reported to the police. The police will only record insurance details where a person has been injured in a road accident.<br><br>Your insurance company should be informed of any accident you are involved in and will offer assistance and advice to you.</font></P></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><font face="Verdana" size="1"><b>If my vehicle is damaged in an accident, who is responsible for moving it?</b></font></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><P><font face="Verdana" size="1"><br>If you are a member of a motoring organisation they will arrange removal. Alternatively a garage nominated by you or a garage contacted via the police will remove the vehicle. The cost of removal is the responsibility of the owner and/or driver.</font></P></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><font face="Verdana" size="1"><b>Will any driver be prosecuted?</b></font></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><P><font face="Verdana" size="1"><br>The police do not apportion blame nor investigate accidents on behalf of insurance companies. The police will only further investigate those accidents involving fatalities or serious injuries and those which involve an element of aggressive behaviour, impatient driving, or the misuse of speed.<br><br>Any incident where a prosecution is decided upon will be forwarded to the Crown Prosecution Service who will consider whether there is sufficient evidence to prosecute and whether or not it is in the public interest to do so.<br><br>Some constabularies operate a Driver Improvement Scheme whereby any driver who has driven carelessly, contributing to or causing an accident may be offered a one and a half days driving course of refresher training instead of prosecution.<br><br>This initiative does not affect an insurance claim, but will clearly address a probable cause of the accident. Rather than punishing the offender it ultimately reduces the likelihood of a careless driver being involved in a similar accident.
</font></P></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><font face="Verdana" size="1"><b>What will the police do, after the accident?</b></font></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><P><font face="Verdana" size="1"><br>If the accident is recorded, the police will always tell you of the result and if they propose to take any further action. Depending on the circumstances, the result may not be known for several months. If you need to enquire about any relevant matter relating to the accident, telephone the nearest station, leaving at least five working days after the date of the accident.</font></P></TD>
