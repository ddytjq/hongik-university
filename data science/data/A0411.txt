	<img src="img/structuredfinance.gif" width="182" height="24" alt="structured finance" border="0">
	<img src="img/glossaryofterms.gif" width="185" height="24" alt="glossary of terms" border="0"><P>

	<table bgcolor="#FFFFFF" width="100%" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="3" border="1" bordercolor="#E4CC8B" bordercolorlight="#E4CC8B" bordercolordark="#8F4444">
			<tr>
				<td class=topnav bgcolor="#E4CC8B">Term</td>
				<td class=topnav bgcolor="#E4CC8B">Description</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Alphabet Loans/Notes</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Where more than one loan is used, they may be designated as "A", "B", "C" etc. Loans/Notes. Alternatively they may be described as 1st Senior, Second Senior etc. Such loans will rank equally but will have different repayment periods.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Bridging Loan</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>A loan used for a short period where there is a certainty of repayment from a specific event e.g. sale of fixed assets.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Bullet</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Repayment of debt in one amount at the end of a fixed period.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>CFADS</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Cash Flow available for Debt Servicing i.e. EBITDA after adjusting for working capital movements, capital expenditure, fixed asset disposals and tax.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Committed/ Uncommitted</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Committed facilities are only repayable at the end of a specific period e.g. 3/5 years whilst uncommitted facilities are repayable on demand (e.g. an overdraft).</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>EBIT/PBIT</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Earnings Before Interest and Tax/Profit Before Interest and Tax</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>EBITDA</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Evergreen</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>A facility with no fixed repayment profile.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Financial Risks</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Risks pertaining to the funding structure such as adverse interest or currency rate movements.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>High Yield Debt/Junk Bonds</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Fixed high rate tradable debt which is usually unsecured.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Institutional Loan Stock</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Debt put in by venture capitalists which may/may not be secured and which bankers regard as quasi-equity.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Junior Debt</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Debt which ranks alongside Senior Debt but is repaid later. Also may be referred to as a "B or C Note" or "Second/Third Senior" if more than one debt instrument is used. The boundaries between Junior and Mezzanine can be blurred&#33;</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Mezzanine Debt</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Debt which ranks and is repaid after Senior/Junior Debt but before institutional loan stock. Generally carries an option/warrant or redemption fee which tends to distinguish it from Junior Debt.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>P/E</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Price/Earnings multiple i.e. the number of years' post tax profit that the business is being valued at.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>PIK</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Payment In Kind - a proportion of interest which is accrued on mezzanine debt and paid either on a certain date or when the business is sold, whichever occurs first.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>PIYC</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Pay If You Can - similar to PIK but only paid if certain cash flow trigger levels are reached.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Revolver/ Revolving Credit</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>A facility which is generally drawn in minimum tranches for specific periods and is available for redrawing during the commitment period. Used for working capital and bridging/project finance and in most syndicated transactions.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Securitised Loans</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Fixed rate debt which is packaged and traded.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Senior Debt</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>"Vanilla" Debt with first ranking priority and repayment. Also may be referred to as an "A Note" or "First Senior" if more than one debt instrument is used.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Subordinated Debt</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Debt which ranks after other forms of debt. e.g. institutional loan stock is subordinated to Senior, Junior and Mezzanine debt. </td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
    			<td class=body valign=top><b>Working Capital</b></td>
				<td class=body valign=top>Funds which are generally tied up in the stock/debtors of the business. A true working capital facility is usually expected to go into credit for certain specified times during the financial year.</td>
			</tr>

	</table>
