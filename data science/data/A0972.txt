                              <A name=works></A><b>How Citibank repayment protection insurance works </b> 
                              <p><b>Life Cover </b></p>
                              <p>In the event of your death, the outstanding balance on your Citibank Credit Card account, less any arrears, will be paid up to a maximum of £25,000.</p>
                              <p><b>Accident, Sickness &amp; Unemployment Cover</b></p>
                              <p>Citibank Repayment Protection Insurance pays 10% of your outstanding balance each month (calculated on the outstanding balance of your account prior to the inception of the claim) if you are off work for at least 30 days in a row due to an accident, sickness or unemployment. Benefits will be paid to your account at monthly intervals until you return to work, 12 monthly benefits have been paid or cover terminates.</p>
                              <p>Successive periods of accident or sickness or successive periods of unemployment will be treated as one claim if there are less than three consecutive months of employment between them. If a period of accident or sickness is immediately followed by a period of unemployment, or vice versa, it will also be treated as one claim. You cannot claim for accident, sickness and unemployment at the same time. A maximum of 12 monthly payments will be made for any one claim and once this has been received you will not be able to claim again until you have returned to employment for a continuous period of at least three months.</p>
                              <p>If you have more than one job, you must lose all jobs in order to claim unemployment benefit.</p>
                              <p>Under current UK legislation all benefits payable are not subject to personal taxation with the exception of any benefit payable if you die which may be subject to inheritance tax</p>
