            <p class="contentsubhead">Retirement Planning</p>
            <p> Planning for retirement is now even more important
              than it ever was, especially when you consider what is happening
              to the State pension. It has been calculated that, if it increase
              in line with prices, the value will be reduced by one third in real
              terms by the year 2020. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Starting your own pension</p>
            <p>These days we are
              all living longer and healthier lives, and it is important that
              when we get older we have the resources to enjoy ourselves. After
              all, years of working should turn into years of relaxing, and it
              is important you have the type of pension to achieve what you want.
              It could be you want to retire early and take that trip of a lifetime
              you've always wanted, or simply want to make sure you have sufficient
              funds to continue doing what you feel like doing. After all, it
              is true that some people go on to spend as many years in retirement
              as they did at work. On that basis, any income earned in a year
              when working has to provide support for two years: one year at work
              and one in retirement. So, planning ahead is important and Yorkshire
              Bank can help. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Topping up your company pension</p>
            <p>If you have the opportunity to join a company
              pension, then it is usually a good idea to join it. What you must
              ensure, however, is that it will provide enough for you to live
              on when your retire, especially if you've ever changed employment,
              joined a scheme later in life, or if your contributions are made
              with only part of your full income. In these cases, it is important
              that the amount is topped up in some way. </p>
            <p>Knowing exactly what to do can be complex,
              but here too, your Personal Banker will be happy to help you find
              the right solution. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Pensions for women</p>
            <p>When you consider that, on average, women live
              up to five years longer than men, it makes good sense that any woman
              who is eligible should start her own pension and as soon as possible.
              There may be a smaller amount of income to invest, due to such things
              as a shorter working life caused by bringing up children, but even
              a small amount will mean you will have some sort of personal safety
              net for the future.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Self-employed pensions</p>
            <p>The world of the self-employed carries a lot
              more risks than that of ordinary employment. A lot of the benefits
              available, especially to do with pensions, are not available, so
              special provisions have got to be made. Your Personal Banker can
              help. A flexible pension for example, allows you to make contributions
              that tie in with your seasonal cash flow. In other words, you pay
              more when business is good and pay less in leaner times. There's
              even an option to hold back contributions if finances are ever needed
              elsewhere. What's more you could claim tax relief on your pension
              contributions through your tax return. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">The value of investments may fluctuate
              and is not guaranteed and you may not get back the full amount invested.
              Tax legislation is subject to change. The value of any tax relief
              is dependent on personal circumstances.</p>
