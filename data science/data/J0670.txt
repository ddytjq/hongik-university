        <p> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><b>Racing In The 
          Rain To Open 2002 At Saratoga.</b> <br>
          </font><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"> The rain 
          failed to stop any racing on Saturday night as Saratoga Speedway opened 
          their 22nd season of auto racing with a double point opening race for 
          the <b>Magic 98.9 FM</b> Crash to Pass cars on a night that ended with 
          a brillant tradional speedway <b>fireworks show</b>. <br>
          <br>
          The opening race of the season was also scheduled to be a double point 
          race for the <b>Bomber</b> class, however due to poor track conditions 
          the 19 Bomber cars that made it out raced for trophy's with no points 
          awarded for any races. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1">The <b>Mike Finneron/Campbell 
          River Couier/Courteny Record</b> sponsored night kicked off with a crowd- 
          voted best appearing car in the crash-to-pass class and the big crowd 
          chose Campbell River's <b>General Jeff May </b>in his freshly painted 
          new # 01 car. <br>
          <br>
          The first race of the season saw Nanaimo's <b>Jeff Haslam</b> put his 
          brand new # 4 car into the winner's square in the B Dash, while <b>Mark 
          Haslam</b>, also in a new, car put his # 47 into the square in the A 
          Dash - his first race since losing the 2001 championship to Campbell 
          River's <b>Mark Stephens</b> by a mere 2 points last November. <br>
          <br>
          With a very slick track the first of three contact races of the season 
          would feature many soft hits which saw many spin outs, which in the 
          end turned out to be a good thing as all the cars made it through the 
          night without anyone close to being heavily damaged.<br>
          <br>
          The first of two heat races saw <b>May</b> take the win in a new car, 
          which surprisly isn't a station wagon but much faster, while Nananose 
          Bay's <b>Randy Makowski</b> left an impression on his first visit to 
          Saratoga by winning the 2nd heat, then the 20 lap main event ahead of 
          <b>May</b> and <b>David Crowhurst</b>. <br>
          <br>
          With no points up for grabs and a slick track, a number of the Bomber 
          drivers passed on racing in the rain, while those who did run did a 
          great job of keeping their cars on the track under conditions that made 
          it hard to see when behind another car.<br>
          <br>
          Black Creeks <b>Jeff VonKampen</b> kicked off the 2002 Bomber season 
          with the D dash win, followed by trophy dash wins for <b>Brian Sullivan</b>,<b>Jason 
          Waters</b> and <b>Darren Larsen.<br>
          <br>
          </b> Heat race wins followed for 12 year-old <b>Austin Wallace</b> and 
          <b>Glen Pinto</b>, while <b>Darrell Larsen</b> took the 15 lap main 
          event. <br>
          <br>
          This Saturday night (April 20th) the <b>Bomber</b> cars return with 
          their double point opening race and will be joined by the <b>Stock Cars,</b> 
          who will also be running their double point opener. They will be joined 
          by the two person <b>Road Hogs</b>. <br>
          <br>
          With 19 cars out for a soggy opening night, the car count looks strong, 
          as does the competion. Leading the way is 2001 2nd place finisher <b>B.J 
          Bradley</b> who will be pushed by the likes of <b>Big Rick Martin</b>,<b>Mike 
          Dionne</b> and <b>Chris Beaulieu</b>, who is behind the wheel of the 
          car Brad McLean won the 2001 championship with. <br>
          <br>
          The <b>Stock Car</b> class made a big comeback last season as 15 to 
          20 cars made the field making for some of best the stock car racing 
          action seen at the speedway in over 20 years.This season's class looks 
          to be just as impressive as returning drivers include 2001 point champion 
          <b>Martin Beaulieu</b>, <b>Rob Leighton</b> in a new car , <b>Mclean</b> 
          in a car <b>Bruce Nelson</b> won the 99 championship with, <b>David 
          Mainse</b> a 3 time speedway champ, while <b>Rob Ryan </b>and <b>Mike</b> 
          and <b>Jim Meers</b> make their return to the class. <br>
          <br>
          Racing action this Saturday night features the <b>Bombers</b>, <b>Stock 
          Cars</b> and <b>Road Hogs.</b> New start times for 2002 have the gates 
          open at 5.30 followed by time trails at 6 and racing set for 7pm. <br>
          <br>
          <b>Guest talk</b>.........bad break for Campbell River's <b>Travis Stephenson</b> 
          as the starter in his big # 77 crash to pass station wagon packed it 
          in before he could even time in his car......# 97 <b>Mike Dionne</b> 
          was busy in the off season as he now has <b>Demo Don Lawerence's</b> 
          # 44 Bomber painted blue, and he also has <b>Rob Leighton's</b> # 24 
          Stock car after Leighton had a new car built which has been described 
          to be very impressive looking..........great to be back for yet another 
          season of covering the auto racing beat and it's my hopes to attract 
          more race fans to what IS the # 1 sport on the north island and is fully 
          worth surporting.</font></p>
