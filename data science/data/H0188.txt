Genetics</B></A></CENTER></TD>
<TD WIDTH="69%" VALIGN="TOP"><P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://nitro.biosci.arizona.edu/zbook/volume_2/vol2.html">Evolution
and Selection of Quantitative Traits</A>. Bruce Walsh, University of Arizona.
This is volume 2 of a two volume work on quantitative genetics and deals
with population genetics. These pages are preliminary drafts for a book
and will probably only be around until the book is published. The chapters
are arranged as a series of Adobe Acrobat files (pdf). The material is extremely
technical, but the book is an incredible resource for the web. Highly recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.ag.auburn.edu/~mwooten/form1.html">Population
Genetics Question Page</A>. Michael Wooten, Auburn University. Here's your
chance to ask an expert a question about population genetics or genetics.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.scar.utoronto.ca/~thompson/course/randomdrift/index.html">Population
Genetics, Random Genetic Drift</A>. Paul Thompson, University of Toronto.
A slide show on the mathematical theory of genetic drift.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.zoology.ubc.ca/~whitlock/bio434/LectureNotes/LectureNotes.html">Biology
434. Population Genetics</A>. Michael Whitlock, University of British Columbia.
A really excellent set of lecture notes and illustrations for a population
genetics course. The lectures begin with an introduction to the basics of
population genetics and move on to random genetic drift, selection, population
structure, and quantitative genetics, to name but a few of the topics. Highly
recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.cc.ndsu.nodak.edu/instruct/mcclean/plsc431/popgen/popgen1.htm">Population
and Evolutionary Genetics</A>. Philip McClean, North Dakota State University.
A nice introduction to the principles of population genetics and natural
selection. Some topics covered are variability, selection, the Hardy-Weinberg
equation, speciation, and evolutionary genetics. Recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.bioweb.uncc.edu/faculty/leamy/popgen/popgen.htm">Biology
4000 - Population Genetics</A>. Larry Leamy, University of North Carolina
at Charlotte. Lecture handouts on genetic drift, inbreeding, and effective
population size.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.netspace.org/MendelWeb/">Mendel Web</A>. Roger
B. Blumberg. Although not technically population genetics, this web sites
contains the seminal paper &quot;Experiments in Plant Hybridization&quot;,
the starting point for the modern study of genetics. The site also has a
series of essays on Mendel and some resources for genetics research.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.zoology.ubc.ca/~whitlock/gradevol.html">Population
and Quantitative Genetics</A>. S. Otto et al., University of British Columbia.
A synoptic and technical, but fairly complete introduction to population
and quantitative genetics. Part of a course at the University of British
Columbia. To get to the good stuff, choose &quot;Overheads&quot;.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.stat.wisc.edu/wright/wright/">Who was Sewall
Wright</A>. Seawall Wright Intitute of Quantitative Biology and Evolution.
A short biography and related material on one of the fathers of population
genetics.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.cs.unc.edu/~plaisted/ce/genetics.html">Population
Genetics Made Simple</A>. David A. Plaisted. This site is an introduction
to some of the more interesting topics in population genetics without the
math. Most of the article is devoted to rates of evolution.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://pespmc1.vub.ac.be/MATHMPG.html">Mathematical Models
of Population Genetics</A>. Principia Cybernetica Web. A highly technical
introduction to some mathematical models in population genetics.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://library.advanced.org/19037/population.html">Population
Genetics</A>. The Gene School. A non-technical introduction to population
genetics. There is a general, simple introduction to population genetics
as well as some games to be played and descriptions of some of the applications
of population genetics in real life.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://earth.agu.org/revgeophys/buckli01/node2.html">Fundamental
Science: Population Genetics of Marine Zooplankton</A>. American Geophysical
Union. An introduction to population genetics with special emphasis on marine
zooplankton.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.uwinnipeg.ca/~simmons/popgen/">The Evolution
of Populations</A>. University of Winnipeg Biology Department. A series
of slides concentrating on the Hardy-Weinberg equation with a little information
on selection.</B></TD></TR>
