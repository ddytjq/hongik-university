  &nbsp;<p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 4" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>England's
  qualification group in 1962 consisted of only Luxembourg and Portugal and we
  won all four games scoring 16 goals and conceding only 2.&nbsp; Once in
  Chile, we narrowly lost 2-1 to the great Hungarians.&nbsp; A defeat against
  Argentina in game two would have sent us quickly home from the tournament.&nbsp; A
  determined performance was needed and indeed delivered by the players, goals
  from Flowers, Charlton and Greaves gave us the much needed 3-1 win and our
  progression chances were resurrected. In the final game, England only needed a point to
  reach the next round, the game was later to be described as the worst of
  the competition as a 0-0 draw was played out in front of a very small crowd
  chanting &quot;Malo, Malo&quot; (meaning terrible).&nbsp; In round 2, England
  travelled to Vina Mel Mar for a showdown with Brazil.&nbsp; We matched the world
  champions in every department and after a goal from
  Hitchens, the match was nicely poised at 1-1.&nbsp; But eventual winners
  Brazil went on to win the game 3-1 with goals from Garrincha(2) and
  Vava.&nbsp;</b></font>
