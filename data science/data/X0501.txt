				<p class="indent20"><font class="normaltour"><script language="javascript">writeToursMenu (162)</script><b>England won the toss and elected to bat. The tourists were all out for 255 in 49.1 overs. India were all out for 250 in 49.5 overs. England win the match by five runs, the series is tied 3-3<br><br></b>Teams:<br>India: Ganguly (capt), Tendulkar, Mongia, Kaif, Sehwag, Badani, Ratra, Kumble, Agarkar, Srinath, H Singh<br>England: Hussain (capt), Trescothick, Knight, Flintoff, Thorpe, Vaughan, Collingwood, Foster, Giles, Caddick, Gough<br><br>In a capacity crowd Wankhede stadium, Nasser Hussain won the toss and opted to bat. The England team remained unchanged from the one that was victorious in Delhi, whilst India drafted in Harhajan Singh to replace Sarandeep Singh. <br><br><b>England innings<br></b>India got off to an explosive start in the first over, Javagal Srinath rocking the tourists with some incisive bowling. The England opener Marcus Trescothick escaped on the very first ball of the innings, Hemand Badani dropping a straightforward catch after a teasing delivery from Srinath. <br><br>The early breakthrough that India always threatened finally materialized, with Knight falling victim to another fine delivery by Srinath. The opener edged behind for Ratra to make an easy catch and see England slump to 1-1.<br><br>Nasser Hussain led a combative fight-back after Knight&#8217;s departure, as England looked to build on Thursday&#8217;s encouraging performance. This they did with conviction, Hussain and Trescothick playing some exquisite shots as they raced to 88-2 before the skipper fell to Ganguly. Hussain went for the slog, only to miscue it into the hands of Harbhajan who took an assured catch on the boundary. <br><br>Despite the loss of their captain for 41, England continued in the same vein, picking off the Indian seamers with ease. Agarkar was enduring a torrid evening; in one over he bowled three wides, such was the pressure exerted by the England batsman. <br><br>Despite the loss of Vaughan for a modest 16 after some quick thinking by Ratra behind the stumps, Trescothick kept the run-rate ticking over in emphatic fashion.<br><br>With 22 overs remaining and 172-3 on the board, an impressive total was looming. Up stepped Harbhajan to almost immediate effect. A devastating spell of spin bowling from the youngster turned the game on its head, as India looked to avenge the demoralizing defeat three days earlier. <br><br>The wicket of England&#8217;s talismanic batsman Graham Thorpe for just six runs began the rout, Ratra claiming his second stumping of the evening after the batsman was deceived by a teasing delivery. <br><br>Four balls later, Trescothick was caught and bowled by the same man to leave the opener stranded five short of his century. Trescothick departed the field with impressive figures of 95 off 80 balls &#8211; an inspired knock and one that should further cement his place in the team for the forthcoming Zimbabwe series.<br><br>The Turbanator was on a roll and new man Paul Collingwood was able to offer limited resistance, brilliantly caught by a diving Virender Sehwag at mid-on after adding just two. Ashley Giles was also trapped by the wily spinner, lasting only two more deliveries before he edged to Sehwag at slip for a duck. <br><br>The 21-year-old James Foster helped ease the pressure, lasting all of 28 balls before man-of-the-moment Harbhajan caught and bowled the youngster to claim his maiden ODI five-wicket haul. It was a compelling performance from Singh who single-handedly turned round a floundering Indian bowling attack.<br><br>Andrew Flintoff confirmed his reputation as a big-hitter, adding an element of steel to England&#8217;s batting attack that was sorely lacking in the middle-order. <br><br>A quick-fire 40 off 50 balls, ably supported by Andy Caddick and then Darren Gough, helped spur the England total to the realms of respectability. Caddick&#8217;s dismissal for seven by Tendulkar after he spooned a shot to Kumble failed to deter the stocky all-rounder, who fed off some loose balls and took England&#8217;s total past the 250 mark. <br><br>In the final over and with the score on 255, Flintoff was eventually dismissed after he looped a ball high to Singh in the covers.<br><br>England set India a winning target of 256.<br><br><br><b>India innings<br></b>The Little Master started in typically boisterous fashion, wowing the Bombay crowd with some stylish strokeplay. <br><br>But then up stepped Gough to silence the crowd in emphatic fashion. <br><br>Tendulkar flapped at a delivery, edging the ball into the dependable hands of Foster behind the stumps. With Tendulkar stranded on 12, it was a vital breakthrough and temporarily lifted the tourists&#8217; hopes of curbing the relentless run flow. <br><br>Undeterred by his partner&#8217;s misfortune, Virender Sehwag continued in scintillating fashion, making the England attack of Gough and Caddick look ordinary. With the first seven overs going for over 50 runs, India were cruising and Hussain&#8217;s men were growing increasingly frustrated. <br><br>Sehwag&#8217;s departure on 31, after being caught off the bowling of Caddick, did little to ease the tourists&#8217; concerns that this game was quickly slipping away from them. With Ganguly firing on all cylinders, only the introduction of Michael Vaughan helped stem the Indian tide. <br><br>The duel attack of Flintoff and Vaughan proved crucial. The pair were able to frustrate the hosts with some tight bowling, as England looked to gain back the initiative. New-man Mongia was undone by an inviting delivery from Vaughan which he looked to slog but missed. Foster was on hand to exact the punishing blow, and Mongia walked on 35. <br><br>Ganguly&#8217;s departure on 80 was the first scalp of the day for Ashley Giles who was struggling to recapture the sort of form that he demonstrated so effectively in Delhi. A somewhat fortuitous wicket, Ganguly edged the ball behind only to see it dribble onto his stumps. The Indian skipper could count himself unlucky after a commendable score that saw eight boundaries, including four sixes.<br><br>England were beginning to sense an opening and under the bright lights of the Wankhede Stadium, India began to wilt. Youngster Mohammed Kaif had a rush of blood to the head, launching a straightforward Flintoff delivery skywards for Hussain to claim.<br><br>Ratra&#8217;s time at the crease was short-lived, caught by Giles on eight after sweeping Vaughan to square leg. After a miserable day with the ball, Agarkar&#8217;s luck with the bat eluded him as well. Caddick was the executioner this time, firing down a bouncer that Agarkar nicked into the air for Foster to make an easy catch.  <br><br>Singh capitulated next on five, caught by Collingwood off the bowling of Flintoff.  Needing 11 off the last over to overhaul England's 255, the all-rounder was also on cue to see off the challenge of Srinath with one ball remaining. <br><br>An impromptu strip-show by Flintoff, ripping his shirt off in jubilation as the last wicket fell, epitomized just how much this victory meant to the young touring side. Watching from the sidelines, Ganguly kicked a bottle in disgust as victory eluded his side once more. At 3-3, India will be sorely disappointed. <br><br><b>India were all out for 250. England win the sixth one-day international by five runs and draw the series 3-3.</b></font></p>
