<!--msthemelist--></table><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">
<p>Where you one of the thousands of frustrated fans who spent hours on end
getting a sore index finger pressing the redial button on your phone over and
over again vainly trying to buy tickets for the crucial Scotland - England
play-off?</p>
<p>Well it seems that there was no point even trying as it doesn't look like any
of the tickets found their way into the hands of genuine fans trying to get
through on the hotline anyway. The sale of tickets for this vital fixture has
been an absolute debacle featuring scandal after scandal.</p>
<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--><table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
  <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/blends-2/blebul1a.gif" width="15" height="15" hspace="13"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">Glasgow City Council employees are known to have used internal phone lines
    to purchase tickets sold from City Council offices. It is also believed that
    Council employees transferred calls from friends wanting to get through
    through internal system. The estimate for number of tickets bought by
    council officials is <b>3,000 - 4,000 </b>!<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
  <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/blends-2/blebul1a.gif" width="15" height="15" hspace="13"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">Six SFA employees have been suspended after they bypassed the hotline to
    purchase tickets.<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
  <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/blends-2/blebul1a.gif" width="15" height="15" hspace="13"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">Many employees of Glasgow computer firms were able to jump the hotline
    queue by using a special number. Many may have bought tickets several times
    over.<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
  <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/blends-2/blebul1a.gif" width="15" height="15" hspace="13"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">British Telecom technicians used specialist equipment to access the
    hotline to buy over 300 tickets.<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
  <!--msthemelist--><tr><td valign="baseline" width="42"><img src="_themes/blends-2/blebul1a.gif" width="15" height="15" hspace="13"></td><td valign="top" width="100%"><!--mstheme--><font face="Trebuchet MS, Arial, Helvetica">A further 300 tickets were bought by staff at the Scottish Exhibition and
    Conference Centre using a computer with a special link to the ticket centre.<!--mstheme--></font><!--msthemelist--></td></tr>
