<a NAME="JARGON_APR"></a>
<p><b>APR</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Annual Percentage Rate. This is the rate of charge on 
a loan calculated to a set formula. It includes not just 
the rate of interest, but also the associated costs. It 
was introduced to provide a meaningful comparison of rates 
charges.
<a NAME="JARGON_ARRANGEMENT"></a>
<p><b>Arrangement Fee</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A fee charged by a lender to enable a borrower to avail 
of some offers and types of mortgages.
<a NAME="JARGON_BRIDGING_LOAN"></a>
<p><b>Bridging Loan</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A short term loan which is sometimes required if you have 
bought a new home before selling your existing home. The loan 
is paid off when you sell your existing home.
<a NAME="JARGON_CAPITAL"></a>
<p><b>Capital Reduction</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A capital reduction occurs when a borrower makes a lump 
sum payment to reduce the capital amount owed on a mortgage. 
It is sometimes called a part redemption.
<a NAME="JARGON_CASHBACK"></a>
<p><b>Cashback</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Some offers give you a cash lump sum when you arrange 
your mortgage.
<a NAME="JARGON_COLLATERAL"></a>
<p><b>Collateral</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Property or some other asset that is used as security against 
the repayment of a loan.
<a NAME="JARGON_COMPLETION"></a>
<p><b>Completion</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The point at which the mortgage is signed and executed and 
all its conditions come into effect. In the sale and purchase 
of property, it is when the balance of the purchase price is 
paid by the purchaser to the seller and in exchange the seller 
hands over the deeds.
<a NAME="JARGON_CONTRACT"></a>
<p><b>Contract</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Written legal agreement between the buyer and seller.
<a NAME="JARGON_CONVEYANCING"></a>
<p><b>Conveyancing</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The legal work involved in buying and selling property.
<a NAME="JARGON_COUNCIL"></a>
<p><b>Council of Mortgage Lenders</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A trade body representing banks, building societies 
and other mortgage lenders. It provides a forum for 
exchange of views on common issues confronting lenders, 
as well as acting as a powerful lobby group on their behalf.
<a NAME="JARGON_COVENANTS"></a>
<p><b>Covenants</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Promises contained in deeds. They are enforceable. 
There are basically two types - positive and negative. The 
positive covenant requires the person bound by it to do 
something, whereas a negative covenant prevents the person 
from doing something.
<a NAME="JARGON_CREDIT_B"></a>
<p><b>Credit Bureau</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
An organisation which maintains a database on the 
credit history of data subjects in order to advise lenders 
and other interested parties of matters relevant to the 
status of the prospective customer.
<a NAME="JARGON_DEEDS"></a>
<p><b>Deeds</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The legal documents that indicate the owner's legal entitlement 
to a property.
<a NAME="JARGON_DEPOSIT"></a>
<p><b>Deposit</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A proportion of the purchase price that the buyer usually pays 
when the contracts are exchanged.
<a NAME="JARGON_EASEMENT"></a>
<p><b>Easement</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Rights of the owner of land over the land of another, such 
as a right of way or a right of light.
<a NAME="JARGON_EQUITY"></a>
<p><b>Equity</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The market value of your home minus any outstanding loans
secured on it.
<a NAME="JARGON_EXCHANGE_CONTRACTS"></a>
<p><b>Exchange of Contracts</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The contract between buyer and seller. When the contract is 
signed both parties are bound to complete the purchase and sale 
of the property.
<a NAME="JARGON_FIXED"></a>
<p><b>Fixed Rate</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
This fixes the interest rate over an agreed period of 
time so during this period you know exactly what your 
repayments will be.
<a NAME="JARGON_FREEHOLD"></a>
<p><b>Freehold</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
When a property is freehold, you own both the property and land 
it is built on.
<a NAME="JARGON_FULL_S"></a>
<p><b>Full Structural Survey</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A comprehensive report by a qualified surveyor on the 
condition of a property to be purchased. The survey can be 
expensive but provides peace of mind as all matters affecting 
the value and condition of the property are reported on.
<a NAME="JARGON_GROUND_RENT"></a>
<p><b>Ground Rent</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
When a property is leasehold this is the annual rent paid to a 
freeholder.
<a NAME="JARGON_GUARANTOR"></a>
<p><b>Guarantor</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A person who promises to be answerable for the debt of another
person.
<a NAME="JARGON_HOME_BUYER"></a>
<p><b>Home Buyers Report</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A report by a qualified surveyor on the condition 
of a property which is being considered for purchase. 
This is less thorough than a full structural survey but 
provides reasonably detailed information at a slightly 
higher outlay than a basic valuation for mortgage purposes.
<a NAME="JARGON_INCOME"></a>
<p><b>Income Multiple</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The factor by which lenders will multiply the gross annual 
salary of mortgage applicants in order to determine the maximum 
borrowing capability.
<a NAME="JARGON_INTEREST_ONLY"></a>
<p><b>Interest Only Mortgage</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The capital is not repaid during the mortgage term by 
monthly repayments. The monthly repayment is for interest 
applicable to your loan. The capital borrowed will need to 
be repaid at the end of the term and this is usually provided 
for by an investment policy. There is a risk that the mortgage 
amount may not be paid in full by the investment policy.
<a NAME="JARGON_LEASEHOLD"></a>
<p><b>Leasehold</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A form of land tenure where a person has rights over a piece 
of land for a specific period. You own the property but pay 
ground rent annually for a fixed number of years.
<a NAME="JARGON_LTV"></a>
<p><b>LTV</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
This stands for 'Loan to Value' and is the loan amount as a 
percentage of the value of the property e.g. a £75,000 mortgage 
on a house valued at £90,000 has a LTV of 83%.
<a NAME="JARGON_MORTGAGE_DEED"></a>
<p><b>Mortgage Deed</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The legally binding contract between the lender and the 
borrower. The main type of mortgage deed used is a legal charge.
<a NAME="JARGON_MORTGAGE_PRO"></a>
<p><b>Mortgage Protection</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
An inexpensive type of diminishing term life assurance 
designed to provide a lump sum in the event of death. The sum is 
usually just sufficient to discharge the mortgage.
<a NAME="JARGON_MORTGAGEE"></a>
<p><b>Mortgagee</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The lender.
<a NAME="JARGON_MORTGAGOR"></a>
<p><b>Mortgagor</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The borrower.
<a NAME="JARGON_NHBC"></a>
<p><b>NHBC</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The National House Building Council which provides 
a defect protection scheme on new houses. Membership of the 
NHBC is conditional on the builder maintaining certain minimum 
standards of construction.
<a NAME="JARGON_NEGATIVE"></a>
<p><b>Negative Equity</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Where the loan amount outstanding exceeds the market value of 
the property held as security.
<a NAME="JARGON_OFFER"></a>
<p><b>Offer of advance</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The formal statement by a lender to a mortgage applicant 
of the amount that it is prepared to lend and their terms and 
conditions attached to this. All offers are issued conditionally 
and therefore not binding on the lender.
<a NAME="JARGON_REDEMPTION"></a>
<p><b>Redemption</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The act of paying off a loan.
<a NAME="JARGON_REMORTGAGE"></a>
<p><b>Remortgage</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A mortgage which is a replacement loan for another 
mortgage - i.e. moving your mortgage without moving house.
<a NAME="JARGON_REPAYMENT_MORTGAGE"></a>
<p><b>Repayment Mortgage</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Designed to pay off your loan over an agreed period of time. 
Monthly repayments consist partly of repayments of capital 
borrowed and partly of the interest on that sum.
<a NAME="JARGON_RETENTION"></a>
<p><b>Retention</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A condition of the mortgage where the lender holds back a 
portion of the advance pending work to be carried out by the
borrower.
<a NAME="JARGON_SEARCHES"></a>
<p><b>Searches</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
Searches are usually undertaken by the solicitor acting for 
the purchaser. For registered land he will search the Land 
Registry and for unregistered land he will search the Land Charges 
Registry. There are several other searches that can be undertaken 
such as local land charges. The purpose is to establish whether 
there are any encumbrances or concerns which may adversely affect 
the value of the property which might cause the purchaser not to 
buy.
<a NAME="JARGON_STAMP_DUTY"></a>
<p><b>Stamp Duty</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A government tax payable on the purchase of a property - a
range of rates apply, depending on the purchase price of the 
property.
<a NAME="JARGON_TERM"></a>
<p><b>Term</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The duration of the mortgage.
<a NAME="JARGON_TITLE"></a>
<p><b>Title</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The ownership of a property.
<a NAME="JARGON_TRANSFER"></a>
<p><b>Transfer of a Mortgage</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The process of transferring the rights of the lender to 
another lending institution. This is governed by a code of 
practice and requires the consent of the borrower.
<a NAME="JARGON_UNDERTAKING"></a>
<p><b>Undertaking</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
A condition of a mortgage where the borrower is obliged to 
carry out certain works within a specified period of time following 
completion of the mortgage.
<a NAME="JARGON_VALUATION"></a>
<p><b>Valuation</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The independent assessment of the property carried out by an 
approved valuer to determine its suitability for mortgage 
purposes only. It is paid for by the borrower but used by the 
lender to decide the amount they are prepared to lend.
<a NAME="JARGON_VENDOR"></a>
<p><b>Vendor</b>
<a HREF="http://www.ulsterbank.com/new/ni/hm/ni_hm_jargon_buster.asp#JARGON_TOP"><span><font style="color: #000066; "><u> (Back to Jargon Buster)</u></font></span>
</a>
<br>
The person who is selling the property.
