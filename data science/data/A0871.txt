      <p>These are some other costs involved in buying a house. You must remember 
        these when you are working out how much you can afford to borrow. </p>
      <p class="bodyBold">valuation fee </p>
      <p>Before any mortgage can be agreed we need a <span class="bodyBold">mortgage 
        valuation report.</span> We can arrange this for you, using our panel 
        of qualified surveyors. You will be charged for this work. </p>
      <p>The <span class="bodyBold">mortgage valuation report</span> is not a 
        survey, but it does identify obvious defects. If you wish, you can have 
        a more detailed <span class="bodyBold">house or flat buyers report and 
        valuation</span>, which reports on the general condition of the property.</p>
      <p> If you want to buy a very old or unusual property, then you might want 
        to have a more comprehensive <span class="bodyBold">structural survey</span> 
        done. Sometimes we need a more detailed survey in order to decide whether 
        to give you a mortgage, and we will charge you for the work involved.  In any event you may wish to obtain a structural survey in respect of your property as it will provide you with more detailed information about the property and any potential problems which may be faced.</p>
      <p class="bodyBold"> legal fees </p>
      <p>The legal work transfers the ownership of a property from one person 
        to another. It is called conveyancing, and is usually done by a solicitor. 
        This can be one of the biggest costs involved in buying a house.</p>
      <p> Most solicitors charge according to how much the property is worth. 
        You should always get a quote before you instruct a solicitor. </p>
      <p>There are other legal fees that you'll have to pay to do with preparing 
        the documentation that provides us with security over the property (called 
        a <span class="bodyBold">legal charge</span> in England, a <span class="bodyBold">standard 
        security</span> in Scotland, and a <span class="bodyBold">mortgage</span> 
        in Northern Ireland), and any other security required. </p>
      <p>We will try and use the same solicitor as you used for conveyancing in 
        order to minimise the cost.</p>
      <p class="bodyBold"> stamp duty </p>
      <p>This is a government tax applied when you buy a property for £60,001 
        or more. </p>
