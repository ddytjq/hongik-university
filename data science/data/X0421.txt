	<td width="75%">
		<b>How did a Hell's Angel register his guns?                                                           </b><br>
		<font size=2>Author: Gary Ball                                          <br>
		Source: The Sudbury Star                                                                                                                                      </font>
	</td>

	<td align=right VALIGN=TOP>		
				Fri.           May 17, 2002
	</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td colspan=2><br>
		<br>
				<P align=justify>Heard the latest out of the nation's capital?</P>
<P align=justify>No?</P>
<P align=justify>Well, would you believe that outlaws (at least one) do register guns, apparently legally and apparently without a whimper from the bureaucratic mess that is the Canadian Firearms Registry.</P>
<P align=justify>Alliance MP Gary Breitkreuz has revealed that Maurice "Mom" Boucher, top dog in Hell's Angels, which cops call Canada's biggest outlaw biker gang, had proper registration for three shotguns and a 9 mm handgun found in his home. </P>
<P align=justify>How can it happen that the $600 million (and counting) boondoggle of gun registration can harass millions of blameless duck hunters and target shooters, but can't keep gun licences out of the hands of high-profile criminals like Boucher? Even before his current murder convictions he had a long criminal record which included sexual assault involving firearms, violent offences and firearms offences.. </P>
<P align=justify>Surely there cannot be a firearms official or police official from Come by Chance to Comox who has not heard of the bloody biker war waged over Quebec by Boucher and his bandits? If Boucher can slip through the cracks in the gun laws, what good are those expensive laws? </P>
<P align=justify>Day after day, week after week, MP Breitkreuz continues to hammer away at this government over the firearms fiasco. When most Canadians, many of us in the firearms community, have surrendered in disgust, Breitkreuz just keeps hammering away at stupidity. Is anyone out there listening? </P>
<P align=justify>I love this country. And I am proud of this country. But it is becoming increasingly difficult to have any faith at all in our current federal government. </P>
<P align=justify>In fact, what passes for government in Ottawa today is as bizarre and other-worldly as anything encountered by Lewis Carroll's Alice as she dropped down the rabbit hole to Wonderland or stepped through the looking glass. </P>
<P align=justify>Please, someone, tell me that the government of Canada is only a bad dream and that I will wake up and find sanity restored. Increasingly it appears that Canada's government is reduced to a dictatorship led by Jean Chretien, with all power tightly clenched in his fists, and those annointed to his cabinet. The struggle among his cabinet ministers to stand first in line to succeed him is an ugly spectacle. </P>
<P align=justify>The New Democratic Party edges closer and closer to becoming a historical footnote. The playground squabbles of the lame old Progressive Conservative Party and the self-involved strutting of the Alliance have consumed both parties, distracting them from any effective role in opposition to a Liberal monarchy. The Bloc Quebecois, dedicated to the fragmentation of the Canadian union need do little but sit back and watch as the government self-destructs. </P>
<P align=justify>Who would you be comfortable electing as an alternative to the current Liberal government? Who could you comfortably call Mr. Prime Minister? I can't think of anyone either. In addition to the shambles of gun control, there is the joke that passes for new animal welfare legislation at the federal level. </P>
<P align=justify>The new law, Bill C15B, is aimed at stiffening fines and penalties for the deliberate abuse of animals. Nobody in Canada opposes tougher punishments for those who drag dogs to their deaths behind cars or skin live cats as "art projects". </P>
<P align=justify>Farmers, hunters, trappers and medical researchers, however, all have serious concerns about the use of the legislation by animal rights activists. </P>
<P align=justify>Bill C15B follows a long string of failed attempts at better legislation in the forms of failed Bill C17, C15 and C15A. </P>
<P align=justify>All lacked a very simple amendment that would make them acceptable. None will protect legitimate animal use by farmers, hunters, anglers, trappers and scientists from harassment. Liz White, of the Toronto-based Animal Alliance, a front-line "no use of animals" group, crows about her organization's role in Bill C15B in a fund-raising letter: </P>
<P align=justify>"It (the legislation) was viciously opposed by the fur trade, researchers, hunters, agri-business, all those who profit or take pleasure in the harming of animals. Their tactics? Lies, fear-mongering and the vilification of people who have compassion for animals," White wrote in a plea for funds. </P>
<P align=justify>Sophisticated fund-raising? White is quite willing to accept regular predictable monthly contributions to the animal rights cause "either through automatic withdrawal from your bank account or by way of credit card." </P>
<P align=justify>In that January plea for funds White bragged that political lobbying and support of politicians "through reward and punishment - rewarding them for doing a good job and punishing them for a bad one" had won the day and Bill C15B was about to come law. </P>
<P align=justify>Gee. Now the Animal Alliance website is issuing a panic alarm. The problem? Their precious animal rights bill C15B is under attack by the Liberal government's own rural caucus of about 40 MPs who want protecting for farmers and others. Caucus members won't support the bill without changes and are standing up to say so. </P>
<P align=justify>Is it possible that from within the ranks of the Liberal backbenches there is finally arising a semblance of an opposition to the current government? </P>
<P align=justify>Is it possible that Liberal backbenchers have had enough and are willing to start to become the conscience of parliament, indeed, the conscience of their own floundering party? Stay tuned. </P>
<P>Gary Ball can be contacted at <A href="mailto:gball@kawartha.com">gball@kawartha.com</A> </P>
<P>This article was orginally published Thursday May 9, 2002 in <A href="http://www.thesudburystar.com/" target=_blank>The Sudbury Star</A>.</P>
