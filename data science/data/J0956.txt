<h1>Willow Springs Raceway</h1>
<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>
Willow Springs Raceway is a complex which includes a 2.5 mile, 9 turn
road course, a distinct, tighter handling course ("The Streets of
Willow Springs"), an oval track, and a dirt track.
Willow Springs is located near Rosamond, California.
</p>
<p>
"The Streets of Willow Springs" may be used in two different
configurations, an 8 turn course with no straights and an 11
turn course with one straight.  It is primarily used for workshops
and clinics.
</p>

<h2><a name="GettingThere">Getting to Willow Springs Raceway</a></h2>
The track is approximately 65 miles from San Fernando Valley.<br>
From Los Angeles take I-5 North to Hwy 14 North.<br>
Exit at Rosamond Blvd/Edwards AFB, and got West (left).<br>
Continue West for approximately 6 miles.<br>
Track is on your right. Can't miss it.<br>

<h3>Hotels & Motels</h3>
<a href="#AsciiMap">Click Here</a> to see an ascii map of the area showing
hotels.
<p>
None of the hotels are very far from the track.  The Rio Mirada and the
Ramada are pretty similiar.  Both are right on the freeway and thus
>easy to find.  Lancaster/Palmdale has about 200,000 people. Rosamond
has about 10,000.  The Essex House is supposed to be pretty nice, I
have never stayed there, but IMHO It looks about the same as the
others.  The Devonshire Inn is also just off the freeway, but Rosamond
has a serious lack of eating establishments IMHO (Although there is a
Taco Bell.)
<p>
For Lancaster/Palmdale Streets running East West area labeled A through
Z, with A being furthest north.  Each street is app. 1 mile apart.
Streets running Norht South are labeld with numbers ## West/East.  The
East/West divider street is called Division.  Major streets are
multiples of 10 ie 10th West, 20th West, etc and they are app. 1 mile
apart.  It is a very easy town to find your way around.

<h2><a name="OtherStuff">Other Stuff near Willow Springs</a></h2>
<h3>Blackbird Air Park</h3>
Ave P & 30th East.  Only place in the world you can see an SR-71, A-12,
and a D-21 on display.  A-12 is/was the CIA version of the SR-71. D-21
is the still mostly secret drone that was piggy backed on the SR-71.
