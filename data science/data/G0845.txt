<H4>Brief Bio:
</H4>
<P>
Edward L. (Ned) Wright received his AB and PhD degrees from Harvard
University, and was a Junior Fellow in the Society of Fellows.
After teaching in the MIT Physics Department, Professor Wright
has been at UCLA since 1981. 
</P>
<H4>Research Interests:
</H4>

<P>
Prof. Wright is interested in infrared astronomy and 
<A HREF="./cosmolog.htm">cosmology.</A>
He was the PI on the 
Next Generation Sky Survey
(<A HREF="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~wright/NGSS/">NGSS</A>)
Midex proposal.
He has been working on the COsmic Background Explorer 
(<A href="http://space.gsfc.nasa.gov/astro/cobe/cobe_home.html" 
title="Cobe Home Page">COBE</A>)
since 1978.
In 1992 he received the NASA Exceptional
Scientific Achievement Medal for his work on the Cosmic Background
Explorer. 
Prof. Wright is an Interdisciplinary Scientist on the Space InfraRed
Telescope Facility (<A
HREF="http://sirtf.jpl.nasa.gov/sirtf/home.html">SIRTF</A>) Science
Working Group.
He has worked on the SIRTF project since 1976.
Prof. Wright has studied 
<A href="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~wright/dust">fractal dust
grains</A> which are able to absorb and emit efficiently at millimeter
wavelengths, and thus may be an important factor in studies of the
cosmic microwave background.
From 1994-1998, he has served as a Science Editor of The Astrophysical
Journal.  In 1995 he was named the
<A HREF="http://www.igpp.ucla.edu/cseol">CSEOL</A> Distinguished
Scientist of the Year.</P>
<P>Prof. Wright is also working on the <EM>Microwave Anisotropy
Probe</EM> <A href="http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov"> (MAP)</A>.
MAP is a mission
to follow-up the COBE discovery of fluctuations in the early Universe.
It will observe the structure of the Universe 300,000 years after the
Big Bang with better angular resolution than the COBE mission, and thus
be able to detect the seeds of present day superclusters of galaxies.
MAP is scheduled for launch on 30 June 2001.
</P>
