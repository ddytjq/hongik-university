<CENTER><B><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=-1>Gradient
Background</FONT></FONT></FONT></B></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica">&nbsp;&nbsp;</FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Ever wonder how the SETUP.EXE
screen gets its cool shaded background coloring? This color shading is
called dithering, and you can easily incorporate it into your forms. Add
the following routine to a form:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>Sub
Dither(vForm As Form)</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>Dim
intLoop As Integer</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.DrawStyle
= vbInsideSolid</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.DrawMode
= vbCopyPen</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.ScaleMode
= vbPixels</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.DrawWidth
= 2</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.ScaleHeight
= 256</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>For
intLoop = 0 To 255</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>vForm.Line
(0, intLoop)-(Screen.Width, intLoop - 1), RGB(0, 0, 255 -intLoop), B</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>Next
intLoop</FONT></FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>End
Sub</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Now, add to the Form_Activate
event the line&nbsp;</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT COLOR="#000099"><FONT SIZE=-1>Dither
ME</FONT></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>This version creates a fading
blue background by adjusting the blue value</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>in the RGB function. (RGB
stands for Red-Green-Blue.) You can create gradients of other colors instead
of blue by changing the RGB call:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Red:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>RGB(255 - intLoop, 0, 0)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Green:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>RGB(0, 255 - intLoop, 0)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Yellow:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>RGB(255 - intLoop, 255 -
intLoop, 0)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>Purple:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1>RGB(255 - intLoop, 0, 255
- intLoop)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial,Helvetica"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>
