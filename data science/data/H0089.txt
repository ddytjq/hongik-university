<A NAME="supback">
<H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Superficial Back Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="trapezius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Trapezius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>external occipital protuberance
	<LI>along the medial sides of the superior nuchal line
	<LI>ligamentum nuchae (surrounding the cervical spinous processes)
	<LI>spinous processes of C1-T12</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>posterior, lateral 1/3 of clavicle
	<LI>acromion
	<LI>superior spine of scapula</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>elevates scapula
	<LI>upward rotation of the scapula (upper fibers)
	<LI>downward rotation of the scapula (lower fibers)
	<LI>retracts scapula</OL>
<LI>Blood: transverse cervical artery
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>spinal Accessory (XI) (efferent or motor fibers)
	<LI>ventral ramii of C3 & C4 (afferent or sensory fibers)</OL></UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="latissimus dorsi"><FONT SIZE=+1>Latissimus dorsi</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>spinous process of T7-L5
	<LI>upper 2-3 sacral segments
	<LI>iliac crest
	<LI>lower 3 or 4 Ribs</OL>
<LI>Insertion: lateral lip of the intertubercular groove
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adduction of humerus
	<LI>medial rotation of the humerus
	<LI>extension from flexed position
	<LI>downward rotation of the scapula</OL>
<LI>Blood: thoracodorsal artery
<LI>Nerve: thoracodorsal nerve, C6,7,8</UL>



<BR>
<P><A NAME="pectoral"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Pectoral Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="subclavius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Subclavius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: first rib about the junction of bone and cartilage
<LI>Insertion: lower surface of clavicle
<LI>Action: assists in stabilizing the clavicle
<LI>Blood: clavicular branch of thoracoacromial artery
<LI>Nerve: nerve to the subclavius, C5,6</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="pectoralis major"><FONT SIZE=+1>Pectoralis major</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial 1/3 of clavicle
	<LI>anterior aspect of manubrium & length of body of sternum
	<LI>cartilaginous attachments of upper 6 ribs
	<LI>external oblique's aponeurosis</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>lateral lip of bicipital groove to the crest of the greater tubercle
	<LI>clavicular fibers insert more distally; sternal fibers more proximally</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adducts humerus
	<LI>medially rotates humerus
	<LI>flexion of the arm from extension (clavicular portion)</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>pectoralis branch of thoracoacromial artery  (runs with lateral pec. nerve)
	<LI>lateral thoracic artery (lesser supply, and runs with medial pectoral nerve)</OL>
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>lateral pectoral nerve, C5,6,7 to clavicular portion
	<LI>medial pectoral nerve, C8,T1 to sternal portion</OL></UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="pectoralis minor"><FONT SIZE=+1>Pectoralis minor</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: outer surface of ribs 2-5 or 3-5 or 6
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of coracoid process of the scapula
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>depresses & downwardly rotates the scapula
	<LI>assists in scapular protraction from a retracted position
	<LI>stabilizes the scapula</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral thoracic artery
<LI>Nerve: medial pectoral nerve, C8,T1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="shoulder"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Shoulder Girdle Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="levator scapulae"><FONT SIZE=+1>Levator scapulae</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: transverse processes of C1-C3 or C4
<LI>Insertion: superior angle of scapula toward the scapular spine
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>elevates the scapula
	<LI>extends and/or laterally flexes the head</OL>
<LI>Blood: transverse cervical artery
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>nerves off cervical plexus, C3,4
	<LI>dorsal scapular nerve, C5</OL></UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="rhomboid minor"><FONT SIZE=+1>Rhomboid minor</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>spinous process of C7 & T1
	<LI>ligamentum nuchae
	<LI>supraspinous ligament</OL>
<LI>Insertion: medial margin of the scapula at the medial angle
<LI>Action: retract scapula
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>deep branch of transverse cervical artery, OR
	<LI>dorsal scapular artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: dorsal scapular nerve, C5, [C4]</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="rhomboid major"><FONT SIZE=+1>Rhomboid major</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>spinous processes of T2-T5
	<LI>supraspinous ligament</OL>
<LI>Insertion: medial scapula from the scapular spine to the inferior angle
<LI>Action: retract scapula
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>deep branch of transverse cervical artery, OR
	<LI>dorsal scapular artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: dorsal scapular nerve, C5</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="serratus anterior"><FONT SIZE=+1>Serratus anterior</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: fleshy slips from the outer surface of upper 8 or 9 ribs
<LI>Insertion: costal aspect of medial margin of the scapula
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>protract scapula
	<LI>stabilize scapula
	<LI>assists in upward rotation</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>lateral thoracic artery supplies the upper part
	<LI>thoracodorsal artery supplies the lower part</OL>
<LI>Nerve: long thoracic nerve, C5,6,7</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="deltoid"><FONT SIZE=+1>Deltoid</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lateral, anterior 1/3 of distal clavicle
	<LI>lateral boarder of the acromion
	<LI>scapular spine</OL>
<LI>Insertion: deltoid tuberosity of humerus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI> abducts arm
	<LI>flexion and medial rotation (anterior portion)
	<LI>extension and lateral rotation (posterior portion)</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>posterior humeral circumflex artery
	<LI>deltoid branch of thoracoacromial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: axillary nerve, C5,6</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="supraspinatus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Supraspinatus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>supraspinous fossa
	<LI>muscle fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion: uppermost of three facets of the greater tubercle of humerus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>abduction of arm (first 15-20°)
	<LI>stabilizes glenohumeral joint</OL>
<LI>Blood: suprascapular artery (poorly supplied)
<LI>Nerve: suprascapular nerve, C5,6</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="infraspinatus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Infraspinatus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>infraspinous fossa
	<LI>muscle fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion: middle facet of greater tubercle of humerus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>external rotation of the humerus
	<LI>stabilizes the glenohumeral joint</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>suprascapular artery
	<LI>scapular circumflex artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: suprascapular nerve, C5,6</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="teres minor"><FONT SIZE=+1>Teres minor</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: middle half of the scapula’s lateral margin
<LI>Insertion: lowest of three facets of the greater tubercle of humerus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>lateral  rotation of the humerus
	<LI>stabilizes the glenohumeral joint</OL>
<LI>Blood: scapular circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: axillary nerve, C5,6</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="teres major"><FONT SIZE=+1>Teres major</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: inferior, lateral margin of the scapula
<LI>Insertion: crest of lesser tubercle (just medial to the insertion of latissimus dorsi)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>assists in adduction of arm
	<LI>assists in medial rotation of arm
	<LI>assists in extension from an flexed position</OL>
<LI>Blood: thoracodorsal artery
<LI>Nerve: lower subscapular nerve, C5,6</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME ="subscapularis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Subscapularis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: subscapular fossa
<LI>Insertion: lesser tubercle of humerus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>medial rotation of the humerus
	<LI>stabilizes the glenohumeral joint</OL>
<LI>Blood: Branches of subscapular artery
<LI>Nerve: upper & lower subscapular nerves, C5,6</UL>

<A NAME="splenius muscles"><BR><P><H2>Splenius Muscles:</H2>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="splenius capitis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Splenius capitis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lower portion of ligamentum nuchae
	<LI>spinous processes of C3-T3(4)</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>superior nuchal line
	<LI>mastoid process of temporal bone</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilateral contraction: extend head & neck
	<LI>unilateral contraction: rotate and laterally bend head & neck to the contracted (same) side</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="splenius cervicis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Splenius cervicis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: spinous process of T3-T6
<LI>Insertion: posterior tubercles of transverse processes of C2-C4
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilateral contraction: extend head & neck
	<LI>unilateral contraction: rotate and laterally bend head & neck to the
	contracted (same) side</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>



<A NAME="erector spinae muscles"><BR><P><H2>Erector Spinae Muscles</H2>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="iliocostalis lumborum"><FONT SIZE=+1>Iliocostalis lumborum</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: common tendinous origin: (same for all lower erector spinae)
	<OL><LI>sacrum
	<LI>iliac crest
	<LI>spinous processes of lower thoracic & most lumbar vertebrae</OL>
<LI>Insertion: lower border of angles of ribs (5)6-12
<LI>Action: (same for all erector spinae)
	<OL><LI>bilateral:
		<OL TYPE=a><LI>extension of vertebral column
		<LI>maintenance of erect posture (pneumonic = <I>I</I> Like Standing)
		<LI>stabilization of vertebral column during flexion,
		acting in contrast to abdominal muscles and the action of gravity</OL>
	<LI>unilateral:
		<OL TYPE=a><LI>lateral bend to same side
		<LI>rotation to same side
		<LI>opposite muscles contract eccentrically for stabilization</OL></OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>

<A NAME="transversospinal muscles"><BR><P><H2>Transversospinal Muscles</H2>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="semispinalis thoracis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Semispinalis thoracis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: transverse processes of T6-T12 vertebrae
<LI>Insertion: spinous processes of upper thoracic & lower cervical vertebrae
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilaterally extends vertebral column, especially head and neck
	<LI>controls lateral flexion to side opposite contraction (eccentric for
	stability)
	<LI>maintains head posture</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="semispinalis cervicis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Semispinalis cervicis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: transverse processes of T1-T6 vertebrae and can go down to lower thoracic
<LI>Insertion: spinous processes of C2-T5(6)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilaterally extends vertebral column, especially head and neck
	<LI>controls lateral flexion to side opposite contraction (eccentric for
	stability)
	<LI>maintains head posture</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="semispinalis capitus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Semispinalis capitus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>transverse processes of T1-T6
	<LI>articular processes of C4-C7</OL>
<LI>Insertion: between superior & inferior nuchal lines of occipital bone
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilaterally extends vertebral column, especially head and neck
	<LI>controls lateral flexion to side opposite contraction (eccentric for
	stability)
	<LI>maintains head posture</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="multifidus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Multifidus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<UL><LI>cervical region: from articular processes of lower cervical vertebrae
	<LI>thoracic region: from transverse processes of all thoracic vertebrae
	<LI>lumbar region:
		<OL><LI>lower portion of dorsal sacrum
		<LI>PSIS
		<LI>deep surface of tendenous origin of erector spinae
		<LI>mamillary processes of all lumbar vertebrae</OL></UL>
<LI>Insertion: spinous process of all vertebrae extending from L5 - C2 (skipping 1-3 segments)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>bilaterally extends vertebral column
	<LI>controls lateral flexion to side opposite contraction (eccentric for
	stability)
	<LI>unilaterally rotate vertebral bodies (column) to opposite side</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="long rotators"><FONT SIZE=+1>Long rotators</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: transverse process of one vertebra
<LI>Insertion: skips one vertebra to insert on the base of spinous process of vertebra above
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>rotate to opposite side
	<LI>bilateral extension</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="short rotators"><FONT SIZE=+1>Short rotators</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: transverse process of one vertebra
<LI>Insertion: base of spinous process of vertebra immediately above
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>rotate to opposite side
	<LI>bilateral extension</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<A NAME="segmental muscles"><BR><P><H2>Segmental Muscles</H2>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="interspinalis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Interspinalis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: spinous processes of each vertebra
<LI>Insertion: to the spinous process of vertebra immediately above
<LI>Action: extension of the vertebrae segments
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="intertransversi"><FONT SIZE=+1>Intertransversi</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: (A to A and B to B)
	<UL><LI>cervical region:
		<OL TYPE=A><LI>from the anterior tubercle of transverse process
		<LI>from the posterior tubercle of transverse process</OL>
	<LI>thoracic region: (poorly developed)
	<LI>lumbar region:
		<OL TYPE=A><LI>lateral aspect of the transverse process
		<LI>mamillary process</OL></UL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<UL><LI>cervical region:
		<OL TYPE=A><LI>to the anterior tubercle immediately above
		<LI>to the posterior tubercle immediately above</OL>
	<LI>thoracic region: (poorly developed)
	<LI>lumber region:
		<OL TYPE=A><LI>lateral aspect of the transverse process immediately above
		<LI>to the accessory process on the vertebra immediately above</OL></UL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>laterally flexes each respective pair of vertebrae
	<LI>(also eccentric muscle contraction provides stability)</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the aorta
<LI>Nerve: dorsal rami of spinal nerves</UL>
