  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'>Anaheim, CA – June 22, 2002 -- Hometown
  hero Wayne Stanfield, a veteran of every running of the Great Race, became
  the Race’s first 5-time winner when he drove car #1, a 1934 Ford Indy Racer
  through the finish gate today in downtown Anaheim, CA.  Stanfield, a plumber
  from nearby Santa Ana, won the 1987, 1994, 1997 and 2001 editions of the
  world’s most prestigious old-car rally, celebrating its 20th </span><span style='font-size:12.0pt;
  line-height:100%;font-family:Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'>anniversary
  this year.  “This year’s Race had it all:  heat, elevation, everything.  To
  do it again for the fifth time is really incredible,” said Stanfield, who now
  has two wins as a driver and three as a navigator.  Teenager David Dingman of
  Nassau, Bahamas, served as the Stanfield’s navigator for the 2002 Great
  Race.  Driving for the powerful Roush Racing team, the pair won all but 2
  days of the 8-day trek, which began in San Antonio, TX.  Four-time Great Race
  winners, Dick Burdick and Wayne Bell, both 20-year veterans, finished second
  in Pro Division in their Thermon Special, a 1916 Hudson H Speedster.</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'>&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'> The father-son team of Howard and
  Douglas Sharp of Fairport, NY, nursed their 1911 Velie H1 Speedster from San
  Antonio, TX to not only become the oldest car to finish the Great Race, but
  also win the Expert division. The 92-year-old car does a top speed of 65 mph
  and originally cost just $600.  “After 11-years, to nurse this hunk of junk
  across the country and win is an unbelievable feeling,” sobbed Howard.  Their
  margin of victory was just 4 seconds over the second-place car.  The Sharps’
  win means that next year they will advance to the Pro Division, dominated by
  Stanfield.</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'>&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'> Other winners include another father and
  son team, Charlie and Bill Stroud, who drove a 1937 Packard 115 Touring Sedan
  to victory in Sportsman Division; a team from Bedford High School in Bedford
  Iowa, scored their second straight win in X-Cup Division, which is restricted
  to high schools and youth groups competing for scholarship money.  Joey Lange
  and Greg Sicard from Ferdinand, IN, took the Rookie Division in their 1929
  Hudson Convertible.</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'>&nbsp;</span></p>
  <p class=MsoNormal><span style='font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-family:
  Arial;font-weight:bold;language:EN'> The Great Race is a world-class
  rally-race awarding a prize purse of $250,000 to competitors in a field of
  vintage machines valued at over $3-million dollars.  For most of the
  participants, the Race offers the adventure of discovering America – and the American
  people – from a unique perspective.  When the Great Race rolls into town, the
  drivers and navigators bask in celebrity welcomes.</span></p>
