    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="1mightyraja.jpg" alt="1mightyraja.jpg (16825 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="300" HEIGHT="274"></td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 1 - Umatilla Hcp 1000m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Raja Lane led with Mighty Raja as Savage
    Attack went three wide followed by Pray In Vain with Chevita on the rails. Little changed
    in the field of five until the 500m where Mighty Raja edged ahead of Savage Attack and
    Raja Lane as Pray In Vain moved wide to make a run and Chevita was checked off heels.
    Savage Attack and Mighty Raja drew level by the 250m but Raja Lane started to come again
    along the inside with Chevita blocked behind them. Inside the final 50m Mighty Raja fought
    strongly to hold his advantage to win by a half length from Raja Lane and Savage Attack.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Mighty Raja is a 3yo bay gelding by
    Devaraja from Almighty Sister and was ridden by Din Azis. He is trained by David Balfour
    for RA Balfour, N Koorey, E Fatiak, BJ Biasi, K Rodley, J Hayward and RJ Richard. Mighty
    Raja was sold through the inaugural Adelaide Magic Millions sale in 2000 and his half
    brother by Baryshnikov is lot 504 at this years sale.</font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="2tiajane.jpg" alt="2tiajane.jpg (16631 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="300" HEIGHT="270"></td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 2 - Greig Hcp 1000m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Interwave reared and became momentarily
    cast in the barrier stalls and was a late scratching after being released. Tia Jane and
    Miss Vodka crossed from wide barriers to lead from Dark Horizon, Showy Wings and Kelmark.
    By the 400m Tia Jane and Miss Vodka held the front as Gucci Guru started to make a run
    from Dark Horizon and Princess Annandon. By the 150m Tia Jane had kicked clear from Miss
    Vodka as Dark Horizon ran on near the rails from Gussi Guru. In the final stages Dark
    Horizon rushed at the leader but Tia Jane was too strong winning by 3/4 length with Miss
    Vodka holding third from Nishikawa.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Tia Jane is a 2yo bay filly by Ascot Knight
    from Eulissa and was ridden by David O'Heare from WA. She is trained by her breeder David
    Baylis for himself, SJ McGregor and SS Rodger.</font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="3madharry.jpg" alt="3madharry.jpg (15761 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="300" HEIGHT="246"></td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 3 - Intergaze Hcp 2400m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">On the Natch led Dark Banner then Olongopo
    and Prince Naseem followed by Astrolante. By the 1800m Prince Naseem who had been wide
    took over from On The Natch and Dark Banner. Little changed to the 1000m where Prince
    Naseem led in a slowly run race from Dark Banner as On the Natch was third ahead of Mad
    Harry who appeared to get on his heels then Olongopo wider. On straightening On
    straightening Dark Banner joined Prince Naseem as Olongopo started to run on from Mad
    Harry. By the 150m Olongopo just led Dark Banner as Mad Harry challenged with Red Astaire
    making a late run. Dark Banner drew to the lead by the 50m but Mad Harry and Red Astaire
    started to fly down the outside. In the run for the line Mad Harry was too good winning by
    a half head from Red Astaire with Dark Banner a short neck away third.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Mad Harry is a 4yo chestnut gelding by
    Prince of Birds from River Ride and was ridden by Linda Meech. He is trained by Terry
    O'Sullivan for Mad no.2 Syndicate.</font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="4stretta.jpg" alt="4stretta.jpg (13125 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="300" HEIGHT="227"></td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 4 - Brackenbury Hcp 1300m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">All Cheer led but wide from Stretta then My
    Sky Rocket followed by Pretty Edgy and Lachaim. Turning for home All Cheer and Stretta
    turned together from My Sky Rocket then Lachaim who bumped Pretty Edgy as St Fraar took a
    run inside them. By the 200m Stretta got the better of All Cheer as St Fraar started to
    charge home from My Sky Rocket. Stretta kicked clear by the 100m as St Fraar started a
    determined challenge. It was to no avail as Stretta proved too strong winning by 3/4
    length from St Fraar with All Cheer third.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Stretta is a 6yo bay mare by Bellotto from
    Squashable and was ridden by Justin Potter. She is trained by Barry Burgess for himself.</font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="5streetparlour.jpg" alt="5streetparlour.jpg (15620 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="300" HEIGHT="254"></td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 5 - Testa Rossa Hcp 1400m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Kooda Tiara led Street Parlour with
    Saville's Anguish wide from Scenicnick then Military Banquet. By the 700m Kooda Tiara
    shared the lead with Saville's Anguish then Scenicnick followed by Street Parlour and
    Military Banquet. On straightening Street Parlour made ground on the rails to join Kooda
    Tiara then Saville's Anguish and Scenicnick. Street Parlour took the lead by the 200m but
    Kooda Tiara started to fight back with Scenicnick running on. Street Parlour and Kooda
    Tiara went stride for stride to the line with Street Parlour winning by a half head with
    Brief Truth storming home for third.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Street Parlour is a 3yo chestnut filly by
    Dolphin Street from Palamour and was ridden by David Tootell. She is trained by Trevor
    Simounds for RD Vawser, SJ &amp; DP I'Anson, C Natt, J Hill, J McIntyre, M Kouts and L
    Smethurst. Street Parlour was offered for sale at the 2000 Adelaide Magic Millions sale
    but failed narrowly to reach her reserve and was passed in.</font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center">&nbsp;</td>
    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 6 - Open Hcp 1300m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Stand By Me led Manetti with Marron Cheval
    between them followed by Border Time then Our Jermyn Street. By the turn Manetti was just
    in front of Stand By Me then Marron Cheval followed by Our Jermyn Street and Border Time.
    On straightening Manetti and Stand By Me shared the front as Our Jermyn Street started to
    run on with Border Time. By the 100m Stand By Me, Our Jermyn Street and Border Time made a
    line of three clear of Aviate. In the final 50m Our Jermyn Street and Border Time came
    away with the former proving strongest to win by a head with Stand By Me third.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Our Jermyn Street is a 5yo chestnut gelding
    by Grosvenor from Street Dancer and was ridden by Paul Gatt. He is trained by Tony McEvoy
    for GW Tsoi. In winning his first South Australian start Our Jermyn Street set a new
    course record of 1.16.08 with Super Lad previously holding the record when winning in
    December 2000. </font></td>
  </tr>

    <td width="50%"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 7 - Kenny's Best Pal Hcp 1800m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">On settling Raja Jazz led Rubiton Road then
    Lord Zenobia and Amarina followed by Matheson Boy. Little changed until they straightened
    at the 600m where Raja Jazz led Rubiton Road with Amarina giving chase as Serein and Moet
    Now started to run on out wide. By the 200m Amarina hit the lead from Evans then Moet Now
    and Serein. Inside the final 100m Evans took the front but Serein had hit top speed from
    Moet Now as Raja Jazz fought back. In the final 50m Evans and Serein came away with Serein
    doing better to win by a half neck with Moet Now third.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Serein is a 4yo bay mare by Snippets from
    Serene City and was ridden by Paul Gatt. She is trained by Peter Jolly for VP Cleveland
    and DA Hurrell.</font></td>

  <tr>
    <td width="50%" align="center"><img src="../vip260501/8eldiablo.jpg" alt="8eldiablo.jpg (18295 bytes)" border="2" WIDTH="235" HEIGHT="267"><p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">El Diablo in May last year.</font></td>
    <td width="50%" valign="top"><font SIZE="2"><p align="center"></font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000"><strong>Race 8 - Devaraja Hcp 1000m</strong></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">Ambroc led early but El Diablo soon raced
    clear with his ears pricked and crossed from a wide barrier. Ambroc was next from Hexham
    then Disco Time and Los Torneros. Little changed to the 400m where El Diablo dashed clear
    from Ambroc as The Big Fella started to run on. By the 200m El Diablo went further in
    front from The Big Fella and Noble Win. El Diablo passed the post eased up to win by three
    lengths from The Big Fella with Noble Win third and Disco Time fourth.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#008000">El Diablo is a 4yo bay gelding by El Moxie
    from Movie Symbol and was ridden by Darren Holland. He is trained by Jeremy Gask for L
    Colbert, RG Gask, J Levy and R Bussenschutt. El Diablo is well known for his antics as an
    unraced 3yo in February last year when he shied while at the beach and, after dropping his
    rider, began heading out to sea. After 300m he was turned but continued to swim parallel
    to shore for a further time before finally being persuaded to head back to the beach. He
    then ran a promising 5th in May before winning down the Victoria Park straight. An
    enforced break followed where a bone chip was removed from his knee and today's race was
    his first start back.</font></td>
