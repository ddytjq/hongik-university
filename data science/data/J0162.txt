      <h2><span style="font-size: 14.0pt">Rally Partners, Inc. Steers the Great 
      Race on New Course</span></h2>
</CENTER>
      </CENTER>
      <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><b>
      <span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Arial">&nbsp;</span></b><span style="font-family: Arial"><font size="2">San 
      Marcos, TX -- Over the past twenty years, the Great Race has had a 
      phenomenal growth and acceptance by automobile enthusiasts and the public 
      as a truly American Icon.&nbsp; The annual trans-continental event, is the only 
      multi-day vintage car rally of its kind in the country and has recently 
      been purchased by Rally Partners, Incorporated.</font></span></p>
      <p class="MsoBodyText" align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Rally 
      Partners, Inc., a thoroughbred group of competitors and business owners, 
      has combined their Great Race experience of 124 years to form a board of 
      directors to focus on the daily operations of the rally.&nbsp; The group of 11 
      investors is committed to maintaining the integrity of the event while 
      bringing to it, a higher level of public interest and competition.</font></p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span style="font-family: Arial">
      <font size="2">“Our vision,” said Dick Burdick, Rally Partners’ Chairman 
      of the Board and CEO, “is to make the Great Race the premiere vintage 
      rally in the nation by continuing its theme of God, Country, and Family 
      Values as it crosses America each year.”</font></span></p>
      <p class="MsoBodyText" align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Rally 
      Partners will conduct business in a democratic fashion to ensure the 
      competitors, sponsors and fans are given the highest quality event 
      possible and all voices are heard.&nbsp; A Board of Directors was formed 
      consisting of business-savvy individuals with a passion for vintage cars, 
      road racing and in particular the honor of participating in the Great 
      Race. </font></p>
      <p class="MsoBodyText" align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Board 
      officers were elected in June by the investors of Rally Partners, Inc. The 
      elections brought in an eclectic team who will work well together to 
      create a solid and exciting Great Race each year.&nbsp; Elected officers are: 
      Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, Dick Burdick of Thermon 
      Manufacturing Company, San Marcos, Texas; First Vice President, Corky 
      Coker of Coker Tire Company, Chattanooga, Tennessee; Vice President and 
      Chief Operating Officer, Wayne Stanfield, five-time Great Race Champion 
      from Costa Mesa, California; Secretary and Treasurer, Susan McClenaghan of 
      Roush Racing, Livonia, Michigan.&nbsp; Additional board members include Michael 
      Dingman, Curtis Graf, Gab Joiner, Buck Kamphausen, Gary Kuck and GR Pike, 
      David Reeder and Jack Roush.</font></p>
      <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span style="font-family: Arial">
      <font size="2">Others who have become invaluable over the years and who 
      have agreed to continue the quest on behalf of the Great Race Board are 
      Sister McRae, who is in her ninth year as Director of Administration and 
      John Classen, a fourteen-year veteran in the position of Director of 
      Competition</font><span style="color:maroon"><font size="2">.</font></span></span></p>

<font size="3"><b>Anaheim, CA - June 22, 2002</b></font>- Hometown hero Wayne Stanfield, a veteran of every running of the Great Race, became the Race's first 5-time winner when he drove car #1, a 1934 Ford Indy Racer through the finish gate today in downtown Anaheim, CA.  Stanfield, a plumber from nearby Santa Ana, won the 1987, 1994, 1997 and 2001 editions of the world's most prestigious old-car rally, celebrating its 20th anniversary this year.  "This year's Race had it all:  heat, elevation, everything.  To do it again for the fifth time is really incredible," said Stanfield, who now has two wins as a driver and three as a navigator.  Teenager David Dingman of Nassau, Bahamas, served as the Stanfield's navigator for the 2002 Great Race.  Driving for the powerful Roush Racing team, the pair won all but 2 days of the 8-day trek, which began in San Antonio, TX.  Four-time Great Race winners, Dick Burdick and Wayne Bell, both 20-year veterans, finished second in Pro Division in their Thermon Special, a 1916 Hudson H Speedster.</font>
