successive European Championship qualifying wins. But their much-vaunted attack

Fernando Morientes twice headed wide from close range, while his Real Madrid
strike partner Raul Gonzalez hit a post when well placed. Brazil offered little
going forward, with Rivaldo having a particularly quiet game, and the closest
they came to scoring were two long shots from Marcos Assuncao and Cafu in the
closing stages. </FONT></P>

<TD COLSPAN="2"> <P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Nov

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B>Team v Denmark</B><BR>
1-Dudu Awat; 2-Alon Harazi, 3-David Amsalem, 4-Arik Benado, 5-Ran Ben-Shimon,
6-Alon Hazan, 7-Tal Banin, 8-Haim Revivo, 9-Amir Turgeman, 10-Eyal Berkovic,
11-Yossi Abuksis. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Denmark all but secured their place in the
European championship finals on Saturday after beating Israel 5-0 in the first
leg of their qualifying play-off. A second minute goal by Denmark's Jon Dahl
Tomasson silenced the 45,000 strong crowd in the National Stadium as the
visitors set about inflicting one of Israel's worst home defeats. Tomasson
added his second goal in the 35th minute as the Israelis seemed almost
incapable of breaking out of their own half and the Danes totally dominated the
game. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Although Israel picked up the pace a little in
the early stages of the second half, the outcome of the match was clear once
Israel's Haim Revivo was sent off for a tackle from behind on Jes Hogh in the
58th. Stig Toefting made it 3-0 with a powerful free kick from the edge of the
area in the 67th minute and Martin Jorgensen headed a high lob over the
outstretched arms of Israel goalkeeper Dudu Awat a minute later. Brian Steen
Nielsen sealed the result in the 73rd minute with a powerful, low shot from the
right. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The result effectively spelt the end of the
reign of Israel's national team coach, Shlomo Scharf, who has said he would not
be seeking to continue after the current campaign. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Nov
8</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Ronnen Harazi, Israel's most prolfic current
scorer, was recalled on Monday in a squad to face Denmark in a European
championship play-off in Tel Aviv on Saturday. Harazi, who scored Hapoel Tel
Aviv's first goal in a 1-1 league draw against Maccabi Tel Aviv on Monday
night, has scored 23 goals in 53 international appearances but had lost his
place in the national side because of poor form. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Goalkeeper Shavit Elimelech, Harazi's club
mate, was also recalled in a 22-man squad after Maccabi Haifa's Nir Davidovich
ruled himself out with a knee injury which could keep him out for several
months, according to his club. Davidovich was first-choice goalkeeper until he
was publicly criticised by Scharf after Israel's 3-2 loss to Cyprus in a
qualifier in Limassol in September. He was subsequently replaced by Hapoel
Haifa's David (Dudu) Awat, who is likely to keep his place against Denmark
despite criticism from Scharf after Israel lost 3-0 to Spain in Albacete in
October. Harazi and Hapoel Haifa's Amir Turgeman are the only strikers in the
squad. Alon Mizrahi, unable to claim a first-team place with French second
division club Nice, was dropped because Scharf said the striker was not
match-fit and could not be considered for international duty. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Defender Ran Ben-Shimon has made a full
recovery after a long absence with a knee injury and will give Scharf an
option, with Shimon Gershon, for the sweeper's role. Ben-Shimon's Hapoel Haifa
club mate Amir Shelah, another defender, was named in the squad, although he is
serving a one-match suspension and will miss the first leg. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The return leg will be played in Copenhagen on
November 17. </FONT></P>

- Spain 3 Israel 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Israel, needing an unlikely victory to
guarantee second place behind group six winners Spain, found themselves
thoroughly outplayed as goals from Fernando Morientes, Cesar Martin and Raul
Gonzalez earned the home side a comfortable win. But Austria's 3-1 win over
Cyprus in the other remaining group game rendered the result in Albacete
irrelevant and kept Israel's hopes alive. Israeli keeper Dudu Awat was at fault
on Spain's first two goals -- headers from Morientes and Cesar after 29 and 37
minutes -- and he might also have done better with the third, whish saw him
palm away a Joseba Etxeberria shot for Raul to tap in. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Oct 10 - Austria 3
Cyprus 1</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"> Cyprus had midfielders Costas Kaiafas and
Milenco Spoljaric sent off in the 31st and 45th minutes repectively. Austria's
Robert Ibertsberger joined them midway through the second half. Austria's
Eduard Glieder opened the scoring in the fifth minute and captain Ivica Vastic
made it 2-0 from a free-kick in the 23rd. Costas Costas replied in the 63rd
minute before second-half substitute Andreas Herzog, back after a seven-week
absence because of an an ankle injury, made it 3-1 with a solo effort in the
81st. </FONT></P>
