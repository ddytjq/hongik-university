            must be expressible in mathematical terms. But Athens at that time

            Pythagoras, whose school developed many women mathematikoi, Plato

        </tr>

          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1696</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"> 
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Brachistochrone 
              problem solved by Jacob and Johann Bernoulli, an early result in 
              the calculus of variations.</font></p>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1712</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Thanks 
            to a campaign waged by Newton, a commission appointed by Royal Society 
            of London President Isaac Newton rules that Leibniz is guilty of plagiarism 
            against Newton in the discovery of calculus. English mathematics and 
            theoretical physics go into decline because those loyal to Newton 
            are hesitant to adopt Leibniz' infinitesimal and his clean, intuitively 
            appealing notation.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1736</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Leonhard 
            Euler begins the field of topology when he publishes his solution 
            of the Konigsberg Bridge problem.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1738</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099"><i>Hydrodynamics</i> 
            by Daniel Bernoulli</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1748</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">The 
            multitalented Euler begins the fields of mathematical analysis and 
            analytical mechanics with <i>Introductio in 
            analysin infinitorum</i>. Euler introduces the formula e<i><sup>ix</sup></i> 
            = cos x + i sin x</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1758</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Joseph-Louis 
            Lagrange finds the complete general solution to the Newtonian equations 
            of motion for a vibrating string, which explains the harmonic relations 
            observed by Pythagoras 22 centuries ago. </font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1770</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Hyperbolic 
            trigonometry -- cosh, sinh -- is developed. </font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49" height="66"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1772</font></b></td>
          <td width="381" height="66"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Henry 
            Cavendish, a wealthy but paranoid recluse, discovers that the electrostatic 
            force is described by an inverse square law similar to gravity, but 
            doesn't tell anyone in the science community.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49" height="66"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1788</font></b></td>
          <td width="381" height="66"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Lagrange 
            further develops the analytical mechanics of Euler when he publishes 
            <i>M&eacute;canique Analytique</i>, 
            revealing Newtonian mechanics to be a rich field of exploration for 
            mathematicians.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1789</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Aristocrat 
            Charles-Augustin de Coulomb, hiding from the French Revolution after 
            the storming of the Bastille, shows that the electrostatic force between 
            electric charges was very well described by an inverse square law, 
            in full analogy with Newtonian gravity. This becomes known as Coulomb's 
            Law, even though Henry Cavendish was the first one to demonstrate 
            it. </font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1793</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Lagrange 
            is arrested during the Reign of Terror, but is rescued by Antoine-Laurent 
            Lavoisier, the founder of modern chemistry. Unfortunately, Lavoisier's 
            career in chemistry is ended when he is taken to meet Madame Guillotine 
            on May 8, 1794.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td width="49"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#339966">1799</font></b></td>
          <td width="381"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099">Pierre-Simon 
            Laplace publishes his work <i>Traité du Mécanique 
            Céleste</i> (Treatise on Celestial Mechanics) using differential 
            equations to solve problems in planetary motion and fluid flow.</font></td>
        </tr>
        <tr align="center" bordercolor="#669999"> 
          <td colspan="2"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#000099"><a href="../math/math1a.html"><b><font color="#339966">next 
            &gt;&gt;&gt;</font></b></a></font></td>
        </tr>
      </table>
    </td>
