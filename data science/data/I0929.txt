Premier League at their May 2002 meeting.</p>

<table align="left"><tr><td><applet archive="FASTImageApplet.jar" code="no.fast.Image.a" codebase="http://www.leaguemanagers.com/media" width="210" height="256"><param name="cabbase" value="FASTImageApplet.cab"><param name="img" value="john-barnwell-serious.fst">

<b>Coaching Qualifications</b></p>
<p class="bodytext"><b>
Q.3A.</b> Before the 2003/04 Season each Manager shall acquire and thereafter, subject to Rule Q.3B, throughout his employment as a manager hold either: </p><p class="bodytext">

	3A.1	A valid Football Association Coaching Diploma; <b>or</b> 3A.2	A valid UEFA Professional Licence. </p><p class="bodytext">

<b>Q.3B.</b> Before the 2010/11 Season each Manager shall acquire and thereafter throughout his employment as a Manager hold a valid UEFA Professional Licence. </p><p class="bodytext">

<b>Q.3C.</b> No Club shall at any time after the commencement of the 2003/04 Season employ any person as a Manager who does not hold the qualifications required by Rules Q.3A and Q.3B. </p><p class="bodytext">

The criteria to receive The Football Association Coaching Diploma has been agreed by The F.A. to be: </p><p class="bodytext">

<b>1.</b> The Football Association Coaching Diploma:  This course will not be available after 2003 and only available in 2003.  This course is essentially a transitional expedient course.

</p><p class="bodytext">To award The Football Association Coaching Diploma (by APL) to candidates who have, or will have by 2003, a minimum of 10 years in The F.A. Premier League, Football League or a comparable League as a Manager as shown below.  This window of opportunity ceases after 2003.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The Football Association Coaching Diploma Proposed APL Awards will be available to the following:</p>

<p class="bodytext"><b>2.</b> If by 2003 the candidate has less than 10 years experience then the candidate will be required to undertake the following modules to complete The Football Association Coaching Diploma:
</p><p class="bodytext">
 - 'A' Licence Holder	 or FA Advanced Licence (or equivalent)  - must complete -50% of The Football Association Coaching Diploma.
</p><p class="bodytext">
-  'B' Licence or F.A. Preliminary Award	- must complete - 75% of The Football   Association Coaching Diploma. </p><p class="bodytext">

- No qualification	- must complete -	100% of The Football Association Coaching Diploma.

</p><p class="bodytext"><b>3.</b>	To retain The Football Association Coaching Diploma, the candidates need to undertake and successfully complete the Refresher Course every four years.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The UEFA Pro Licence will, of course, be available every year to holders of the UEFA 'A' Licence with the appropriate experience.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The Football Association Coaching Diploma 'window' will only be provided in 2003.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The purpose behind the rule changes is to provide enhanced professionalism in the management and coaching at 1st team level in The F.A. Premier League
</p><p class="bodytext">
"On the basis of other countries establishing a potential workforce that is better trained and prepared the degree of instability and wastage will be reduced," said The F.A. Technical Director and LMA Chairman, Howard Wilkinson.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The F.A. Premier League is now the only major League in Europe that does not have mandatory qualifications as a requirement for its coaches, yet the degree of instability in Managerial terms is one of the highest in Europe
</p><p class="bodytext">
"Such instability ultimately raises costs for the clubs not only in settlement payments, but in the overall running costs (e.g. changes in player preferences) associated with change of the man at the top on a too regular basis." says Wilkinson.
</p><p class="bodytext">
Stable clubs not only have greater success on the field of play but also have reduced costs compared to costs at similar unstable clubs. </p><p class="bodytext"> "I am sure clubs will wish to take the opportunity to maintain and raise standards whilst reducing overall costs," said Howard Wilkinson
</p><p class="bodytext">
The proposed rule changes have the unanimous support of The F.A. Board, F.A. Council and F.A. Technical Control Board, the latter of which is exclusively made up of Chairmen from The F.A. Premier League and Football League clubs.  The proposals have also been developed through consultation with the LMA, PFA, The F.A. Premier League and The Football League.
</p><p class="bodytext">
The proposed rule change will also meet the requirements of UEFA's Licensing System for clubs entering the Champions League from 2004 onwards, in that UEFA have stipulated that the Head Coach of competing clubs must hold the appropriate coaching qualifications.
</p><p class="bodytext"></p>
