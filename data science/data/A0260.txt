<h1>The Answers</h1>
<a name="loanprotection">&nbsp;</a>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Who can benefit from Personal Loan Protection?</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">A. Loan protection is available to anyone over 18 and under 
  65 who is working more than 16 hours a week. It makes no difference if you are 
  employed or self-employed, you can still benefit from Personal Loan Protection.</p> 
<p><a name="whatbenefits">&nbsp;</a></p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. What benefits do I get from taking Personal Loan Protection?</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">A. By taking our comprehensive Personal Loan Protection, 
  should the unfortunate happen and you are unable to work either due to an accident, 
  sickness or unemployment, our cover will protect your payments. Thus giving 
  you and your family peace of mind that your payments will still be met.</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">It also provides life cover (with a maximum benefit of up to £22,000), so in the unfortunate event of your death, your loan would be paid off, so your family wouldn't have to bear the burden.</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">In addition, if your loan is for a motor vehicle, the loan protection provides GAP cover, which means that in the event of your vehicle being written off by your insurance company, it pays the difference between the insurance company payout and the outstanding balance of the loan, so that you are not left out of pocket. The cover also pays the motor policy excess up to £250.</p>   
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<a name="redundant">&nbsp;</a><p class="faqquestion">Q. What happens if I am made redundant?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. By taking Personal Loan Protection, should the unfortunate 
  happen and you are made involuntarily redundant, we will continue to make your 
  payments for up to 12 months, or until you return to work whichever is the sooner. 
  Payments will start when you have been out of work for more than 30 days.</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">*Over 500 people are made redundant in the UK every day, and 60% of unemployed people are out of work for six months or more, with this invaluable cover it would be another worry off your mind, allowing you to concentrate on more important things.</p> 
<p><a href="#"><img src="/media/images/but-backtotop.gif" width="120" height="20" border="0" /></a></p>
<a name="suddenlyinjured">&nbsp;</a><p class="faqquestion">Q. What happens if I am suddenly injured or taken ill? </p> 
<p class="faqanswer">A. If you can't work due to an accident or sickness, again 
  Personal Loan Protection provides invaluable cover. Our policy will continue 
  to make your payments until you're well enough to go back to work. Even if your 
  illness lasts the entire length of your loan, we'll make up to 60 monthly payments. 
  (In the case of a Car Purchase Plan loan you will be covered for up to 48 months). 
  Payments start when you have been out of work for 30 days or more.</p> 
<p><a name="personalloanprotection">&nbsp;</a></p>

<p class="faqquestion">Q. What is not covered by Personal Loan Protection?</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">A. As you would expect there are some common sense exclusions 
  to our Personal Loan Protection. The main ones are detailed below, full details 
  are available on request if required.</p> 
<p>For unemployment cover :</p>
<ul>
<li>If your unemployment starts within 30 days of you taking out the loan.</li>
<li>Voluntary unemployment</li>
</ul>
<p class="faqanswer">For accident & sickness cover :</p> 
<ul>
<li>A disability or illness that you knew about in the 12 months before your loan started</li>
<li>A disability caused by the normal symptoms of pregnancy</li>
<li>Self-inflicted injuries</li>
</ul>

<p class="faqanswer">For life cover :</p> 
<ul>
<li>If your death results from suicide within 12 months of the start date of your loan.</li>
</ul>

<p class="faqanswer">For GAP cover :</p> 
<ul>
<li>If your motor vehicle does not have comprehensive motor insurance</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="#"><img src="/media/images/but-backtotop.gif" width="120" height="20" border="0" /></a></p>
<a name="protectmypayments">&nbsp;</a>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Is it possible to only protect my payments against redundancy?</p> 
<p class="faqanswer">A. At present we don't offer a policy which just protects 
  against redundancy. Our comprehensive cover provides all round cover, so no 
  matter what might happen, whether it be an accident, sickness, unemployment 
  or even death, we believe in providing a package of benefits which will provide 
  for you in any eventuality.</p>

<h2>Car Purchase Plan</h2>

<a name="cpprestrictions">&nbsp;</a>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Are there any special conditions or restrictions to 
  the Car Purchase Plan? 
<p class="faqanswer">A. No. The car belongs to you and unlike many car finance 
  schemes, you do not need to put down a deposit to obtain a Car Purchase Plan, 
  you are not penalised for exceeding an annual mileage, and neither do you have 
  to have comprehensive motor insurance.</p>
<p><a href="#"><img src="/media/images/but-backtotop.gif" width="120" height="20" border="0" /></a></p>
<a name="cppendoftheloanperiod">&nbsp;</a><p class="faqquestion">Q. What happens to my car at the end of the loan period?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. You own your car from day one. At the end of the Car 
  Purchase Plan you have two options:</p>
<p class="faqanswer">
1. Pay off the deferred amount and simply take out a new plan to make your next 
purchase<br />  
2.	Continue making the same fixed monthly repayments until the whole amount is cleared<br /> 
</p>
<p><a name="cppearly">&nbsp;</a> </p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Can I repay my loan early?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. Yes, this is possible. However, you will be charged an 
  early settlement fee.</p>
<p><a name="cppmaximum">&nbsp;</a></p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. What is the maximum loan term?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. The maximum loan term on a Car Purchase Plan is 4 years.</p>
<p><a name="cppborrow">&nbsp;</a></p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. What is the maximum amount I can borrow?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. The maximum loan amount is £20,000</p>
<p><a name="cppsecured">&nbsp;</a> </p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Is Car Purchase Plan a secured or an unsecured loan?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. Both the Alliance & Leicester Car Purchase Plan and Personal 
  Loan products are unsecured products</p>
<p><a href="#"><img src="/media/images/but-backtotop.gif" width="120" height="20" border="0" /></a></p>

<h2>Personal Loan</h2>

<a name="plearly">&nbsp;</a>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Can I repay my loan early?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. Yes, this is possible. However, you will be charged an 
  early settlement fee.</p>
<p><a name="plmaximum">&nbsp;</a></p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. What is the maximum loan term?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. The maximum loan term on a Personal Loan is 5 years.</p>
<p><a name="plborrow">&nbsp;</a></p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. What is the maximum amount I can borrow?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. The maximum loan amount you can borrow over via the Internet 
  is £20,000. If you choose to apply for a loan over the telephone or through 
  one of our branches, the maximum amount you can borrow falls to £15,000.</p> 
<p><a name="plsecured">&nbsp;</a> </p>
<p class="faqquestion">Q. Will the loan be secured against my house?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. No. Both the Alliance & Leicester Car Purchase Plan and 
  Personal Loans are unsecured products.</p>

<h2>Existing loan customers</h2>

<a name="elexisting">&nbsp;</a><p class="faqquestion">Q. What should I do if I have an existing Alliance & Leicester loan?</p>
<p class="faqanswer">A. If you have an existing Loan with Alliance & Leicester, 
  you can apply for more money whenever you need it. As an existing customer, 
  it needn't cost you any more each month. You can simply adjust your current 
  loan arrangement to suit you. Alternatively, if you can afford to increase your 
  loan repayments each month, you could clear your current loan and have even 
  more money to spend. You can apply on-line, or call <b>08705 55 55 55</b>.</p>
