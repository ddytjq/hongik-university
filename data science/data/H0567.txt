					<h3>1 - 10 of 11</h3><p><strong><a href="http://www.geneethics.org/">Australian Gene Ethics Network</a></strong><br />The Australian Gene Ethics Network is a federation of groups and individuals who promote critical discussion and debate on the environmental, social and ethical impacts of genetic engineering technologies. User-friendly presentation.

</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://bioethics.georgetown.edu/bioline.htm">Bioethicsline</a></strong><br />BIOETHICSLINE, an online bibliographic database supported by the National Library of Medicine and the National Human Genome Research Institute, covers bioethics literature in the health sciences, law, religion, philosophy, and social sciences.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.cordis.lu/biomed/">Biomedicine and Health (BIOMED 2)</a></strong><br />The European Commission's Research and Development Information Service (CORDIS) offers access to the results of BIOMED 2, one 
of the three specific programmes of the Fourth RTD Framework Programme (1994-1998). The programme aimed to improve medical knowledge and the health of the European population by strengthening the scientific basis. </p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.cre.gu.se/">Centre for research ethics (CRE), Göteborg</a></strong><br />The Centre for research ethics (CRE) is a non-profit foundation and part of Göteborg University, Sweden. The primary task of the CRE is to do research in the area of research ethic and to offer training and courses. Overview on current seminars, research projects and publications.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.gene-watch.org/">Council for Responsible Genetics (CRG)</a></strong><br />The UK-Council for Responsible Genetics (CRG) is a non-profit bioethic organization devoted to fostering public debate about the 
social, ethical, and environmental implications of the new genetic technologies.
</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.cordis.lu/rtd2002/science-society/ethics.htm">European Research Area - Ethics</a></strong><br />The European Commission promotes responsible research in Europe in harmony with the ethical values of all Europeans. This site guides researchers towards reference texts in the area of ethics in research, the European legislation and international conventions, as well as international declarations.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.genethik.de/">Genethik</a></strong><br />A private, but professional overview on genetics from scientific and ethical points of view. Scientific reviews, special reports about genetic therapy, stem cell therapy and AIDS, such as a line-up of the current genethical opinions from Germany. </p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nuffield.com/bioethics/">Nuffield Council of Bioethics</a></strong><br />The Nuffield Council of Bioethics considers the ethical issues raised by the new developments in medicine and biology. List of the council members, current inquiries and press releases.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www4.od.nih.gov/oba/">Office of Biotechnology Activities (OBA)</a></strong><br />The Office of Biotechnology Activities (OBA) of the National Institutes of Health monitors scientific progress in human genetics research in order to anticipate future developments, including ethical, legal, and social concerns.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.onlineethics.org/">Online Ethics Center for Engineering and Science</a></strong><br />The Online Ethics Center for Engineering and Science provides information on ethically significant problems that arise in our work life. Glossary and a lot of useful links.</p>
