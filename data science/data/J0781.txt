    1987</b></font> </p>
    <p><img SRC="images/lada4.gif" ALT="lada4.gif (3408 bytes)" width="87" height="54"></td>
    <td width="577" style="text-indent: 2px; border-bottom: 2px solid rgb(253,200,247); padding-left: 2px"><br>
    <a NAME="The Beginnings"></a><font SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial"><b><u>The
    Beginnings</u></b></font> <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0"><b>Danny Fois</b>
    and <b>Mike Wiseman</b> both met at college in 1980 and after talking about rallying for
    years, began campaigning an overweight, overgeared and over</font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="-1"> </font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">the top Fiat
    powered twin-cam Lada in late 1987. The <b>LADS RALLY TEAM</b> was duly formed and ready
    to set the rallying world alight....only somebody forgot to bring the matches!</font></p>
    <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">The Lads soon gained a reputation based
    not on their ability but on their endless tales of woe. This first car expired at the end
    of 87 following a major roll in a local forest complex. The Lads were to return at the
    start of</font><font SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial"> </font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">the next season with an all new lightweight version of the twin-cam
    Lada.<br>
    </font><font SIZE="+0">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    <img SRC="images/photo11.gif" ALT="photo11.jpg (21234 bytes)" width="300" height="226"><br>
    </font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">Being in a class with everything over
    2000cc, class awards were very few and far between. Nevertheless the Lads enthusiasm and
    high profile public image ensured that every one knew who they were!!</font></p>

    <area href="#The Beginnings" shape="rect" coords="3, 0, 29, 12"></map><img src="images/lads_up.gif" alt="lads up.gif (939 bytes)" border="0" usemap="#FPMap0" width="30" height="13"><br>

    <font SIZE="+3" COLOR="#FF0080" FACE="Arial"><b>1991<br>
    to<br>
    1994</b></font> <p><img SRC="images/fiatlogo.gif" ALT="fiatlogo.gif (914 bytes)" height="25" width="84"></td>
    <td width="577" style="text-indent: 2px; border-bottom: 2px solid rgb(253,200,247); padding-left: 2px"><br>
    <font SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial"><b><u><a name="Back to Basics"></a>Back to Basics</u></b></font> <p><font SIZE="+0"><font COLOR="#FF00FF">In 1991, the swap to a front wheel
    drive Fiat Uno was an exercise in &#145;back to basics&#146;. Rather than opt for ever
    more powerful cars, the Lads chose the up to &#145;1300cc baby class&#146; and spent time
    learning technique and the different skills required with front-wheel drive machinery.
    Fois adapted quickly to FWD and we were soon on the pace of the class front runners,
    culminating in 1994 by winning the <a HREF="#1994">ACSMC Southern area stage rally
    championships</a>, not only in the 1300 class but taking the overall honours as well. <br>
    </font>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

    <td width="123" align="center" valign="top" background="back5.gif"><br>
    <font SIZE="+3" COLOR="#FF0080" FACE="Arial"><b>1995<br>
    to<br>
    1997</b></font> <p><img SRC="images/ford.gif" ALT="ford.gif (1783 bytes)" width="75" height="35"></td>
    <td width="577" style="text-indent: 2px; border-bottom: 2px solid rgb(253,200,247); padding-left: 2px"><br>
    <font SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial"><b><u><a name="Formula 2"></a>Formula 2</u></b></font> <p><font SIZE="+0" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial">To compete in the RAC Rally was always one of the
    Lads goals, and in order to do so, it was necessary to make the change to a current
    &#145;homologated&#146; production car.</font></p>
    <p><font SIZE="+0"><font COLOR="#FF00FF">In 1995 the Lads chose the now
    highly popular Formula 2 route, with front wheel drive non-turbo cars up to 2 litres, and
    opted for the rare but underdeveloped Ford Escort RS 2000.<br>
    </font>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    <img SRC="images/photo14.gif" ALT="photo14.jpg (20625 bytes)" width="300" height="226"><br>
    </font><font SIZE="+0" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial">The 95 and 96 seasons proved to be the
    Lads most competitive and exhausting seasons so far. As well as two RAC rallies, both
    seasons were filled with full Championship commitments with the BTRDA Clubmans National
    Rally Championship series. Like all the well supported modern rally championships, the
    competition is so tight with the final result going down to the last event each year. The
    Lads finished a creditable <a HREF="LADS_results.htm#1995">4th in class in 95</a> and
    <a HREF="LADS_results.htm#1996">3rd
    in 96</a>. </font></p>
    <p><font SIZE="+0" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial">Despite these successes, the RAC rally has
    proved an altogether more difficult challenge. An accident on the 95 rally led to
    retirement, whilst suspension damage finally ended their 96 challenge. The RAC is always
    guaranteed to provide the toughest challenge for any rally car, but the Group N machinery
    like the RS 2000 with their essentially standard gearbox and suspension components, make
    it particularly prone to damage. </font></p>
    <p><map name="FPMap2">
    <area href="#The Beginnings" shape="rect" coords="3, 0, 29, 12"></map><img src="images/lads_up.gif" alt="lads up.gif (939 bytes)" border="0" usemap="#FPMap2" width="30" height="13"><br>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="123" align="center" valign="top" background="back5.gif"><br>
    <font SIZE="+3" COLOR="#FF0080" FACE="Arial"><b>1998</b></font> <p><img SRC="images/cosworth.jpg" ALT="cosworth.jpg (5155 bytes)" height="16" width="106"></td>
    <td width="577" style="text-indent: 2px; border-bottom: 2px solid rgb(253,200,247); padding-left: 2px"><br>
    <font SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial"><b><u><a name="Cosworth"></a>Cosworth</u></b></font> <p><font SIZE="+0"><font COLOR="#FF00FF">After &#145;learning the ropes&#146; in
    Formula 2 the Lads decided to once again seek the reason for starting rallying in the
    first place.... &#146;The Grin Factor&#146;. Rather than chase round the country committed
    to a championship series, the Lads chose to go the full monty and with the essential
    support of Essex based <a HREF="LADS_sponsors.htm#French Motorsport">French Motorsport</a>
    acquired a Group A Ford Escort RS Cosworth and set about having some serious fun!! <br>
    </font>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    <img SRC="images/photo5.gif" ALT="photo5.jpg (20960 bytes)" width="300" height="226"><br>
    </font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">After a few undercover outings, the
    Escort Cosworth emerged in Lads Pink paint scheme for the start of the 1997 Network Q RAC
    Rally. &nbsp; </font></p>
    <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">The Lads past fortunes in the RAC Rally
    don&#146;t make particularly good reading.&nbsp; Their 1995 rally ended after leaving the
    road in a North Yorkshire Forest, damaging the radiator and forcing their retirement early
    on the second day.&nbsp; The Lads 1996 RAC rally also ended prematurely although this time
    through mechanical failure. </font></p>
    <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">The rally can never be won on the first
    day but can often be lost. The Lads plan had been simple.....keep it neat and tidy and
    wait for the real rally to start on the Monday morning. The plan worked almost too well
    with the Lads enjoying the mix of stages and holding an encouraging 70th place at the end
    of the first leg after originally being seeded 115th out of 170. Day two saw some dreadful
    weather conditions in Mid-Wales and the day was marred by some serious accidents. The Lads
    survived the mayhem to make it through to the final day, for the first time ever!</font></p>
    <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">Into day three and the Lads had settled
    into a pace that would maintain their position and hopefully preserve the car. After three
    days of tough treatment, the end was almost in sight but car was beginning to make ominous
    noises.&nbsp; After two non-finishes the Lads were desperate for a result this year and
    the finish line at Cheltenham Racecourse couldn&#146;t have come soon enough. </font></p>
    <p><font SIZE="+0"><font COLOR="#FF00FF">After almost 1500 miles and
    three long days behind the wheel, the Lads finished their first RAC Rally with a
    creditable <a HREF="LADS_results.htm#1997">59th position</a> overall and the 32nd amateur crew home, much
    to the delight of the Lads service crew and their growing fan club!<br>
    </font>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    <img SRC="images/photo13.jpg" ALT="photo13.jpg (17871 bytes)" width="300" height="226"><br>
    </font><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">For the 1998 season the Lads against a
    full championship series, but instead would tackle some selected rounds to suit. The year
    began with the Bournemouth Winter Rally, the opening round of the Mintex National Rally
    Championship. The Lads were lying second in class and well into the top 20 when the car
    went into two wheel drive. Not long after it went into no wheel drive and the Lads retired
    on the spot. Next up was the Astra Stages in North Wales. With four wheel drive now
    restored and some overnight snow before the rally, the Lads were immediately on the pace
    and ended the day 3rd in class and 8th overall, their first top ten finish on a national
    event.</font></p>
    <p><font COLOR="#FF00FF" FACE="Arial" SIZE="+0">The best was yet to come however and the
    Lads entered the Essex based Kune Tune Rally early in July. For the first time ever, the
    Lads were regarded as pre-event favourites and they lived up to their billing by scoring
    their <a HREF="LADS_results.htm#1998">first overall victory</a>. The decision to return to the &#145;Grin
    Factor&#146; seems to have been a good one...cos the Lads are still grinning.</font></p>
