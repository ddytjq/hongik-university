  &nbsp;<p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 4" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>As
  in 1966, no British team &quot;qualified&quot; for the World Cup.&nbsp; England of
  course, were invited as champions of the world.&nbsp; The Mexicans didn't want
  us to retain the trophy and showed this by whistling and jeering our flag
  during the opening ceremony.&nbsp; The first game of the group pitched us
  against Romania and a single goal by Geoff Hurst settled the tie, but the next
  game was against champions-elect, Brazil.&nbsp; An army of Brazilian
  supporters drove around England's hotel in the early hours of the morning
  making as much noise as possible to try and disturb the champions, and it
  worked.&nbsp; In the 98 degrees heat, the already tired England team left the
  field at half time looking good at 0-0 after Gordon Banks had made a brilliant
  save from a great Pele header.&nbsp; In the second half though, Pele set up
  Jairzinho to thunder a shot into the back of the net for the only goal of the
  game.&nbsp; England could and indeed should have equalised
 through Jeff Astle, the ball unfortunately fell
  to his weaker left foot just outside the 6 yard box but his shot went wide of
  the post.&nbsp; In the final game, England were fortunate to beat
  Czechoslovakia with a penalty and progress to the quarter finals as
  runners-up.&nbsp; If we had won the group, our
  path to the final would have been Peru and
  then Uruguay, but instead it was to be a re-run of the 66 final, though not
  the result.&nbsp; The team moved from Guadalajara to Leon to face the Germans,
  but star keeper Gordon Banks was taken ill with food poisoning and his
  replacement Peter Bonetti was later unfairly to be blamed for this
  defeat.&nbsp; Mullery and Peters had given us a 2-0 lead but goals from
  Beckenbauer and a clever Uwe Seeler header forced the game into extra
  time.&nbsp; Hurst had a perfectly good goal disallowed before Muller scored
  the winner, 3-2.</b></font>
