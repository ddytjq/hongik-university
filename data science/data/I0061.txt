<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2"><strong>Qualifying Phases Explained<br>
</strong>Five 'Groups' are involved in finally establishing the 32-team UEFA Champions
League. To begin with, the title-holders and the 15 'Group 1' clubs automatically qualify
for the UEFA Champions League. <br>
'<strong>Group 1</strong>' consists of the league champions from the nine top-rated
national associations from the UEFA Table of Coefficents, and the runners-up from the six
top-rated national associations from the same ranking. </font></p>

<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2">Three other groups are involved in the
three-round qualifying process. </font>

<ul>
  <li><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2"><strong>Q1:</strong> The First Qualifying Phase
    will consist of 22 clubs. These clubs, who make up '<strong>Group 4</strong>', are the
    league champions from the national associations ranked from 27th to 48th in the UEFA Table
    of Coefficients.</font></li>
  <li><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2"><strong>Q2:</strong> The Second Qualifying Phase
    will consist of 28 clubs. The 11 winners from the First Qualifying Phase are joined by 17
    clubs from '<strong>Group 3</strong>', which comprise the 11 champions from the
    associations ranked from 16th to 26th in the UEFA Table of Coefficients as well as the six
    runners-up from associations ranked from 10th to 15th.</font></li>
  <li><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2"><strong>Q3: </strong>The clubs who advance from
    the Third Qualifying Phase will participate in the UEFA Champions League. This 14 clubs
    who advance from the Second Qualifying Phase are joined by the 18 clubs from '<strong>Group
    2</strong>'. These 18 clubs are drawn from four sources: firstly, there are the six
    third-placed clubs from the national associations ranked from 1st to 6th, secondly, there
    are three fourth-placed clubs from the national associations ranked from 1st to 3rd,
    thirdly there are three runners-up from the national associations ranked from 7th to 9th
    and, finally, there are the six champions from the national associations ranked from 10th
    to 15th in the UEFA Table of Coefficients.</font></li>
</ul>

<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2"><strong>UEFA Champions League<br>
</strong>Once the qualifiers from the Third Qualifying Phase are known, the make-up of the
32-club UEFA Champions League is complete. Two group phases are completed before the
competition reverts to the traditional knock-out finish! The UEFA Executive Committee has
decided that matches in the competition from this stage will be played on Tuesday and
Wednesday evenings.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2">In <strong>UEFA Champions League Group Phase 1</strong>,
the 32 qualifying clubs are divided into eight groups of four. In league format, each club
plays one another both at home and away, meaning six matchdays for the participants.
&nbsp; </font></p>

<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2">At the end of UEFA Champions League Group Phase
1, the eight group winners and runners-up qualify for the <strong>UEFA Champions League
Group Phase 2</strong>. Here, four groups of four clubs exist, and, again, clubs play each
other on a home-and-away basis, meaning six further matchdays.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica" size="2">At the culmination of UEFA Champions League Group
Phase 2, the four winners and runners-up advance to the <strong>Quarter-Finals</strong>,
when the competition adopts the knock-out format that currently exists. Both the
Quarter-Finals and <strong>Semi-Finals</strong> will be played over two legs, and at the
end of this process, two teams will contest the UEFA Champions League <strong>Final</strong>.</font></p>
