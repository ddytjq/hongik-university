<a href="http://www.ipac.caltech.edu/Outreach/Edu/discovery.html">
DISCOVERY OF INFRARED</a>
<br>
Learn about Sir William Herschel and how he
discovered infrared light.
<ul>
<a href="http://sirtf.caltech.edu/Education/Herschel/herschel.html">
Discovering Infrared - The Herschel Experiment</a>
<br>  
A simple, low cost
classroom or science fair activity in which students can discover
infrared for themselves.  This site includes a detailed description
of the experiment, an example with pictures, and a place for students
to display results online.
</ul>

<p>
<a href="http://www.ipac.caltech.edu/Outreach/Edu/importance.html">
INFRARED ASTRONOMY - AN OVERVIEW</a>
<br>
What is Infrared Astronomy and what are the major advantages of viewing
the Universe in the infrared?

<p>
<a href="http://www.ipac.caltech.edu/Outreach/Edu/Windows/irwindows.html">
ATMOSPHERIC WINDOWS</a>
<br>
Which wavelengths of infrared light can be observed from the ground and which
are blocked by our atmosphere?  This site shows why observatories need
to be sent into space, discusses infrared wavelength bands, sky transparency,
and sky brightness.

<p>
<a href="http://www.ipac.caltech.edu/Outreach/Edu/Regions/irregions.html">
NEAR, MID AND FAR INFRARED</a>
<br>
This site goes over the division of infrared light into three regions: 
near, mid and far-infrared.   It presents an overview of objects that appear
and disappear from view as we look at the Universe in each of these 
spectral regions.
