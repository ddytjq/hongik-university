<p>A fabulous 3 day tournament, hosted by Jay and Jill Butters, in Trefriw,
North Wales, United Kingdom. This was the most sociable tournament I've
ever been to. Most of the people (over 40 !) could stay at the Butters
spacious house or camp in the garden. A large number of throwers came from
the continent (Germany, Denmark, Switzerland) and challenged the British
throwers to excellent performances.
<p>The long distance tournament was scheduled for Monday. We had one round
of 5 throws. The wind was fairly stable in direction and strength, and
the sun was shining about 50 % of the time. You could say these are ideal
conditions. We noticed, however, that the boomerangs didn't fly as far
as they appeared. There must have been some sort of uneven airflow in the
region where we threw our boomerangs, because they always tended to "sink"
just before the apex, making a return flightpath less probable. Booms that
usually go out > 140 m under such conditions constantly failed to return.
Anyway, Markus won his first LD tournament and was (is !) completely over
the moon. My performance was mediocre, to say the least. Adam McLaughlin,
whose brother recently broke the British record in <a href="shrews01.htm">Shrewsbury</a>,
threw a Voyager, made by Oli Thienhaus, he obtained at the auction held
the night before. Oli originally threw over 140 m with it. The British
record is definitely in danger again !
