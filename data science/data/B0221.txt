        <p>ISA - the Individual Savings Account - is a tax efficient savings option which replaced TESSAs and PEPs in April 1999.</p><p>What Investment Options does an ISA offer?</p><p>There are three ISA options:</p><p>Maxi ISA - Invest in stocks and shares only, or stocks and shares, cash and life assurance from a single manager <br>Mini ISA - each of the above components from different managers <br>TESSA-only ISA - for the capital only from your maturing TESSA. This does not affect subscription limits to your Maxi or Mini ISA. <br>At Progressive we offer the Cash Mini ISA and the TESSA-only ISA.</p><p>The Cash Mini ISA</p><p>You can open a Cash Mini ISA for as little as £10. You can add to this at *any time up to a limit of £3,000 per current tax year*. You cannot subscribe to a Maxi ISA or another Cash Mini ISA in the same tax year, although you can subscribe to a Mini Stocks and Shares ISA and to Mini Life Assurance ISA. If you would like more information on the Cash Mini ISA, feel free to ask an advisor at any Progressive branch.</p><p>* A limit of £3000 in the current tax year (2000-2001), and £3000 in each subsequent tax year until April 2006</p><p>Existing Tessa?</p><p>If you have a TESSA maturing, the good news is you can invest your Entire capital in a TESSA-only ISA. This will not affect your annual ISA allowance/limit. The TESSA-only ISA is similar to the TESSA 2 option in that you can take your original investment further.</p><p>How do I open an ISA?</p><p>To open an ISA you must be over 18 and resident in the UK. You can only open one Maxi ISA per tax year or 3 Mini ISA's (One of each component) per tax year. This means if you are married, you and your spouse can each have an ISA. From the 6th April 2001 providing you are 16 or 17 and rersident in the UK, you can open either a mini cash ISA or the cash component of a MAXI ISA. You need to bring with you relevant identification such as a current driving licence or full passport, verification of your address (for example, a utility bill) and present these to a customer services representative at any Progressive branch. When the account is set up, we'll issue you with a passbook so that you can keep track of all your transactions.</p><p>Interest<br>Interest is calculated daily from the day after the account is opened to the day before you withdraw your money. Interest rates are variable but you can be sure that at Progressive we aim to offer you a highly competitive return on your investment.</p><p>Interest paid is exempt from income tax.</p><p>Interest is paid annually on 1st January.</p><p>Withdrawals</p><p>You can withdraw up to £250 a day in cash at any Progressive branch or agent. Amounts above £250 are paid by cheque. Withdrawals do not affect deposit limits. Once the limits have been reached for the tax year, investors may make no further subscriptions, regardless of withdrawals.</p><p>If you change your mind...</p><p>There is a 14-day cooling-off period from the date of opening during which you can opt to close your account for any reason, subject to our normal cheque clearing requirements. We will return your original investment plus interest earned.</p><p>What do I do now?</p><p>Call into any Progressive branch and make the most of your tax efficient savings with an ISA today.</p><p>Cash Mini ISA</p><p>The cash deposit component of an ISA is subject to the Banking Code and is not an investment under the Financial Services Act 1986.</p><p>The Government has issued CAT standards for each of the three ISA components covering:</p><p>Reasonable Charges <br>Easy Access <br>Fair Terms <br>The Society is committed to meeting or exceeding the CAT standards. Tax assumptions are those currently applicable and are subject to statutory change.</p>
