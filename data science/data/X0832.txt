				<font class="newsdate"> Monday, December 3, 2001 13:29 (GMT)</font><br>
				<img src="/1.gif" width="1" height="18"><br>
				<font class="news">The buildup to the First Test in Mohali was unprecedented. The performance of England's batsmen during the first day was not.<br><br>The tourists began brightly, with Nasser Hussain and Marcus Trescothick taking the upper hand against an inexperienced seam-bowling attack featuring three Test debutants - Sanjay Bangar, Iqbal Siddiqui and Tinu Yohannan.<br><br>But like so often before, the loss of a few English wickets led to a middle-order collapse that enabled India to reclaim the ascendancy.<br><br>Trescothick and skipper Hussain had taken England to a quick-fire 107-1 at lunch after recovering well from the loss of Mark Butcher in the first over of the day for just four runs.<br><br>However, Trescothick went for 66 when he failed to offer a shot at a ball that pitched just outside and nipped back to remove his off-stump, handing Yohannan his second wicket on his Test debut.<br><br>That brought in Graham Thorpe on 129-2 and after some early scares, it looked like the Surrey left-hander and Hussain could put England into a dominant position on a pitch that was expected to be more of a help to the seamers than the spinners.<br><br>But when Siddiqui, who had hardly threatened the England batsmen, forced Thorpe to edge a ball to VVS Laxman at second slip for 23, it was set up nicely for an Indian onslaught.<br><br>Step forward Harbhajan Singh and Anil Kumble.<br><br>Kumble was first to strike as he grabbed the vital wicket of Hussain, who offered Laxman a simple bat-pad catch for his third claim of the day.<br><br>It was a disappointing way to go for the England skipper, who had looked assured for long periods but began to look susceptible as he took on the spinners.<br><br>He had been dropped by wicketkeeper Deep Dasgupta in the 70s, hit Harbhajan for a six but then edged a ball just short of the lively Laxman before he finally went for 85.<br><br>Despite the 33-year-old failing to reach the 10th century of his Test career, he will be encouraged by his knock of 85 that came from 147 balls and included 14 boundaries.<br><br>The loss of Hussain, however, saw England slip to 200-4 and gave the home side a huge confidence lift.<br><br>Mark Ramprakash and Andrew Flintoff saw the tourists to tea, but their defiance was short-lived, with the former finding Shiv Sunder Das to hand Harbhajan his first wicket of the day.<br><br>Flintoff didn't last much longer as his attempt to hit Harbhajan all the way to Delhi only went as far as the safe hands of Kumble at gully.<br><br>Wicketkeeper James Foster joined Craig White in the middle and faced 16 balls before he exited without scoring, out lbw to become the third victim for Harbhajan.<br><br>White remained England's great hope, but the Yorkshireman had no answer to the turning of the screw as Kumble got in on the act, picking up his second wicket of the day courtesy of a Rahul Dravid catch as England were left dumbstruck on 229-8.<br><br>It was left to Harbhajan to wrap up the tail and he did so in the space of two balls. Richard Dawson (5) and Matthew Hoggard (0) made sure the man known as 'The Turbanator' collected his fifth five-wicket performance of his brief career. The 21-year-old from Jullundur finished with figures of 5-51 to complete the turnaround in fortunes for the home side.<br><br>India's response was solid, with openers Dasgupta and Das seeing off England's own inexperienced new-ball attack of Hoggard and James Ormond.<br><br>The day finished on a slightly disappointing note for India when Butcher bowled Das for two to leave them on 24-1.<br><br>But with the likes of Sachin Tendulkar, Dravid and Laxman still to take on an England bowling lineup further weakened by the loss of the injured Ashley Giles, it promises to get even better for the hosts on day two.</font><br>
