	<td width="75%">
		<b>Sierra Club needs some cheese with their Ontario whine                                              </b><br>
		<font size=2>Author: Phil Morlock                                       <br>
		Source: Guest Editorial                                                                                                                                       </font>
	</td>

	<td align=right VALIGN=TOP>		
				Tues.          June 18, 2002
	</td>
	</tr>
	<tr>
		<td colspan=2><br>
		<br>
				<P align=justify>It is a pitiful but revealing commentary on the ethics of the environmental lobby to see the U.S. based Sierra Club launching a media blitz across Ontario in an attempt to discredit the motives of millions of Ontario residents who simply want to take their kids fishing so they may experience their magnificent outdoor heritage . The Sierra Club has launched a full scale attack in the press on the integrity of thousands of dedicated conservation volunteers who donate their time and effort to successfully protect and improve fish and wildlife populations and habitat across the province. </P>
<P align=justify>As one of those volunteers, I believe it is time to call a spade a spade - although I have yet to ever encounter a member of the Sierra Club getting their hands dirty using a shovel at a fish or wildlife conservation project. </P>
<P align=justify>The Sierra Club justifies its misleading press campaign by claiming to be opposing Bill 135 - the Heritage Hunting and Fishing Act which will legally recognize the rights of anyone to fish or hunt in accordance with the law in Ontario. The proposed legislation has the support of Premier Ernie Eve's government and the Ontario Liberals. The public consultation resulted in an overwhelming 97 per cent of respondents indicating support . </P>
<P align=justify>If the government protects my right to take my kids fishing, the Sierra Club says this will discriminate against "non-consumptive" users of the outdoors - people who want to go camping, hiking or bird watching. Wait a minute - isn't this the same Sierra Club who is lobbying to ban mountain bikes on public lands out west ? Oops - so much for claiming to represent the non-consumptive users. </P>
<P align=justify>The facts are, led by the efforts of people who fish and hunt, the success of fish and wildlife management and restoration of wild species on the North American continent stands as the greatest environmental success story in the history of the world. The very principles of protected wild areas, parks and conservation as a science were promoted and developed by people who hunt and fish. Groups like the Sierra Club have positioned themselves to claim credit to an "environmentalist" title to which they are not entitled - at least not if investing time, money and sweat equity into fish and wildlife restoration and habitat improvement counts for anything. </P>
<P align=justify>In Ontario it counts for a great deal. In every region of the province, thousands of men, women and young people who fish and hunt are actively involved donating tens of thousands of hours in front line conservation efforts - often in partnership with the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources. Ontario MNR has pioneered some of the most progressive public volunteer programs in the world - and they are funded by hunting and fishing license revenues. Fish and wildlife are a barometer of the quality of our environment, and when they benefit the very quality of our lives is improved. </P>
<P align=justify>People fish and hunt for some very profound and deeply personal reasons that are as relevant today as ever. Although we are in the minority in our increasingly urban society, groups like the Sierra Club do not have a right to misrepresent our motives and impose their will on the outdoor heritage activities of millions of Ontarians. </P>
<P align=justify>According to the Sierra Club, "A book written on the conservation principles of the Ontario Federation of Anglers and Hunters would be a candidate for the smallest volume ever penned." Not true based on what I witnessed first hand as the Chair of the Ontario Fish and Wildlife Advisory Board where our responsibilities included advising the Minister of Natural Resources on all fish and wildlife programs in Ontario. </P>
<P align=justify>During my two year term, we received multiple suggestions and extensive science based submissions from the OFAH on a wide range of positive initiatives for the benefit of multiple species of fish and wildlife. Over the same period, we did not receive a single suggestion, proposal or submission on behalf of any fish or wildlife species from the Sierra Club. </P>
<P align=justify>Actions speak louder than press campaigns. </P>
<P align=justify><EM>(Phil Morlock was the Chair of the inaugural Ontario Fish &amp; Wildlife Advisory Board, and is a wildlife biologist by profession )</EM> </P>
