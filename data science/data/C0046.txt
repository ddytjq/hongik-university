            <p class="MsoBodyText" style="margin-top:0cm;mso-pagination:widow-orphan" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB" class="font">We 
              have made further progress towards the goal of becoming an Integrated 
              Lloyd’s Vehicle based on the Chaucer managed syndicates.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>With our joint venture company, Chaucer Dedicated Limited, 
              we now control for the 2001 underwriting year £163.3 million of 
              Chaucer syndicate capacity, representing 63.7%.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>Following the acquisition in April 2001 of a further shareholding 
              in Chaucer Dedicated, our own underwriting interest in Chaucer syndicates 
              for the 2001 year is £107.1 million. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt" class="font">The 
              raising of new capital in April 2001 is a very significant step 
              for the Group and will be of great benefit.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>The majority of the funds will be used to increase the Group’s 
              underwriting participations for the 2002 underwriting year.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>The strengthened balance sheet should also enable the Group 
              to replace existing gearing policies on more advantageous terms. 
              </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt" class="font">The 
              results we are now reporting comprise a number of elements and, 
              as was explained last year, in this transitional phase from ‘spread’ 
              to ‘integrated’ underwriting, shareholders may not find the position 
              as easily understandable as a conventional insurance company.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>For the year to 31 December 2000, we account for calendar 
              year fees, expenses and investment income and gains.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>However in underwriting terms, we account for the results 
              of the 1998 year of account and make provisions for expected losses 
              in 1999 and 2000.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Over 
              80% of underwriting participations for 1998 are based on syndicates 
              managed by third parties.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; 
              </span>The position will become more transparent in subsequent years, 
              particularly as we will move to annual accounting next year.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>A pro forma annual accounting basis of our results will be 
              included in the published accounts. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoBodyText" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span class="font"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">Current underwriting markets </span></b></span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" style="mso-outline-level: 1" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">Despite 
              the poor underwriting conditions that prevailed during much of 2000, 
              significant changes are finally taking place and it is with this 
              new momentum that we are optimistic for the 2001 year and beyond. 
              </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" style="mso-outline-level: 1" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">The 
              2000 underwriting year is expected to produce a loss for the Lloyd’s 
              market and it is only the lack of catastrophes in that year that 
              will prevent market losses being in line with the 1998 and 1999 
              years of account.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>For 
              much of the year, the rating environment in the marine, non-marine 
              and aviation markets showed little, if any, improvement over 1999 
              and resulted in a number of syndicate cessations.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>Motor did however continue the improvement in conditions 
              that started in 1999. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" style="mso-outline-level: 1" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">The 
              renewal season for 2001 has finally confirmed that the long awaited 
              rating improvements have appeared in many classes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>The motor market has also continued to gather momentum and 
              is witnessing significant rate increases. The reinsurance market 
              has experienced major rate and deductible strengthening.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>This increased cost of reinsurance protection has had the 
              effect of raising direct rates in most classes of business. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoBodyText" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span lang="EN-US" style="mso-bidi-font-family:

              to the policy threshold.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>As

            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">The 
              1998 year of account for syndicate 587 has been left open due to 
              the uncertainty that arises pending the resolution of a reinsurance 
              dispute. The 1998 year of account loss stated above is as determined 
              at 31 December 2000 and may be subject to change depending on the 
              outcome of the dispute. The dispute involves four Australian reinsurance 
              companies and we are hopeful that the issue will be resolved in 
              2001. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">This 
              time last year, we resolved to address the very disappointing losses 
              that had been reported for syndicate 587’s 1997 year of account 
              and forecast for the open years.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>Although these losses are in line with the market average 
              in the motor market, which has experienced a dreadful period of 
              underwriting losses, the Group and the syndicate resolved that the 
              situation was unacceptable. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">On 
              1 May 2000, Mary Towndrow, formerly deputy underwriter, was appointed 
              as Active Underwriter of the syndicate.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>A new divisional board, under the Chairmanship of Bob Stuchbery, 
              the Group’s Underwriting Director, was set up with the remit to 
              review every aspect of the syndicate’s activity.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>This exercise has taken place during 2000 and each line of 
              business, claims procedure and syndicate operation has been examined 
              and where necessary either eradicated or redefined.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>Extensive work has been carried out by external actuaries 
              and the syndicate has recruited a personal lines actuary to head 
              the new Management Information Department. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">We 
              are confident that the hard work undertaken over the last year has 
              placed the syndicate in a strong position whereby it will be able 
              to benefit fully from the improved trading conditions and the syndicate 
              will explore all possible avenues to ensure that in the future it 
              provides profitable returns to its capital providers.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span> </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">We 
              are pleased with the profitable result produced by our Marine syndicate 
              1084.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>This result compares 
              favourably with the marine market as a whole, which for the 1998 
              year of account has made a significant loss.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>The forecast loss for the syndicate’s 1999 year is the first 
              since Hugh Hayward became active underwriter for the 1989 year.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>In view of the number and size of insurance catastrophes in 
              1999 and the syndicate’s large excess of loss book, the forecast 
              compares favourably with its peer group. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">We 
              are obviously disappointed that our Non-marine syndicate 1096 is 
              showing losses for 1998 and 1999.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>However once again the forecasts stands up well in comparison 
              with its market peers and, to date, the syndicate is showing a good 
              progress for 2000. </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoPlainText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="color: #330066" class="font">Chaucer 
              also managed Aviation syndicate 173, which underwrote through a 
              consortium arrangement with Amlin syndicate 2001. The 1998 result, 
              being the syndicate’s final year, is a loss of 15.6% and the year 
              of account has been reinsured to close into Amlin’s syndicate 2001. 
              </span></font></p>

normal"><span style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">Quota Share Reinsurance arrangements 
              </span></b></span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoBodyText" align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#330066"><span style="mso-ansi-language:
EN-GB" class="font">In May 2000, we entered into a capital provision quota share 
              reinsurance arrangement with Zurich Insurance Company whereby Zurich 
              agreed to procure the provision of a £4.95 million letter of credit 
              as funds at Lloyd’s for Chaucer Holdings.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>In return the Group has agreed to pay to Zurich the profits 
              (or receive compensation for losses) on an £11.0 million underwriting 
              line on syndicate 587 for the 2000 year of account.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; 
              </span>This arrangement enabled the Group to substitute the letter 
              of credit for investments of comparable value which were deposited 
              at Lloyd’s.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The investments 
              were then sold and the proceeds have been used to complete the financing 
              of the freehold building at Whitstable and the balance used to pay 
              underwriting losses.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The 
              arrangement has continued for the 2001 year with an increased Letter 
              of Credit of £6.0 million supporting capacity of £15.0 million on 
              syndicate 587. </span></font></p>

              Holdings.<span style="mso-spacerun:

              million on syndicate 1096 for the 2001 year of account. </span></font></p>
