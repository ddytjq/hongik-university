    <b>FIFA World Player of the Year 2001</b><br>
<b><font face="Arial, Sans-Serif" size="4" color="#000080" class="ab4">
Luis Figo crowned for the first time </font></b><br>
<font face="Arial, Sans-Serif" size="3" class="a3">

<p>

<table width=100 cellpadding=0 cellspacing=8 border=0 align="right">

<a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33128+E">Luis Figo</a> is the 2001 <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+events/playergala/index+E">FIFA World Player of the Year</a>. The talented Portuguese playmaker heads the list for the first time ever, having finished second in last year’s vote. England captain <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33127+E">David Beckham</a> came second in the voting, with Figo’s Real Madrid team-mate, <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33129+E">Raúl</a> of Spain, finishing third. In the women’s category, USA star <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33106+E">Mia Hamm</a> topped the list ahead of China’s <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33108+E">Sun Wen</a> and her fellow American <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?s7:gp::67173+article+33109+E">Tiffeny Milbrett</a>.

    <font face="verdana, arial, sans-serif" size="2" class="v2">

    <b>FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking</b><br>
<b><font face="Arial, Sans-Serif" size="4" color="#000080" class="ab4">
France, Costa Rica and Honduras take awards</font></b>
<p>

France, Costa Rica and Honduras received special prizes at today’s FIFA World Player Gala in Zurich for their exploits this year in the FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking.
<p>
<img src="http://a1801.g.akamai.net/f/1801/2004/3d/www.fifa.com/images2/logo_HON.gif" width="70" height="90" BORDER="0" VSPACE="0" HSPACE="8" align="left" alt="Federación Nacional Autónoma de Fútbol de Honduras">

This one was a real turn up for the books... Australia's World Cup-ending defeat by Uruguay in their final game undoubtedly played a significant role in the Team of the Year award going to Honduras, who incidentally also had to shunt their World Cup aspirations onto the buffers when they were beaten in their last game in Mexico City. No other team could amass so many points (and distinguish themselves as giant-killers) as the Hondurans did in their best matches.

The FIFA Committee for Security Matters and Fair Play agreed to award Paolo di Canio for a special act of good sportsmanship in an English Premiership match last season. Faced with the possibility of scoring an easy goal, di Canio chose instead to catch the ball with his hands and thus stop the play in order to allow the injured opposing goalkeeper to be treated.
<p>
A teenage Ghanaian player, Sumaila Abdallah, will receive a FIFA Fair Play diploma for having saved the life of an opponent with the kiss of life when the latter was knocked unconscious in a local match in Ghana. Both awards will be presented on the occasion of the FIFA World Player gala in Zurich on 17 December.
