    <div align="center"><center><table border="0" cellpadding="3" width="95%">
      <tr>
        <td><font color="#004000" size="1"><p align="center"></font><img
        src="../../../../images/Logos/winning_hoops6.gif" width="164" height="50"

        <td width="92%">
          <h2 align="center"><font size="1" color="#000000">Introduce Defense to
          Young Players With 10 Principles</font></h2>
          <h3 align="center"><font color="#000000"><small><small>By<br>
          </small>Patrick Suessmuth<br>
          Head Coach<br>
          Erin Hoops BC, Hillsburgh, Ontario, Canada</small></font></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>The 10 Defensive
          Principles that follow are a good framework for teaching defense to
          beginning players.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">1. Wet Blanket</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>This is where
          defense begins.&nbsp; A young player, never having had coaching, in
          unlikely to follow their opponent.&nbsp; They tend to follow the ball
          and lose focus on what they are supposed to do on defense.&nbsp; To
          overcome this initial problem, simply instruct new players to
          &quot;cover their opponent like a wet blanket.&quot;&nbsp; Expand the
          wet blanket concept by stressing defensive fundamentals and hard work.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">2. Between</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Once novice
          defenders are covering their opponents like a wet blanket, they're
          ready for the first adjustment.&nbsp; It is simple.&nbsp; Always cover
          the opponent in a way that places the defender between the opponent
          and the basket at all times.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">3. Stance</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>&quot;Butt down,
          hands up.&quot;&nbsp; In developing this principle I ask the players,
          &quot;How do sprinters start races?&quot;&nbsp; The answer is low,
          down in a crouch.&nbsp; This gives them more initial power and greater
          initial speed. That is what we need on defense; power and speed.&nbsp;
          Being low and balanced gives you 50 percent more speed than if you are
          standing up straight.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">4. Key Prevent</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>The concept here
          is block the slash, i.e., don't let your opponent cut across the key
          (or anywhere else for that matter. Prevent the cut. Step in their
          path.&nbsp; Take/initiate the bump that will occur.&nbsp; Be rugged.
          Physically prevent them from getting into the key.&nbsp; Yes, it is
          legal and use it.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">5. Force Left</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>If the opponent is
          naturally right handed, make them use their left hand, so that they
          are forced left.&nbsp; To do this, defend to take away the person's
          path toward the right.&nbsp; Give them a path to their left.&nbsp;</small></small></font></p>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>In initially
          teaching the &quot;force-left&quot; principle, have the person doing
          the checking be over-extended on the right side of the opponent.&nbsp;
          Anything less than this does not get the force-left concept
          across.&nbsp;</small></small></font></p>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>When a player is
          equally good with both hands, this principles changes to
          &quot;line-force&quot;, or forcing the opponent toward the nearest
          sideline or baseline using it as an extra defender.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">6. Help</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>When their
          opponent does not have the ball, have them move to a position where
          they can help the person guard the opponent who has the ball.&nbsp; If
          the ball does down the side, everyone should be on the ball side
          (strong side) over the center line dividing the court the length of
          the court.&nbsp; While you're teaching this, over-emphasize moving to
          the help side, Without this, kids seem to miss the point.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">7. Deny</font></small></small></h3>
          <blockquote>
            <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080">Basic Deny</font></small></small></h3>
            <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>The defender
            positions themselves:</small></small></font></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>On the
                strong side (ball side) of their opponent</small></small></font></li>
              <li>
                <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Between
                their opponent and the basket but semi in the passing lane.</small></small></font></li>
              <li>
                <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Have an arm
                extended so the hand is in the passing lane with the palm toward
                the ball.</small></small></font></li>
            </ul>
            <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080">Frontal Deny</font></small></small></h3>
            <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>This occurs with
            post players and others when their opponent is more than a half
            court away from the ball.&nbsp; Defenders should position themselves
            fully between the ball and their opponent. Look to steal or deflect
            a long pass.&nbsp; The fronting distance is dictated by the passer's
            capability.&nbsp; As the distance of the ball from the opponent
            decreases, they should decrease the distance from their opponent
            until eventually they're playing a basic blanket deny.</small></small></font></p>
          </blockquote>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">8. Box Out</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Every defender, on
          every shot will box out.&nbsp; (People must yell out
          &quot;shot&quot;). Teaching box out an rebounding I leave to you. I
          teach my team to turn and put their butt into their opponent's belly
          and hold this position for two seconds.</small></small></font></p>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">9. Never Switch</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>This is a
          controversial principle.&nbsp; Some coaches disagree with this
          concept.&nbsp; I use it with players once they have to start to cope
          with effective picks because I do not want my players to quit when
          they encounter a pick.&nbsp; Switching is okay once people have
          learned to fight over picks.</small></small></font></p>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>In this principle:</small></small></font></p>
          <ul>
            <li>
              <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Fight/Squeeze
              through the space between the pick and your check.&nbsp; Go over,
              around and spin off picks.&nbsp;</small></small></font></li>
            <li>
              <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Call,
              &quot;double&quot; on picks and double-team the person with the
              ball.</small></small></font></li>
            <li>
              <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Work to never
              need to switch. Your players should take pride in this aggressive
              defensive aspect of the game.</small></small></font></li>
          </ul>
          <h3 align="left"><small><small><font color="#008000">10. Communication</font></small></small></h3>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Your players
          should constantly talk on defense.&nbsp;&nbsp;</small></small></font></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><i><font color="#000080">&quot;Right or
          Left&quot;</font></i><font color="#000000">--To alert a teammate on
          upcoming picks.</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080"><i>&quot;Double&quot;</i></font><font color="#000000">--To
          call out when a double team should occur.</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080"><i>&quot;Dead&quot;-</i></font><font color="#000000">-You
          are checking a person with the ball who has used up their dribble.</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080"><i>&quot;Slide&quot;</i></font><font color="#000000">--Call
          this and help you teammate move between your opponent and you. It is
          fair to pull and push a teammate to help get them through faster.</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080"><i>&quot;I've Got
          Left/Right&quot;</i></font><font color="#000000">--Use this when
          you've dropped off your opponent and are taking the potential drive
          away from a teammate's check who has the ball.&nbsp; This allows your
          teammate to adjust their defense accordingly.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><small><small><font color="#000080"><i>&quot;Help&quot;</i></font><font color="#000000">--Alerts
          your teammates that you have lost your opponent on their drive and
          need someone to help you defensively.&nbsp;</font></small></small></p>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Use these simply
          one work cues to help teach situation and communication.</small></small></font></p>
          <p align="left"><font color="#000000"><small><small>Be sure to visit
          other great Winning Hoops feature articles here on the Highway.</small></small></font>
