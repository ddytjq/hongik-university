					<h1>The Non-Human Domain: The Gen-Lex Package</h1>
					<p class="description"><strong>The idea of elaborating an all encompassing law on gene-technology in the non-human domain was rejected in favor of the Gen-Lex Package. The package foresees the adaptation of existing laws to cope with the issues raised by gene-technology. The Law on Environment (USG) is to function as the central law for the better coordination of the revision. The USG will be binding for all organisms as long as a stricter law does not already apply.</strong></p>
				</td>
			</tr>

			<tr>
				<td width="25%" valign="top" class="side"> &nbsp;</td>
				<td width="75%" valign="top">
			<p>With the choice of the USG as the main law of the "Gen-Lex", the supervision of the revision is shifted from the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/bvet/">BVET</a> to the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/buwal/">BUWAL</a>. The Bundesrat formulated the main points of the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/buwal/presse/2000/d0001191.htm">new guidelines for gene-technology</a> on January 19, 2000:</p>
			<ul>
				<li><p>The dignity of all living organisms.</p></li>
				<li><p>Biological diversity.</p></li>
				<li><p>Strict responsibility for producers.</p></li>
				<li><p>Case-to-case examinations of demands for dissemination of genetically modified organisms.</p></li>
				<li><p>Transparence and communication.</p></li>
			</ul>
			<p>In addition to significant <a href="http://www.admin.ch/uvek/doku/presse/2000/d/00030106.pdf">alterations in the Law on Environment</a> some other laws were revised, adapted to the USG and coordinated. The information pertaining to these changes in the USG and the draft thereof were published on March 1, 2000.</p>
			<p>Moreover, the Laws on <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/923_0/">Fishing (BGF)</a>, on <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/922_0/index.html">Hunting (JSG)</a>, on <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/814_20/">Lakes and Rivers (GSchG)</a> and on <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/921_0/">  Woods (WaG)</a> were modified to match the revision of the USG.</p>
			<p>The revision of the <a href="http://www.admin.ch./ch/d/sr/2/232.14.de.pdf">Law on Intellectual Property (PatG)</a> has been postponed until the end of the revision of the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/i2/0.232.142.2.de.pdf">European Agreements on Intellectual Property</a>.</p>
			<p> New laws concerning issues closely linked to gene-technology are treated independently from the revision of the USG, e.g. the 	<a href="http://www.admin.ch/bag/chemikal/gesetz/d/chemges.htm">Law on Chemical Products</a> and the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/bag/heilmitt/gesetz/d/">Law on Therapeutic Products	</a>, which are mentioned in the context of the IDAGEN report.</p>
		</td>
			</tr>

				<td width="75%" valign="top">
			<h2>The Federal Law on the Protection of the Environment (<a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/814_01/">Umweltschutzgesetz, USG</a>)</h2>
			<p>Status as of October 21, 1997</p>
			<p>The USG is the basic law of the Gen-Lex Package. The revision of the USG stipulates the protection of the dignity of all living organisms. This dignity will be defined by the Ethics Commission of the Non-Human Domain (EKAH). In this context the definition of environmental influence has been broadened in order to provide the necessary protection in the handling of genetically modified or pathogenic organisms. The Federal Commission for Biological Security (EFBS) will exert a strict control regulating the safety of the dissemination of GMOs and of the use of the latter in closed systems. Both dissemination and use of GMOs are subject to separate approval. The risks for the environment in the context of biodiversity and environmental sustainability, i.e. sustainable use, have to be taken into account and adapted to the international rules on biodiversity.</p>
			<p>In general, the handling of GMOs is dictated from production to disposal by an obligation to act prudently and responsibly. This responsibility is also addressed in the USG.</p>
			<p>Furthermore, there is a legal obligation to communicate and clarify information to the consumers.</p>
			<br /><h3>Already implemented:</h3>
			<ul>
				<li><p>the <a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/8/814.012.de.pdf">Ordinance on Accidents (StFV)</a>
				</p></li>
				<li><p> the <a href="http://www.uvek.admin.ch/doku/presse/1999/d/99082505.htm">Ordinance on Dissemination (FrSV)</a>
				</p></li>
				<li><p> the <a href="http://www.uvek.admin.ch/doku/presse/1999/d/99082507.pdf">Ordinance on Closed Systems (ESV)</a>
				</p></li>
				<li><p> the <a href="http://www.uvek.admin.ch/doku/presse/1999/d/99082508.pdf">Ordinance for the Protection of Employees from Biological Dangers (SAMV)</a>
				</p></li>
			</ul>
			<p>Supervision: <a href="http://www.admin.ch/buwal/">BUWAL</a>
			</p>

				<td width="75%" valign="top">
			<h2>Federal Law on the Protection of Animals (<a href="http://www.admin.ch/ch/d/sr/4/455.de.pdf">Tierschutzgesetz, TSchG</a>)</h2>
			<p>Status as of July 1, 1995:</p>
			<p>Revision of stockbreeding in general (prohibiting breeding under painful conditions) and the addition of a clause regulating gene-technology experiments on animals, i.e. transgenic animals. The most important changes concern the production, breeding, keeping and use of animals and the preservation of their dignity.</p>
			<p>Supervision: <a href="http://www.admin.ch/bvet/">BVET</a>
			</p>
		</td>

			<p>Stricter regulations concerning the declaration and approval of GMOs and products in which they are used.</p>
