<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Interest"><font color="#0000FF">Interest</font></a>
- <small>Interest is calculated on a daily basis from the day following the date of
investment until the date prior to withdrawal. If you ask, we will give you a full
explanation of how we work out interest.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><a href="#Top">Back </a>to the top</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong><a name="Interest Rate Variation"><font color="#0000FF">Interest
Rate Variation</font></a> - <small>Variations are publicised in the Times and Surrey
Mirror and in the Society's offices.</small></strong></font></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><a href="#Top">Back </a>to the top</font></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="A.E.R."><font color="#0000FF">A.E.R.</font></a> -<small>
This is the annual equivalent rate if interest is credited to the account. Except where
stated, interest is paid or added on 30th September and 31st March. Interest rates are
variable. Variations are publicised in selected newspapers and a list is available on
request.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><a href="#Top">Back </a>to the top</font></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Cleared Funds"><font color="#0000FF">Cleared Funds</font></a>
- <small>Although the Society is pleased to accept investment by cheque, we do ask that
you allow a period of seven clear working days to elapse before withdrawing against such
investment.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><em><strong><font face="Arial"><small>In the case of unpaid cheques, no interest will
be earned and any interest so credited on the assumption that your cheque would be met
will be taken from the account.</small></font></strong></em></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><a href="#Top">Back </a>to the top</font></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Cheques"><font color="#0000FF">Cheques</font></a> -<small>
Cheques drawn by the Society on a customer's account, to a third party, will not normally
be stopped. &nbsp;However, in extreme circumstances this can be done, provided the account
holder notifies the Society in writing and pays the <a href="#Tariff of Charges">tariff
charge</a>. Cheques must be presented within six months or they become out of date. &nbsp;
Warrants drawn by the Society must be presented within three months or they will become
out of date.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Confidentiality"><font color="#0000FF">Confidentiality</font></a>
- <small>We will treat all your personal information as private and confidential (even
when you are no longer a customer). We will not reveal your name and address or details
about your account to anyone, including other companies in our group, other than in the
following four exceptional cases when we are allowed to do this by law.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong><small>1. If we have to give the information by law.</small><br>
<small>2. If there is a duty to the public to reveal the information.</small><br>
<small>3. If it is in our interests to give the information. But we will not use this as a
reason for giving information about you or your accounts (including your name and address)
to anyone else including other companies in our group for marketing purposes.</small><br>
<small>4. If you ask us to reveal the information, or if we have your permission.</small><br>
</strong><br>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Identification"><font color="#0000FF">Identification</font></a>
- <small>In accordance with the code of Banking Practice and Money Laundering Regulations
this Society has a legal responsibility to confirm the true identity of all applicants
seeking to open an account. In order to comply with these requirements for POSTAL
APPLICATIONS, we will need sight of <u>FOUR</u> of the following; Original documents <u>TWO
FROM EACH SECTION</u>.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><u><small>SECTION ONE</small></u><br>
<small>a) driving licence.</small><br>
<small>b) pay-slip which is less than three months old.</small><br>
<small>c) bank statement, building society statement or credit card statement which is
less than three months old.</small><br>
<small>d) mortgage statement which is less than three months old.</small><br>
<small>e) P60 which must be for the current year.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><u><small>SECTION TWO</small></u><br>
<small>a) Utility bill (gas, electricity, water, telephone or council tax) which is less
than three months old.</small><br>
<small>b) Inland Revenue communication.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small><u>FOR PERSONAL APPLICATIONS</u> we will need sight
of <u>TWO</u> of the following; Original documents <u>ONE FROM EACH SECTION</u>.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>SECTION ONE</small><br>
<small>a) passport</small><br>
<small>b) identity card</small><br>
<small>c) driving licence</small><br>
<small>d) credit card</small><br>
<small>e) birth certificate</small><br>
<small>f) pay-slip which is less than three months old</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>SECTION TWO</small><br>
<small>a) Utility bill (gas, electricity, water, telephone or council tax) which is less
than three months old.</small><br>
<small>b) Inland Revenue communication.</small><br>
<small>c) bank statement, building society statement or credit card statement which is
less than three months old.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>If the account is to be opened in joint names, the
Society will require proof of identity of all applicants. All documents will be returned
promptly.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Taxation"><font color="#0000FF">Taxation</font></a>
- <small>On 6th April 1991 the system for taxing interest paid by banks, building
societies and other financial institutions was changed. You can now arrange for the
interest on your accounts to be paid with no tax deducted if you are not liable to income
tax. That is, your total taxable income is less than your total tax allowance. Further
details are available on request from the Society.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Building Society's Investor Protection Scheme"><font
color="#0000FF">Building Society's Investor Protection Scheme</font></a> - <small>The
Holmesdale Building Society is a member of the Building Societies Investor Protection
Scheme established under the Building Societies Act 1986. Payments under this scheme are
limited to 90%&nbsp; of an investor's total shares and/or deposits subject to a maximum
payment to anyone investor of £18,000 (or the sterling equivalent of £20,000 euro if
greater). Most shares and deposits denominated in sterling, other European Economic Area
currencies and ECU made with offices of the building society within the European Economic
area are covered. Further details are available on request from the Society.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Cooling-Off"><font color="#0000FF">Cooling-Off</font></a>
- <small>If you are unhappy about your choice of account within 15 days of opening it, we

any notice period or any additional charges. If you change your mind you must inform us in

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><a name="Joint Accounts"><font color="#0000FF">Joint
Accounts</font></a> - <small>Two or more persons may jointly hold a share, unless the
terms of the share forbid a joint holding. Persons jointly holding a share shall be
entitled to choose the order in which they are named in the Society's records. However,
the first named of the joint holders will be regarded by the Society as the representative
joint holder.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>All joint holders of a share may authorise in writing
one of their number to withdraw the share or receive interest or bonus paid in respect
thereof. However, if one joint holder is given authority to operate the account and the
Society becomes aware of a dispute between the joint holders then both signatures will be
required to operate the account. These circumstances may prevail in the case of a divorce
or separation. If this is the case you should both come to the Society to arrange for the
joint accounts to be closed and new accounts opened in your sole names. If no such
authorisation is made all joint holders must sign notices of withdrawal and interest and
bonus payments will be made in the names of all joint holders.</small><br>
<small>Any reference in the Rules to any number of members of the Society shall be read as
if any shares held jointly were held by the representative joint holder only.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>No joint holder of shares, other than the
representative joint holder, may attend a meeting except as a proxy, or exercise any right
conferred by the Rules on a member.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>A member of the Society who is a representative joint
holder and who holds shares at the voting date is entitled to vote on a resolution subject
to the Rules of the Society.</small></font></strong></p>

<p><strong><font face="Arial"><small>Notice of every Annual General Meeting or Special
General Meeting shall be sent to the representative joint holder.</small></font></strong></p>
