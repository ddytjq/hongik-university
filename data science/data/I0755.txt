 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Sheffield United</H1><H2 class="entry">Bramall Lane</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/SheffUtd-Ground1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/SheffUtd-Ground1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Lever Stand" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Lever Stand</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/SheffUtd-Ground2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/SheffUtd-Ground2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Bramall Lane Stand 
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Bramall Lane Stand 
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Away fans are housed in the Bramall Lane End, normally just the
lower tier is used although for some games the upper tier is
allocated instead or even both for bigger games. The stand is covered and
seated and has no obscured views. It also offers food and beer and is one
of a growing number that now sell the once rare Chicken Balti pie.
</P><P>Drums or other musical instruments are not permitted inside the ground and
flags are only permitted if there is room to lay them out on unused seats.
The ground is 15 mins walk from the town centre.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=S24SU&Title=Bramall+Lane+(Sheffield+United)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>There are ten spaces for wheelchair users in the Bramall Lane End which
are free of charge (helper pays &pound;5.00). There are three designated
seats for the blind and partially sighted.  
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>Getting a drink around Bramall Lane can be a problem. The once
away-friendly 'Sheaf House' no longer allows away fans and both 'The
Railway' and 'The Golden Lion' are very much Blades pubs and only allow
small numbers of well behaved away fans.
</P><P>Many fans are now forced to drink in the few town centre pubs that allow
customers in football colours (most don't !).
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Railway
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/SheffUtd-Railway-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/SheffUtd-Railway-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Railway
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Railway
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Railway is near to the away turnstiles on Bramall Lane and usually
lets away fans in (if well behaved). It is a small pub which serves a good
selection of real ales. The Railway gets very full on matchdays with both
home and away fans.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">Sheaf House
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/SheffUtd-Sheef-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/SheffUtd-Sheef-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Sheaf House
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Sheaf House
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p></P><P><FONT COLOR=RED>The Sheaf House no longer admits away fans</RED>.
</P><P>Sheaf House is further up Bramall Lane, away from the town centre and
past the ground but still only about 5 minutes from the ground. It has
it's own car park out the front and a beer garden to the side with kiddies
play area. It also has a large screen TV and pool and snooker tables. 
</P></p><H5 class="entry">Golden Lion
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/SheffUtd-Lion-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/SheffUtd-Lion-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Golden Lion
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Golden Lion
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Golden Lion is on Alderson Road which is to the right off Bramall Lane
after passing Sheaf House on the left. There is an enclosed beer garden
to the side of the pub. Whilst normally packed with Blades fans they do
allow small numbers of away fans in for most games.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>There is a Fish &amp; Chip shop on Shoreham Street next to the ground and 
several burger vans are normally around the ground.
</P><P>There is also plenty of choice in the city centre where most of the major
food chains are represented.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>There are a couple of cash machines at the petrol station on the corner of Bramall Lane/John Street near to the away end.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is S2 4SU</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Sheffield is approached via the M1. If approaching from the North exit at
junction 33 and take the A57 towards Sheffield. At the Park Square
roundabout take the exit onto the A61. Bear right onto Shoreham Street
(still the A61). At the end of Shoreham Street turn right onto St Mary's
Road. Bramall lane is left at the roundabout by St Mary's Church.
</P><P>If approaching from the south exit the M1 at junction 29 and follow the
A617 towards Chesterfield for just less than 5 miles and then bear right
onto the A61 towards Sheffield. Follow this for ten miles and then turn
left onto the A621 (Bramall Lane) and the ground should be on your right.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is no parking available at the ground but there is a good supply of
street parking.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>The ground is approximately 15 minutes walk from the station. From the
station turn left down Shoreham Street and then turn right onto St
Marys Road and then left onto Bramall Lane.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Sheffield
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Sheffield has plenty of accommodation to suit all tastes and budgets as
you would expect from a major city.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/sheffiel.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Whilst most Blades fans seem to be pre-occupied with their neighbours
across town, trouble in the city centre after games is not uncommon, but
how much is this is actually connected to football is debatable. 
</P><P>More than one Sheff Utd fan suggested keeping colours hidden whilst
walking to the station after the game.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>Recently the stewards on Sheff United's turnstiles became worthy of a
mention, after almost strip searching one of the guides writers (they even
closely examined keys and pens!). They were then passed a rucksack which
they didn't even bother to open. They also refused admittance to two fans
for using bad language whilst approaching the turnstiles. 
</P><P>This apart, the stewards inside the ground were friendly enough.
</P><P>The police were very noticeable and in far more numbers than you would
expect for a Division 1 game.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Sheffield are known for one of the most unique footballing songs sung
today, their 'Greasy Chip Butty Song' is sung to the the the tune of
Annie's Song.
</P><P><pre>
You fill up my senses, ('light up' in some variants)
Like a gallon of Magnet,
Like a packet of Woodbines,
Like a good pinch of snuff,
Like a night out in Sheffield,
Like a greasy chip butty,
Oh Sheffield United,
Come thrill me again.
</pre>
</P><P></P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Sheffield is a very good night out whether its the pubs, clubs and
restaurants of the city centre or Sheffield's own superclub Gatecrasher.
</P><P>Clubs colours are definite no around town, partly for the
unwanted attention from the locals, but also most pubs operate a
no-colours policy.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>Sheffield has all the attractions of a major city including its own
shopping complex 'Meadowhall". For ideas of how to
pass your time try checking out <a class="entry" href="http://www.sheffieldcity.co.uk">http://www.sheffieldcity.co.uk</a>
</P></p><P>You can also use the following to search for events in the area</p><BR><CENTER>
