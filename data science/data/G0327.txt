<h1 align="center"><u>Globular Clusters</u></h1>
<p>Globular clusters are spherical concentrations of stars typically 100
light years across and containing thousands of stars.  Globular clusters
are very old, at least 10 billion years old and were presumably formed
when the galaxy was still forming.  They are scattered in a spherical
halo surrounding our galaxy and take hundreds of millions of years to
orbit it.</p>
