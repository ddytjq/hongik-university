One modification					is a methyl group. Methylation requires S-adenosyl methionine					as methyl donor. 					<LI>Targets of methylation include bases, such as some A residues					in <A HREF="MG2323.html">rRNA</A>s and the terminal G of <A HREF="MG233.html">cap structures</A>, and the 2'OH of riboses, such as those near the caps of nuclear					RNA polymerase II transcripts and in internal positions in rRNA.									</UL></TD>				<TD COLSPAN="2"><IMG ALIGN="right" SRC="i2351.gif"></TD>			</TR>			<TR>				<TD COLSPAN="2">				<UL>					<LI>Some RNAs, in particular tRNAs, contain a variety of modified					bases. These base modifications are created by a variety of enzymes,					some of which act on <A HREF="MG2322.html">tRNA precursors</A> and others of which act on mature tRNAs. 					<LI>Changing the position of the bond between uridine and ribose results					in an altered uridine, called pseudouridine (<FONT FACE="Symbol">Y</FONT>). <FONT FACE="Symbol">Y</FONT> is found in rRNA and in tRNA				</UL></TD>				<TD><IMG ALIGN="left" SRC="i2352.gif"></TD>			</TR>		</TABLE>		</P>		<UL>			<LI>The following is known about 2'O-methylation of eucaryotic rRNA:			<UL>				<LI>Only specific ribose residues are methylated.				<LI>The methylation of any one specific ribose depends on the presence				of a small nucleolar RNA (snoRNA) which has a short stretch of				sequence complementary to the rRNA sequence immediately surrounding				the ribose whose methylation the snoRNA is required for.				<LI>There are many snoRNAs guiding ribose methylation.  The rRNA-complementary				sequence on these snoRNAs is located between two sequences (box				C and box D) conserved among all these snoRNAs. 				<LI>There are two C box--rRNA complement--D box regions in each snoRNA.				 The two regions are held together by base-paired stem strucutres.				<LI>Box C+ D snoRNAs are associated with three specific proteins.			</UL>		</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A HREF="#Facts">Facts</A> | <A HREF="#Interp">Interpretations</A> | <A HREF="#Info">Further Info.</A> | <A HREF="#bottom">Other Pages</A></P>		</CENTER>		<H4>Interpretations</H4>		<UL TYPE="circle">			<LI>The variety of modifications provides a richness of potential			signals not possible with the basic four nucleotides.			<LI>In eucaryotes, the signal for nucleotide residue modification			is the sequence immediately surrounding the modifiable residue.			 The signal is recognized by an RNA molecule capable of base pairing			with the signal.  			<LI>The enzyme responsible for methylation must be brought in contact			with its RNA substrate by its interaction with conserved structures			(C and D boxes) in the signal recognizing snoRNA.		</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A NAME="Interp"></A><A HREF="#Facts">Facts</A> | <A HREF="#Interp">Interpretations</A> | <A HREF="#Info">Further Info.</A> | <A HREF="#bottom">Other Pages</A> <BR>			</P>		</CENTER>		<H4><A NAME="Info"></A>Further information</H4>		<UL TYPE="square">			<LI>These modifications do not alter the <A HREF="MG241.html">coding</A> capacity of those RNAs that are mRNAs. <A HREF="MG2361.html">Code-altering changes</A> are known. <IMG SRC="nav4.gif" WIDTH="69" HEIGHT="70" ALIGN="right" USEMAP="#nav4B1F889C9">			<MAP NAME>			</MAP>			 			<LI>Conversion of U to <FONT FACE="Symbol">Y</FONT> also is mediated by snoRNAs in eukaryotes.  These are characterized			by a conserved box H and an ACA 3' tail.  As with the snoRNAs			directing ribose methylation, each of these snoRNAs has two sites			for target RNA recognition.			<LI>Modification of tRNAs in eucaryotes and both tRNAs and rRNAs in			procaryotes does not require snoRNAs.  Recognition of the modification			target is directly by the modifying enzyme.			<LI>RNAs also undergo <A HREF="MG232.html">cleavages</A> and the <A HREF="MG234.html">addition</A> of <A HREF="MG233.html">nucleotides</A>. 		</UL>		<CENTER>			<P><A NAME="bottom"></A><A HREF="MG234.html">Last</A> | <A HREF="MG239.html">Vocabulary</A> | <A HREF="MG23.html">Overview</A> | <A HREF="#Facts">Top</A> | <A HREF="MG236.html">Next</A> <BR>			<BR>			</P>		</CENTER>		<P>		<HR>		</P>		<P>This is page 235 of <A HREF="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</A> by <A HREF="http://opbs.okstate.edu/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</A>, &copy; 1997, 1998, 1999<BR>		<HR>		</P>		<ADDRESS>E-mail inquiries to <A HREF="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu">U.
