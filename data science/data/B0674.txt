<P><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="a"></a><font color="#3362a7">WHAT 
  IS AN ISA?</font> <a href="#top">Back to top</a></font> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">From 6th April 1999 TESSAs 
  and PEPs were replaced by a single tax-efficient savings scheme called the Individual 
  Savings Account or ISA. In contrast to TESSAs and PEPs, you can choose where 
  you save your money. There are three options or components available to you 
  which are:-</font> 
<ul>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Cash</font> </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Stocks and Shares</font> 
  </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Life Insurance</font> 
  </li>
</ul>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">It is up to you whether 
  you want to invest in one component or all three. There are three different 
  types of ISA - Mini ISA, Maxi ISA and a special TESSA Only ISA.</font> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In order to invest in an 
  ISA you must be a UK resident and at least 16 years of age.</font>

<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="b"></a><font color="#3362a7">WHY 
  DO WE NEED ISAs?</font> <a href="#top">Back to top</a></font> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">It is the Government who 
  have been responsible for the introduction of ISAs. ISAs have been introduced 
  because of a primary concern that there are around six million people in the 
  UK who do not save money. In addition there is a feeling that in the past the 
  benefits of tax efficient savings have been confined largely to the better-off. 
  As a result of this, proposals for the ISA were announced by the Chancellor 
  in the July budget of 1997. ISAs have been designed with two major objectives 
  in mind:</font> 
<LI><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">to develop and extend the 
  savings habit</font> 
<LI><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">to ensure that tax relief 
  on savings is fairly distributed</font> 
  <P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">As with TESSAs and PEPs, 
    any returns on investments placed in an ISA will be completely free of Income 
    and Capital Gains Tax.</font>

    is due to mature you will be able to transfer the capital balance of your 
    TESSA account into an ISA following maturity without affecting your annual 
    ISA investment limit or you can transfer into a TESSA Only ISA.</font>

  <P><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="e"></a><font color="#3362a7">WHAT 
    ARE THE BENEFITS OF ISAs?</font> <a href="#top">Back to top</a></font> 
<ul>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Money invested in an 
    ISA will enjoy the following tax benefits:-</font> <br>
    <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">- Free from income tax 
    payable on interest earned</font> <br>
    <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">- Not subject to Capital 
    Gains Tax<br>
    </font><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">- 10% tax credit 
    on all dividends received until 5th April 2004</font> </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">ISAs have been given 
    a guaranteed life of ten years.</font> </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">There will be no lock 
    in period. This will allow you access to your money at any time.</font> </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">There will be no minimum 
    subscription. This means that you could open an ISA with as little as &pound;10.</font> 
  </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">ISAs can only be held 
    in individual names. For e.g. husband/wife will each be able to open separate 
    ISA accounts.</font> </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Investors will be able 
    to transfer the capital from a matured TESSA account into an ISA without affecting 
    the ISA subscription limits, thus maximising their tax free savings.</font> 
  </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Investors can choose 
    to invest in either cash deposits, stocks and shares and/or life insurance. 
    Furthermore investors can choose whether to invest their money in each of 
    these components with one ISA Manager or with three separate ISA Managers.</font> 
  </li>
  <li><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Some ISAs will meet CAT 
    standards. This means that investors choosing to invest in one of these ISAs 
    can be sure that charges will be low, they will have easy access to their 
    money and the terms will be easy to understand and fair.</font> </li>
</ul>

<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><B><a name="f"></a></B><font color="#3362a7">WHAT 
  IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN MINI AND MAXI ISAs?</font></font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">With an ISA you can invest 
  in cash, equities or life insurance, or any combination of the three. There 
  are three types of ISA: Mini ISAs, Maxi ISAs and TESSA-Only ISAs</font> </p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The Newcastle Building Society 
  are an approved ISA Manager and we can offer you Mini Cash ISAs and, if you 
  have a maturing TESSA, a TESSA-Only ISA. We can also refer you to Norwich Union 
  who can advise on a range of ISA options including equities ISAs</font> </p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">A Maxi ISA is managed by 
  one ISA Manager only and enables you to invest the maximum amount allowed each 
  tax year. The investment can be wholly in equities (&pound;7,000 in 2001/2002) 
  or you may include a cash and/or life insurance element. </font> </p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Alternatively, you can have 
  one of each of the three Mini ISAs (cash, equities and life insurance), with 
  different ISA Managers for each. You do not have to hold all three elements. 
  You can invest in either Mini ISAs or a Maxi ISA, but you cannot open or invest 
  in both in any one tax year. This means that if you decide to take out, or have 
  already taken out, a Mini Cash ISA this tax year, you cannot take out a Maxi 
  ISA until the next tax year</font><font size="2" color="#000080" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">.</font> 
</p>

<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="g"></a><font color="#3362a7">WHAT 
  IS A TESSA ONLY ISA?</font> <a href="#top">Back to top</a></font> </p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">As an alternative to transferring 
  the capital from a matured TESSA into a Maxi ISA or Mini Cash ISA you could 
  choose instead, to transfer this into a special TESSA Only ISA. You will not 
  be permitted to make further contributions to a TESSA Only ISA although your 
  annual investment limit, through a Maxi ISA or Mini Cash ISA, will remain unaffected.</font> 
</p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">As with TESSAs and PEPs 
  the Government have limited the amount of money which you can invest in an ISA 
  in any one tax year.</font> </p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Each tax year you will be

<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="i"></a>WHAT ARE 
  THE CAT STANDARDS? <a href="#top">Back to top</a></font> </p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In launching the ISA the 
  Government particularly want to help people who are not currently saving to 
  get started and people who are saving to save more. Since many ordinary savers 
  are concerned about getting a poor deal and being caught out by complicated 
  small print, the Government have decided to introduce the idea of CAT standards 
  for ISAs.</font> </p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The CAT standards will concentrate 
  on three features:-</font> </p>
<LI><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">fair Charges</font> 
<LI><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">easy Access</font> 
<LI><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">decent Terms</font> 
  <P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The introduction of CAT 
    standards will ensure that if an ISA meets the CAT standards the charges will 
    be low, you will have easy access to your money and the terms will be easy 
    to understand and fair.</font> 
  <P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">It should be remembered, 
    however, that there is no obligation for every ISA to meet these standards. 
    Indeed an ISA meeting the CAT standards may not necessarily be the best option 
    for you. For example, you might find that a Mini Cash ISA which does not meet 
    the CAT standards because it restricts the number of withdrawals you can make, 
    offers a better rate of return.</font>
