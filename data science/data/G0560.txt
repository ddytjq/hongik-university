            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"> 
              <!-- #BeginEditable "title" --><b>National Astronomy Education Projects:</b> 
              A Catalog<!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>
          </td>

        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" --> 
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="2"><font size="3" color="#6666CC"><b>2. 
              Workshops and Trainings for Teachers (College)</b></font></a></font> 
            </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Association of Physics Teachers: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has an NSF-funded project called TYC21, which brings together 
                regional groups of community college physics and astronomy instructors 
                [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>, or consult the 
                web site: <a href="http://www.aapt.org/programs/tyc21/tyc.html" target="_blank">http://www.aapt.org/programs/tyc21/tyc.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Astronomical Society, Education Office: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                sponsors workshops and sessions for college teachers at its semi-annual 
                meetings. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sponsors workshops and symposia on college astronomy teaching 
                at its annual meetings. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Image 
                Processing for Teaching</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                [see listing in <a href="naep03.html#5">section 5</a>]: </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Also helps facilitate training for college teachers. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Radio Astronomy Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Offers a 3 day workshop for undergraduate college professors focusing 
                on Radio Astronomy. Contact: Sue Ann Heatherly, NRAO, P.O. Box 
                2, Green Bank, WV 24944 (304-456-2209) </font> 
            </dl>
            <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table 
            of contents</a></font> 
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="3"></a>3. 
              Curriculum and Information Materials</b></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Remember 
              this is only a listing of projects, not of all publications. </font> 
            </p>
            <p> 
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project ASTRO's <i>The Universe at Your Fingertips: An Astronomy 
                Activity and Resource Notebook,</i> 800+ pages of activities and 
                resources for teachers at all levels, especially grades 4-12. 
                A sampling is available at the web site <a href="http://www.aspsky.org/astro/universe.html" target="_blank">http://www.aspsky.org/astro/universe.html</a>. 
                Available through the ASP Catalog. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Provides information packets and resource lists for beginners 
                on various astronomical topics, such as black holes, debunking 
                astrology, selecting a first telescope, etc. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Center 
                for Astrophysics (CfA): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project ARIES is a discovery-based, physical science program built 
                around big themes from astronomy for grades 3-6. Three modules 
                (Time; Light and Color; Astronomy I) are available for purchase 
                from Cobblestone Publishing, Inc., 7 School Street, Peterborough, 
                NH 03458 (800-821-0115) . Five more modules will be available 
                beginning in February, 1998. For more information contact: Bruce 
                Ward (617-495-9798); <a href="http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/cfa/sed/ARIES" target="_blank">http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/cfa/sed/ARIES</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Center 
                for Astrophysics (CfA): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project STAR & Project SPICA were NSF-supported programs that 
                developed activity-based curriculum & workbooks for teaching astronomy 
                in secondary schools. Materials can be purchased from Kendall 
                Hunt Publishers, P.O. Box 1840, Dubuque, IA 52004 (1-800-228-0810). 
                (The projects also left a legacy of some 200 trained "astronomy 
                resource agent" teachers around the U.S.) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Challenger 
                Center: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                has developed and is developing a wide range of class activities 
                and mini-cuuricula, such as Cosmic EdVentures, Marsville, and 
                Mars City Alpha. [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Jet 
                Propulsion Lab:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                For those who do not live near a NASA center (see below), it is 
                often possible to get NASA lithographs and booklets by writing 
                to: Teaching Resource Center, CS-530, JPL, 4800 Oak Grove Dr., 
                Pasadena, CA 91109. Write on school stationery and indicate what 
                mission or missions you are interested in. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Lawrence 
                Hall of Science (LHS): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Great Explorations in Math & Science (GEMS), Planetarium Activities 
                for Student Success (PASS) are two series of superb hands-on activity 
                guides for teaching astronomy in grades 3-9. Available from the 
                Eureka store, Lawrence Hall of Science, University of California, 
                Berkeley, CA 94720-5200 (510-642-1016); <a href="http://www.lhs.berkeley.edu/publications.html" target="_blank">http://www.lhs.berkeley.edu/publications.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA: 
                </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A 
                colorful series of booklets, posters, prints & other materials 
                on space astronomy is available through NASA teacher resource 
                centers around the country. Contact a local NASA center or: Education 
                Division, NASA Headquarters, Washington, DC 20546 for current 
                list of centers. The list of available materials changes constantly. 
                (See also NASA CORE in section 4 and Jet Propulsion Lab above.) 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Optical Astronomy Observatories (NOAO):</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                The new Educational Outreach Office has begun to design a variety 
                of materials and activities for teachers and students, such as 
                "Frequently Asked Questions about Being an Astronomer." See their 
                web site at <a href="http://www.noao.edu/outreach" target="_blank">http://www.noao.edu/outreach</a> 
                or write to them at P.O. Box 26732, Tucson, AZ 85726; (520-318-8230) 
                e-mail: <a href="mailto:outreach@noao.edu">outreach@noao.edu</a>. 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Radio Astronomy Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                To get some high school-level radio astronomy activities developed 
                by teachers, write: Sue Ann Heatherly, NRAO, P.O. Box 2, Green 
                Bank, WV 24944 (304-456-2209) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>National 
                Science Teachers Association (NSTA):</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Develops and distributes a range of books and activity collections 
                for teaching astronomy in grades K-12. Ask for their catalog. 
                [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>New 
                Mexico State University: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Dr. Bernard McNamara is devising a series of astronomy exercises 
                and activities that develop model building and critical thinking. 
                Contact him at: Dept. of Astronomy, Box 30001, Dept. 4500, New 
                Mexico State U., Las Cruces, NM 88003 </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Pacific 
                Science Center: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                AstroAdventures Curriculum, a series of astronomy activities for 
                grades 3-12, assembled by respected astronomy educator Dennis 
                Schatz, and supported by a NASA Space Grant. Contact: The Explore 
                More Store, PSC, 200 Second Ave. North, Seattle, WA 98109 (206-443-2870) 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>SETI 
                Institute:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Life in the Universe Curriculum Project is developing supplementary 
                science curricula and accompanying materials focusing on SETI 
                themes for elementary and middle school students. Contact : SETI, 
                2035 Landings Dr., Mountain View, CA 94043 (650-961-6633); <a href="http://www.seti.org" target="_blank">http://www.seti.org</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Space 
                Science Institute: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Provides curriculum materials related to NASA missions or traveling 
                science exhibitions. Materials are available to download on SSI's 
                home page [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>]. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Stanford 
                Solar Center:</b></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                </font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                A series of web-based curriculum activities (including images 
                of the Sun from many cultures) at <a href="http://solar-center.stanford.edu" target="_blank">http://solar-center.stanford.edu</a>. 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>University 
                of Texas McDonald Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has posters, activities, CDs of the Star Date radio program, and 
                planetary fact sheets. Contact at RLM 15.308, U. of Texas, Austin, 
                TX 78712 (512-471-5285); <a href="http://stardate.utexas.edu" target="_blank">http://stardate.utexas.edu</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Young 
                Astronauts Program: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Produces simple activities and materials on space science for 
                youngsters; has many local chapters. Contact at 1308 19th St., 
                NW, Washington, DC 20036 (202-682-1984); <a href="http://www.yac.org" target="_blank">http://www.yac.org</a> 
                </font> 
            </dl>
