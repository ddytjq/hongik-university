<td valign="top">The Office of Biological and Environmental Research manages the Department's Biological and Environmental Research (BER) program.  BER's 
extraordinary legacy spans <A HREF="./ober-lay.pdf">50 years</A> and includes these three areas:

<ul>
<A HREF="./lsd_top.html">Life Sciences</a>
<BR><A HREF="./msd_top.html">Medical Sciences</a>

<BR><a href="./ESD_top.html">

Environmental Sciences</a>

<br>  
</ul>

The mission of the BER program is to develop the knowledge needed to identify, understand, and anticipate the long-term health and environmental consequences of energy production, development, and use.  This mission is carried out through the program's support of <A HREF="./peerreview.html">peer-reviewed research</A> at <a href="http://www.energy.gov/aboutus/history/map_labs.html"> DOE
National Laboratories</a>, universities, and private institutions.  The  research is also designed to provide science in support of the Energy Policy Act of 1992.<p> 
 
The BER vision is to bring revolutionary solutions to energy-related biomedical and environmental challenges.<p>

<A NAME="congress"></A>The legislative bases of BER are the Atomic Energy Act of 1954 (<A href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/42/ch23.html">42 U.S.C. 2011 et seq.</A>), the Energy Reorganization Act of 1974 (<A href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/42/ch73.html"> 42 U.S.C. 5801 et seq.</A>), the Department of Energy Organization Act of 1977 (DOE Act; <A href="http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/42/ch84.html"> 42 U.S.C. 7101 et seq.</A>), and the Energy Policy Act of 1992 (<A href="http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c102:H.R.776.ENR:">Public Law 102-486</A>, October 24, 1992).  Annual appropriation laws provide funding and guidance for the program's activities. In the <A             href="http://www.senate.gov/~budget/republican/reference/cliff_notes/clifftoc.htm">Congressional budget process</A>, the BER program is governed by the four <A href="http://www.senate.gov/committees/comm_about.html">Congressional Committees</A> shown below.  Each Committee contains a subcommittee who specifically considers the <A href="http://www.cfo.doe.gov/budget/01budget/science/sci2.pdf">BER portion</A> of the <A href="http://w3.access.gpo.gov/usbudget/fy2001/guidetoc.html">President's Budget Request</A>.</td></tr>
