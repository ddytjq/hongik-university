				<p class="indent20"><font class="normaltour"><script language="javascript">writeToursMenu (161)</script><b>India won the toss and elected to field. England scored 271-5 from their 50 overs. India finished on 269-8. The tourists win by two runs but are 3-2 down in the series</b><br>Teams:<br>India: Ganguly (capt), Tendulkar, Mongia, Kaif, Sehwag, Badani, Ratra, Kumble, Agarkar, S Singh, Srinath<br>England: Hussain (capt), Trescothick, Knight, Flintoff, Thorpe, Vaughan, Collingwood, Foster, Giles, Caddick, Gough<br><br><b>England innings</b><br>On a pitch fine-tuned for batting, Sourav Ganguly won the toss and opted to field. A capacity crowd at the Feroz Shah Kotla Stadium saw Nick Knight and Marcus Trescothick take to the field to open the batting for the tourists. <br><br>England&#8217;s line-up was conspicuously different from the one that capitulated at Kanpur. Andy Caddick returned in place of Matthew Hoggard, and injuries to Jeremy Snape and Ben Hollioake saw James Foster and Ashley Giles reclaim their places. <br><br>Sarandeep Singh was drafted into the Indian side at the expense of Harbhajan Singh. Hussain&#8217;s men got off to a brisky start, Trescothick and Knight making the most of a quick outfield. <br><br>Trescothick was the pick of the two batsman with five boundaries in his 35 before mistiming a Ajit Agarkar delivery to new-boy Sarandeep.<br><br>Nick Knight was gaining in confidence, fashioning his second successive half century as England looked to capitalize on a slow, flat pitch. The left-hander forged a productive partnership with Hussain, sharing a second wicket stand of 117, before the skipper was caught behind off Sachin Tendulkar for 49. Knight continued to play the Indian seamers with ease, racing to his first ODI century for four years. He achieved his ton in 126 balls, hitting seven fours in the process.<br><br>England&#8217;s progress was galvanized by the rich form of Andrew Flintoff, who lived up to his big-hitting reputation as the tourist&#8217;s run-rate soared to 10 an over during an explosive spell.<br><br>His partnership with Knight was worth 80 runs before the century hero was run out by Agarkar on 105. Flintoff was joined at the crease by Paul Collingwood, who was looking to build on some impressive performances of late for England.<br><br>Flintoff&#8217;s attacking instinct eventually got the better of him, lofting the ball off Javagal Srinath for Mohammad Kaif to take an easy catch. Flintoff&#8217;s contribution was crucial, adding 52 runs to the England total off just 39 balls. Thorpe&#8217;s stay at the crease was short-lived. The left-hander faced only six balls before he was bowled clean by a wonderful in-swinger from Agarkar. <br><br>Vaughan came in late to steer England to a competitive total of 271-5, setting the hosts a victory target of 272.<br><br><b>India innings</b><br>India got off to a blistering start, with man-of-the-moment Virender Sehwag and Sachin Tendulkar hitting a succession of boundaries as the hosts took control.<br><br>Then against the run of play, England clinched the wicket they craved the most. Tendulkar edged a Caddick delivery outside off-stump having made 18 off 16 balls, leaving India on 53-1. Despite the loss of the Little Master, Sehwag continued to pick off the England bowling attack and hinted at a fruitful partnership with Ganguly who was gradually gaining in confidence. <br><br>Gough then struck, removing Sehwag with a delivery that was nicked to Knight in the slips. It was a gutsy performance from the opener, who finished on 42 runs from 36 balls, including seven fours.<br><br>With Mongia being caught behind the stumps after flapping at a Flintoff delivery for 20, it was up to the captain to steer his side to within sight of England&#8217;s large total. This he did emphatically, leading his side to an imposing 211-4 before his wicket fell to Giles on 74.<br><br>The left-arm spinner, coming into the side in place of the injured Jeremy Snape, struck at a crucial time with India looking on course to wrap up the series. <br><br>Ganguly&#8217;s departure was the turning point of the match and the beginning of a match-winning performance from the England spin bowler. Soon after Ganguly&#8217;s wicket, Mohammad Kaif departed after they had shared a fourth-wicket stand of 111.<br><br>Kaif had notched up a commendable 46 from 64 balls before he spooned an easy catch to Graham Thorpe. <br><br>Giles was on the comeback trail and the wickets of Hemang Badani and Ajay Ratra soon followed to set up an exhilarating finish. <br><br>Badani played the ball into the hands of the bowler, whilst Ratra was stumped by Foster behind the wicket. Foster&#8217;s elation was for all to see - the youngster had earlier been guilty of missing two gilt-edged stumping opportunities. <br><br>Giles then despatched Anil Kumble who lasted all of six balls, before he left the field on two. <br><br>Only through the spirited resistance of Ajit Agarkar did India ever look like surpassing the total set by the tourists. <br><br>India needed 10 from the last over but Gough soon dashed any hopes of the victory to clinch the series, producing an exquisite six balls to finally kill off the game. Agarkar left the field with noteworthy figures of 36 off 24 balls, but it was not enough for India to salvage the match.<br><br><b>England won by two runs to pull a match back in the ODI series. India now lead 3-2 going into the final game on Sunday.</b></font></p>
