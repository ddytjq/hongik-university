    background="../../../../../images/Backgrounds/paper_back.gif"><h2>Talking X's and O's -
      <font color="#800080">Coaching Box</font></h2>
      <h2 align="center"><font color="#FF0000">Speed Wins Feature Clinic</font></h2>
    <div align="center"><center><table border="0" width="95%">
      <tr>
        <td>
          <h3 align="center" style="text-align:center"><font size="1">Basketball
          Speed Skills</font></h3>
          <h3 align="center" style="text-align:center"><font size="1">By</font></h3>
          <h3 align="center" style="text-align:center"><font size="1">Peter
          McLean, MS</font></h3>
          <h3 align="center" style="text-align:center"><font size="1">Director
          – Speed Wins Ltd.</font><font size="1"><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></h3>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">&nbsp;<o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">Ask any coach or player in the
          NBA today and they will tell you that <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">SPEED
          WINS.</i><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>From Alan
          Iverson to Latrell Spreewell, the players that are making the biggest
          impact on the modern game combine explosive speed, agility and
          quickness to complete the scope of their game.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>For you to be a successful high school coach you need to
          incorporate a speed aspect to your game plan.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Because the high school coach can not recruit speed, this skill
          must be developed using cutting edge training programs.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>I will outline some of the important drills and concepts that
          will help you incorporate a speed, agility and quickness program into
          your practice schedule. This clinic will address two of the most
          important topics <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">Speed
          Wins</i></b> stresses in our speed development program:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">running mechanics and
          developing an explosive first step.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </b></font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">Top speed is not the skill we
          want to teach in a basketball program.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>It is rare for a basketball player to reach top speed in the
          coarse of a game.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>But a
          player must be able to Start, Stop and Change Direction with
          precision, explosion and authority.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>There are two components that must be trained for the athlete
          to incorporate these skills into his/her game.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>We have to stimulate both the musculo-skeletal system and the
          neurological pathways that control the motor skills we are training.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>If a player has either muscular weakness or a neurological
          deficiency, full speed potential will never be realized.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>When <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">Speed
          Wins</i></b> trains athletes we concentrate on developing the proper
          motor skills first, then we work on the muscular component.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>This allows for the body to adapt to the new skills in a
          non-fatigued state.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Fatigue
          is the enemy of efficient motor skills.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Once the athlete has learned the skills to perfection, we can
          incorporate a conditioning and power component to the training. <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal">Remember: perfect training leads to perfect performance</b>.<font color="#FF0000"><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText" align="center" style="text-align:center;mso-outline-level:
1"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt"><font size="1"><font color="#FF0000">Speed
          Mechanics</font><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></span></b></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">The first skill <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">Speed
          Wins</i> </b>trains is proper running form.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Most young athletes do not run correctly.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>They display bad upper body mechanics, poor posture and
          inefficient ground force development.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>These simple drills will help correct these problems.<o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText" align="center" style="text-align:center;mso-outline-level:
1"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><u><font size="1" color="#FF0000">Form
          Running<o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></u></b></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText" style="mso-outline-level:1"><font size="1">a)<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Arm Acton Drill</b><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
          </span>This drill may be the most important drill in this sequence as
          arm action is usually non-existent in the young athlete<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal">.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Speed Wins</i></b> spends a considerable amount of time on
          upper body form, but this drill will help demonstrate proper arm
          action.</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText" style="mso-outline-level:1"><font size="1">Player
          will kneel on floor.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Set
          arms at 90’ at the elbow.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Hands
          should be lightly fisted, and relaxed.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Rear arm starts at the back pocket, front hand at the cheek or
          chin.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>On coaches cue back
          hand drives forward, front hand drives back.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>This is one cycle.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Repeat until perfect.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The
          cue we use is <i style="mso-bidi-font-style:normal"><u>pocket to cheek</u>.</i>
          We stress this on every drill!</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">b) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">High
          Knees</b> - Knees to 90’, foot dorsiflexed (flexed up), good arm
          action from shoulder, land on toes!</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">c) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Butt
          kickers</b> - Forward body lean, gluteus tight, heels to hips, land on
          toes</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">d) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Side
          Sliders</b> - Low hips (defensive position), feet parallel, and stay
          on toes</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">e) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Backward
          Shuffle</b> - Low hips, forward body lean, drive with proper arm
          action<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span></font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">f) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Carioca</b>
          - Keep hips square to front, try to achieve good counter action
          between upper and lower body, smooth precise feet, stay on toes</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">g) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Fast
          Feet Drill</b> – cue “quite feet”, we stress soft foot plant,
          coordinated upper and lower body, feet no more than 6” of the ground</font><font size="1"><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText" align="center" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><u><font size="1" color="#FF0000">Force
          Development<o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></u></b></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">To ensure a more explosive first
          step it is imperative for each stride cycle to be as efficient as
          possible.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Every time the foot makes contact with the playing surface,
          maximal power must be attained.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>You
          may have the strongest athlete in the league, but if he/she can not
          transfer this strength to the foot strike point, all of this strength
          is wasted.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>I will outline
          some of the drills we use to improve ground force mechanics and the
          explosive first step.<o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">a) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Wheeling
          Drill</b><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>This
          drill will be done while leaning against a wall,<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>arms fully extended<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>torso
          at about a 60’ angle.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Athlete
          brings one knee up to 90’, on cue drive the foot down to the ground
          and scrape the sole of the shoe against the ground and bring knee
          around to the start position.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>This is one cycle.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>We
          describe the action as “scraping dirt off of the sole of the
          shoe”,<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>the action
          of the leg will be rotational.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The
          drill should be done very deliberately and with explosive action.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
          </span>10-20 cycles on each side</font></p>
          <p class="MsoNormal"><font size="1">b) Power<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">
          <span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Stride</span></b><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt"><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
          </span>The athlete lines up with feet parallel, hip width apart and
          arms at side. On cue the athlete takes one explosive step out in
          perfect running position.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Head
          up, back leg extended, chest over front knee.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>It is important to be sure that the rear ankle, knee, hip and
          shoulders line up at between 45’-50’.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Repeat this drill until it is perfect.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Be sure to note the arm position.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Check to ensure countered arms and legs,<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>90’ at elbow joint, and pocket to shoulder hands (see above).<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </span><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </span></font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">c)<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Two Step Power Stride</b><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
          </span>same as above but continue with the other leg.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Be sure to check that athlete does not over stride . The chest
          should be over the foot.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>If the foot is set beyond the chest, the stride is too long
          and will reinforce bad mechanics.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Be sure to cue the arm action.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
          </span>10 repeats on each side</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">c<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">)<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Bounding</b><span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>From neutral position, drive the lead foot and leap on
          contact<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>alternate feet
          and bound 20 yds.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>This
          drill will trigger the neural systems to react quickly to stimulus
          from the ground.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>For the
          athlete to properly execute this drill, he/she must generate high
          forces and propel the foot to the ground forcefully.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>Coach should correct any athlete that double hops or skips.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>This drill must stress a single quick touch on each foot
          contact.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>When bounding is
          successfully attempted, the athlete will look as if he/she is power
          running.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>3-4 sequences of 20 yards each</font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">d) <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">Power
          Stride – Bound</b><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp; </span>Athlete
          will perform power stride and continue into a bound.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>This is a very difficult drill for the novice so be prepared to
          spend some time working on the mechanics of this drill.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>The <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal">power stride
          –bound</b> incorporates all of the concepts we have spoken about in
          the clinic.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>We stress
          proper mechanics, good arm action, driving from the toes, explosive,
          precise leg drive and quick turnover.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>This will be the final drill in this drill sequence.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span><o:p>
          </o:p>
          </font></p>
          <p class="MsoBodyText"><font size="1">You should try to incorporate
          these drills into the beginning of the practice session so that all of
          the neural components are stimulated and ready for the remainder of
          the training session.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>These
          skills work best when the athlete is fresh and mentally alert.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>As the season develops these drills can become part of the
          conditioning or the functional warm up of any practice.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
          </span>I also recommend that some of these drills be used as part of
          your pre-game warm up.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The
          same principles apply to the game environment as the practice session,
          we want the muscles to be stimulated in the precise firing order to
          maximize performance.</font></p>
