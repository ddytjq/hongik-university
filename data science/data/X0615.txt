				<font class="newsdate"> Sunday, December 2, 2001 18:45 (GMT)</font><br>
				<img src="/1.gif" width="1" height="18"><br>
				<font class="news">Former England captain Alex Stewart gives us the lowdown on what it's like to be a touring player in India.<br><br><b>Q: Before we look at the prospects for the England side can you just tell me why did you choose not to tour?</b><br>A: A couple of reasons really. I&#8217;ve toured with England for the last 12 years, I think I&#8217;ve played the last 60-odd Test matches on the trot. I want to spend some time at home with my family, I&#8217;ve got two young children and I wanted to spend some time with them in the build-up to Christmas.<br><br><br><b>Q: And there was an injury as well, but you didn&#8217;t initially bring that up as the reason?</b><br>A: Well the selectors were fully aware of my elbow injuries. Towards the end of the season I had both of them operated on, both quite major operations and now I&#8217;m having physio and rehab work done and hopefully in early January I can pick a cricket bat up again and continue my career.<br><br><br><b>Q: Having been to India before with England, can you give us an oversight of what the tour will be about and what the team are likely to face?</b><br>A: First of all it&#8217;s going to be tough. India don&#8217;t lose too many Test matches over there, I think they&#8217;ve lost one series in the past 15-20 years.<br><br>So It's going to be tough, it'll be more like the cricket we played last winter [against Pakistan and Sri Lanka], a lot of turning pitches, plenty of spin bowling, people around the bat and not too seamer-friendly for the quicker bowlers. <br><br>So it&#8217;s going to be tough, but before we left to go to Pakistan and Sri Lanka last year most people thought we'd go out there and lose, but we came back with two good wins and ideally the same will happen this winter.<br><br><br><b>Q: That experience has got to be good for the confidence of the players that went out there?</b><br>A: Yeah exactly and to the ones who didn&#8217;t because you can see what you can do when you play well. If you go out there and think you will lose you will lose. If you go out there and think you can win you give yourself half a chance.<br><br><br><b>Q: What did England go out with to Pakistan and Sri Lanka with that made them winners?</b><br>A: We went out there with a positive frame of mind. It&#8217;s important to stay in the game, stay in each session and don&#8217;t let the game drift away from you. And we did that very well last winter and that&#8217;s the type of thing they&#8217;ve got to repeat this winter.<br><br><br><b>Q: Things have been made more difficult by the lack of their first-choice opening bowling partnership in Darren Gough and Andrew Caddick. Will their replacements be able to cope?</b><br>A: They&#8217;ve got to, they&#8217;ve been given an opportunity and they&#8217;ve got to take it with both hands. It&#8217;ll not be easy, like I say its not easy bowling seam out there. You&#8217;ve got to swing the ball and try and add variety to your bowling and learn on your feet. So they're going to miss Caddick and Gough, but they've got to stand up and be counted and do their job.<br><br><br><b>Q: What does it feel like going to India as a tourist, do you try and experience the culture or do you stay away from the limelight?</b><br>A: I think this one will be slightly different bearing in mind recent events. There&#8217;s going to be a lot of security. Wherever you go they love cricket, it&#8217;s like a second religion in India so you get recognized. But subject to security you&#8217;ve got to go out there and enjoy the culture, you cant just sit in your hotel bedroom otherwise you&#8217;ll not enjoy your life and you won't enjoy your cricket.<br><br><br><b>Q: With the absence of experienced players such as yourself, Caddick and Gough, who will the less experienced players be looking to for guidance? </b><br>A: They'll be looking at Graham Thorpe, Marcus Trescothick, along with Nasser Hussain to lead from the front with their batting, their experience and what they achieved last winter.<br><br>Mark Butcher's just come off a wonderful summer so he'll be looking to do well. It'll be a new experience for him playing in that part of the world. I think he'll open the batting so he should be in before the spinners come on then he can accelerate his scoring and look to take the attack to them.<br><br>Duncan Fletcher [the England coach] has done an excellent job and I wouldn't hear a bad word said about him, but once they're out there its down to the players to perform.<br><br><br><b>Q: And who are the men England will have to watch out for?</b><br>A: The Indian cricket team basically. They're a wonderful side in their own country, full of flair, full of natural talent and like I said they don't lose many games. They've got the best batsmen in the world in Sachin Tendulkar, you add Sourav Ganguly, [Rahul] Dravid and [VVS] Laxman and that's a pretty formidable batting lineup. Srinath is a top class bowler and you've got Anil Kumble and Harbhajan Singh as well.</font><br>
