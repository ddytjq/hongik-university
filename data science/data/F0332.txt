	<font size=4><div class=cutline>Product Review:</div></font><BR>
	<font size='-1'>If you have been developing software for as long as I have, you have seen your share of report writers. In my younger days I even tried to create my own. And believe me, after you have tried to write your own, you begin to appreciate what makes for a good report writer. You notice the little details that make a product special. ActiveReports 2 is one of those products. Let me share with you some of the major features, and tell you why I have fallen in love with this product.  <h4>ActiveX Designer</h4>  ActiveReports is based on the ActiveX Designer specification! That means it becomes part of your Visual Basic design environment. You can design, develop, and test your reports in VB! You can add controls and OLE objects such as graphs straight into the report - the same controls you have used all along and know how to use. It even has the ability to add watermarks to your pages. So you can have that shaded "DEMO" on your demo versions of reports. You could even place your company logo on your reports so you can impress your boss!  <h4>Preserving Your Legacy</h4>  ActiveReports has the ability to import your existing reports from several popular report writers. This allows you to take those existing reports, regardless of their source, and be up and running from the start. The thought of switching report writers becomes close to painless!  <h4>One Syntax, No Waiting</h4>  I love the fact that I can write all my formulas in Visual Basic. ActiveReports requires no new scripting language or funny formula parameters to learn, because I'm working with VB code.  But it doesn't stop there - the objects are exposed through COM for complete control, and things like printing options and sections can all be manipulated through VB code.  <h4>The Ins and Outs</h4>  ActiveReports supports ADO, DAO, RDO, and XML for getting data into your report. It allows you to print your reports, of course, but it also has a client viewer so that you can allow reports to be viewed over the Internet. You can also use the export filters to export to RTF, PDF, Excel, HTML, TIFF, and Delimited Text Files.
