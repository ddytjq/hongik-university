      <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="security"></a>Security 
        Tips </font></b></font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Belongings 
        insurance can reimburse you for unavoidable losses you may experience, 
        but it's worth remembering that all policies expect you to take reasonable 
        steps to safeguard your property. If you don't, any claim you may make 
        may be rejected. Here are <b>watchout's</b> list of basic and inexpensive 
        Dos and Don'ts, courtesy of Philip Field, Crime Prevention Design Adviser 
        of The Metropolitan Police Service -</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font color="#FF6600">Do:</font> 
        </b>lock all doors and windows when you go out and at night. One-third 
        of burglaries are opportunist crimes taking place through open windows 
        without forcing entry. Door locks should conform to British Standard 3621/1980 
        and include a mortice deadlock as well as a latch.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font color="#FF6600">Don't:</font></b> 
        forget the back door. The majority of break-ins take place in daylight, 
        and the intruders often get in downstairs at the rear of the premises.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font color="#FF6600">Do:</font> 
        </b>close the curtains and leave a light on when you are out for the evening. 
        Make it look as though your house is occupied.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font color="#FF6600">Don't:</font></b> 
        leave door keys in 'hiding places' like doormats/flower pots, or where 
        they can be reached through the letterbox.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font color="#FF6600">Do:</font> 
        </b>Mark your valuables with your postcode using a kit from the DIY store 
        or borrowed, free of charge, from your local Crime Prevention Officer, 
        and display a sticker showing that you have done so to hopefully deter 
        burglars. Photograph items of value, and record serial numbers of electrical 
        goods. This gives the Police more chance of tracing your property, and 
        can help you make an insurance claim. </font></p>
