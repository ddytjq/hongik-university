    <td width="100%" align="center" colspan="2"><b>Portman Reports Record Year As New Residential Lending Hits &pound;2 Billion And Assets Soar By 32%</b></td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td width="100%" colspan="2"><br>
<p align="center">Competitive products and acquisition of Sun Bank plc boost assets to &pound;9 
billion </p>
<p>Portman, the UK&#146;s fourth largest building society, reports exceptional 
results for 2001, demonstrating its continued success as an independent mutual 
organisation.</p>
<p>&#183; &pound;30 million returned to members, showing the benefits of remaining a mutual</p>
<p>&#183; Total assets up by 32% to &pound;9 billion</p>
<p>&#183; New residential lending of &pound;2 billion &#150; a record performance and one-third 
ahead of natural market share</p>
<p>&#183; Net residential lending of &pound;900 million &#150; 50% above natural market share</p>
<p>&#183; Net retail receipts of &pound;676 million &#150; 50% above natural market share </p>
<p>&#183; Net interest margin reduced from 1.28% to 1.22%, reflecting the Society&#146;s 
competitive product pricing </p>
<p>&#183; &pound;135 million new capital raised</p>
<p>&#183; Costs efficiently contained &#150; management expenses ratio down from 0.85% to 
0.82%</p>
<p>&#183; A 9% increase in pre-tax profits to &pound;59 million.</p>
<p>Commenting on the Society&#146;s performance, Robert Sharpe, Chief Executive says, 
&#148;These results provide indisputable evidence of our continued success in the 
retail savings and residential mortgage markets. Despite the challenges 
presented by this year&#146;s economic environment, we remained competitive for both 
savers and borrowers.&#148; </p>
<p>&#147;The acquisition of Sun Bank plc during 2001 is a significant achievement and 
will enable us to enter new segments of the mortgage market using their 
expertise and competitive products. Portman members will benefit from the 
additional profits the bank will generate.&#148;</p>
<p>Some examples of how Portman has moved forward in 2001:</p>
<p>&#183; Improvements to the way in which customers can communicate with us: </p>
<p>&#183; Continued commitment to our branch network</p>
<p>&#183; Introduction of a mobile branch service in Devon </p>
<p>&#183; Expansion of our head office Contact Centre team, dealing with telephone, 
e-mail and postal enquiries</p>
<p>&#183; Redevelopment of our internet site, to improve on-line facilities and 
communication</p>
<p>&#183; Invitation to all of our members to give us their views through the 2001 
AGM mailing - resulting in more than 66,000 responses </p>
<p>&#183; Introduction of regular meetings with members to encourage face to face 
dialogue with our senior management.</p>
<p>&#183; Benefits for new and established customers included:</p>
<p>&#183; The introduction of a Rollover Bond, for existing members only, with 40,000 
people taking advantage of this product and depositing a total of &pound;230 million 
in only 6 weeks</p>
<p>&#183; The launch of a new telephone sharedealing service </p>
<p>&#183; The achievement of Investors in People accreditation, which is further 
evidence of the Society&#146;s commitment to training, developing and retaining its 
people to ensure a high standard of customer service. </p>
<p>Robert Sharpe continued: &#147;We believe strongly in transparency and member 
participation. The views of our members continue to help us decide on new 
business initiatives. We remain committed to retaining our mutual status, as we 
believe this to be in the best interests of members, both savers and borrowers.&#148;
</p>
<p>&#147;At the end of December, Tim Tanner, who had advertised and co-ordinated 
support from 400 members through a website, submitted a valid Director 
Nomination Form and will stand as a candidate for director of the Society. It 
has long been the Society's policy that candidates for election should have 
relevant experience and skills. Our members will have the opportunity to decide 
whether Mr Tanner is appropriately qualified in the regard&#148;, he concluded.</p>
<p>Notes to Editors:</p>
<p>During 2001, the Society was named Dorset&#146;s Business of the Year and was 
awarded the Mortgage Finance Gazette Arts Award for its support of the 
Bournemouth Symphony Orchestra. Portman also achieved mortgage-related awards 
from independent magazines. </p>
<p>Based in Bournemouth, Portman serves over one million members through a 
network of over 100 branches, based predominantly in the south of England. 
Portman&#146;s website can be located at www.portman.co.uk and provides a wide range 
of information about the Society&#146;s products and services.</p>
