					<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Other Sports at Wembley</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b><br>
					Athletics</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					Probably the first great national celebration in Britain in the post-war years was the Olympic games at Wembley Stadium. London had been allocated the 1944 games that were obviously impossible to organise during wartime. Wembley took up the baton in 1948, effectively underwriting the games.<br>
					<br>
					Turf covering the athletics track of the Empire Games of 1924 and 1925 was removed and given a top dressing of cinders from the fireplaces of Leicester. (History does not record why Leicester's fireplaces were chosen above all others).<br>
					<br>
					Star of the games were Dutch housewife Fanny Blankers-Koen who won four athletics gold medals, and the great Czech runner Emil Zatopek who left a distinguished 10,000 metre field for dead to set a new Olympic record in the event.<br>
					<br>
					The winners plaque still sits between the twin towers, in front of the Olympic Flame - and can be seen on the Wembley Tour.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Greyhound Racing</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					While football made Wembley famous, it was dogs that paid the rent during the early years. In 1927 the owners of Wembley invested heavily to establish the stadium as 'the Ascot of greyhound racing'. On the first night, 50,000 people turned up to watch the first race. Sadly the evening bordered on the farcical with a dog catching the hare in the third race and the hare, when freed, going over a back marker.<br>
					<br>
					Nevertheless, dog racing was a key part of Wembley life and it continued to attract crowds in the tens of thousands until the 1960s. 'Racing ended at Wembley in December 1998'.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Speedway</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					Two years after greyhound racing began at Wembley, the stadium owners selected speedway as a potential money-spinner. After a difficult start, when rival promoters prevented the stadium from staging international events, the stadium prospered as the home track of national league team, the Wembley Lions.<br>
					<br>
					By the time the Lions won their first title, speedway was a profitable exercise. In 1937 the stadium attracted a crowd of 85,000 for the world championship and numbers initially grew after the war. In 1948 the Wembley Lions had a fan club with 60,000 members and, for one league meeting against fellow Londoners West Ham, an 85,000 crowd was augmented by 20,000 fans who were locked out and had to listen to a broadcast relay of the action taking place inside the stadium.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Boxing</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					Boxing is more usually associated the adjoining Wembley Arena (or Empire Pool as it was originally known) with only two major fights being held in the bowl of the stadium for the World Heavyweight Championship.<br>
					<br>
					The first of these was Casius Clay (as Mohammed Ali was then known) versus Henry Cooper, a long time local resident. Cooper had Ali on the floor in the fourth round, but the bell sounded as the count was on five. Ali then went on to a tremendous comeback, beating Cooper in the next round when the fight was stopped.<br>
					<br>
					In 1995 Briton Frank Bruno was more successful in a Wembley fight when he beat Oliver McCall to take the WBC Heavyweight Crown.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>American Football</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					Wembley has also played host to sports more usually found on the other side of the Atlantic.<br>
					<br>
					At the height of interest in American Football, Wembley hosted games by top American teams with 82,000 turning out to see the Chicago Bears beat the Dallas Cowboys 17-6 in August 1986.<br>
					<br>
					Wembley was home team to the London Monarchs from March 1991 to April 1992. The teams best attendance was the 61,000 crowd who watched the team take lose a tight match 17-20 to the Barcelona Dragons in July 1991. However crowds declined and the last crowd to watch the Monarchs was 19,000 - still respectable but not enough to justify using a stadium the size of Wembley.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Baseball</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					Oddly even baseball has been played on the Wembley turf. On the 16 June 1934 a few strings pulled by the American Ambassador, were enough to let two teams from USS New Orleans play on the turf. American servicemen again played baseball at the Stadium in 1943 as the US Air Force took on US Ground Forces.<br>
					<br>
					</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Cricket</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533"><br>
					<br>
					A charity Crickathon was held in the Stadium on 28 September 1991. It was rained off.<br>
