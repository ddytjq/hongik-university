<FONT FACE="Arial Rounded MT Bold" SIZE=2 COLOR="#808000"><P>Team members, alternates and grooms will attend an eight-day training session starting July 25<SUP>th</SUP>, at Poplar Place Farm.   We leave for NAYRC on August 4<SUP>th</SUP>, returning August 11<SUP>th</SUP>.   Our two 2-star riders will compete on a combined team with competitors from other Areas.  Combined teams are announced at NAYRC.  </P>
<P>Our competitors will be accomplished by our Young Riders coach, Kyle Carter, team vet, Dr. Chris Newton, Rood and Riddle Equine Hospital, Lexington, KY, and Fran Summerlin, Area III Young Riders Coordinator.  Horses will be shipped from Georgia to Illinois by H&amp;H Hudson, Ocala, FL.   </P>
<P>Riders are busy raising funds to help offset the cost of this trip.  If you can help in any way, please contact Fran Summerlin.</P>
</FONT><I><FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE=4 COLOR="#808000"><P>Novice/Training Camp</P>
</I></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial Rounded MT Bold" SIZE=2 COLOR="#808000"><P>To help prepare our younger riders for future 3-day competition and as a training session for the Team Challenge, we are offering a 2-day training camp, Aug 3-4, for a limited number of Novice and Training Young Riders.  Riders will participate in mounted and dismounted instruction each day. Young Riders interested in being on the Area III Young Riders Novice and Training Teams for the October 19-20 Team Challenge in Lexington, KY are encouraged to attend this session and will be given priority consideration. Remaining spaces will be filled in the order registration forms are received. Details were emailed to our membership.  If you did not receive a copy, contact Kathy Oplinger, at 706-635-7918.</P>
</FONT><I><FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE=4 COLOR="#808000"><P>Spring Clinic</P>
</I></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial Rounded MT Bold" SIZE=2 COLOR="#808000"><P>The June clinic at Poplar Place Farm was enjoyed by some 45 Young Riders.  We had great instruction, a super facility and favorable weather.  Our Young Riders received lessons from Kyle Carter, Werner Geven, Hilda Hick Donahue, Mike Winter and Tiffany Loudon.   Hilda Donahue led a lunch time discussion on preparing for 3-day competitions and, along with Charlotte Cloudsdale, demonstrated the do's and don'ts of the jog out on Saturday evening.  Following the demonstration, each of the NAYRC candidates jogged their horses for our coach, vet and selectors.</P>
</FONT><I><FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE=4 COLOR="#808000"><P>Low Score Awards</P>
</I></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial Rounded MT Bold" SIZE=2 COLOR="#808000"><P>We've studied the upcoming schedule and selected the following fall events for awarding Low Score Prizes to our members:  Farewell Farm, Flat Creek, Middle Tennessee, J3 and Canterbury.  </P>
<P>At these selected events, we award a prize to the lowest scoring, dues paying Young Rider in each division.  The Young Rider does not need to win the division or even place in the ribbons.  If they are a member of the Area III Young Riders and have the lowest score of the Young Riders in that division, we recognize their effort regardless of placing.  This program would not be possible without the support of our sponsors and the Area III Organizers!  Our appreciated to both groups!</P>

</FONT><I><FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE=4 COLOR="#808000"><P>The Future Looks Bright</P>

Thank you, parents and riders!  A complete list of volunteers will be published in the next update.</P>
