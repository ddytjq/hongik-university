<A NAME="seeding"></A><H2>Seeding Information</H2>

<P><I>Qualifying tournament will comprise 10 groups of five teams with winners qualifying for finals and runners up playing off in pairs with aggregate winners qualifying for finals also.</I>

<P><I>Portugal qualified as hosts.</I>

<P><B>Top seeds:</B> <I>Belgium, Czech Republic, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Romania, Spain, Sweden and Turkey.</I>

<P><B>Second seeds:</B> <I>Croatia, Denmark, England, Netherlands, Poland, Russia, Scotland, Slovenia, Ukraine and Yugoslavia.</I>

<P><B>Third seeds:</B> <I>Austria, Bulgaria, Finland, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Israel, Norway, Slovakia and Switzerland.</I>

<P><B>Fourth seeds:</B> <I>Belarus, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Cyprus, Estonia, Georgia, Latvia, Lithuania, Macedonia, Northern Ireland and Wales.</I>

<P><B>Fifth seeds:</B> <I>Albania, Andorra, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Faroe Islands, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Malta, Moldova and San Marino.</I>

<P><I>Seeding was based on points gained in 2002 World Cup Qualifying and 2000 European Championship Qualifying (excluding playoff matches) matches divided by the number of matches played.</I>

<A NAME="dates"></A><H2>Match Dates</H2>

<P><I>Group matches: 2002 -> 7-8 September, 12-13 October, 15-16 October. 2003 -> 29-30 March, 1-2 April, 7-8 June, 10-11 June, 6-7 September, 11-12 October.</I>

<P><I>Playoffs: 15-16 and 18-19 November 2003.</I>
