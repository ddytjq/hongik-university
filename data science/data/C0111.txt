		<td bgcolor="#ffebcd"><font face="Verdana,geneva,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif" size="2">The schemes for <a href="http://www.studentwatchout.co.uk/page.mv?p=belongings">General Belongings</a> usually give you Basic Cover (whether just in your place of residence or anywhere around the campus etc.) against Fire and Theft, plus Storm, Flood and other suchlike. They don't cover simple Loss or Breakage. All Risks Cover, usually on specified items of value, will include these extra risks, and will also extend to anywhere in the UK, sometimes worldwide. It is a good idea to get All Risks cover for anything of value which you'll be carting around outside your room(s) &#150; but remember that it still won't include mechanical breakdown or wear-and-tear. 		<td align="right" valign="top" bgcolor="#ff6600"><font size="4" color="white"><b>A</b></font><font size="1" color="white"><b>:&nbsp;</b></font></td>
		<td bgcolor="#ffebcd"><font face="Verdana,geneva,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif" size="2">The excess is the first part of a valid claim which has to be carried by the policyholder (e.g. if there is a &pound;50 excess, a &pound;500 claim will merit a &pound;450 payout). The main reason for this is to avoid petty claims like losing a pen or library book. However on some types of insurance such as health or motor, you can greatly reduce the premium by taking a voluntary excess of, say, &pound;500. NB this is one of the appeals of the <a href="http://www.studentwatchout.co.uk/page.mv?p=watchoutcc">Complete Computer Cover</a> policy offered by Summit - there is no excess.</font></td>
