</FONT><HR>Here is a clip from my column in RPM Racing News this week concerning the Outlaw broadcasts on SpeedTV.<P>While Ohio Sprint Speedweek is usually the biggest news in Ohio racing over the fourth of July, a bigger story jumped out at me during one of the Speedweek races. Prior to the racing at K-C on Wednesday, I got into a conversation with Brad Doty. Brad and I got onto the subject of the televised Outlaw shows coming up on SpeedTV. I asked him when he had to go to Charlotte to do the voiceover for the first broadcast. This is where he dropped the bomb on me. He told me that it doesn’t look good for him to be doing the broadcasts this year. All of the races are being taped and having the announcing taped over the video in the studio in Charlotte. Bobby Gerould is doing the pit reporting at the races to be included in the broadcasts. Brad told me that SpeedTV is planning on using a team of Winston Cup drivers as the voiceover announcers. As of now they are looking to use Jeff Gordon, Tony Stewart, and Ken Schrader as the commentary crew. Their logic is that the “star power” of these drivers will bring in a bigger viewing audience. A better plan for a bigger viewing audience might be to air the program in a time slot other than 11:00 p.m. on a Thursday night. So as of now, it looks like these Outlaw show broadcasts will not include our own Brad Doty. They also plan on condensing the racing for each night that they tape into a one hour program. Subtracting out the commercial time means that we are looking at a whole night of racing and all of the interviews and features being shown in a span of 44 minutes. Brad asked me to spread the word of this and for anyone that doesn’t agree with, or like, this set up, to please e-mail SpeedTV. You can e-mail them at: <A HREF="http://www.speedtv.com." TARGET=_blank>www.speedtv.com.</A> On the menu on the left side of the page, click on feedback and fill out the form voicing your displeasure over this.
