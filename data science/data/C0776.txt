                                    <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                    <b> </b> </font> </td>
                                  <td background width="398" height="100%">
                                    <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman" lang="EN-US">Wednesday</span>
                                    <span lang="EN-US" style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;"> 14th
                                    November, 2001<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText"><font color="#003399" size="5" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>BIBA
                                    survey shows brokers want better service
                                    from insurers</b></font><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size:18.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;"><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText2"><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">The
                                    ninth annual survey of brokers commissioned
                                    by the British Insurance Brokers’
                                    Association (BIBA), the UK’s leading
                                    insurance trade organisation shows overall
                                    satisfaction with insurer service has
                                    remained consistent since last year, with
                                    only a handful of insurers performing better
                                    than in 2000.<span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    </span><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText3"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">The
                                    survey, carried out by Business &amp; Market
                                    Research, confirms that the key elements of
                                    service are competitive premiums and
                                    flexibility of underwriting.&nbsp; Also
                                    increasingly important is the insurer’s
                                    long term commitment to the broker market.&nbsp;
                                    Insurers need to perform well in these areas
                                    to encourage brokers to place business, with
                                    service and relationship factors
                                    particularly important for Commercial Lines
                                    brokers.</span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText3"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Said
                                    Mike Williams, chief executive of BIBA,
                                    “This year’s research shows that
                                    although results are fairly consistent,
                                    service standards still leave room for
                                    improvement. However, we know that insurers
                                    are aware of the problem areas and to their
                                    credit are prepared to talk to BIBA about
                                    the issues and how they might be overcome.
                                    We will be doing the research again next
                                    year, and will be looking at similar issues,
                                    but asking more in-depth questions.”</span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText2"><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;"><b>Degree
                                    of satisfaction with insurer service</b><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Overall
                                    levels of satisfaction have not changed, and
                                    brokers would like to see improvements in a
                                    number of areas, particularly in Commercial
                                    Lines.&nbsp; In general, brokers with
                                    partnerships do not perceive they receive a
                                    significantly better level of service from
                                    their preferred insurers.<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <h1><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:10.0pt;
font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Important
                                    elements of service<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></h1>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">The
                                    top four service elements for Personal Lines
                                    remain the same as in 2000: competitive
                                    premiums 68%, flexible underwriting 44%,
                                    long term commitment to the broker market
                                    40% and speed of claims settlement 36%.<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">For
                                    Commercial Lines again the same top four as
                                    last year: competitive premiums 59%,
                                    flexible underwriting 53%, long term
                                    commitment to the broker market 41% and
                                    access to underwriting decision makers 36%.&nbsp;
                                    For all intermediaries long term commitment
                                    to the broker market has increased in
                                    importance since last year.<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <h1><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:10.0pt;
font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;">Other Issues<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></h1>
                                    <ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Brokers
                                        said that the biggest issues facing them
                                        are a shrinking market and direct sales.
                                        For Commercial Lines, Direct Sales
                                        stayed static at 15% vs.14% in 2000;
                                        Personal Lines stayed static at&nbsp;
                                        31%.&nbsp;&nbsp; The Shrinking Market
                                        was mentioned as being important by 19%
                                        Commercial Lines brokers and 10% of
                                        Personal Lines.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">GISC
                                        – the introduction of GISC was cited
                                        as the third biggest issue.&nbsp;</span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Asked
                                        specifically about it, the majority were
                                        in favour of the introduction of GISC
                                        with 60% of Commercial Lines and 53% of
                                        Personal lines intermediaries either
                                        strongly or slightly in favour.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                        Only about a quarter of those
                                        interviewed were against.
                                        &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The need for a
                                        body to regulate the business, and that
                                        GISC will bring more professional
                                        standards to the industry were the main
                                        reasons given for being in favour.</span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Profit
                                        sharing – A substantial majority were
                                        either strongly or slightly in favour of
                                        profit sharing, with 42% of Commercial
                                        Lines and 34% of Personal Lines brokers
                                        strongly in favour.</span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">B</span><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">roker
                                        Categories – Those interviewed were
                                        ambivalent towards broker categories,
                                        with the majority either not sure or not
                                        in favour.&nbsp;</span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Insurance
                                        Company mergers – This remains an
                                        issue for brokers, who cite lack of
                                        choice and a deteriorating service as
                                        the main effects.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                        The main way brokers cope (47% of
                                        Commercial Lines) is actively to manage
                                        the volumes of business they place with
                                        insurers.</span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Telephone
                                        Service Centres – Asked about their
                                        reaction to the increasing use of call
                                        centres, brokers said the main
                                        disadvantages were the lack of a
                                        personal touch and “untrained
                                        staff”.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; These are
                                        the priority areas for insurers to
                                        improve.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                        Although some brokers can see advantages
                                        in the trend towards telephone service
                                        centres, 49% of Personal Lines and 58%
                                        of Commercial Lines intermediaries can
                                        see none.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                    </ul>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size:11.0pt;mso-bidi-font-size:
10.0pt;font-family:Arial;mso-bidi-font-family:&quot;Times New Roman&quot;"><b>Notes
                                    for editors:</b><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">The
                                    aim of the research, which is benchmarked
                                    against the 2000 survey, is to:<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">&nbsp;</span></p>
                                    <ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Identify
                                        key elements of service, which affect
                                        intermediary choice of, and satisfaction
                                        with, insurance companies.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Benchmark
                                        participating insurers’ performance
                                        against these key elements, enabling
                                        them to identify areas for improvement
                                        and track progress.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Identify
                                        key issues/concerns impacting on
                                        intermediaries’ business – e.g.
                                        technology, GISC, profit sharing.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">10
                                        major insurers participated.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">1003
                                        telephone interviews were carried out
                                        during September and October 2001 with
                                        general insurance intermediaries –
                                        split Commercial and Personal Lines and
                                        across a representative sample of the
                                        market in terms of size and geographic
                                        spread.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">37%
                                        of Commercial Lines and 22% of Personal
                                        Lines interviewees were BIBA members.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo9;tab-stops:list .5in"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Intermediaries
                                        were asked to rate up to three insurers
                                        on a range of issues, which have been
                                        identified as being important when
                                        placing new insurance business.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </span></li>
                                    </ul>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">The
                                    survey is confidential and rankings of
                                    individual insurance companies are therefore
                                    not for disclosure.<br>
