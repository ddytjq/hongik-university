          <P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>WASHINGTON&nbsp;-- The name is always at or near the top of high school basketball's national rankings. The name is all over the rosters of various postseason all-star games. The name is synonymous with prep hoops.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The name? Oak Hill Academy.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>There are other schoolboy programs active and visible on the national scene year in and year out, but the little, Baptist boarding school in a place called Mouth of Wilson, Va., has become the sport's mecca.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Coach Steve Smith's program is the cream of the crop in spite of playing its home games in a tiny gym on a tiny campus in an even tinier town in Southwestern Virginia&nbsp;-- or maybe it's the cream of the crop because of that.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Our environment is conducive to that," said Smith, who just finished his 17th season as Oak Hill's head coach. "In other places, there are other things to do."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And in Mouth of Wilson?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Nothing," said Carmelo Anthony, the school's McDonald's All-American this year, "except school and ball."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>No wonder, then, that the Mighty Warriors have won four national championships since 1993, including last year's undefeated season. This year's team went 32-1 and finished second to Dallas Lincoln's 40-0 squad in USA Today's final national rankings.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Or that the school's alums in the last 15 years include guys like Rod Strickland, Cory Alexander, Junior Burrough, Jeff McInnis, Ron Mercer, Steve Jackson, William Avery, Sagana Diop, Jules Camara and Rashaad Carruth.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Or that Smith has over 500 wins in only 17 years. Or that he's here in the District coaching the White Team in preparation for the Jordan Brand Capital Classic Thursday night at MCI Center.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Or that two of his players from this winter&nbsp;-- Anthony and Wake Forest-bound guard Justin Gray&nbsp;-- are playing on the opposing Red Team. Or that another&nbsp;-- Cincinnati-bound guard Chadd Moore&nbsp;-- is representing the U.S. Team in Thursday evening's regional undercard.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The name is all over all the time. The name is Oak Hill.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Now it might be at the highest level it's ever been, I believe," Smith said. "It's gotten to the point where I may have my best group yet every single year."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>It's not hard to see why.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"You get your work done," Gray said during Tuesday's Cap Classic media day here at Children's Hospital, "and then you play until you can't play no more, until you're dog tired. With all we do, you have to be dedicated to your game."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And to getting better. Oak Hill isn't in a high school league or any sanctioned organization. The Warriors can work out year-round -- and they do.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"They play pickup every day in the off-season," Smith explained. "They lift weights five days a week. Most kids don't do that in the off-season. Ours just keep going at it because we can. We can do more than other high schools."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Which is why some critics think Smith's troops should be ineligible for national polls like the one in USA Today.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But Smith doesn't just get the best of prep basketball's best. He takes what he gets&nbsp;-- he hardly even has to recruit anymore&nbsp;-- and he makes it better.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Gray is a perfect example.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The 6-foot-2 combo guard arrived as a junior in the fall of 2000. He could shoot&nbsp;-- Gray always could shoot&nbsp;-- but he needed to improve his ball-handling skills and add some much-needed weight and strength.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But the Charlotte, N.C., native ended up starting last season&nbsp;-- with Carruth and Diop, both McDonald's All-Americans, and Billy Edelin (at Syracuse) and Mario Boggan (going to Florida).</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He solidified his ACC-caliber standing last spring with a standout performance at the Boo Williams Invitational in Hampton, Va., and then continued his solid play throughout the summer season with the Charlotte Royals.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Now he's headed to Wake to play in the ACC after emerging as a legitimate star this winter for the Warriors.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I wouldn't have had the choices I had if I hadn't gone to Oak Hill," Gray said. "It gave me the confidence and allowed me to see where I was. It showed me I could play with the best, and once you get that confidence, you feel like you can be on top of your game and play with anybody."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He can, he has, and he will Thursday night at MCI.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"There are a lot of great players around you," Anthony said. "All 10 players on the court during practice and pickup are going to be good, they're going to get after it&nbsp;-- and don't nobody like to lose down there."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Good thing they so seldom do.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>CANADIAN PRIDE</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Denham Brown would be one of the best prep basketball players in America&nbsp;-- except for the fact that he doesn't live in America. The UConn-bound swingman is from Toronto.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he's one of the best all-around scorers set to enter the NCAA ranks this coming fall. His Canadian roots? The only reason more people don't know that already. But he's ready to strut his stuff Thursday night in the Cap Classic.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"This is one of the biggest opportunities to show what Canadian ball is all about," he said Tuesday afternoon at media day here at Children's Hospital. "If I do a great job here, I can prove something for all Canadian players."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And for himself. Not that he should still have to show that he belongs. The 6-5 wing, after all, scored 111 points for West Hill High School in a Toronto Scarborough league game back in early February.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He made 25 two-point jumpers, 16 three-pointers and a few layups, dunks and free throws on his way to the Canadian single-game scoring record in his high school finale. He had 52 points at half and 93 at the end of the third quarter.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The game was 32 minutes long.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Guys were just rushing at him," said Ro Russell, Brown's AAU coach with the Grassroots Canada program. "He was getting double- and triple-teamed, but he was in a zone."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And no jokes about the level of competition.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Some people couldn't score that many if you gave them 32 minutes alone in their driveway," UConn assistant Tom Moore said. "His offensive ability, his body, his toughness&nbsp;-- we think he's physically ready to play in the Big East right now."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>So do the folks up in Toronto.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He really is a scoring machine," Grassroots assistant Wayne Dawkins said. "You don't see anyone who scores like Denham. He can shoot contested threes from deep in the corners. It just doesn't matter."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And it's true: Brown is one of the best in this year's crop of college-bound talent&nbsp;-- maybe the best&nbsp;-- in creating space to get off just about any shot from just about anywhere against just about anybody. He's a pro-caliber shooter. Right now.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>According to Russell, Brown wrapped up his schoolboy career with more than 3,500 career points, 1,500-plus rebounds, 800-plus assists and 400-plus steals. Who knows what that means up there?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But at the very least, the Toronto-based hoops guru said, it means "he's the best high school player ever to come out of Canada to this point."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And it also means this: He'll be one of the best players out there Thursday night, and he'll be an immediate contributor for Jim Calhoun's program in Storrs, Conn.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>No matter that he talks a little funny. No matter that his address is North of the Border.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's an all-star game," Brown said, "so I just want to have a good time. This is for the fans and the people of America to see our talent."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>His in particular. Because it's high time they do.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Aaron Spears</B> averaged 12 points a game as a junior last year at Chicago's Dunbar High School. He was little more than a valuable space-eater for the Illinois Warriors last summer on the AAU circuit. This winter, though, he spent his senior season dominating Public League ball in the Windy City, putting up 28 points and 15 rebounds per outing. The difference? About 30 pounds. That's how much weight the big guy lost in the summer and fall. "I could run faster, I could get up and down the court, and I was more agile," he said Tuesday at Cap Classic media day. "And I added skills. I worked all summer on my game."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Andre Iguodala</B> got his release from Arkansas following the abrupt dismissal of coach <B>Nolan Richardson</B>&nbsp;-- and he's not looking back. The rangy wing from Springfield, Ill., is going to announce for either Kansas or Arizona after the Wendy's Windy City all-star game Saturday in Chicago. "The whole year and the whole tournament, Kansas ran up and down in transition, and that's my type of game," he said Tuesday. "Arizona is the same thing." Iguodala was one of this year's more glaring McDonald's snubs. Don't think that won't be in the back of his mind when he takes the floor Thursday night at MCI.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Hal Pastner</B> was in Hampton, Va., for this past weekend's Boo Williams Invitational, and he was trumpeting his upcoming Kingwood Classic. The annual event ? set this year for April 26-28 in Houston's northern 'burbs&nbsp;-- has a total of 220 teams in all divisions. It's also one of this spring's few sanctioned events&nbsp;-- in other words, it's open for Division I college coaches to evaluate the talent on hand. "It's the only one in the country sanctioned that weekend," Pastner said. "The coaches can see the best juniors and also sophomores, freshmen and eighth graders. It's going to be a real boost for the college coaches."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** The best championship game at Boo Williams wasn't in the 17-and-under division. It was the 16-and-under game pitting the Jackson Tigers from Mississippi and the Alabama Lasers. <B>Al Jefferson</B>, the touted sophomore post terror from Prentiss, Miss., lived up to his billing. But he wasn't the only big-time 10th grader worth watching on Sunday. <B>Charles Rhodes</B>, a 6-8 relative unknown from Forest, Miss., was plenty good as well. Watch out for this group on this year's 16 circuit&nbsp;-- and then next year on the 17s.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Slam Dunk to the Beach tournament boss <B>Bobby Jacobs</B> was in New York last Thursday night for the McDonald's game. He drove back to his Delaware home after the festivities in Madison Square Garden, caught a few hours of winks, worked a half day on Friday and then drove down to Hampton, Va., for the Boo Williams Invitational. He was courtside at HU's Holland Hall shortly after 6 p.m. Two things: (1) That's why folks think the man's a nut, and (2) that's why the same folks think his tournaments are the best in all of high school basketball. Guy does his homework.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Jacobs' new baby&nbsp;-- the War on the Shore&nbsp;-- is set for the last weekend of this coming November. "It's going to revolutionize the world of prep basketball," said Jacobs, ever the promoter&nbsp;-- but he might be right. The 12-team field includes New England powers such as New Hampton, Winchendon, Worcester Academy, Maine Central Institute and St. Thomas More, Southeastern players Fork Union, Hargrave Military and Laurinburg Institute and up-and-coming Long Island program Our Savior New American. The gym in Milford, Del., holds 1,200, Jacobs said, and he expects it to be packed.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** So what the heck is the so-called Jordan Brand? According to a release in the Nike-issued press packet for this game, it is "a premium brand of men's footwear, apparel and accessories inspired by the dynamic legacy, vision and direct involvement of <B>Michael Jordan</B>." So there. The Brand&nbsp;-- or just <I>Jordan</I>&nbsp;-- debuted in 1997. The overall Air Jordan franchise, of course, started back in 1984, MJ's rookie year with the Chicago Bulls.</FONT></P>
