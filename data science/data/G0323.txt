              A Catalog<!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>

        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" --> 
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="4"><font size="3" color="#6666CC"><b>4. 
              Audiovisual & Media Materials</b></font></a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Again, 
              we note that only projects or educational organizations are listed, 
              not simply commercial producers of video. </font> </p>
            <p> 
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>American 
                Association of Physics Teachers (AAPT): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has a modest catalog of audiovisual materials for teaching physics, 
                which includes some astronomy [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific (ASP): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Produces and distributes slide sets on astronomical topics with 
                extensive booklets of captions and background material, as well 
                as many videotapes and CD-ROM's. Ask for their free, illustrated 
                catalog (1-800-335-2624) or check their web site [see <a href="naep05.html#appendix">appendix</a>] 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Center 
                for Astrophysics (CfA): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                The Private Universe Project is producing a series of videotapes 
                on student misconceptions in science and strategies for promoting 
                conceptual change. Contact: Nancy Finkelstein (617-496-7676) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Coast 
                Telecourses: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Developed 26 half-hour episodes of <i>Universe: The Infinite Frontier,</i> 
                a new educational TV show. Contact at: 11460 Warner Ave., Fountain 
                Valley, CA 92708 (714-241-6109); <a href="http://sun.ccc.cccd.edu/~ct/cthome/frame.html" target="_blank">http://ct.ccc.cccd.edu/default.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Finley-Holiday 
                Films: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                This commercial company has been designated by both the Jet Propulsion 
                Laboratory and the Space Telescope Science Institute to distribute 
                slides and videos to educators at reduced cost. Contact them for 
                a current catalog: P.O. Box 619, Whittier, CA 90608 (800-345-6707); 
                <a href="http://www.finley-holiday.com" target="_blank">http://www.finley-holiday.com</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Lunar 
                and Planetary Institute: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Produces and distributes slide sets, CD-ROMs, and other educational 
                materials on planetary science concepts. Contact: 3600 Bay Area 
                Blvd., Houston, TX 77058 (281-486-2175); <a href="http://cass.jsc.nasa.gov/lpi/" target="_blank">http://cass.jsc.nasa.gov/lpi/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                CORE: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Distributes a wide range of NASA audiovisual materials at low 
                cost to teachers. Ask for their catalog & updates. Contact at: 
                Lorain County JVS, 15181 Route 58 South, Oberlin, OH 44074 (216-774-1051, 
                ext 293, 294) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NOVA 
                Television Programs: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                With support from NSF, these are among the very best science programs 
                for teaching. Educators can obtain copies of shows at reasonable 
                cost by calling WGBH (the Boston Public TV station that produces 
                NOVA) at 1-800-255-9424. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>The 
                Planetary Society: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Produces and distributes slides and videoas about the solar system 
                and the search for life. See the catalog in their <i>Planetary 
                Report</i> magazine or their web-site at <a href="http://planetary.org/store/" target="_blank">http://planetary.org/store.html</a>. 
                Their address is 65 N. Catalina Ave., Pasadena, CA 91106, (626-793-5100). 
                </font> 
            </dl>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="naep.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="5"></a>5. 
              Computer Materials or Projects</b></font> </p>
            <dl> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Astronomy 
                Software List Project: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                John Mosley of the Griffith Observatory (and <i>Sky & Telescope's</i> 
                primary software reviewer) will supply an up-to-date listing of 
                astronomical software for $2.00. Make check out to John Mosley, 
                7303 Enfield Ave., Reseda, CA 91335. (It's a great resource.) 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>The 
                Astronomy Village I and II: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                CD-ROM, Mac-based supplementary curriculum for 9th grade students. 
                Contact: Tom Pie, NASA Center for Educational Technologies, Wheeling 
                Jesuit College, 220 Washington Ave, Wheeling, WV 26003 (304-243-2388) 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Exploration 
                in Education (ExInEd): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Project directed by Robert Brown at the Space Telescope Science 
                Institute to produce inexpensive Macintosh discs and CD-ROMs of 
                images (ranging from Hubble Space Telescope images to paintings 
                of an asteroid hitting the Earth) and background material in HyperCard 
                format. See their web site at <a href="http://www.stsci.edu/exined/" target="_blank">http://www.stsci.edu/exined/</a>. 
                Discs are available through the Astronomical Society of the Pacific 
                catalog. </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Hands-On 
                Universe Project: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Trains teachers to do computer processing of student-acquired 
                astronomical images. Contact: Amelia Marshal, Lawrence Berkeley 
                Labs, U. of California, Berkeley, CA 94720 (510-486-5236) Further 
                information available at <a href="http://hou.lbl.gov" target="_blank">http://hou.lbl.gov</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Image 
                Processing for Teaching: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                This multi-disciplinary project (led by Richard Greenberg at the 
                U. of Arizona) trains teachers to use image processing to stimulate 
                student interest in science. A range of materials and teacher 
                workshops is available from Image Processing for Teaching, Lunar 
                and Planetary Laboratory, University of Arizona, PO Box 210092, 
                Tucson, AZ 85721-0092 (520-621-2234); <a href="http://ipt.lpl.arizona.edu" target="_blank">http://ipt.lpl.arizona.edu</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Joint 
                Education Initiative: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Develops CD-ROMs & Internet projects for teachers in geologic 
                and planetary sciences. Contact: Robert Ridky, 3433 A. V. Williams 
                Bldg., U. of Maryland, College Park, MD 20742 (301-405-2324) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Lowell 
                Observatory: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Has an innovative interactive exhibit simulating a night at an 
                observatory, with plans available to other institutions. Contact 
                Bill Buckingham, Lowell Obs., 1400 East Mars Hill Rd., Flagstaff, 
                AZ 86001 (602-774-3358). </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>MicroObservatory 
                (Center for Astrophysics): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Developing fully automated, CCD-equipped telescopes with Internet 
                links for remote observing by students. (Ken Brecher at Boston 
                University is also a leader of this project, but contact the Education 
                Dept. at the Center for Astrophysics; address on last page.) <a href="http://mo-www.harvard.edu/MicroObservatory" target="_blank">http://mo-www.harvard.edu/MicroObservatory</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Center for Educational Technologies: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Internet-based middle school curriculum and other resources for 
                educators available. Further information available at www.cotf.edu. 
                or contact Tom Pie, NASA Center for Educational Technologies, 
                Wheeling Jesuit College, 220 Washington Ave, Wheeling, WV 26003 
                (304-243-2388) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Imagine the Universe Project (formerly the High Energy Astrophysics 
                Learning Center): </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Free CD-ROM of the Learning Center and StarChild educational websites 
                with information and activities. Further information is available 
                at <a href="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/" target="_blank">http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                K-12 Internet in the Classroom: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Initiative to help K-12 schools use the Internet for Space Science 
                education. Offers a variey of projects, some without charge, to 
                schools across the United States. Internet initiative videos are 
                available at the cost of distribution from NASA CORE [see <a href="naep03.html#4">section 
                4</a>]. <a href="http://quest.arc.nasa.gov" target="_blank">http://quest.arc.nasa.gov</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>NASA 
                Spacelink: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                An electronic aeronautics and space resource designed to provide 
                current and historical educational information to teachers and 
                students located at <a href="http://spacelink.msfc.nasa.gov" target="_blank">http://spacelink.msfc.nasa.gov</a>. 
                For futher information contact Spacelink, Code CL01, NASA Marshall 
                SFC, Huntsville, AL 35812 (205-544-6360) </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Passport 
                to Knowledge: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                An ongoing series of "electronic field trips to scientific frontiers" 
                which uses interactive television and online computer networks 
                to "take" students to frontier research areas research. Contact: 
                Passport to Knowledge, P.O. Box 1502, Summit, NJ 07902-1502 (800-626-LIVE) 
                <a href="http://quest.arc.nasa.gov/antarctica/passport.html" target="_blank">http://quest.arc.nasa.gov/antarctica/passport.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Project 
                CLEA: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Produces innovative computer-based astronomy lab exercises for 
                undergraduates on IBM and Mac. Contact at: Dept. of Physics, Gettysburg 
                College, Gettysburg, PA 17325 (717-337-6028); <a href="http://www.gettysburg.edu/project/physics/clea/CLEAhome.html" target="_blank">http://www.gettysburg.edu/project/physics/clea/CLEAhome.html</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Remote 
                Access Astronomy Project: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Computerized telescope and dial-in data distribution (using images 
                from many telescopes) with image processing software & activities. 
                Contact them c/o Dept. of Physics, University. of California, 
                Santa Barbara, CA 93106 (805-893-7240); e-mail: <a href="mailto:raap@rot.ucsb.edu">raap@rot.ucsb.edu</a>; 
                <a href="http://www.deepspace.ucsb.edu/rot.htm" target="_blank">http://www.deepspace.ucsb.edu/rot.htm</a> 
                </font> 
              <dt> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Science 
                Information Infrastructure Education Project: </b></font> 
              <dd><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">
