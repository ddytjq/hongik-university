        <p align="left"><font face="Arial"><strong>PREFACE<br>
        </strong></font><font face="Times New Roman">This essay
        presents a coherent theory on the origin of birds and a
        theory on the evolution of flight. </font></p>
        <p align="left"><font face="Times New Roman">The theory
        is based on a concept of the &quot;pre-bird&quot;, the
        hypothetic sequense of stages through which the birds may
        have evolved from quadrupedal diapsides through gradually
        transformed stages. Each stage is with regard to
        behaviour, physiology and anatomy presumed to be adapted
        to environments which actually existed at the relevant
        times.</font></p>
        <p align="left"><font face="Times New Roman">The method
        is thus a holistic application of anatomical-functional,
        environmental, and evolutionary considerations. </font></p>
        <p align="left"><font face="Times New Roman">It is
        demonstrated that the evolution of the anatomical,
        physiological and behavioral preconditions of the
        flapping flight in birds <i>may</i> be explained as the
        adaptation to a supplementary tree-climbing behaviour of
        a group of bipedal, ground-living archosaurs.</font></p>
        <p align="left"><font face="Times New Roman">It is
        concluded that a habit of flapping of the forelimbs
        initiated the evolution of the flight in birds and that
        this habit <i>may</i> have been present in the avian
        lineage before any kind of wings had arisen.</font></p>
