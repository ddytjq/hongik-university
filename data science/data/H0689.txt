<p><FONT SIZE="+2" COLOR="#FFCC00">Entamoeba coli</font></p>
</font>
<table width="750">
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td height="81" colspan="3"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The 
      ability to identify amoeba based on nuclear structure in iodine wet mount 
      preparations is limited. Cysts of the amoeba are usually granular. Cysts 
      of the mature pathogenic E. histolytica contain four nuclei while those 
      of the non-pathogenic E. coli contain up to eight nuclei. </font></td>
  </tr>
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td height="225" colspan="2"><img src="image/102ecolitroph.jpg" width="225" height="225" border="0" alt="102 - E coli troph" lowsrc="image/low/102ecolitroph.jpg"></td>
    <td width=75% height="213" valign="middle"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The 
      single nucleus of the trophozoite is often visible in unstained preparations 
      with the non-compact karyosome that is eccentrically located.</font></td>
  </tr></table>

 
<table width="750" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="2"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Sometimes the nuclei 
        may contain a central karyosome with uniform, peripheral chromatin and 
        may be confused with the pathogenic Entamoeba histolytica, as in the E. 
        coli at left.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">A distorted Giardia 
        cyst is also evident in this field.</font></p>
    <td width=225> <img src="image/29ecoli.jpg" width="225" height="225" align="right" alt="29 E coli" lowsrc="image/low/29ecoli.jpg"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="750">
  <tr> 
    <td width=225><img src="image/28ecolicyst.jpg" width="225" height="225" lowsrc="image/low/28ecolicyst.jpg" alt="28 - E. coli cyst"> 
    <td> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Immature E. coli cysts 
        containing four or fewer of the eight nuclei may be difficult to distinguish 
        from E. histolytica. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">But not so in these 
        wet preparations where 5-7 nuclei can be seen. </font> </p>
    
    <td width=200 align="right"><img src="image/100ecolicyst.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="100 - E coli cyst" align="right" lowsrc="image/100ecolicyst_low.jpg"> 
  </tr></table>
<table width="750" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="2"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Sometimes uninucleate 
        cysts can also be seen with a large central glycogen vacuole pushing the 
        nucleus to the margin of the cyst. Note the eccentrically placed karyosome. 
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">A dark Giardia cyst 
        is also evident in this phase mount. </font></p>
    <td width=26%><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><img src="image/30ecoli.jpg" width="225" height="225" border="0" alt="30 - E coli cyst and dark Giardia cysts" align="middle" lowsrc="image/low/30ecoli.jpg"></font>
  </tr>
</table>

      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">These two trophozoites 
        demonstrate some typical features of the parasite. </font></p>
      <ul>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1">karyosomes 
          are eccentrically positioned </font></li>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="-1">perpheral
