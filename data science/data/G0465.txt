<div class="subheader"><a href="http://universe.gsfc.nasa.gov/roadmap/cosmicj.html" title="SEU  2000 Roadmap">SEU Year 2000 Cosmic Journeys Roadmap</a></div>
<p>
<div class="mainheader">Solicitation of Community Input to the SEU Roadmap</div>

<p>NASA's Structure and Evolution of the Universe (SEU) Theme is updating its <a href="http://universe.gsfc.nasa.gov/roadmap/cosmicj.html" title="2001 Roadmap">Roadmap</a> in support of the <a href="http://spacescience.nasa.gov/">NASA Office of Space Science (OSS)</a> strategic planning process.  The Roadmap and Strategic Plan will cover the years 2003-2028.  A <a href="/roadmap/team.html">Roadmap Team</a> has been formed with the following <a href="/roadmap/charter.html">Charter</a>. The team will report back to the <a href="http://spacescience.nasa.gov/adv/">Structure and Evolution of the Universe Subcommittee (SEUS)</a>. The roadmapping process will be completed by the fall of 2002.</p>

<p><font color="Red">The SEU Roadmap Team solicits community input in the form of white papers </font> describing mission concepts (principally for the years 2011+ after <a href="http://constellation.gsfc.nasa.gov/">Constellation-X</a>, <a href="http://glast.gsfc.nasa.gov/">GLAST</a>, <a href="http://lisa.jpl.nasa.gov/">LISA</a>, and <a href="http://astro.estec.esa.nl/SA-general/Projects/Planck/">Planck/Herschel</a>) or strategic activities (e.g., laboratory astrophysics, multi-mission technology development, theoretical efforts) which support the SEU theme. Requirements for the structure of submitted white papers are described <a href="#format">below</a>. They may be submitted electronically <a href="#submit">here</a>. <font color="Red">The deadline is January 31, 2002.</font></p>

<p>The roadmap team will be actively soliciting white papers on some missions and activities.  To avoid duplication of effort, you are urged to discuss your ideas with like-minded colleagues and/or members of the roadmap team before submitting a white paper.</p>

<p>The current members of the Roadmap team are: Sterl Phinney (chair), Paul Hertz (ex-officio, NASA/HQ), Sean Carroll, Sarah Church, Roy Gould (E/PO), Craig Hogan, Rocky Kolb (ex-officio), Dan Lester, Steven Kahn, Robert March, Michael Shull, Simon Swordy, and Nicholas White. Contact information is <a href="/roadmap/team.html">here</a>.</p>

<div class="subheader">Scope of NASA's Structure and Evolution of the Universe Theme.</div>

<p>The science objectives of the SEU Theme are:</p>
<ul>
<li>To understand the structure of the universe, from its earliest beginnings to its ultimate fate.
<li>To explore the cycles of matter and energy in the evolving universe.
<li>To explore the ultimate limits of gravity and energy in the universe.
<li>To elucidate the fundamental laws of physics.
</ul>

<div class="subheader">Scope of the 2003 Roadmap</div>

<p>The 2003 Roadmap will cover the years 2003-2028.
The 2003 Roadmap will consider all space missions (with the exception of those which should be realized as Explorer missions -see <a href="#format">criteria</a>) and related scientific and techological activities relevant to the SEU science objectives. This includes observations of the universe using particles, gravitational waves, or electromagnetic radiation of any wavelength and tests of the fundamental laws of physics relevant to astrophysics and cosmology.  The 2000 SEU Roadmap, covering the years 2003-2023, may be found <a href="http://universe.gsfc.nasa.gov/roadmap/cosmicj.html">here</a>.

<p>The SEU science objectives may be focused across a broad range of astrophysics and are intended to be complimentary to the science objectives of <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/index.html">NASA's Astronomical Search for Origins Theme</a> (including the major Origins missions: <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/ngst.html">NGST</a>, <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/sim.html">SIM</a>, <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/tpf.html">TPF</a>).  Previous SEU Roadmaps have identified high priority mission sets which support the breadth of SEU science.  NASA realizes that all high priority science objectives cannot be realized first.  The <a href="/roadmap/charter.html">charter of the 2003 SEU Roadmap Team</a> directs the Roadmap Team to identify and articulate the highest priority science objective and the mission set needed to address that objective.  It is anticipated that the Roadmap Team will identify additional high-priority science objectives and missions that do not support the highest priority science objective; these objectives and missions will be identified in an appendix to the Roadmap.  Implementing these other objectives and missions will be addressed in future Roadmaps. <em>All high priority science objectives cannot be implemented first.</em></p>

<a name="format"><div class="subheader">Format of White Papers</div></a>

<p>We solicit two categories of white papers. Both are subject to</p>
<ul>
<li>Page limit: Four (4) text pages, plus up to two (2) pages of figures. 
<li>Format: PDF or PostScript.
<li>Deadline: January 31, 2002.
</ul>

<p><b>Category 1: Missions</b></p>

<p>Descriptions of mission concepts (principally for the years 2011+ after Planck/Herschel, LISA, GLAST, Constellation-X).  Mission concepts must be those which cannot or should not be funded as part of NASA's Explorer line (e.g. becausethey cost more than $180M or because a non-NASA partner contributes more than 25% of the mission cost).</p>

<p><b>Category 2: Non-mission Activities</b></p>

<p>Descriptions of major non-mission activities which could be funded by the SEU theme.  Examples include, but are not limited to, laboratory astrophysics, multi-mission technology or software development, and theoretical efforts.</p>

<p>The mission-concept white papers may follow any format fitting in the page limit above, but must clearly address each of the following seven points.</p>

<ol>
<li>What are the principal science goals of this mission, and what new areas of discovery space does it open?

<li>How does the mission bear on science priorities as expressed in the various National Academy survey reports?
<p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.nap.edu/books/0309066352/html/">NAS Committee on Gravitational Physics (1999)</a>
<li><a href="http://books.nap.edu/books/0309070317/html/R1.html#pagetop">NAS Astronomy and Astrophysics Survey Commitee (2001)</a>
<li><a href="http://www.nap.edu/books/0309074061/html/index.html">NAS Committee on Physics of the Universe (2001)</a>
<li><a href="http://www.nap.edu/books/0309073421/html/index.html">NAS Physics Survey Overview Committee (2001)</a>
</ul>
</p>
<li>How does the proposed mission or its technology address <a href="http://www.aip.org/enews/fyi/2001/133.html">national priorities</a> other than curiosity driven-research? This may be especially important if NASA is to justify new funding in the fiscal climate, with new demands on a severely constrained budget.<br>   

<li>How does the mission build upon accomplishments of other NASA space science missions, and fit into the <a href="http://spacescience.nasa.gov/admin/pubs/strategy/2000/">strategic plan</a> under which OSS now operates?  Is this mission in part a technological precursor to more advanced missions?  If so, what are they and their goals?

<li>How do the timescale and goals of this mission compare with those of <a href="http://sci.esa.int/content/news/index.cfm?aid=1&cid=1&oid=24804">non-NASA</a> missions? Are there likely to be strategic advantages in international collaboration?

<li>What precursor R&amp;D activity is required to ensure the success of the mission? In what areas of advanced technology would investments most strongly impact cost and performance? In what research areas would investments most strongly impact data analysis and interpretation?

<li>What kind of partnerships are envisioned for this mission (e.g., academic, international, other US government agency, industrial)?
</ol>

<hr>

<div class="mainheader"><a name="submit">Submission of White Papers</a></div>

<p>Note: By default, all submitted white papers will be considered public, and will be publicly posted on the Roadmap Web site <a href="/docs/roadmap/submissions.html">here</a>. If (e.g., because of industrial agreements) it is absolutely necessary that the documents have private proprietary sections, these should be separated from the main document, clearly identified, and submitted separately. <here link to some non world-readable ftp site, whose contents will NOT be posted publicly on the web, but in some password-protected form> These portions will be available to the Roadmap committee, the SEUS, and the working groups from which they may seek advice, but will not otherwise be made public. <font color="Red">Deadline is 5pm EST Jan 31, 2002.</font></p>

<p>White papers (in PDF or PostScript format) may be emailed to <a href="mailto:tyler@lheapop.gsfc.nasa.gov">tyler@lheapop.gsfc.nasa.gov</a> with a subject line containing the words "SEU Roadmap white paper", "Category <i>n</i>" [1 or 2], "&lt;<i>title of white paper</i>&gt;".</p>
