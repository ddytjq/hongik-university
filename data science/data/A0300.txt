		  <p class="contentsubhead">Home Loan Choices</p>
		  <p>When you buy a house, you not only need to think about the money you are borrowing, but also how you're going to repay it.</p>
		  <p>There are a number of different home loan options which you can repay your loan in different ways:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="#cairhl" class="link">Capital and Interest Repayment Home Loans</a></li>
<li><a href="#iol" class="link">Interest Only Loans</a></li>
<li><a href="#how" class="link">How Much Can I Borrow?</a></li>
</ul>
<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="cairhl"></a>Capital and Interest Repayment Home Loans</p>
<p>With a Capital & Interest Repayment Home Loan your monthly repayment is made up of two elements:</p>
<ul>
<li>Firstly you cover the interest on your Home Loan and,</li>
<li>secondly you pay back a portion of the capital you borrowed.</li>
</ul>
<p>Each time you make a payment, the amount of capital you owe to the Bank is reduced. At Northern we calculate the interest due on your Home Loan daily, so as the amount of capital outstanding on your Home Loan reduces, the amount of interest you pay also decreases.  At the end of the term, your Home Loan will be repaid in full.</p>
<p>This type of mortgage must be protected by a Life Assurance policy which will repay the amount outstanding on your Home Loan if you or your partner, if insured in join names, should die. There are a number of different types of Life Assurance used to cover this type of Home Loan:</p>
<ul>
<li>Mortgage Protection Plan<br>
This type of Life Protection provides a lump sum if you or your partner, if insured in join names, die during the term of your Home Loan. The amount payable reduces in line with the outstanding balance on your loan</li>
<p></p>
<li>Life Assurance Plan<br>
This type of Life Protection also provides a lump sum if you or your partner, if insured in join names, die during the term of your Home Loan, however in this case the amount payable remains constant throughout the term of your loan.</li>
</ul>
<p align="right"><a href="#top" class="link">Back to Top</a></p>
<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="iol"></a>Interest Only Loans</p>
<p>With this type of Home Loan your monthly payment to the Bank covers only the interest on your Home Loan. You do not repay any of the capital so at the end of the term, you still owe the Bank the original amount borrowed. If you apply for an investment backed Home Loan, you will need to make arrangements to repay the loan at the end of the agreed period.</p>
<p>For example:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Endowments</strong><br>
In addition to funding an investment policy for the future repayment of your Home Loan, an Endowment policy provides life assurance which protects your dependants by repaying the Home Loan if you die before the end of the term.</li>
<p></p>
<li><strong>Pension Plans</strong><br>
A Pension Plan Home Loan may be suitable if you are self employed or have a personal pension plan. It is similar in principle to an endowment Home Loan, however part of your monthly repayment is used to make regular contributions to an authorised pension provider which invests money in a fund free from UK taxes. The aim is to build up funds which should pay off your Home Loan and provide you with a pension for your retirement. You will also need a separate Life Assurance policy to pay off your Home Loan if you were to die during the term.</li>
</ul>
<p align="right"><a href="#top" class="link">Back to Top</a></p>
<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="how"></a>How Much Can I Borrow?</p>
<p>Before you start looking for a new home, it makes sense to know how much you can borrow, although we cannot make a definite Home Loan offer until you have a specific property in mind.</p>
<p>There are three main areas to look at when working out how much you can borrow.</p>
<p>
<ul>
<li>How much we can lend you</li>
<p></p>
<li>How much you can afford to repay</li>
<p></p>
<li>The value of the house (this is not always the same as the price or cost of the house)</li>
</ul>
<p>If you are applying for a Home Loan on your own, you can borrow up to three times your gross annual income, excluding any overtime or bonuses. Joint applicants can borrow either three times the higher income plus the second income or two and a half times the joint income.</p>
<p>These guidelines are designed so that you can cope with your Home Loan repayments each month, whilst leaving enough to cover your other commitments. It is important to be realistic about what you can afford to repay each month. Interest rates can vary a great deal, even over a short period of time, so bear in mind that if you buy when interest rates are low, you should budget for possible future increases in your monthly repayments.</p>
<p>We also take into account the value of the property you want to buy. We can lend you up to 95% of the cost or valuation of the property, whichever is lower.</p>
