<TABLE WIDTH="100%">
<TR>
<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">England became the 16th and final team to
qualify for next year's European Championships despite playing poorly and
crashing to a 1-0 home defeat at the hands of Scotland in the second leg of
their play-off on Wednesday. Scotland's fully-deserved win on the night was
secured by a 39th minute Don Hutchison header but England qualified 2-1 on
aggregate following their 2-0 win at Hampden Park on Saturday. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Scotland, who had not beaten England since 1985
and not scored against them in their previous five matches, were by far the
better, balanced and more enterprising team against the &quot;auld enemy&quot;
who were over-run in midfield, played like strangers and did not have one shot
on target in the entire match. Their only salvation was that they just about
defended well enough to stifle the Scottish attack. But goalkeeper David Seaman
had to made one stunning point-blank save from a diving header from Christian
Dailly with 10 minutes to play. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The only goal came when Callum Davidson broke
on the left, and sent Neil McCann away as Sol Campbell slipped. McCann crossed
high into the box and Hutchison rose above Tony Adams to head home. That sent
the 7,000 Scottish fans into raptures, but in the end they had to be content
with inflicting a first defeat on Kevin Keegan in his ninth match in charge.
</FONT></P>
</TD>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">PLAYOFF 1st Leg -

<TABLE WIDTH="100%">
<TR>
<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Paul Scholes capitalised on some woeful
defending to score two first half goals as England beat Scotland 2-0 at Hampden
Park in the first leg of their European Championship playoff on Saturday. The
victory moves England to the threshold of next year's finals and they should
duly take their place after the sides meet again in the second leg at Wembley
on Wednesday. </FONT></P>
</TD>
<TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="http://www.empics.com"><IMG
SRC="GIfs/sve5.jpg" ALT="{short description of image}" WIDTH="102" HEIGHT="127"

<TD><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Scholes opened the scoring with a superbly
executed goal after 21 minutes when he controlled a long pass out of midfield
from Sol Campbell on his chest, taking it past Scottish skipper Colin Hendry,
before running on and firing it past goalkeeper Neil Sullivan. Scottish manager
Craig Brown had gambled on playing the talismanic Hendry despite his lack of
first team appearances at Rangers this season -- but another bad defensive
error allowed Scholes to make it 2-0 after 42 minutes. His Manchester United
team mate David Beckham whipped in a well-struck free-kick almost from the
touchline wide on the right, and the Scottish defence allowed the unmarked
Scholes a free header which powered past Sullivan into the net. It was his
ninth goal in his 19th appearance. </FONT></P>

second half and the once-mighty Hampden &quot;roar&quot; became barely a
whisper. The Arsenal centre-back partnership of Tony Adams and Martin Keown,
along with Campbell, playing in an unfamiliar right-back position, were all
impressive as Scotland were comfortably held at bay after the break. While
never a classic, the match was certainly an occasion to remember. </FONT></P>

Scotland 2-0 in Euro'96. Scotland have now failed to score against England in
their last five meetings and have still not beaten their old enemy since a 1-0
win here in 1985. The record now reads England 45 wins, Scotland 40 with 24
matches drawn. Even if Scotland improve on those figures and win at Wembley on
Wednesday they will have to score their biggest win there since a 3-1 victory
50 years ago to advance to the finals. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">A sturdy but unimpressive Czech Republic held
the Netherlands to a 1-1 draw at the Philips Stadium on Saturday despite some
late all-out attacking from the Dutch. The draw means Dutch coach Frank
Rijkaard has now gone 11 matches without a victory. A second half goal from
Jaap Stam put the hosts ahead when the Manchester United defender converted a
Ronald de Boer free kick just before the hour. But less than 10 minutes later
the Czechs pulled level when Benfica's Karel Poborsky pushed the ball through
to towering Anderlecht striker Jan Koller, who knocked it easily passed
goalkeeper Edwin van der Sar. The Dutch had at least three good chances to sew
matters up in the last five minutes, but failed to find the net. </FONT> </P>

several changes for the warm up game in Holland on Saturday, replacing
Liverpool midfielders Patrick Berger and Vladimir Smicer with Slavia Prague's
Ivo Ulich and Martin Cizek of TSV 1860 Munich. Smicer will miss the match in
Eindhoven because of a hamstring injury and Berger has flu. Also out are
Teplice striker Pavel Verbir who has a broken toe and Tennis Borussia Berlin
sweeper Jan Suchoparek who has back problems. Pavel Kuka of VfB Stuttgart,
himself recovering from an injured knee, and Slavia Prague's Pavel Vlcek will
replace them in the squad. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The Czech Republic completed a perfect European
Championships qualifying campaign on Saturday with a 2-0 win at home over the
Faroe Islands. In steady rain at Prague's Letna Stadium, Anderlecht's lanky
striker Jan Koller opened the scoring in the 11th minute, picking up a loose
ball just inside the box and hitting a skidding shot in off the left post.
Despite a spirited performance by the visitors and having midfielder Patrik
Berger sent off for a flagrant foul in the second half, Pavel Verbir's header
off a Karel Poborsky cross sealed the win in the closing minutes. The win meant
the Czechs, who were already the first side to clinch a spot in next year's
European Championship by sewing up group nine in June, ended with an
unprecedented 30 points having won all 10 of their matches. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000"><FONT COLOR="#0000A0"><B>Oct 5
- Scotland 1 Bosnia 0</B></FONT></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000">John Collins fired Scotland
into the European championship play-offs with a clinical penalty in a 1-0 win
over Bosnia on Tuesday. Although Scotland had two games -- they face Lithuania
in Glasgow on Saturday -- to secure the three points required to reach the
play-offs, they rarely played with any fluency. Indeed, Bosnian striker Elvir
Baljic, came closest to breaking the deadlock until David Hopkin earned the
penalty when he tumbled under a challenge from Bosnian defender Sergej
Barbarez. Collins showed typical coolness from the spot as he slotted a left
foot kick past debutant goalkeeper Adnan Guso. Hopkin almost added a second
goal in the 76th minute with a low shot but Baljic gave Scotland a fright just
six minutes from the end with a 20-metre shot that crashed off the bar. </FONT>
</P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000"><FONT COLOR="#0000A0"><B>Oct 9
- Estonia 1 Bosnia 4</B></FONT></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000"> Estonian striker Andres Oper,
from Danish side Aalborg, gave his team a fourth-minute lead, running on to a
ball kicked from the halfway mark and blasting it past the keeper from inside
the box. But Baljic answered in the 42nd minute with a direct free kick from
just outside the left-hand corner of the box that took goalkeeper Martin Kaalma
by surprise, sneaking just over the line at the near post. The second half
belonged entirely to the Bosnian striker, as repeated good saves by Kaalma
failed to keep Baljic from three more goals. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000"><FONT COLOR="#0000A0"><B>Oct 9
- Scotland 3 Lithuania 0</B></FONT></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial" COLOR="#000000">Hearts striker McSwegan, who
was partnered by Celtic teenager Mark Burchill in an experimental move, staked
his claim for a permanent place with a fine goal in the 50th minute which
effectively killed off the game. McSwegan moved onto a fine pass from Burchill,
who had outpaced the Lithuanian defence, and fired a left foot shot past
goalkeeper Pavel Leus from 12 metres. Burchill, who made his debut last Tuesday
against Bosnia as a substitute, ought to have opened his account for Scotland
in the 48th but the 19-year-old Celtic player's penalty struck the post and it
was Don Hutchison who finished off the rebound to put the hosts 1-0 up.
Scotland's third goal came a minute from the end when substitute Colin Cameron
fired in a right-foot volley. </FONT></P>
