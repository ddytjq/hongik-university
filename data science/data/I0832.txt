Vidmar turns down Bluebirds
10 Jul<br>
			<SPAN Class='gentext' ><P>The Australian international Tony Vidmar has decided not to join Cardiff City.</P><P>The 33-year-old-defender was released by Rangers at the end of last season after a five-year spell at Ibrox.</P><P>"He seemed very keen and he had a good look around Cardiff," Bluebirds boss Lennie Lawrence told the club's official website.</P><P>"His was a professional decision, it wasn't about money, the area or the potential. I'm still trying to attract the same type of player as I would do if we had got promoted.</P><P>"Sometimes I will succeed and sometimes I won't. I didn't with Tony Vidmar because he feels that the gap between the Scottish Premier League and the second division is too great for him to take at this particular time in his life and I respect and understand that"</P><P>Vidmar, who has won 48 caps for his country, moved to Rangers from Dutch side NAC Breda in 1997 and went on to start over 100 games for the Glasgow giants.</P>
