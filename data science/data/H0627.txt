href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4>Interpretations<a name="Interp"></a></h4>		<ul type="circle">			<li>The nucleotide sequence is the map of the <a href="MG112.html">molecular chromosome</a> with the highest possible resolution.			<li><a href="http://www.genome.ou.edu/SeqStrategy.html">Determination</a> of the entire nucleotide sequences of free-living organisms is expensive and is usually conducted by large sequencing centers.			<li>The availability of the complete nucleotide sequence of an organism provides a ready source of <a href="../MGW4/MG425.html">probes</a> for correlation of <a href="MG113.html">morphological</a> or <a href="MG111.html">genetic</a> chromosomes with the molecular chromosome.			<li>The sequences also contain all the information necessary to specify the complete organism.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a><br>			</p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Info"></a>Further information</h4>		<ul type="square">			<li>Up-to-date <a href="http://www-fp.mcs.anl.gov/~gaasterland/genomes.html">lists</a> of completed and on-going genome projects are available.			<li>In September 2000, there were over 900 <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/Entrez/Genome/main_genomes.html">genomes</a> finished or being sequenced. in the databases. Most were viruses (557), but over 100 were bacteria, 13 were archaea and 157 were eukaryotes.			<li>&quot;Completely&quot; sequenced often means &quot;mostly&quot; sequenced. Highly repetitive sequence regions, which are difficult to sort through are often omitted. The degree to which &quot;completed&quot; sequences are annotated also varies with the organism.			<li>Comparison of genome sequences reveals considerable <a href="MG124.html">synteny</a>.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a name="bottom"></a><a href="MG11232.html">Last</a> | <a href="MG1129.html">Vocabulary</a> | <a href="MG1123.html">Overview</a> | <a href="#Facts">Top</a> | <a href="MG113.html">Next</a><br>			<br>			</p>		</center>		<p>		<hr>		<img height="72" width="108" src="divlogo2.gif" align="right"></p>		<p>This is page 11233 of <a href="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</a> by <a href="http://opbs.okstate.edu/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</a>, &copy; 1997, 1998, 2000-1<br>		<hr>		</p>		<address>E-mail inquiries to <a href="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu">U.
