<h1>Gordon recovers from emergency stop, defends at Dover</h1>

DOVER, Del. -- Jeff Gordon is the defending Winston Cup champion, but the 24-year-old is still learning a few things.
<p>
Gordon won his fourth NASCAR race of the year by running away with the 500-lap Miller 500 on Sunday at Dover Downs International Speedway, although halfway through the race an extra pit stop forced Gordon to use a new weapon -- patience.
<p>
Gordon was strong early and late in the race, but handling
problems dropped him as far back as 14th forcing the 24-year-old to fight his way back to the front. He did fight back, winning by about four
seconds over Hendrick Racing teammate Terry Labonte.
<p>
&quot;I wasn't screaming today,&quot; said Gordon, who admits he
sometimes gets too excited when he gets behind in a race. &quot;I was
very patient.&quot;
<p>
Gordon led 172 laps before losing the lead on lap 185 and going
into the pits for a standard tire change and fuel stop. 
However, the car didn't handle right and Gordon was forced back
into the pits only 10 laps later, coming in during one of the
race's four caution periods. 
<p>
&quot;The car was perfect after that stop,&quot; said Gordon, who won the
fall race at Dover last year and captured Sunday's 500 with an average speed of 122.741 mph.
<p>
The stop dropped Gordon back to 14th, and he would fight hard
 -- but patiently -- for about 60 laps to emerge back among the front runners.  Gordon found enough room on the inside of Earnhardt to take the
lead at about lap 370. 
<p>
&quot;I went out there and just tried to keep the car clean and
tried to get back up to the front,&quot; he said. &quot;I just didn't want
to get caught up in somebody else's wreck or cause a wreck by being
too impatient.&quot;
<p>
Gordon was followed by Labonte and Dale Earnhardt, giving Chevrolet Monte Carlos a sweep of the top three spots. Fourth-place Ernie Irvan had the top finishing Ford Thunderbird, and was the only other car on the lead lap.
<p>
Gordon made it a rout in the last 100 laps, running lower on the
high-banked concrete track than the rest of the contenders. It
became obvious that only a late caution could restore the
competitive nature of the event.
<p>
	   It never happened, and Gordon's pit stop on lap 444 was two
seconds faster than that of Earnhardt. The stop cost Earnhardt
second place, with Labonte moving ahead of him.
<p>
&quot;Once were were inside 10 laps to go, I thought we were pretty
good,&quot; Gordon said. &quot;But you never know in this business. In 
NASCAR racing, anything can happen but luckily it went all the way.
We were hoping that it would.&quot;
<p>
Earnhardt is winless since March 10. &quot;We did all we could, but it wasn't good enough to win today,&quot; said Earnhardt, winless since March 10. &quot;That'another top five finish, and that's seven straight ... but we need a win.&quot;
<p>
For winning from the pole position, where he qualified with a track record, Gordon will add the $38,000
Unocal Bonus to his winnings. Gordon took home a total purse
of $138,730.
<p>
Dale Jarrett, the winner of last weekend's Coca-Cola 600, had a
tremendous race going, leading 83 laps, until a spinout with about 125
laps to go ruined his chances at victory. 
<p>
In the Winston Cup points standings, Earnhardt, Labonte and
Gordon will be the top three with only 200 points separating the
three. 
<p>
The Miller 500 was the 12th race in the 31-race Winston Cup
series.  The next NASCAR event is the UAW-GM Teamwork 500 on June 16 at
Pocono Speedway in Pennsylvania.
<p>
