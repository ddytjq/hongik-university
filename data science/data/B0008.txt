      <p><b><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">IMPORTANT NOTES</font></b></p>
      </font></u> 
      <p> <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Interest rates and 
        account terms are variable unless special conditions, described in the 
        Terms and Conditions of those accounts, say otherwise.</font></p>
      <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>AER (Annual Equivalent 
        Rate)<br>
        </b></font><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><br>
        AER stands for Annual Equivalent Rate. It illustrates what the gross interest 
        would be if interest was paid and added to the account each year. All 
        advertisements for savings products will contain an AER so you can compare 
        more easily what return you can expect from your savings over time.<br>
        <br>
        The gross rate is the contractual rate of interest payable before the 
        deduction of income tax specified by law.</font></p>
      <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Income tax </b><br>
        <br>
        Interest will be paid net (this means after we have deducted income tax, 
        currently 20%) unless you have completed an appropriate form to comply 
        with Inland Revenue regulations. The net rates shown in this leaflet are 
        examples only and have been rounded. They assume income tax of 20% has 
        been deducted.</font></p>
      <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">If you are a non-taxpayer 
        or, if your total taxable income is only just more than the starting point 
        for paying tax, you may be able to claim a repayment of the tax deducted 
        from the Inland Revenue.</font></p>
      <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">If you pay income 
        tax at the basic rate, you will have no more tax to pay on your interest. 
        If you are a higher rate taxpayer you will have to pay additional tax 
        on the interest to cover the difference between the tax deducted and the 
        higher rate due.</font></p>
      <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The tax position affecting 
        our accounts is subject to changes in the law.<br>
        <br>
        <b>Payment of interest </b><br>
        <br>
        The interest is payable at the following times.</font></p>
