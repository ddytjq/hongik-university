          comes to delivering the key consumer markets to a sponsor</font></b></td>

    </table>
    </font>
    <font FACE="Arial" SIZE="2">
      <p align="left">
      CART's lineup of races for 2003 is shaping up to be its best schedule  to date.&nbsp; One by one CART is trashing the races with low 
      attendance, and replacing them with absolute winners - races that not only 
      deliver a large attendance, but ones that are conducive to wooing 
      sponsors.<p align="left">
      It's no secret that racing is expensive.&nbsp; It costs a lot of money to 
      run a race team, and sponsorship is the lifeblood of the sport.&nbsp; CART 
      and its teams have to fight NASCAR, IRL, F1 and ALMS for its share of 
      sponsor dollars.&nbsp; The consumer markets that a race series can deliver 
      is important when trying to land the next big deal.&nbsp; Sponsors like to 
      use races as a backdrop to entertain its most important employees and 
      customers.&nbsp;They also like to go into a market before and during an 
      event and use the race as part of their marketing strategy. In that regard, CART is #1 in the world.<p align="left">No other race series in the world can deliver to a 
    sponsor, the value CART can bring.&nbsp; Not only does CART deliver the 
    powerful #1 consumer market in the world, the NAFTA market (USA, Canada and Mexico), it also delivers Europe 
    (Germany and UK), the Pacific Rim (Japan, Australia and eventually China) 
    and is poised for a possible return to Brazil and the South American market.&nbsp; 
    Certainly F1 brings almost the same global reach, but the cost to enter F1 
    and participate as a sponsor is astronomical.<p align="left">In today's 
    global economy there are not too many sports that can deliver a sponsor the 
    multitude of markets CART and F1 can.&nbsp; Most sports, and even the IRL 
    and NASCAR, are strictly domestic products that appeal to a single domestic 
    audience.&nbsp; Because CART can deliver the same worldwide reach that F1 
    can, but at a bargain price, it's just a matter of time before sponsors 
    stand up and take notice.&nbsp; Some already are.&nbsp; However, CART shot 
    itself in the foot for 2002 by not having its International TV package in 
    place by the start of the season.&nbsp; Now they are scrambling to get it 
    done by the 2nd race in Long Beach, and even then, it won't be anywhere near 
    as far reaching as it needs to eventually be.<p align="left" style="margin-bottom: 12">
    With that said, CART is on the right track toward building a solid 22 race 
    schedule in the coming years.&nbsp;
