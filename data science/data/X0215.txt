<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>The weekend was a blast.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>just on short note now:</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>it started bad: Christophe
was hit by a boom and had to be brought to the hospital. Luckilly he came
back after an hour, but the frenchies decided to go home after that.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>wind was very variable.
saturday it was pretty weak. scores were not so high: Manuel 159, Uwe 158,
Tib 136.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>lot of peeps got a sunburn.
(me included). We were pretty much surprised by newcomer Brigitt. She a
very strong throw. and after lending her a boom, she cranked out 65 m ok.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>on the second day the wind
picked up. for me it was too strong, but others like Markus and Ingo could
really lean into it.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>both made a very nice throw
in the final round, just barely returning, perfect throws.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>Manuel was testing a lot,
not wanting to loose too many booms (yea, there were high grass areas all
around the field). After almost loosing his world record boom, he decided
to try other, slightly inferior booms: well in the final round he got a
nice sequence of 170/180/190m flights. impressive, as usual.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>Since we were so hyped by
Birgitts performance on saturday, we knew that the old womens world record
was in danger. I added some weight on the carbon fiber boom I gave her
(aha, add more weight) and Markus and Uwe sure helped also coaching her.</font></font>
<br><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font size=-1>she had a 86 m throw and
we were partying. then she even bettered it her score to 88m. Way to go!</font></font><img SRC="muhl1.jpg" height=640 width=480>
