				<font class="news">A hot topic for discussion of late has been the non-inclusion of Sarandeep Singh in the Indian team for the Ahmedabad Test. On a pitch conducive to spin and against opponents who were beaten black and blue in the First Test at Mohali, it is felt that selecting a third spin bowler would have been the more positive move.<br><br>Picking seven batsman &#8211; including Dasgupta who qualifies as a batsman following his hundred at Mohali &#8211; is a rather defensive move and it has been argued as something uncalled for against this side, which is being dismissed as among the weakest English teams ever to tour India.<br><br>The six batsman-four bowler policy is considered handy against opponents like Australia and South Africa while against England at home, it is felt that the five batsman-five bowler policy would yield better dividends.<br><br>That said, it must be admitted that the selectors can hardly be blamed for their line of thinking. In the first place, a third spinner can be played only at the expense of a batsman and which of the batsmen can be dropped?<br><br>The four in the middle order &#8211; Rahul Dravid, Sachin Tendulkar, Sourav Ganguly and Venkatsai Laxman &#8211; have an aura of permanency about them. Virendra Shewag, after missing two games, one unofficial and one official, had to return, particularly after his hundred on debut in South Africa.<br><br>Shiv Sundar Das is the one specialist opening batsman who is performing reasonably well and Deep Dasgupta picked himself after his century at Mohali, if not exactly on his showing behind the stumps.<br><br><br>That left only the four bowling slots to be filled and the selectors picked the best quartet for the job in Javagal Srinath, Tinu Yohannan, Harbhajan Singh and Anil Kumble. Indeed, for all practical purposes there were only four bowlers who did the job successfully in Mohali, with Sanjay Bangar and Iqbal Siddiqui counting together as one on two counts &#8211; the restricted opportunities they got and the limited promise they showed.<br><br>When India suddenly ran into deep trouble in the first innings of the Ahmedabad Test, one was thankful that the side had depth in the batting and indeed this did play a role in the home team recovering from the mire of 93-4 to their final total of 291. On the other hand it was argued, quite justifiably, that had India fielded five bowlers, England in all probability would not have got 407. <br><br>It must be admitted here that Sarandeep has been a rather unlucky bowler.<br><br>He had a successful Test debut against Zimbabwe a year ago, picking up six wickets. Like Harbhajan Singh, he too is an attacking off spinner and Erapalli Prasanna, who knows a thing or two about this mode of bowling, never hesitates to sing Sarandeep&#8217;s praises.<br><br>In racing parlance, Harbhajan won the off spinner&#8217;s slot against Australia last season by the proverbial short head and the rest, as the cliché goes, is history.<br><br>To Sarandeep&#8217;s credit, he has never lost heart and the selectors have shown that they hold him in high regard by including him in the squad for the Tests against England.<br><br>Kumble&#8217;s return, along with Harbhajan&#8217;s awesome record against Australia, has blocked his path for a comeback. But he has stayed in the spotlight, thanks also to his five-wicket haul against the Englishmen while playing for the Board President&#8217;s XI last month. <br><br>At the moment, unfortunate as it may seem, Sarandeep can get in only when a third spinner is played. But his presence has certainly kept Harbhajan on his toes.<br><br>As it is, midway through the current series, there can be little doubt that Kumble has reclaimed the number one spinner&#8217;s slot which the off spinner hijacked during Kumble&#8217;s year long absence, chiefly because of his mesmeric exploits against the Aussies.<br><br>If Harbhajan is not able to maintain his hard won reputation, he may find himself displaced as the second spinner by Sarandeep. In the meantime, Sarandeep can only hope that the selectors decide to play three spin bowlers in the final Test at Bangalore.<br><br><i>- Partab Ramchand</i><br><br><b>COMMENT</b><br>Do you agree with Partab's comments?
