				<p class="indent20"><font class="normaltour"><script language="javascript">writeToursMenu (165)</script><b>England won the toss and elected to bat first in a rain-delayed 39-over game. They scored 218-7 from their 39 overs. India scored 219-2 in 29.4 overs</b><br>Teams:<br>India: Ganguly (capt), Tendulkar, Mongia, Kaif, Sehwag, Badani, Ratra, Kumble, H Singh, Agarkar, Srinath<br>England: Hussain (capt), Trescothick, Knight, Flintoff, Thorpe, Vaughan, Collingwood, Hollioake, Snape, Gough, Hoggard<br><br>A patient crowd eventually saw play get underway, albeit over two hours later than anticipated due to heavy rain. The crowd were nonetheless in typically buoyant spirit, eager to see if India could maintain the good form they showed in Chennai.<br><br>The hosts were without VVS Laxman, who was dropped in favor of Mohammad Kaif, who was making his ODI debut. James Foster was missing from the England side because of illness, so Graham Thorpe was brought back in an otherwise unchanged side.<br><br><b>England innings</b><br>On a slow pitch that looked to favor the batsman early on, Hussain won the toss and elected to bat. With each innings being cut back to 39 overs because of the late start, the emphasis was on quick runs and England duly obliged with Nick Knight and Marcus Trescothick fashioning a blistering start. <br><br>With the run-rate ticking over at seven runs an over and the sweep stroke being used to good effect, the Indian seamers struggled to contain a batting attack that was brimming with self-confidence. <br><br>Ajit Agarkar was unable to recapture the sort of form that he enjoyed in Chennai &#8211; in one over the paceman conceded 18 runs at the hands of an in-form Trescothick. <br><br>India made the first breakthrough, claiming the scalp of Trescothick on 18. The England batsman mistimed a Kumble delivery, sending it high into the air for Mongia to take a straightforward catch. The wicket didn&#8217;t seem to stem the tourists&#8217; flow, as new batsman Andrew Flintoff continued to hit out with some exuberant stoke-play.<br><br>England brought up their 100 with a massive six from Andrew Flintoff, who hit Kumble over his head into the stand. The century was reached in just under 15 overs as England sought to maintain the pressure that was beginning to frustrate the hosts. Nick Knight made his 50 to move England onto 103-1.<br><br>Despite the steady progress of the tourists, India&#8217;s bowlers hinted at an imminent breakthrough with England&#8217;s batting becoming increasingly haphazard. Flintoff&#8217;s attacking instinct eventually got the better of him, launching a Kumble delivery skywards for Tendulkar to claim. <br><br>Harbhajan Singh then began to make his mark and capitalize on some loose batting from England. The youngster claimed the wicket of Knight for 74, then restricted the tourists further with some incisive bowling and the wicket of skipper Nassar Hussain.<br><br>Hussain mistimed an exquisite slower ball which lofted into the hands of the bowler. Hussain was caught and bowled for only 15 and England were beginning to wobble.<br><br>Up stepped Ganguly to devastating effect. With runs of only four and six respectively on the board, Vaughan and Collingwood both fell foul to a spell of inspired bowling by the Indian skipper.<br><br>Despite some positive work from Thorpe on his return to the international arena, England were unable to forge any meaningful partnerships. Hollioake was the next to depart from the field after scoring just 13, caught by Sehwag after a slower ball from Javagal Srinath lured the all-rounder into the shot.<br><br>Despite the middle-order collapse, England finished on 218-7 with Thorpe left on 36 not out.<br><br>India need 219 to win off 39 overs.<br><br><br><b>India innings</b><br>England started the brighter of the two sides with Darren Gough and Matthew Hoggard looking lively in the opening overs. Gough appeared to have made an immediate breakthrough with his first delivery to Sachin Tendulkar, the ball appeared to nick the Little Master&#8217;s bat, but the umpire deemed otherwise.<br><br>Virender Sehwag had been promoted up the order in place of the injured Ganguly. The Indian skipper had limped out of the action during the 33rd over of England innings after suffering a recurrence of a hamstring injury. <br><br>This was soon followed by a frenzied lbw appeal by Hoggard against Sehwag. Television replays suggested that the tourists had a case for the dismissal, but once again the umpire held firm and rejected England&#8217;s shouts.<br><br>Stand-in wicketkeeper Trescothick also missed a crucial catch when Sehwag edged a Hollioake delivery when he was on 33.<br><br>Despite these scares, India very quickly began to dictate the play. With Virender Sehwag in exceptional form &#8211; even outscoring his more illustrious teammate - the hosts always threatened to dwarf England&#8217;s modest total.<br><br>Sehwag raced to his fifth one-day 50 off just 36 balls, before he was dismissed by Collingwood for 82. It was an inspired knock off just 62 balls and helped India reach 149-1 after just 20 overs.<br><br>Tendulkar was in an equally unforgiving mood and reached the personal milestone of 11,000 runs in one-day cricket. <br><br>The Indian run-rate was relentless, and when Ganguly was bowled by Gough on 26, Mongia merely continued where his teammate left off with some more stylish strokeplay.<br><br>The scoring spree left India needing just over three runs per over in the closing stages and Tendulkar claimed victory in emphatic fashion, hitting an outrageous six down the ground to go 3-1 up in the series and finish on 87 not out.<br><br><b>India finished on 219-2, reaching their victory target of 218 in just 29.4 overs.</b></font></p>
