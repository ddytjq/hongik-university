<h2>Professional experience</h2>

<h3><b>Part-time Consultant,</b> Dec. 1999 - present</h3>

<ul>
    <li><i>Analog VLSI design</i> related to image sensors.</li>
</ul>

<h3><b>Post-Doctoral Scholar,</b> Jan. 1999 - present</h3>

<p><a href="http://www.caltech.edu">California Institute of
Technology</a>, Pasadena, CA</p>

<ul>
    <li><i>Design of a MEMS gyroscope</i> intended to be combined
        with a visual sensor for flight control of tiny air
        vehicles. Designed to be fabricated by a new surface
        micromachining technology under development at the
        Caltech Micromachining Lab (Prof. Y.C.&nbsp;Tai), using
        parylene as a mechanical material.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
    <li><em>Design of a visual sensor</em> exploiting movements
        and vibrations for high-resolution image sensing and
        noise rejection.</li>
</ul>

<h3><b>Project Manager,</b> Dec. 1996 &#150; Dec. 1998</h3>

<p><a href="http://www.csem.ch">CSEM SA</a>, Advanced
Microelectronics Division, Neuchâtel, Switzerland</p>

<ul>
    <li><i>Head of a research project</i> resulting in the
        successful development of a system emulating the
        functionality of a moving eye. Combination of optical
        devices, mechanical components, analog VLSI image sensors
        and analog VLSI control chips. Own technical
        contributions: system design and simulation, design of
        the analog control chips. Project started in Jan. 1995,
        total budget&nbsp;1.1M$. </li>
</ul>

<ul>
    <li><i>Deputy head</i> of a research section of 11 design
        engineers and scientists (annual budget 2M$), with
        contributions to research planning, budgeting, human
        resources planning, and financial follow-up.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
    <li><i>Completion of a Ph.D. thesis</i> in parallel to
        regular employment. The thesis describes a non-linear
        control function synthesis technique, and incorporates
        research results of the moving eye project mentioned
        above.</li>
</ul>

<h3><b>R&amp;D Engineer, </b>Mar. 1992 &#150; Nov. 1996</h3>

<p><a href="http://www.csem.ch">CSEM SA</a>, Integrated Circuits
&amp; Systems Division, Neuchâtel, Switzerland</p>

<ul>

<ul>
