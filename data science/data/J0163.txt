<h1>That's excitement: Gordon takes NASCAR Pontiac 400</h1>
<hr>RICHMOND, Va. -- Defending Winston Cup champion Jeff Gordon, who opened the year with back-to-back last place finishes, put it all together Sunday to win the NASCAR Pontiac Excitement 400 at Richmond International Raceway.<p>
Gordon was a contender throughout, pulling into the lead before an accident by Darrell Waltrip on lap 358 began a series of cautions. Gordon kept his Chevrolet Monte Carlo ahead of a bunch of Fords during three race restarts to take the victory.<p>
&quot;It's been a tough couple of races,&quot; said Gordon, a winner for the 10th time in his five-year Winston Cup career. &quot;We needed this one so bad. It's a day we've been looking for a while.&quot;<p>
Gordon was involved in a race-ending crash on the ninth lap of the Daytona 500 and was forced from last week's Goodwrench 400 with mechanical problems. Last year, Gordon set the qualifying record at Richmond with 124.757 mph. <p>
Dale Jarrett, who seemed to inherit the luck that helped Gordon to the title last year, brought his Ford Thunderbird home second for the second consecutive week. Roush Racing teammates Ted Musgrave, Jeff Burton and Mark Martin rounded out the top five.<p>
&quot;This was awesome today,&quot; said Jarrett, who should own an 86-point lead in the Winston Cup standings to be officially released Monday. &quot;We've had a chance to win every race this year. Everything is going well and we have to see if we can keep it going.&quot; <p>
Rusty Wallace finished seventh, only the third time in the last 28 races on short tracks that he has finished outside of the top five. Dale Earnhardt, who entered the day tied atop the points standings with Jarrett, finished 31st. <p>
Bobby Hamilton, who was leading the Goodwrench 400 before getting tapped by Earnhardt and knocked out of the race, was involved in a bizarre post-race incident with John Andretti. <p>
Andretti rammed his Ford Thunderbird into the rear of Hamilton's Pontiac Grand Prix after the race in apparent retaliation for a bump he received from Hamilton near the end of the race. <p>
When Hamilton was hit by Andretti, he braked and the two drivers slid into the infield retaining wall. Andretti drove away, but Hamilton acknowledged that the drivers had their on-track differences last year. <p>
The Pontiac Excitement 400 opened NASCAR's short-track season--races run at tracks which measure less than one mile. The track at Richmond is a ¾-mile oval. <p>
This was the third race in the 31-event NASCAR Winston Cup series. The Purolator 500 on March 10 in Atlanta is the next event.<p><hr>
