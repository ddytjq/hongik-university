<HR WIDTH="100%" SIZE=7 ALIGN=left COLOR="0000ff"><B><I><FONT SIZE=4 COLOR="000080" FACE="Arial">About the Ocean Studies Board</FONT></I></B><BR>
<HR WIDTH="100%" SIZE=7 ALIGN=left COLOR="0000ff"><BR>
<B><FONT SIZE=4 COLOR="000080" FACE="Arial">Statement of Purpose</FONT></B>
<TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0>
<TR VALIGN=top><TD WIDTH="79%"><FONT SIZE=2 COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">The Ocean Studies Board was established by the National Research Council to advise the federal government on issues of ocean science, engineering, and policy. In addition to exercising leadership within the ocean community, the Board undertakes studies at the request of federal agencies, Congress, or other </FONT><A HREF="/dels/osb.nsf/44bf87db309563a0852566f2006d63bb/89dad4873e1f48888525690b0052ed57?OpenDocument"><B><FONT SIZE=2 COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">sponsors</FONT></B></A><FONT SIZE=2 COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">, or upon its own initiative. The Board explores the science, policies, and infrastructure needed to understand and protect coastal and marine environments and resources.</FONT></TD><TD WIDTH="21%"><CENTER><A HREF="/dels/osb.nsf/files/Challenges.pdf/$file/Challenges.pdf"><IMG SRC="/dels/osb.nsf/c4b1b2f55494b57b852566f30061cc40/3e61a67fa3bc5fda8525684f0075e40c/Content/0.986?OpenElement&FieldElemFormat=gif" WIDTH=94 HEIGHT=117 ALT="Challenges in Ocean Policy, an Ocean Studies Board brochure"></A><BR>
<BR>
<A HREF="/dels/osb.nsf/files/Challenges.pdf/$file/Challenges.pdf"><B><FONT SIZE=2 COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">Challenges in Ocean Policy</FONT></B></A></CENTER></TD></TR>
</TABLE>
<BR>
<FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">In recent years, the Board has conducted studies in the following areas:</FONT><BR>
<UL><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">the status of marine and coastal environments</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">the ocean's role in the global climate system</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">technology and infrastructure needs</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">ocean-related aspects of national security</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">fisheries science, management, and policy</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">living and nonliving marine resources</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">ocean education</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">reviews of specific agency programs, and</FONT><LI><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">the future of the discipline in the United States and abroad</FONT></UL><BR>
<FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">The wide-ranging expertise of the Board members reflects this broad agenda.</FONT><BR>
<BR>
<B><FONT SIZE=4 COLOR="000080" FACE="Arial">Operating Principles</FONT></B><BR>
<HR WIDTH="100%" SIZE=7 ALIGN=left COLOR="000080"><BR>
<B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">The Board provides an open forum for those interested in ocean issues to bring technical and policy concerns for discussion and possible action. A primary responsibility of the Board is to initiate studies and ensure that they are carried out successfully. As appropriate, studies can be developed and overseen jointly with other NRC Boards. In selecting projects, the Board attempts to be responsive to the requests of sponsors while also engaging in proactive activities related to ocean sciences, engineering, and policy.</FONT></B><BR>
<BR>
<B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">The Board meets three times a year. Activities at these meetings include:</FONT></B><BR>
<UL>1. <B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">presenting the results of recently completed studies,</FONT></B><BR>
2. <B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">reviewing the progress of studies underway,</FONT></B><BR>
3. <B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">considering new studies for initiation, and </FONT></B><BR>
4. <B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">sharing information about subjects of importance to the ocean community.</FONT></B></UL><BR>
<B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">Individual studies are carried out under the Board's oversight within a definite time frame and budget as agreed with the sponsor(s). Typically, a committee of 8 to 15 members, with a diverse range of expertise and perspectives, is convened to address a particular question or set of questions. All committee members serve pro-bono and are screened to avoid any potential conflicts of interest. Before the committee meets, one of the volunteer experts is appointed to serve as the committee chair, and a member of the OSB staff is assigned as the study director. The chair and study director work closely together to ensure a successful process and final product.</FONT></B><BR>
<BR>
<B><FONT COLOR="800000" FACE="Arial">Each committee meets periodically to investigate the many facets of the problem described in its statement of task, to hear from outsiders, to deliberate, and to develop a report of its findings, conclusions, and recommendations, based on the available evidence. A draft report is then subjected to intensive review by a group of independent, anonymous experts. After the revisions are made by the committee, the report must be officially approved by the institution. The report is then transmitted to the agency or organization that sponsored the study, and released to the public. The goal of our process is to produce advice that is authoritative, objective, and consensus-based.</FONT></B><BR>
