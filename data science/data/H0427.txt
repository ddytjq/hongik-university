        <p align="center"><font face="Comic Sans MS" color="#0000FF" size="5">Introduction:</font><p align="center"><font face="Comic Sans MS" color="#0000FF" size="5">&quot;Perl
        and it's Application to Proteomics&quot;</font>
        <p><font face="Comic Sans MS" color="#0000FF" size="3">If you have the
        mass spec. protein database searching tool SEQUEST provided by Thermo
        Finnigan Corporation then you already have Perl
        installed on your PC, that means that you can start playing
        today.&nbsp; Don't know what Perl is, then find
        out with an introductory article at &quot;The Perl
        Journal&quot;.&nbsp; Starting to get interested, find out &quot;<a href="http://bio.perl.org/GetStarted/tpj_ls_bio.html" target="_blank">How
        Perl Saved the Human Genome Project</a>&quot; with an article by Lincoln
        Stein at the
        BioPerl Project.&nbsp; If you want to get Perl go to the <a href="http://www.perl.com/CPAN-local/README.html">CPAN</a>&nbsp;
        or <a href="http://www.activestate.com/" target="_blank">Active State</a> sites, then
        you can write and run scripts to do almost anything you want.&nbsp; The
        O'Riley series of books on Perl are about the best books on the
        subject.&nbsp; For additional references take a look at;&nbsp; Learning Perl, by Schwartz and
        Christiansen Programming Perl, by Wall, Schwartz and Christiansen Perl
        Cookbook, by Christiansen and Torkington.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Comic Sans MS" color="#0000FF" size="3">I am currently using the Perl programming language
        in my proteomics work to automate the tasks of data
        entry, data base searching, and&nbsp; data reduction.&nbsp;
        This new routine can take thousands of mass spectra search them against
        one or several databases and makes sense out of what would otherwise be chaos.&nbsp; To
        learn more about this particular Perl program or if you think that you
        have an interesting project that we could collaborate on send me an e-mail
        at <a href="mailto:mattsweeney@earthlink.net">mattsweeney@earthlink.net</a>.&nbsp;</font><p><font face="Comic Sans MS" color="#0000FF" size="3">To
        learn more about the power of the Perl programming language check out
        the links below.&nbsp;</font>
