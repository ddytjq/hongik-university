<H1>
Popular Cosmology Books
</H1>

<UL>
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0763700304/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Origin and Evolution of the Universe"</A> edited by Zuckerman and
Malkan.  A UCLA Center for the Study of the Evolution and Origin of Life
(CSEOL) Symposium.  1996, Jones and Bartlett Publishers.  I wrote the 
leadoff article in this collection.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/046501576X/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Very First Light"</A>, by John Mather and John Boslough, 1996.
BasicBooks.  The true history of the COBE project written by the Project
Scientist.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0935702407/nedwrightscosmol">
"Afterglow of Creation"</A> by Marcus Chown.  Good science journalism.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0684854228/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Five Ages of the Universe : Inside the Physics of Eternity"</A>
by Fred Adams &amp; Greg Laughlin.   -- The very distant future of our 
Universe.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0521629756/nedwrightscosmol">
"Our Evolving Universe"</A> by Malcolm Longair.  -- A book with
beautiful pictures and a good non-technical introduction to cosmology.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0674242424/nedwrightscosmol">
"Einstein's Greatest Blunder? : The Cosmological Constant and Other
Fudge Factors in the Physics of the Universe"</A> by Donald Goldsmith.
I haven't read 
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0738200689/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Runaway Universe : The Race to Discover the Future of the Cosmos"</A>
by the same author, but it covers the accelerating expansion
seen in the recent supernova data.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0465037402/nedwrightscosmol">
"Quintessence : The Mystery of the Missing Mass in the Universe"</A> by 
Lawrence Krauss.  An update of "The Fifth Essence".  Dark Matter.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0465053149/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Origin of the Universe"</A> by John D. Barrow.  Covers a wide range of
topics: inflation, including the chaotic and perpetual versions,
wormholes and space-time foam, the anthropic principle, with nice
illustrations.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0521423031/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Origins of our Universe"</A> by Malcolm Longair.  A small book based
on the Christmas lectures at the Royal Society.  Out of print.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0738200336/nedwrightscosmol">
"Before the Beginning : Our Universe and Others"</A> by
Martin Rees.  There are no equations, but also no graphs, figures or
illustrations of any kind.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0716745038/nedwrightscosmol">
"Ripples in the Cosmos"</A> by Michael Rowan-Robinson.   Has the same
erroneous <A HREF="./tvtmrrs2.html">T vs t</A> graph as "Shadows of
Creation".  Out of print.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0716721570/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Shadows of Creation"</A>, by Michael Riordan and David Schramm.
Dark matter.   Has the same            
erroneous <A HREF="./tvtmrrs2.html">T vs t</A> graph as "Ripples in the
Cosmos".  Out of print.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0385263260/nedwrightscosmol">
"Coming of Age in the Milky Way"</A> by Timothy Ferris.  A more
extensive coverage of the material in the "Creation of the Universe"
show on PBS.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0385472145/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Alchemy of the Heavens: Searching for Meaning in the Milky
Way"</A>, by Ken Croswell.  A good book about <A HREF="./milkyway.html">our
Galaxy.</A>
<A NAME="TF3M">
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0553246828/nedwrightscosmol">
"The First Three Minutes"</A> by Steven Weinberg.  Old but good treatment
of light element synthesis.  Out of print.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/071671812X/nedwrightscosmol">
"The Big Bang"</A> by Joseph Silk.  Many typos, unfortunately.
<LI>
<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0716760207/nedwrightscosmol">
"A Short History of the Universe"</A> by Joseph Silk.
<A HREF="./silkshort.html">Many typos</A>, unfortunately.
</UL>
