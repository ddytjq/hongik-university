      <p>Booking agents and consultants make their living by selling hunts on 
        a 10-15% commission, so they may tend to book the more expensive hunts 
        first.&nbsp; These hunts may not be the best quality hunt or the best 
        value for your money!&nbsp; My group of honest hardworking outfitters 
        specialize in affordable western big game hunts.&nbsp; They hunt quality 
        private land, which renders high hunter satisfaction on representative 
        animals. </p>

        goose hunts, Wyoming turkey hunts and have access to other big game hunts

      <h4 align="center"> </h4>
      <h4 align="left"><u><font color="#993300"><b>Things to think about when 
        booking a guided hunt</b></font></u></h4>
      <p><b>Look for private land hunts.</b> These hunts can generate high success 
        and generally have some type of track record.&nbsp; Outfitters pay premium 
        rates for leases, so they usually do a very good job managing hunting 
        pressure.</p>
      <p><b>Book at least a year in advance.</b>&nbsp; You may avoid outfitter 
        price increases and you can spread the cost of your hunt over 12-24 months.</p>
      <p><b>Get in shape.</b>&nbsp; Although most of my private land hunts are 
        not that physically demanding, being in shape is a healthy lifestyle and 
        will allow you to remain in the field longer. </p>
