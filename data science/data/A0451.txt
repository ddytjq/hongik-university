		              <p class="contentsubhead">BACS - Direct Credit</p>
            <p>Whether you are a small business or large organisation 
              you can benefit from using the Direct Credit System as it enables 
              you to transfer funds electronically directly into a specified bank 
              or building society account. </p>
            <p>A company making as few as 50 cheque payments 
              a week for any of the following reasons may realise substantial 
              annual savings:- </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Paying Salaries and wages to employees 
              and reimbursing expenses</p>
            <p>This has now become the standard &quot;best 
              practice&quot; in payroll administration as :- </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Some 94% of the population in Britain have 
                a personal account with a Bank or Building Society. (Source : 
                BACS &quot; Direct Credit for Paying Employees&quot; Jan 98) </li>
              <li class="contenttext">Currently over 70% of all salary and wage 
                payments are now made directly to personal accounts. (Source : 
                BACS &quot; Direct Credit for Paying Employees&quot; Jan 98) </li>
              <li class="contenttext">It is estimated that over 150,000 organisations 
                of all sizes pay their employees by Direct Credit resulting in 
                5 million wages being processed every week and over 12 million 
                salaries every month. (Source : BACS &quot; Direct Credit for 
                Paying Employees&quot; Jan 98) </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Paying essential costs involved in running 
              your business</p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Direct Credit is an efficient method for 
                paying essential bills such as rates, rent, electricity etc. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Paying regular suppliers</p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">A recent study found that 96% of companies 
                would be willing to receive payments by Direct Credit with many 
                making this their preferred payment method. (Source : BACS &quot; 
                Direct Credit An Introduction&quot; Jan 98) </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Preferred by Business</p>
            <p>The benefits of using Direct Credit in particular, 
              for paying salaries and wages are summarised as follows:- </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Saving your Business time and money</strong></li>
              <p>Eliminates the cost of cheque stationery 
                and where relevant, the overheads of automated cheque production. 
                Indeed, independent research has shown that paying employees by 
                cash or cheque can be up to six times more expensive than paying 
                by Direct Credit. (Source : BACS &quot;Direct Credit for Paying 
                Employees&quot; Jan 98) </p>
              <li><strong>Savings in administration time</strong></li>
              <p>Removes time-consuming laborious administration 
                associated with preparing cheques, collating payment with payslips 
                and removes the employee downtime involved in their distribution 
                and collection. </p>
              <li><strong>Improved Cash Flow Management</strong></li>
              <p>The certainty of payment date facilitates 
                optimum cash flow management and removes the need to fund the 
                payroll before the employee's accounts are credited. Each payment 
                file generates just a single debit to the employer's account on 
                a specified date. </p>
              <li><strong>Greater Security</strong></li>
              <p> Eliminates the security risks and costs 
                involved in delivering and holding cash on the premises which 
                in turn may also reduce insurance costs. </p>
              <li><strong>Greater Reliability</strong></li>
              <p>There is no risk of encoding faults and clearing 
                delays to which paper credits are vulnerable. </p>
              <p>The facility for multiple payments by bank 
                giro credit will be withdrawn by the Banking Industry at the end 
                of 1999. </p>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Preferred by Employees</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Immediate Access to Pay Cheque</strong></li>
              <p>Unlike cheques, Direct Credit gives the employee 
                cleared funds guaranteed to be available on pay day. Removes the 
                risk of payment being delayed in the post. </p>
              <li><strong>Greater Security</strong></li>
              <p>Eliminates the security risks that the employee 
                may face from robbery or the loss of wage packets. </p>
              <li><strong>Greater Reliability</strong></li>
              <p>Money is automatically credited to their 
                account on known dates even when they are sick, on holiday or 
                on shift work outside normal hours. </p>
              <li><strong>Improved Cash Management</strong></li>
              <p>Employees may choose to pay essential bills 
                by Direct Debit and may have the opportunity to receive discounts 
                for early payment. Once all bills are paid they are able to budget 
                on the remaining funds available. </p>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Payment Process </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Information Required </p>
            <p>In order to make payments by Direct Credits 
              you will need the following details of Employees Bank/Building Society 
              account to be credited :- </p>
            <ol>
              <li class="contenttext">Sort code </li>
              <li class="contenttext">Account Number </li>
            </ol>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Process is completed in 3 days </p>
            <p>Day 1 : INPUT in accordance with the stipulated 
              timetable<br />
              Day 2 : PROCESS accepted data<br />
              Day 3 : ENTRY COMPLETED with Beneficiary being credited and Originator 
              debited simultaneously<br />
            </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">You remain in Control </p>
            <p>You have the option of submitting Direct Credit 
              payment instructions up to 30 days prior to payment date. </p>
            <p>You have the capability of altering the payment 
              information after the instructions have been submitted provided 
              your Bank is notified prior to a specified cut-off time. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Accessing the Direct Credit System</p>
            <p>There is a choice of methods to access the 
              system either :- </p>
            <ol>
              <li><strong>Indirect Access</strong></li>
              <p> You may choose to access the system through 
                the TeleBank&reg; Business BACS module (information can be found 
                from our home page). This is often more suitable for smaller businesses 
                or those collecting a limited number of payments. </p>
              <li><strong>Direct Access</strong></li>
              <p>The system can be accessed directly from 
                your office by BACSTEL, the telecommunications link. Originators 
                collecting large volumes of Direct Credits find this method faster 
                and more cost effective than indirect access. </p>
            </ol>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Requirements</p>
            <p>In order to access the system through BACSTEL 
              you will require a PC, appropriate modem and required software. 
            </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Availability</p>
            <p>Software can be obtained from your Bank or 
              a proprietary software supplier. Solutions are also available for 
              mid-range and mainframe computer systems. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Security</p>
              <p>All transmissions are secured using passwords unique to each submission. 
              Confirmation of receipt is sent back down the telephone line to 
              notify successful transmission. </p>
            <p>If you require any further information please</p>
			<p>
            <a href="/about/201.php" class="link">e-mail us</a> or <br />
              telephone <strong>+ 44 113 2325203</strong> or <br />
              call into your local Yorkshire branch. </p>
              <p>We look forward to hearing from you. </p>
