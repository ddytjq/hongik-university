<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>ARLINGTON, Va.&nbsp;-- Travis Garrison might be a McDonald's All-American. Or he might not be. Heading into the final stages of his high school career, though, this much is non-negotiable: Kid's one tough nut.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The 6-foot-8 DeMatha Catholic forward re-aggravated a nagging right ankle injury Saturday night in a game against Paul VI here at Marymount University. He went down hard late in the first quarter.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I thought it was broken," he said after DeMatha's 54-33 win. "But when we taped it up, it didn't feel as bad."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>So he sat out the second quarter. But after halftime&nbsp;-- in spite of a heavy tape job and an air brace&nbsp;-- back in he went.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"To see him go down early like that, it kind of shook us up," legendary Stags coach Morgan Wootten said. "But he came back and just took everyone up. He comes back under any circumstance. He's a great competitor."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Not to mention a versatile forward.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The injury, actually, forced the Maryland-bound Garrison to rely on that ability to do so many things so well&nbsp;-- or at least well enough.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He could no longer make sharp cuts. He could no longer come around screens on the perimeter and launch jumpers. So he did the only thing he could&nbsp;-- the things necessary to get his Stags the win.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He was active around the basket, he was active on the glass, and he was active with some putbacks&nbsp;-- all en route to 13 third-quarter points and 17 overall to go along with 12 boards and three blocks.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And the victory in the regular-season finale: DeMatha won its third straight Washington Catholic Athletic Conference championship with a 13-3 league record. The Stags finished with a 25-3 overall mark.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Now come the playoffs&nbsp;-- with the Maryland-bound Garrison leading the way.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I like the fact that he's able to play both forward positions fairly well," said Dave Telep, a national recruiting analyst for TheInsidersHoops.com. "I think he's going to be great in that role at Maryland."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Especially considering the Terrapins' reputation for getting the most out of their talent.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I think Travis has a chance to be a great college player," Wootten said. "But I want to see him have a great finish to his high school career."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>That, presumably, would include a roster spot in the McDonald's All-American set for April 4 at Madison Square Garden.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Garrison's good. He's very good. But is he McDonald's good?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The soft-spoken forward is one of this season's "maybes." Every year these kids emerge. The current crop includes Brad Buckman, the Texas-bound forward from Austin; Michel Thompson and Sean Dockery, two Duke-bound prospects from the Chicago area; Jarrett Jack, a Georgia Tech-bound point from Fort Washington, Md., via Worcester, Mass.; Elijah Ingram, a St. John's-bound point from Jersey City, N.J.; and Matt Walsh, a Florida-bound swingman from Fort Washington, Pa.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>There are 24 spots on the nation's most prestigious All-American team&nbsp;-- 20 based solely on votes from a 33-member panel and four more "wild cards" of sorts&nbsp;-- and always there are more than 24 deserving nominees.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I think he's a strong candidate for it," Wootten said. "It'll be interesting to see."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Garrison's been schooled for four years at Wootten's storied Hyattsville, Md., prep basketball powerhouse. He did great things in Hackensack, N.J., at last summer's adidas-sponsored ABCD Camp.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But he chose to run in the off-season mainly with DeMatha's summer league team rather than a more high-profile club squad on the more high-profile AAU circuit. (His summer school, no doubt, had something to do with that decision.)</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And out-of-season performances and summer exposure&nbsp;-- like it or not&nbsp;-- have come to have tons of influence on McDonald's voters. Perhaps too much.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's their call," Garrison said. "I think I could be and I really want to be. If it doesn't happen, I'll just keep working, but I'd be very excited if I got it."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Says here he should. Says here he will.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Wootten, after all, is the Selection Committee Chairman. He's been one of the most respected names in high school basketball for four and a half decades. Check that: The man is one of the sport's all-time icons.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And his role as patriarch of the McDonald's Game&nbsp;-- along, of course, with former UCLA mentor John Wooden&nbsp;-- can do nothing but help Garrison's cause.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I don't necessarily think Travis is among the 24 best players in the country," Telep said. "But I'd love to have him on my team."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Even on the McDonald's team.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><B><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>THE BASKETBALL ACADEMY&nbsp;... WHAT'S UP WITH THAT?</FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Calling all holier-than-thou armchair columnists. Calling all the quick-talking, shallow-thinking, pie-in-the-sky moralists. Calling all opinionated eggheads looking for a new bone to pick.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>There's this thing called The Basketball Academy, it's down in Bradenton, Fla., and it's ripe fodder for just such a soapbox.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The high school kids enrolled full-time at the Academy take classes at the on-campus Pendleton School. The school and the training facilities are both on the posh International Management Group (IMG) campus.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>IMG has six sports-specific academies&nbsp;-- the Nick Bollettieri Tennis Academy, the David Leadbetter Golf Academy and the newer, less-well-known academies for soccer, basketball, baseball and ice hockey.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Anyone can receive training from the staffs at the various training facilities. At least anyone with the will, the time and sufficient cash flow.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Joe Abunassar is the head of The Basketball Academy. His reputation has drawn pros to Bradenton for workout sessions (Chauncey Billups and Austin Croshere, for example) as well as some college stars (Arizona's Jason Gardner and Indiana's Jared Jeffries).</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The "basketball students"&nbsp;-- the high school and/or post-graduate kids&nbsp;-- pay $10,000 a year in tuition and another $23,000 for the full-time hoops education. They train all morning, they go to class all afternoon, and then they return for some more individual skill work in the evening.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The only reason the school exists is to provide academics for the kids who are there training," TBA coach Mike Moreau said. "It's not a school with sports; it's a sports academy that has a school.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"We're a professional training environment. Our aim is to get basketball players to the highest level they can possibly achieve. But our kids go to the beach. They have fun. It's not an assembly line or a robotic factory."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Even so, at least from a purely American, healthy-body, healthy-mind way of thinking, that sounds sort of sketchy, right? So where's the outrage?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Here's the sad truth: The only reason more folks haven't jumped on the bash-this-idea bandwagon is that TBA's prep school team isn't good enough and hasn't played enough games to really merit much attention of any sort.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Moreau's squad lost often and lost big last week in Smithfield, R.I., at the National Prep School Invitational.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Pendleton fields a regular high school basketball team that plays a standard Florida High School Activities Association Schedule. The post-grads, however, don't have many similar teams to even play down in the Sunshine State.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But when IMG's prep squad does get better players, when it does start playing more games, when it does start traveling around the country to showcase its talent and its idea&nbsp;... watch out.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The academics-should-come-first ethics cops will emerge en masse.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"For decades," Moreau said, "baseball players have been going from high school to the pros." And training <I>like pros</I> when in high school. If they're even <I>in school</I>. Golfers, too, and tennis players and gymnasts and ...</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And know what? The folks at IMG/The Basketball Academy/Pendleton&nbsp;-- whatever it's called&nbsp;-- are way more straightforward and honest about their intentions than many, many, many prep "schools" out there in the amateur basketball scene.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And know what else? This thing's going to work. With some financial aid, with a growing reputation, kids will want to go to Bradenton. Big-time kids. Pro-caliber training? Half a day of school? Sounds good.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Academics and athletics are a partnership, no matter how you look at it," Moreau said, "and I'd certainly think we're not going to be the only one of our kind for much longer."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** To answer everybody's question concerning <STRONG>LeBron James</STRONG> and his status as this week's <I>Sports Illustrated</I> cover boy: Yes, he is that good. The 17-year-old Akron, Ohio, wunderkind will continue to bring unprecedented publicity to the national grassroots scene&nbsp;-- not to mention the Nike-adidas sneaker spat that serves as its ever-present undercurrent. Witness <STRONG>Danny Ainge</STRONG>'s quote in <STRONG>Grant Wahl</STRONG>'s SI piece: "If LeBron came out this year, I wouldn't even have to think about it. I'd take him No. 1."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Although there's this to note: The SI cover jinx seems to be kicking in for LeBron's team at St. Vincent-St. Mary. Coach <STRONG>Dru Joyce</STRONG>'s Irish have now lost two in a row&nbsp;-- a defeat in Trenton, N.J., to Oak Hill as part of the Prime Time Shootout and the most recent setback this past weekend in Youngstown, Ohio, to George Jr. Republic of Grove City, Pa. St. V's is No. 15 in Student Sports' rankings this week.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <STRONG>Shannon Brown</STRONG> is asserting himself as one of America's top junior prospects heading into the spring and summer. The 6-3 shooting guard from Proviso East High School in Maywood, Ill., hit for 50 points last week in a win over Hinsdale South. PEHS, which also features Illinois-bound point man <STRONG>Dee Brown</STRONG>, is 19-2 overall.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I came out and I just couldn't miss," Brown told the Chicago Tribune. "After Dee went out with his third foul, I just put the team on my back. You dream of this type of game, but you never think it will come true." Kansas coach <STRONG>Roy Williams</STRONG> was in attendance, too.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** A thought: Who'd win in a game between last year's Willowridge team and this year's Dallas Lincoln bunch? Then-seniors <STRONG>T.J. Ford</STRONG> (Texas), <STRONG>Daniel Ewing</STRONG> (Duke) and <STRONG>Kenny Taylor</STRONG> (Baylor) teamed to give Willowridge an extremely impressive unbeaten season&nbsp;-- but Oak Hill did the same and took the mythical national championship.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>This winter's Lincoln team&nbsp;-- with SMU-bound point man <STRONG>Bryan Hopkins</STRONG> and Georgia Tech-bound big guy <STRONG>Chris Bosh</STRONG>&nbsp;-- is now 34-0. The Tigers, though, control their championship destiny because of OHA's loss earlier this month to Mater Dei of Santa Ana, Calif. Life just isn't fair sometimes.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** West Virginia coach <STRONG>Gale Catlett</STRONG>'s abrupt retirement last week has left Mountaineer signees <STRONG>Kevin Pittsnogle</STRONG> and <STRONG>Jabbar Young</STRONG> with some thinking to do. The question for the pair of big men: Stay or go? Pittsnogle, for one, is from Martinsville, W.Va., and is very much an in-state type of kid.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Sports America, Inc., spokesman <STRONG>Kevin Foley</STRONG> says the McDonald's All-American rosters are scheduled to be unveiled Feb. 27 live on ESPN's evening SportsCenter. The game's come a long way since <STRONG>Bob Geoghan</STRONG> started the thing back in 1977, no?</FONT></P></DIV>
