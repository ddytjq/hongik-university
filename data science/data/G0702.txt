<P>
Characteristic X-rays in the range from 0.1 to 100 keV are produced by gas 
heated to high temperature (millions of degree), high-energy particles with 
interaction with magnetic fields (synchrotron radiation) or photons (inverse 
Compton radiation). Hence, observations in the X-ray band are a particularly good 
diagnostical tool to study the high-energetic state of matter in the universe.
<P>
Primary scope of this project is to give a comprehensive insight into the X-ray 
source components of nearby galaxies, e.g. mass-accreting compact objects such 
as neutron stars or black holes, supernova remnants and supernova explosions. 
Also, the distribution and evolution of the hot interstellar medium is studied
based on X-ray imaging spectroscopy of nearby galaxies with the 
<a href="http://wave.xray.mpe.mpg.de/rosat">ROSAT</a>,
<a href="http://chandra.harvard.edu/">Chandra</a> and 
<a href="http://xmm.vilspa.esa.es/">XMM-Newton</a> observatories.
