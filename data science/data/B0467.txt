		<td><h2>Tax-free savings<br></h2></td>
	</tr>
	<tr> 
		<td>
			<img src="/images/spacer.gif" height="1" width="10" alt="spacer">
		</td>
		<td> 
            <p class="plaintext">
			There are obvious advantages to saving your money in an account where you are not paying tax on the interest earned.  Tax on savings interest is normally deducted at 20% for basic rate tax payers.  If you are a higher rate taxpayer you'll have to pay the extra tax through your tax return and subsequent tax coding.<br>
			<br>
			If you live in the UK, there are a number of ways in which you can save without paying tax on any interest earned.  We offer two:</p>
            <ul class="plaintext">
	            <li>Take out a mini cash Individual Savings Account (ISA).</li>
	            <li>Register to have any interest on a savings account paid gross, i.e. without tax deducted.</li>
		    </ul>
			<br>
			<p class="biggreentext">ISAs</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			An ISA provides a tax efficient means of saving but there are limits on the amount you can invest in each tax year.<br>
			<br>ISAs, or Individual Savings Accounts, were developed by the Government in 1999 to replace PEPs (Personal Equity Plan) and TESSAs (Tax Exempt Special Savings Account).</p>
			<p class="boldtext">Where do I start?</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			There are two main types of ISA, a Mini ISA and a Maxi ISA.<br>
			<br>
			Broadly, you can invest in cash, stocks and shares or life insurance, either together or, with up to three different product providers.<br>
			<br>
			You can take out one Maxi ISA or up to three Mini ISAs in any tax year, which runs from 6th April to 5th April the following year.  Check our <a href="/savings/branch_based/isa/what_is/index.jsp">What is an ISA?</a> section for more details including investment limits.</p>
			<p class="boldtext">TESSA-only ISA</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			There is a 3rd type of ISA - a TESSA-only ISA.  If you still have a TESSA and it is coming up for maturity, consider investing in a <a href="/savings/branch_based/isa/index.jsp">TESSA-only ISA</a>.  These are special ISAs in which you can re-invest all the capital (up to a maximum of £9,000) from your matured TESSA.  The capital is the money you have paid into your TESSA and does not include interest paid.  Any money you put into a TESSA-only ISA, which has come from a matured TESSA, does not count towards annual Maxi or Mini ISA limits.	</p><br>
			<p class="biggreentext">Interest paid gross</p>
			<p class="plaintext">
			If your annual income is less than your personal tax allowance, you may be able to register to have any interest on your savings paid gross, i.e. without tax deducted.  The best place to check whether you are eligible to have interest paid gross is the <a href="http://www.inlandrevenue.gov.uk/taxback/index.htm" target="_new">Inland Revenue</a> web site.<br>
			<br>
If you are eligible, complete an R85 form and let your savings provider have it.  They will do the rest.  If you are one of our customers <a href="/contact_us/index.jsp">Contact us</a> for a form.

			</p><br>
