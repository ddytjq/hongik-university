<H1><IMG ALT="[CWC logo]" ALIGN=middle SRC="http:/images/compcen.gif" > Recommended Books</H1>

<HR>
<P>
It is always difficult to recommend books for any course and none more so that a C programming course - there are plenty of book to chose from. It is important to get a book that suits your reading style and also provides you with plenty of worked examples.<P>

A suggested reading list is as follows:<P>

<B>Title:</B> The C Programming Language ANSI C Version<BR>
<B>Author:</B> Kernighan & Ritchie<BR>
<B>Publisher:</B> Prentice Hall Software Series<BR>
<B>ISBN:</B> 0-13-110362-8<BR>
<B>Comment:</B> Ideal reference book and goes well beyond the level of this course. It is expensive but the serious C programmer will kick themself if they don't buy this book.
<P>

<B>Title:</B> ANSI C - Made Easy<BR>
<B>Author:</B> Herbert Schildt<BR>
<B>Publisher:</B> Osborne McGraw-Hill<BR>
<B>ISBN:</B> 0-07-881500-2<BR>
<B>Comment:</B>
<P>

<B>Title:</B> Learning to Program in C</B><BR>
<B>Author:</B> N. Kantaris<BR>
<B>Publisher:</B> Babani<BR>
<B>ISBN:</B> 0-85934-203-4<BR>
<B>Comment:</B> A good cheap beginners guide.
<P>

<B>Title:</B> Illustrating ANSI C</B><BR>
<B>Author:</B> Donald Alcock<BR>
<B>Publisher:</B> Cambridge University Press<BR>
<B>ISBN:</B> 0-521-42483-6<BR>
<B>Comment:</B> A good book for the mathematically inclined.
<P>

<B>Title:</B> C - The Complete Reference<BR>
<B>Author:</B> Herbert Schildt<BR>
<B>Publisher:</B> Osborne McGraw-Hill<BR>
<B>ISBN:</B> 0-07-881263-1<BR>
<B>Comment:</B>
<P>

<B>Title:</B> Numerical Recipies in C<BR>
<B>Author:</B> W.H.Press, et al<BR>
<B>Comment:</B> An advanced level book with, as the name implies, ready-made solutions to your programming problems.
