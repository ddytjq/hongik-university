                              <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman" lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" color="#000080" size="3"><span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">7
                              September 2001</span></font></span><br>
                              <br>
                              <span style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman" lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" color="#000080" size="4">Independent
                              Insurance Company Limited<br>
                              (In Provisional Liquidation)<br>
                              <br>
                              Requests by Premium Credit Limited for payment of
                              outstanding instalments</font></span><br>
                              <br>
                              <br>
                              <font face="Arial" size="2">A number of brokers
                              have brought to our attention letters sent to
                              their clients by Premium Credit Limited,
                              requesting confirmation within a period of two
                              weeks that the clients will honour their agreement
                              to pay outstanding instalments.<br>
                              <br>
                              Members will recall that BIBA took Counsel's
                              Opinion shortly after the appointment of the
                              Provisional Liquidators, and advised members of
                              its understanding of the position with regard to
                              both private individuals (with specific reference
                              to the Consumer Credit Act) and to bodies
                              corporate. We understand that Premium Credit
                              Limited has confirmed that it will not pursue
                              private policyholders for payment of outstanding
                              instalments.<br>
                              <br>
                              We have maintained our view that the position of
                              borrowers which are corporate bodies is likely to
                              be rather different. The advice we have received
                              suggests that such borrowers are unlikely to
                              benefit from the protection of the Act, and that
                              their liability to pay may well be determined by
                              the particular terms of the contract under which
                              the instalment arrangements were made. This advice
                              remains unaltered.<br>
                              <br>
                              BIBA has now seen further Counsel's Opinion
                              advising on this subject. In the view of Counsel,
                              borrowers are obliged to comply with the terms of
                              credit agreements notwithstanding the status of
                              the insurance company to whom premiums were to
                              have been made. Although it is clear that there
                              was a connection between Premium Credit and
                              Independent, in his view the obligations
                              undertaken by the borrowers were independent of
                              any insurance agreement and the borrower is not
                              relieved of the obligation to comply with the
                              terms of the credit agreement.<br>
                              <br>
                              It has been suggested that the doctrine of
                              frustration may apply, and that the finance
                              contracts had been frustrated by Independent's
                              collapse. Counsel states that the subject matter
                              of the credit agreements he has seen is not
                              specifically referable to the health or otherwise
                              of Independent. Case law (J.Lauritzen
                              AS.Wijsmuller B.V. the Super Servant Two) (1990) 1
                              Lloyds Report 1 per Bingham L.J.) suggests that
                              frustration cannot be invoked here.<br>
                              <br>
                              There is also some suggestion that the existence
                              of some arrangement under which instalments were
                              collected on behalf of Independent might cast
                              doubt on the finance company's ability to enforce
                              the agreement. Counsel's does not believe it can
                              be said that the monies were to be collected on
                              Independent's behalf. He suggests that the
                              agreements between the parties remained
                              essentially separate.<br>
                              <br>
                              We must point out that these comments are provided
                              to support BIBA members in their discussions with
                              clients. Our view remains that, irrespective of
                              the strength of the arguments, or of legal advice,
                              it is not possible to determine in advance the
                              eventual outcome of any action for recovery of
                              sums allegedly due for payment.<br>
                              <br>
                              Brokers should remind their clients of the
                              potential consequences should they decide not to
                              continue to make instalment payments. BIBA also
                              recommends that clients should be advised to take
                              their own legal advice, particularly with regard
                              to interpretation of any premium finance
                              agreements which may be in force.<br>
                              </font></p>
