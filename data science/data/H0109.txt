<B><A NAME="healthstudents">HEALTH RESOURCES FOR STUDENTS</a></B>
<ul>
<li><A HREF="http://kidshealth.org/">KidsHealth</a><BR> 
KidsHealth has health information about children from before birth through adolescence. Created by The Nemours Foundation Center for Children's Health Media, the site has separate areas for kids, teens, and parents - each with its own design, age-appropriate content, and tone. 

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.healthfinder.gov/justforyou/">HealthFinder.gov: Just for You</a><br> has information selected specifically for individuals with common interests and concerns. The <a href="http://www.healthfinder.gov/kids/">Kids</a> section as it has interactive games and activities, information on safe web navigation, and art projects all aimed at helping kids understand how to protect their bodies and minds. Other sections address interests by age, ethnic origin and occupation. 
<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.niehs.nih.gov/kids/">National Institute of Environmental Health Sciences: Kids' Pages </a> <br>include many on screen and hands on activities. <BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.cdc.gov/health/diseases.htm">Health Topics from A to Z</a> <br>presented by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention is a very comprehensive list of government material listed according to name of the disease. Other health topics included in this list are pages for baby, children's, adolescent, women's, men's and senior health. Many pages use technical language.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/aso/tryit/doctor/#">Doctor Over Time</a><br> shows you, the viewer, how doctors in 1900, 1950, and 1998 would have handled the same afflictions. All you have to do is complain to the doctor, then see how he or she responds. Both Shockwave and text versions are posted.

<BR><BR>

<LI><A HREF="http://vm.cfsan.fda.gov/~dms/educate.html#kids">For Kids and Teens</a><br>  
A number of activities to help kids understand food safety are presented by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration's Center for Food Safety and Applied Nutrition (FDA-CFSAN).  Students looking for research project ideas, guidelines and resources will find <A HREF="http://vm.cfsan.fda.gov/~comm/students.html">Student Resources</a> especially helpful.

<B><A NAME="elsi">ETHICAL, LEGAL AND SOCIAL ISSUES</a></B>
<UL>
<li><A HREF="http://www.nih.gov/sigs/bioethics/">Bioethics Resources on the Web</a><br>
The National Institutes of Health (NIH) has assembled an extensive and wellorganized list of bioethics links related to education, research involving human participants and animals, medical and health care ethics, and the implications of applied genetics and biotechnology.

<BR><BR>                         

<li><A HREF="http://ajobonline.com/">Bioethics.net:  the American Journal of Bioethics Online</a>
<br>This site has articles on current and on-going bioethical issues including such topics as "Who Owns Life?", "Assisted Suicide", "Cloning and Genetics", and "Emergency Room Ethics". The "Bioethics for Beginners" provides a good place to start.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF=http://www.lbl.gov/Education/ELSI/ELSI.html>Ethical, Legal and Social Issues in Science</a><br>
This site, developed by Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, provides several scenarios that can be used to stimulate classroom discussion and debates on topics such as breast screening and indoor and outdoor pollution.  Many relevant links to web resources are provided to assist teachers and students.

<BR><BR>

<LI><A HREF="http://www.bioethics.umn.edu/">University of Minnesota, Center for Bioethics</a><BR>
Their State Fair exhibit on stem cell research and the <cite>Bioethics Examiner </cite>newsletter are "don't miss" parts of this site. 
</UL>

<B><A NAME="virtualfieldtrips">VIRTUAL FIELD TRIPS AND LABS</a></B>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.biografx.com/cvp/cvpindex.html">The Cell Visualization Project Home Page</a><br>
Computer reconstructions of a human white blood cell from serial transmission electron micrographs are presented as a brief guided tour and as still photos. Although more organelles will be added in the future, enough are included for this site to be useful to anyone introducing cell structure.

<br><br>

<li><a href=http://www.exploratorium.edu/>The Exploratorium Home Page</a><br>
This is the home page of the San Francisco Exploratorium.  Check it out for information on science education resources, great interactive exhibits, access to an ongoing bioethics discussion group, and more!

<br><br>

<li><a href=http://sln.fi.edu/biosci/heart.html>The Heart</a><br>
This site, constructed by The Franklin Institute Science Museum, is a virtual exploration of the heart.  It includes examinations--both graphic and text--of cardiac development, structure, vessels, and more.  Even includes information on maintaining a healthy heart and monitoring the heart's function.

<BR><BR>

<LI><A HREF="http://www.hhmi.org/grants/lectures/multimedia/">HHMI Interactive Web</a><br>
Topics in cardiology, human genetics, immunology and neurophysiology are explored using Java, Shockwave, Quicktime, and RealPlayer. Instructions for downloading the necessary software to use the demonstrations, as well as other Technical Tips are included.

<br><br>

<LI><A HREF=http://commtechlab.msu.edu/sites/dlc-me/zoo/>Microbial Zoo</A><BR>
This virtual field trip through a microbial zoo contains images and descriptions of microscopic organisms and the habitats in which they live.

<br><br>

<li>The <a href="http://www.life.uiuc.edu/plantbio/cell/">Virtual Cell</a><br>

This site lets you explore the structure of a virtual plant cell. You can zoom in on organelles, cut or turn them, look at real EM images and more.

<br><br>

<li>The<a href=http://www.nlm.nih.gov/research/visible/visible_human.html> Visible Human Project</a><br>
The Visible Human Project creates complete anatomically detailed, three dimensional representations of the male and female human body.  The representations are created from CAT, MRI and cryosection images taken at one millimeter intervals from cadavers.
</ul>

<B><A NAME="genetics">GENETICS</a></B>
<UL>  

<li><A HREF="http://www.hhmi.org/GeneticTrail">Blazing a Genetic Trail</a><br>
Now an updated, online, user-friendly version with its own glossary. Genetics is personalized in this online version of the popular publication from Howard Hughes Medical Institute.

<BR><BR>
<LI><a href="http://vector.cshl.org/">DNA Learning Center</a><br>
The award winning Cold Spring Harbor DNA Learning Center has information at all levels from basic genetics to advanced bioinformatics. This site uses animations, quizzes, diagrams and more to explain genetics principles and their implications and applications. <a href="http://vector.cshl.org/ygyh/index.html">Your Genes, Your Health</a> provides insights into the nature, diagnosis and treatment of genetic diseases. 
     
<BR><BR>

<LI><A HREF="http://bioinformatics.weizmann.ac.il/cards/">GeneCards: genes, proteins and diseases</A><BR>
A database integrating information about the functions of human genes and their products, and of biomedical applications based on this knowledge.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://gslc.genetics.utah.edu">Genetic Science Learning Center</a><br>
Recently expanded and reorganized, this web site includes sections on Basic Genetics, Genetic Disorders, Genetics in Society and Genetic Thematic Units. Low cost genetics activities, information on how to get equipment, notices of workshops, information on DNA, are resources of particular interest to teachers.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.pbs.org/gene/">A Question of Genes:Inherited Risks</a><br>
This site includes many resources designed to go with the Public Broadcasting System video of the same name. Material found here helps students to look at the social, ethical and emotional consequences of genetic testing. 

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF= http://ehr.aaas.org/ehr/books/index.html>Your Genes, Your Choices</a><br>
"<cite>Your Genes, Your Choices</cite>" is an online book that presents hypothetical situations about genetics at the beginning of every chapter, then goes on to explain the science behind the issue. A great job of personalizing the issues so students see a reason to learn this material.
</ul>

<UL>
<li><A HREF=http://vm.cfsan.fda.gov/~mow/intro.html>The "Bad Bug" Book</a><br>
This handbook, developed by the Food and Drug Administration, provides basic facts regarding foodborne pathogenic microorganisms and natural toxins. Some technical terms have been linked to the National Library of Medicine's Entrez glossary.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.cellsalive.com/">CELLS Alive!</a><br>
Check out this site for unique images of microorganisms that make you sick, and the blood cells that do battle to keep you well.  Includes links to sites offering further information on microbiology, infectious diseases and cell biology.

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF=http://www.cdc.gov/>The Centers for Disease Control (CDC)</a><br>
One of my favorite sites is the CDC homepage as I can get recent statistics on various diseases.  This year I was able to download stats on the occurrence of HIV is both females and in California and give them to my students as we started our AIDS unit.<br>
(Theresa Peters, '96 AE Fellow)

<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF=http://www.tulane.edu/%7Edmsander/Tutorials/Pox/Pox1.html>A Dose of Pox</a><br>
An interactive tutorial that steps you through the discovery and ultimate eradication of smallpox using a series of questions.  At each step, information and images are provided to help broaden ones appreciation of this scientific feat! 


<BR><BR>

<li><A HREF="http://www.utmb.edu/medmicro/">Medical Microbiology Textbook</a>
This website contains the entire Medical Microbiology textbook by Baron.  The textbook includes all the images and figures in the printed form of the text.

<br><br>

<li><A HREF="http://people.ku.edu/~jbrown/bugs.html"> Microbiology, Bugs in the News!</a><br>
Lots of information, with pictures on microbiology. Read about cryptosporidium, AIDS and HIV, flu, <i>E. coli,</i> the immune system and lots more.
</ul>
