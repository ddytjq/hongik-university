        <b><i><font face="    U001TUltBol" size="7">
        <div>
          <p align="center">Speed Talk Coming To Charter Communications</p>
          <font face="    Arial CE" size="4">
          <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
          </font><font size="4">
          <p align="left">&nbsp;</p>
          <p>(ST. CLOUD, MN) Speed Talk, Central Minnesota’s only racing
          television program is set to make it’s debut on Charter
          Communications, Cable Channel 10 in the St. Cloud area starting
          Monday, July 1st. The show is hosted by veteran motorsports announcers
          Jason Dale and Dean Reller. Each half-hour show features interviews
          with some of the area’s best drivers and other racing personalities
          from around the country. The show will air every Monday at 6 PM
          throughout the racing season.</p>
          </font>
        </div>
        <p align="center">&nbsp;Grow At I-94 Raceway</p>
        </font></i></b><font size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        </font><b><font face="    Arial CE" size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        <p align="justify">By Dean Reller</p>
        </font></b><font size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        <p align="left">(SAUK CENTRE, MN) With the addition of ASA sanctioning,
        interest is booming in the Late Model division at the I-94 Raceway in
        Sauk Centre. Over 30 drivers have already pre-registered and several
        more are expected to join the list before the first green flag waves on
        Saturday April 27th. Some of the drivers already registered include 2001
        Modified Champion Stacy Rossell, 2001 Great North Legends Champion Rob
        Thurston, former Late Model Track Champion Eugene Dick, who will have
        son Mike as a teammate this year. Several veterans such as Jesse Klug,
        Tim Olson, Paul Noskowiak and Trent Snyder are also slated to return.
        2000 and 2001 Track Champion Bryan Roach plans on racing when his NASCAR
        REMAX Challenge Series schedule allows. ASA Crew Chief Nate Thiesse
        plans on running when he is not touring with the ASA Racing Series.</p>
        <p align="left">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Drivers will see increased awards at
        the end of the year as the Late Model Champion will walk away with a
        minimum of $3,000 from the new Point Fund. Hawk Performance Group
        recently announced its involvement with ASA involving both the Touring
        Series and its Member Tracks of which I-94 Raceway is now associated.
        Details of this program will be coming soon, but they are anticipated to
        be additional bonuses available on a weekly basis.</p>
        <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For additional information and driver profiles,
        check the official track web site at www.i-94raceway.com.</p>
        </font>
      </div>
      <br>
      <div>
        <b><i><font face="    U001TUltBol" size="7">
        <p align="center">Practice Day Is Set At I-94 Raceway</p>
        </font></i></b><font size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        </font><b><font face="    Arial CE" size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        <p align="justify">By Dean Reller</p>
        </font></b><font size="4">
        <p align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        <p align="left">(SAUK CENTRE, MN) With just a few short weeks until the
        April 27th start of the racing season, officials at the I-94 Raceway in
        Sauk Centre have scheduled Practice Day for Saturday, April 20th from 10
        AM until 6 PM. All drivers are encouraged to attend the practice and all
        New drivers are strongly encouraged to attend the Rookie Orientation to
        be held at the track at 2 PM. Track officials will be on hand to discuss
        rules and answer questions.</p>
        <p align="left">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; To be eligible for year-end awards,
        all drivers must be registered and hold an ASA Track Membership and
        compete in at least 75% of the season’s races. The 2002 Points Fund
        will have a minimum of $10,000 available. First place in the Late Models
        will be $3,000, while the top Modified and Super Stock driver will each
        receive $1,500. The Legends Champion will receive $750, with the Thunder
        Car Champion getting $500 and the UCAR Champ will take home $300.</p>
        <p align="left">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Tires will be available for purchase
        at the track during this event.&nbsp; In&nbsp;case&nbsp;of rain,
        Practice Day will be held on Sunday, April 21st.</p>
        <p align="left">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Anyone wishing to work at the I-94
        Raceway can stop by, fill out an application and interview for positions
        during Practice Day at the track.</p>
        <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; For additional information and any updates, check
        the official track web site at www.i-94raceway.com.</p>
        </font>
