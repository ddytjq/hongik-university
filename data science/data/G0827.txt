<H1>The Origin of Fluctuations in the CBR</H1>
<P></P>
<P>There are a variety of competing theories for the origin of large-scale
structure and the fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background radiation.
These models fall into two categories: (1) causal topological defect models,
such as <A HREF="cs_home.html">cosmic strings</A> and textures, or (2) <A HREF="inf_home.html">inflationary scenarios</A>. </P>
<P>Examples of the different cosmic microwave fluctuation patterns that they
create are shown below for maps of a few degrees. The random features of
inflationary models and cosmic strings are quite similar, being quite distinct
from the stronger features for other defects. Experimental improvements
in the study of such small scale CMBR fluctuations should lead to discriminating
tests between these different models. </P>
<P></P>
<P><IMG SRC="images/cmbr_origin.gif" WIDTH="567" HEIGHT="592" SGI_SETWIDTH SGI_SETHEIGHT SGI_FULLPATH="/local/euterpe/epss/public_html/cosmology/images/cmbr_origin.gif"> </P>
<P></P>
<P>An example of a full sky map of fluctuations produced by cosmic strings
can be seen below. The statistical properties of this map can be directly
compared with the COBE maps and they show satisfactory agreement. Discerning
experimental tests await studies on smaller angular scales. </P>
