  <hr color="#0000FF">
  <p align="left" style="word-spacing: 0; line-height: 100%; text-indent: 0; margin: 0"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">By
  1968, the number of entrants was increased to 31 and a the more familiar group
  stage was introduced.&nbsp; As in previous world cup competitions, the home
  nation championships was doubled as qualification for the quarter final
  stage.&nbsp; We beat Wales and Northern Ireland both home and away but
  unfortunately lost at home to Scotland and only managed a draw up at Hampden
  Park.&nbsp; England still finished top of the group due to the Jocks loosing
  in Ulster and only drawing in Wales.&nbsp; The quarter finals were played on a
  home and away basis and the World Cup winners were drawn against European
  holders Spain.&nbsp; At Wembley, Bobby Charlton scored the only goal and in
  Madrid, Hunter and Peters scored to earn us an impressive 2-1 victory and a
  passage to the next round.&nbsp; The USSR and Yugoslavia joined us in Italy
  for the semi finals.&nbsp; After a 0-0 draw, the hosts beat the USSR on the
  toss of a coin in the first match (and to think, some people don't like
  penalties) whilst we lost 1-0 to Yugoslavia in the other, Italy eventually won
  the final 2-0 after a replay.&nbsp; England did however beat the USSR 2-0 with
  goals from Charlton and Hurst to finish the competition in 3rd place.</font></p>
