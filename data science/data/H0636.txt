					<h3>1 - 10 of 142</h3><p><strong><a href="http://www.academicinfo.net/biotech.html">Academic Info</a></strong><br />Academic Info, an independent Internet subject directory, is a useful gateway to quality educational resources. There are some interesting links related to biotechnology.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.iia.msu.edu/absp/">Agricultural Biotechnology Support Project (ABSP), Michigan</a></strong><br />Presentation of the Agricultural Biotechnology Support Project (ABSP) of the Institute of International Agriculture at the Michigan State University. The project aims to help developing countries to use and manage biotechnology.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agbios.com/default.asp">Agriculture and Biotechnology Strategies Inc. (AgBioS)</a></strong><br />Agriculture & Biotechnology Strategies Inc. (AgBioS), a Canadian company, consultes the agricultural biotechnology industry, government departments and producer groups. Their website offers general agricultural biotechnology related information and a huge database of articles related to biosafety, environmental impact and ethics.

</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agweb.com/">AgWeb.com</a></strong><br />AgWeb.com is decision tool in agriculture and designed to enable both producers and agri-business to gain the fullest benefit from the Internet as a business tool. All information, products and services are sorted into topic subsets. Daily news.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.mbb.yale.edu/acb/">Alternative Careers in Biosciences Homepage</a></strong><br />This interesting site at Yale provides resources for graduate students and postdoctoral fellows who may be interested in pursuing careers that differ from the traditional routes of academic or industrial research.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.amsci.org/amsci/amsci.html">American Scientist</a></strong><br />The American Scientist is the magazine of Sigma Xi, a non-profit scientific research society. Sigma Xi awards grants annually to promising young researchers, holds forums on critical issues, science education, science policy and the public understanding of science.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.agt-info.org/">Association of Genetic Technologists (AGT)</a></strong><br />The Association of Genetic Technologists (AGT) is a non-profit organization established to promote cooperation and exchange of information among those engaged in classical cytogenetics, molecular and biochemical genetics, and to stimulate interest in genetics as a career.
</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.aba.asn.au/">Australian Biotechnology Association</a></strong><br />Homepage of the Australian Biotechnology Association, a national body of companies and individuals dedicated to the development of the Australian biotechnology industry. Special educational leaflet series produced for schools as an introduction to biotechnology and genetical engineering.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.bioscinet.bbsrc.ac.uk/">BBSRC Bioscience Network</a></strong><br />Information on the BBSRC Bioscience Network.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.iacr.bbsrc.ac.uk/notebook/courses/guide/index.html">Beginner's guide to Molecular Biology</a></strong><br />This Beginner's guide to molecular biology offers useful links and information on molecular biology for students and course material for teachers. </p>
