				<p class="indent20"><font class="normaltour"><script language="javascript">writeToursMenu (163)</script><b>India won the toss and elected to field. England scored 250-7, setting India a victory target of 251. India were all out for 234<br><br></b>Teams:<br>India: Ganguly (capt), Tendulkar, Laxman, Mongia, Sehwag, Badani, Ratra, Kumble, H Singh, Agarkar, Srinath<br>England: Hussain (capt), Trescothick, Knight, Vaughan, Collingwood, Hollioake, Flintoff, Snape, Foster, Gough, Hoggard<br><br><b>England innings<br></b>Nasser Hussain&#8217;s abysmal luck with the toss continued at a full house Barabati Stadium this morning. Ganguly opted to field and England&#8217;s openers Nick Knight and man-of-the-moment Marcus Trescothick took to the crease.<br><br>Whilst India&#8217;s line-up remained unchanged from the team that beat England on Saturday, the tourists drafted in all-rounder Ben Hollioake for Ashley Giles. It was widely expected that Graham Thorpe, having recovered from illness, would reclaim his place in the side, yet was surprisingly omitted. On an indifferent pitch that favored the bowlers early on, Knight and Trescothick made a breezy start, quickly reaching a run-rate of five per over. Srinath and Agarkar opened the bowling for the hosts and despite conceding a high run-rate, hinted at an imminent breakthrough.<br><br>Then in the fifth over, with the scored on 24, Marcus Trescothick pulled an Ajit Agarkar delivery high to Dinesh Mongia who took and easy catch. With the left-hander starting to find his rhythm, the wicket arrived at a crucial time and was followed in quick succession by Nick Knight after scoring just 14. Srinath struck this time, Knight miscuing a short ball that lofted high and into the hands of Harbhajan Singh.<br><br>India were gaining in confidence when Ganguly decided to introduce himself and Kumble into the attack. Hussain appeared to relish the new partnership, producing some classy stroke play with Michael Vaughan proving more than an able partner.<br><br>Two key moments swung in the tourist&#8217;s favor, suggesting that an element of luck had finally returned to Hussain&#8217;s men. The captain edged a shot to the slip fieldsman Laxman who was unlucky not to make amends for his dismal fielding performance last week, the ball scrambling past him.<br><br>Hussain was then very nearly run out after some good work by the young wicket-keeper Ajay Ratra. The English skipper was judged in but the decision could have gone either way.<br><br>However, the captain&#8217;s luck didn&#8217;t last. A rush of blood to the head saw Hussain launch the ball skywards after Indian skipper Ganguly bowled a teasing delivery. It was an unnecessary shot and was easily caught by Agit Agarkar at mid-on. To compound the skipper&#8217;s misery, he was just four runs short of his half-century.<br><br>Vaughan was fortunate to avoid being taken by Agarkar two overs later, sweeping the ball high into the air only to see the fielder fumble the chance. Vaughan built on his luck, fashioning an impressive 50 off 60 balls.<br><br>England went into kamikaze mode after the drinks interval. With the help of a bemusing decision by the third umpire, new batsman Collingwood escaped another scare. Collingwood pulled a Tendulkar delivery to the boundary and it was clearly caught by the fielder and as he fell over the boundary threw the ball back into play. The third umpire though otherwise and a six was given.<br><br>England were not quite so lucky next time, Vaughan attempting a reverse sweep and opting for a quick single when it was never a viable option. Vaughan was run out and left the field with a credible innings of 63.<br><br>Collingwood continued to fashion a tidy score and eventually reached 50 to help stabilize the tourist&#8217;s hopes of building a respectable target. Collingwood remained solid as his teammates fell around him. <br><br>Flintoff lasted all of nine balls; the hard-hitting all-rounder completely missing Harbhajan&#8217;s straightforward delivery. The Indian spinners then took the game to England, frustrating the tourists with some incisive bowling. <br><br>Hollioake marked a dismal return to the international arena, just when England needed him to hold the middle order together. Kumble proved his undoing with a lovely ball that the all-rounder completely misjudged and removed him for a duck.<br><br>England continued to make steady progress with Collingwood forging a productive partnership with Jeremy Snape. However the Indian bowlers continued to restrict the tourist&#8217;s progress with Srinath in particular bowling consistently good length and line.<br><br>Snape was eventually run out on 22 by Agarkar, after he slipped on the crease. The 21-year-old James Foster saw out the remaining of the innings with Collingwood who finished on 71 from 78 balls.<br><br>England scored 250-7 and set India a target of 251 to win.<br><br><br><b>India innings<br></b>India started their innings in commanding fashion, Sourav Ganguly and Sachin Tendulkar hitting a host of boundaries off the duel batting attack of Darren Gough and Matthew Hoggard. Hoggard looked the more comfortable of the two batsman, with Gough enduring a torrid time at the hands of Tendulkar, who was picking him off with ease.<br><br>Against the run of play and with India starting to pull away, England struck. Hoggard bowled a full delivery to Ganguly that nicked behind for Nick Knight to catch.<br><br>Although the skipper&#8217;s dismissal was a blow to the hosts, Tendulkar continued to show glimpses of devastating form. In one brutal over, the Little Master hit Gough for two consecutive fours &#8211; a humbling experience so early on in the innings and he was promptly taken off for Andrew Flintoff.<br><br>England will also rue a number of missed catches &#8211; including the vital wicket of Tendulakar. Tendulkar got a thick edge on a Hoggard delivery, only for the bowler to see the ball dropped by Trescothick. Excruciating for England, history almost repeated itself the very next over.<br><br>In what was Flintoff&#8217;s first over, new batsman Dinesh Mongia clipped his delivery down the leg-side only to see Knight parry the ball. To compound England&#8217;s bad luck, Mongia was only on two at the time.<br><br>Hollioake was brought on to slow down India&#8217;s relentless pursuit of 251, bowling a clever mixture of slow and medium-paced balls. Snape was also drafted in, bowling a neat opening over.<br><br>Just when there was no foreseeable sign of dislodging Tendulkar from the crease, England got the break they so desperately required, albeit in a somewhat fortuitous manner. <br><br>Hollioake bowled to Mongia who drove a shot straight down the pitch, only for the bowler to palm it onto the stumps. Tendulkar was a foot out of his crease and was promptly dismissed. England may have to rely on such good fortune if they are to snatch an improbable victory.<br><br>England, sensing an opening, then exploited it cruelly. Up stepped Mongia who cut Snape square to the off-side and went for a single. At the other end, Laxman was sluggish to the point of pedestrian, and was promptly stumped after some good work from Collingwood and wicketkeeper Foster. Laxman walked on three.<br><br>Virender Sehwag was the next to depart, rashly over-hitting a Gough delivery when a more conservative shot would have sufficed. Knight was on hand to help Gough claim his first wicket of the afternoon. India were wobbling and England could sense it.<br><br>India continued to falter and Mongia was the next batsman to make the long walk back to the pavilion, agonisingly stranded on 49. Hitting Collingwood down the leg side, he opted to race for two runs. Diving for the crease, his bet was well inside when Foster took the bails, but he had failed to ground it. The third umpire agreed and to his sheer disbelief, Mongia walked.<br><br>The onslaught failed to cease. Hemang Badani launched the ball skywards after miscuing a Hollioake slow ball for Flintoff to make an easy catch. However, hope of a famous victory was short-lived.<br><br>England again stole the initiative when Anil Kumble was caught by Collingwood off Gough.
