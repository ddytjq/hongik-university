            <p><span class="lefttext11bold">Your Tax Liability<br>
              </span>Before you can calculate what your income from your property 
              will be after taking into account all necessary expenditure, you 
              should recognise that the profits from renting property are taxable. 
              However you will be able to offset some of th costs you incur as 
              a landlord against the tax.</p>
            <p><span class="lefttext11bold">Case Study</span><br>
              Brian has decided to invest in a buy to let property. he has taken 
              advice from a letting agent and found a suitable property to let 
              out for a rental income of &pound;600 per month. he will be liable 
              for tax on the income received from the property but he can offset 
              the mortgage payments and other costs to reduce his liability.</p>

      <span class="blocktext"><br>
      </span><span class="lefttext12bold">Product Description</span><span class="blocktext"><br>
      <br>
      When you buy a property to let out, you are becoming a landlord. Owning 
      investment property is not like owning your own home, you are effectively 
      running a small business.<br>
      You will need to find a minimum 25% deposit and retal income must be 125% 
      to 130% of the interest payments on the mortgage (to cover mortgage costs 
      of &pound;400 a month a rental income of at least &pound;500 a month will 
      be required). You will also be required to produce valid tenancy agreements 
      or a letter from a local letting agency confirming the likely rent.<br>
      As well as your mortgage payments you will need to pay for buildings insurance, 
      maintenance costs and consider contents cover if your property is furnished. 
      You will need to ensure that you can cover your mortgage payments for periods 
      when you are receiving no rental income because the property is empty or 
      the tenants have fallen behind with their payments. Also consider how you 
      would cover interest rate rises increasing your mortgage payments and you 
      not being able to increase rent from your tenants immediately.<br>
