<P><font face="Verdana,Geneva,Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif" size="-1">Dear Adult Riders,</p>  <p>Your Adult rider program has
offered you 2 clinics already - Ralph Hill clinic in January and Jim Graham
clinic in April. We have one more clinic with Julie Burns  June 9- 10 at
Poplar Place in Hamilton Ga. If you are interested please contact me. Cost
will be around $125 for adult rider members this includes stabling. We will
also have a dinner and a lecture on Saturday evening and catered lunches. So, come
get some excellent instuction and have fun with the adults after we ride. This
is a new course and The Stegmans have graciously said we may use their
beautiful new facility. If you are not a member of the Area III Adult Rider
program just send me $20 to join.</p> <p>The Chronicle of the Horse Adult team
champs will be at the Virginia Horse Trials Lexington Va November 3-4. This is
where a lot of you adult riders really shine and last year we brought home
the blue ribbon in Preliminary and Novice teams. We need to go to Virginia
and meet the challengers bring home a clean sweep of blue in Novice  Training
and Preliminary. Join us in Virginia for the fun. We don't care if you just
want to have fun on a team or that you want to be a serious competiter we
have spots for all level of riding the most important thing is to enjoy ones
self then lets bring home the blue. ok? Let me know if you are interested in
going to Virginia we may have enough funds that the Adult rider program can
pay for your stall at the champioships.</p> <p>Hope everyone is having a, safe and,
successful season</p> <p>Patte

</P>  
<P>The Adult Team Championship (ATC) is a wonderful opportunity for Adult Riders to 
experience riding for their Area and Team in a Championship setting. Area III has been 
very sucessful in years past. We need to send teams to defend our winning titles. This 
show is also to be a fun event for Asult Riders so we send "fun" teams as well. We need 
riders that just want to meet other adults and have fun on a challenging course and we 
need serious Adult Riders to bring home the Blue. More than anything though, we need 
to have fun loving riders who want to represent Area III and enjoy the competition.  
Let me know if you are interested in the clinics. It's first come- your check is your 
reservation.</P> 
<P>Best to all for this new millenium.</P>  
<P>—-Patte Clement</P>

<P><b>Chronicle of the Horse Adult Team Championships</b></P>
<P>The Chronicle of the Horse Adult Team Championship will be at the Tryon Horse Trials Sept. 30 and Oct. 1, 2000 . Area III Adult Riders need to be present. Read the Omnibus to check on your elegibility. We need novice, training, and preliminary riders to represent Area III.<P>
<P>How to be an Area III Adult Team Rider</P>
<ul>
<li>join the Area III adult rider program</li>
<li>want to have fun and meet new adult riders</li>
<li>want to win the blue for your team</li>
<li>want to ride to have fun - let the ribbons come as they may - no pressure</li>
</ul>
<P>We do like to take the blue ribbon but the real object is to meet other adults that ride from all over the country and to have a good time. Then we can come home with the Gold. Adult riders put a lot of dollars, time and energy into their horses, there should be a reward, be it a good time or a ribbon for your best achievements. The Adult Rider Championships are for everyone. So let me know if you can go and ride on a team. Make up your own Team and ride for Area III or I will place you on a Team. If you are competitive or not we have a place for you. We need to plan ahead so let me know right away. Write me at: 200 Jabo Drive, Killen, AL, 35645 or e-mail chuckdeez@aol.com or fax at (256) 757-0704.</P>
