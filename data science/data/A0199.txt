		  <p class="contentsubhead">Glossary of Terms</p>
		    <table border=0 cellspacing=0 cellpadding=0 width=100%>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Acceptance<br>
                  of<br>
                  Contract</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The point at which the buyer and seller have committed themselves to the deal</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Annual<br>
                  Percentage<br>
                  Rate</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The total cost of a loan, taking into account all costs and fees, expressed as a rate of charge. This allows the Customer to compare like with like when comparing offers from different lenders</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Chattels</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The removable items included in the sale of property eg. carpets, blinds, light fittings, TV aerials. The term does not include fixtures and fittings</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Commission</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The fee payable to an Estate Agent for selling a property. The fee must be paid by the person authorising the Agent to act, usually the vendor</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Completion Day</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The day you become the official owner of the property and can move into your new home</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Conveyancing</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The legal work involved in the buying and selling process</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Deposit</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  An amount of money placed in trust as evidence of commitment to buy. If the purchase is completed it will be applied towards the purchase price</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Fixtures and<br>
                  Fittings</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  Things that are affixed to the property and generally cannot be removed without causing damage to the property. They cannot be removed when a property is sold unless the contract permits it. The vendor must generally make good any damage caused to the property by the removal</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Freehold</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  If you have the freehold of property or land, you own it indefinitely</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Ground Rent</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The annual fee a leaseholder pays a freeholder</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Joint Sole<br>
                  Agency</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  You instruct two Agents to sell your property in co-operation with each other and they share the commission regardless of which one actually makes the sale</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Lease</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  A contract by which the freeholder of a property allows someone else to occupy it under certain conditions and for a given length of time</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Leasehold</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The ownership of a lease</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Lessor</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The person who grants a lease</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Mortgage</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  A legal document which pledges goods or property as security for repayment of a loan</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Mortgagee</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  An institution that grants a Home Loan to buy a property</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Mortgagor</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  Someone who takes out a Home Loan to buy a property</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Mortgage Guarantee Insurance</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  An additional charge for loans which are above 80% of the cost or valuation of the Property, whichever is lower, to cover against the higher risk involved with loans of this size</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Multiple Agency</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  You instruct several Estate Agencies to sell your property and the Agent which makes the sale gets the commission</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Right of Way</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  A person may have the right to cross your property to gain access to their own property or there may be a general pathway across the land</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Sole Agency</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  You instruct one estate Agent only to sell your property, normally for a restricted period</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Stamp Duty</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  Government Tax on property purchases, and applies to purchases of over &#163;60,000 as follows:
                  <p></p>
                  <table border=0 cellspacing=0 cellpadding=0 width=100%>
                    <tr valign="top">
                      <td width=100 align=left class="contenttextbold"><u>Price</u></td>
                      <td width=100 allign=left class="contenttextbold"><u>Duty Payable</u></td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr valign="top">
                      <td width=100 align=left class="contenttext">&#163;60,000 or less</td>
                      <td width=100 allign=left class="contenttext">Nil</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr valign="top">
                      <td width=100 align=left class="contenttext">&#163;60,000 to &#163;250,000</td>
                      <td width=100 allign=left class="contenttext">1%</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr valign="top">
                      <td width=100 align=left class="contenttext">&#163;250,000 to &#163;500,000</td>
                      <td width=100 allign=left class="contenttext">3.0%</td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr valign="top">
                      <td width=100 align=left class="contenttext">over &#163;500,000</td>
                      <td width=100 allign=left class="contenttext">4.0%</td>
                    </tr>
                  </table>
                </td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Title</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The legal right to ownership of the property</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Title Deeds</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  Documents showing who owns a property</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Total Amount<br>
                   Payable</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  The total cost of repaying a loan over the term</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Transfer Deed</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  Purchase document which transfers ownership from the seller to the buyer</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Valuation</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  A written report by a registered valuer, showing their opinion of the value of the property</td>
              </tr>
              <tr>
                <td valign="top" class="contenttextbold"><br>
                  Vendor</td>
                <td class="contenttext"><br>
                  A person who offers a property for sale</td>
              </tr>
