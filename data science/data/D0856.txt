<FONT COLOR="#FFFFFF" face="sans-serif">I think the browse-through traffic is pretty small, but then I think that ALL of them are that way, except some parts of MP3.com. Even there, I get the feeling that much of their big numbers there aren't so much from Joe Q. Public wandering around as it is from the artists themselves. I think the most meaningful results will always come from promoting off-site, and sending everyone directly to your page. We know how meaningless and unhelpful the "charts" can be in all of these kind of places, since there's no controls of any kind placed on appropriateness of genre or the quality of both performance and recording. To me it all comes down to who's got the cleanest mechanisms and interface for both making a good-looking, informative page, and delivering the "goods" with the least hassle.</FONT><BR>
