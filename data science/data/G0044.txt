<H1><U>WebPages, Outreach Sites, Tutorials</U></FONT></H1>

<DL>
<DT><A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/">Imagine the Universe</A>
from the High Energy Astrophysics Science Archive Research Center at
NASA Goddard Space Flight Center is intended for High School students 
and above with two levels of information geared toward High-Energy 
Astronomy.  Includes
<A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/science/science.html">Imagine 
Science</A> with sections on astronomical objects from stars to quasars,
<A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/sats_n_data/sats_n_data.html"">
Satellite Missions</A>, a
<A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/teachers/teachers_corner.html">
Teachers' Corner</A>,
<A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/ask_astro/ask_an_astronomer.html">
Ask a NASA Scientist</A>, and a 
<A HREF="http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/dictionary.html">
Dictionary</A> of astronomical terms.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://spacelink.msfc.nasa.gov">NASA Spacelink</A>
<DD>NASA's Internet System for Education incl. education resources
directory, instruction materials, lesson plans, curriculum guides,
software, mission information & images.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www.windows.umich.edu">Windows on the Universe</A>
<DD>University of Michigan site with Solar System materials, astronomy and 
space science references and images aimed at a variety of levels, teachers' 
resources, links to data sources, and news items.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://tommy.jsc.nasa.gov/~woodfill/SPACEED/SEHHTML/seh.html"> 
Space Educator's Handbook.</A> 
<DD>NASA space education resources including Space Movies, Science Fiction as 
a teaching tool, Space Calendar, and other educational resources dealing with
space exploration and science. Also includes downloadable versions of the
files for Macintosh and PCs (ToolBook/Windows).
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www.intothecosmos.com/">A Wormhole in the Cosmos</A>
<DD>News, resources, space images on topics of astronomical interest.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www.stsci.edu/exined/">Explorations in
Education</A> - Space Telescope Science Institute
<DD>
Electronic picturebooks compiled HST and other Space images in Mac
hyperstack format.  Stored at the <A HREF="http://oposite.stsci.edu/pubinfo">
Space Telescope Science Institute</A> along with a number of other 
<A HREF="http://oposite.stsci.edu/pubinfo/edugroup/educational-activities.html">
educational activities</A>.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://hou.lbl.gov/">Hands on Universe</A> - Lawrence Berkeley Lab
<DD>DOE/NSF funded project for high school students to request imaging 
observations from the Leuschner Observatory 30" telescope for educational 
research.  Non participants may download FITS format images.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/StarChild/">The StarChild
Project</A> - NASA/GSFC Learning Center for K-12 Astronomers.
<DD> Dual level introduction to the Solar System, the Universe, Space Stuff 
and more.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www-hpcc.astro.washington.edu/" </A>University of
Washington Hypermedia Web Server </A>
<DD> 
Includes Cosmological Simulations, and other materials from the UW HPCC/ESS 
Project (including a page of 
<A HREF="http://www-hpcc.astro.washington.edu/scied/">
science and mathematics education resources</A>).
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www2.ari.net/home/odenwald/cafe.html">Astronomy Cafe</A>
conducted by NASA Astronomer, Dr. Sten Odenwald answers questions about 
Astronomy and what it's like to be an Astronomer.
<P> 
<DT><A HREF="http://www.badastronomy.com/">Bad Astronomy</A> takes well aimed 
shots at the misuse of Astronomy &amp; Physics in the media and other popular 
culture.  A great way to challenge students' assumptions about the Universe 
and put them on the right track.
<P>
<DT><A HREF="http://www.eia.brad.ac.uk/btl/">Bradford Robotic Telescope 
Observatory</A>
<DD> 
Observatory exploration, multimedia guide to Stars and Galaxies.
