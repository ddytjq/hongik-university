<P>
<FONT SIZE="+1" FACE="HELVETICA, ARIAL, SAN_SERIF" COLOR="#000000"></FONT>Graphics from <cite><B>Essential Cell Biology</B></cite> are uploaded into the Graphics Gallery for <a href="../../AB/GG/">online viewing</A> and for <a href="../../AB/GG/garland_PDFs/gar_PDF_indx.html" onClick="window.open('', 'garlandList', 'width=400,height=450,resizable,scrollbars,toolbar')" TARGET="garlandList">printing</A>. These full-color schematics have been meticulously constructed specifically for students taking a basic course. Both the text and illustrations are known for their clarity. A <A HREF="../../AB/GG/garland_PDFs/gar_PDF_indx.html" onClick="window.open('', 'garlandList', 'width=400,height=450,resizable,scrollbars,toolbar')" TARGET="garlandList">list</A> of Adobe Acrobat (.pdf) versions of each image has been provided to make it easy to print handouts and overheads.

<p>
<b>Genetic illustrations</b> from the <strong><A HREF="http://www.nhgri.nih.gov/" TARGET="gg">National Human Genome Research Institute (NHGRI)</A></strong> are now included in the Graphics Gallery for <a href="../../AB/GG/">online viewing</A> and for <a href="../../AB/GG/nhgri_PDFs/nhgri_PDF_indx.html" onClick="window.open('', 'nhgriList', 'width=400,height=450,resizable,scrollbars,toolbar')" TARGET="nhgriList">printing</A>. These illustrations are provided by the NHGRI's <A HREF="http://www.nhgri.nih.gov/DIR/VIP/" TARGET="gg">Office of Science Education and Outreach (OSEO)</A>, established to increase the working knowledge of human genetics and genetic technologies among the general public, health care professionals, media, educators and students. They also provide a <A HREF="http://www.nhgri.nih.gov/DIR/VIP/Glossary/" TARGET="gg">"Talking Glossary of Genetic Terms"</A>, and <A HREF="http://www.nhgri.nih.gov/DIR/VIP/Learning_Tools/research_technique.html" TARGET="gg">"Research Technique Fact Sheets"</A> to aid in the understanding of these illustrations and human genetics. A <a href="../../AB/GG/nhgri_PDFs/nhgri_PDF_indx.html" onClick="window.open('', 'nhgriList', 'width=400,height=450,resizable,scrollbars,toolbar')" TARGET="nhgriList">list</A> of Adobe Acrobat (.pdf) versions of each image has been provided to make it easy to print handouts and overheads.

<P>

<p>
<strong><A HREF="AEPC/SEP/">Fred Hutchinson C R C Science Education
Partnership</A><BR>pBLU TRANSFORMATION LAB</strong>, written as a
scenario in which students working as teams are challenged to improve
the efficiency of bacterial transformation, was produced by the Fred
Hutchinson Cancer Research Center working with local teachers.</P>

</li><LI>
<p>

<strong><A HREF="/HE/CDC/mediasharp/">MediaSharp,</A></strong> a collaborative project sponsored by the <A HREF="http://www.cdc.gov">Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)</A>, helps young people critically evaluate different ways that media normalize and glamorize unhealthy behaviors.   Focusing on two widely promoted products, alcohol and tobacco, MediaSharp activities provide teachers with background materials and encourage them to adapt the materials to other health areas.  These activities give students the tools to analyze how these products are promoted in marketing, entertainment, and news. </p>

a number
of frequently asked questions about the science, potential benefits, and
potential risks of gene testing.
