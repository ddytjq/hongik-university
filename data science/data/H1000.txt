        <h1 align="center"><b><font size="+3">On-Blot, <i>In Situ </i>Digest</font></b></h1>
      </td>
      <td width="121" height="9" align="center" bordercolor="#FFFFFF" bgcolor="#FFFFCC">
        &nbsp;
      </td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td width="560" height="9" align="center" bordercolor="#FFFFFF" bgcolor="#FFFFFF">&nbsp;</td>
      <td width="121" height="9" align="center" bordercolor="#FFFFFF" bgcolor="#FFFFCC">&nbsp;</td>

    <tr>
      <td width="571" height="53" align="left" bordercolor="#FFFFFF" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" rowspan="9" valign="top">

<b>This was procedure adapted from the following article:</b><br>
<font size="2">Wong, S. C., Grimley, C., Padua, A., Bourell, J. H., and Henzel,
W. J. (1993) Peptide Mapping of 2-D Gel Proteins by Capillary HPLC. Tech. In
Prot. Chem. IV, 371-378</font>
<p><font size="4">Reagents:</font></p>
<ol>
  <li>Methanol</li>
  <li>Acetonitrile</li>
  <li>Water</li>
  <li>DTT, Clealands reagent</li>
  <li>Tris-Base</li>
  <li>HCl</li>
  <li>Promega modified trypsin</li>
  <li>Iodoacetic acid (IAA) <font size="1"><a href="../../Card/cmc/notes.htm">(see
    tips on this reagent, step 4 on this link)</a></font></li>
  <li>PVP,&nbsp; polyvinylpyrolodone&nbsp;</li>
</ol>
<p><font size="4">Solutions and Stocks:</font></p>
<ol>
  <li>1 M DTT <font size="1">(in water, keep this stock frozen at -20 캜)</font></li>
  <li>0.5 M Tris-Base / 6M guanidine-HCl / 5 mM EDTA / 10% acetonitrile / 7 mM
    DTT /&nbsp; pH 8.5 with HCL <font size="1">(note: pH with HCl before adding
    the acetonitrile, starts out around pH 10. Add DTT fresh to a portion of
    this solution right before reduction)</font></li>
  <li>200 mM IAA in 0.5 M NaOH make fresh each time</li>
  <li>10% Acetonitrile</li>
  <li>0.25% PVP&nbsp; in 0.1% acetic acid</li>
  <li>100 mM Tris-Base, pH 8.0, 10 % acetonitrile</li>
</ol>
<p><font size="4">Procedure:</font></p>
<ol>
  <li>Cut out the band or spot as narrowly as possible, avoid touching the blot,
    wear gloves.</li>
  <li><font size="3">Wet excised blot with 1-3 킠 of methanol in a small
    tube.&nbsp; </font><font size="1">(the membrane is very hydrophobic and must
    be wetted with methanol.&nbsp; Do not let the membrane dry out during the
    procedure.)</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Reduction.&nbsp; Reduce with 100 킠 of Solution 2 at 45
    캜 for 60 min.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Cool and add 10 킠 of Solution 3, let react for 20 min. in
    the dark.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Wash the blot 3X with 500 킠 Solution 4.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">To block the blot incubate in 200 킠 of Solution 5 for 20
    minutes on a room temp. shaker.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Wash the blot 3X with 500 킠 Solution 4.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Transfer blot to new tube.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Wash the blot 3X with 500 킠 Solution 4. </font><font size="1">(this
    extensive wash and transfer is necessary to remove excess PVP 360, which
    will give UV and mass spectral interference if not removed.)</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Digest with 0.2 킽 Promega modified trypsin in 100 킠 of
    Solution 6 for 24 hours at 37 캜.</font></li>
  <li><font size="3">Concentrate on a SpeedVac to 20 탅, inject 5 킠 onto a
    capillary column for LC/MS analysis.</font></li>
</ol>
