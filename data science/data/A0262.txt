    <p>Good Morning, CALM RETURNS TO CAUTIOUS MARKETS</p>

There was yet more intervention by the Bank of Japan yesterday as it sold yen and bought dollars to try and weaken the yen in an effort to protect the struggling economic recovery in the country, but there was little lasting effect. Comments from George Bush in which he raised doubts over Washington's foreign exchange policy by saying the dollar would seek its level based on market forces also helped keep the dollar lower despite some positive data from the US showing durable goods orders rose 0.6% in May.The stockmarkets felt the backlash of the WorldCom scandal as it shattered confidence. The minutes of the meeting are due later today and should provide more clues about the state of the US recovery. Other data due today includes US GDP Q1 data expected to be 5.6% q/q.
