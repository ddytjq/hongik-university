            <p><b><span lang=EN-GB
style='font-size:12.0pt;font-family:"Times New Roman"'><del cite="mailto:Ewen%20Gilmour" datetime="2001-09-06T09:47"></del></span></b><span class="font">Chaucer 
              Holdings PLC (&quot;Chaucer&quot;) is pleased to announce that it 
              has signed heads of agreement for the acquisition by Chaucer of 
              the whole of the issued share capital of CNA’s Lloyd’s corporate 
              member company, CNA Corporate Capital Limited (&quot;CNACCL&quot;).</span></p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font">The consideration for the 
              acquisition will be satisfied by the issue to CNA of 8.4 million 
              new ordinary shares in Chaucer at 69p per share, representing a 
              premium of 10% to the closing mid-market price of 63p per share 
              on 7 September 2001.  As a consequence of the proposed transaction, 
              CNA will become a significant shareholder in Chaucer, holding 9.1% 
              of its share capital as enlarged by the issue of the new ordinary 
              shares. </p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font">A senior member of the 
              CNA management team will be appointed as a non-executive director 
              of Chaucer. </p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font">On acquisition, CNACCL 
              will have net assets (consisting of cash and/or investments) of 
              approximately £5.8 million, which will be available to provide additional 
              funds at Lloyd’s to participate on Chaucer’s three ‘in house’ syndicates 
              (587, 1084 and 1096) beginning with the 2002 year of account.  Reinsurance 
              arrangements will be put in place to ensure that CNACCL’s net assets 
              cannot be eroded by virtue of its exposure to 2001 and prior years 
              of account underwriting.  Chaucer will also pay additional consideration 
              to CNA which will be referable to the taxation benefits it derives 
              from utilising CNACCL’s existing tax losses.</p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font">The transaction will also 
              result in Chaucer Syndicates Limited acquiring the right to manage 
              Syndicate 1229 from CNA Underwriting Agencies Limited for which 
              it will receive a fee.  It is anticipated that Syndicate 1229’s 
              2001 year of account will be closed into Syndicate 1096, subject 
              to the usual requirements relating to reinsurance to close.  It 
              is expected that the majority of CNA’s Syndicate 1229 staff will 
              join the Chaucer Group.</p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font">The transaction will be 
              conditional, inter alia, upon receipt of all requisite Lloyd’s and 
              other regulatory consents and the satisfactory conclusion of due 
              diligence by all parties.  Further details of the terms of the transaction 
              will be released once definitive agreements have been entered into 
              between the parties.</p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>Bernard L. Hengesbaugh, 
              Chairman and CEO of CNA, said:-</b></span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">“We are delighted to be maintaining 
              our presence in the Lloyd’s market by the acquisition of a strategic 
              stake in Chaucer.  We look forward to working with Chaucer at this 
              exciting stage of the insurance cycle.”</span></p>
            <p style='text-align:justify' class="font"><b>Ewen 
              Gilmour, Managing Director of Chaucer, commented:</b></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">“The decision of CNA Financial 
              Corporation to take a significant shareholding in Chaucer reflects 
              its confidence in the Lloyd’s market and in particular in our Company. 
              The £5.8 million of funds made available to us as a result of our 
              acquisition of CNA Corporate Capital together with the £20 million 
              we successfully raised in March means Chaucer is extremely well 
              placed to respond to the many underwriting opportunities that are 
              emerging.”</span> </p>
