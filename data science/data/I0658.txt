          <td valign="top" width="100%" colspan="3"> <b class="newsheading">ENGLAND 
            2 ALBANIA 0</b>
<p><span class="engfanz">The ecstasy surrounding the famous victory 
              in Munich on Saturday evening carried itself all the way to Newcastle's 
              St James Park as Sven Goran Eriksson's side continued their attempts 
              for automatic qualification for next years World Cup, but it was 
              far from easy.<br>
              <br>
              Eriksson talked before the game of complacency and not to expect 
              a hat full of goals against the team bottom of group 9. Albania 
              have travelled well in their recent qualification games, only losing 
              2-1 in Finland and Germany and 1-0 in Greece. England struggled 
              for three-quarters of the game in Albania in March and did well 
              to win 3-1.<br>
              <br>
              England playing in an all white kit, and for the first time in 33 
              games started the game with the same eleven. Albania's starting 
              eleven included seven players that started the 3-1 defeat in Tirana 
              six months ago.<br>
              <br>
              England could have had a penalty on three minutes when Owen fell 
              in the box after colliding with the Albanian keeper Strakosha but 
              the referee waved away the appeals.&nbsp;<br>
              <br>
              Nick Barmby missed a great chance on 7 minutes when he met a Beckham 
              cross unchallenged but he put his header wide, but should have at 
              least hit the target. England had another chance on 8 minutes but 
              Heskey could not get a touch onto Barmby's low ball across the goal.<br>
              <br>
              England just launched attack after attack in the opening 10 minutes, 
              but could not find the opening goal.<br>
              <br>
              The buzz of the opening 10 minutes fizzled out and England's next 
              real chance came on 27 minutes when Rio Ferdinand headed well over 
              from a Beckham corner. Albania were showing the expected resistance 
              now and did venture towards the England goal from time to time in 
              search of a shock goal.<br>
              <br>
              England discovering that they would after all have to patient kept 
              chipping away at the Albania defence, with Owen creating a few half 
              chances from inside the box.<br>
              <br>
              Beckham caused the Albania keeper some worries with a long range 
              free-kick just before half-time.<br>
              <br>
              The deadlock was finally broken on 44 minutes Scholes playing a 
              beautifully chipped through ball that found Michael Owen inside 
              the box and the Liverpool player finish was world class. Owen again 
              proving to be the man for the big occasion scoring the opening goal 
              as he did in Tirana.<br>
              <br>
              Steven Gerrard was booked in first-half injury time for a wild challenge, 
              but Owen's goal did steady the nerves at the break.<br>
              <br>
              A very slow and scrappy start to the first half where England created 
              little and Emile Heskey was forced out of the game with an injury 
              to be replaced by Robbie Fowler on 55 minutes.<br>
              <br>
              Fowler should have made it two on the hour when Owen's scuffed shot 
              fell to his Liverpool team-mate who made it easy for the keeper 
              to push his effort away.<br>
              <br>
              Steve McManaman replaced Nick Barmby in the 64th minute as England 
              went in search of a second.<br>
              <br>
              Albania's best chance of the game came in the 78th minute Murati's 
              long range effort causing Seaman a few problems. The game was now 
              a mess as England struggled to find any shape and allowing Albania 
              way too much of the ball.<br>
              <br>
              Skipper Beckham went very close on 85 minutes with a long range 
              effort that was well pushed away by Strakosha, from the resulting 
              corner Owen's header was well saved.<br>
              <br>
              Robbie Fowler calmed a nervous St James turning with the ball just 
              outside the box, nutmegging a defender and chipping the ball over 
              Strakosha for a beautifully taken goal.<br>
              <br>
              Not a very good performance by England certainly suffering a hangover 
              from the exploits in Munich and the last fifteen minutes were very 
              very nervous as they held on to a narrow win.&nbsp;<br>
              <br>
              Just as Sven Goran Eriksson called for everyone not to get carried 
              away with the win over Germany we should not get too carried away 
              with this poor performance - three points thankfully in the bag, 
              and top of group 9.<br>
              <br>
              Bring on Greece…………………………..</span><br>
              <br>
            </p>
