<p align="left">
<font face="tahoma">
<font size="2">
	Start VB, after it's done loading, you should see the screen "New Project" (unless you have disabled that screen), and select Standard.EXE. After doing so you will be brought to VB's <B>development environment</b>. In the middle you have a gray window, which is what you will be developing on. It's called a <B>Form</b> (Form1 by default), on the left of that form you see a Toolbox window that has bunch of icons, which are called <B>Controls</b>. Controls are one of the things your project will consist of in it. To the right of "Form1" you should see two other windows, one is the <B>Project Explorer</b>, which holds all the forms (gray windows), and other important things we will get into later. The other window is the <B>Properties</b> window which holds the Form's and Control's properties in your project.
</p>

<p align="center">
<img src="http://www.angelfire.com/ny3/visualbasic/images/vbnewpro.jpg"><center>
</p> 


<p align="center">
<font face="tahoma">
<font color="blue">
<font size="2">
<img src="http://www.angelfire.com/ny3/visualbasic/images/tip.jpg">Tip: This may look confusing at first, but believe me it gets easier once you have spent a little time playing around with everything there.
</font>
</font>
</p>

<p align="left">
<font face="tahoma">
<font size="2">
	The Form is where everything happens. You add controls and codes. Lets look at what the property window can do for Form1.
<p align="center">
<img src="http://www.angelfire.com/ny3/visualbasic/images/vbde.jpg"
</p> 

<p align=left>
<font face="tahoma">
<font size="2">
<font color="blue">
	Properties for a Form</font> (switching them will switch the way Form1 looks/acts etc...)
</p>

<p align=left>
<font face="tahoma">
<font size="2">
<font color="red">
	BackColor</font> - Switches the background color of the form, which is gray<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	BorderStyle</font> - Determines how the form's border looks, and if it's sizable or not<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	Caption</font> - Determines what the top of the form says, by default it says "Form1" <Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	Enabled</font> - Determines if the form is usable/notusable<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	Font</font> - Determines the Form's Font (note you won't be able to change the forms captions font with this)<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	Icon</font> - Determines the Form's Icon, by default they have a form icon on the form<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	KeyPreview</font> - Determines if clicking a button on the keyboard does something to the app (ex; when an app is running the user clicks the Esc button on the keyboard, it closes the app) which requires Codes<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	MaxButton</font> - Determines if you want the form to be able to be maximized with the Maximize button<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	MinButton</font> - Determines if you want the form to have a minimize button<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	MDIChild</font> - Determines if this form will be inside of a MDI Parent form... Like AOL<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	MousePointer</font> - What the mouse pointer looks like over the form<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	ShowInTaskbar</font> - Determines if your program shows in the windows taskbar like VB for example, and most other applications running in Windows<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	StartUpPosition</font> - Where the form starts up at run time, which you can also position in the "Form Layout" window<Br>
<Br>
<font color="red">
	Visible</font> - Determines if the form is visible or not<Br> 
</p> 

<p align=left> 
<font face="tahoma">
<font size="2">
	These are the most important properties of a form, once you get a hold of these you can and will relate better to many control's properties...
</p>
