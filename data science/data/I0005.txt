class="NewsBody"><P>Spain top the access list for the 2002/03 UEFA club competitions – and England have gained a fourth UEFA Champions League place. <BR><BR><B>Final access list </B><BR>UEFA published the final access list for the 2002/03 UEFA club competitions on Friday, as the clubs enjoy their current winter break from this season’s club competitions before they resume in February.&nbsp;<BR><BR><B>Five-season results</B><BR>The access list for all member associations for the 2002/03 campaign is based on results over the previous five seasons – from 1996/97 to 2000/01.&nbsp;<BR><BR><B>Coefficient calculations</B><BR>The coefficient calculations are based on the results obtained by all of an association’s clubs in European competitions over the past five seasons (two points for a win, one point for a draw and no points for a defeat, and half of these points for qualifying-round matches).&nbsp;<BR><BR><B>Allocation of places</B><BR>The associations’ ranking resulting from these coefficients determines the allocation of places per association for both the UEFA Champions League and UEFA Cup. <BR><BR><B>Association entries </B><BR>The access list indicates the number of clubs that each association will be allowed to enter for next year’s club competitions, as well as the entry stage when each individual club will start their campaign.&nbsp;<BR><BR><B>Switches possible</B><BR>Some switches may become necessary at the end of the season if a club wins either the UEFA Champions League or the UEFA Cup, and also qualifies at the same time for one of the two competitions via their domestic league. <BR><BR><B>UEFA CEO congratulates Spain </B><BR>UEFA Chief Executive Gerhard Aigner said: “I offer my congratulations to Spanish football for once again coming top of our club ranking list.” <BR><BR><B>Transparency guaranteed </B><BR>“This system guarantees transparency in our qualifying system for European competitions,” he added, “and ensures that qualification combines the twin measures of international and domestic success.”&nbsp;<BR><BR><B>Best wishes</B><BR>“I wish every club that dreams of European competition good luck for the new year and the second half of the season,” he added. </P>
