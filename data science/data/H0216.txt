<TD WIDTH="70%" VALIGN="TOP"><P><B>There are very few web sites specifically on adaptation and natural
selection. However nearly every one of the general evolution sites has a
section on natural selection and the reader is directed to the <A HREF=
"genevol.htm">general evolution page</A> of <I>Nearctica</I>.&nbsp;</B></P>

<P>&nbsp;</P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.sprl.umich.edu/GCL/paper_to_html/selection.html">Evolution
and Natural Selection</A>. Introduction to Global Change, University of
Michigan. This page from this great university course deals with natural
selection, selection, and adaptation.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://www.ucmp.berkeley.edu/vertebrates/flight/enter.html">Vertebrate
Flight Exhibit</A>. University of California at Berkeley Museum of Paleontology.
Learn about the evolution of flight in vertebrates and see how flight adaptations
have occurred independently (convergently) in different vertebrate lines
(e.g. birds, bats, pterodactyls). The site includes information about the
physics of flying and its evolutionary origins in different vertebrate lines.
Recommended.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://dynamics.org/~altenber/PAPERS/CAEE/">Complex Adaptations
and the Evolution of Evolvability</A>. Gunter P. Wagner and Lee Altenberg.
A paper on gene structure and how modularity of this structure can be used
as the basis of models of how complex adaptations can arise. Highly technical.</B></P>

<P><IMG SRC="../pics/globe.gif" WIDTH="20" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="BOTTOM" NATURALSIZEFLAG=
"3"> <B><A HREF="http://144.92.19.120/carroll/adaptationism/index.html">Adaptationist
Stories</A>. Jeremey C. Ahouse. A group of people got tired of &quot;Just
so Stories&quot; of human and animal characteristics caused by genes and
adaptation without the benefit of real research or experimental evidence.
They have a collected a series of such stories from newspapers and other
sources.</B></TD></TR>
