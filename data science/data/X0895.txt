			<font face=arial,helvetica size=3 color="#330000">
			<B>2002 Events</B>
			</font>
			<br>
			<br>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=1 color="#660000">
			Dates are subject to change.  The International Tennis Hall 
			of Fame is a nonprofit organization dedicated to preserving the 
			history and heritage of tennis and its champions.  For more information 
			on the International Tennis Hall of Fame & Museum call 401-849-3990</font>
			<br>
			<br>
	
	<!-- May  -->		
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Friday, May 10 
			</font>
			<br>
			
			<b>GRASS COURTS OPEN</b>
			<br>
			For over a century, the opening of the historic grass courts and the Newport 
			Casino Lawn Tennis Club at the International Tennis Hall of Fame has been one of 
			Newport's most anticipated events.  For club membership, group and individual 
			instruction or public court rental, call 401-846-0642.
			<br>
			<br>
	
	<!-- June  -->		
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Thursday, June 6
			</font>
			
			<br>
			<b>NEWPORT INTERNATIONAL FILM FESTIVAL</b>
			
			<br>
			Bring a blanket and sit "picnic-style" on the lawns of the Tennis Hall of Fame for the 
			fourth annual Newport International Film Festival.  For more information call 401-421-ARTS.
			<br>
			<br>
	
	<!-- July -->		
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			July 6-7 
			</font>
			<br>
			<b>QUALIFYING ROUNDS</b><br>
			Tennis players compete in this 2-day tournament to qualify for the 
			Miller Lite Hall of Fame Tennis Championships starting on July 8. 
			
			<br>
			<br>
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			July  8 - 14
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2>
			<br> 
			<b> "NEWPORT TENNIS WEEK"</b>
			<BR>
			
			Miller Lite Hall of Fame Tennis Championships.<br>
			Top men in professional tennis come to Newport to compete in the Miller Lite Hall 
			of Fame Tennis Championships, the ATP's only grass court event in North America.  
			Tickets are on sale now.  Call 401-849-6053 or <a href="championship/ticket_orderform.html">order online at tennisfame.com</a>.
			<br>
			<br>
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Saturday, July 13 
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 >
			<br> 
			<b>ANNUAL HALL OF FAME INDUCTION CEREMONY</b>
			<BR>
			
			Part of Newport Tennis Week, the International Tennis Hall of Fame will hold the official induction 
			ceremonies for Pam Shriver and Mats Wilander. For tickets, call 401-849-6053 or <a href="championship/ticket_orderform.html">order online at tennisfame.com</a>.
			<br>
			<br>

			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Saturday, July 20  
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 >
			<br> 
			<b>BLACK SHIPS FESTIVAL</b>
			<BR>
			
			Taiko Drum Concert<br>
			The Taiko Drummers from Japan perform on Bill Talbert Center Court.  For more information please 
			call the Japan-American Society of Rhode Island at 401-846-2720.
			<br>
			<br>
	<!-- August 2002 -->		
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Friday, August 9 
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 >
			<br> 
			<b>OPENING NIGHT OF JVC JAZZ FESTIVAL</b>
			<BR>
			
			Headliners of the Newport Jazz Festival perform at the Newport Casino, site of the first Newport 
			Jazz Festival in 1954. For information call 401-847-3700.
			<br>
			<br>
			
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			Saturday, August 24 
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 >
			<br> 
			<b>DESTINATION HEALTH</b>
			<BR>
			
			Destination Health is a day long health fair organized by Health Magazine.  
			Learn about Yoga, take part in health screenings, and enjoy cooking 
			demonstrations, music, clinics, wine tastings, health exhibitions and more. 
			<br>
			<br>
	<!-- September  -->		
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 color="#660000">
			FRIDAY, SEPTEMBER 6 
			</font>
			<font face=arial,helvetica size=2 >
			<br> 
			<b>"NEWPORT IN NEW YORK" GALA & SILENT AND LIVE AUCTION</b>
			<BR>
			
			On Friday, September 6, the Hall of Fame will hold it's 22nd Gala at the Waldorf=Astoria 
			Hotel in New York City. Join hosts Tony Trabert, Fred Stolle and John Newcombe as we salute Pam 
			Shriver & Mats Wilander, and highlight Tennis' Great Rivalries.  Many Hall of Famers, including Chris Evert &
			 Martina Navratilova will be on-hand as we remember great moments in tennis history.  For more information, 
			 please call the Event Office at (212) 843-1740.  
			<br>

						<b>Ivan Lendl: Fitness & Focus</b><br>
						The Atrium Gallery spotlights the incredible tennis career of Ivan Lendl, inducted into the Hall 
						of Fame this year. Lendl captured 8 career singles titles in Grand Slam events and reached an astounding 19 Slam singles finals. His strength, power and physical fitness enabled him to reach 8 consecutive US Open finals (1982-89), winning three, 1985, '86, '87.  The center of this new exhibit showcases these 3 US Open trophies, along with his 2 Australian Open and 3 French Open Championship trophies, the GTX Pro-T Racquet made especially for Lendl by Adidas, and a unique photograph of the 1980 Czechoslovakian Davis Cup team, after winning their first and only title. Also on display are numerous photos and personal memorabilia of Lendl's dominant career.
						<br><br>

							<b>2001 Hall of Fame Enshrinee: Mervyn Rose</b><br>
							This tribute exhibit illustrates the career of Australian Mervyn Rose, a 2001 Hall of Fame inductee. In the 1950's Rose was ranked five times in the world's Top Ten (1953, '54, '55, '57, '58) and was a member of Australia's 1951, '52 and '57 Davis Cup victories.  Among the items on view are Rose's 1957 Davis Cup trophy, 1953 Canadian Open Singles Trophy, 1954 Australian Open and Wimbledon Doubles Championship trophies, his 1957 Wimbledon Mixed Doubles Championship trophy and his 1958 French National Singles Trophy, as well as images and photographs from his remarkable career as both a player and a coach.
							<br><br>
							
							<b>Collection Focus: Vic Seixas</b>
							<br>Trophies and photos help showcase the career of American Vic Seixas, winner of the 1953 Wimbledon Singles title and the Wimbledon Mixed Doubles titles from 1953-56, U.S. Singles Champion in 1954, and member of the U.S. Davis Cup Team from 1951-1957. Collection highlights include the 1952 USLTA Men's Doubles Championship Trophy that Seixas won with 2001 Inductee Mervyn Rose, along with his U.S. National Singles Championship Trophy won in 1954. 
							<br><br>
							
							<b>Czech Legends</b>
							<br>Displays the influence of the top Czech players from Karel Kozeluh to Martina Navratilova and Ivan Lendl. Items on view include autographed tennis balls by Karel Kozeluh from 1934, the outfit worn by Jana Novotna at her 1998 Wimbledon Singles win, and the tennis racquets used by Cyril Suk and Helena Sukova in their 1996 Wimbledon Mixed Doubles Championship.
