They are not <a href="../MGW2/MG22.html">transcribed</a> into RNA.			<li>The satellite DNAs belong to the <a href="MG112282.html#High">highly repetitive</a> kinetic class of DNA sequences.		</ul>		<p>		<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">			<tr>				<td rowspan="6">					<ul>						<li>In rye molecular chromosomes, members of some highly repeated sequence families separate direct repeats of other repeated sequence families.						<li>In the diagram below, each different pattern represents a different repeat family. Some density gradient satellites are organelle DNA. Yet, the term &quot;satellite DNA&quot; is usually used as a synonym for tandemly highly repeated DNA.			<li>Repeated sequences may be <a href="MG112287.html">interspersed</a> with other repeated sequences.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other type="square">			<li>Human DNA has five satellites.			<ul>				<li>Four ( I to IV) have short reiterated sequences of less than 100 bp containing divergent GGAAT repeats.				<li>The <a href="MG1364.html">alphoid satellites</a>, also found in other primates, are made of 171 bp repeats and are A-T rich.			</ul>			<li>Human highly repeated DNA also includes SINES and LINES families, whose members are found <a href="MG112287.html">interspersed</a> among human <a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/cgi-bin/Entrez/framik?gi=455025&db=Nucleotide">globin gene</a> and other loci.			<li>In maize, the highly repeated DNA consists of remnants of various families of transposable elements (<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov:80/entrez/query.fcgi?cmd=Retrieve&db=PubMed&list_uids=10688140&dopt=Abstract">rev</a>).			<li>Other kinetically distinguished classes of DNA are the <a href="MG112285.html">moderately repetitive</a> and the <a href="MG112286.html">unique</a> DNA.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a name="bottom"></a><a href="MG112283.html">Last</a> | <a
