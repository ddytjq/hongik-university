	<td colspan="2"><br>
	<br>
	
				<P>The Seventh Annual Ontario Family Fishing Weekend is coming soon to a body of water near you! Prepare to "Catch the Fun!" and fish licence-free on the weekend of July 7-9, 2000. </P>
<P>Family Fishing Weekend was created to remind people of the recreational and economic value of fishing and the importance of sustaining and enhancing the resource for future generations. This year's focus, "Take a Friend Fishing" reflects on the value and importance of Ontario's aquatic resources. All anglers are reminded to take a friend with them to learn the art of angling during the 2000 Ontario Family Fishing Weekend. </P>
<P>For the entire weekend, 12:01 a.m. Friday July 7 through to 11:59 p.m. Sunday July 9, the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources will lift the mandatory licence requirement for all Canadian resident adult anglers. This weekend of licence-free fishing will give many people the opportunity to discover, or rediscover, one of Ontario's finest natural recreational activities. Point your browser to <A target=_blank href="http://www.familyfishingweekend.com/">www.familyfishingweekend.com</A> for more details and event information. </P>
<P>Angling is a multi-billion dollar industry in Ontario. Three million people wet lines every summer in this province! Ontario Family Fishing Weekend is a wonderful way for people to rekindle an interest in fishing, or to discover the activity for the first time, and that's the key; people who are more aware of the environment are more likely to protect the environment. </P>
<P>Anglers who fish without a licence must abide by the limits set by the province's Resident Conservation Licence. All other fees and regulations apply during the weekend and will be enforced. For details on fishing regulations and conservation rules, please consult the 2000 Recreational Fishing Regulations Summary, available at your local angling licence issuer. </P>
<P>Be on the lookout for Family Fishing Weekend activities in your area! These events include active education programs for kids and families, tips, demonstrations and guided fishing opportunities. You can call 1-800-667-1940 for event information in your area. </P>
<P>The weekend is made possible through the work of a Steering Committee which includes representatives of the Canadian National Sportfishing Foundation, Conservation Ontario, Northern Ontario Tourist Outfitters, Ontario Federation of Anglers and Hunters, Resorts Ontario and the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources. </P>
<H2>A few facts about fishing</H2>
<H3>Did you know....?</H3>
<UL>
<LI>3 million people fish in Ontario 
<LI>Anglers contribute over $3 billion to the economy each year 
<LI>Anglers fish for relaxation, food, to get away and to enjoy nature 
<LI>Ontario has about 175 different species of fish 
<LI>among the largest - lake sturgeon. The largest known lake sturgeon was netted from Lake Superior in 1922, it was 310 lbs. (140 kg.), 7ft,11in. (241.4cm) in length. 
<LI>among the smallest - fathead minnow. A "lunker" fathead will reach 2 inches (51mm). 
<LI>Most people know you can tell the age of a fish from its scales, reading them like tree rings. On old fish however, the edges of the scales wear away, and it is more accurate to count the rings in certain jaw or ear bones. 
<LI>among the oldest fish - lake sturgeon. Fish 50-80 years old are not unusual. The oldest known lake sturgeon - a 200 pounder that was 154 years old. 
<LI>rainbow smelt smell like cucumbers! 
<LI>smallmouth bass can change colour like a chameleon to match their surroundings 
<LI>fish gills are over three times more efficient than our lungs 
<LI>bass and sunfish "dads" protect their eggs and young. In June they are easily spotted in shallow water hovering over nests of clean pebbles. They are quite aggressive and easily caught. But even if quickly released, the time away from the nest will result in loss of eggs or young to hungry predators. For this reason, avoid fishing in nesting areas during this vulnerable time. 
<LI>the Ontario Federation of Anglers and Hunters established a province-wide registry to recognize the largest, legally angled fish caught in Ontario waters. Some current records: largest lake sturgeon (angled): 168 lbs.; largest lake trout: 63.1 lbs.; largest muskellunge (muskie): 65 lbs; bluegill 1.8 lbs.; rock bass: 3.0 lbs. 
<LI>similar licence-free fishing weekends occur in other Canadian provinces including Saskatchewan, Manitoba and Nova Scotia. 
<LI>83% of all fishing occurs between April and October each year. 
<LI>fish cannot survive without proper habitat: clean water, undisturbed bottom and natural shorelines 
<LI>practice proper catch and release techniques to help ensure that the fish you don't want to harvest have an excellent chance of survival </LI></UL><SMALL>
