<B>Meirhaeghe Repeats World Cup Win in Canada:<P></B>

<I>Vancouver, B.C., CAN (July 7, 2002)</I> -- <B>Filip Meirhaeghe</B> <I>(BEL, Specialized) </I>
maintained his winning form to earn a second-straight cross-country victory 
this week, as the 2000 Olympic silver medallist earned his sixth career world 
cup victory on a cloudy day in Grouse Mountain, Canada. Meirhaeghe rode a 
brilliant race to take the lead in lap three, never looking back as he beat 
<B>Christoph Sauser</B> <I>(SUI, Volvo Cannondale)</I> by 57 seconds in the seven-lap event.<P>

90 competitors were entered into Sunday afternoon's race, as cloudy and cool 
weather threatened rain, but never produced any drops during the 1 hour, 49 
minute event. Racing on one of the world's most technically challenging 
courses at Grouse Mountain, the men had to be especially wary of preserving 
their equipment and preventing flat tyres on the bumpy and rocky track. The 
race's finishing time was shorter than normal, because of the extreme nature 
of the course and the toll it took on so many riders. Only 36 riders finished 
on the lead lap.<P>

Meirhaeghe finished the first lap of the race in 3rd position, after <B>Lado 
Fumic</B> <I>(GER, Team T-Mobile)</I> took an early lead with an impressive start. 
Current World and World Cup Champion - and local favorite - <B>Roland Green </B>
<I>(CAN, Trek-Volkswagen)</I> rode in 2nd position, while <B>Roel Paulissen</B> <I>(BEL, 
Rainer-Wurz)</I> and <B>Christophe Dupouey</B> <I>(FRA, Giant)</I> rode in 4th and 5th, 
respectively. Paulissen would soon suffer two flat tyres though, and be out 
of contention along with <I>Motorex-Bianchi</I> riders <B>Julien Absalon</B> <I>(FRA)</I> (broken 
chain) and <B>Jose Hermida</B> <I>(ESP)</I> (flat tyre).<P>

Round one winner <B>Bart Brentjens</B> <I>(NED, Giant Bicycle)</I> was in 7th position at 
this point, while behind rode Sauser in 9th. Sauser had been caught behind a 
crash at the very start and lost valuable time, but worked hard to move back 
into contention. In the 3rd lap Meirhaeghe was riding at the front, but was 
trailed closely by Green, Dupouey and Brentjens, while Sauser moved into 5th 
position, followed by <B>Marco Bui</B> <I>(ITA, Lanabau/Rainer-Wurz)</I> and <B>Kashi Leuchs</B> 
<I>(NZL, Volvo-Cannondale). </I><P>

By the end of the 3rd lap Meirhaeghe has broken free of the pack, and was 23 
seconds ahead of Green, Brentjens and teammate Dupouey, as Sauser chased 
alone in 5th at :40 back. Leuchs moved past Bui to take 6th position, as the 
field produced 15 minute laps over wooden bridges, steep drops and rocky 
climbs.<P>

At the end of the 4th lap Meirhaeghe increased his lead to 35 seconds over 
the trio of Green, Dupouey and Brentjens - but Sauser was attacking hard and 
moved all the way to 2nd position a half lap later. <P>

The sixth lap began with Sauser chasing in 2nd place at :33 seconds, while 
Dupouey and Brentjens rode together at one minute, Green now riding in 5th 
place another 13 seconds back, with Leuchs closing in at 6th place.<P>

On the final lap Meirhaeghe rode steadily and maintained his lead of :41 over 
Sauser, while Brentjens powered away from Dupouey on a descent and chased at 
1:19 - Dupouey now 20 seconds behind. Leuchs made contact with Green at this 
point, just after the two came through to begin the lap. Within minutes 
Leuchs was in 5th position with Green in 6th at 2:15 back.<P>

Minutes later the race was finished, as Meirhaeghe crossed the line :57 ahead 
of Sauser, 1:38 ahead of Brentjens and 2:02 ahead of Leuchs. Dupouey had 
passed a fading Roland Green as well in the final lap, taking the final 
podium position and finishing at 2:23.<P>

With his win, Meirhaeghe maintains the overall lead ahead of #2-ranked 
Brentjens, and keeps the blue Tissot leader's jersey going into the 5th and 
final round of racing in Les Gets, France on September 8th - after the UCI 
World Championships in Kaprun, Austria at the end of August.<P>

<B>Filip Meirhaeghe, 1st Place:</B>

<BLOCKQUOTE><I>"I got the lead in the 3rd lap, but the chasers were on my wheel, so I knew I 
had to go. I felt good, but was nervous before the race with only two 
remaining. I also had a hard choice before the race, deciding between a full 
suspension and a hardtail on this course. I chose the hardtail, which wasn't 
as good on the descents but was better on the climbs. I also use more 
pressure in my tires, which is harder on the descents."</I><P>

<I>"I like longer courses, but this race was good. It's also uneven, and it's 
hard to find your rhythm because you're always changing from climbing to 
descending. If it's not the most challenging course on the world cup, it's 
close."</I></BLOCKQUOTE>

<B>Christoph Sauser, 2nd Place:</B>

<BLOCKQUOTE><I>"I felt good today, but had a bad start when I had to stop because of a crash 
in front of me at the beginning. I was last into the first descent and had to 
push really hard to move up. Filip was always up there and I just wished I 
had been up there with him. Unless he had a problem or a flat, he'd win - so 
I also rode a safe race."</I></BLOCKQUOTE>

<B>Bart Brentjens, 3rd Place:</B>

<BLOCKQUOTE><I>"With two laps left I still felt good with enough energy to go, so I dropped 
Christophe [Dupouey] near the finish. But he almost came back again. The new 
parts of the course were a challenge, and I lost a couple seconds on the 
drop-offs. I couldn't follow Sauser on the downhills - I know my limits."</I></BLOCKQUOTE>


<I>-Ari Cheren</I>
