German keeper Oliver Kahn may have won the adidas Golden Ball Award award but he couldn't match David Beckham for the platinum ball.
<p>

A small slice of a platinum football signed by the England captain fetched more than three million yen ($25,460) at a Japanese charity auction - some 700,000 yen more than a similar piece autographed by Kahn, organisers said on Thursday.

South Africa captain Lucas Radebe wants to continue his international career after winning his long battle against injury.
<p>

Radebe was sidelined for 15 months but regained fitness to captain Bafana Bafana in all three of their games at the 2002 FIFA World Cup Korea/Japan.
<p>

Radebe told South African newspapers on Wednesday he wanted to compete in the 2006 FIFA World Cup Germany&#153;.
<p>

"I'm yearning for more time in the Bafana shirt," he said in an interview in the Cape Times.
The 33-year-old defender said he wanted to continue his international career as long as he was not "overplayed" by his country.<a href="/Service/MR_A/41225_E.html" target="_top"><img width="20" height="15" hspace="0" vspace="0" border="0" align="absmiddle" src="http://a1801.g.akamai.net/f/1801/2004/3d/www.fifa.com/images/icons/continues_orange.gif" alt=""></a>

 Change at the head of the <br>FIFA general secretariat </font></b>
<p>


<img width="105" height="80" border="0" hspace="8" vspace="2" src="http://a1801.g.akamai.net/f/1801/2004/3d/www.fifa.com/images2/fifalogo-b.gif" align="left">
In line with the agreement reached with the FIFA Executive Committee on 31 May 2002, 4 July 2002 marked the end of General Secretary Michel Zen-Ruffinens employment with FIFA. The Executive Committee and Mr Zen-Ruffinen agreed during the meeting in Seoul that he would be responsible for the running of the 2002 FIFA World Cup, but working relations would end by mutual consent after the tournament.
<p>
The administration of world footballs governing body will until further notice be headed by <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?M2:gp::67173+mrel/Display+41043+E">Dr. Urs Linsi</a> (Switzerland), who was appointed Acting General Secretary by the FIFA Executive Committee at its meeting in Tokyo on 28 June.
