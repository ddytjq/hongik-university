<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B>Team v Rep of Ireland</B><BR>
1-Rustu Recber; 2-Ali Eren Beserler, 3-Ogun Temizkanoglu, 4-Tayfur Havutcu,
5-Alpay Ozalan, 6-Arif Erdem 7-Abdullah Ercan, 8-Okan Buruk, 9-Hakan Sukur,
10-Sergen Yalcin, 11-Tayfun Korkut. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Turkey sealed a place in Euro 2000 on Wednesday
with a 0-0 draw against Ireland that the hosts should have won comfortably.
Turkey went through on the away goals rule after the 1-1 stalemate in a tight
game in Dublin on Saturday. But playing on the fringes of August's earthquake
zone in the city of Bursa, the Turks looked the better side -- except in front
of goal where Hakan Sukur and Okan Buruk squandered two or three chances each.
Sometimes it was nerves, twice it was the bar that prevented a goal crowning
the flowing Turkish attacks orchestrated by a delightful Sergen Yalcin. Yalcin
ran one-on-one against Irish goalkeeper Dean Kiely in the last minutes but
fired into his legs. Fighting broke out among the players at the end. Fans were
also involved. Ireland have failed to reach the last three major tournaments
after losing in play-offs. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">NOV
13</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B>Team v Rep of Ireland</B><BR>
1-Rustu Recber; 2-Ali Eren Beserler, 3-Ogun Temizkanoglu, 4-Tayfur Havutcu, 5-
Alpay Ozalan, 6-Abdullah Ercan, 7-Tayfun Korkut, 8-Umit Davala, 9-Hakan Sukur,
10-Sergen Yalcin, 11-Hakan Unsal </FONT></P>
<TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0">
<TR>
<TD ALIGN="CENTER" VALIGN="TOP"><A HREF="http://www.empics.com"><IMG
SRC="GIfs/ivt1.jpg" WIDTH="127" HEIGHT="107" BORDER="0"></A><BR>
<FONT SIZE="-1"><I><FONT SIZE="-1">Stephen Carr takes on Ercan

<TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP"><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Ireland gave away
a needless penalty in the final 10 minutes on Saturday to hand Turkey a 1-1
draw in the first leg of their Euro 2000 playoff. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">A superb 79th minute finish by Robbie Keane had
given the hosts a well-deserved lead at Lansdowne Road but just four minutes
later Blackburn midfielder Lee Carsley pushed the ball away with his hand while
lying on the pitch. </FONT></P>
</TD>
</TR>
</TABLE>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Referee Anders Frisk had no hesitation pointing
to the spot and Tayfur Havutcu stroked his penalty past substitute goalkeeper
Dean Kiely. The result puts Turkey, runners-up to Germany in group three,
firmly in the driving seat as away goals will count double at the end of 90
minutes of the second leg. Ireland, who had been seconds away from automatic
qualification from group eight before a last minute Macedonian equaliser saw
Yugoslavia grab the top spot, must now at least earn a 1-1 draw in the
intimidating Bursa Stadium on Wednesday to avoid elimination. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">NOV 9</FONT></B>
</FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Prolific Turkish striker Hakan Sukur is all but
certain to win his 50th cap on Saturday leading the Turkish attack against
Ireland in their Euro 2000 play-off in Dublin. Sukur is likely to play as a
lone striker in the first leg of the tie, backed by a five-strong midfield. The
lanky and often unshaven Sukur has been the highest-scoring player in the
Turkish first division for the last three seasons for Istanbul side
Galatasaray. Strong in the air and quick to grab chances in the box, Sukur has
scored 26 international goals, more than any other Turk before. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Service in the Ireland match is likely to come
from the midfield trio of Sergen Yalcin, Arif Erdem and Tayfun Korkut. Abdullah
Ercan and Tugay Kerimoglu are likely to play a more defensive role. Sukur's
talents have drawn interest from overseas but his unhappy three-month stint at
Torino in 1995 has raised doubts over his ability to work outside Turkey. At
the time Sukur famously said he missed Turkish kebabs and was often homesick.
But at 28, the striker says he is now ready to try a move overseas at the end
of the season. <BR>
<B>Probable side: </B>Rustu Recber; Ogun Temizkanoglu, Alpay Ozalan, Ali Eren
Beserler; Tugay Kerimoglu, Tayfun Korkut, Abdullah Ercan, Sergen Yalcin, Arif
Erdem; Hakan Sukur </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">OCT 9</FONT></B>
</FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"> Turkey held Germany to a 0-0 draw and will now
take part in the play-offs for next year's championships in the Netherlands and
Belgium after a match that saw both sides fail to make the most of their
chances. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Moldova coach Ivan Danilyants has submitted his
resignation after a disappointing season, federation officials said on
Wednesday. Danilyants, whose contract had been due to expire at the end of the
year, decided to quit after Moldova finished their Euro 2000 qualifying
campaign in last place in group three without a win and with just four points
from eight matches. Under his two-year tenure as head coach, Moldova won only
two of 13 games. Pavel Ciobanu, president of the Moldova Football Federation,
told local media a new coach would be named within two months. Danilyants'
assistant, Alexandru Matsura, who is also Moldova's under-21 coach, is
considered a frontrunner for the vacant job. </FONT></P>

<TD COLSPAN="2"><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0"> Oct 9
- Germany 0 Turkey 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">As many Turkish fans as Germans packed out the
Olympic stadium in Munich and despite a bright start from the Germans, Turkey
plagued a listless German defence throughout the first half with German 'keeper
Oliver Kahn doing well to keep out chances from Sergen Yalcin and Tayfun
Korkut. Ribbeck had strong words with his team at half-time, substituting
Liverpool's Dietmar Hamann for Christian Nerlinger, and Germany made a stronger
start to the second half. A strong curving drive by captain Lothar Matthaeus in
the 46th minute went just wide and Turkish goalkeeper Rustu Recber did well to
stop a volley by AC Milan striker Oliver Bierhoff one minute later.
Middlesbrough's Christian Ziege was unable to repeat the hat-trick he scored
against Northern Ireland in Germany's last qualifier and was substituted in the
75th minute by Marco Bode. There were heart-stopping moments for the Germans
late in the game when the Turks went very close to scoring, including a shot
into the side netting by Okan Buruk. </FONT></P>
</TD>

<TR>
<TD COLSPAN="2"> <P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Oct 9
- Finland 4 Northern Ireland 1</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Two goals from Joonas Kolkka helped Finland to
a deserved 4-1 victory over Northern Ireland on Saturday in a European
championship group three match between two teams who had already been
eliminated. The PSV Eindhoven forward secured victory for the home side with
goals in the 73rd and 83rd minutes. Jonatan Johansson had given Finland a lead
in the ninth minute but Jeff Whitley equalised for the visitors just before the
hour. Liverpool defender Sami Hyypia restored the lead four minutes later
before Kolkka tidied things up for the Finns, who finished third in the group
behind Germany and Turkey. </FONT></P>
