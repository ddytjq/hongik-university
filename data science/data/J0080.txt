<h1>'Iron Man' Labonte strong in First Union win</h1>

NORTH WILKESBORO, N.C. -- Sunday's NASCAR First Union 400 was a triple crown of accomplishments for Terry Labonte:
<p>
<li>Labonte tied Richard Petty's consecutive race streak at 513, outrunning Jeff Gordon in a nose-to-tail duel around the
.625-mile oval over the final 10 laps to claim his first win of
1996 and his 17th career Winston Cup victory. 
<p>
<li>Labonte collected the UNOCAL bonus, an
additional check for $129,200 for winning the race from the
pole, leading a five-car Chevrolet parade accross the finish line.
<p>
<li>Labonte -- NASCAR's &quot;Iron Man&quot; -- tied Richard Petty's consecutive race
streak by starting his 513th straight Winston Cup event. 
<p>
&quot;I always felt that the thing that excited me most (about tying
Petty's record) was that we had the chance to win the race,
too,&quot; Labonte said. 
<p>
Dale Earnhardt finished third and has been in the Top 10 in 31
of 35 races at North Wilkesboro.  He was followed by Robert
Pressley and Sterling Marlin.  It is a career-best finish for
Pressley, last year's runner-up for Rookie of the Year. 
<p>
Earnhardt and Labonte developed a still-heated rivalry when both
were rookies in 1979. 
<p>
&quot;Congratulations to Terry Labonte,&quot; Earnhardt said. &quot;It's quite
a bonus, and he deserves it.  He's been a tough competitor all
these years.&quot; 
<p>
Whether you call it silver or bright iron-man grey, Labonte's
special-edition Kellogg's Corn Flakes Chevy was quick throughout
the race, leading for more than 150 laps. 
<p>
&quot;The difference was the pit stop at the end,&quot; Labonte said. &quot;I
was glad it was Jeff.  We raced each other clean.&quot; 
<p>
Bad luck once again haunted Rusty Wallace. With less than 35
laps to go, Wallace was in the lead when John Andretti and Geoff
Bodine -- two cars that were at least two laps behind the
leaders -- got tangled coming out of Turn 4. Andretti spun into
the wall, sliding across the track and tapping the car of Wally
Dallenbach Jr. and then sliding in front of Wallace, and the
cars came together head-on. 
<p>
Andretti was sent backwards into the water-filled barrels at the
pit road entrance, sending a cascade of water onto pit road and
forcing the engine of Andretti's car to catch fire.  NASCAR
officials were forced to close pit road while crews dried the
water, which could be hazardous to pitting drivers. 
<p>
&quot;It's really upsetting because the car was that good,&quot; said
Wallace, a three-time winner at North Wilkesboro and a top short-track performer. &quot;I tried to avoid (Andretti), but
there was just no where to go.&quot; 
<p>
Andretti wasn't exactly happy about the accident, either. He blased Bodine after the race, saying &quot;(Bodine) has little regard for my safety or others out
there.&quot; 
<p>
Andretti said he felt the tap was intentional, and that
it was the second time Bodine had done this to his car this
year. 
<p>
Two laps before the accident, Mark Martin had emerged from the
garage in his repaired Ford Thunderbird. Martin had been the
only driver to leave the course to that point, and the race had
only two caution flags for relatively minor incidents involving
Jimmy Spencer and Elton Sawyer. 
<p>
The preliminary Winston Cup points standings still have Dale
Jarrett with 1,063 points, two more than Earnhardt.  Labonte is
third with 1,004 points, followed by Ricky Craven with 987 and
Ricky Rudd with 973. 
<p>
Gordon, the defending Winston Cup champion, recorded his fifth
straight top-three finish after finishing next-to-last in the
first two races of the season.  He is sixth with 970 points. 
<p>
The First Union 400 consisted of 400 laps around the North
Wilkesboro Speedway. NASCAR likely will cancel the autumn race
here because of deteriorating track conditions. 
<p>
This was the seventh of 31 races on the Winston Cup circuit. The
next race is the Goody's Headache Powders 500 in Martinsville,
Virginia on April 21. 
<p>
