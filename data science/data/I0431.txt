<TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP"><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Ireland gave away
a needless penalty in the final 10 minutes on Saturday to hand Turkey a 1-1
draw in the first leg of their Euro 2000 playoff. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">A superb 79th minute finish by Robbie Keane had
given the hosts a well-deserved lead at Lansdowne Road but just four minutes
later Blackburn midfielder Lee Carsley pushed the ball away with his hand while
lying on the pitch. </FONT></P>
</TD>
</TR>
</TABLE>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Referee Anders Frisk had no hesitation pointing
to the spot and Tayfur Havutcu stroked his penalty past substitute goalkeeper
Dean Kiely. The result puts Turkey, runners-up to Germany in group three,
firmly in the driving seat as away goals will count double at the end of 90
minutes of the second leg. Ireland, who had been seconds away from automatic
qualification from group eight before a last minute Macedonian equaliser saw
Yugoslavia grab the top spot, must now at least earn a 1-1 draw in the
intimidating Bursa Stadium on Wednesday to avoid elimination. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">NOV
12</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Roy Keane and Denis Irwin are fit to play for
Ireland Saturday's Euro 2000 play-off first leg against Turkey while Kenny
Cunningham and Ian Harte could also make it, Irish officials said on Thursday.
Keane, who missed Ireland's last three qualifiers, will strengthen the midfield
while his Manchester United colleague Irwin, who looks to have overcome a knee
injury, provides a calming influence at full back. </FONT></P>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Two curling free kicks from Sinisa Mihajlovic
earned Yugoslavia a 2-2 draw and eliminated Croatia from the European
Championship on Saturday in a politically-charged qualifying match on Saturday.
Mihajlovic's high-flying free-kick in the 26th minute found Fiorentina striker
Predrag Mijatovic unmarked in the area and he headed home just six minutes
after Croatia had taken the lead through Alen Boksic. Mihajlovic worked his
magic again five minutes later, from almost the same spot on the right, this
time finding Dejan Stankovic who put Yugoslavia in front. Mario Stanic
equalised but the Yugoslav team managed to hold out despite losing defender
Zoran Mirkovic sent off at the end of first half. </FONT></P>

<TD COLSPAN="2"> <P><FONT FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#000000" FACE="Arial"><A

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Sep 8 - Macedonia 2
Yugoslavia 4</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Savo Milosevic stunned the Macedonian crowd
after just 10 seconds by calmly chipping the ball over advancing keeper Petar
Milosevski after being sent clear by skipper Dragan Stojkovic straight from the
kick-off. The visitors doubled their lead after three minutes when Boban
Babunski volleyed a teasing Dejan Stankovic cross past his own keeper. The
Yugoslavs kept firing on all cylinders and went 3-0 ahead in the 14th minute
through Dejan Stankovic, who chested down a long pass by Predrag Mijatovic and
beat Milosevski from the edge of the penalty box. The home side then missed a
couple of clear chances before Stojkovic and Miroslav Djukic carved another
opening for Ljubinko Drulovic, who gave Milosevski no chance with a powerful
shot from close range. Macedonia salvaged some pride in the second half through
long range efforts by Artem Saciri and Sasa Ciric, as the visitors sat back on
their four-goal cushion and missed a hatful of chances. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Sep 8 - Malta 2 Rep of
Ireland 3</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Goals from Robbie Keane and Gary Breen had
given the Irish a two goal cushion after just 20 minutes but Malta, without a
point to date, hit back with a Brian Said goal and a David Carabott penalty
midway through the second half. Staunton blasted home the winner from 25 metres
in the 72nd minute and Irish keeper Alan Kelly made two great saves in the
closing minutes to secure the 3-2 victory. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Sep 4- Croatia 1 Rep
of Ireland 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">In the final minute of the game, aa hopeful
60-metre pass by Robert Kovac found Arsenal's Suker in the Irish penalty area.
Suker, top scorer in the 1998 World Cup, controlled the ball with his famous
left foot and flicked it past Ireland goalkeeper Alan Kelly, all in one move.
The home team dominated the game, attacking in waves and creating at least two
dozen chances, including a first half effort by Suker that was disallowed for
offside. Mario Stanic rattled the Irish bar in the 58th minute but resolute
Irish defence kept the game alive until the dying seconds. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Sep 1 - Rep of Ireland
1 Macedonia 0</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Ireland took a valuable step closer to the Euro
2000 finals with a convincing 2-1 win over group eight rivals Yugoslavia on
Wednesday. Mark Kennedy sealed victory in the 69th minute of the long-awaited
match, originally planned for June 5 but postponed because of the Kosovo
conflict, after a torrid 15-minute spell of end-to-end play. The Manchester
City striker crashed home a 30-metre shot over the head of Yugoslav goalkeeper
Aleksandar Kocic. After an edgy and goalless first half which Ireland dominated
but failed to capitalise on several chances, Coventry City's man of the match
Robbie Keane opened the scoring in the 54th minute from an accurate cross from
midfielder Roy Keane. Just six minutes later, Yugoslavia levelled with a long
cross from Predrag Mijatovic which found Dejan Stankovic in the box and he
headed past goalkeeper Alan Kelly. Ireland manager Mick McCarthy said the
victory gave Ireland an edge going into Saturday's Zagreb showdown against
their major challengers Croatia. </FONT> </P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">Sep 5 - Yugoslavia 3
Macedonia 1</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Macedonia, cheered by a small but vociferous
band of supporters, were the brighter starters on Sunday but and missed two
good chances in the opening 15 minutes. Striker Georgi Hristov shot wide from
close range and Yugoslav keeper Ivica Kralj tipped Toni Micevski's superb shot
over the bar moments later. The Yugoslavs regained their composure and took a
deserved 36th minute lead through Stojkovic, back in the side after a rib
injury, after a Predrag Mijatovic cross found him at the far post. Mijatovic
was the provider again nine minutes after the interval when he put Stojkovic
through with a defence-splitting pass and the skipper blasted the ball into the
roof of the net to double the lead. Macedonia threw men forward and got their
reward in the 64th minute when Sasa Ciric sent Kralj the wrong way from the
penalty spot after second-half substitute Zarko Serafimovski was felled in the
box. Savicevic sealed Yugoslavia's victory moments after coming on as a
replacement for Stojkovic, when Boban Babunski deflected his speculative shot
from the left flank past helpless keeper Petar Milosevski. </FONT> </P>
