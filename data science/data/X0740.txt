                                <P ALIGN=LEFT><A HREF="./advertise.html"><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The Hockey Phreak</FONT></A><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"> : </FONT><A
                                         HREF="./players.shtml"><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">For Players</FONT></A><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"> : </FONT><A HREF="./goaltenders.html"><FONT SIZE="-2"
                                         FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">For Goaltenders</FONT></A><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"> : Goaltending Terminology<BR><BR></FONT><FONT COLOR="#000000" SIZE="+1"
                                         FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"><B>GOALTENDING TERMINOLOGY<BR>
                                  <font size="1"><BR>
                                  </font></B></FONT><font size="1" face="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This 
                                  is a list of some goaltending terms and their 
                                  meanings. If you have anything to add to this 
                                  list, please feel free to </font><font size="1"><A HREF="mailto:thephreak@hockeyphreak.com"><FONT FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">email</FONT></A><FONT
                                         FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"> 
                                  us.</FONT></font><FONT SIZE="-1"
                                         FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"><BR>
                                  <BR></FONT><DIV ALIGN="CENTER">
                                        <TABLE ID="Table8" BORDER=0 BGCOLOR="#FFCC00" CELLSPACING=3 CELLPADDING=4 WIDTH="85%">
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Angle <BR>(playing the Angle):</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Playing the Angle is a method that a goalie can use to minimize uncovered net. This is done by placing 
                                                    yourself in a direct line with the shooter, and often coming a little out of the net to block more of it. When playing the angle come out so that your body is placed directly on the vertical bar 
                                                    in relation to the shooter.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99" HEIGHT=19>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Blocker:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">A blocker is a piece of equipment that is worn on the hand of the goaltender (depending if they are right or 
                                                    left-handed. Right-handed goaltenders wear the blocker on their right hands.) The blocker is rectangular in shape and is curved inwards towards the middle. It is used to hold the stick, protect 
                                                    the wrist area from pucks, and can be used to direct shots away. The blocker should be held off to the side, with the goaltender's arm pressed to his side, at a height that keeps the stick flat 
                                                    on the ice.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Bufferfly Save (or Position):<BR>&nbsp;</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">A butterfly save is named so because of its position. It is characterized by the goaltender's low position 
                                                    to the ice, the way his pads splay along the ice and the position of his stick in front of the five hole. The glove is kept up and open in this position. If you would like to learn more about 
                                                    this save, visit our </FONT><A HREF="./saves.html"><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Making Saves</FONT></A><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"> page.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Five Hole:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The five hole is the space between the goaltender's leg pads. It is notorious for being the most vulnerable 
                                                    spot for a goaltender, because it is nearly impossible to cover all of the time. Many players aim specifically for that area, knowing the goaltender's frustration.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Holes<BR>(the other ones):</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">According to Jacques Plante, there are five positions that the goaltender must always cover. The one-hole is 
                                                    the space the corner of the net, on the ice, on the goalie's stick side. The two-hole is the space at the corner of the net on the goalie's glove side. The three-hole is on the goale's glove 
                                                    side, in the upper corner. The four-hole is on the stick site, near upper corner, and the five hole, as mentioned above, is the space between the goalie's pads. To view a diagram, click </FONT><A
                                                     HREF="holes.gif" target="_blank"><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">here</FONT></A><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Leg Pads:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The leg pads are one of the most important pieces of equipment for a goaltender. They are strapped onto the 
                                                    legs to protect them, as well as increase blocking ability. They may not be more than 12 inches wide, and should come to about three inches above the knee. If your pads are too long, your balance 
                                                    will be off, and if they are too short, you will lack the proper knee protection. When purchasing leg pads, note that the center of the player's knee should be positioned at the bottom of the 
                                                    center knee roll, with the pad securely attached at the goal skate and properly located on the player's leg. As the pad breaks in, the knee will be directly behind the center knee roll.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Leg Pad Save:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is a save made with any part of the leg pads. There are a number of leg pad saves, such as the 
                                                    butterfly save and the two-pad save.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Lie:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The lie is the angle created between the handle of a goalie's stick and the paddle. The higher the lie is, 
                                                    the closer the stick resembles the letter &quot;L&quot;.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC" HEIGHT=19>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Paddle:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is the thick part of the goalie's stick. It should remain flat on the ice as much as possible, and is 
                                                    used for poking the puck away, clearing the puck and covering the five-hole.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99" HEIGHT=19>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Poke Check:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The poke check is used for poking the puck away from the opposition. It is done by sliding your hand up the 
                                                    stick and thrusting it forward towards the puck. </FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Screen Shot:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">A screen shot is when a shot is made on goal while the goaltender's view is blocked by other players between 
                                                    the goalie and the shooter. These players are often placed intentionally in front of the goaltender, but sometimes can be the goalie's own teammates, trying to &quot;help out&quot;.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99" HEIGHT=19>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Shuffle:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is a technique for lateral movement. It is used when the puck is relatively far from the net. The 
                                                    goalie will slide his or her legs, one at a time, in the desired direction. The disadvantage is that this can leave the five-hole open.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC" HEIGHT=19>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Skate Save:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is a save made with the goaltender's skate. It is characteristic of the goaltender dropping to one knee 
                                                    and poking the puck away with their skate.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Stacking the Pads:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is a type of save where the goaltender attempts to place his body across the length of the net to keep 
                                                    the puck from entering a relatively unguarded net. This is also known as a Two-Pad save.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Stick Save:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Also called a set, the stance is the position the goaltender takes during the majority of the time they are 
                                                    not actively making saves. A proper stance has the goaltender's weight on their toes, their knees bent, their catch glove at their knees and their stick flat on the ice.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">T-Push:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This is a technique used by the goalie to move in a lateral direction. A goaltender performs a t-push by 
                                                    directing his outside skate in the direction he or she wishes to move, and pushing with both legs. This method is most effective when the puck is close to the net, provided the five-hole is 
                                                    covered.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC">
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Telescoping:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">This techniques is an advanced form of &quot;Playing the Angles&quot; where the goalie moves into a position 
                                                    so that the net is effectively covered and the distance travelled is minimized.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
                                            <TR>
                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="37%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99" HEIGHT=156>
                                                    <TABLE BORDER=0 CELLSPACING=0 CELLPADDING=5 WIDTH=135 NOF=TI>
                                                        <TR>
                                                            <TD><P ALIGN=RIGHT><B><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Trapper:</FONT></B></TD>
                                                        </TR>
                                                    </TABLE>
                                                </TD>
                                                <TD WIDTH="62%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">The trapper, a piece of equipment worn on the left-hand (for right handed goalies, and on the right hand for 
                                                    left-handed goalies), is similar in shape to a fielder's mitt in baseball. It is commonly referred to as the catch glove, and is a strong point for many goaltenders. The catch glove can be used 
                                                    to stop play when the goaltender catches the puck in it and then brings the glove in close to his body to prevent it from being knocked away. Trapper saves are often called glove saves.</FONT></TD>
                                            </TR>
