									<td align="left" class="FeaturedPlayerArticleHeadline" colspan="2">
									Laws Of The Game </td>
								</tr>
								<tr>
									<td class="FeaturedPlayerArticleBody" align="left" valign="top" colspan="2">
									<br />
									 <P><FONT face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size=2><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 1 - The Field of Play</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Dimensions<BR><BR></STRONG>The field of play must be rectangular. The length of the touch line must be greater than the length of the goal line.<BR><BR>Length: minimum 90 m (100 yds)<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; maximum 120 m (130 yds)<BR>Width:&nbsp;&nbsp;minimum 45 m (50 yds)<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; maximum 90 m (100 yds) <BR><BR><STRONG>International Matches</STRONG><BR><BR>Length: minimum 100 m (110 yds)<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; maximum 110 m (120 yds) <BR>Width:&nbsp; minimum 64 m (70 yds)<BR>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; maximum 75 m (80 yds)<BR><BR><STRONG><FONT color=navy>Field Markings</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR>The field of play is marked with lines. These lines belong to the areas of which they are boundaries.<BR>The two longer boundary lines are called touch lines. The two shorter lines are called goal lines.<BR>All lines are not more than 12 cm (5 ins) wide.<BR>The field of play is divided into two halves by a halfway line.<BR>The centre mark is indicated at the midpoint of the halfway line. A circle with a radius of 9.15 m (10 yds) is marked around it.<BR><BR><STRONG>The Goal Area</STRONG><BR><BR>A goal area is defined at each end of the field as follows:<BR>Two lines are drawn at right angles to the goal line, 5.5 m (6 yds) from the inside of each goalpost. These lines extend into the field of play for a distance of 5.5 m (6 yds) and are joined by a line drawn parallel with the goal line. The area bounded by these lines and the goal line is the goal area.<BR><BR><STRONG>The Penalty Area</STRONG><BR><BR>A penalty area is defined at each end of the field as follows:<BR>Two lines are drawn at right angles to the goal line, 16.5 m (18 yds) from the inside of each goalpost. These lines extend into the field of play for a distance of 16.5 m (18 yds) and are joined by a line drawn parallel with the goal line. The area bounded by these lines and the goal line is the penalty area.<BR>Within each penalty area a penalty mark is made 11 m (12 yds) from the midpoint between the goalposts and equidistant to them. An arc of a circle with a radius of 9.15 m (10 yds) from each penalty mark is drawn outside the penalty area.<BR><BR><STRONG>Flagposts<BR></STRONG><BR>A flagpost, not less than 1.5 m (5 ft) high, with a non-pointed top and a flag is placed at each corner.<BR>Flagposts may also be placed at each end of the halfway line, not less than 1 m (1 yd) outside the touch line.<BR><BR><STRONG>The Corner Arc</STRONG><BR><BR>A quarter circle with a radius of 1 m (1 yd) from each corner flagpost is drawn inside the field of play.<BR><BR><STRONG>Goals<BR></STRONG><BR>Goals must be placed on the centre of each goal line.<BR>They consist of two upright posts equidistant from the corner flagposts and joined at the top by a horizontal crossbar.<BR>The distance between the posts is 7.32 m (8 yds) and the distance from the lower edge of the crossbar to the ground is 2.44 m (8 ft).<BR>Both goalposts and the crossbar have the same width and depth which do not exceed 12 cm (5 ins). The goal lines are the same width as that of the goalposts and the crossbar. Nets may be attached to the goals and the ground behind the goal, provided that they are properly supported and do not interfere with the goalkeeper.<BR>The goalposts and crossbars must be white.<BR><BR><STRONG>Safety<BR></STRONG><BR>Goals must be anchored securely to the ground. Portable goals may only be used if they satisfy this requirement.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decisions of the International F.A. Board<BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Decision 1<BR></STRONG>If the crossbar becomes displaced or broken, play is stopped until it has been repaired or replaced in position. If a repair is not possible, the match is abandoned. The use of a rope to replace the crossbar is not permitted. If the crossbar can be repaired, the match is restarted with a dropped ball at the place where the ball was located when play was stopped.<BR><STRONG><BR>Decision 2<BR></STRONG>Goalposts and crossbars must be made of wood, metal or other approved material. Their shape may be square, rectangular, round or elliptical and they must not be dangerous to players.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 3<BR></STRONG>No kind of commercial advertising, whether real or virtual, is permitted on the field of play and field equipment (including the goal nets and the areas they enclose) from the time the teams enter the field of play until they have left it at half-time and from the time the teams re-enter the field of play until the end of the match. In particular, no advertising material of any kind may be displayed on goals, nets, flagposts or their flags. No extraneous equipment (cameras, microphones, etc.) may be attached to these items.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 4<BR></STRONG>There shall be no advertising of any kind within the technical area or within one metre from the touch line and outside the field of play on the ground. Further, no advertising shall be allowed in the area between the goal line and the goal nets.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 5<BR></STRONG>The reproduction, whether real or virtual, of representative logos or emblems of FIFA, confederations, national associations, leagues, clubs or other bodies, is forbidden on the field of play and field equipment (including the goal nets and the areas they enclose) during playing time, as described in Decision 3.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 6</STRONG><BR>A mark may be made off the field of play, 9.15 metres (10 yds) from the corner arc and at right angles to the goal lines to ensure that this distance is observed when a corner kick is being taken.<BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 2 - The Ball</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Qualities and Measurements</STRONG><BR><BR>The ball is:<BR><EM>- spherical<BR>- made of leather or other suitable material</EM><BR>- <EM>of a circumference of not more than 70 cm (28 ins) and not&nbsp; less&nbsp;than 68 cm (27 ins)</EM><BR><EM>- not more than 450 g (16 oz) in weight and not less than 410 g (14 oz) at the start of the match</EM><BR><EM>- of a pressure equal to 0.6 - 1.1 atmosphere (600 - 1100 g/cm2) at sea level (8.5 lbs/sq in 15.6 lbs/sq in)<BR></EM><BR><STRONG>Replacement of a Defective Ball</STRONG><BR><BR>If the ball bursts or becomes defective during the course of a match:<BR><EM>- the match is stopped</EM><BR>- <EM>the match is restarted by dropping the replacement ball at the place where the first ball became defective&nbsp;<BR></EM>If the ball bursts or becomes defective whilst not in play at a kick-off, goal kick, corner kick, free kick, penalty kick or throw-in:<BR><EM>- the match is restarted accordingly</EM><BR>The ball may not be changed during the match without the authority of the referee.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decisions of the International F.A. Board</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 1<BR></STRONG>In competition matches, only footballs which meet the minimum technical requirements stipulated in Law 2 are permitted for use.<BR>In FIFA competition matches, and in competition matches organised under the auspices of the confederations, acceptance of a football for use is conditional upon the football bearing one of the following three designations:<BR>The official "FIFA APPROVED" logo,<BR>or<BR>The official "FIFA INSPECTED" logo,<BR>or<BR>The reference "INTERNATIONAL MATCHBALL STANDARD"<BR><BR>Such a designation on a football indicates that it has been tested officially and found to be in compliance with specific technical requirements, different for each category and additional to the minimum specifications stipulated in Law 2. The list of the additional requirements specific to each of the respective categories must be approved by the International F.A. Board. The institutes conducting the tests are subject to the approval of FIFA.<BR>National association competitions may require the use of balls bearing any one of these three designations.<BR>In all other matches the ball used must satisfy the requirements of Law 2.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 2</STRONG><BR>In FIFA competition matches and in competition matches organised under the auspices of the confederations and national associations, no kind of commercial advertising on the ball is permitted, except for the emblem of the competition, the competition organiser and the authorised trademark of the manufacturer. The competition regulations may restrict the size and number of such markings.<BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 3 - The Number of Players</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Players<BR></STRONG><BR>A match is played by two teams, each consisting of not more than eleven players, one of whom is the goalkeeper. A match may not start if either team consists of fewer than seven players.<BR><BR><STRONG>Official Competitions</STRONG><BR><BR>Up to a maximum of three substitutes may be used in any match played in an official competition organised under the auspices of FIFA, the confederations or the national associations.<BR>The rules of the competition must state how many substitutes may be nominated, from three up to a maximum of seven.<BR><BR><STRONG>Other Matches</STRONG><BR><BR>In other matches, substitutes may be used, provided that:<BR><EM>- the teams concerned reach agreement on a maximum number</EM><BR><EM>- the referee is informed before the match</EM><BR>If the referee is not informed, or if no agreement is reached before the start of the match, no more than three substitutes are allowed.<BR><BR><STRONG>All Matches<BR></STRONG><BR>In all matches the names of the substitutes must be given to the referee prior to the start of the match. Substitutes not so named may not take part in the match.<BR><BR><STRONG>Substitution Procedure</STRONG><BR><BR>To replace a player by a substitute, the following conditions must be observed:<BR><EM>- the referee is informed before any proposed substitution is made</EM><BR><EM>- a substitute only enters the field of play after the player being replaced has left and after receiving a signal from the referee</EM><BR><EM>- a substitute only enters the field of play at the halfway line and during a stoppage in the match</EM><BR><EM>- a substitution is completed when a substitute enters the field of play<BR>- from that moment, the substitute becomes a player and the player he has replaced ceases to be a player</EM><BR><EM>- a player who has been replaced takes no further part in the match</EM><BR><EM>- all substitutes are subject to the authority and jurisdiction of the referee, whether called upon to play or not</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Changing the Goalkeeper</STRONG><BR><BR>Any of the other players may change places with the goalkeeper, provided that:<BR><EM>- the referee is informed before the change is made</EM><BR><EM>- the change is made during a stoppage in the match </EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>If a substitute enters the field of play without the referee's permission:<BR><EM>- play is stopped</EM><BR><EM>- the substitute is cautioned, shown the yellow card and required to leave the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- play is restarted with a dropped ball at the place it was located when play was stopped</EM><BR>If a player the ball is next out of play</EM><BR>For any other infringements of this Law:<BR><EM>- the players concerned are cautioned and shown the yellow card</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Restart of Play</STRONG><BR><BR>If play is stopped by the referee to administer a caution:<BR><EM>- the match is restarted by an indirect free kick, to be taken by a player of the opposing team from the place where the ball was located when play was stopped&nbsp;</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Players and Substitutes Sent Off</STRONG><BR><BR>A player who has been sent off before the kick-off may be replaced only by one of the named substitutes.<BR>A named substitute who has been sent off, either before the kick-off or after play has started, may not be replaced.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decisions of the International F.A. Board</STRONG><BR><BR>Decision 1<BR>Subject to the overriding conditions of Law 3, the minimum number of players in a team is left to the discretion of national associations. The Board is of the opinion, however, that a match should not continue if there are fewer than seven players in either team.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 2<BR></STRONG>A team official may convey tactical instructions to the players during the match and <BR>he must return to his position after giving these instructions. All officials must remain within the confines of the technical area, where such an area is provided, and they must behave in a responsible manner. <BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 4 - The Players' Equipment</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Safety<BR></STRONG><BR>A player must not use equipment or wear anything which is dangerous to himself or another player (including any kind of jewellery).<BR><BR><STRONG>Basic Equipment</STRONG><BR><BR>The basic compulsory equipment of a player is:<BR><EM>- a jersey or shirt</EM><BR><EM>- shorts - if thermal undershorts are worn, they are of the same main colour as the shorts</EM><BR><EM>- stockings</EM><BR><EM>- shinguards<BR>- footwear<BR></EM><BR><STRONG>Shinguards</STRONG><BR><BR><EM>- are covered entirely by the stockings</EM><BR><EM>- are made of a suitable material (rubber, plastic, or similar substances)<BR>- provide a reasonable degree of protection</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Goalkeepers<BR></STRONG><BR><EM>- each goalkeeper wears colours which distinguish him from the other players, the referee and the assistant referees</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>For any infringement of this Law:<BR><EM>- play need not be stopped</EM><BR><EM>- the player at fault is instructed by the referee to leave the field of play to correct his equipment</EM><BR><EM>- the player leaves the field of play when the ball next ceases to be in play, unless he has already corrected his equipment</EM><BR><EM>- any player required to leave the field of play to correct his equipment does not re-enter without the referee's permission</EM><BR><EM>- the referee checks that the player's equipment is correct before allowing him to re-enter the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- the player is only allowed to re-enter the field of play when the ball is out of play</EM><BR>A player who has been required to leave the field of play because of an infringement of this Law and who enters (or re-enters) the field of play without the referee's permission is cautioned and shown the yellow card.<BR><BR><STRONG>Restart of Play<BR></STRONG><BR>If play is stopped by the referee to administer a caution:<BR><EM>- the match is restarted by an indirect free kick taken by a player of the opposing side, from the place where the ball was located when the referee stopped the match&nbsp;</EM><BR><BR><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 5 - The Referee</FONT></STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>The Authority of the Referee</STRONG><BR><BR>Each match is controlled by a referee who has full authority to enforce the Laws of the Game in connection with the match to which he has been appointed.<BR><BR><STRONG>Powers and Duties</STRONG><BR><BR>The Referee:<BR><EM>- enforces the Laws of the Game</EM><BR><EM>- controls the match in co-operation with the assistant referees and, where applicable, with the fourth official</EM><BR><EM>- ensures that any ball used meets the requirements of Law 2</EM><BR><EM>- ensures that the players' equipment meets the requirements of Law 4<BR>- acts as timekeeper and keeps a record of the match</EM><BR><EM>- stops, suspends or terminates the match, at his discretion, for any infringements of the Laws</EM><BR><EM>- stops, suspends or terminates the match because of outside interference of any kind</EM><BR><EM>- stops the match if, in his opinion, a player is seriously injured and ensures that he is removed from the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- allows play to continue until the ball is out of play if a player is, in his opinion, only slightly injured</EM><BR><EM>- ensures that any player bleeding from a wound leaves the field of play. The player may only return on receiving a signal from the referee, who must be satisfied that the bleeding has stopped</EM><BR>- <EM>allows play to continue when the team against which an offence has been committed will benefit from such an advantage and penalises the original offence if the anticipated advantage does not ensue at that time</EM><BR><EM>- punishes the more serious offence when a player commits more than one offence at the same time</EM><BR><EM>- takes disciplinary action against players guilty of cautionable and sending-off offences. He is not obliged to take this action immediately but must do so when the ball next goes out of play</EM><BR><EM>- takes action against team officials who fail to conduct themselves in a responsible manner and may at his discretion, expel them from the field of play and its immediate surrounds</EM><BR><EM>- acts on the advice of assistant referees regarding incidents which he has not seen</EM><BR><EM>- ensures that no unauthorised persons enter the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- restarts the match after it has been stopped</EM><BR><EM>- provides the appropriate authorities with a match report which includes information on any disciplinary action taken against players, and/or team officials and any other incidents which occurred before, during or after the match</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Decisions of the Referee</STRONG><BR><BR>The decisions of the referee regarding facts connected with play are final.<BR>The referee may only change a decision on realising that it is incorrect or, at his discretion, on the advice of an assistant referee, provided that he has not restarted play.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decisions of the International F.A. The Assistant Referees</STRONG><BR><BR></FONT><STRONG>Duties<BR></STRONG><BR>Two assistant referees are appointed whose duties, subject to the decision of the referee, are to indicate:<BR><EM>- when the whole of the ball has passed out of the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- which side is entitled to a corner kick, goal kick or throw-in</EM><BR><EM>- when a player may be penalised for being in an offside position</EM><BR><EM>- when a substitution is requested</EM><BR><EM>- when misconduct or any other incident has occurred out of the view of the referee</EM><BR><EM>- when offences have been committed whenever the assistants are closer to the action than the referee (this includes, in particular circumstances, offences committed in the penalty area)</EM><BR><EM>- whether, at penalty kicks, the goalkeeper has moved forward before the ball has been kicked and if the ball has crossed the line</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Assistance<BR></STRONG><BR>The assistant referees also assist the referee to control the match in accordance with the Laws of the Game. In particular, they may enter the field of play to help control the 9.15m distance.<BR>In the event of undue interference or improper conduct, the referee will relieve an assistant referee of his duties and make a report to the appropriate authorities.<BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 7 - The Duration of the Match</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Periods of Play</STRONG><BR><BR>The match lasts two equal periods of 45 minutes, unless otherwise mutually agreed between the referee and the two participating teams. Any agreement to alter the periods of play (for example to reduce each half to 40 minutes because of insufficient light) must be made before the start of play and must comply with competition rules.<BR><BR><STRONG>Half-Time Interval<BR></STRONG><BR>Players are entitled to an interval at half-time.<BR>The half-time interval must not exceed 15 minutes.<BR>Competition rules must state the duration of the half-time interval.<BR>The duration of the half-time interval may be altered only with the consent of the referee.<BR><BR><STRONG>Allowance for Time Lost</STRONG><BR><BR>Allowance is made in either period for all time lost through:<BR><EM>- substitution(s)<BR>- assessment of injury to players</EM><BR><EM>- removal of injured players from the field of play for treatment</EM><BR><EM>- wasting time</EM><BR><EM>- any other cause</EM><BR>The allowance for time lost is at the discretion of the referee.<BR><BR><STRONG>Penalty Kick</STRONG><BR><BR>If a penalty kick has to be taken or retaken, the duration of either half is extended until the penalty kick is completed.<BR><BR><STRONG>Extra Time</STRONG><BR><BR>Competition rules may provide for two further equal periods to be played. The conditions of Law 8 will apply.<BR><BR><STRONG>Abandoned Match</STRONG><BR><BR>An abandoned match is replayed unless the competition rules provide otherwise.<BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 8 - The Start and Restart of Play<BR></FONT></STRONG><BR><STRONG>Preliminaries</STRONG><BR><BR>A coin is tossed and the team which wins the toss decides which goal it will attack in the first half of the match.<BR>The other team takes the kick-off to start the match.<BR>The team which wins the toss takes the kick-off to start the second half of the match.<BR>In the second half of the match the teams change ends and attack the opposite goals.<BR><BR><STRONG>Kick-off</STRONG><BR><BR>A kick-off is a way of starting or restarting play:<BR><EM>- at the start of the match</EM><BR><EM>- after a goal has been scored</EM><BR><EM>- at the start of the second half of the match<BR>- at the start of each period of extra time, where applicable</EM><BR>A goal may be scored directly from the kick-off.<BR><BR><STRONG>Procedure</STRONG><BR><BR><EM>- all players are in their own half of the field</EM><BR><EM>- the opponents of the team taking the kick-off it is kicked and moves forward</EM><BR><EM>- the kicker does not touch the ball a second time until it has touched another player<BR></EM>After a team scores a goal, the kick-off is taken by the other team.<BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>If the kicker touches the ball a second time before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the to the goal line at the point nearest to where the infringement occurred.<BR>A dropped ball to restart the match after play has been temporarily stopped inside the goal area takes place on the goal between the goalposts and under the crossbar, provided that no infringement of the Laws of the Game has been committed previously by the team scoring the goal.<BR><BR><STRONG>Winning Team</STRONG><BR><BR>The team scoring the greater number of goals during a match is the winner. If both teams score an equal number of goals, or if no goals are scored, the match is drawn.<BR><BR><STRONG>Competition Rules</STRONG><BR><BR>For matches ending in a draw, competition rules may state provisions involving extra time, or other procedures approved by the International F.A. opponents</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Offence<BR></STRONG><BR>A player in an offside position is only penalised if, at the moment the ball touches or is played by one of his team, he is, in the opinion of the position</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>No Offence<BR></STRONG><BR>There is no offside offence if a player receives the ball directly from:<BR><EM>a goal kick<BR></EM>or<BR><EM>a throw-in<BR></EM>or<BR><EM>a occurred.<BR><BR><STRONG>Penalty Kick<BR></STRONG><BR>A penalty kick is awarded if any of the above ten offences is committed by a player inside his own penalty area, irrespective of the position of the ball, provided it is in play.<BR><BR><STRONG>Indirect Free Kick</STRONG><BR><BR>An indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team if a goalkeeper, inside his own penalty area, commits any of team-mate</EM><BR><EM>- touches the ball with his hands after he has received it directly from a throw-in taken by a team-mate</EM><BR>An indirect free kick is also awarded to the opposing team if a player, in the opinion of the referee:<BR><EM>- plays in a dangerous manner</EM><BR><EM>- impedes the progress of an opponent</EM><BR><EM>- prevents the goalkeeper from releasing the ball from his hands</EM><BR><EM>- commits any other offence, not previously mentioned in Law 12, for which play is stopped to caution or dismiss a player</EM><BR>The indirect free kick is taken from where the offence occurred.<BR><BR><STRONG>Disciplinary Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>Only a player or substitute or substituted player may be shown the red or yellow card.<BR><BR><STRONG>Cautionable enters or re-enters the field of play without the referee's permission <BR>7. deliberately leaves the field of play without the referee's permission<BR><BR><STRONG>Sending-Off own penalty area)<BR>5. denies an obvious goal-scoring opportunity to an opponent moving towards the player's goal by an offence punishable by a free kick or a penalty kick<BR>6. uses offensive or Board</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 1<BR></STRONG>A penalty kick is awarded if, while the ball is in play, the goalkeeper, inside his own penalty area, strikes or attempts to strike an opponent by throwing the ball at him.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 2<BR></STRONG>A player who commits a cautionable or sending-off offence, either on or off the field of play, whether directed towards an opponent, a team-mate, the referee, an assistant referee or any other person, is disciplined according to the nature of the offence committed.<BR><BR><STRONG>Decision 3<BR></STRONG>The goalkeeper is considered to be in control of the ball by touching it with any part of his hand or arms. to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred</EM><BR>If, after the ball is in play, the goalkeeper deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR>- <EM>a direct occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR><BR><FONT color=red><STRONG>LAW 14 - The Penalty Kick</STRONG><BR><BR></FONT>A penalty kick is awarded against a team which commits one of the ten offences for which a direct Law</EM><BR><EM>- decides when a penalty kick has been completed</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Procedure<BR></STRONG><BR><EM>- the player taking the penalty kicks the ball forward</EM><BR><EM>- he does not play the ball a second time until it has touched another player<BR>- the ball is in play when it is kicked and moves forward</EM><BR>When a penalty kick is taken during the normal course of play, or time has been extended at half-time or full time to allow a penalty kick to be taken or retaken, a goal is awarded if, before passing between the goalposts and under the crossbar:<BR><EM>- the ball touches either or both of the goalposts and/or the crossbar, and/or the goalkeeper</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>If the referee gives the signal for a penalty kick to be taken proceed</EM><BR><EM>- if the ball enters the goal, the kick is retaken</EM><BR><EM>- if the ball does not enter the goal, the kick is not retaken</EM><BR><BR>The goalkeeper infringes the Laws of the ball enters the goal, the kick is retaken</EM><BR><EM>- if the ball does not enter the goal, the kick is not retaken</EM><BR><EM>- if the ball rebounds from the goalkeeper, the crossbar or the goal does not enter the goal, the kick is retaken</EM><BR><BR>A player of both the defending team and the attacking team infringe the Laws of the Game:<BR><EM>- the kick is retaken</EM><BR><BR>If, after team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred</EM><BR><BR>The kicker deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- a direct free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR><BR>The ball is touched by an outside agent as it moves forward:<BR><EM>- the kick is retaken</EM><BR><BR>The ball rebounds into the field of play from the goalkeeper, the crossbar or the goalposts, and is then touched by an outside agent:<BR><EM>- the referee stops play</EM><BR><EM>- play is restarted with a dropped ball at the place where it touched the outside agent&nbsp;</EM><BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 15 - The Throw-In</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR>A throw-in is a method of restarting play.<BR>A goal cannot be scored directly from a throw-in.<BR>A throw-in is awarded:<BR><EM>- when the whole of the ball passes over the touch line, either on the ground or in the air</EM><BR><EM>- from the point where it crossed the touch line</EM><BR><EM>- to the opponents of the player who last touched the ball</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Procedure<BR></STRONG><BR>At the moment of delivering the ball, the thrower:<BR><EM>- faces the field of play</EM><BR><EM>- has part of each foot either on the touch line or on the ground outside the touch line</EM><BR><EM>- uses both hands</EM><BR><EM>- delivers the ball from behind and over his head<BR></EM>The thrower may not touch the ball again until it has touched another player.<BR>The ball is in play immediately it enters the field of play.<BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>Throw-in taken by a player other than the goalkeeper</STRONG><BR>If, after the ball is in play, the thrower touches the ball a second time (except with his hands) before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM> <BR>If, after the ball is in play, the thrower deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- a direct free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR><EM>- a penalty kick is awarded if the infringement occurred inside the thrower's penalty area<BR></EM><BR><STRONG>Throw-in taken by the goalkeeper</STRONG><BR>If, after the ball is in play, the goalkeeper touches the ball a second time (except with his hands), before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM> <BR>If, after the ball is in play, the goalkeeper deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- a direct free kick is awarded to the opposing team if the infringement occurred outside the goalkeeper's penalty area, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred </EM><BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team if the infringement occurred inside the goalkeeper's penalty area, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR>If an opponent unfairly distracts or impedes the thrower:<BR><EM>- he is cautioned for unsporting behaviour and shown the yellow card<BR></EM>For any other infringement of this Law:<BR><EM>- the throw-in is taken by a player of the opposing team</EM><BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 16 - The Goal Kick</FONT><BR></STRONG><BR>A goal kick is a method of restarting play.<BR>A goal may be scored directly from a goal kick, but only against the opposing team.<BR>A goal kick is awarded when:<BR><EM>- the whole of the ball, having last touched a player of the attacking team, passes over the goal line, either on the ground or in the air, and a goal is not scored in accordance with Law 10</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Procedure</STRONG><BR><BR><EM>- the ball is kicked from any point within the goal area by a player of the defending team</EM><BR><EM>- opponents remain outside the penalty area until the ball is in play</EM><BR><EM>- the kicker does not play the ball a second time until it has touched another player<BR>- the ball is in play when it is kicked directly beyond the penalty area<BR></EM><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR>If the ball is not kicked directly into play beyond the penalty area:<BR><EM>- the kick is retaken</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Goal kick taken by a player other than the goalkeeper</STRONG><BR>If, after the ball is in play, the kicker touches the ball a second time (except with his hands) before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred</EM> the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR><EM>- a penalty kick is awarded if the infringement occurred inside the kicker's penalty area</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Goal kick taken by the goalkeeper<BR></STRONG>If, after the ball is in play, the goalkeeper touches the ball a second time (except with his hands) before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM> <BR>If, after the ball is in play, the goalkeeper deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- a direct free kick is awarded to the opposing team if the infringement occurred outside the goalkeeper's penalty area, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred </EM><BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team if the infringement occurred inside the goalkeeper's penalty area, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR>For any other infringement of this Law:<BR><EM>- the kick is retaken</EM><BR><BR></FONT><FONT color=navy><STRONG><FONT color=red>LAW 17 - The Corner when:<BR><EM>- the whole of the ball, having last touched a player of the defending team, passes over the goal line, either on the ground or in the air, and a goal is not scored in accordance with Law 10</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Procedure<BR></STRONG><BR><EM>- the ball is placed inside the corner arc at the nearest corner flagpost<BR>- the corner flagpost is not moved</EM><BR><EM>- opponents remain at least 9.15 m (10 yds) from the ball until it is in play<BR>- the ball is kicked by a player of the attacking team</EM><BR><EM>- the ball is in play when it is kicked and moves</EM><BR><EM>- the kicker does not play the ball a second time until it has touched another player</EM><BR><BR><STRONG>Infringements/Sanctions</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>Corner kick taken by a player other than the goalkeeper</STRONG><BR>If, after the ball is in play, the kicker touches the ball a second time (except with his hands) before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM> <BR>If, after the ball is in play, the kicker deliberately handles the ball before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- a direct free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred&nbsp;</EM><BR><EM>- a penalty kick is awarded if the (except with his hands) before it has touched another player:<BR><EM>- an indirect free kick is awarded to the opposing team, the kick to be taken from the place where the infringement occurred</EM> The team captain has this responsibility.<BR>Before the start of kicks from the penalty mark the referee shall ensure that only an equal number of players from each team remain within the centre
