xmlns:msxsl="urn:schemas-microsoft-com:xslt" xmlns:user="http://mycompany.com/mynamespace"><TR><TD class="NewsBody"><P>As various league and cup competitions come to an end, the shape of the 2002/03 UEFA club competitions becomes clearer. Listed below is the provisional list of participants for the 2002/03 UEFA Cup. </P>
<P><B>Format </B><BR>• Assoc: The table lists the national associations and the title holders in this column. <BR>• Rank: The 'Rank' column shows the place of each club in their domestic championship. For Albania, only the domestic cup winners (or beaten finalists) and league runners-up can enter the UEFA Cup, but for Greece, four clubs compete in the competition, with the cup winners (or beaten finalists) and the third-, fourth- and fifth-placed clubs in their domestic league qualifying for the first round. In some cases, the winners (or beaten finalists) of the domestic league cup are also eligible to qualify for the competition. In addition, there are three first round places on offer to the 'winners' of the UEFA Intertoto Cup, a place in the first round for the winners of the 2001/02 UEFA Cup - should they not already qualify for the UEFA Champions League - as well as three 'Fair Play' spots in the qualifying round. <BR>• Entry: This column explains the entry level for each club in the competition. For example, for the Swiss qualifiers all enter at the qualifying round stage, while the Dutch qualifiers enter at the next stage of the competition, namely the first round. <BR>• Club: Clubs listed in bold text have qualified to participate in the 2002/03 competition as their domestic league or national cup competition has finished. Clubs appearing in normal text denotes provisional participation. </P>
<P><B>Last updated:&nbsp;</B>18 June 2002</P>
