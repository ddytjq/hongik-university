</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Visiting fans are seated in the lower section of the Brideford Stand
which is covered. This stand looks slightly unusual as only two thirds of
it has an upper tier. Forest fans are given the upper tier for almost all
games. Both food and beer are available.
</P><P>Whilst banners are normally permitted, drums aren't.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=NG25FJ&Title=The+City+Ground+(Nottingham+Forest)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>Disabled visitors are allocated spaces in front of Bridgeford Stand
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>The away pub of choice is the 'Larwood and Voce' which is located on the
cricket ground site near the away end. This is usually full of visiting
fans and also serves food.
</P><P>Whilst not really a football pub The Vat & Fiddle, (Queens Bridge Rd,
attached to Castle Rock brewery) also normally admits well behaved away
fans and serves food.
</P><P>For a "real" pub with large selection of real ales try the Stratford
Haven, Stratford Road in West Bridgeford (next door to the Co-op, ten
minute walk to the ground). Both the food and beer come recommended, but
may be more expensive than elsewhere.
</P><P>Away fans are also welcome in small numbers in the Trent Navigation next
to Notts County's ground.
</P><P>The Trent Bridge Inn, which lies between cricket ground and City
Ground is strictly home fans only and doorman check match tickets so even
away fans no wearing colours won't be admitted. Another pub to steer clear
of the The Newhouse between the BBC building and Broadmarsh Centre Car
Park in town as it's very much a Forest pub. It is also recommended that
visiting fans avoid the Southbank on the corner of Trent Bridge as it
isn't to welcoming.
</P><P>Drinking in the town centre may be possible, but be discreet with colours.
</P><P>Other guides also mention The Aviary which we have been reliably informed
is being refurbished (and renamed) and will no longer welcome football
fans.
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">Larwood and Voce
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Forest-LarwoodandVoce1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Forest-LarwoodandVoce1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Larwood and Voce
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Larwood and Voce
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Larwood and Voce is on Fox Road, next to the Trent Bridge Cricket ground,
about 5 minutes walk from the away turnstiles. The pub is split into 2 sections on matchdays
 with half the pub being given to away fans. This is the designated away pub and gets packed in the run 
 up to kickoff, resulting in a good atmosphere. The beer is served in plastic glasses and there is no real ale.
 Food is advertised but it gets too busy to even think about trying to eat in there.
</P><P></P></p><H5 class="entry">Stratford Haven
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Forest-StratfordHaven1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Forest-StratfordHaven1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Stratford Haven
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Stratford Haven
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Stratford Haven is a modern pub about 10 minutes walk from the ground. While not a football pub they do accept away
fans and it is an excellent choice for anyone wanting real ale or food. There is a very varied menu on offer, which while it isn't
cheap isn't to badly priced. As well as offering a wide choice of real ales, they also offer a wide choice of lagers.
</P><P>To get to the Stratford Haven walk past the Larwood and Voce ( as if approaching from the ground) into Hound Road. At the end 
of Hound Road turn left down Bridgeford Road. The Stratford Haven is on Stratford Road which is of to the right and near to 
the Co-op.
</P><P></P></p><H5 class="entry">Trent Navigation
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Notts-TrentNavigation-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Notts-TrentNavigation-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Trent Navigation
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Trent Navigation
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Trent Navigation is close to Notts County's ground. While away fans are accepted it isn't the most welcoming.
</P><P></P><P></P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>The is a McDonalds close to the away end. It is visible from the main
road over Trent Bridge between the away end, past Trent Bridge Cricket
Ground and towards the bridge. 
</P><P>There are two Chippies on that stretch of shops, plus a Greek takeaway, a
Chinese takeaway, an Indian Restaurant, as well as an Italian on the
approach to Trent Bridge. Two Burger vans are also situated between the
club shop and the Trent Bridge Inn.
</P><P>The chippie on the corner of the road approaching the away end (next to
the Toyota Garage) "The Bridgeford Fish Restaurant" is head and shoulders
the better of the two (unless you want to take advantage of the Pizza's at
the other).This is also the nearest food outlet on the walk to both the
Stratford Haven and also the Larwood and Voce to the away end.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>There is a Midland cash machine at the ground on Pavilion Road.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is NG2 5FJ</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>There is a Ladbrokes in the away end and there is also a Ladbrokes shop on the corner of Trent
Bridge.
</P></p><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>From The South
</P><P>Leave the M1 at Junction 24 and exit onto the A453 towards
Nottingham. After passing through Clifton and past the Trent University go
straight on at the traffic lights and head onto the B679 towards
West Bridgeford (8 miles after leaving the Motorway). After just over a
mile turn left onto the A60 and the ground is on the right a short way 
down here. 
</P><P>From The North
</P><P>Leave the M1 at Junction 26 onto the A610 towards Nottingham. Just over
4 miles later, at the traffic lights at the roundabout take the third exit
(A6008), head through two more sets of traffic lights and head straight on
at the next roundabout. After another five sets of lights turn right at a
major junction (A60) and then turn right again onto the Ring Road.
this runs past the City Ground. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is some parking available behind the Brian Clough Stand which you
may be able to use if you get there early enough. Other alternatives are
street parking across the river near the County Ground and a council 
car park on the Victoria Embankment. The Victoria Embankment is 15-20
minutes walk away and is accessed via the Clifton Bridge complex. There is also limited 
parking available off Fox Road at the Trent Bridge Cricket Ground.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>The ground is a 20 minute walk from the station. Exit the station onto
Queens Road and turn left. At the end turn right onto London Road and
follow this over the river via Trent Bridge. The ground will be on your
left.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Nottingham Midland
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Nottingham offers a good selection of accommodation, with plenty of options around Trent Bridge Cricket
Ground. The usual large Hotel chains are represented in the town centre.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/nottingh.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Forest's traveling contingent is known for its good humour and whilst not
always as noticeable at home they are still a lively bunch. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Forest Fans are known for their renditions of "You've Lost That Loving
Feeling".
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Nottingham is very good for a night out with lots of pubs, clubs and
people in the town centre. For rock fans Nottingham has one of the best
rock clubs in the country, "Nottingham Rock City".
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>For ideas of what to do in Nottingham other than watch football check
out <A class="entry" href="http://www.nottscc.gov.uk/tourism/THEMES/index.htm"> 
www.nottscc.gov.uk/tourism/THEMES/index.htm</a>.
