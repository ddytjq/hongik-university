    <td width="100%" valign="top" align="left" class="normal"
    background="../../../../images/Backgrounds/paper_back.gif"><h2>Talking X's &amp; O's- <font
    color="#0000FF">Coaches Library</font></h2>
      <h2 align="center">Video Review<br>
      <font face="PhyllisD">by Tip O'Dowd</font></h2>
      <div align="center"><center><table border="0" width="95%">
          <tr>
            <td><h3><font size="1"><font color="#000000"><img border="1" src="../../../../images/Reviews/dbliss_4-man_flex_review.gif" align="right">4-Man
              Flex<br>
              by Dave Bliss<br>
              Championship Book &amp; Video Productions<br>
              2001<br>
        Color, VHS, 50 minutes<br>
        Cost $39.95<br>
              <br>
              </font><a href="../../../../hoopstore/mall_index_backup11-29-99.htm"><img
        src="../../../../hoopstore/Images/bin_button.gif" alt="bin_button.gif (2965 bytes)"
        border="0" WIDTH="96" HEIGHT="30"></a> </font><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; font-family: Arial"><font size="1">(item
              #BV-1635A)</font></span></h3>
              <h3><font color="#FF6600" size="1">What, Why and How of the 4-Man
              Flex</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Baylor University coach, Dave Bliss, has done a wonderful
              job in putting together a video that not only answers the
              “what” regarding the 4 Man Flex,<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>but also answers the “why” and the “how”.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>In understanding the benefits of the 4 Man Flex, you
              receive additional knowledge of motion offensive schemes in
              general and you become armed with the tools necessary to make
              adjustments both offensively and defensively during your own
              hardwood chess matches.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>If
              deciding to implement the offense yourself, Coach Bliss is
              succinct in his delivery and adds positional intricacies which
              contribute to the overall strength of this video.<o:p>
              </o:p></span></font></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF6600" size="1">Advantages of the 4-Man Flex</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">For those with doubts on anything
              in basketball that begins with “4”, he conjures up the image of
              “noon hour pick-up basketball.”<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>where the 4 on 4 half-court game is almost always more <img border="0" src="../../../../images/Reviews/if_we_get_two.gif" align="left" hspace="10" vspace="5"> offensively
              productive than 5 on 5.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The
              key is says is the “key.”<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>With
              his 4 Man Flex offense, Coach Bliss alleviates the congestion in
              the paint and allows for cleaner cuts and better vision for ball
              handlers.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The fifth
              offensive man is used in a number of different ways also
              demonstrated in the video.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Why
              does Coach Bliss opt for the 4-man versus the 5-man flex, he
              offers &quot;the random nature of motion offense makes it
              difficult to teach, and for players, difficult to comprehend.&quot;<o:p>
              </o:p></span> <span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">These problems are simplified through the use of the 4 Man
              Flex.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>If Coach Bliss,
              with his experience (Baylor, Oklahoma, SMU, New Mexico), and a
              stable full of Division I horses feels that it is difficult to
              teach a 5 man motion offense and that it is harder still for his
              kids to understand it, then where does that leave most of us?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              Adding this video to your library will provide you the necessary
              progressions</span> to teach the offense and
              the background/ in-depth knowledge to modify it to your particular
              team.<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <h3><font size="1" color="#FF6600">Good Combination of White Board
              and On Court Demonstration</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Throughout the 49 minute clinic, Coach Bliss utilizes a
              number of teaching techniques to get his message across.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>Moving from the practice court to the white board and then
              back again, a viewing coach at home can easily pick up not only
              the flow or execution, but also the step-by-step methods of
              teaching this offense effectively.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>Additionally, the video utilizes “spot lighted” game
              footage to further illustrate the teaching points.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>While the game clips are very beneficial, there is a bit
              more movement than the specific teaching point may be looking for
              at the time (in other words, the game footage gets “ahead” of
              the teaching point occasionally.)<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>Stay with it.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Once you’ve seen the entire video, you’ll be very
              comfortable with the game clips on the second look.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span><o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF6600" size="1">Dissecting the 4-Man Flex</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">The video is broken into 6 sections and is presented in a
              building block fashion from both the philosophical and technical
              perspectives:<o:p> Attributes of a Good Offense, Advantages of the 4 Man Flex<o:p>,
              Forming the Diamond<o:p>, the 5th Man Incorporated<o:p>, Entries<o:p>,
              and Adjustments<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt"><img border="0" src="../../../../images/Reviews/the_back_screen_is.gif" align="right" hspace="10" vspace="10">Part I (“Attributes”)/ Part II (“Advantages”):<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>While there are a myriad of attributes which can lead to
              “good offense”, Coach Bliss picks out<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>4 as being key to offensive success:<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">1.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Must produce a good shot.<o:p>
              </o:p><br>
              2.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Proper spacing<o:p>
              </o:p><br>
              3.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Good reversal opportunities.<o:p>
              </o:p><br>
              4.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp; </span>Rebounding opportunities.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span><o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">As you get a closer look at the 4 Man Flex, you’ll see that
              the offense was built to maximize these areas. The first 3
              attributes lead to Coach Bliss’ belief that in this offense
              &quot;if we can get two back picks, we'll get a good shot
              opportunity.&quot;</span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Particularly interesting is that in accomplishing the first 3
              attributes, Coach Bliss’ foresight allows the offense to take
              advantage of number 4, rebounding.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
              </span>Aside from relieving congestion in the paint, coaches who
              are from small schools or who don’t have the option of
              recruiting will love Coach Bliss’ insistence that due to the
              constant changing of positions and potential reliance on as few as
              4 players, this offense will compensate for a lack of height and
              talent.<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF6600" size="1">The Key is the Diamond</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Part III (Forming the diamond). The “diamond” is the
              heart of this offense and is the shape that allows for the
              movement and floor balance that you’ll see from Coach Bliss’
              teams.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The meat of
              the clinic begins here and there is no shortage of grade A beef.
              Coach Bliss fills this section with amplification on what the
              viewer is seeing.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Quotes
              such as; &quot;the back screen is set out of the keyhole, the down
              screen in the keyhole.&quot;</span> <span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">are then followed by the all important “why” that is
              missing in so many instructional sports videos.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>This added “why” information allows coaches at all
              levels to benefit from it's viewing.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
              </span>It is also the understanding that stems from this effort
              that will allow coaches to make adjustments on their own as they
              take this offense from the video tape into their own gyms.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>Specific emphasis in this section is also spent on the
              “cutters” who keep the offense moving.<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Part IV (“5<sup>th</sup> Man”) provides emphasis on
              incorporating the 5<sup>th</sup> man into the offense to
              compliment the diamond that we saw built in part III.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;
              </span>Also introduced in this section is a topic that concerns
              any coach running a “patterned” offense:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>defensive familiarity with the rhythm of the offense and
              ball movement. Flexible options (such as a “Princeton backdoor
              cut”) are introduced along with the key element that the
              offensive player should be looking for in order to <img border="0" src="../../../../images/Reviews/if_you_have_a_mismatch.gif" align="left" hspace="10" vspace="5"> initiate a
              “break” from the offensive pattern.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>As a follow-up, Coach Bliss then shows how to reform the
              diamond and keep the all-important spacing.<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">Parts V and VI take into account that we are usually hooping
              in an imperfect world.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>In
              the “Entries” section, the viewer sees how to quickly enter
              this offense from a number of different offensive sets (i.e. 1-4
              or as part of the secondary break.)<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>No instructional video worth it’s weight in dry-erase
              markers would be complete without the “Adjustments” shown in
              the final section.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>One
              example focuses on taking advantage of mismatches.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>As the players in this offense are constantly switching
              position, it is inevitable that you will find an advantage in the
              post, be it a 6 foot, 9-incher being guarded by someone 6’
              5” or a 5 foot, 9-incher being guarded by someone 5’5”.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>While this offense is designed as much for the “open 3”
              as it is the entry down low, Coach Bliss shows a number of
              situations where we’ll stop the rotation to take advantage of
              the mismatch in the paint.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Simply
              put:<o:p>
              &quot;if you have a mismatch in the paint, don't give it up.&quot;</o:p>
              </span></font></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF6600" size="1">Simple Teaching, Simple
              Adjustments, Good Results</font></h3>
              <p><font size="1"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">If the “pattern” breaks down due to players making
              adjustments, this section also shows you both “how” to fix it,
              and “who” is responsible for making the fix.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>Dribbling/ driving options are also hit heavily here.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
              </span>The “drive and kick” and “spin out” discussion
              presents more easily recognizable keys and counters to defenses
              that attempt to anticipate the flow of the offense.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
