<h2>World of Dermatophytes: A Pictorial</h2>
<h3><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">What are Dermatophytes? 
  </font></h3>
<p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Dermatophytes are <b>fungi</b> 
  that can cause infections of the skin, hair, and nails due to their ability 
  to utilize keratin. </font></p>
<p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The organisms colonize the 
  keratin tissues and inflammation is caused by host response to metabolic by-products. 
  These infections are known as <b>ringworm</b> or <b>tinea</b>, in association 
  with the infected body part. </font></p>
<p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Occasionally the organisms 
  do invade the subcutaneous tissues, resulting in kerion development. </font></p>
<p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The organisms are transmitted 
  by either direct contact with infected host (human or animal) or by direct or 
  indirect contact with infected exfoliated skin or hair in combs, hair brushes, 
  clothing, furniture, theatre seats, caps, bed linens, towels, hotel rugs, and 
  locker room floors. </font></p>
<p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Depending on the species 
  the organism may be viable in the environment for up to 15 months. There is 
  an increased susceptibility to infection when there is a preexisting injury 
  to the skin such as scares, burns, marching, excessive temperature and humidity. 
  </font></p>
<p>
<font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> Dermatophytes are classified 
as <B>anthropophilic</b>, <b>zoophilic</b> or <b>geophilic</B> according to their 
normal habitat. 
<p><B>Anthropophilic</B> dermatophytes are restricted to human hosts and produce 
  a mild, chronic inflammation. 
<p><B>Zoophilic</B> organisms are found primarily in animals and cause marked inflammatory reactions in humans who have contact with infected cats, dogs, cattle, horses, birds, or other animals. This is followed by a rapid termination of the infection. 
<p><B>Geophilic</B> species are usually recovered from the soil but occasionally infect humans and animals. They cause a marked inflammatory reaction, which limits the spread of the infection and may lead to a spontaneous cure but may also leave scars.

<p>The dermatophytes consist of three genera:
<ul type="square">
<li><B>Epidermophyton</B> produces only macroconidia, no microconidia and consists of 2 species, one of which is a pathogen. 

<li><b>Microsporum</b> - Both microconidia and rough-walled macroconidia characterize Microsporum species. There are 19 described species but only 9 are involved in human or animal infections.  
<li><b>Trichophyton</b> - 
When produced the macroconidia of Trichophyton species are smooth-walled. There are 22 species, most causing infections in humans or animals. </ul>
<p>At the National Centre for Mycology - 
<ul>
<li>about 58% of the dermatophyte species isolated are Trichophyton rubrum
<li>27% are T. mentagrophytes
<li>7% are T. verrucosum
<li>3% are T. tonsurans</ul> 
<p>Infrequently isolated (less than 1%) are Epidermophyton floccosum, Microsporum audouinii, M.  canis, M. equinum, M. nanum, M. persicolor, Trichophyton equinum, T. kanei, T. raubitschekii, and T. violaceum.
