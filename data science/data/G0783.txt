              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In 
              the last few decades, the planets and moons with which we share 
              our cosmic neighborhood have stopped being mere points of light 
              in the sky and have been revealed as worlds that we (or at least 
              our robot representatives) can explore up close and in detail. Images 
              and data from the surface of Mars or from orbit around the moons 
              of Jupiter have profoundly transformed our understanding of the 
              solar system. As a result, the number of books and articles being 
              written about the exploration of the solar system has become - well 
              - astronomical. Below, we list a very small selection of our favorite 
              solar system readings and web sites to get armchair explorers, teachers, 
              and students started.</font></p>

            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a name="1"><font color="#6666CC"><b>General 
              Books on the Solar System</b></font></a> </font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Beatty, J. Kelly, et al., eds. <b>The New Solar System</b>, 4th 
                ed. 1999, Sky Publishing/Cambridge U.Press. The definitive introduction 
                to our current knowledge, with articles by top planetary scientists. 
                Can be a bit technical in places. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Fraknoi, 
                Andrew; Morrison, David; and Wolff, Sidney. <b>Voyages To the 
                Planets</b>. 2000, Saunders College Publishing/ Harcourt Brace. 
                A non-technical introductory textbook, with analogies and background 
                material, about our solar system and planets around other stars. 
                <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kluger, 
                Jeffrey <b>Journey Beyond Selene</b>. 1999, Simon & Schuster. 
                A journalist chronicles several lunar and planetary exploration 
                missions, including Galileo. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Lewis, 
                John <b>Worlds Without End: The Exploration of Planets Known and 
                Unknown</b>. 1998, Perseus. A noted astronomer discusses how planets 
                form and how the current appearance of the solar system (and other 
                planetary systems) reflects their origins. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">McNab, 
                David & Younger, James<b> The Planets</b>. 1999, Yale U. Press. 
                Overview of the solar system; accompanies a TV program. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Miller, 
                Ron and Hartmann, William <b>The Grand Tour: A Traveler' s Guide 
                to the Solar System</b>, 2nd ed. 1993, Workman. Lavishly illustrated 
                beginners' primer. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Morrison, 
                David <b>Exploring Planetary Worlds</b>. 1993, Scientific American 
                Library/W. H. Freeman. Clear, up-to-date, non-technical survey. 
                <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sagan, 
                Carl <b>Pale Blue Dot</b>. 1994, Random House. An eloquent and 
                impressive introduction to planetary exploration. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Taylor, 
                Stuart <b>Destiny or Chance: Our Solar System and Its Place in 
                the Cosmos</b>. 1998, Cambridge U. Press. A modern overview of 
                how our system formed. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="2">General 
              Articles on the Solar System</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Hartmann, W. <b>"The Great Solar System Revision"</b> in Astronomy, 
                Aug. 1998, p. 40. How our views have changed in the last 25 years. 
                <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kross, 
                J. <b>"What' s in a Name"</b> in Sky & Telescope, May 1995, p. 
                28. How worlds are named. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Wood, 
                J. <b>"Forging the Planets: The Origin of Our Solar System"</b> 
                in Sky & Telescope, Jan. 1999, p. 36. Good overview. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Yulsman, 
                T. <b>"From Pebbles to Planets"</b> in Astronomy, Feb. 1998, p. 
                56. Comparing the origin of our system to others. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="3">Books 
              or Articles about Mercury</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Chapman, C. <b>"Mercury's Heart of Iron"</b> in Astronomy, Nov. 
                1988, p. 22. Good introduction. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Gingerich, 
                O. <b>"How Astronomers Finally Captured Mercury"</b> in Sky & 
                Telescope, Sep. 1983, p. 203. A history of early observations. 
                <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nelson, 
                R. <b>"Mercury: The Forgotten Planet"</b> in Scientific American, 
                Nov. 1997, p. 56. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Strom, 
                R. <b>"Mercury: The Forgotten Planet"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Sep. 1990, p. 256. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Strom, 
                Robert <b>Mercury: The Elusive Planet</b>. 1987, Smithsonian Institution 
                Press. Best book on the inner planet. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="4">Books 
              or Articles about Venus</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Cooper, Henry <b>The Evening Star: Venus Observed</b>. 1993, Farrar, 
                Straus, and Giroux. Good primer on Venus exploration, with a focus 
                on the Magellan mission. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Grinspoon, 
                D. <b>"Venus Unveiled"</b> in Astronomy, May 1997, p. 44. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Grinspoon, 
                David. <b>Venus Unveiled</b>. 1997, Addison-Wesley. Well-written 
                introduction. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kargel, 
                J. <b>"Rivers of Venus"</b> in Sky & Telescope, Aug. 1997, p. 
                32. On lava channels. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Robinson, 
                C. <b>"Magellan Reveals Venus"</b> in Astronomy, Feb. 1995, p. 
                32. <br>
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Stofan, 
                E. <b>"The New Face of Venus"</b> in Sky & Telescope, Aug. 1993, 
                p. 22. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
