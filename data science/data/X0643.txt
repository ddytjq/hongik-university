				<font class="news"><center><b>INDIA INNINGS</b><br>191 all out off 48.3 overs</center><br>Ganguly wins the toss for the third time in a row and elects to bat again. The Indian captain (still limping with his bad knee) opens with Mongia. <br>Hondo grabs his first ODI wicket trapping Mongia LBW for 4. Laxman takes guard as the batsmen are tied down by some accurate swing bowling (18 off first 6 overs). India's first boundary (from Laxman) comes up in the seventh over, as VVS starts to chance his arm (and bat) with the field up. Two more Laxman boundaries quickly follow as Hondo goes for 11 in an over, but Hondo gains revenge having Laxman caught behind for 20. <br>Dravid joins Ganguly, who 'dances' down the wicket to take a wild swing at Hondo and gets cleaned up for 11. <br>India slumps to 51 for 4 when Dravid is caught at fly slip off Mbangwa for 6. Exposed in the middle order, Kaif and Bangar face an uphill struggle to post a respectable total. Kaif (6) is dropped behind by Taibu off Mbangwa. <br>The pair diligently add 32 in ten overs. Kaif opens his shoulders with a 6 off Mbangwa to post a top score of 27. Streak spills Kaif's skyrocketed skier at midwicket - this could prove costly as the batsmen now look to move the score along - taking 20 off two overs (101 for 4 after 25 overs). <br>The 50 partnership comes up in the 27th over (82 balls) as the meagre run rate increases to 4 an over. Bangar (36) slogs a Marillier full toss straight to Hondo at deep midwicket - 137 for 5. This brings to a close an excellent and much-needed recuperative 86-run partnership. <br>Batting sensibly Kaif reaches his first ODI 50. A brilliant catch by Marillier removes Ratra for 3 off Grant Flower. Agarkar then holes out to Streak off Flower for 3 - 155 for 7. Kaif finally goes for a great 56 - caught by Wishart off Streak. <br>India is in trouble at 157 for 8 and ten overs left. Sarandeep joins Harbhajan at the crease with only Khan to follow. A few lusty blows follow from Harbhajan, but Sarandeep is caught by Carlisle off Marillier for 3 (169 for 9).<br> Khan joins Harbhajan for a desperate last wicket partnership. After some well placed shots from Harbhajan, last man out Khan holes out to Marillier at long on (off Hondo) for 2. India finish on 191 all out.<br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b><br>10 for 1 Mongia lbw Hondo 4 (off 14 balls)<br>38 for 2 Laxman ct Taibu b Hondo 20 (off 24 balls 3x4)<br>49 for 3 Ganguly b Hondo 11 (off 21 balls 1x4)<br>51 for 4 Dravid ct Wishart b Mbangwa 6 (off 11 balls 1x4) <br>137 for 5 Bangar ct Hondo b Marillier 36 (off 67 balls 3x4) <br>147 for 6 Ratra ct Marillier b G Flower 3 (off 12 balls) <br>155 for 7 Agarkar ct Streak b G Flower 3 (off 7 balls) <br>157 for 8 Kaif ct Wishart b Streak 56 (off 78 balls 2x4 1x6) <br>169 for 9 S Singh ct Carlisle b Marillier 3 (off 7 balls) <br>191 all out Khan ct Marillier b Hondo 2 (off 12 balls) <br>H Singh not out 24 (off 28 balls 3x4) <br><br><b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 17 for 1 <br>10 overs = 42 for 2<br>15 overs = 54 for 4<br>20 overs = 76 for 4 <br>25 over = 101 for 4 <br>30 overs = 117 for 4 <br>35 overs = 143 for 5 <br>40 overs = 157 for 8 <br>45 overs = 171 for 9 <br><br> <center><b>ZIMBABWE INNINGS</b><br>197 for 4 off 44.3 overs - Wins by 6 wickets</center><br>Campbell and Ebrahim cautiously open the innings, knowing they are without the support of the injured Andy Flower and have a long tail to protect. Agarkar makes the first breakthrough - bowling Ebrahim for 3 (11 for 1). Pinch-hitter Friend arrives and blasts an early boundary showing his intent. 14 taken off a Khan over - including four overthrows. The dangerous Friend is bowled off his pads by Khan for 15 (39 for 2). <br>With Friend back in the pavilion, Campbell and Grant Flower make steady progress at 4 an over. With a maiden, Harbhajan temporarily puts the breaks on (67 for 2 of 18 overs), but the batsmen continue to easily pick up the required run rate. The 50-run partnership comes up off 82 balls, the hundred soon follows in the 25th over. Zimbabwe looks well in control (needs 85 off 25 overs at 3.4), but a couple of quick wickets could change all that. Campbell reaches his third successive fifty in the series off a sedate 87 balls. He then goes deep for a huge six off Mongia to prove he is far from one-dimensional. <br>The 100-run partnership comes off 146 balls as the game drifts away from India. Taking his first ODI wicket, Sarandeep makes the breakthrough, Campbell stumped by Ratra for 71. <br>Carlisle comes to the crease with victory in sight, unless Ganguly can conjure up some magic. But this miracle looks less and less likely as the bowlers cannot produce anything to seriously worry the batsmen, who seem well set for victory. The run rate has dropped to 2 an over off last ten and Ganguly has run out of ideas and options. <br>After Carlisle and Flower move the score along, Flower is caught behind off Agarkar to give India a fleeting hope. Wishart hastens the end wth a massive six - five need to win, then ends it with another huge six to propel Zimbabwe to 197 for 4 off 44.2 overs for a crushing six wicket victory that was a stroll in the park. <br><br><b>FALL of WICKETS</b><br>11 for 1 Ebrahim b Agarkar 3 (off 12 balls) <br>39 for 2 Friend b Khan 15 (off 13 balls 2x4) <br>144 for 3 Campbell st Ratra b S Singh 71 (off 119 balls 7x4 1x6) <br>173 for 4 G Flower ct Ratra b Agarkar 49 (off 88 balls 7x4) <br>Carlisle not out 21 (off 33 balls 2x4) <br>Wishart not out 17 (off 12 balls 2x6)<br> <br><b>RUN RATE</b><br>5 overs = 11 for 0<br>10 overs = 44 for 2 <br>15 overs = 63 for 2 <br>20 overs = 74 for 2 <br>25 overs = 107 for 2 <br>30 overs = 134 for 2 <br>35 overs = 144 for 3 <br>40 overs = 175 for 4 <br><br> <b> TEAMS</b><br>Changes: Anil Kumble is unfit and is replaced by Sarandeep Singh, while Andy Flower (injury) and Gary Brent (sickness) are replaced by Pommie Mbangwa and Douglas Hondo.<br>India: Ganguly, Mongia, Bangar, Dravid, Laxman, Kaif, Ratra, Agarkar, S Singh, H Singh, Khan.<br>Zimbabwe: Campbell, Wishart, Carlisle, Ebrahim, G Flower, Friend, Streak, Marillier, Taibu, Mbangwa, Hondo. <br><br><b>MATCH OFFICIALS</b><br>Umpires: DD Sharma & V Chopra <br>Third Umpire: S Shastri <br>Match Referee: JR Reid <br><br><b>PITCH REPORT</b><br>"A typical Indian pitch - rock hard and dry as a bone with a few tiny cracks.
