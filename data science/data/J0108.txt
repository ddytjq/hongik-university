<h1>Gordon chases down Bodine, wins at Pocono</h1>

LONG POND, Pa. -- Jeff Gordon passed Geoff Bodine with 54 laps to go and never let up, easily winning the NASCAR Winston Cup UAW-GM Teamwork 500 at
Pocono Raceway.
<p>
Gordon benefited most from a decision to go with a four-tire
change during a pit stop on the 136th lap.  Bodine decided to go
with only two new tires, and Gordon was able to run Bodine down
and grab the lead. 
<p>
Gordon really showed the strength of his Chevrolet Monte Carlo
as he held nearly a four-second lead over the Ford Thunderbird
of second place Ricky Rudd.  Bodine fought his way by Mark
Martin to take third, with Martin fourth and Bobby Hamilton, who
passed Morgan Shepherd on the 196th lap, in fifth. 
<p>
Gordon atoned for the biggest blunder
of his brief career Sunday. After toying with the field a year ago, Gordon missed a shift on
a restart with seven laps remaining and watched helplessly as the
leaders drove away while he battled to hang on for a 16th-place
finish.
<p>
&quot;We just hung in there all day long,&quot; Gordon said. &quot;I don't know if this
place owed us one or not, but we've run very strong here.  We've
been capable of winning here many times and something's
happened, so you can imagine what was going through my mind at 
the end of this race.&quot;
<p>
But this time there was no reason to be embarrassed. In fact,
the fourth-year driver nearly did that to a field of 41 cars. He
led seven times while leading for 94 of 200 laps in his Hendrick
Motorsports Chevrolet, and collected $96,980 from a purse totaling
$1.2 million.
<p>
Gordon became the first driver this year to surpass $1 million
in earnings. His total for the season is $1,069,482, and his career
earnings stand at $7,967,801.
<p>
The victory was the 14th of his career and second in a row for
Gordon. No other driver has won more than twice this year.
<p>
Winston Cup points leader Dale Earnhardt's car went sour with 
about 75 laps remaining, denying him an eighth consecutive Top 5 finish.  The results will put Earnhardt, Terry Labonte and 
Gordon within 64 points of each other after 13 events. Gordon reduced his deficit from 182 points to 64.
<p>
Earnhardt has dominated the points race through consistency.
Beginning with a fourth-place finish at Bristol on March 31, he had
seven consecutive top-five results in his Chevrolet. He wound up 32nd
after failing to finish a race for the first time since last
August, a span of 22 events.
<p>
Despite a lead that now is 52 points over defending race winner
Labonte, Earnhardt, seeking an unprecedented eighth series title, had expressed frustration because he had
not won since March 10 in Atlanta.
<p>
Rudd has had several solid performances this season and finished
with a season-best second.  Rudd has a 13-season streak of
recording at least one win on the line this year. 
<p>
&quot;This is a brand new race car and we just need to get busy and
build some more like it,&quot; Rudd said.  &quot;It was not a winning car
today. I think we were close. It's something we can make
adjustments on.&quot; 
<p>
Several drivers had outstanding performances while many of the
usual front-runners didn't race up to their usual standards. 
Martin, who has had a rough year on the Winston Cup circuit,
Bodine, Hamilton and Shepherd were among those who really showed
a lot during today's race. 
<p>
&quot;The last year and a half has been pretty agonizing for all of
us,&quot; said Bodine, who posted his best finish since winning the
Tyson Holly Farms 400 at North Wilkesboro Raceway in October
1994.  Bodine's younger brother Todd finished 10th. 
<p>
The early part of the race was highlighted by a terrific battle
between Derrike Cope and Hut Stricklin, although both have had
their efforts crippled by mechanical and body problems. 
<p>
Rusty Wallace, who led in the middle of the race and was
particularly strong, fell off the pace with transmission
problems after a pit stop on the 130th lap.  Wallace was
shooting for his first superspeedway victory since 1994, when he
won at North Carolina, Michigan and swept a pair at Dover. 
<p>
&quot;The car was running great, lightning fast,&quot; Wallace said. 
&quot;Knowing we're going to Michigan and Indianapolis with a hot new
car and a hot engine makes me feel good.&quot; 
<p>
Dale Jarrett, who came into the event fourth in points, left the
race on the 38th lap with a blown engine.  Jarrett suffered a
rib injury during practice on Friday and didn't qualify until
Saturday. 
<p>
Ernie Irvan, Jarrett's teammate, was the victim of an early
accident, as was Bobby Labonte.  Irvan was 15th in points and
Bobby Labonte was ninth coming into Sunday's event. 
<p>
Gordon's 14th career victory ties him with Dick Hutcherson and
LeeRoy Yarbrough for 35th place on the all-time Winston Cup wins
list. 
<p>
This was the third time a race was won from the pole this year,
a feat accomplished last by Gordon at Dover two weeks ago.
Gordon will pad his winnings by $7,600 for winning from the
pole. He is the first winner from the pole at Pocono since 
Bodine won here in 1994, and is only the sixth racer to win from 
the pole in 37 races at Pocono. 
<p>
