        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="1">W</a>hen does Barclays report
        financial results?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The full year and interim results are announced in
        February and August respectively. The 2001 Results were announced on 14th February 2002.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="2">W</a>here can I obtain a copy of the
        Report and Accounts?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The reports can be accessed on this web site. A summary
        document will be sent out to shareholders in March each year.&nbsp; Extra copies may be
        ordered by e-mail from <a href="mailto:shareholder.enquiries@barclays.co.uk">shareholder.enquiries@barclays.co.uk</a>
        or by phoning 0117 935 3848.&nbsp;</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b><a name="2a">W</a>hat is Barclays Sharestore?</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Barclays Sharestore allows shareholders to hold their
        Barclays shares electronically in uncertificated form, within a nominee account
        administered by our registrars.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The benefits of holding shares in this way can be
        summarised as follows:&nbsp;</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">You will receive an annual statement setting out details
        of how many Barclays shares you own and you need no longer worry about losing your share
        certificates.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">When you sell your shares in Barclays Sharestore you
        will&nbsp;receive your money more quickly and will receive a £2 discount on the
        commission that you are charged.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b><a name="2b">H</a>ow do I join Barclays Sharestore?</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">You should contact our Registrar on 0870 702 0196 and ask
        for a Barclays Sharestore Deposit Form and a set of terms and conditions.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b><a name="2c">W</a>hat do I do if I want to sell my
        shares held in Barclays Sharestore or buy more?</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">If you decide to sell some or all of the shares you hold
        in Barclays Sharestore or buy more, you should phone Barclays Stockbrokers on 0845 604
        0077. They will ask you for your Shareholder Reference Number (SRN) which will appear on
        your Statement of Entitlement and will take your instructions to buy or sell over the
        telephone.&nbsp;</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">If you hold shares in Barclays Sharestore you can only
        buy or sell them through Barclays Stockbrokers.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b><a name="2d">H</a>ow much will it cost me to buy or
        sell shares through Barclays Sharestore?</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Commission is charged as follows:</font></p>

        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Stamp Duty reserve tax currently at 0.5% is payable on
        all share purchases. If you hold your shares in Barclays Sharestore you will receive a £2
        discount on the commission charged on any sale of Barclays shares.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><strong><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a name="W">W</a>hat is Barclays e-view?</font></strong></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Barclays e-view is a service offered by our Registrar,
        Computershare Investor Services PLC. Barclays e-view allows shareholders to receive their
        Annual Report or Annual Review and Summary Financial Statement, The Notice of Annual
        General Meeting and other shareholder documents electronically.&nbsp; With this service
        shareholders can download only those pages of shareholder documents that are of particular
        interest. Shareholders can also appoint a proxy online, that is someone to vote for you at
        shareholder meetings, and to check your shareholding at any time, day or night.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="3">D</a>oes Barclays pay a cash
        dividend?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Yes, Barclays PLC has paid dividends on its ordinary
        shares every year without interruption since its incorporation in 1896.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Barclays usually pay dividends twice yearly in May and
        October. Details of the amount of dividend to be paid appear in the half year and year end
        Financial Results which are announced in August and February each financial year and in
        the Interim Report advertisement and Annual Report and Annual Review and Summary Financial
        Statement sent to all shareholders in March.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="4">W</a>hen was your most recent
        dividend paid?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The second interim dividend for the year ended 31st December
        2001 of 43.5p was paid on 26th April 2002 in respect of shares registered in the books of
        the Company at the close of business on 1st March 2001. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="5">C</a>an I take my dividend in shares?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Shareholders may have their dividends reinvested in
        Barclays PLC shares by participating in the Dividend Reinvestment Plan. The plan is
        available to all shareholders provided that they do not live in or are subject to the
        jurisdiction of any country where their participation in the plan would require Barclays
        or the plan administrator to take action to comply with local government or regulatory
        procedures or any similar formalities. Any shareholder wishing to obtain details of the
        plan and a mandate form should contact The Plan Administrator to Barclays, PO Box 82, The
        Pavilions, Bridgwater Road, Bristol, BS99 7NH. Those wishing to participate for the first
        time in the plan should send their completed mandate form to the plan administrator before
        10th September 2002 for it to be applicable to the payment of the interim dividend on
        1st October 2002. Existing participants should take no action unless they wish to alter
        their current mandate instructions in which case they should contact the plan
        administrator. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="6">C</a>an I receive my cash dividend
        directly into my bank or building society account?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Yes, you should complete a Dividend Payment Instruction
        Form and return it to the Registrar. Click here to download the <a href="forms.html">form</a>.</font></p>

        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="7">W</a>hen do I receive my dividend tax
        voucher?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">Barclays issue a consolidated tax voucher for the
        financial year in late October to those shareholders who have their dividends mandated to
        their bank or building society account. Shareholders who have a dividend cheque sent to
        their registered address receive a voucher with each payment.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="8">W</a>hat do I do if I need a
        duplicate tax voucher?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">You should contact the Registrar who will issue a
        duplicate. There is an administration charge of £5.87 for each voucher requested. Cheques
        should be made payable to Computershare Investor Services PLC and should be mailed with
        your request.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <font SIZE="2"><p></font><strong><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a name="8a">A</a>s an
        ex-Woolwich shareholder what are the tax implications following the acquisition by
        Barclays?</font></strong></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">The Inland Revenue confirmed to us that UK tax paying
        shareholders who held their Woolwich shares as a capital asset would obtain
        &quot;roll-over&quot; relief, and therefore have no UK capital gains tax or corporation
        tax liability, to the extent that they received Barclays shares under the Offer. To the
        extent that shareholders received cash under the Offer, they may have a UK tax liability
        depending upon their individual circumstances. UK shareholders who received their Woolwich
        shares on the conversion of the Woolwich in July 1997 will not have a tax base cost in
        respect of such shares. Such shareholders receiving new Barclays shares will have a nil
        base cost in such shares, and any cash received under the Offer will be a capital gain. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="9">D</a>oes Barclays offer a direct
        purchase programme?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">No.</font></p>

        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="10">H</a>ow do I confirm the number of
        shares that I hold?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">You can access your personal share information on this
        website by entering your Shareholder Reference Number (SRN). This number can be found on
        your share certificates or Statement of Entitlement (for those in Barclays Sharestore).
        Alternatively you can phone the Registrar on 0870 702 0196.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><a href="faqs.html#top"><img src="../images/backtotop.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" width="122" height="20"></a></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2"><strong><a name="11">W</a>hat should I do if I lose my
        share certificate?</strong></font></p>
        <p><font face="Verdana" size="2">You should contact the Registrar and ask for a
        replacement. You will be sent a form of indemnity to sign and return. A new certificate
        will then be sent to you. Please note that there is a small charge for this service. If
        you hold your shares through a CREST account, you will not hold any share certificate(s).
        Instead, you should receive a statement indicating the number of shares you hold from the
        broker or financial institution through whom you bought the shares. If you lose this,
        replacement statements can be issued as required.</font></p>
