<table><td><img src="./images/litespd.gif"></td><td><b>
Imagine taking a trip at the speed of light. The "ultimate speed limit" 
according to <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/einstein/index.html">
Einstein's</a> Theory of 
<a href="http://www.ncsa.uiuc.edu/Cyberia/NumRel/UnwindClockUni.html">Special 
Relativity</a>; c = 186,000 miles/sec = 300,000 km/s = 3 x 10<sup> 10 </sup> 
cm/s (= 1 light-year/year).  The 
effects of Special Relativity will have a profound effect upon space travel.
Because mass increases as one's velocity approaches c, travel near the speed 
of light will be very expensive and will require spacecraft designs far 
different from those imagined on Sci-Fi TV programs.  A phenomenon called 
<i>time dilation</i>, will cause space explorers traveling near the speed of 
light to age more slowly than their compatriots left behind on earth.  For now 
we will ignore the details and set off on a journey to explore the Universe.
</b></td></table>

A suite of 
<a href="http://www.jpl.nasa.gov/mars/index.html">NASA Missions</a> are now 
carrying out detailed examintation of Mars. Mars Pathfinder landed on Mars 
in July 1997; its Soujourner rover transmitted back spectacular images and 
other data for over 3 months, despite a planned lifetime of 1 week.
Communication was lost with the spacecraft in October 1997.  Since May 1998
<a href="http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/mgs/index.html">Mars Global Surveyor</a> 
has been orbiting the planet imaging and mapping its surface.
The <a href="http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msp98/orbiter/">Mars Climate Orbiter</a>
mission, which was to begin operation in September 1999 was lost due to a 
<a href="http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msp98/news/mco990930.html">navigation error</a> caused by confusion between metric and English units.
The recent failures of the Mars Climate Orbiter
and its companion, the
<a href="http://mars.jpl.nasa.gov/msp98/lander/">Mars Polar Lander</a>,
which lost contact during Mars landing in December 1999.are reminders of 
the continuing difficulty of space exploration.
