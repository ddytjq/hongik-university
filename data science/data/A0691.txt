												<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="1">
							How do I input my One Time Password?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							Your One Time Password is a 16-digit password made up of alphabetic and numeric characters. It will be sent to you on your Internet Banking Welcome Letter. To reveal the password it will be necessary to peel back the password strip at the bottom right hand side of the letter.<p>Letters must be input as UPPERCASE, numbers are identified by an underscore.<p>The one time password is only used once. When the password has been entered correctly, you will be invited to change it to a password of your choice.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="2">
							How do I input my Security Questions?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							When registering for Internet Banking, you will be required to answer three out of a possible six security questions. <p>Please note that your answers are case-sensitive; if you used upper and lower-case letters when you first answer the security questions, you should continue to type your answers using the same format.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="3">
							How do I input my Passwords?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							When registering for Internet Banking, you will be required to choose a password.  This password must be between 6 and 16 characters, and must contain at least one alphabetic and one numeric character. Alphabetic characters can be in either upper or lower case. However, the password must always be input in the same format.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="4">
							What do I do if I have forgotten my Password?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							If you have forgotten your Internet Banking password, please call the Internet Banking Help Desk on <nobr>08456 000 333</nobr>.  For security reasons you will have to re-register for the service. We will arrange for a new One Time Password to be sent to you. This will enable you to re-register for the service.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="5">
							What do I do if my password details are invalid?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							Your Internet Banking password is input in upper or lower case and must be entered in exactly the same format each time you log on to the service.<p>If you are unable to remember your password, or the details are showing as invalid, please call through to the Internet Banking Help Desk on <nobr>08456 000 333</nobr>.  We will arrange for a new One Time Password to be sent to you.  This will enable you to re-register for the service.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="6">
							What do I do if the answer to my security question is invalid?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							Your answers are case sensitive, and must be entered in exactly the same format each time you log on.<p>If you receive the message "the answer to the security question is incorrect please call IB support for assistance", for security reasons you will be blocked from the Internet Banking service. Please call the Internet Banking Help Desk on 08456 000 333 to have your access re-set. <p>Once you regain access to the service, you may wish to visit the Admin section within Internet Banking.  This section allows you to change both your Internet Banking password, and security answers.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="7">
							What security information do I require when using Internet Banking?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							You will need: <ul><li>Your ten digit customer identification.<li>Your password.<li>The answer to one of the three security questions you have completed.</ul>							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="8">
							Can my password be anything I choose?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							Yes, the password is entirely of your choice. However, it must be between 6 and 16 characters and must contain at least one alphabetic and one numeric character. Alphabetic characters can be either upper or lower case, and must be input in the same format after selection.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="9">
							What are my responsibilities with my password?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							You must keep your password secret at all times and not divulge it to anyone, including Yorkshire Bank employees. If you believe that someone else knows your password please change it immediately. <b>Our staff will never ask for your Internet Banking password</b>.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
														<p class="contenttextbold"><a name="10">
							Where can I find my customer number?							</p>
							<p class="contenttext">
							Your customer number is issued in your welcome pack for Telephone Banking. If you cannot find this number please call the Telephone Banking Help Desk on 0845 7 365 365, who will arrange for a copy of the number to be posted out to you.							</a></p><p align="right"><a href="#top"><img src="../g_images/but_top.gif" alt="Back to Top" border="0" /></a></p>
