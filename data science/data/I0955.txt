 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Leeds United AFC</H1><H2 class="entry">Elland Road</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Ground1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Ground1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Elland Road" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Elland Road</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Ground2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Ground2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Elland Road" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Elland Road</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Ground3-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Ground3-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Elland Road" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Elland Road</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Ground5-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Ground5-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Cheese Wedge 
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>The Cheese Wedge 
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>For a Premiership ground Elland Road isn't great for visiting fans. Away
fans are allocated a section of the South Stand (normally just the South
East corner stand aka The Cheese Wedge) and whilst it boasts
bars/food kiosks and a Ladbrokes kiosk, seats and a roof, it does suffer
from a chronic lack of toilets (only 4 ladies toilets in total, and not
many more gents) and a general poor state of repair. 
</P><P>No seats in the Cheese Wedge are obscured and only the back few rows of
the south stand may have the odd annoying post.
</P><P>Previously a rumored ice hockey arena in the shadow of Elland Road may
have forced major ground renovations in coming seasons, however we have
had a report that this is no longer going ahead.
</P><P>Elland road is a tad unusual because the players tunnel and directors box
are not on the half way line, but about 2/3 of the way along. This is
because at some point in history the entire stadium (including the pitch)
was moved several hundred yards to accommodate a new stand. After the move
the club decided not to move the directors seats or players tunnel.
</P><P>Leeds have recently been granted permission to build a new ground on the outskirts of Leeds. 
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=LS110ES&Title=Elland+Road+(Leeds+United+AFC)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Ticket Info</H3><P><B>Fans are reminded that tickets for visiting stands should be purchased through their own club, not the club they are visiting.</B></P><p></P></p><p>Ticket prices for visiting fans range from £20-£27 for adults and from £14-£18 for concessions.
</P></p><p>The ticket office can be contacted on 0113 2261000 or via <a class="entry" href="mailto:tickets@lufc.co.uk">email</a>. Ticket information can be obtained by 
clicking <a class="entry" href="http://www.lufc.com/tickets/home_games.asp">here</a>
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>Leeds is not the best place for a pre match beer. Some people report
that The Peacock across from the ground lets away fans in for some games.
The Drysalters on the Leeds Ring-Road also lets both home and away fans in
and is only about 10mins from
the ground. There isn't a lot more choice around the ground and those that
do exist aren't away friendly.
</P><P>There is a lot of choice in the town centre, but many pubs are less than
friendly to away fans, club colours are not recommended.
</P></p><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Dry-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Dry-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Drysalters
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Drysalters
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Peacock
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Leeds-Peacock-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Leeds-Peacock-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Peacock
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Peacock
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Peacock is situated right opposite the ground. We have had mixed
reports on their acceptance of away fans. Several concur that Man Utd and
Sunderland are normally not welcomed but other clubs should be OK, whilst
others have stated it is home only. It is likely that the door policy is
governed by who the visiting team are.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>The excellent United Fisheries chippie is right opposite the ground, next door
is The Cracked Egg Cafe and two doors away is a newsagent. There are also
normally a few burger vans around. There is a Burger King on the Leeds
Ring Road, approx 10 mins walk.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is LS11 0ES</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Elland Road is very easy to find from the motorway and very well signposted.
Simply exit the M621 at junction 2 onto A643 Elland Road.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>Whilst the main club car park normally has very limited parking there are 
plenty of overspill car parks off Elland Road itself. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>Leeds station is quite a trek from Elland Road (around  2 miles). Catch
one of many regular match day buses or a taxi, especially because the
route isn't particularly obvious.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Leeds
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Leeds has plenty of hotels to choose from.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/leeds.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Leeds do have a reputation to trouble and whilst the days of The Service Crew
in the 80's are behind us, trouble is not uncommon especially in the town
centre. However as normal it a case of the few giving the entire fan base
a bad name.
</P><P>It may be best to keep club colours out of sight, especially in the town
centre.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>The installation of new CCTV cameras that track fans all the
way from the town centre to ground and over 100 stewards now make visiting
Elland Road much safer. It also allows the visible police presence kept
to a minimum (normally a dozen) until called upon.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Leeds is a very good night out, plenty of choice whatever your tastes.
Players have been known to frequent The Majestyk nightclub in the city
centre.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>The White Rose shopping centre is just a couple of miles south of Elland
Road, and Leeds Centre is just a couple of miles north.
