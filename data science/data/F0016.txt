<BR><A NAME="2"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>2. The Concepts</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Imagine a spaceship flying through
space at 1000 m/s.&nbsp; It is deep in interstellar space with nothing
around it.&nbsp; What forces are acting on it?&nbsp; The answer: none!</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>It will continue to fly through
space at 1000 m/s forever if no force acts on it.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The ONLY reason why objects will
change velocity (direction and/or speed) is if a force is applied to it.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>What is a force you ask?&nbsp; A
force is an external push or pull.&nbsp; A force is also a vector.&nbsp;
It has a magnitude and a direction associated with it.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Right now gravity is exerting a force on you: it is pulling you toward
the center of the earth.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Also, the only reason an object will
change displacement is if it has some velocity.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Suppose now that the hand of God
reaches down and starts pushing the spaceship forward.&nbsp; There is now
a force applied to the ship.&nbsp; With any force applied, an acceleration
is induced.&nbsp; With our new acceleration, the velocity starts to increase.&nbsp;
If God doesn't stop pushing the ship, then soon it will go very fast!</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-2><A HREF="#0">[Back to Top]</A></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><IMG SRC="Line3.jpg" HEIGHT=2 WIDTH=400>
<BR><A NAME="3"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>3. Look, I just
want to make my guy jump</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Ok, I'm getting to this.&nbsp; We
have our jumping dude Joe.&nbsp; Joe's</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>initial position is on the floor
or platform that is not moving.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The user presses a key and Joe should
leap into the air.&nbsp; You need</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>to keep track of three things about
Joe:&nbsp; His position, velocity,</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>and acceleration.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Initially, his position will be at
the floor.&nbsp; Once the key is hit,</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>we simply give Joe a very fast velocity
in the upward direction.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Then every frame/sec/update/whatever
decrease his upward velocity</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>by a constant amount.&nbsp; That's
it!</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The constant decrease is actually
an acceleration in the downward</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>direction.&nbsp; The horizontal
motion of Joe is unaffected.&nbsp; So while</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe is flying through the air, you
can change his forward/backward</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>movement as normal.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Side note:&nbsp; In real life, it
is impossible to move forward/backward</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>when jumping straight up.&nbsp;
But I find games that allow forward/</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>backward movement while jumping
more playable and fun than just jumping</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>straight up.&nbsp; And who said
games are realistic anyway?</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>What should be the acceleration that
we are applying?&nbsp; On earth, the</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>acceleration of Joe will be 9.81
m/s^2 in the downward direction.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Unfortunately, meters per second
squared means nothing to the computer.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The acceleration must be determined
based on your game's update rate.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Usually guessing and tweaking is
the best way to achieve this.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>So our pseudo-code is as such:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.pos.y = 0; (Or wherever floor
is)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.vel.y = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.accel = 10;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.jumping = NO;</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; If (jump key is pressed)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.vel.y
= 100;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.jumping
= YES;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (Joe.jumping == YES)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
/* Move Joe if we are jumping */</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.vel.y
= Joe.vel.y - Joe.accel</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.pos.y
= Joe.pos.y + Joe.vel.y</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (Joe.pos.y &lt;= 0)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
/* Check to see if Joe has hit floor */</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.vel.y
= 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.jumping
= NO;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>End Loop</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>For the first time the jump key is
pressed, the position of Joe will</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>suddenly jump off the floor.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>As he goes higher, Joe begins to
slow down because the velocity</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>is constantly decremented.&nbsp;
Joe will eventually stop moving up, and</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>begin to move down.&nbsp; He will
move faster and faster until</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>he reaches the floor again.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe's velocity and position in the
y direction at the end of each loop</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>will appear as such:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop0 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp;&nbsp; 0&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y =&nbsp;&nbsp; 0</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop1 : Joe.vel.y = 100&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 100</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop2 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 90&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 190</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop3 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 80&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 270</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop4 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 70&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 340</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop5 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 60&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 400</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop6 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 50&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 450</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop7 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 40&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 490</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop8 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 30&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 520</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop9 : Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 20&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 540</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop10: Joe.vel.y =&nbsp; 10&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 550</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop11: Joe.vel.y =&nbsp;&nbsp;
0&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.pos.y = 550</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop12: Joe.vel.y = -10&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 540</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop13: Joe.vel.y = -20&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 520</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop14: Joe.vel.y = -30&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 490</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop15: Joe.vel.y = -40&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 450</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop16: Joe.vel.y = -50&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 400</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop17: Joe.vel.y = -60&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 340</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop18: Joe.vel.y = -70&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 270</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop19: Joe.vel.y = -80&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 190</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop20: Joe.vel.y = -90&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = 100</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop21: Joe.vel.y =-100&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y =&nbsp;&nbsp; 0</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Notice that Joe is going very fast
when he hits the floor.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>If Joe does not hit the floor, or
the floor disappears (i.e. he jumps</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>off a platform) then eventually
Joe will be going very fast.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>We need to give him a limiting velocity.&nbsp;
This is the fastest velocity</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe can go by falling.&nbsp; In
real life this is caused by air resistance.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>This is easy enough to do.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The modified pseudo-code is such:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.pos.y = 0; (Or wherever floor
is)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.vel.y = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.accel = 10;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Joe.jumping = NO;</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (jump key is pressed)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.vel.y
= 100;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.jumping
= YES;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (Joe.jumping == YES)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
/* Move Joe if we are jumping */</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; if (Joe.vel.y
> -100)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; /* Limit
Joe's velocity to -100 */</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.vel.y = Joe.vel.y - Joe.accel</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
Joe.pos.y = Joe.pos.y + Joe.vel.y</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (Joe.pos.y &lt;= 0)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
/* Check to see if Joe has hit floor */</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.vel.y
= 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Joe.jumping
= NO;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>End Loop</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The added loop will not decrease
Joe's velocity if he is already moving</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>downward at a speed of -100.&nbsp;
Thus we have added his limiting velocity.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-2><A HREF="#0">[Back to Top]</A></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><IMG SRC="Line3.jpg" HEIGHT=2 WIDTH=400>
<BR><A NAME="4"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>4. Other dimensions</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Ok, the above example is all well
and good, but some game sprites</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>do not just go up and down.&nbsp;
Some go side to side while they are</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>jumping.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Easy!&nbsp; It just so happens that
the x and y values of velocity and</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>acceleration are independent!&nbsp;
So while you resolve the y position</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>and velocity of Joe, you can move
Joe in the x direction normally.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>It will appear that Joe follows a
parabolic path, which is what he</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>would follow in the real world.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The same goes for 3 dimensions.&nbsp;
x,y,z velocities and accelerations are</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>all independent of one another.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-2><A HREF="#0">[Back to Top]</A></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><IMG SRC="Line3.jpg" HEIGHT=2 WIDTH=400>
<BR><A NAME="5"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>5. Spaceman Spiff</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Another example where kinematics
applies is an asteroids type game.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>You fly around in a zero-g environment,
but can thrust forward in</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>any direction.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>In this case gravity is not a factor.&nbsp;
What do we use instead?&nbsp; We</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>use the thrust of the spaceship
as our acceleration.&nbsp; We simply break</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>up our acceleration vector into
x and y directions, and apply the same</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>techniques as above.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>To implement this we get the pseudo-code:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>pos.x = 0;&nbsp; pos.y = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>vel.x = 0;&nbsp; vel.y = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>acc.x = 0;&nbsp; acc.y = 0;</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Loop</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; if (thrust key pressed)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; {</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; resolve_direction_vector;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; acc.x =
scale_x * 10;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; acc.y =
scale_y * 10;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; }</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; vel.x = vel.x + acc.x;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; vel.y = vel.y + acc.y;</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; pos.x = pos.x + vel.x;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; pos.y = pos.y + vel.y;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>End Loop</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Now we have a step we have not seen
before: resolve_direction_vector.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>We need this because the ship can
move in any direction.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>What this step does is get the appropriate
directions vector and</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>break it down into an x vector and
y vector.&nbsp; Once broken down, the</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>resolve_direction_vector step will
set scale_x and scale_y.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><IMG SRC="Vectors.gif" HEIGHT=225 WIDTH=225><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Given an angle for our direction
from 0 to 365, we can determine</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>the scale_x and scale_y variables.&nbsp;
There are various ways to</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>do this, but an easy way (not optimized)
is to use trig:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>scale_x = cos(angle);</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>scale_y = sin(angle);</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The trig functions will take care
of negative values.&nbsp; That is, if</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>the ship is facing West (angle =
180 degrees), the values for</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>scale_x and scale_y:&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
scale_x = -1;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; scale_y = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>And our ship will accelerate in
the negative x direction.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>How about if the ship is pointed
in a Northeast direction (angle = 45)?</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>We get:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>scale_x = .707</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>scale_y = .707</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Which means the ship is accelerating
by 7.07 in the y direction, and</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>by 7.07 in the x direction.&nbsp;
The illusion you see the ship</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>accelerate by 10 in a 45 degree
direction.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>You will want to limit the maximum
velocity of the ship, or else</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>it will soon be going faster than
the user can see.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>If you want, some people like a damping
field.&nbsp; That means eventually,</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>the ship slows down by itself.&nbsp;
To do this, simply decrement the</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>velocity towards zero every loop.&nbsp;
(e.g. if velocity is positive,</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>subtract; if velocity is negative,
add)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-2><A HREF="#0">[Back to Top]</A></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<BR><A NAME="6"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>6. The REAL equations</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>This section explains the real equations.&nbsp;
If you wish to do some</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>more complex kinematics, this section
will give you everything you</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>need to resolve the equations.&nbsp;
If not, then the above text is</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>plenty for a lot of applications.&nbsp;
For more explanations, consult</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>a basic physics book.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>We define three key variables with
respect to an object:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>displacement, velocity, and acceleration.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Force is related to acceleration
in the following way:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>(Incidentally, Newton's second law
of motion)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;Eqn 1</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>; F = M * a</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>In English: Force equals mass times
acceleration.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>So in our spaceship example, the
hand of god can cause a small</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>interceptor with little mass to
accelerate faster than a huge</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>dreadnought with much mass.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The relationship between displacement,
velocity, and acceleration</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>are:</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;Eqn 2</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;&nbsp; s = s0 + v0*t + 0.5*a*t^2</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The current displacement is equal
to the initial displacement, plus</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>the velocity times time, plus one-half
times acceleration times</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>time squared.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;Eqn 3</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>; v = v0 + a*t</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The current velocity equals the
initial velocity plus acceleration</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>times time.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;Eqn 4</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>; v^2 - v0^2 = 2*a*s</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The current velocity squared minus
the initial velocity equals two</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>times acceleration time displacement.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>With these 4 magic equations, almost
all physics motion problems</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>can be resolved.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>These equations are all based on
constant acceleration (which fits</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>in fine with most computer applications)</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-2><A HREF="#0">[Back to Top]</A></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><IMG SRC="Line3.jpg" HEIGHT=2 WIDTH=400>
<BR><A NAME="7"></A><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>7. How do these
equations work?</FONT></FONT></B><B><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT></B>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Let us take a ball thrown directly
up into the air.&nbsp; This is a</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>one-dimensional situation, because
the ball is only going up or</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>down.&nbsp; Let's take s0 to be
the position where we release the ball</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>up into the air.&nbsp; Since we
have an initial position, I'm going to</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>be referring to the current displacement
of the ball as position.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The term position will be reference
from s0.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Ok, so we throw the ball up into
the air. The instant we release</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>the ball (t = 0) we know:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>s0 = 0;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>v0 = 10 m/s in the up direction
(about 22 miles per hour)</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>a = ?</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Hmm. What is the acceleration?&nbsp;
For any object near the earth's</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>surface, the force due to gravity
can be found with:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;Eqn 5</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>; Fg = M*g</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Where M is the mass and g is the
gravitational constant.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>For earth, g = 9.81 m/s^2.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>A bit of math now.&nbsp; Combine
Eqns 1 &amp; 5</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>; Fg = M*a&nbsp; ->&nbsp; M*g =
M*a&nbsp; ->&nbsp; g = a</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>;</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Whoa! The masses seem to have canceled!&nbsp;
That's interesting.&nbsp; For</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>any falling object, the acceleration
is not affected by the mass.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Does this mean that a hippo will
fall as fast as a Ping-Pong ball?</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Yes they will: in a vacuum.&nbsp;
But I digress...</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>So the acceleration of the ball equals
g, or 9.81 m/s^2.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>But the acceleration is in the DOWNWARD
direction, so we say a = -g</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Using Eqn 3, we get:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; v&nbsp; =&nbsp; v0 + a*t&nbsp;
=&nbsp; 10 m/s +&nbsp; (-9.81 m/s^2) * 0 s&nbsp; =&nbsp; 10 m/s.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>If we use Eqn 2, we also get s =
0. Big deal, huh?</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Let's take a look at 0.5 sec later:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; v&nbsp; =&nbsp; 10 + (-9.81)
* 0.5 s&nbsp; = 5.1 m/s</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The ball has slowed down, which is
what we expect.&nbsp; Eventually it</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>will stop.&nbsp; To find this, we
simply set v = 0 and solve for t.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>v&nbsp; =&nbsp; 0&nbsp; =&nbsp;
10 + (-9.81) * t</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Solving for t, we find t = 1.019
seconds.&nbsp; Thus 1.019 seconds after</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>we throw the ball up, it stops in
mid-air.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Ok, we know it stops, but where is
it?&nbsp; To find this, we use Eqn 2:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; s&nbsp; =&nbsp; 0&nbsp; +&nbsp;
10 * 1.019 + 0.5 * (-9.81) * 1.019^2&nbsp; =&nbsp; 5.1 meters</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>That is 5.1 meters above the spot
where we released it.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>What goes up must come down, but
how fast?&nbsp; How fast is our ball</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>going when we catch it?&nbsp; When
we catch the ball, we know that</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; s = 0.&nbsp; So use Eqn 4.&nbsp;
After some calculations, we find</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; v = sqrt( v0^2 ).&nbsp; That's
easy, right? v = v0.&nbsp; Well...sort of.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>There is actually two solutions to
this equation.&nbsp; +v0 and -v0.</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>What does this mean?&nbsp; It means
whenever the ball is at position</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; s = 0, then it is either
going up at v0 or down at v0.&nbsp; Since</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>we know it was going up when we
threw it, it must be coming down</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>at the same velocity.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>The last thing we find is what time
it comes down:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Use Eqn 3:</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>&nbsp; v&nbsp; =&nbsp; -v0&nbsp;
=&nbsp; v0 + (-9.81) * t</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>Result: t = 2.038 seconds.&nbsp;
If we throw a ball up at 10 m/s, then</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>2.038 seconds later, we will find
the same ball in our hand.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>

<P><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>This section was just to show how
the equations work.&nbsp; In an</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>actual program, you wouldn't simply
stick these equations in because</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>you would have to keep track of
t for every single object.&nbsp; Every time</FONT></FONT>
<BR><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1>something jumped, you'd reset that
object's personal t.</FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Verdana"><FONT SIZE=-1></FONT></FONT>
