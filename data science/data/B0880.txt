      <p><b>The calculation</b><br>
        Our income criteria is usually: </p>
      <table width="380" border="0" cellspacing="2" cellpadding="2">
        <tr align="left" valign="top"> 
          <td width="100" height="121" class="lefttext11"> 
            <p class="lefttext11bold">If your loan does not exceed 80% LTV, or<br>
              If your loan does not exceed 90% LTV and the mortgage term does 
              not exeed 20 years</p>
            </td>
          <td width="280" bgcolor="d1e0ef" height="121" class="lefttext11"> 
            <p class="blocktext"><i>Single Income</i></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><i>3.25 times main gross annual income </i></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p class="blocktext"><i>Joint Incomes</i></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><i>The higher of:<br>
                3.25 times main gross annual income plus lower gross annual income, 
                or<br>
                2.75 times joint gross annual incomes.</i></p>
            </blockquote>
            </td>
        </tr>
        <tr align="left" valign="top"> 
          <td width="100" height="94" class="lefttext11bold">If your loan is between 
            81% and 95% LTV </td>
          <td width="280" bgcolor="d1e0ef" height="94" class="lefttext11">
            <p><i>Single Income</i></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><i>3.00 times main gross annual income </i></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><i>Joint Incomes</i></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><i>The higher of:<br>
                3.00 times main gross annual income plus lower gross annual income, 
                or<br>
                2.50 times joint gross annual incomes.</i></p>
            </blockquote>
          </td>
        </tr>
        <tr align="left" valign="top">
          <td width="100" height="2" class="lefttext11"><b><span class="lefttext11bold">Self 
            Employed Applicants</span></b><span class="lefttext11bold"><i><br>
            </i></span><i> </i></td>
          <td width="280" bgcolor="d1e0ef" class="lefttext11"> 
            <p class="blocktext"><span class="blocktext"><i>3 times the average 
              of the last 3 years net profits from the last 3 years accounts.</i></span></p>
            </td>

      <p><u><b>Bonuses/Overtime/Commission</b></u><br>
        These may be taken into account depending on your circumstances and their 
        regularity. If payments are guaranteed, we normally add the annual gross 
        total to your annual gross income. If payments are regular but not guaranteed, 
        we normally use half of their value.<br>
        <br>
        <b> <u>Credit Agreements/Loans/Other Commitments</u><br>
        </b>We may reduce the amount you can borrow if we feel that by taking 
        on a mortgage you would be over committed. If we do take them into consideration, 
        we normally deduct the annual cost from your gross annual salary.<br>
        <br>
        <b><u>Employment</u></b><br>
        We normally write to your current employer or accountant asking for details 
        of your employment, and asking them to confirm details such as:<br>
        - Length of Employment <br>
        - Job Title <br>
        - Basic Annual Income <br>
        - Details of any regular overtime, bonus or commission <br>
        - Whether you are under any form of notice or redundancy <br>
        - Confirmation that your employment is full time and permanent <br>
        If you have been in your job for less than 6 months, details of your previous 
        employment over a 3 year period will need to be obtained and confirmed.<br>
        <br>
        <b><u>Self Employed</u></b><br>
        We normally write to your accountant and request the last 3 years accounts. 
        They must be completed and confirmed by your Accountant who should be 
        professionally qualified and have either of the following qualifications; 
        A.C.A. Chartered Accountant or A.C.C. A Certified and Corporate Accountant. 
        If you prepare your own accounts we need your Inland Revenue approved 
        assessments.<br>
        <br>
        <b><u>Flexibility</u></b><br>
        If your circumstances do not fit into the above criteria or if you are 
        self employed and do not have 3 years full accounts, please contact us 
        as we have the flexibility to regard each applicant as an individual. 
      </p>
