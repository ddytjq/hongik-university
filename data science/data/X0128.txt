<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>WESTON, Mass.&nbsp;-- Hassan Fofana walked into Middlesex Magic practice on Thursday night and began to do what he always does.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He smiled. He shook hands. He talked with his teammates. And then he smiled some more.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Hassan's just the greatest kid on the planet," Middlesex coach Mike Crotty said during his AAU team's workout here at the Rivers School in suburban Boston. "He's so fun to be around. He's a very good student, and he's a wonderful, wonderful human being."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The big kid from the West African nation of Togo isn't a bad little basketball prospect, either ? in large part because he's not little at all.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He didn't play as a sophomore at Massachusetts' Ayer High School&nbsp;-- he wasn't allowed due to the state's transfer rules&nbsp;-- but he averaged 15 points, 15 rebounds, seven blocks and four assists as a junior this past winter at Holy Name in Worcester.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But stats shmats. These are the numbers that matter on the Fofana front: 6-foot-9, 250 pounds. Dude's a big boy.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's a physical force," New England man-about-hoops Mike Procopio said after Wednesday's session. "There's a reason for him to be in the game: He can lean on anybody in the country."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he can do that for just about any program in the country.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Maryland, Wake Forest, Rhode Island, UConn, UMass and Boston University are among the schools that sent reps to Worcester last month to offer scholarships.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"But I have an open mind to everyone," Fofana said. "No favorites."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Not yet at least.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Fofana has other things to worry about. He needs an age waiver to play his senior season at Holy Name&nbsp;-- he turned 19 last month&nbsp;-- so there's a chance he might end up at a prep school next year.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>In the meantime, though, the Magic is his ticket to exposure.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He traveled last summer to Las Vegas and Orlando with the summer club, and he played in Providence, R.I., at the Hoop Group's Jam Fest earlier this spring. This coming weekend he'll head down to North Carolina's Triangle for the Tournament of Champions.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he's definitely found a home the Bay State since coming to this country almost two years ago and first joining Crotty's program for a September 2000 tournament in New Haven, Conn., at Yale.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>All because of a call from Ayer coach John Kelly.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He said he had a kid who wanted to play some fall basketball," Crotty said. "I told him I'd let him play but that he'd be the last guy off the bench."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Although not for long. Four high-major Division I coaches asked about him at halftime&nbsp;-- simply because he looked big and got up and down the floor a bit.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's worked out tremendously for Hassan and for the Middlesex Magic," Crotty continued. "I've never seen a guy his size run like he does&nbsp;-- he runs like a freakin' three&nbsp;-- and he's become fast friends with everybody."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Particularly with Casey Gibbons.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Cornell-bound shooting guard started to host Fofana at his family's Weston home before and after Magic practices and games last year.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He and my son really hit it off," said Gary Gibbons, Casey's father. "He spoke broken English, but he had the most unbelievable smile, and he's just grown on us. We treat him like another son."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Chores and all.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He does everything," Gibbons said. "He does the dishes. He makes sure his room is clean. He's better than the other kids. He's just a kind, gentle soul."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Which is great&nbsp;-- except, of course, when he's on the court.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Fofana started off rather raw a year and a half ago. Still is. But no wonder: He didn't play the game in Togo other than an occasional and ragged pickup game.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It wasn't like here," he said. "I never really got into basketball that much. I played soccer&nbsp;-- midfield&nbsp;-- but I'm not a soccer fan anymore. I'm a basketball fan now. I just saw everyone playing over here. It's a fun game."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And a game he's getting better at.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Fofana showed a deft passing touch during Wednesday's scrimmage. He rebounded well and kept the ball high, he hit some decent-looking 12- to 15-foot jumpers, and he finished gimmes around the basket.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's coming along," he said, "but it's taking time. When you put time into it, everything just goes step by step. I'm trying to use my body better and learn better footwork&nbsp;-- just trying to get good."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"His offense needs some work," Procopio added, "but who cares? He rebounds and plays defense, and that makes him a hot commodity in college basketball these days."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>A hot commodity? For Crotty and his Middlesex kids, he's just Hassan, the big African with the big smile.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>A (FUTURE) KENTUCKY COLONEL</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Was there really ever any question that Michael Brock was going to end up picking Eastern Kentucky as his collegiate destination?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The 6-7 combo forward from Independence, Ky., plays his summer ball with Eddie Ford's Kentucky HoopStars. Eddie Ford, of course, is the father of Travis Ford, the former UK point guard who's now the head coach at EKU.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Folks in the recruiting world call that an "in"&nbsp;-- a big-time "in"&nbsp;-- and it bore fruit earlier this spring when Brock committed to the Colonels.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I've known him since I started playing with Eddie last year," the rising senior said Wednesday night when asked about his college coach-to-be. "Eddie was talking to me about some decisions I was going to have to make.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"So I started thinking about it, and I've been praying about it for a while, and Eastern seemed like the place where I need to be."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And a place that needs Brock.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Simon Kenton High standout averaged 22 points and 11 rebounds this past winter en route to all-state honors while playing the post more than anything else.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he's only gotten better&nbsp;-- and more versatile&nbsp;-- in various spring events with the HoopStars. Next up is this weekend's trip to Bloomington, Ind., for the Nike Memorial Day Classic.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's been playing extremely well," Ford said. "When he gets with better players, and when he plays against better players, his game steps up. His strength&nbsp;-- his upside&nbsp;-- is that he shoots the ball so well from the perimeter."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Which is what he's going to need to do to play the three in the underrated Ohio Valley Conference&nbsp;-- and that's the plan.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"They're wanting me as a three man," Brock said. "My greatest strength is my speed, strength and jumping ability. I've been working on my ball-handling and my outside shot."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>First, though, he has one more prep season to make a bid for the state's coveted Mr. Basketball award.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I don't know if I've really been mentioned," Brock said. "But I hope so. I think I can kind of be the sleeper."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Two of college basketball's all-time heavyweights picked up early commitments this week from current juniors. Rugged Minnetonka, Minn., combo forward <B>Kris Humphries</B> went with Duke, and Durham, N.C., swingman <B>Bobby Perry</B> gave his pledge to Kentucky. Humphries is one of the elite talents in the Class of 2003. He averaged 26.5 points and 11.1 rebounds a game this past winter en route to Minneapolis Star Tribune Metro Player of the Year honors. Perry, a 6-7 wing from Hillside High, projects to be a solid four-year player with the Wildcats&nbsp;-- but certainly not a superstar with realistic aspirations as an early NBA entrant. And perhaps that's why he was so attractive to <B>Tubby Smith</B> and the Wildcats. He averaged 25 points and 12 rebounds as a junior.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Tough blow for <B>Tim Blackwell</B>: The Cameron, Mo., shooting guard broke his tibia in a nasty fall at last weekend's St. Louis Eagles Invitational. He'll be out for at least six weeks. Which means no more AAU ball for the time being&nbsp;-- and no Nike Camp. Shame. Blackwell was terrific earlier this month at the Howard Pulley event in Minneapolis. He was beginning to draw more high-major interest&nbsp;-- from Oklahoma and others&nbsp;-- but now things appear to be a bit on hold.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Traditional New York City Catholic powers Archbishop Molloy and Christ the King headline <B>John Rhodes</B>' field set for next December's Beach Ball Classic. A couple strong North Jersey programs&nbsp;-- from Don Bosco and Seton Hall Prep&nbsp;-- are also committed to the annual Myrtle Beach hoops extravaganza. Of more local note, <B>Major Wingate</B> and his Wilson High team from Florence, S.C., are supposed to be on hand, as are UNC target <B>Brian Johnson</B> and coach <B>Joe Wootten</B>'s Bishop O'Connell squad from Arlington, Va.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** While on the topic of fields already scheduled for holiday tournaments in the fairly distant future, <B>Hal Pastner</B>'s 2002 Academy National Invitational is set, and it'll look something like this: Eight of Nike's premier schoolboy programs will make up the national bracket, and there'll also be an eight-team Texas tourney, with Heritage Christian, from the Houston area, and Cedar Hill, from the Dallas region, leading the way. But the national field is set to welcome, among others, high-flying twins <B>Lodrick Stewart</B> and <B>Rodrick Stewart</B> from Seattle's Rainier Beach High; top-notch Class of 2004 point man <B>Andre Allen</B> and Memphis Booker T. Washington; high-major swingman <B>Trevor Ariza</B> and Los Angeles Westchester; Jersey point <B>Mike Nardi</B> and St. Patrick; Big Apple big man <B>Shagari Alleyne</B> and Rice; and coach <B>Steve Smith</B>'s perennially powerful Oak Hill Academy Warriors.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Eddie Ford</B> has a warning for kids on the AAU circuit who saw a mostly disinterested <B>Michael Bush</B> this spring: The 6-3 football-basketball superstar from Louisville, Ky., had a phenomenal showing two weekends ago at a tournament in Atlanta. "You may see a new Michael Bush," the Kentucky HoopStars coach said earlier this week. "Just remember what I'm saying. He's obsessed." Bush averaged 15.6 points a game this past winter for coach <B>Bryce Hibbard</B>'s Male Bulldogs. The coveted football recruit&nbsp;-- he's a wide receiver/quarterback/safety/linebacker&nbsp;-- caught 63 balls for 15 touchdowns and 1,090 yards last fall. He ran for 11 TDs and passed for two more en route to first-team all-state honors. "But I told him he was playing soft," Ford said, "that he wasn't pushing himself. So he started taking people to the hole, carrying them with him, and dunking on people. Even the kids on the other teams were, like, 'Wow.'"</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Every kid at the Winchendon School has to play seasonal sports. Even the basketball players. Which is why visitors to the Massachusetts prep powerhouse right now might see Louisville-bound wing <B>Francisco Garcia</B> and Manhattan point guard commitment <B>Kenny Minor</B> playing indoor soccer. Or post-grad dunker extraordinaire <B>DeMario Eddins</B> trying his hand at baseball. Or big-time rising senior center <B>Martin Iti</B> participating in intramural tennis. "It's pretty comical to watch these guys," Winchendon coach <B>Mike Byrnes</B> said. "There are balls flying all over the place." As for a piece of actual real news out of the Winch: <B>Karron Clarke</B>, the hoopster formerly known as Elijah Clarke, is earmarked for the school next season. The physical swingman from Brooklyn helped Coney Island prodigy <B>Sebastian Telfair</B> lead Lincoln to the Public League city title this past winter.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** The long recruitment of <B>Cortez Davis</B> appears to be over&nbsp;-- finally. The 6-7 wing from North Carolina's Laurinburg Institute signed an institutional letter this week with Rutgers. Davis, originally from Maryland, committed to Maryland as a junior. That didn't work out. He committed to Memphis earlier this spring, and that didn't come through, either. Now, if all goes according to plan, Scarlet Knights coach <B>Gary Waters</B> has a pretty nice late spring addition.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Illinois filled one of its three Class of 2003 scholarship openings by taking a commitment from Dallas Lake Highlands star <B>Warren Carter</B>. The 6-8 post man used last month's Houston Kingwood Classic to boost his stock.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Think <B>LeBron James</B> needs to play in a summer circuit event called the Tournament of Champions? Think again. The 6-7 boy wonder from Akron, Ohio, participated in a Cleveland Cavaliers workout Wednesday afternoon&nbsp;-- he got the invite from Cavs coach <STRONG>John Lucas</STRONG>&nbsp;-- and apparently threw down a dunk so impressive that it stopped play. "It was so unbelievable that you wouldn't have believed it if you would've seen it on replay," former Toronto Raptors assistant <B>Johnny Clark</B> told the Cleveland Plain-Dealer. "I've seen a lot of basketball, but I've never seen anything like that." Really? Anyone else get the impression that the LeBron frenzy is feeding the LeBron frenzy to such a ridiculous extent that even NBA folks are now stoking the fires? Perhaps to set the bar so ludicrously high that it'll be impossible for The Chosen One to fulfill expectations once he enters the League in 2003? Just a thought. Now for a thought from current NBAer <B>Jumaine Jones</B>: "He's the most developed high school player I've ever seen, and he has another year of high school," he told the paper. "He's very mature physically and mentally. Unlike most high school players, he wants to do more than just score. He likes to get everyone involved. He could play in the league right now." And thus continueth The Hype.</FONT></P>
