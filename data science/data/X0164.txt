                                <FONT SIZE="+1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"><B>INTERNET 
                                MAILING LISTS<BR>
                                <BR>
                                </B></FONT><font size="1" face="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Well, 
                                if you don't know about mailing lists, now's a 
                                fine time to learn. There are different kinds 
                                of mailing lists around. There are the kind that 
                                are sporadic and only used for updates and news, 
                                which is the kind of mailing lists that we currently 
                                run. If you'd like to subscribe to ours, and get 
                                information on when these pages are updated, then 
                                you will find a little box on the left that lets 
                                you subscribe and unsubscribe to our mailing list. 
                                Other mailing lists are run by their own servers 
                                and allow people to recieve emails from many different 
                                people who are subscribing to the same list by 
                                only having to send email to one address. These 
                                lists usually send out 10 or 20 to billions of 
                                messages a day... well, maybe not billions. If 
                                you are interested in this kind of mailing list, 
                                commonly called a listserv or discussion list, 
                                then we have the names of many lists available 
                                and directions on how you can subscribe to them. 
                                These lists come in two forms... normal, or digest. 
                                Normal will send you individual emails every time 
                                someone send email to the list. Digest holds the 
                                emails for a week (generally it's a week) and 
                                sends them all at once in one large email. Good 
                                for conserving space. You can subscribe to one 
                                or the other, if it gives you the option. Note 
                                that below, if there are two methods of subscribing, 
                                the one with digest or a d tacked on to the end 
                                is generally the digest method. We will be setting 
                                up our own discussion list too if we have enough 
                                interest generated, so if you would like to see 
                                The Hockey Phreak set up a list, let us know. 
                                In the meantime, subscribe to the lists below 
                                at your own risk. We cannot be held responsible 
                                if you drown in your own email. Well, enough said...<BR>
