<center><h2>Learn to Program with Visual Basic 6</h1></center>

<FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE=5><P ALIGN="CENTER">Chapter 4</P></font><p>

<h2>Summary</h2><p>

<FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2><P>In this chapter, you followed my class on a tour of Visual Basic properties and methods. You had your first look at the exercises that will guide you through the China Shop Project.</P>
<P>Along the way, we discussed the default behavior of the Visual Basic form, and at the end of the class, you had a chance to see a Visual Basic method in action.</P>
<P>There are two basic stages to creating a program: designing the user interface, which involves placing controls onto your form and modifying their properties; and writing and checking the code.</P>
<P>Forms and controls are types of </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>object</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2>. Objects have </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>properties</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2>, which are characteristics that we can modify, such as their name, or their appearance.</P>
<P>Objects also have </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>methods</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2>, which are actions that we can perform on them. Methods differ from </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>events</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2>, which are occurrences that the object will respond to. An </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>event handler</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2> is a section of code that is executed when an event happens.</P>
<P>When saving a project you first save the forms (using extension .frm), then the project (using the extension .vbp). It's important to remember that forms and projects have two names associated with them; one of these is the name used to refer to it in code (set in Properties), while the other is its saved name. Don't try to refer to the saved name in code, as it won't be recognized!</P>
<P>The Name property should use Hungarian notation (three letters to indicate the control) and no spaces. Make a habit of this right from the start, because otherwise you'll either have to work with a mix of conventions, or go through and change all your names halfway through (which is dangerous, as it can lead to </FONT><B><FONT FACE="Times" SIZE=3>orphaned code</B></FONT><FONT FACE="Palatino" SIZE=2>).</P>
