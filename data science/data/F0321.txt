<TR><TD>
&nbsp;
<P>

  Terry Pratchett's Discworld is a ridiculous romp through a fantasic world carried on the back of a giant space-turtle.
Pratchett has a way with words that turns the most trivial event into an adventure! Utterly fascinating.
<P>
<FONT SIZE="-1">Note that you can easily read these books in any order. Prices are for books
(paperback where available). Other media are much more expensive.</FONT>
  <P>

  <TABLE WIDTH="100%" CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0">
    <TR><TD BGCOLOR="#7FCFFF" ALIGN="Center"><B>Special</B></TD></TR>
  </TABLE>
  <P>

    <TABLE WIDTH="100%"><TR>
    <TD>
      <CENTER>
        <B>The Science of Discworld</B><BR>
        <FONT SIZE="-1">368 pages, paperback.</FONT>
        [<A HREF="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0091874777/vbhelper/">Amazon.com</A>]
        [<A HREF="http://www.amazon.co.uk/exec/obidos/ASIN/0091874777/vbhelper0e/">Amazon.co.uk</A>]
      </CENTER>
      <BR>
      Despite its title, this book is <B>not</B> about the science of Discworld. Rather it is about the science
      of our world using Discworld characters as a sounding board to provide interesting and amusing contrast.
      Did you know that:
        <UL>
          <LI>Scientists weren't sure the first nuclear chain reaction would stop before blowing up the world?
          <LI>The Earth once had a natural nuclear reactor in Oklo, Gabon?
          <LI>The planet Mercury will probably leave orbit and possibly the solar system in about a billion years?
          <LI>It took 50 years for scientists to accept the idea of continental drift?
          <LI>In 2020, NASA hopes to launch a squadron of spacecraft to form an interferometric telescope with a baseline of several thousand miles that can easily image planets in other solar systems?
        </UL>

        Space elevators, DNA, general relativity, quantum mechanics, pulsars, ... just about everything presented
        in a clear, not-too-technical way.
        <P>
        <FONT SIZE="-1">
            Note: It may be faster and cheaper to buy this book from Amazon.co.uk even if you are in the US.
        </FONT>
