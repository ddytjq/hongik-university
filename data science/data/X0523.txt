<B>Meirhaeghe and Stropparo Win Mont Sainte-Anne Cross-Country:<P></B>

<I>Mont Sainte-Anne, CAN (June 30, 2002)</I> -- <B>Annabella Stropparo</B> <I>(ITA, Be-One) </I>
won her career's first-ever world cup cross-country race Sunday, when she and 
multi-winner <B>Filip Meirhaeghe</B> <I>(BEL, Specialized)</I> added their names to the 
list of legends who have won on this long running course. As thousands of 
spectators looked on, racing took place on a warm and humid Sunday afternoon, 
on a very technical and difficult 5.7 km course around this green mountain 
resort.<P>

With the cross-country portion of the Tissot-UCI Mountain Bike World Cup now 
more than half over, the race for championship points has heated up, as 
Alison Dunlap (USA, Luna Chix) takes the overall lead<!-- , along with ____  -->. 
Racing was fierce for every position today, as most of the top riders in the 
world came to North America for these two Canadian races.<P>

<B>MEN: </B>

Soon after the women completed their five laps, it was time for the 92 men to 
campaign six laps in the warm summer sun. And, after a high-speed and dusty 
start loop on a relatively flat circuit around the infield, it was time for 
the steep opening climb that defines this Mont Sainte-Anne course. <P>

In the first lap a group of ten distinguished themselves by pulling away from 
the pack. Included in the group was 1998 World Champion<B> Christophe Dupouey </B>
<I>(FRA, Giant)</I>, current World Champion <B>Roland Green</B> <I>(CAN, Trek-Volkswagen)</I>, 
winner from round 2<B> Christoph Sauser</B> <I>(SUI, Volvo Cannondale)</I>, <B>Lado Fumic </B>
<I>(GER, Team T-Mobile)</I>, <B>Julien Absalon</B> <I>(FRA, Motorex-Bianchi)</I> - and world cup 
winner <B>Filip Meirhaeghe</B><I> (BEL, Specialized)</I>. Several seconds back, <B>Ryder 
Hesjedal</B> <I>(CAN, Subaru Gary Fisher)</I> chased for the lead group, while the 
remaining riders were strung around the course.<P>

<I>"I felt strong, but figured that Dupouey and Sauser would be stronger,"</I> said 
Meirhaeghe afterward. <I>"I thought they'd be tough today."<P></I>

<I>"I did feel good,"</I> confirmed Dupouey.<I> "It's been more than a year since I had 
this good a sensation. It was a mistake to follow Filip though, I was riding 
too safely as I was afraid I would blow."<P></I>

This situation remained for lap two, as the group stayed mostly together 
while changing positions within. By the third lap Meirhaeghe and Sauser had 
moved to the front, getting a slight gap on the others. Green, Fumic and 
Dupouey were riding as a trio 30 seconds back, while<B> Seamus McGrath</B> <I>(CAN, 
Haro/Lee Dungarees) </I>rode alone in 6th position, followed by Absalon, Bui, 
Brentjens and Hesjedal.<P>

The fourth lap was the defining moment of the race, as Sauser flatted on the 
first rocky descent of the lap as he and another former downhiller in 
Meirheaghe flew down the descent, 34 seconds in the lead. Sauser would 
eventually abandon, giving Meirhaeghe an added mental boost as he found 
himself riding alone and in the front. Behind him, Fumic, Dupouey and Green 
chased, while Bui and Absalon were at 2:07 back.<P>

<I>"I was hammering full gas on the climbs, and taking it easy on the descents. 
So when Susi flatted, I had open road" </I>said Meirhaeghe. <I>"I felt strong on the 
uphills as well as the technical sections, and the heat didn't bother me."<P></I>

In lap five Meirhaeghe added to his lead, riding through the course's most 
technical root section with 1:30 on 2nd place Fumic and 1:40 on Dupouey - 
both of whom ran through the section that - of the leaders - only Meirhaeghe 
and Sauser were able to ride.<P>

Absalon and Hesjedal were riding together at this point, 3:25 from the lead, 
while Fumic rode another 35 seconds back, Green chasing him closely. Roel 
Paulissen and McGrath were in 8th and 9th, 4:26 from the lead.<P>

In the sixth lap Dupouey passed Fumic to take over 2nd position, while 
Absalon dropped Hesjedal to secure 4th position - Hesjedal in 5th. And, with 
Meirhaeghe riding clear of the pack and the podium seeming set, a secondary 
race developed behind the podium positions in the final lap. <P>

Roland Green attacked a quartet of riders, which included McGrath, Paulissen 
and <B>Kashi Leuchs </B><I>(NZL, Volvo-Cannondale)</I>. McGrath tried to control Paulissen 
to let his fellow Canadian gain points and go for the podium - as Green 
finished 6th, McGrath 7th and Paulissen 8th.<P>

Minutes later Meirhaeghe finished in 1st, after 2 hours, 1:18 of racing. 
Dupouey finished 2nd at 2:03:32, while Fumic, Absalon and Hesjedal rounded 
out the podium.<P>

For Meirhaeghe, who recently won a Swiss MTB race and finished 17th in the 
Tour of Austria, this race represents his return to form. <I>"Last year was a 
struggle for me after the Olympics, and this winter I trained more and was 
able to do better - it's been on my mind to win here, as this is one of my 
favorite courses and I wanted a win in North America."<P></I>

<I>"This was good race for me,"</I> added third place Fumic. <I>"I had a difficult week 
where I broke two of my fingers, so it was hard to grip the bars. The course 
was maybe too hard, and I lost control in the downhills."<P></I>

With his win this week, Meirhaeghe took over the leader's jersey, with 
Brentjens and Paulissen next in the ranking.<P>

<I>-Ari Cheren</I>
