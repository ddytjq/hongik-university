            </b><font size="1" color="#003399"><br>
            -<i>Oceania Championships </i>(1935-2001):<br>

            <p><font size="1" color="#003399"><i>-World Championships</i>

            (4th Place), 1998 (9th Place).</font></p>
            <p><font size="1" color="#003399"><i>-Olympic Games </i>(1936-2000):&nbsp;
            1956 Melbourne (12 Place), 1964 Tokyo (9th Place), 1972 Munich (9th
            Place), 1976 Montreal (8th Place), 1980 Moscow (8th Place), 1984 Los
            Angeles (7th Place), 1988 Seoul (4th Place), 1992 Barcelona (6gh

        <tr>
          <td width="21%" valign="top"><b><font size="1" color="#003399">Coaches
            Hall of Fame</font></b>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Ken Watson -</b>
            Served as Australia's first Olympic coach in 1956 and played a major
            role in the growth of basketball in the proceeding decade.</font> </p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Lindsay Gaze -</b>
            Coached many U-16 and U18 National teams in the early 1960's before
            moving on to become one of, if not the most successful coach in
            Australia basketball history as National Team Head Coach in 4
            Olympic games (1972, 1976, 1980, 1984).&nbsp;</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Brian Kerle -</b>
            Another coaching legend in the NBL where until recently he held the
            record for the most career wins for a coach in the NBL at 282. Twice
            named National Coach of the Year.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Bob Turner -</b>
            Won&nbsp;224 games as an NBL coach and now highly active in
            Basketball Australia.&nbsp; Has also coached Australian Wheelchair
            National Team. Coached Sydney Kings and is now part owner of the
            team.</font> </p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Barry Barnes -</b>
            One of Australia's most successful coaches with 4th places finishes
            at both the 1996 Atlantic Olympic Games and 2000 in Syndey.
            Assistant Coach in two other Olympic games and won 191 games in his
            NBL career.&nbsp;</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Brian Goorjian -</b>
            This past season became the all time NBL leader in coaching career
            wins as he surpassed Brian Kerle.&nbsp; Now has more than 300 career
            wins.&nbsp; Currently coach of the Victoria Titans and recently
            named Head National Coach for the Boomers.&nbsp; First American to
            be named National Team Coach.</font> </p>
            <p>&nbsp;</td>
          <td width="20%" valign="top"><b><font size="1" color="#003399">Players Hall of Fame</font>
            </b>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Lindsay Gaze -</b> Participated
            as a player in 3 Olympic Games (1960, 1964, and 1968).&nbsp; Still
            coaching at Melbourne with more than 270 career NBL victories.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Andrew Gaze -</b>
            Son of the legendary coach Lindsay but none-the-less a record
            breaker in his own right.&nbsp; Named 7 time MVP of the National
            Basketball League, having averaged over 30 points per game in his
            whole career.&nbsp; Was also a member of the 1999 San Antonio Spurs
            NBA Championship team.&nbsp; All-NBL First team 14 straight years
            (1986-2000).&nbsp; Plays for the Melbourne Tigers.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Luc Longley -</b>
            Was 7th player picked in the 1st round of the 1991 NBA draft and the
            first Australia ever to play in the NBA. Played 11 seasons in the
            NBA for four different teams most noticeably as part of the Chicago
            Bulls NBA Championship team in 1997 and 1998.&nbsp; Also played on
            two Olympic teams in 1988 and 1992.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Mark Bradke -</b>
            5 Times All-NBL first team, 5 time NBL All-Star and played 1997-98
            season with the Philadelphia 76ers in the NBA.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Perry Crosswhite -</b>
            One of the most prominant names in Australian basketball history
            appearing in 3 Olympic games in 1972, 1976, 1980 for the
            Boomers.&nbsp;</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Shane Heal -</b>
            Starting Point Guard on the 1996 and 2000 Olympic teams and have
            more than 150 caps to his credit in a National team jersey.&nbsp;
            Holds the NBL record for most point in a game at 61.&nbsp; Was an
            NBA All-Star for 7 consecutive years. Known for his tough play and
            deadly three point shot he also spent 1997 season in the NBA with
            the Minnesota Timberwolves.</font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>Leroy Loggins -</b>
            This naturalized player was 10 times named to the All-NBL team.
            times NBL MVP and led both Brisbane and West Adelaide to NBL
            championships.&nbsp; No player in NBL history aside from Andrew Gaze
            has won more awards.</font></p>

            <p align="left"><font color="#003399" size="1"><b>History of the
            Game <br>
            in Australia</b></font></p>
            <p><font color="#003399" size="1">The game was first established<br>
            in 1927 in the state of Victoria.<br>
            Most of the teams had either<br>
            military or church affiliations.<br>
            <br>
            The National Federation of <br>
            Basketball was founded in<br>
            1939 which later because <br>
            known as the Amateur <br>
            Basketball Union of Australia<br>
            which put on the first <br>
            Australian Championships in <br>
            1946.<br>
            <br>
            The game gained in popularity<br>
            in the late 1940's with the <br>
            increase in post-World War II<br>
            immigrants from the Baltic<br>
            states and later in the 50's <br>
            those leaving Hungary.<br>
            <br>
            The Morman church also had<br>
            a big influence on the growth<br>
            of the game at this time as<br>
            well with the influence of<br>
            De Lyle Condie who began<br>
            coaching a more modern<br>
            style of offensive play.<br>
            <br>
            Two of the greatest influences<br>
            on basketball's growth were<br>
            undoubtedly Ken Watson (the<br>
            first Olympic coach in 1956)<br>
            and Lindsay Gaze who played<br>
            a major role in the growth of<br>
            Basketball in Victoria as<br>
            well at representing Australia<br>
            as a player (1960, 1964, and<br>
            1968) in the Olympic Games<br>
            and then as Head Coach in <br>
            four successive Olympics<br>
            (1972, 1976, 1980, 1984).<br>
            <br>
            Today Australia is one of<br>
            the world's most potent<br>
            team in International <br>
            competition as exhibited by<br>
            their excellent showings in<br>
            the most recent Olympics.</font></p>
