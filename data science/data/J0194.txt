            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><font color="#000000" face="Arial" size="5"><b>CART
            &amp; IRL - it's becoming clearer where we're going</b></font>
            </p>
            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center">&nbsp;
            </p>
            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 6" align="center"><b><font size="2" color="#000000">
            &nbsp;</font><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial" size="2">by Mark Cipolloni</font><font color="#0066FF"><br>
            </font><font color="#000000" face="Arial" size="2">December 26, 2001</font></b></p>
        </td>
          </tr>
  </center>
          <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#000000" valign="middle" align="center" width="480" height="21">
            <p align="left"><font color="#FFFFFF" size="3" face="Arial"><b>Go to
            our <a href="http://autoracing1.motorsportforum.com">forums</a>
            to discuss this article</b></font>
        </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
          <TD align=right bgcolor=#FFFFEE vAlign=top width=640>
      <p align="left">
          <a href="../Subscribe/signup.asp">
          <img border="0" src="../images/Pagestuff/Subscription/SubscribeNowButton.gif" align="right" width="200" height="74"></a><font face="Arial" size="2">In his very first press
      conference, new CART President Chris Pook made three bold statements - 1)
      &quot;we need to make it easy for our teams and drivers to participate in
      the Indy 500,&quot; 2) we would encourage our teams to run even more IRL
      races, and we would hope IRL teams would want to run some of our
      races,&quot; 3) we will work to try and avoid date conflicts with the IRL
      in future years.&quot;&nbsp; It's now becoming clearer where the sport of
      Indy Car Racing should be headed.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2"><b>The Car</b></font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">With CART adopting an engine
      formula similar to the IRL's, the groundwork is now laid whereby teams
      will be able to switch between the two series much easier.&nbsp; CART just
      needs to make one more key decision for 2003 - what the spine of Champ
      Car will be.&nbsp; As I understand it, what I present below is what is
      being discussed between CART, the IRL and the chassis manufacturers.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">What happens if the spine of
      the cars are the same for both the IRL and CART starting in 2003?&nbsp;
      Just about everything on an Indy Car hangs off the monocoque and the
      engine.&nbsp; Mated together, these two pieces form the structural
      backbone of an Indy Car, i.e. the 'spine'.&nbsp; They are carbon fiber
      and aluminum unit, strong enough to protect a driver in all but the most
      severe accidents, and stiff enough to transfer static and dynamic loads
      transversely, longitudinally and diagonally between fore and aft, and left
      and right, suspension members.&nbsp; In a large building, think of it as
      the steel frame.&nbsp; In your house, think of it as the wood frame.
      Certainly a monocoque is not a 'frame' structure, it's more of a structural
      box or shell, but suffice it to say everything hangs off this 'main structural
      backbone' that is designed using 3D finite element analysis.&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">If this main structural unit
      is the same for both the IRL and CART, then many of the pieces that hang
      off of it can be unique to each series.&nbsp; If CART wants to use
      different sidepods, they can.&nbsp; If CART wants to use different wings
      (aerofoil and underbody), they can.&nbsp; If CART wants to use a different
      transmission, they can.&nbsp; If CART wants to use a different airbox,
      they can.&nbsp; If CART wants a better sounding exhaust system, they can.&nbsp;
      If CART wants a slightly higher revving engine, they can.&nbsp; Whatever CART thinks it needs to change to differentiate itself from the
      IRL it can, be it performance changes or simply appearance changes.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Then, Reynard, Lola, Dallara,
      G-Force can build cars for both series if they so choose, and any team who
      wants to participate in races run by the other series, can buy a 'CART
      Kit', or an IRL Kit' and go racing. If a CART teams wants to run in the
      Indy 500, they order an IRL Kit from one of the chassis makers, take off
      the CART pieces, and put on the IRL pieces.&nbsp; If an IRL team wants to
      run some CART races because their sponsors are interested in a particular
      market CART serves, they can purchase a 'CART Kit.'&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Before you start calling them
      'Kit Cars' think again.&nbsp; A Kit Car, in the true sense of the word,
      would be made by a single manufacturer.&nbsp; These would not be Kit Cars
      at all.&nbsp; In fact, the competition between the manufacturers would be
      stiff.&nbsp; I use the word 'kit' loosely to imply a set of parts that
      would make a car either an IRL car or a CART Champ Car.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">One other point here - the
      CART Kit can be thought of as a road racing kit in some respects since the
      majority of their races are on road courses.&nbsp; Similarly, the IRL kit
      can be thought of as an oval kit.&nbsp; Hence, instead of having a CART
      Kit and and IRL Kit, perhaps it should be an 'oval kit' and a 'road course
      kit'.&nbsp; Then teams can switch between series even easier.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial"><b>The Organization</b></font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Certainly CART and the IRL can
      continue to battle each other to win over complete control of Indy car
      racing.&nbsp; However, look where that has gotten us the past five or six
      years - two badly weakened series limping along on one leg.&nbsp;&nbsp; We need to convince
      Tony George and CART to merge together under one governing body called
      'IndyCar', the name CART used to have.&nbsp; Similar to Football's and
      Baseball's American and National Leagues, Tony George would have full
      control of the IRL Division, the Chris Pook and the CART Board would have full control of
      the CART Division.&nbsp; The CART Division would be a publicly traded
      company, as it is today.&nbsp; In essence, the governing body would be
      like a holding company of a major corporation.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial"> I would propose that one person be
      selected to be the Commissioner of IndyCar racing.&nbsp; A person that
      would be jointly elected by the IRL and CART to lead a governing body for
      IndyCar racing, much like the FIA has a Max Mosley that steers F1, F3000
      and other series.&nbsp; That person must be someone who is respected
      worldwide by the racing community, is well respected by the sponsors,
      commands a presence when he walks into the room, can be neutral, and wants
      to see CART and the IRL co-exist under one umbrella.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">If both series are controlled
      under a single governing body, then Marlboro, Kool and other tobacco
      advertisers can be on the sidepods of a car at any event, be it a CART
      division event or an IRL division event.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial"><b>The Schedule</b></font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">It's hard to imagine that CART
      and the IRL can avoid all date conflicts, but I did my best to layout proposed
      2004 race schedules (2004 rather than 2003 because I doubt both sides
      could agree to something this novel that quickly) that come close.&nbsp; The only time you will
      see conflicts are when CART is racing outside of North America, i.e. when
      schedule conflicts between the two series would not matter at all.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Numerous times on these pages I
      have proposed CART and the IRL agree to reinstate a Triple Crown and
      promote a healthy rivalry between the two series, a rivalry that all
      successful sports must have to prosper.&nbsp; My proposed schedule
      includes that.&nbsp; Points would be awarded in both series for the Triple
      Crown races and the field size increased so most teams from both series
      can start the race..</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">You will see some new races on
      the both the CART and IRL schedule, ones I feel should be added, or have
      been rumored to be coming.&nbsp; Likewise, I had to make some very hard
      decisions, but you will see some existing
      races dropped, either because of poor attendance, declining attendance or
      inadequate track width and/or safety.&nbsp;&nbsp; You will also notice
      that CART and IRL are in Florida in February and March, to expand NASCAR's
      <i>Daytona Speedweeks</i> into a larger <i>Florida Speedweeks</i>, a race
      fans euphoria.&nbsp; Since a lot of out-of-state people take winter
      vacation and college Spring Break in Florida during this 2-month period, a
      longer Speedweeks (than what we currently have in just Daytona) would give
      race fans that many more choices to wrap vacation in with racing.</font></p>

      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial"><b>Conclusion</b> - What I
      have presented is a way for CART and IRL to coexist peacefully.&nbsp; To
      share a common chassis and engine platform with almost no date
      conflicts.&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial"> The entities would jointly promote 'Indy Car' racing,
      especially the Triple Crown races when they race together.&nbsp; I have
      avoided both series racing on the same day (except two, but that's when
      CART is overseas in China and Germany).&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">I have also presented a way
      for Indy Car fans to enjoy their type of motorsports almost every weekend,
      just like NASCAR fans get to do, without TV programming conflicts.&nbsp;
      And most importantly, I hope I have presented something for all the
      constituents in CART and the IRL to gain from a stronger IRL and
      CART series, united under one governing body called IndyCar.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
