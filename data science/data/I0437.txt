class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Grimsby-Ground13-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Grimsby-Ground13-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Concourse
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Concourse
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Blundell Park is actually located in Cleethorpes not Grimsby. Away fans 
are seated in the Osmond Stand, Its not the newest of stands, but it does have a
bar. Choice is not the bars strong point as the only alcohol.. is lager,
served only pints. It also has a small choice of snacks and pies and whilst there are two serving area the police
won't let you move from one to the other with beer.
</P><P>The stand itself has seen much better days and almost every seat either has a view obscured by a low roof or is behind
one of several supporting pillars, alternatively seats in front of the pillars are not covered by the roof leaving the
occupants to feel the full force of the elements. Like Maine Rd, Blundell park has a temporary "Gene Kelly" stand 
(a group of additional uncovered seating for those "Singing In The Rain"), whilst slight more sheltered than Maine Road it is still
not a great place to be on a wet night. The club have also been criticized for not have child/OAPs reductions available
in this area.
</P><P>Update: We have been told that after 101 year at Blundell Park Grimsby are currently planning to build a new stadium 
(planning permission is being
sought).
</P><P>Flags and banners were permitted on our last visit, but drums were not.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=DN357PY&Title=Blundell+Park+(Grimsby+Town)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Leaking Boot
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Grimsby-Boot1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Grimsby-Boot1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Leaking Boot - Main Entrance" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Leaking Boot - Main Entrance<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Grimsby-Boot2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Grimsby-Boot2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Leaking Boot - Georges Bar
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Leaking Boot - Georges Bar
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The away pub of choice, the Leaking Boot is actually a hotel with several bars,
away fans are normally allocated the Lounge Bar (left at the front door) and Georges Bar (round the left
side of the Boot). Georges has the better atmosphere, access to the beer garden, Sky Sports and a pool
table (covered before games).
</P><P>The Boot normally gets a visit from the local constabulary, but they are friendly enough.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Punch Bowl
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Grimsby-Punchbowl-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Grimsby-Punchbowl-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Punch Bowl
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Punch Bowl
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>Located on the sea front between Cleethorpes station and Blundell Park its
designed for the tourists but well behaved away fans are welcomed. The food looked
excellent, but was a little more expensive than elsewhere.
</P><P>The Landlord and his family are big Grimsby fans and are more than happy to spend time talking
about the clubs when its not too busy.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">Schuberts
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Grimsby-Schubets-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Grimsby-Schubets-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Schuberts
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Schuberts
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>Located on Market St, it was very quiet, but with two pool tables and Sky Sports it makes a
good starting point.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Blundell Park Hotel
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><p>Located right outside the ground (the picture above of Blundell Park is taken
from its doorway) it is very much a locals pub, but does allow away fans in
for most games.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>Grimsby is the fish and chip capital of the world, a particularly excellent one
is located a couple of doors away from the Leaking Boot, elsewhere you
can't miss them as the seem to be located every few hundred yards. The quality 
is also superb.
</P><P>A drive through McDonald is located right outside the ground (the golden arches
are just about visible on the picture of the ground above).
</P><P>Cleethorpes has a good range of places to eat including a KFC on Market St and
a host of Indian and Italian restaurants.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>Most major banks are represented in both Cleethorpes and Grimsby
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is DN357PY</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>There is no bookmakers in the ground. The nearest one is a William Hill's in the row of
shops between the 'Leaking Boot' and the ground.
</P></p><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Head towards Grimsby on the M180 and stay on it when it becomes the A180. This
is Grimsby Rd which runs though Grimsby, right past the ground and then
into Cleethorpes.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is plenty of street parking in Cleethorpes and even some around the ground. People travelling on
coaches should be warned that the official coach drop off point is in Grimsby itself, quite a walk from the ground.
You may want to consider a whip round to convince the driver to drop you off outside The Leaking Boot and save you
a walk of several miles.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>It is just a short walk down the promenade to the ground. Cross the track
at the level crossing and follow the road and you'll arrive at Grimsby Rd, just 
by the Leaking Boot.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Cleethorpes
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Being a popular seaside resort Cleethorpes isn't short of B&Bs, one
standing out from the crowd and [R] is <a class="entry" href="http://www.clee-house.co.uk">The
Clee House</a>, where £50 gets you a pleasant double room with en-suite
facilities and a landlord that couldn't be more friendly and helpful. It
is used a lot by away fans and has several rooms with full disabled
facilities.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/cleethor.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Grimsby are noted for their inflatable fishes and extreme silliness. Trouble
is no longer common.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Grimsby have been responsible for numerous silly chants over the years, including
a number of Christmas Carols and even renditions of the Hawaii Five-O (Complete with actions).
</P><P>Away fans gain no credit for 'Sing when your fishing' which is sung by Grimsby themselves
or other Fish related humour (Who ate all the Fish ?). Fully aware of visitors fondness to
their fish and chips the locals respond to 'You're s*** and you stink of fish' with 'We p*** on
the fish and chips'.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Cleethorpes is a lively and surprisingly good night out, check out Bootleggers and
The Voodoo Lounge. Plenty of busy pubs to choose from.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>Cleethorpes is your average English seaside town with attractions to keep the entire
family busy. Not least is the Pleasure Island theme park
(pleasure_island@compuserve.com), other attractions include Time Trap (Time
Travel in Grimsby Town Hall prison cells !), Waltham Windmill,
Cleethorpes Light Railway, 'Deep Sea' Aquarium, The Jungle (a zoo) and The
Humber Estuary discovery center. It will come a no surprise that
Grimsby plays host to The National Fishing Heritage Centre. 
</P><P>Shoppers may want to head for Freshney Place shopping centre with over 100
shops.
