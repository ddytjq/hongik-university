              Bob Stuchbery, Underwriting Director, stated:&nbsp; <br>
              &#147;The last six months have seen good progress in building and 
              developing the Chaucer business with the commencement of Chaucer 
              Consortium Underwriting and two new turnkey syndicates. The motor 
              market which was the first area to enter the down cycle is now the 
              first to emerge and we look forward to a strong performance in 2001. 
              Although recovery is still slow, other markets have passed their 
              low points and we anticipate further improvements next year.&#148;</span></p>

            <p class="font" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><b>Introduction</b></p>
            <p class="font" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><br>
              Whilst current underwriting trends are now moving in the right direction, 
              our interim results are affected by the need to make further provisions 
              for the 1999 underwriting year. In addition, equity markets started 
              the year at record highs and consequently we realised a loss when 
              moving the balance of our equity investments into fixed interest 
              securities at the end of February. These two items obscure what 
              is otherwise a satisfactory improvement to a position of surplus 
              income over expenses.</p>
            <p class="font" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><br>
              <b>Results </b></p>
            <p class="font" style="margin-top:0cm" align="left"><br>
              We report a loss before tax of &pound;1.8 million for the six months 
              ended 30 June 2000 compared to a profit before tax of &pound;0.5 
              million for the six months ended 30 June 1999. Earnings per share 
              showed a loss of 5.2p (30 June 1999 &#150; loss of 0.2p). The undiluted 
              net tangible asset value per share at 30 June 2000 has fallen to 
              73.4p (30 June 1999 &#150; 79.3p). It has been decided to reduce 
              the interim dividend from that paid last year in order to retain 
              resources within the Group. Accordingly, your Directors have declared 
              an interim dividend for the period of 0.5p net per share (30 June 
              1999 &#150; 1.0p). The dividend will be paid on 30 October 2000 
              to shareholders on the Company&#146;s register at close of business 
              on 29 September 2000. Following the 59% election for the scrip alternative 
              for 31 December 1999 final dividend, a scrip alternative will again 
              be offered to shareholders for this year&#146;s interim and your 
              directors have agreed to elect for shares instead of the cash dividend. 
              The principal constituents of the (loss)/profit before taxation 
              are summarised below: <br>

              in accordance with Lloyd&#146;s preference for the Chairman of a 
              Lloyd&#146;s managing agency to be independent of active underwriting 
              responsibilities, John Parton will become Non-Executive Chairman 
              of Chaucer Syndicates Limited. Hugh Hayward will become Deputy Chairman 
              of that company and will continue to chair meetings of its executive 
              directors.<br>

            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Current underwriting 
              markets </b></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><br>
              The motor market has been particularly poor for the last few years 
              and our motor syndicate has experienced heavy losses for 1997, 1998 
              and 1999. However, rates have been improving since the end of 1998 
              and after reviewing and adjusting the syndicate&#146;s book of business, 
              we reported in May that early figures for the 2000 year were considerably 
              improved. We are pleased to confirm that this trend has continued 
              and anticipate a return to profitability. In the marine, aviation 
              and non-marine markets in which we operate, premium rates had fallen 
              to unacceptably low levels and claims in 1998 and 1999 were particularly 
              high. We can now see evidence of hardening in selective areas and 
              we believe that these markets have passed their low point. The 2000 
              year will still be difficult but we remain cautiously optimistic 
              about 2001. </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><br>
              <b>Forecasts for the 1998 and 1999 underwriting years </b></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><br>
              We have received forecasts for the 1998 and 1999 underwriting years 
              prepared for the Lloyd&#146;s Syndicate Quarterly Returns at 30 
              June 2000. The position for those syndicates comprising the Group&#146;s 
              spread portfolio and our own in house managed syndicates is set 
              out below. For the 1998 year, the Group underwrote &pound;60.0 million 
              of capacity on a portfolio of 47 third party managed syndicates 
              and, through its shareholding in Chaucer Dedicated Limited, a &pound;10.0 
              million interest in Chaucer managed syndicates. The mid-points of 
              the syndicate forecasts show a loss of 8.3% on capacity. We have 
              in place a stop loss policy that covers total losses in excess of 
              5.75% of capacity up to a level of 10% and at the last year end 
              made provisions to the policy threshold. As the level of forecast 
              losses remains within the policy limits, there is no further provisioning 
              to be made at this stage. <br>
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font">For the 1999 
              year, the Group underwrote &pound;58.5 million of capacity, of which 
              &pound;42.9 million was allocated to Chaucer managed syndicates 
              and &pound;15.6 million to syndicates managed by other agents. In 
              addition, the Group had a further interest in &pound;16.7 million 
              of Chaucer managed capacity through Chaucer Dedicated. The mid-points 
              of the syndicate forecasts show a loss of 10.8% and if profit making 
              syndicates are ignored the loss increases to 11.0%. At the last 
              year end, provisions were made up to the Group&#146;s stop loss 
              policy threshold of 5.75% but as the mid point forecasts of loss 
              making syndicates exceed the 10% limit of the policy, it is necessary 
              to provide a further &pound;0.8 million. </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Quota Share 
              Reinsurance of Motor Interest </b><br>
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font">In May, we entered 
              into a capital provision quota share reinsurance arrangement with 
              a major European reinsurance company whereby the Reinsurer agreed 
              to procure the provision of a &pound;4.95 million letter of credit 
              as funds at Lloyd&#146;s for Chaucer Holdings. In return the Group 
              has agreed to pay to the Reinsurer the profits (or receive compensation 
              for losses) on an &pound;11,000,000 underwriting line on syndicate 
              587 for the 2000 year of account. Unless cancelled before 1 October, 
              the agreement will automatically continue into the next year of 
              account. These arrangements have enabled the Group to substitute 
              the letter of credit for investments of comparable value which were 
              deposited at Lloyd&#146;s. The investments were then sold and the 
              proceeds have been used to complete the financing of the freehold 
              building at Whitstable and the balance used to pay underwriting 
              losses. </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Chaucer Europe 
              </b> <br>
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font">Last year, in 
              line with plans to expand our underwriting business, we established 
              two new European businesses in Germany and Denmark. Progress has 
              been steady and good trading relationships established in order 
              to take advantage of the up-turn. <br>
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Syndicate 
              587 office </b></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><br>
              The new 35,000 square foot Whitstable office for the motor syndicate 
              has now been completed and staff moved in at the end of August. 
              It is a great tribute to all those involved that this large and 
              complex project was completed on time. Operating the syndicate from 
              a single modern building should greatly aid efficiency, management 
              and staff retention. The Group&#146;s existing freehold building 
              in Canterbury was sold to the developer as part of the transaction. 
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Investments 
              </b></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><br>
              The investment portfolio at 1 January 2000 comprised 15.1 million 
              units in the Aberdeen UK Growth Unit Trust and 88.6 million units 
              in the Aberdeen Fixed Interest Unit Trust. At the end of February 
              2000, in order to generate more income and to reduce volatility, 
              we decided to sell the Company&#146;s remaining holding of Aberdeen 
              UK Growth Unit Trust (realising a loss of &pound;1.4 million) and 
              invest the proceeds of &pound;13.3 million in the Aberdeen High 
              Yield Bond Unit Trust. In May and early June as a result of the 
              substitution of the &pound;4.95 million letter of credit for investments 
              referred to above, we sold 17.2 million units in the Aberdeen Fixed 
              Interest Unit Trust. The Company&#146;s investments now comprise 
              71.7 million units in the Aberdeen Fixed Interest Unit Trust and 
              13.4 million units in the Aberdeen High Yield Bond Unit Trust. </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Turnkey management 
              of syndicates </b><br>
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font">In March 2000, 
              we announced our appointment by the Mitsui Marine and Fire Insurance 
              Company Limited of Japan to provide a turnkey managing agency service 
              to enable them to set up a new corporate syndicate at Lloyd&#146;s 
              which commenced business on 1 April 2000. In June 2000, we were 
              appointed by Sorema SA, a subsidiary of Groupama SA of France, to 
              provide a similar service for their new corporate syndicate which 
              commenced business on 1 July 2000. These new syndicates are important 
              indications of the confidence of major overseas insurance companies 
              in Lloyd&#146;s and Chaucer. The turnkey syndicates provide useful 
              additional income and overhead absorption for the Chaucer Group. 
              </span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font"><b>Chaucer managed 
              capacity in 2001 </b></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" align="left"><span class="font">Managed capacity 
              on our own syndicates for 2001 is targeted to increase by &pound;10 
              million over 2000 levels through a 12.5% increase in our non marine 
              syndicate, 1096. As yet it is difficult to predict how Lloyd&#146;s 
              Names will react to restrictions imposed on their underwriting through 
              solvency deficits and increases in risk based capital ratios. It 
              is possible that Names&#146; capacity will fall heavily for the 
              2001 year, in which case we will aim to make up capital shortfalls 
              through quota share reinsurance treaties. We are in discussions 
              with a potential new partner who may provide &pound;20 million of 
              underwriting support to the Chaucer in house syndicates for the 
              2001 year</span>.</p>

            <p class="font" align="left">In the first four Lloyd&#146;s capacity

            <p class="font" align="left"><b>Chaucer Consortium Underwriting Limited 
              <br>
              </b> </p>
            <p class="font" align="left">On 13 September we announced the launch 
              of Chaucer Consortium Underwriting Limited (&#147;CCUL&#148;) to 
              manage consortium arrangements, both for Lloyd&#146;s syndicates 
              (including those managed by Chaucer) and Treasury approved insurance 
              companies. <br>
              <br>
              CCUL will commence operations for the 2001 year of account and is 
              expected to manage approximately US$40 million of premium income. 
              The company will be fifty one per cent owned by Chaucer, with the 
              balance being held by CCUL management. CCUL&#146;s management team 
              will be led by Colin Gilbert, aged 41, who was previously employed 
              by the Crowe Insurance Group. He has over 20 years&#146; experience 
              in the insurance market and has been the lead underwriter of a number 
              of consortia covering personal accident and space risks. </p>
            <p class="font" align="left"><b>Outlook <br>
              </b><br>
              We expect the motor market to show further improvement over the 
              next year. Substantial rate increases have been applied in a number 
              of areas and figures from the first eight months have improved radically 
              over those recorded a year ago. <br>
              <br>
              The improvements in premium rates and conditions in other markets 
              continue to relate to specific classes and are not yet as widespread 
              as we would have wished, although they point to good improvements 
              for 2001. <br>
