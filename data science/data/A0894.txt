<span class="bodytextbold">
These days more parents than ever are paying for a private or university education for their children.  So how do you fund it without breaking the bank?  With your Freestyle Mortgage, there’s a cost-effective and practical way to fund your children’s further education.
</span>

<br><br>
<span class="bodytextbold">Cost of Education</span>
<br><br>
<span class="bodytext">When it comes to funding further education, costs can start mounting considerably.  In fact, the average total cost of studying for a three year degree has doubled in the past five years to almost £21,000*</span>
<br><br>
<span class="bodytextbold">Funding an Education</span>
<br><br>
<span class="bodytext">Funding an education for your children is likely to be the most important – and one of the most expensive – investments you’re likely to make.  So how can you plan for it?  Traditionally, the options have been limited to just three: long term savings, paying from your income, or taking out a loan.
<br><br>
Saving in advance is not as easy as it sounds, savings plans have to be started when your children are still very young otherwise this option probably won’t be sufficient.  Paying student expenses straight from your income is not a viable solution for most parents – could you afford a bill of over £7,000* per year, per child?  A personal loan can also be an expensive option and you wouldn’t want to take out a new loan each year.</span>
<br><br>
<span class="bodytextbold">Cash Reserve</span>
<br><br>
<span class="bodytext">With your Freestyle Mortgage, there’s a cost-effective and practical way to fund your children’s further education.
<br><br>
You can simply borrow from your Cash Reserve and repay over a term to suit you.  Using your Cash Reserve is one of the most affordable and flexible means of funding a brighter future for your children – and it’s at mortgage rates too!
<br><br>
Even if your children’s university days are a long way in the future, you can start preparing now by overpaying on your mortgage (either regularly or with lump sums) and build up a sizeable fund for future fees.
<br><br>
In short, it’s an efficient and cost-effective way to give your child a great start in life.</span>
