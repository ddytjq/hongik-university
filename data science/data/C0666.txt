                                Acquisition of Royal &amp; SunAlliance Investments - Friends Ivory &amp; Sime Meets First Milestone,Appoints Senior Management Team
                              
                            </B>
                          <P ALIGN="right">17 May, 2002</P>
                        </SPAN>
                    
                        <SPAN CLASS="bodyText">
                            
                                <p>Following the announcement last month by Friends Ivory &amp; Sime plc (FIS), of its agreement to acquire Royal &amp; SunAlliance Investments (R&amp;SAI), Howard Carter, who continues as Chief Executive Officer, FIS plc, today announces that following completion of the acquisition, the Executive Management Team responsible for running the enlarged business will be:</p><p><strong>Robert Talbut</strong> as Chief Investment Officer<br><strong>Nick Criticos</strong> as Managing Director, Retail Funds<br><strong>Peter Arthur</strong> as Managing Director, Institutional Funds and Investment Trusts<br><strong>Ken Back</strong> as Chief Operating Officer<br><strong>Ian Paterson Brown</strong> as Finance Director</p><p><strong>Howard Carter</strong> said, "The acquisition of R&amp;SAI is transformational for FIS, doubling our funds under management to £70 bn and propelling us into the top-ten active investment managers in the UK.  I have therefore taken this opportunity to put in place an appropriate management structure and appoint the best executive management team drawn from both organisations to realise the potential that this deal provides."</p><p><strong>Robert Talbut</strong> (41) has been appointed as Chief Investment Officer and will join the board of FIS plc on completion of the acquisition of R&amp;SAI.  Robert will have overall responsibility for the ongoing development and co-ordination of FIS plc investment management activities that will double to £70bn funds under management from 1 July 2002.  He currently holds the position of Managing Director of Investment Management at R&amp;SAI.</p><p><strong>Nick Criticos</strong> (43) has been appointed as Managing Director, Retail Funds and will join the board of FIS plc on completion of the acquisition of R&amp;SAI.  Nick will have overall responsibility for driving forward the combined retail business that will include Unit Trusts and OEICs under the new ISIS brand.  He currently holds the position of Managing Director, Retail at R&amp;SAI.</p><p><strong>Peter Arthur</strong> (45) has been appointed Managing Director, Institutional Funds and Investment Trusts and will continue as an Executive Director on the board of FIS plc.  Peter will have overall responsibility for development of the enlarged institutional business, including the insurance business of Friends Provident and R&amp;SA, as well as the Investment Trust business.  He currently holds the position of Managing Director, Retail and Institutional Business at FIS plc.</p><p><strong>Ken Back</strong> (46) will continue his role as Chief Operating Officer and as an Executive Director on the board of FIS plc.  Ken will have overall responsibility for the ongoing management and development of Friends Ivory &amp; Simes support functions and operations.  In addition, he will have business responsibility for the enlarged property asset management operations.</p><p><strong>Ian Paterson Brown</strong> (48) has been appointed as Finance Director and will join the board of FIS plc on completion of the acquisition of R&amp;SAI.  Ian will have overall responsibility for Finance and Financial Reporting across the group.  He currently holds the position of Company Secretary and Head of Compliance at FIS plc.</p><p>Commenting on the appointments, Sir David Kinloch, Chairman, Friends Ivory &amp; Sime plc added,  "I am delighted that, following completion of the acquisition, Robert Talbut, Nick Criticos and Ian Paterson Brown will join the board of FIS plc.  Together with Howard Carter, Peter Arthur and Ken Back, this new management team will represent a formidable force in UK asset management to take forward the newly enlarged business and achieve our strategic objectives."</p><p>For Further information</p><table width="100%"><tr><td valign="top">Howard Carter</td><td>Chief Executive Officer Friends Ivory &amp; Sime</td><td
