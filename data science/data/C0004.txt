    <td valign="top"> 
      <p><span class="heading"> <span class="body"><br>
        Income Protection Description</span></span><span class="body"><span class="title"><br>
        <br>
        What is Income Protection (also known as PHI)?</span> <br>
        It is a health insurance policy which remains in force for a set term 
        and is designed to pay you a regular income (after a waiting period) if 
        you become sick or disabled /during the term of the policy, until you 
        return to work, the plan term ends or you die.<br>
        <br>
        <span class="title">Its aims <br>
        </span>To provide you with a replacement income if you become sick or 
        disabled during the term of your policy.<span class="title"><br>
        <br>
        Your commitment <br>
        </span>You agree to pay a regular monthly or yearly premium for the life 
        of the policy.<span class="title"><br>
        <br>
        Risk factors</span> <br>
        Your own circumstances may change, forcing you to stop paying premiums 
        and therefore losing the benefit of your cover. The benefit payable is 
        based on your income and, therefore, if your income reduces the benefit 
        will reduce accordingly.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">How soon will I start to receive 
        my replacement income?<br>
        </span>You can choose to receive an income after a waiting period of 4, 
        8, 13, 26 or 52 weeks from the date on which you first become sick or 
        injured.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">Why do I need a waiting period?<br>
        </span>The waiting period is the period between becoming disabled and 
        benefit starting, during which no income is payable under the policy. 
        You should select a waiting period which takes into account any income 
        you may continue to receive from your employment immediately after becoming 
        disabled. The longer the waiting period, the lower the cost of cover.<span class="title"><br>
        </span></span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title"> How long will my PHI plan remain 
        in force?<br>
        </span>You can choose at outset when you want the plan to end. Subject 
        to a minimum term of five years, you may choose your 50th, 55th, 60th 
        or 65th birthday.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">How ill or disabled do I have 
        to be to claim a replacement income?<br>
        </span>Providing your injury or illness genuinely prevents you from working 
        (in accordance with the definition of disability on which your plan is 
        based) you will be eligible to receive an income once your selected waiting 
        period has expired.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">How do I make a claim? <br>
        </span>A claim form will need to be completed.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What level of benefit am I eligible 
        to receive?</span> <br>
        You can choose any level of benefit but the maximum you can insure is 
        65% of your income prior to becoming disabled, less the Single Person's 
        State Benefit payable on incapacity. An upper limit is imposed for those 
        who have high earnings. In the event of a claim, the maximum benefit payable 
        will depend on whether you are receiving income from other sources (such 
        as an employer's pension scheme), in which case your benefit may be reduced.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">Can I claim for the same illness 
        more than once?</span> <br>
        Yes. With some policies if, after having received an income, you return 
        to work but suffer a recurrence of the same illness within six months, 
        your replacement income will resume immediately without the need for a 
        further waiting period.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I change jobs?</span> 
        <br>
        The cost of your cover is based partly on your occupation at the time 
        you took the plan out. If you change your occupation, you should inform 
        the Insurer since this may affect the assessment of any subsequent claim.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I return to work 
        on a part-time basis?</span> <br>
        A proportionally reduced benefit may be paid for a period of time.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I return to work 
        in a different, lower-paid job? <br>
        </span>The benefit payable may be reduced in proportion to the difference 
        between the earnings at the time of disability and those under the new 
        employment.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I become unemployed?</span> 
        <br>
        If you become unemployed, cover will continue for a further 12 months, 
        subject to the waiting period selected. As long as premiums are paid, 
        you may be entitled to income protection, payable in the event of an illness 
        or injury.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I become pregnant?</span> 
        <br>
        In normal circumstances you will be covered. If, however, your illness 
        or disability is confirmed to be the result of your pregnancy or childbirth, 
        the waiting period you have selected will not begin until three months 
        after the day on which your pregnancy ends.</span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What other benefits are offered?</span> 
        <br>
        <span class="body">Your premiums can be guaranteed not to increase during 
        the term of the policy (except if you choose the increasing option or 
        you change jobs to a different risk category). Or you can arrange for 
        your premiums and benefits to remain at the same level for the entire 
        term of the plan, or to increase in line with the Retail Prices Index 
        (up to a maximum of 10% per annum). Your premiums will be waived for as 
        long as benefit is being paid </span> </span></p>
      <p><span class="body"><span class="title">What happens if I die?<br>
        </span><span class="body">Your plan will lapse.</span><br>
        <br>
        <span class="title">What happens if I want to surrender the policy? </span> 
        <br>
        <span class="body">The contract has no cash-in value at any time unless 
        you have a 'Unit Linked' contract.</span><span class="title"><br>
        <br>
        Termination of policy<br>
        </span>The plan will terminate at the age specified on the policy document 
        or on your earlier death. If premiums are not paid within 28 days of the 
        due date, the policy will automatically lapse. However, premiums will 
        be waived if you are in receipt of benefits. The policy may not be cancelled 
        by the Insurer during the policy term except for non-payment of premiums.</span></p>
