<p><b>BYLAWS OF THE<br>
</b>WORLD FLYING DISC FEDERATION<br>
(a Colorado Nonprofit Corporation)</p>

<p>(as revised by the 2000 Congress in Germany)</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE I - PURPOSES</b></p>

<p>The World Flying Disc Federation (&quot;WFDF&quot;) is
organized exclusively for educational purposes and to foster
national and international amateur sports competition within the
meaning of and pursuant to Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal
Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (or under the corresponding
provision of any future United States Internal Revenue law),
including, but not limited to, such purposes as:</p>

<p>1. To serve as the international governing body of all disc
sports, with responsibility for sanctioning world championship
events, establishing uniform rules, setting of standards for and
recording of world records;</p>

<p>2. To encourage and protect the spirit of flying disc play;</p>

<p>3. To encourage disc play throughout the world and promote the
establishment of new national flying disc associations, advising
them on all flying disc activities and general management; <br>
4. To promote public awareness and acceptance of disc play as
sport;</p>

<p>5. To provide a forum for discussion of all aspects of disc
play among the various membership organizations; and</p>

<p>6. Consistent with above principles, to transact any and all
other lawful business or businesses for which a corporation may
be incorporated pursuant to the Colorado Nonprofit Corporation
Act, as it may be amended from time to time.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE II - MEMBERSHIP</b></p>

<p>1. <b>Qualifications for Membership.</b> There shall exist the
following classes of membership:</p>

<p>1.1<b> Regular:</b> National flying disc associations which
meet all of the following criteria, receive preliminary approval
of WFDF's Board of Directors and final approval of the Congress:</p>

<p>(a) must be organized with bylaws acceptable to WFDF;</p>

<p>(b) must be open to participation without regard to race,
color, religion, national origin, sex or sexual orientation;</p>

<p>(c) must represent a minimum membership of 50 disc players;</p>

<p>(d) must hold an annual meeting and/or have another
representative mechanism for electing a board of directors;</p>

<p>(e) must fairly represent the interests of disc players in the
country;</p>

<p>(f) must pay dues as established by the Congress;</p>

<p>(g) only one regular member per country shall be permitted.</p>

<p>1.2<b> Organizational:</b> Disc sports organizations which
meet all of the following criteria, receive preliminary approval
of WFDF's Board of Directors and final approval of the Congress:</p>

<p>(a) must be organized with bylaws acceptable to WFDF;</p>

<p>(b) must be open to participation without regard to race,
color, religion, national origin, sex or sexual orientation;</p>

<p>(c) must represent a minimum membership of 50 disc players;</p>

<p>(d) must hold an annual meeting and/or have another
representative mechanism for electing a board of directors;</p>

<p>(e) must fairly represent the interests of participants of the
applicable disc sport worldwide;</p>

<p>(f) must pay dues as established by the Congress;</p>

<p>(g) only one organizational member per disc sport shall be
permitted.</p>

<p>1.3<b> Associate:</b> National flying disc associations or
disc sports organizations which meet all of the following
criteria, receive preliminary approval of WFDF's Board of
Directors and final approval of the Congress:</p>

<p>(a) must be organized with bylaws acceptable to WFDF;</p>

<p>(b) must be open to participation without regard to race,
color, religion, national origin, sex or sexual orientation;</p>

<p>(c) must hold an annual meeting and/or have another
representative mechanism for electing a board of directors;</p>

<p>(d) must fairly represent the interests of all disc players in
the applicable sport or country;</p>

<p>(e) must pay dues as established by the Congress;</p>

<p>(f) associate membership shall not be permitted for any
national flying disc association or disc sports organization in
the event that the nation or disc sport, as applicable, is
currently represented in WFDF by a regular or organizational
member.</p>

<p>1.4<b> Provisional:</b> National associations or disc sports
organizations which meet all of the following criteria, receive
preliminary approval of WFDF's Board of Directors and final
approval of the Congress:</p>

<p>(a) must be open to participation without regard to race,
color, religion, national origin, sex or sexual orientation;</p>

<p>(b) must fairly represent the interests of all disc players in
the applicable sport or country.</p>

<p>(c) provisional membership shall not be permitted for any
national flying disc association or disc sports organization in
the event that the nation or disc sport, as applicable, is
currently represented in WFDF by a regular, organizational or
associate member.</p>

<p>2. <b>Vote.</b> Each regular and organizational member in good
standing shall have one vote in matters requiring a vote of the
membership. Each associate member in good standing shall have one
vote on play-related issues only. As used herein, the term
&quot;play-related issues&quot; shall not include the election of
officers or directors of WFDF or fiscal matters of WFDF. All
regular, organizational and associate members shall have one vote
on any resolution to dissolve WFDF. Provisional members shall
have no voting rights. A member shall be considered in good
standing for voting purposes if it has paid its membership fees
prior to the annual meeting, and has fulfilled all its other
obligations to WFDF.</p>

<p>3. <b>Annual Meetings.</b> The annual meeting of the
membership shall be called the Congress, and shall be held each
calendar year in conjunction with a World Flying Disc
Championship at a place and time to be fixed by the Board of
Directors. The purposes of the annual meeting shall be to: (a)
elect directors and officers, (b) review and approve the previous
year's financial statements and auditors' reports, (c) approve
WFDF's annual budget and elect an Audit Committee, (d) authorize
the basic strategic direction and policies of WFDF, (e) serve as
a forum for discussion of general disc sports issues, and (f) to
take such other action as may properly come before the Congress.
The Secretary shall cause to be given to each member notice of
the time and place of the annual meeting not less than four (4)
months prior to the date of such scheduled meeting. Members must
submit all motions for consideration at the annual meeting to the
Board of Directors not less than three (3) months prior to the
date of such scheduled meeting, and the Board shall forward to
all members a meeting agenda, along with copies of all motions,
reports and other relevant material for consideration at the
Congress, not less than two (2) months prior to the date of such
scheduled meeting.</p>

<p>4. <b>Special Meetings and Actions Taken Without a Meeting.</b>
Special meetings of the Congress may be called by the Board of
Directors. The Secretary shall cause to be given to each member
notice of the time, place and purpose of such meeting not less
than two (2) months prior thereto. The Board shall call a Special
Meeting of the Congress upon the written demand of twenty-five
percent (25%) of the regular and organizational members in good
standing. Whenever the members are required or permitted to take
any action by vote, such action may be taken without a meeting on
written consent, setting forth the action so taken, signed by all
members entitled to vote thereon.</p>

<p>5. <b>Quorum.</b> So long as any meeting of the Congress has
been duly called as provided herein, the presence, in person, by
telephone conference or by proxy, of any Regular or
Organizational member shall constitute a quorum and shall be
necessary for the transaction of any business. The consent of a
majority of the members present and voting shall be required for
passage of any measure before the Congress.</p>

<p>6. <b>Proxies.</b> Each member entitled to vote at a meeting
of the Congress or in an action taken without a meeting shall
authorize a person or persons to vote on behalf of such member by
proxy. A proxy shall be in writing, signed by a member and
revocable at the pleasure of the member executing it. Such proxy
must be delivered to the Secretary prior to the meeting or action
and shall be valid only for the meeting or action named therein.</p>

<p>7. <b>Membership Dues.</b> The annual dues of members shall be
determined from time to time by the Congress, upon recommendation
of the Board. The Congress may in its discretion offer
memberships on an other than annual basis, and shall determine
appropriate sanctioning fees for world championship tournaments
and other tournaments sanctioned or approved by WFDF.</p>

<p>8. <b>Hold Harmless.</b> Each member, as a condition of its
membership, agrees to hold WFDF and its directors, officers,
coordinators, agents and consultants harmless from claims of any
kind, nature or description arising out of the member's
participation in or observance of any game, tournament or event
sponsored or sanctioned by WFDF.</p>

<p>9. <b>Meetings By Telephone.</b> Members may hold or
participate in a meeting of the Congress by means of conference
telephone or similar communications equipment provided that all
such persons so participating in such meeting can hear each other
at the same time.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE III - DIRECTORS</b></p>

<p>1. <b>Management of the Corporation.</b> WFDF shall be managed
by the Board of Directors which shall consist of not less than
five (5) directors. Directors shall be selected without regard to
race, color, religion, national origin, sex or sexual
orientation. The Board shall consist of the President, the
Secretary, the Treasurer, and the Chairs of the Team Disc Sports
and Individual Disc Sports Committees, as elected by the
Congress, along with such at-large directors as the Congress
shall determine. The Congress may also elect one (1) deputy
director, who shall serve as a full voting member of the Board in
the event that any director resigns or is removed prior to the
end of his/her stated term.</p>

<p>2. <b>Election and Term of Directors.</b> An annual election
will be held every second Tuesday of November to elect directors
to hold office for the designated term of office. Each director
shall hold office until the expiration of the term for which he
or she was elected and until his or her successor has been
elected and shall have qualified, or until his or her resignation
or removal. Directors shall serve for two year terms, to be
staggered as determined by the Congress.</p>

<p>3. <b>Increase or Decrease in Number of Directors</b>. The
number of directors may be increased or decreased by vote of the
Congress. No decrease in number of directors shall shorten the
term of any incumbent director.</p>

<p>4. <b>Removal and Resignation.</b> Any director or directors
may be removed by a majority vote of the Congress. A director may
resign at any time by giving written notice to the Board. Unless
otherwise specified in the notice, the resignation shall take
effect upon receipt thereof by the Board.</p>

<p>5. <b>Annual Meetings.</b> There shall be no less than two (2)
meetings of the Board annually, at such places and times to be
fixed by the Board. The Secretary shall cause to be given to each
member of the Board notice of the time and place of the annual
meeting not less than thirty (30) days prior to the date of such
meeting.</p>

<p>6. <b>Special Meetings and Actions Taken Without a Meeting.</b>
A special meeting of the Board may be called by the President or
by the majority of the Board. The Secretary shall cause each
director to be notified of the time, place and purpose of such
meeting not less than ten (10) days prior thereto. A vote of the
Board taken without a meeting may be called by the President or
by a majority of the Board. Whenever members of the Board are
required or permitted to take any action by vote, such action may
be taken without a meeting on written consent, setting forth the
action so taken, signed by all members of the Board of Directors.</p>

<p>7. <b>Quorum.</b> The presence of not less than a majority of
the members of the Board shall constitute a quorum and shall be
necessary for the transaction of business. The consent of a
majority of the full Board, whether present and voting or not,
shall be required for passage of any measure before the Board.</p>

<p>8. <b>Committees.</b> The Board may designate such temporary
or standing committees as it shall deem advisable, and such
committee or committees shall have such powers as may be
specified by resolution of the Board. In addition to such
committees as may be authorized by resolution of the Board, there
shall exist the following standing committees:</p>

<p>8.1. <b>Working Committee.</b> The Working Committee shall
consist of the President, the Secretary and the Treasurer. The
Working Committee shall administer the daily affairs of WFDF, and
shall have and exercise the authority of the Board in the
management of WFDF, except as may be limited by resolution of the
Board or by law.</p>

<p>8.2<b> Rules Committee.</b> The Rules Committee shall oversee
the compilation, revision and distribution of the WFDF Rule Book.
The Rules Committee shall consist of at least one (1)
representative of each disc sport included in the WFDF Rule Book,
and a Chair to be elected by the Congress.</p>

<p>8.3.<b> Team Disc Sports Committee.</b> The Team Disc Sports
Committee shall address such issues as may affect participants in
team disc sports and oversee the world championship competitions
for each of the team disc sports, which shall be held in
accordance with policies established by the Congress. Bids for
such world championships shall be solicited and approved by the
Team Disc Sports Committee or a subcommittee designated for such
purpose, subject to the approval of the Congress. There shall
exist two (2) standing subcommittees, overseeing issues relating
to the sports of Ultimate and Guts, and such other subcommittees
as shall be determined from time-to-time by the Congress. The
Team Disc Sports Committee shall consist of a Chair to be elected
by the Congress, and the chairs of each of the subcommittees. All
decisions of the Team Disc Sports Committee may be appealed to
the Board, and the rulings of the Board on such matters shall be
subject to approval by the Congress.</p>

<p>8.4.<b> Individual Disc Sports Committee.</b> The Individual
Disc Sports Committee shall address such issues as may affect
participants in individual disc sports and oversee the world
championship competitions in each of the individual disc sports,
which shall be held in accordance with policies established by
the Congress. Bids for such world championships shall be
solicited and approved by the Individual Disc Sports Committee or
a subcommittee designated for such purpose, subject to the
approval of the Congress. There shall exist standing
subcommittees overseeing issues relating to Disc Golf, Field
Events, Double Disc Court and Freestyle, along with such other
subcommittees as shall be determined from time-to-time by the
Congress. The Individual Disc Sports Committee shall consist of a
Chair to be elected by the Congress, and the chairs of each of
the subcommittees. The Individual Disc Sports Committee shall
also have responsibility for maintaining world records, and for
proposing revisions to the standards for such world records. All
decisions of the Individual Disc Sports Committee may be appealed
to the Board, and rulings of the Board on such matters shall be
subject to approval by the Congress.</p>

<p>8.5.<b> Audit Committee.</b> The Congress shall elect an Audit
Committee consisting of not less than two (2) members, none of
whom may also be members of the Board. The Audit Committee shall
review the financial records of the Corporation, and report to
the Congress no less than annually.</p>

<p>9. <b>Meetings by Telephone</b>. Members of the Board or any
Committee designated thereby may hold or participate in a meeting
of the Board or such Committee by means of conference telephone
or similar communications equipment provided that all such
persons so participating in such meeting can hear each other at
the same time.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE IV - OFFICERS</b></p>

<p>1. <b>Election, Appointment, Term.</b> Except as otherwise
provided herein, officers shall be appointed by the Board and
shall serve for the designated term. All officers may be
reappointed or reelected to succeeding terms without limitation.</p>

<p>2. <b>Removal, Resignation, Salary.</b> Any officer appointed
by the Board may be removed by the Board with or without cause.
In the event of the death, resignation or removal of an officer,
the Board in its discretion may appoint a successor to fill the
unexpired term. Any two or more offices may be held by the same
person, except the offices of President and Secretary. The
compensation, if any, of all officers shall be fixed by the
Congress.</p>

<p>3. <b>President.</b> The President shall be elected by the
Congress and shall serve for a term of two (2) years. The
President shall preside at all meetings of the Congress and of
the Board, and shall act as Chair of the Working Committee. The
President shall be the chief executive officer of WFDF. The Board
of Directors may authorize the President to enter into or execute
and deliver any instrument in the name of and on behalf of WFDF.
Such authority may be general or confined to specific instances.</p>

<p>4. <b>Vice President.</b> In the absence of the President, or
in the event of his/her inability or refusal to act, the Vice
President (if any) shall perform the duties of the President, and
when so acting, shall have all the powers of and be subject to
all of the restrictions upon the President. The Vice President
(if any) shall perform such other duties as from time to time may
be assigned to him or her by the President or by the Board of
Directors.</p>

<p>5. <b>Treasurer.</b> The Treasurer shall be elected by the
Congress and shall serve for a term of two (2) years. The
Treasurer shall serve on the Working Committee and shall act as
the treasurer and controller of WFDF. The Treasurer shall have
the care and custody of all the funds and securities of WFDF and
shall deposit all such funds in the name of WFDF in such bank or
trust company as the Board of Directors may elect. He or she
shall keep an account of all monies received and expended for use
of WFDF, and shall sign all checks, drafts, notes and orders for
the payment of money authorized by the Board of Directors. The
Treasurer shall also negotiate all loans and/or other financial
transactions on behalf of WFDF at the direction of the Board. The
Board may, in its sole discretion, authorize the Treasurer to
make such payments as may be customary and normal in the ordinary
course of business; all extraordinary disbursements must be
approved by the Working Committee, subject to the discretion of
the Board. The Treasurer shall maintain an up-to-date list of
members in good standing, shall present a report on the financial
affairs of WFDF to the annual meetings of the Congress and the
Board of Directors, shall be responsible for the filing of all
tax returns (if any) as may be required from time to time and
shall prepare a report to the membership to be published in the
newsletter each year.</p>

<p>6. <b>Secretary.</b> The Secretary shall be elected by the
Congress and shall serve for a term of two (2) years. The
Secretary shall have the duty to see that all notices are duly
given in accordance with the provisions of these Bylaws or as
required by law, shall keep the minutes of the meetings of the
Board of Directors and the Congress in a book or books provided
for such purposes, and shall maintain a file of such other books
and papers of WFDF as the Board of Directors may direct. The
Secretary shall prepare, under the direction of the Board of
Directors, periodic reports as may be required under state law.</p>

<p>8. <b>Other Officers.</b> The Board shall appoint such other
officers as may be necessary to conduct the business of WFDF. In
addition, the Board may designate special consultants or agents
at any time. Compensation, if any, for all such officers,
consultants and agents shall be fixed by the Board, so long as
such officers, consultants or agents are not Board members or
affiliated with Board members.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE V - INDEMNIFICATION</b></p>

<p>Any person who shall at any time serve, or have served, as a
director or officer of WFDF, of any other enterprise at the
request of WFDF, and the heirs, executors, and administrators of
such person, shall be indemnified by WFDF against all costs and
expenses reasonably incurred in the defense of, or paid in the
settlement of, any claim, action, suit or proceeding, to the
extent such indemnification is permitted by the Colorado
Nonprofit Corporation Act, as amended. WFDF may in its
discretion, but shall not be required to, advance to any such
person expenses reasonably incurred in connection with such
claim, action, suit or proceeding, and may indemnify and advance
expenses to an employee or agent of WFDF to the same extent as to
an officer or director. The foregoing indemnification shall not
be exclusive of any other rights to which those indemnified may
be entitled to under any agreement, vote of members, or
otherwise.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE VI - FISCAL YEAR</b></p>

<p>The fiscal year of WFDF shall commence on January 1 of each
year and end on December 31.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE VII - THE NEWSLETTER</b></p>

<p>WFDF shall publish, not less than two (2) times annually, a
newsletter which shall serve as the primary vehicle for the
dissemination of information to the members. The newsletter shall
be distributed to all members as a benefit of membership in WFDF.
The newsletter shall be required to publish minutes of all
meetings of the Board and the Congress, and shall be required to
publish a financial report from the Treasurer not less than once
annually.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE VIII - AMENDMENTS</b></p>

<p>These Bylaws may be amended, repealed or altered in whole or
in part by a majority vote of the Congress. Proposed amendments
to the Bylaws may be submitted to the Board by any member at any
time at least three (3) months prior to the next scheduled
meeting of the Congress.</p>

<p><b>ARTICLE X - DISPUTE RESOLUTION</b></p>

<p align="left">The Board shall have the authority to adjudicate
disputes between members, subject to subsequent approval of the
Congress. Such disputes may include, but shall not be limited to,
disputes regarding eligibility for membership, eligibility for
individual participants in world championship events, rulings
with regard to world records and rulings with regard to
disciplinary issues concerning tournaments or events sanctioned
by WFDF. The Congress shall establish policies for the on-site
adjudication of disputes at tournaments or other events
sanctioned by WFDF. In addition, the Board may, in its
discretion, appoint one or more ad hoc committees to investigate
the relevant issues and make appropriate recommendations to the
Board. </p>
