	<H5>CAREER HISTORY - The '90's</H5>
		<TABLE CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="4">
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1990</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Started in the CAMS Non-Turbo Production Car series in EA Falcon Multipoint S-Pack 6.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1991</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Won ARDC Production Car Championship.
			<LI>Runner-up in Australian Production Car Championship, winning rounds one, two and four.
			<LI>Received ARDC Most Improved Driver Award.
			<LI>Competed in inaugural James Hardie 12 Hour at Bathurst in a VN V6 Commodore.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1992</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Won first round (both heats - one wet, one dry) of ARDC Production Car Series at Amaroo.
			<LI>Purchased new EBSS Ford 4.0 Falcon.
			<LI>Won ARDC Series.
			<LI>Runner-up Australian Production Car Championship.
			<LI>Won three rounds of the Australian Production Car Championship including the support race at Bathurst, setting a lap record for the class.
			<LI>Competed in New Zealand rounds of the Nissan Mobil 500 Series in EB Falcon converted to Group N specifications, leading Group N Class in Wellington for 3.5 hours.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1993</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Won Australian Production Car Championship.
			<LI>Runner-up in ARDC Production Car Series.
			<LI>Winner of Winfield Triple Challenge at Eastern Creek.
			<LI>Lap record holder at Amaroo Park.
			<LI>Won four out of six Australian Production Car Championship races, with two second places.
			<LI>From a total of 22 starts, recorded 14 wins and six seconds.
			<LI>Winner of 1993 Sandown 500 Production Car Class (finished 8th outright).
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1994</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Competed in James Hardie 12 Hour with Australia's first LPG production car.
			<LI>Winner of the 1994 Truck Racing Championship SBR Class.
			<LI>Competed at the Tooheys 1000 support race debuting in a Formula Ford.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1995</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Competed in the Fordcare Formula Ford National Championship.
			<LI>Finished seventh outright and won the Dunlop Rookie of the Year award.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
		    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1996</B></TD>
		    <TD>
			<UL>
			<LI>Debut year in Group A VR Commodore.
			<LI>Competed sucessfully at Indy and the Calder Park and Oran Park rounds of the Shell Australian Touring Car Championship (SATCC).
			<LI>Winner of the Privateer Cup race at Clader Park together with the Best Presented Car and Crew award for the weekend.
			</UL>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
			<TD COLSPAN="2">
				<TABLE CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="0">
				<TR>
				    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1997</B></TD>
				    <TD VALIGN="top">
					<UL>
					<LI>Won the ARDC AMSCAR Series.
					<LI>Six wins from nine starts, including two pole positions.
					<LI>4th Privateer at the Primus 1000 at Bathurst.
					</UL>
					</TD>
				    <TD ROWSPAN="2" ALIGN="center"><IMG SRC="mrtrks.JPG" WIDTH=350 HEIGHT=260 BORDER=0 HSPACE="5" ALIGN="RIGHT" ALT="Mal with trophy"></TD>
				</TR>
				<TR>
				    <TD VALIGN="top"><B>1998</B></TD>
				    <TD VALIGN="top">
					<UL>
					<LI>FAI 1000 Classic Bathurst Privateer Cup winner.
					<LI>10th outright FAI 1000 Classic Bathurst.
					<LI>Third outright Privateer Cup in the Shell Australian Touring Car Championship.
					<LI>Privateer Dash winner, Calder Park.
					</UL>
					</TD>
				</TR>
				</TABLE>
			</TD>
		</TR>
		<TR>
			<TD COLSPAN="2">	
			<P><HR COLOR="#FF6F0F"></P>
			
			<P>Mal has also devoted considerable time to the publicity and promotional area. He has been invited as guest commentator by Channel 7 and Channel 10, giving clear recognition to his extensive knowledge and dedication to the sport, and also, importantly, highlighting his communication skills.</P>
			<P>From 1991 to 1997, Mal was on the Board of the Australian Racing Drivers Club, being the youngest ever director.</P>
