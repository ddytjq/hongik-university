<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">LOUISVILLE, Ky.&nbsp;-- Verdell Jones plays the guitar. He also coaches a summer basketball team fueled by some of the best high school talent in Illinois.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"I'm a musician," the Champaign-based Ft. Sooy "No Limit" boss said last weekend here at the Kentucky HoopStar Classic.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"I do Christian gospel. And I don't go to a gig and get better. It's working by myself in my room that gets me better. The same situation applies in basketball."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Especially at the grassroots level.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">But critics of the series of prominent national summer tournaments&nbsp;-- known most often as the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) circuit&nbsp;-- wonder if the system helps kids get better.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Does it hone the talent on hand? Does it train the players involved? Or does it do little but give them a heightened sense of entitlement and an overblown sense of self? And is it good or bad for the overall game?</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Only one thing's for sure: This brand of basketball is shaping the sport as a whole&nbsp;-- and at all levels&nbsp;-- here at the onset of the 21st century.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"A couple years ago, you had two big guys on the court," said Mike Theus, who coaches his Louisiana Select club. "Now, because of AAU, you don't always have two big guys. You might have four guards and a big or two bigs and three guards. It's changed the way the game is played."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">In ways both good and bad&nbsp;-- and in ways somewhere in between.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"It's just different," said Bob McKillop, the head coach at defending Southern Conference champ Davidson. "It's a reflection of the experiences kids are having."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">And the style of basketball they're playing.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">There's always a tournament. Or a Classic or a Showcase or a Rumble or a Jamboree. Somewhere. Kids are always shooting threes. They're always running and jumping and dunking.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">And they're almost always pushing the tempo.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Coach Williams "Wigs" Pearson's Alabama Lasers started this month by winning a 17-and-under championship in Hampton, Va., at the prestigious Boo Williams Invitational, and they did it without anyone taller than 6-foot-7.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">How? By setting a frenetic pace with quick-quick point man Mike Davis, swingman Deshawn Danzey and willowy tourney MVP Michael Ford.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The Virginia-based Fairfax Stars, meanwhile, lost to Alabama in the Boo semis. They made it all the way to the title game the next weekend in Villanova, Pa., at the Charlie Weber Invitational. Coach Curtis Symonds' upstart squad did it primarily with guards and wings.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"We don't have much size," standout Star Ricky Lucas said after one win in suburban Philly, "but we play hard, we play together, and we get up and down the floor."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">So do Jones' "No Limit" kids&nbsp;-- and they wouldn't have it any other way.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"It's fun," 6-5 sophomore point guard Shaun Livingston said. "We want to get a 20-point lead and get out and run. That's what I'm trying to do."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">He's hardly the only one.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Ask any Division I prospect what he's looking for in a college program. Some say education. Some say proximity to home. Many say opportunity to play right away. But most say up-tempo style of play.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The college coaches are listening. They want these kids to play at their schools&nbsp;-- and they need to fill seats&nbsp;-- so they're ready and willing to run and gun.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Did the kids' preferences lead to the coaches' philosophies. Or was it the other way around? Not sure. But this much is certain: AAU's playing a part.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"I think it's made the game faster," Western Carolina coach Steve Shurina said earlier this month at the Final Four in Atlanta.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"We play that style, so we like kids who are going to be able to play that type of game, but the skills are lacking. There's so much playing going on and less working on your skills."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"There are fewer kids who can shoot the ball," said Mo Cassara, the coach of the high-powered prep team at Worcester Academy in Massachusetts. "That's because of the up-and-down nature of AAU. There's very little defense played, and there's very little 'system.'" Not for all programs. Some, obviously, are better than others.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"It all depends on how long the kids have been playing together," said Mike Hart, the head man at the St. Andrew's School in Barrington, R.I.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"The teams that just bring in kids for weekends, they're the ones that stand around and play a lot of one-on-one, but some other teams practice and play together and look like high school teams."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">But those are in the minority.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"High school is more about fundamentals, and the summer is more about athleticism," said Gary DeCesare, the coach at Bronx schoolboy powerhouse St. Raymond's.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">""I think the kids lack fundamentals overall. The play is sloppy. I see kids getting to college much less polished."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The DI coaches see that, too.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"There's a tremendous emphasis on offense and pushing the ball and athleticism in AAU basketball," McKillop explained.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"Because of that, players are getting so much better in the open-court game, and coaches are now pulling the trigger and allowing their players significantly more freedom and flexibility in the attack mode. That's evident in college basketball, and it's obvious in the NBA."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">And it's not just about the style of play.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"It's more of an attitude," said Bryce Hibbard, the head man at Louisville's Male High School. "It's almost like some of these teams spoil the kids with all this gear and the travel. All they're looking for is, 'What can you give me?'"</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">One thing they're given, of course, is travel&nbsp;-- and lots of it.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Stops in New York, New Jersey, Chicago, Indianapolis, Florida, Las Vegas and Los Angeles are the norm. And those are just for the biggest tournaments.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"I think kids come in expecting more," Bowling Green assistant Artie Pepelea said. "They expect to travel because they've already been to Vegas and California or even Hawaii and Europe."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">And they expect gear&nbsp;-- shoes, shorts, jerseys, gym bags, etc.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"There's a good side and a bad side to AAU," said Jones, whose Ft. Sooy team wears AND1 sneakers. "But in order to get better, you've got to play the best."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Jones and his Ft. Sooy crew are scheduled to be in Houston this coming weekend, in Minneapolis the weekend after that, in Atlanta in June and all over the country&nbsp;-- from New York to Las Vegas to Los Angeles&nbsp;-- in July.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"Iron sharpens iron&nbsp;-- that's a Biblical passage&nbsp;-- and you've got to get your kids seen," Jones explained. "You've got to have them out there."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Even if it means more travel and more games&nbsp;-- and less practice time.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT size=3>HAL OF A TOURNAMENT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Hal Pastner doesn't need much. The Houston-based man-about-basketball is usually pretty fired up about something or other. This week, though, the Hal Hype is running on overdrive.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Pastner and his Houston Hoops program is ready to host some 200-plus teams in various age groups at the Kingwood Classic. The Friday-to-Sunday tourney is set for sites all over the city.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"This thing is massive," he said Tuesday morning. "I'm going 23 hours a day. It's the largest 48-hour basketball event ever put on in this country."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Or any country, for that matter&nbsp;-- at least hopefully.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Play in the 66-team 17-and-under bracket starts Friday night at 7 and wraps up with Sunday afternoon's championship game.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">The largely Nike-affiliated field includes the SoCal All-Stars, the Portland Legends, the Georgia Stars, the Arkansas Wings, the Kentucky HoopStars, the Houston Hoops, the Houston Superstars, the Tennessee Travelers, the St. Louis Eagles and the Albany City Rocks.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Also expected to be there: Bloomington Red, California Team Select, Memphis YOMCA, Belmont Shore, Team Texas Elite, Louisiana Select and Ft. Sooy "No Limit."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">Some of the top players who are supposed to be on hand: Ndi Ebi, the 6-10 swingman from the Houston Hoops; Brian Randle, the Peoria standout who runs with the St. Louis Eagles; Springfield, Ill., shooting guard Richard McBride of Ft. Sooy; and super sophomore point guard Andre Allen of Memphis YOMCA.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">All those teams. All those players. All those games. Might Pastner get any sleep this weekend?</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">"Of course not," he said.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** Don't expect <B>David Dees</B> to get out of Dayton&nbsp;-- or at least the state of Ohio&nbsp;-- without a fight. The 6-3 junior shooting guard with the football physique is getting the most interest right now from UD and Xavier. He was terrific while playing for the Greater Cincinnati Basketball Association this past weekend in Louisville. Dees, a bright kid with a 1090 SAT score already, averaged 23 points a game this winter for coach Ralph Grant's team at Dayton Christian. "I have a lot to work on," he said. "I don't ever want to be happy with what I've accomplished."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** Fort Lauderdale, Fla., swingman <B>Jimmy Tobias</B> earned tournament MVP honors in helping <B>Eric Alston</B>'s Coast to Coast AAU program to victory this past weekend at the Spring Break Festival in Coral Springs. Tobias, out of perennially strong Dillard High School, scored 18 points in the championship game against the Tallahassee Wildcats. High-major post prospect <B>Chris Richard</B> from Lakeland was excellent, too, tallying at least 15 points and 10 rebounds in every game he played.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** There's something about those guards from St. Louis powerhouse Vashon. Missouri-bound combo threat <B>Jimmy McKinney</B>, of course, was the program's senior star this year. But juniors <B>Dewayne Polk</B> and <B>Donny Jackson</B>&nbsp;-- both little big guys at 5-9&nbsp;-- were pretty darn good, too, helping coach <B>Floyd Irons</B>' team to a spot in the national top 10. Now they're leading the St. Louis Gameface AAU troops. "He'll bring them along," Gameface coach <B>Derek Bump</B> said of the legendary Irons. "He teaches them how to get better. They've got big futures, those two."</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** Washington, D.C., post man <B>Will Bowers</B> is a 7-footer with a 1,300 SAT. Yet it took a stellar performance a couple weekends ago in Houston at the Texas Superstar Classic to super-charge his recruitment. Many, many schools are suddenly interested, although Notre Dame, Charlotte, Florida State, Providence and Ohio State appear to be the leaders at this point. Bowers averaged 12 points, 7.5 rebounds and 2.5 assists this past winter for coach <B>Mike Glick</B>'s Archbishop Spalding squad. He plays his summer ball with <B>Paul Bowden</B>'s Baltimore Select group.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** Brooklyn big man <B>Chris Taft</B> has had plenty of guests during this April's evaluation period. According to recruiting analyst <B>Dave Telep</B> of TheInsidersHoops.com, Villanova, Pittsburgh, Wake Forest, Syracuse, North Carolina and Georgia Tech have made trips to watch him work out at Xaverian High School. Taft told Telep that he wants to visit UNC, Pitt, 'Nova and Duke.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** The last of the three St. Anthony Class of 2002 guards made his college decision this week. <B>Donald Copeland</B>, a 5-10 point man from the Jersey City juggernaut, committed Tuesday to Seton Hall. Copeland teamed the last two seasons with fellow backcourt stars <B>Elijah Ingram</B> (St. John's) and <B>DeWayne Lee</B> (St. Joe's) to lead <B>Bob Hurley</B>'s Friars to back-to-back Tournament of Champions state titles. Copeland averaged 15 points, six assists and four steals as a senior.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify">*** One of the plum jobs in Indiana's high school ranks opened up last week. Coach <B>Steve Witty </B>resigned from his post at Indianapolis' Ben Davis High. The 55-year-old Witty finished his 13-year tenure at the school with a record of 266-68. The Giants, 21-3 this past season, won state championships in 1995 and '96.</P>
