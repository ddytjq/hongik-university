<H1>Jewson Eastern Counties League</H1>
<H2>Founded: 1935</H2>
<H3>Founder Members</H3>
<P>Bury Town, Chelmsford, Clacton Town, Colchester Town, Crittal Athletic,
Gorleston, Great Yarmouth Town, Harwich &amp; Parkeston, Ipswich Town, King's
Lynn, Lowestoft Town and Thetford Town. Great Yarmouth Town and Lowestoft Town
have been in continuous membership of the league since it started</P>
<H2>Area Covered</H2>
<P>The Eastern Counties League covers Norfolk, Suffolk, Essex and the Eastern
part of Cambridgeshire.</P>
<H2>Structure</H2>
<P>2 divisions, 40 clubs<BR><A HREF="#ecl">Premier Division</A> (22 clubs)<BR><A
HREF="#ecl1">Division One</A> (18 clubs).</P>
<P>The league operated with a single division until 1988 when the existing
division became the Premier Division and a new Division One was added.</P>
<HR SIZE="4">

<H3>Promotion: 1 club to the <A HREF="docmarten.html">Dr Martens League</A>
(subject to meeting the League's entry criteria)</H3>
<H3>Relegation: 2 clubs to <A HREF="#ecl1">Division One</A></H3>
<H3>Composition 1999-2000</H3>
<P>Bury Town, Clacton Town, Diss Town, Fakenham Town, Felixstowe Port &amp; Town,
Gorleston, Great Yarmouth Town, Halstead Town, Harwich &amp; Parkeston, Histon,
Ipswich Wanderers, Lowestoft Town, Maldon Town, Mildenhall Town, Newmarket Town,
Soham Town Rangers, Stowmarket Town, AFC Sudbury, Warboys Town, Watton United,
Woodbridge Town, Wroxham.</P>
<H4>Joining</H4>

<H4>Leaving</H4>
<P>Relegated to Division One: Ely City</P>
<H4>Merger</h4>
