<img width="145" height="59" border="0" vspace="3" hspace="10" align="left" src="http://a1801.g.akamai.net/f/1801/2004/3d/www.fifa.com/images/fifacoke.gif" alt="FIFA/Coca-Cola Ranking">
Five-time World Champions Brazil reclaim their place at the head of the table, making them the top achievers in the new FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking. As is customary in the wake of the World Cup finals, the pecking order is turned upside down as teams that caused a stir in Korea and Japan reap the rewards in terms of points and ranking. After surrendering first place to France earlier in the year, the Seleção are once again number one in world football. The Brazilians’ seven consecutive wins, and early exits by France and Argentina, left only one team from the leading trio in the running for top spot and the dazzling boys from Brazil can now once again settle back on football’s throne. FIFA World Cup™ runners-up Germany (5th, up 6) missed out on promotion to number two in the FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking when they were defeated in the final, but they are nonetheless back in the Top Ten. Compared with the last ranking, England (8th, up 4) gain considerable ground, while Colombia (9th, down 5) and Italy (10th, down 4) both slide down several places.

Turkey’s unexpected run of victories catapults them to heady new heights (12th, up 10) from which they can set their sights on the Top Ten. Meanwhile, Mover of the Month Korea Republic (22nd, up 18) reclaim their place as Asia’s top team for a second time since the end of 1998. Senegal’s surprise performances give them an astounding 11-place boost up the table, making them the third-best African team in the FIFA/Coca-Cola World Ranking, behind the well-established powers of Cameroon and Nigeria.
<p>

In the lower half of the table, the world watched as Bhutan (199th, up 3) notched up a victory against Montserrat to claim a place in the top 200.
<p>

Of the six matches in which the victors amassed scores in excess of the highly elusive 50-point mark, Denmark’s 2-0 victory over France (52.6 points), Germany’s 8-0 demolition of Saudi Arabia (52.5 points), and England’s 3-0 win against Denmark (51.6 points) were the pick of the bunch.
