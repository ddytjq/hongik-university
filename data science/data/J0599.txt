            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><b><font face="Arial" size="5">Is
            CART's problem really a lack of Americans?</font></b>
            </p>
            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 6" align="center"><b><font size="2" color="#000000">
            &nbsp;</font><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial" size="2">by Mark Cipolloni</font><font color="#0066FF"><br>
            </font><font color="#000000" face="Arial" size="2">January 16, 2001</font></b></p>
        </td>
          </tr>
  </center>
          <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#000000" valign="middle" align="center" width="480">
            <p align="left"><font color="#FFFFFF" size="3" face="Arial"><b>Go to

            to discuss this article</b></font>

          <tr>
          <TD align=right bgcolor=#FFFFEE vAlign=top width=640>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">CART has too many foreign
      drivers!&nbsp; We hear that statement uttered often as the reason CART
      lacks the following NASCAR enjoys in the USA.&nbsp; But is that REALLY the
      reason, or is it CART's inability to effectively market what I view as a
      distinct asset - drivers from around that world competing on a diversity
      of circuits for the title
      Best-Of-The-Best?</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">It stands to reason that if
      you draw from a larger worldwide talent pool rather than just a national
      talent pool, you are bound to end up with a higher caliber of driver
      talent.&nbsp; Genetically there is no reason why one country's drivers
      should be any better than any other.&nbsp; The trick is to find the best
      talent, and to give it the opportunity to rise to the top.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Frankly I'm tired of hearing
      that CART has too few American's and too many foreign drivers.&nbsp; CART
      team owners do NOT go out of their way to hire foreign drivers on purpose,
      they hire who they think can best get the job done.&nbsp; Sure there are
      some drivers in CART who get a ride because they bring sponsorship money
      with them, but that is no different than NASCAR, F1 or the IRL.&nbsp;
      There are a certain percentage of ride-buying drivers in all forms of
      motorsports.&nbsp; It's inevitable because auto racing always has and
      always will be an expensive sport.&nbsp; The trick for any sanctioning
      body is to make its series popular so that team owners can find sponsors
      on their own and not rely on a driver bringing one.&nbsp; That way they are
      free to pick who they think is the best driver available instead of having
      to settle for the one that brings the most money.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Drivers fall into four
      categories - 1) Those that bring no money and are hired strictly for their
      talent, 2) Those that bring no money but the sponsor insists on the best
      available driver
      from a certain country which increases their chances of landing a ride, 3)
      Those that bring some money, thereby increasing their chances of landing a
      ride, 4) Those that bring the majority of the money through personal
      sponsor relationships.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Given the above four categories,
      let's examine just how many drivers in CART land rides that fall into each
      category.&nbsp; Because of ongoing misinformation spread by those who want
      to discredit CART for their own malevolent reasons, the results will likely surprise you.</font></p>

      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">As can be seen from the above
      analysis, 65%, or a full 2/3 of CART drivers have been hired strictly
      based on talent.&nbsp; Another 22% have been hired based on talent, but
      the sponsor insisted on the best driver available from a specific
      country.&nbsp; That means 87% of all CART drivers have been hired based on
      talent alone.&nbsp; The remaining 13% have been hired because of their
      ability to bring sponsorship money to a team.&nbsp; That may or may not
      mean they are good drivers, just that money had some influence on them
      being hired.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Based on the 13% we conclude, therefore,
      that CART does NOT have an inordinate amount of ride-buying drivers.&nbsp;
      In fact, it has less than some other popular series, less in fact than the
      IRL which accuses CART of the very thing it is guilty of.&nbsp; Which brings us
      back to the argument that CART has so few Americans because of all the
      ride-buying foreign drivers.&nbsp; We would like to put an end to that
      misconception and disinformation once and for all.&nbsp; Certainly we wish
      all of the
      drivers were hired strictly on talent alone, but as we said earlier,
      auto racing is an expensive sport and has always had a certain percentage
      of ride-buying drivers.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Which then brings us back to the
      argument that CART needs more American drivers.&nbsp; Certainly one could
      argue that more American drivers would increase interest by the American
      fans.&nbsp; However, I will argue that while American fans would like to
      cheer for American drivers, deep down inside they would prefer to cheer
      for the best American drivers against the best the world has to offer.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">When Mario Andretti became the
      first American driver to win a US Grand Prix race at Long Beach in 1977,
      the American crowd went wild.&nbsp; They were rooting for Mario because he
      was the best American driver and he had beaten Austrian Niki Lauda and
      South African driver Jody Scheckter in a down and dirty race long dogfight
      on the streets of Long Beach that day.&nbsp; He had beaten the best in the world at the time and the crowd
      went wild. It was a special moment in motorsports.&nbsp; We have witnessed
      similar scenes in Brazil when Ayrton Senna won in
      front of his home crowd.&nbsp; Ditto for Jackie Stewart, Jim Clark, Nigel
      Mansell, Michael Schumacher, etc.&nbsp; Shall I go on?&nbsp; No, I think
      you get the point, beating the best the world has to offer is like winning
      a gold medal in the Olympics, it is something special, very special.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">While watching Dale Earnhardt
      win last year's race at Talladega in a miraculous drive from mid-pack in a few short laps, the large throng on hand witnessed a very
      special moment in American motorsports, but to the rest of the world, it
      went unnoticed.&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">As CART increases its presence
      in the global market, like F1, it will be better served if the starting
      grid is comprised of the best drivers from around the world.&nbsp; NASCAR
      failed miserably in Japan, drawing crowds much smaller than CART, because
      NASCAR brought strictly an American show - American drivers in low-tech American
      cars.&nbsp; The world is accustomed to F1.&nbsp; It is recognized as a
      true World Championship because the best drivers from around the world
      compete on race tracks worldwide to determine who is best.&nbsp; If
      a typical F1 grid consisted of, say, 90% British drivers, F1 would quickly
      lose its status as a World Championship, and be viewed as strictly a
      British Championship series.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">What would serve CART best is
      for it to develop the best farm system it can for American drivers, and
      help those drivers win rides based on talent and not because lack of money
      stopped them on the way up the ladder.&nbsp; And that is sort of what CART
      is doing in their new relationship with Skip Barber.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">
              A key component of the CART-Skip Barber Scholarship Ladder is bringing young talented drivers into a system where their development is not hindered with large financial obstacles. Ensuring that their talents will not go unnoticed builds upon the foundations of the partnership.&nbsp;<br>
              <br>
              "Talent can go further in this system than in any other without big money being a factor. The Barber-CART Scholarship Ladder will invest over $1.3 million annually in the future stars of open-wheel racing," said Skip Barber. "Many of the drivers that come through our schools and series aspire to race in CART. I am very pleased that we and CART are able to come together to ensure that those racer's dreams have a better chance of becoming reality through the assistance that we offer coupled with CART's ability to promote these future talents."&nbsp;</font>
      </p>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Although these drivers will be
      'bringing' money so-to-speak, they will be doing so because they were able
      to prove that they were the best qualified driver for the ride.&nbsp; That
      is very different than a driver getting a ride strictly because of money,
      when in fact they may be what some would call a 'moving chicane'.&nbsp;
      And when those drivers reach the top rung, Champ Cars, the owners will have little doubt that those drivers can get the job done
      for them.&nbsp; Then if they bypass a driver from their ladder series,
          very possibly an American driver, it will likely be
      because they found someone even better in the world.</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Maybe it's the purist in me,
      but I dream of the day when I see flags waving throughout the CART
      grandstands from all over the world, carried by fans cheering on their
      countrymen, in a battle to determine who is the true world champion, the
      driver who can win on small ovals, superspeedways, street courses and road
      courses.&nbsp; To me, the driver who can win the CART Championship, is the
      best driver in the world, worthy of the title World Champion.&nbsp; That
      is what CART must strive for, not to be a copy-cat of NASCAR or the IRL,
      strictly American Championship series, but to be like F1, but only a more
      diverse challenge.</font></p>

      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">It seems everyone has an
      opinion of what CART should be (including us), yet sometimes we feel CART
      itself doesn't know who it is.&nbsp;It tries to be everything to
      everybody.&nbsp; It doesn't have a clear direction.&nbsp;&nbsp;In this <a href="../001022WhoYouAre.htm">related
      article</a> we asked, what CART is and made a case for what CART needs to
      be?</font></p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">American or international?</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Oval or road course?</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Foreign drivers or
          American drivers?</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">Turbocharged or Naturally
          Aspirated?&nbsp; And so on....</font></li>
      </ul>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">In another <a href="../001105CorporateVisionary.htm">related
      article</a> we wrote that CART's leader must be a corporate visionary, the
      one person who gets all of CART's staff, the press, the sponsors, and the fans to
      understand clearly what CART is all about.&nbsp; Getting everyone rowing
      in the same direction to get the message out what CART is, and it's starts
      right at the top with CART's President.&nbsp; What do we think CART's
          message should be?&nbsp; CART is:</font></p>
      <ol>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Not all-American like NASCAR,
          but American and Canadian, Brazilian, Japanese, etc.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Not the IRL, but more,
          much more.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Not about American
          drivers, but about the best drivers</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Not about ride-buying
          drivers, but about ride-winning drivers</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">Not about just ovals or road
          courses, but about small ovals, big ovals, street circuits and road
          courses</font></li>
      </ol>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">It's time CART start being
      CART, and it's time the whole world clearly understand what CART is - the
      best and diverse open wheel race series in the world.&nbsp; And should it
          have more American drivers?&nbsp; Only if they are indeed the best
          available.&nbsp; To settle for anything less, is to accept mediocrity.&nbsp;
          Is auto racing a Broadway play, a show?&nbsp; Or is auto racing a
          sport where the best athletes do battle for supremacy?</font></p>
          <p align="left"><font size="2" face="Arial">You decide.</font></p>
