<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"> Belgium pulled off a shock 3-1 win over Italy
in a friendly international on Saturday thanks to two goals in the final 20
minutes. Belgium took a surprise lead in the sixth minute when Branko Strupar
met a corner from Marc Wilmots with a firm header and a well-positioned Gilles
De Bilde headed in from close range. Italy bounced back in the 26th minute with
a spectacular debut goal from Parma left winger Paolo Vanoli. Mbo Mpenza's
headed clearance fell to Vanoli 20 metres out and his rocketing drive gave
Belgium Ronald Gaspercic no chance. But while Italy enjoyed the bulk of
possession after the break, it was the Belgians who took the lead when Marc
Wilmots headed home a right wing cross from Regis Genaux in the 70th minute.
Five minutes from time, Belgium completed the victory when Yves Vanderhaeghe
fired in a low shot which Gianluigi Buffon could only parry out to Bart Goor
who scored. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B><FONT COLOR="#0000A0">NOV
12</FONT></B></FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Injuries to key players have forced Italy coach
Dino Zoff to radically alter his personnel and tactical approach for Saturday's
friendly with Belgium. Experienced defenders Paolo Maldini, Alessandro
Costacurta and Alessandro Nesta are all missing with injuries and on Friday
Juventus midfielder Antonio Conte left the squad's training camp with a knee
problem. Inter Milan striker Christian Vieri will also be absent from as he
rests a troublesome foot injury. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">But having lost the spine of his side, Zoff has
a chance to experiment with a new formation and give a chance to players who
have been kept on the fringes during the successful Euro 2000 qualification
campaign. Italy's performances in qualifying were effective but unspectacular
and Zoff seems ready to try out a 3-4-3 formation which should harness the best
attacking talent he has avaliable. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Francesco Totti, the 23-year-old AS Roma
striker is set to start as part of a three-pronged attack alongside the
Juventus duo of fit-again Alessandro Del Piero and Filippo Inzaghi. Parma
midfielder Paolo Vanoli is likely to start on the left flank, giving Italy some
genuine width - something they have lacked in recent games. The new three man
defence will feature Ciro Ferrera of Juventus making his first appearance for
the national team since January 1998. Ferrera picked up an injury prior to
France '98 which meant he missed the tournament and he was unable to regain his
spot during Euro 2000 qualification. But with the regular pairing of Nesta and
Costacurta missing he can stake a claim for a place in the finals squad.
</FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial"><B>Probable Team:</B> Gianluigi Buffon;
Christian Panucci, Ciro Ferrara, Fabio Cannavaro; Diego Fuser, Demetrio
Albertini, Dino Baggio, Paolo Vanoli; Alessandro Del Piero, Francesco Totti,
Filippo Inzaghi. </FONT></P>

<TD ALIGN="LEFT" VALIGN="TOP"><P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Denmark all but
secured their place in the European championship finals on Saturday after
beating Israel 5-0 in the first leg of their qualifying play-off. A second
minute goal by Denmark's Jon Dahl Tomasson silenced the 45,000 strong crowd in
the National Stadium as the visitors set about inflicting one of Israel's worst
home defeats. Tomasson added his second goal in the 35th minute as the Israelis
seemed almost incapable of breaking out of their own half and the Danes totally
dominated the game. </FONT></P>
</TD>

<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">Although Israel picked up the pace a little in
the early stages of the second half, the outcome of the match was clear once
Israel's Haim Revivo was sent off for a tackle from behind on Jes Hogh in the
58th. Stig Toefting made it 3-0 with a powerful free kick from the edge of the
area in the 67th minute and Martin Jorgensen headed a high lob over the
outstretched arms of Israel goalkeeper Dudu Awat a minute later. Brian Steen
Nielsen sealed the result in the 73rd minute with a powerful, low shot from the
right. </FONT></P>
<P><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Arial">The result effectively spelt the end of the
reign of Israel's national team coach, Shlomo Scharf, who has said he would not
be seeking to continue after the current campaign. </FONT></P>

Friday. His absence would leave the way open for Sunderland's goalkeeper Thomas
Soerensen who would make his international debut. MSV Duisburg's stocky
midfielder, Stig Toefting has recovered and is fit, but Tottenham's Allan
Nielsen will almost certainly miss the clash after failing to train at all
since Denmark's arrival in Israel earlier in the week. </FONT></P>
