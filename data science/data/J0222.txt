<H1>Road Racing Center of the East</H1>
<P>
<H2>Introduction</H2>
<P>
Lime Rock Park is a 1.53 (1.54 with the chicane) mile road course located
in Lime Rock, Connecticut, in the extreme Northwestern corner of the state.
The track is located on CT Route 112, just east of Millerton, New York and
south of Lakeville, CT.
</p>
<P>
Lime Rock Park opened in 1957, and has hosted a remarkable variety of races
over the years, including most of the various IMSA series, most of the various
<a href="/Organizations/SCCA/">SCCA</A> series, and NASCAR
Busch Grand National (North). The facility is too small to support Indycar,
Formula One, or NASCAR Winston Cup. In recent years Lime Rock has repaved
the entire track, widened the pit lane, significantly improved the restrooms,
and paved the roadways through the paddock.
</p>
<P>
Pro races make up a very small part of the Lime Rock Schedule;
one or two SCCA National races and a large number of SCCA regionals occur
at the track during the course
of a year, as well as a large number of Club Dates put on by organizations
such as the Porsche Club of America, the BMW Car Club of America, Bonnet
&amp; Boot, and so forth. Finally, the East Coast headquarters of the Skip
Barber Racing School is located nearby in Lakeville, and Lime Rock is one
of the most popular sites for Barber schools and races.
</p>
<P>
Lime Rock is not beloved by all the residents of the area. Since 1963, Lime
Rock has operated under the terms of a consent decree that limits the noise
levels and operating hours of the track. As a result, there is never any
Sunday racing at the track; unmuffled dates are restricted to a specific
number of Fridays, Saturdays, and Holidays; and operation of race engines
is limited to a specific range of hours of the day (which varies according
to the rather complex terms of the consent decree.)
</P>
<p>
Lime Rock Park has its own web page at
<a href="http://www.limerock.com/">http://www.limerock.com/</a>.
</p>

<H2>
  <A name="HistoricalNote">Historical Note</A>
</H2>
<P>
Lime Rock Park was built in the mid to late 1950s by Jim Vaill, on the site
of a cornfield/gravel pit on land owned by his father. Vaill's friend Jack
Fisher owned an MG-TC, and they often drove it rapidly in circles in the
cornfield at the top of the hill; in real life Vaill operated the gravel
pit that was on the site, in the north face of the hill (the gravel pit was
located roughly where the A Paddock restrooms and the Driving Impressions
store are now located.)
<P>
A rumor developed that a race track was being built on the site, and a number
of SCCA members showed up to investigate one day in 1955. Upon reflection,
Vaill decided that a race track sounded like a good idea to him, so he laid
out a course on an aerial photograph, selected one of his bulldozers, and
went to work. He relocated Salmon Creek to the north side of the main
straightaway; to this day, heavy rain storms give the impression that Salmon
Creek is trying reestablish itself in A Paddock.
<P>
Vaill's original plans were for both the short circuit that we know today,
and an extended 3 mile long "Mountain Circuit", which would have used the
hill east of the track. The trees were bulldozed for the mountain circuit,
but it was never built. The path of the "Mountain Circuit" may be traced
today on foot by the intrepid.
<P>

</H2>
<H3>
  From New England
</H3>
<PRE>
1) Take the best route (whatever that may be) to US 7 in
    Northwestern Connecticut.
2) Turn West onto CT 112 towards Lime Rock
3) Pass the outfield entrance to Lime Rock Park (it will be on your left)
4) After a short distance, Left towards the infield entrance to
    Lime Rock Park
5) Left at the infield entrance
6) for race registration, right onto dirt road, park near registration
    shack
7) otherwise, follow paved road to gate
</PRE>
<H3>
  From Albany, NY, and points west of Albany on the NYS Thruway (I-90)
</H3>
<P>
Two versions of this route are provided; the "not Towing" version is quicker
and more scenic but includes some twisty roads and steep hills; the Towing
route is more straightforward and provides better passing areas.

</H4>

6) In Claverack, Left at Traffic Light onto NY 23.
7) Straight, crossing Taconic Parkway
8) Right onto NY 22 South to Millerton, NY
9) Left at Traffic Light onto US 44 East
10) Bear right onto CT Route 112
11) Straight at 4 way Stop
12) Straight at 4 way Stop
13) At foot of long downhill, Right towards the Infield Entrance
14) Left at the infield entrance

     shack

<H2>
  <A name="RacersGuide">A Racers' Guide to Lime Rock</A>
</H2>
<H3>
  Registration
</H3>
<P>
Registration is located at the infield entrance. When entering the grounds
for a Club race, turn right, leave the paved road, and pull forward to the
spot where a veritable multitude of tow vehicles, trailers, and race cars
are parked. The registration shed is the small grey building to the north
of this parking area. For normal SCCA club race dates, driver &amp; crew
registration is on the south side of the shack, and race official registration
is on the north side of the shack. As always, have your SCCA membership card
and photo id ready.
<H3>
  Paddock
</H3>
<P>
After registration, put your paddock sticker in the windshied of your tow
vehicle (left side) and head north through the fence towards the gate. The
guards at the main gate will expect to see your wristband. Follow the paved
road to the junction where the paddock road leads down the hill; the guard
here will expect to see a parking sticker for A Paddock or B Paddock before
letting you down the hill. Follow the road down; B Paddock will be visible
on your left, and A paddock is around to your right. Concession stands and
restrooms are located in A Paddock.
<H3>
  Tech Inspection
</H3>
<P>
For a normal Club Race date, Tech inspection is at the Tech tent, which is
located at the west end of pit lane, between the Pit Lane and the
Concession stand.
The Tech/Impound lane is usually on the pit side of the road adjacent to
the new Tower. Because of one-way roads (which are enforced by
track security on race day), when bringing a car to Tech
(or when entering Impound after a race), enter the Tech/Impound
lane from the end adjacent to Pit Out.
<P>
Scales are usually located in the tech tent. The pad is not
guarenteed to level, so corner
weights are not particularly useful, although the total weights are generally
quite good.
<P>
<H3>
  False Grid
</H3>
<P>
The False Grid is at the west end of Pit Lane; entry is from B Paddock. Normal
grid practice is for groups of cars at the start of a race or session to
enter the track by turning left out of the false grid, directly onto the
track from Pit In (rather than passing down pit lane.) Once the track is
hot, however, individual cars must pass down pit lane and enter the track
conventionally via Pit Out.
<P>
<H3>
  Impound
</H3>
<p><i>Note that the following procedure may change
in the near future, as plans are being made to set up a new
tech building near the Men's room.</i>
</p>
<P>
Leaving the track, a Scrutineer _may_ be stationed at pit out with an Impound
sign specifying which cars are to be impounded. Do _not_ expect him or her
to know which cars are in the to finishing positions in a class; sometimes
they do know, but often (particularly for small bore Production/GT and for
complex combinations of SS &amp; IT) they have no idea who won in the slower
classes in a race group.
</p>
<P>
If you are to report to impound, turn right exiting the pits and prepare to
line up either along the right side next to the tower, or possibly turn
left into the dirt impound lot.
</p>
