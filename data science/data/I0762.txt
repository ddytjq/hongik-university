          <td colspan="2" class="newsheading1" height="30">OWEN NOT FULLY FIT</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td colspan="2" class="engfanz"> 
            <p>Michael Owen has admitted he was not fully fit for England's World 
              Cup quarter-final against Brazil. Owen was receiving treatment on 
              his groin injury for 11 hours a day but the problem had still not 
              cleared up by Friday's Shizuoka clash.</p>
            <p>He said: &quot;I didn't train all week, except for the day before 
              the Brazil game. I had treatment every day for 11 hours a day. When 
              it first happened it didn't feel like a bad injury. But then the 
              next day it just went tight. There was nothing torn. It was just 
              a niggle but I could feel it as I went out to play against Brazil.</p>
            <p>&quot;I could run but I couldn't go flat out. But it was a game 
              I didn't want to miss. It's not every day you are able to face Brazil 
              in the quarter-finals of the World Cup.&quot;</p>
          </td>

          <td colspan="2" class="newsheading1" height="30">IT'S OVER</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td colspan="2" class="engfanz"> 
            <p>England are out of the World Cup after losing 2-1 to Brazil. England 
              had a fantastic start to the game going in front through Michael 
              Owen but Brazil fought back just before half-time Rivaldo pulling 
              the score level. Brazil went in front on 50 minutes with Ronaldinho's 
              cross catching out David Seaman going over the Arsenal's keepers 
              head into the net. Ronaldinho was then sent off for a foul on Mills 
              but England looked jaded and could not get back into the game. A 
              disappointing exit because England did not do themselves justice, 
              maybe it was the heat or as Sven Goran Eriksson said his players 
              were tired England did not do enough to test the Brazilians up front.</p>
            <p><a href="reports/v_brazil_world_cup_02.html">Match stats, teams, 
              reports here &raquo;&raquo;&raquo;&raquo;</a></p>

              Ferdinand, Ashley Cole (Sheringham 79), Beckham, Scholes, Butt, 
              Sinclair (Dyer 56), Owen (Vassell 79), Heskey</p>
            <p>Subs Not Used: Bridge, Brown, Joe Cole, Fowler, Hargreaves, James, 
              Keown, Martyn, Southgate<br>
            </p>

            <p>Subs Not Used: Belleti, Ceni, Denilson, Dida, Juninho Paulista, 
              Junior, Kaka, Luizao, Polga, Ricardinho, Vampeta.</p>

          <td colspan="2" class="newsheading1" height="30">MATCH COMMENTS</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td colspan="2" class="engfanz"> 
            <p>Sven oran Eriksson tried hard to hide his disappointment after 
              he saw England go out of the World Cup with a poor second half performance. 
              Sven said: &quot;I had hoped we should have done better with 11 
              against 10. At the end we knocked in balls instead of being patient. 
              Maybe we were a little tired. We were chasing, chasing and it ended 
              up that I do not think we had one shot on goal. </p>
            <p>&quot;We had some good efforts but they were always blocked. When 
              Brazil had the ball they were good at keeping it. We did well in 
              the first half to half-time,&quot; he added. &quot;They scored in 
              the last minute and the start of the second half.</p>
            <p>&quot;We did not use our advantage very much. I congratulate Brazil. 
              I hope they can reach the final and win it. We did make a small 
              mistake at the end of the first half when we let them score. Maybe 
              the same happened at the beginning of the second half.&quot;</p>
          </td>
