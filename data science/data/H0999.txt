					<h1>Switzerland: Embryonal Research Law (ERL): Consultation Opened 22 May 2002 </h1>
					<p class="description"><strong>The ERL should fill the legal gaps that currently exist in the area of embryonic research. Regulations are particularly needed for the handling of surplus em-bryos from in vitro fertilisation (IVF), and to answer the question of whether Switzerland will in the future be allowed to use surplus embryos to obtain em-bryonal stem cells. In the production of embryonal stem cells, embryos that are approximately 4 days old are destroyed. The new law should also regulate whether Switzerland will permit the import of embryonal stem cells. According to current regulations, the domestic production of embryonal stem cells is for-bidden, because embryos have to be destroyed. It is, however, not forbidden to import embryonal stem cells, because stem cells do not fall under embryo protection. 2002-05-22
 2002-05-27</strong></p>
				</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td width="25%" valign="top" class="side">&nbsp;</td>
			
				<td width="75%" valign="top">
				
					
					
					<p>The following regulations should control research on embryos and prevent misuse:</p>
<p>1. It is forbidden to produce embryos for research purposes or to import such embry-os.</p>
<p>2. It is forbidden to import embryonal stem cells that originate from embryos specifi-cally produced for that purpose.</p>
<p>3. Under certain conditions, embryonal stem cells may be imported. Such embryonal stem cells must originate from surplus embryos.</p>
<p>4. The production of embryonal stem cells from domestic surplus embryos should be allowed.</p>
<p>5. It is forbidden to develop surplus embryos older than 14 days (= the time point of attachment of the embryo to the uterus wall).</p>
<p>6. The donation of embryos must be voluntary and may not be remunerated.</p>
<p>7. The surplus embryos and embryonal stem cells may only be used for specific, im-portant research purposes and on no account for commercial purposes.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p class="info"><strong>Further information can be found under: </strong><br />
