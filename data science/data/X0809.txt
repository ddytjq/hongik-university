    <td width="50%"><strong><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Race 1 - Tayasu Tsuyoshi
    at Kambula Hcp 2400m </small></font></strong><p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>On
    settling West End Girl led from Lead On then Rose of Siena followed by Annihilation then
    Bitski wide and starting to pull. Passing the 1600m West End Girl led Lead On and Rose of
    Siena as Bitski started a forward move on the outside. By the 1200m West End Girl led
    narrowly from Bitski then Rose of Siena and Lead On. With 800m to run Bitski raced well
    clear of West End Girl as little else changed. Turning for home Rose of Siena hit the
    front from Bitski then Lead On with Red Astaire running on wider as Hobert turned the
    widest of all. At the 200m mark Rose of Siena was in front from Bitski with a break to
    Annihilation and Lead On. Just as the race looked over Bitski switched to the outside to
    run home. By the 100m Bitski joined the front running Rose of Siena as Red Astaire and
    Annihilation fought out third from Hobart. In the final 50m Bitski proved too strong
    winning by 1 1/4 lengths from Rose of Siena with Red Astaire third. </small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Bitski is a 12yo chestnut gelding by Bit of a
    Skite from Ski Deep and this was his 19th win from 160 starts. Bitski was ridden by Cheree
    Buchiw and is trained by Barry Burgess for himself. </small></font></td>
  </tr>

    Lindsay Park Hcp 1400m </small></font></strong><p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Prince
    D'Or went to the front from Ghaazi then Saratoga Boy from Renano Brightia and Zip
    Expulsion with Work and Play next. Little changed until they turned for home where Ghaazi
    joined Prince D'Or as Saratoga Boy took a run near the rails and Work and Play ran on out
    wide. Past the 200m Saratoga Boy led Prince D'Or as Work and Play started to charge home.
    Saratoga Boy and Work and Play came away to fight it out with Work and Play proving
    stronger to win by a neck with Mount Batten running on for third. </small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Work and Play is a 3yo grey gelding by
    Kenvain from Holyport Lass and was ridden by Joe Bowditch. He is trained by Mark Kavanagh
    for G Somerfield, P Dempsey, D Millard, P Hills, C Dycer, PT Dempsey, G Harvey, R Goodwin,
    S Moon and M Wright. </small></font></td>

    Buffalo Power moved forward around the Ex Files and Gianni. With 400m left Raja Jazz was
    joined by Buffalo Power as The Ex Files tried to escape a pocket. By the 200m Raja Jazz
    and Buffalo Power led as The Ex Files switched across heels to put in a run as
    Accelerating started to run down the outside. Inside the final 50m The Ex Files cruised
    away to win untouched by 1 1/2 lengths to Buffalo Power with Accelerating third. </small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>The Ex Files is a 5yo brown mare by Danger

    Oakside Park Hcp 1000m </small></font></strong><p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Irish
    Jantee appeared to rear at the start as Sir Archer, Hand Pass and Seattle Ken disputed the
    early lead. This trio stayed in front until the 200m where Hand Pass took control from Sir
    Archer with Hightari and Irish Jantee starting to run on wider on the track. Hand Pass led
    inside the final 100m but Hightari made a determined challenge down the outside from Sir
    Archer as Comic Jewel tried to run on near the rails. In the final 25m Hightari hit the
    front to win by a neck from Hand Pass with Sir Archer just holding third from Irish
    Jantee. </small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Hightari is a 7yo bay gelding by Jevington

  <tr>
    <td width="100%" colspan="2"><strong><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Race 7 -
    Keltrice at Rangal Park Hcp 1450m </small></font></strong><p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>Paling Gagah started slowly as Trimaha appeared to stumble out of
    the gates dislodging his jockey. Los Torneros led from Mr Iceman then Marron Cheval
    outside On The Snip then North Corner. Little changed until they turned for home on the
    outer track where Los Torneros led Mr Iceman as Marron Cheval held On The Snip in a pocket
    and Obscenic started a run down the centre. With 400m to go Mr Iceman hit the front as On
    The Snip found a clear run and started to make ground with Rare Gold switching across
    heels to put in a run. By the 200m On The Snip hit the front from Rare Gold who changed
    course to run on inside On The Snip. In the final 50m On The Snip held on despite drifting
    out on the track to win by a half length from Rare Gold with Zip Infatuation third. </small></font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>On The Snip is a 4yo chestnut horse by
    Snippets from Lisset and was ridden by Travis Creek. He is trained by Dan O'Sullivan for
    Hands &amp; Heels Pristine Racing Syndicate. </small></font></td>
