<p><br>
<strong>
<font FACE="Verdana">What does the term <a NAME="Non-Conforming">Non-Conforming</a> mean?<br>
</font></strong><font FACE="Verdana" SIZE="2">   This term 
                        is used to describe people who are unable to fit 
                        the strict lending terms laid down by high street 
                        lenders. High street and direct lenders increasingly use 
                        sophisticated credit scoring techniques to determine 
                        whether consumers qualify for a mortgage. These 
                        assessment methods exclude a large number of people who 
                        for example cannot prove their income or who may have 
                        credit problems due to County Court Judgements, mortgage 
                        arrears or IVAs or who may have been declared 
                        bankrupt in the past.<br>

<p><br>
<strong>
<font FACE="Verdana">What is <a NAME="Kensington Variable Rate">Kensington Variable Rate</a> and 
                        how is it calculated? <br>
</font></strong><font FACE="Verdana" SIZE="2">Kensington Variable Rate is the 
                        rate of interest set by Kensington Mortgage Company from 
                        time to time to be our standard variable rate. 
                        Kensington Variable Rate can be varied at anytime, 
                        however it will never be more than 3% above the 3 month 
                        London Inter-bank Offer Rate (LIBOR) rounded up to the 
                        nearest 0.05% on the date on which it is set. The actual 
                        interest rate charged will be Kensington Variable Rate 
                        plus the appropriate product margin (please see 
                        individual product information sheet for precise 
                        details).<br>
