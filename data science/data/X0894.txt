<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>NEW YORK&nbsp;-- T.J. Ford is from Texas and plays his college ball for the home-state Longhorns. Chris Thomas is from Indiana and plays at Notre Dame. Aaron Miles is from Oregon and plays for Kansas.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Last year at this time, though, the three point guards were all in the same place&nbsp;-- participating in the McDonald's All-American Game at Durham's Cameron Indoor Stadium&nbsp;-- and they all played well.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And this year? Same thing. All of them had terrific freshman seasons. Coincidence? Don't think so.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Some of this year's McDonald's-caliber point men&nbsp;-- some of the less hyped kids in this game&nbsp;-- might use Thursday night at Madison Square Garden in a similar way. Those kids being Elijah Ingram and Dee Brown and Daniel Horton.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's definitely a big opportunity," said Ingram, a St. John's-bound Jersey City, N.J., product who's bound to be a local favorite at the Garden. "But I'm not too concerned with that. I'm just going to try to have fun."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Ditto for Horton.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"No matter how well I do in this game, I'm going to have a good freshman season regardless," said the 6-3 Cedar Hill, Texas, native. "They're two different things. This is just for fun."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But also for show. And to prove their college-ready mettle.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>These point guards are darn good. Most people just don't seem to know it. Or at least they don't seem to acknowledge it.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>North Carolina signee Raymond Felton gets plenty of attention&nbsp;-- the future Tar Heel from Latta, S.C., was named the Naismith High School Basketball Player of the Year&nbsp;-- but some of the other elite point guards in this year's class haven't been showered with such hype.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The scouts are here to see the big men," said Brown, a 6-foot, corn-rowed lightning bolt out of Maywood, Ill. "But there are some great guards here. These guards are sharp."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>No matter what some folks say&nbsp;-- or don't say.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I don't get all the attention all the time," Brown added. "It just happened to fall like that. Somebody has to get the ink, but I don't care. I'm not unhappy at all about all these guys getting hype. Because they're that good."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Yet so is Brown. Even if folks give him relatively little media love. But maybe that's not such a bad thing. Particularly for point guards.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Good lead guards, after all, might be unsung and under-publicized in spite of doing a great job. Or maybe&nbsp;being&nbsp;unsung and under-pubbed as a point guard&nbsp;<I>is</I> a good job. Think John Stockton or&nbsp;-- for those who are no longer fans of short shorts&nbsp;-- Steve Blake.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Quiet. Understated. Yet effective.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The point is about leadership," Ingram explained. "You've got to lead your team. It's the most important spot on the floor. The point guard runs the team."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"You do what you need to do to help your team win," Horton said. "Score, distribute, play defense&nbsp;-- you have to be a winner and a leader."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Horton, a first-team all-state pick for two years running in Texas, spent the majority of his court time out on the wing for the undersized squad at Cedar Hill. He still managed to win MVP honors in Lewes, Del., at the post-Christmas Slam Dunk to the Beach tournament.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Brown averaged 27 points, six assists and four rebounds this winter for Maywood's Proviso East High School.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And Ingram started all four of his seasons for venerable coach Bob Hurley at St. Anthony. He took last month to guide the Friars to their second straight Tournament of Champions title in the Jersey prep ranks.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's a better ball-handler than most people give him credit for, and he's a great shooter, but he's a better point guard than most people give him credit for," Long Island-based recruiting guru Mike Sullivan said. "He's very good."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he should be from the get-go next year at St. John's. Thursday night's game at the Garden could serve as a sneak preview.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Just like it was for Miles and Thomas and T.J.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>TRAVIS IS TOP TERP</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Maryland signee Travis Garrison watched his future teammates win the national championship&nbsp;-- the national freakin' championship&nbsp;-- from a downstairs suite Monday night at New York's Hotel Inter-Continental.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And then he started to think.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The expectations are going to be unbelievable," the 6-8 DeMatha-trained combo forward said during Wednesday's practice session at Basketball City on Manhattan's Lower West Side. "The coaches and fans are going to want us to go back to the Final Four."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And win it&nbsp;-- again.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Much was made earlier this week about the Maryland roster. Coach Gary Williams' squad became the first team since 1979 to win the title without so much as a single McDonald's All-American.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But here comes Garrison. He's ready to change that factoid. And how does he feel about such a lofty label some seven and a half months before his first game in College Park?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I'm going try not to pay too much attention to that," he said. "I'm just going to go in and do the best I can."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>That should be good enough.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Garrison, although he now has the McDonald's pedigree, very much fits the Maryland mold. He's versatile&nbsp;-- he can play the three or the four&nbsp;-- and he's sneaky good. If a McDonald's All-American can even be a sleeper star, well, Garrison is the one.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And don't expect him to be the last McDonald's kid Williams and his staff are able to recruit over the next couple years. Success begets success. And national championships beget big-time talent.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Even though that's not exactly the typical formula for the most recent Terp teams chock-full of overachievers.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The dynamics of the program could change," national recruiting analyst Dave Telep said. "But I'm not sure that's necessarily a bad thing. I think Gary Williams would welcome more pros coming into his program."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And then develop them in the same way he's helped to develop once-thought-to-be second-tier talents like Juan Dixon and Lonny Baxter and Drew Nicholas.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And then? <I>Really</I> Fear the Turtle.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoBodyText2 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Maybe more than any program in the country," Telep added, "Maryland develops their talent, and I'm not sure they get enough credit for that. And the players are in place to do this again."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Garrison being one of them.</FONT></P>

<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** From the "duh" department: Roanoke, Va., marksman <B>J.J. Redick</B> won the three-point contest Monday night during the Sprite Slam 'N' Jam at Christ the King in Queens. He scored 17 points in the first round&nbsp;-- a round consisting of 25 shots, with five money balls&nbsp;-- and 19 points in the second. But he wasn't the best shooter in the gym. Tennessee signee <B>Shanna Zolman</B>, the all-time leading scorer in Indiana girls hoops history, tallied 22 and 23, respectively, in the two rounds. Syracuse-bound swingman <B>Carmelo Anthony</B> topped Floridian big-timer <B>Amare Stoudemire</B> in the slam dunk competition.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Lost in Michigan's big-time fray this winter&nbsp;-- Mr. Basketball <B>Paul Davis</B> of Rochester Hills, <B>Anthony Roberson</B> of Saginaw, premier juniors <B>Olu Famutimi</B> and <B>Dion Harris</B>, etc.&nbsp;-- was 6-10 post player <B>Chris Grimm</B>. The Marquette-bound center averaged 19.8 points, 15.1 rebounds and five blocks a game for Brighton. "The thing I admire most about him is that in the locker room, he's just another guy on the team," Bulldogs coach <B>Dan Christner</B> told the Detroit Free Press. "It was real tough to get near the basket against us this year. He became a real good defender for us."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Deep South blue-chippers <B>Jackie Butler</B> and <B>Mike Williams</B> are bound to make up one of the AAU circuit's top frontcourt duos this spring and summer. How good is the pair from the Southeast Raptors? Butler, a 6-9 post from McCombs, Miss., averaged 24.3 points, 15 rebounds and five blocks per game for MHS this year as a junior. Williams, out of Camden, Ala., is a monster on the blocks who has drawn comparisons to <B>Chris Webber</B>. "I'll take Butler and Mike Williams and put them up against any big men in the country," Raptors coach <B>Mark Komara</B> told PrepStars.com's <B>Jeff Goodman</B>.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** One more Mickey-D's note: <B>Gary McKnight</B> is the coach of the West team. <B>Bob Farrell</B> is head man for the East. McKnight hails from Mater Dei High School in Santa Ana, Calif., where he's won 92 percent of his games over the last 19 years. He has four state championships and six SoCal titles to his credit. Farrell heads up the program at Seton Hall Prep in West Orange, N.J., where he's compiled a 517-134 record in 25 seasons.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** No one calls Saginaw guard Anthony Roberson by his first name. Not no one. But most everybody&nbsp;-- especially those close to him&nbsp;-- call him "Peep." Always have. Always will. Why? "I came out with my eyes closed for the first couple of minutes (after I was born)," Roberson said Wednesday afternoon. "Then my dad came and tried to get me to open them. I peeped at him. And I kept peeping at him." Hence Peep. A simple answer to a simple question.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Slam Dunk to the Beach czar <B>Bobby Jacobs</B> said earlier this week that Beaumont, Texas, big boy <B>Kendrick Perkins</B> and coach <B>Andre Boutte</B>'s Ozen High School squad is committed to play in next December's post-Christmas Lewes, Del., tournament. The 6-10 Perkins might be America's best Class of 2003 prospect this side of Ohio icon <B>LeBron James</B>.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Memphis standout and Virginia signee <B>Derrick Byars</B> led a Tennessee all-state team that also included sophomore sensation <B>Andre Allen</B> and intriguing junior <B>Marc Gasol</B>&nbsp;-- yes, the younger brother of <B>Pau Gasol</B>, the Memphis Grizzlies rookie.</FONT></P>
