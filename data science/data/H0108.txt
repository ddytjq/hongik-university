<DL>
<A NAME="A"></A>	
<dt>
<h3>Aerial hyphae/mycelium</h3></dt>
		<DD>hyphae above the agar surface</DD>

<dt><h3>Alternate</h3></dt>
	<dd>used to describe arthroconidia in a chain separated by cells which break down to release the conidia</dd>

<dt><h3>Anamorph</h3></dt>
	<dd>asexual, ”imperfect” reproductive stage</dd>

<dt><h3>Annellide</h3></dt>
<dd>a conidiogenous cell which elongates and leaves a scar ring with the production of each conidium</dd>

<dt>
    <h3>Anthropophilic</h3>
  </dt>
<dd>dermatophytes that are restricted to human hosts</dd>

<dt><h3>Antler hyphae</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae terminating in broad irregular branches resembling antlers</dd>

<dt><h3>Arthroconidium</h3></dt>
<dd><A HREF="#conidia">conidia</A> formed in chains by the breaking up of a hyphae at the point of septation.  The resulting cell may be rectangular or barrel shaped and thick or thin walled, depending on the genus.</dd>

<dt><h3>Ascocarp</h3></dt>
<dd>fruiting structure of the sexual reproductive stage with internally formed asci</dd>

<dt><h3>Ascospore</h3></dt>
<dd>sexual spore produced in a sac-like structure known as an ascus. Commonly there are 4 to 8 ascospores in an ascus.</dd>

<dt><h3>Ascus</h3>
<dd>a sac like structure that contains ascospores</dd>

<dt><h3>Aseptate</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae without cross walls. Characteristic of Zygomycetes.</dd>

<A NAME="B"></A>
<dt><h3>Biseriate</h3></dt><dd>a vesicle in Aspergillus bearing two layers of cells - <A HREF="#metulae">metulae</A> and <A HREF="#phialide">phialides</A></dd>

<dt><h3>Blastoconidium</h3></dt><dd><A HREF="#conidia">conidium</A> formed by budding along a hyphae, pseudohyphae, or single cell, as in the yeasts.</dd>

<A NAME="C"></A>
<dt><h3>Chlamydospore</h3></dt><dd>thick-walled somewhat resistant structure formed at end of hyphae (terminal) or in the middle of hyphae (intercalary). Also a vesicle formed by Candida albicans.</dd>

<dt><h3>Clamp connection</h3></dt>
<dd>small, curved hyphal outgrowth at a septum forms a looped bridge between two adjacent cells. Formed only in Basidiomycetes.</dd>

<dt><h3>Clavate</h3></dt>
<dd>club-shaped</dd>

<dt><h3>Cleistothecium</h3></dt>
<dd>round or oval sexual fruiting body without an opening and with asci irregularly dispersed in centre</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="collarette">Collarette</A></h3></dt>
<dd>flared or straight-sided cell wall remnant at the tip of a <A HREF="#phialide">phialide</A> or base of a <A HREF="#columella">columella</A></dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="columella">Columella</A></h3></dt>
<dd>dome-shaped top of a <A HREF="#sporangiophore">sporangiophore</A>, contained in a 
<A HREF="#sporangium">sporangium</A></dd>

<dt><h3>Conidiogenous</h3></dt>
<dd>structure that produces conidia</dd>

<dt><h3>Conidioma</h3></dt>
<dd>an asexual, conidium-bearing structure</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="conidiophore">Conidiophore</A>	</h3></dt>
<dd>specialized hyphal structure that serves as a stalk on which conidia are formed</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="conidia">Conidium (pl. conidia)</A></h3></dt>
<dd>asexual reproductive structure that forms externally on the side or the end of the hyphae or conidiophore</dd>



<dt><h3>Cottony texture</h3></dt>
<dd>high dense aerial mycelium</dd>

<A NAME="D"></A>
<dt><h3>Dematiaceous</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae, conidia, or spores that are brown to black</dd>
<dt><h3>Denticle</h3></dt>
<dd>small hair-like projection on which a solitary conidium is formed</dd>
<dt><h3>Dichotomous</h3></dt>
<dd>branching of hyphae into two equal branches that are each equal in diameter to the hyphae from which they originated</dd>
<dt><h3>Dimorphic</h3></dt>
<dd>having two distinct forms. Usually grow filamentously under environmental conditions and convert to special parasitic form (often yeast) in vivo.</dd>

<A NAME="E"></A>
<dt><h3>Ectothrix</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td>
<IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/ectosm.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Ectothrix"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
infection around the outside of the hair shaft
</font></td></tr></table></dd>

<dt><h3>Endospore</h3></dt>
<dd>spore produced within the spherule of Coccidioides immitis</dd>

<dt><h3>Endothrix</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td>
<IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/endosm.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Endothrix"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
infection with arthroconidia within the hair shaft
</font></td></tr></table></dd>


<dt><h3>Exudate</h3></dt>
<dd>droplets of liquid on the surface of a colony</dd><dt><h3>Favic chandeliers</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae terminating in broad irregular branches resembling antlers. Typical of Trichophyton schoenleinii.</dd>

<A NAME="F"></A>
<dt><h3>Faviform</h3></dt>
<dd>blunt, branched hyphal ends</dd>


<dt><h3>Foot cell</h3></dt>
<dd>in Aspergillus, a hyphal cell at the conidiophore base; 
<br>in Fusarium, the sharply angled end of a <A HREF="#macroconidium">macroconidium</A> where it attaches to a <A HREF="#conidiophore">conidiophore</A></dd>

<A NAME="G"></A>
<dt><h3>Geophilic</h3></dt>
<dd>usually recovered from soil, occasionally infect humans and animals</dd>
<dt><h3>Glabrous</h3></dt>
<dd>smooth; without or almost without aerial hyphae.</dd>
<dt><h3>Globose</h3></dt><dd>round</dd>
<dt><h3>Granular</h3></dt><dd>dense conidia on surface of colony; resembling sugar granules</dd>


<A NAME="H"></A>
<dt><h3>Hulle cell</h3></dt>
<dd>refractile, thick-walled cell, varying in shape, which occurs in some Aspergillus species</dd>
<dt><h3>Hyaline</h3></dt>
<dd>not pigmented, colourless.</dd><dt><h3>Hyphae</h3></dt><dd>septate or aseptate filament of a fungus; many together compose the mycelium.</dd>

<A NAME="I"></A>
<dt><h3>Intercalary</h3></dt>
<dd>forming in the middle of a hyphae</dd>



<A NAME="J"></A><A NAME="K"></A><A NAME="L"></A>

<A NAME="M"></A>
<dt><h3><A NAME="macroconidium">Macroconidium</A></h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td><IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/macrocon.jpg"  WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Macroconidia"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">larger of two types of conidia produced  by the same organism;<br>usually is multicelled</font></td></tr></table></dd>


<dt><h3><A NAME="metulae">Metulae</A></h3></dt>
<dd>branch upon which a <A HREF="#conidia">conidiogenous</A> cell is borne; on a vesicle in Aspergillus or terminal branches in Penicillium</dd>

<dt><h3>Microconidium</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td><IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/microconidia.jpg"  WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Microconidia"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
<dd>the smaller of two types of <A HREF="#conidia">conidia</A> produced by the same fungus</font></td></tr></table></dd>

<dt><h3>Moniliform</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae with swellings at regular intervals like a string of beads</dd>

<dt><h3>Muriform</h3></dt>
<dd>having both transverse and longitudinal septa</dd><dt>

<A NAME="N"></A>
<h3>Nodular bodies</h3></dt>
<dd>knot of twisted hyphae</dd>

<A NAME="O"></A>
<dt><h3>Onychomycosis</h3></dt>
<dd>infection of the nails</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="ostiole">Ostiole</A></h3></dt>
<dd>opening in wall of fruiting body through which <A HREF="#conidia">conidia</A> are released</dd>
<dt><h3>Ovoid</h3></dt>
<dd>egg-shaped</dd>


<A NAME="P"></A>
<dt><h3>Pectinate</h3></dt>
<dd>
<table><tr><td>
<IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/pectinate.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Pectinate hyphae"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">hyphal ends which have protuberances resembling a comb</font></td></tr></table>
</dd>


<dt><h3>Pedicel</h3></dt>
<dd>short filament which attaches a conidium to the hyphae</dd>

<dt><h3>Penicillus</h3></dt>
<dd>brush-like <A HREF="#conidia">conidiogenous</A> structure with 1-3 levels of branches called <A HREF="#metulae">metulae</A> and a terminal <A HREF="#phialide">phialide</A></dd>

<dt><h3>Perithecium</h3></dt>
<dd>flask-shaped or rounded fruiting body with opening (<A HREF="#ostiole">ostiole</A>) and asci arranged at base of structure</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="phialide">Phialide</A></h3></dt>
<dd>conidiogenous cell which neither elongates or enlarges; may have a cup-shaped opening or <A HREF="#collarette">collarette</A></dd>
<dt><h3>Powdery texture</h3></dt>
<dd>profusion of conidia on surface of colony; resembles flour</dd>
<dt><h3>Pseudohyphae</h3></dt>
<dd>chains of cells formed by budding that, when elongated, resemble true hyphae; they differ from true hyphae by being constricted at the septa, forming branches that begin with a septation, and having terminal cells smaller than the other cells.</dd>
<dt><h3>Pycnidium</h3></dt>
<dd>conidioma in which conidiogenous cells line the interior cavity and are released through an opening or ostiole; formed by Coelomycetes</dd>
<dt><h3>Pyriform</h3></dt>
<dd>pear-shaped or teardrop-shaped</dd>

<A NAME="Q"></A>
<A NAME="R"></A>
<dt><h3>Racquet hyphae</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae with club-shaped cells, the larger end of one cell being attached to the smaller end of an adjacent cell.</dd>
<dt><h3>Reflexive branching</h3></dt>
<dd>branches that grow forward and backward at acute angles to the hyphae</dd>
<dt><h3>Rhizoid</h3></dt>
<dd>root-like, branched hyphae</dd>
<dt><h3>Rugose topography</h3></dt>
<dd>colony in which furrows radiate out from the centre</dd>

<A NAME="S"></A>
<dt><h3>Sclerotium</h3></dt>
<dd>hyphae closely interwoven and cemented together into a hard resistant body</dd>

<dt><h3>Scotch Tape Mount</h3></dt>
<dd>The scotch tape mount is used for examining the microscopic structures of filamentous fungi. With forceps pick up a piece of clear, transparent tape and touch the surface of the colony. Place the tape onto a drop of mounting media on a slide; add another drop and a coverslip. Examine with a phase contrast microscope.</dd>

<dt><h3>Septate</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td><IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/septatehyphae.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Septate hyphae"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">having cross walls</font></td></tr></table>
</dd>



<dt><h3>Sessile</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr>
<td><IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/sestearmic.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Sessile teardrop microconidium"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">arising from side of hyphae or conidiogenous cell
</font></td></tr></table></dd>


<dt><h3>Spherule</h3></dt>
<dd>large, round structure containing endospores; characteristic of Coccidioides immitis in infected host material under direct microscope examination.  Spherules do not grow on routine artificial mycology media.</dd>

<dt><h3>Spiral hyphae</h3></dt>
<dd><table><tr><td><IMG SRC="../../image/mycology/spiral.jpg" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="100" BORDER="0" ALT="Spiral hyphae"></td>
<td><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">
hyphae forming coiled or corkscrew-like turns
</font></tr></table></dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="sporangiophore">Sporangiophore</A></h3></dt>
<dd>specialized hyphal branch or stalk bearing a sporangium</dd>

<dt><h3>Sporangiospore</h3></dt>
<dd>asexual spore produced in a sporangium</dd>

<dt><h3><A NAME="sporangium">Sporangium</A></h3></dt>
<dd>closed sac-like structure in which asexual spores (sporangiospores)are formed.  The sporangium is borne on a stalk known as the sporangiophore.</dd>

<dt><h3>Stolon</h3></dt>
<dd>horizontal, aerial (runner) hyphae which gives rise to rhizoids and sporangiophores</dd>

<dt><h3>Sympodial</h3></dt>
<dd>a succession of <A HREF="#conidia">conidia</A> formed on opposite sides of hyphal stalk so that the axis pushes past the terminal conidium to form a zig-zag structure</dd>

<A NAME="T"></A>
<dt><h3>Teleomorph</h3></dt>
<dd>sexual reproductive stage of a fungus</dd>
<dt><h3>Thallus</h3></dt><dd>fungal colony, mycelium</dd>
<dt><h3>Truncate</h3></dt>
<dd>conidium with a scar flattened off at the point of attachment</dd><dt><h3>Umbonate topography</h3></dt>
<dd>colony with centre button-shaped elevation</dd>

<A NAME="U"></A>
<dt><h3>Uniseriate</h3></dt>
<dd>in Aspergillus, a vesicle bearing a single layer of phialides</dd>

<A NAME="V"></A>
<dt><h3>Velvety texture</h3></dt>
<dd>low aerial mycelium with smooth nap, as in velvet cloth</dd>

<dt><h3>Verrucose topography</h3></dt>
<dd>colony furrowed or convoluted</dd>

<dt><h3>Verticil</h3></dt>
<dd>group of conidiogenous cells radiating out from a single point like spokes in a wheel</dd>

<dt><h3>Vesicle</h3></dt>
<dd>inflated end of a conidiophore or sporangiophore</dd>

<A NAME="W"></A>
<dt><h3>Woolly texture</h3></dt>
<dd>colony with high aerial mycelium which appears slightly matted</dd>

<A NAME="X"></A>
<A NAME="Y"></A>
<dt><h3>Yeast-like colony</h3></dt><dd>soft, pasty, smooth colony; usually without filamentous growth</dd>

<A NAME="Z"></A>
<dt><h3>Zoophilic</h3></dt>
<dd>found primarily in animals, occasionally infect humans

</DL>
