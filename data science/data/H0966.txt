      <td width="534" bgcolor="#999999" valign="top" height="718"> 
        <p class="tfbig"><span class="tfbigbold">What Does That Word Mean</span>?</p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Antibody.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Globulin produced by the body that combine with 
          antigens, bacteria, cells or toxins and neutralize them.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Antigens, Antigenic.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Proteins or other substances which, when introduced 
          into the body, stimulate the production of an antibody.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Asymptomatic.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Displaying no symptoms; if you have a disease but 
          are asymptomatic, you don't have the typical signs of the disease.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Diarrhea.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">You know - 'the runs'. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Enteric.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">In the intestine. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Enteritis.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Disease marked by inflammation of the intestines, 
          usually accompanied by diarrhea. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Gastrointestinal.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Of, relating to or involving both the stomach and 
          intestine. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Hello Kitty!</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A cute trademark from Japan. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Impish.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Like an imp; mischievous. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Hydroplaning.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Skimming over the surface of water; when water builds 
          up under a car's tires, such that the tires lose contact with the road, 
          the car is said to hydroplane. </span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Liminal.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Situated at the threshold; barely qualifying or 
          perceptible. A liminal person or character is at the threshold between 
          two societies or cultures. We don't use this word anywhere in our story, 
          but it's a fun one to know.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Salmonella.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A genus of aerobic bacteria that are all pathogenic 
          for man and other warm-blooded animals, and usually associated with 
          various types of food poisoning, with acute gastrointestinal inflammation. 
          Named &quot;Salmonella&quot; because a guy named Salmon isolated it, 
          not because you get it from salmon.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Salmonellosis.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Disease caused by Salmonella bacteria.</span></p>
        <p> <span class="tfbig">Salmonella paratyphi A.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A particular variant within the Salmonella genus 
          of bacteria. Infection with S. paratyphi A usually presents as enteric 
          or paratyphoid fever.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Serological.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Having to do with serums - you know, antibodies, 
          antigens, immunology, stuff like that.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Serotype, Serovar.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A microorganism group which has a common set of 
          antigens.</span></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">SUV.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">&quot;Sport Utility Vehicle,&quot; although Adam 
          claims it really means &quot;Socially Unacceptable Vehicle.&quot;</span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Typhoid.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Okay, so this is a little bit confusing. On the 
          one hand, &quot;typhoid&quot; can mean 'Of, relating to, or typical 
          of the disease typhus.' On the other hand, it can mean &quot;typhoid 
          fever,&quot; which is a separate disease. </span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Typhoid Fever.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A disease usually caused by infection by bacteria 
          of the Salmonella genus. See 'Typhoid.' </span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Typhoid Mary.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">Nickname for the notorious Mary Mallon, an asymptomatic 
          carrier of Salmonella typhi, who worked as a household cook and infected 
          an untold number of people in New York in the early 1900s. To call someone 
          a 'Typhoid Mary' is to accuse them of spreading disease recklessly.</span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Typhus, Typhus Fever.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">A disease caused by rickettsia, but which has some 
          symptoms in common with Typhoid Fever. See 'Typhoid.' </span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Worst-case Scenario.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">The worst that can possibly happen; a situation 
          where something has gone wrong.</span></p>
        <p></p>
        <p></p>
        <p><span class="tfbig">Yuck.</span><br>
          <span class="tfmed">An expression meaning 'gross' or 'that's disgusting.' 
          Not to be confused with &quot;yuk,&quot; which is an interjection often 
          used reduplicatively to express amusement or in imitation of a sardonic 
          laugh.</span></p>
