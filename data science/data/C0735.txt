<TD><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1"><B>Pension Planning</B></FONT></TD>
<TD ALIGN=RIGHT><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1">7/11/2000</FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD COLSPAN=2><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1">What To Do With Your Pension Fund<BR>&nbsp;</FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD COLSPAN=2><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1">      <p> Go back a little more than five years, and if you were the retiring 
        member of a personal pension plan, a retirement annuity contract or an 
        executive pension plan, there was very little you could do once you had 
        taken your tax-free cash other than buy a fixed annuity. Now, you have 
        a range of options, which include:</p>
       
      <p><b>With-Profit Annuities<br>
        </b>These annuities draw on the traditional strength of with-profit investments. 
        The underlying investments of a fixed annuity are fixed interest securities, 
        but the funds underpinning a with-profit annuity are invested in a diversified 
        portfolio of equities, bonds and property. This means the with-profit 
        annuity has the potential to provide higher returns &#151; although this 
        is not guaranteed.</p>
      <p> Most with-profit annuities allow you to select your starting level of 
        income within broad limits, based on an assumed bonus rate (commonly between 
        0-5%). At the upper end, your initial pension would be similar to the 
        income that a fixed-rate annuity would provide. The lower you choose the 
        starting level to be, the greater the scope there is for future growth 
        in your income. </p>
      <p><b>Unit-Linked Annuities</b><br>
        The idea behind a unit-linked annuity is simple: instead of a fixed cash 
        payment, your regular income is the value of a pre-determined number of 
        units in your chosen fund(s). You can choose the initial level of income, 
        balancing current payments against future scope for income growth. You 
        can even have your own self-select fund, if you wish. However, you must 
        be prepared for an income that fluctuates with movements in investment 
        values.</p>
      <p> This volatility of income does not suit everyone, but if you want the 
        greatest potential returns and can accept the risks involved, unit-linked 
        annuities are well worth examining.</p>
       
      <p><b>Income Drawdown</b><br>
        also known as Pension Fund Withdrawal (PFW). Since its introduction in 
        1995, income drawdown (PFW) has become very popular. The principle is 
        straightforward: within broad limits you make regular withdrawals from 
        your pension fund rather than buy an annuity.This can carry on until as 
        late as your 75th birthday, when, under current legislation, you must 
        buy an annuity.</p>
      <p> Income drawdown (PFW) gives you the opportunity to vary your income 
        within limits set by the Inland Revenue, perhaps to help you ease into 
        retirement. It can also offer better lump sum death benefits than any 
        form of annuity. Income drawdown (PFW) is probably only right for a minority 
        of people when they reach retirement because the income benefits could 
        turn out to be lower than the annuity would provide. Investments could 
        underperform and interest rates could fall. The income is less dependable 
        than can be achieved from a pension annuity.</p>
       
      <p><b>Rolling Annuities<br>
        </b> These are a recent innovation, which offer a blend of the certainty 
        of the fixed-rate annuity and the growth potential of investment-based 
        schemes. At the outset part of your pension fund is used to buy a five-year 
        fixed annuity and the balance is invested in your chosen fund(s). After 
        that, you can repeat the process every five years, although you have to 
        buy a whole of life annuity once you reach the point that the next five-year 
        period would take you beyond age 85.</p>
      <p> Choosing the right option needs expert advice. Even if, in the end, 
        you opt for the security of the traditional annuity, it pays to seek advice 
        to ensure you obtain a competitive annuity rate. The right decision at 
        retirement can make all the difference to your standard of living for 
        many years. <i> </i></p><BR>&nbsp;</FONT></TD></TR>
