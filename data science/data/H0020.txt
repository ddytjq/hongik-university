It has been established so that graduate students interested in Molecular Cell Biology can study under the direction of qualified faculty from a variety of departments and disciplines. In this way, graduate education occurs in the context of a focused research theme, rather than under the constraints of traditional Department lines. Research interests of the participating faculty range from the most basic aspects of protein and nucleic acid structure, physical biochemistry and biophysical analysis of membrane channels, through cellular mechanisms of nerve transmission, signal transduction, gene regulation and nuclear structure, to more medically applied aspects of virology and host-parasite interactions. Systems currently under investigation include prokaryotes, yeast, plants, Drosophila, and a variety of vertebrate models. Presently, participating faculty in the program are drawn from 7 different departments (Biochemical Pharmacology, Biochemistry, Biological Sciences, Biophysical Sciences, Microbiology, Oral Biology, and Pharmacology and Toxicology) in 4 different schools (Arts & Sciences, Dentistry, Pharmacy, and Medicine).</font>
