<a name="questions"></a><h1>Questions and Answers</h1>

	<h2>DO I NEED A FIREARMS PERMIT? </h2>
 
<p>A Tourist Firearms Import Permit is required. This is obtained upon entering Auckland International Airport from the New Zealand Police, at a cost of NZ$25.00 cash. Please have currency available so you are not delayed in making your connecting flight to Palmerston North. </p>
 
 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
 
 
<h2>What type of firearm should I bring? </h2>
 
<p>We recommend .270, 30.06, 7x57 or similar rifles, shooting minimum 140gr bullets and 60 rounds of ammunition.</p>
 
 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
 
 
<h2>What clothing and accessories should I bring?</h2>
 
<p>Fleece/woolen shirts, vest and trousers.<br>
Good, comfortable socks.<br>
Warm, rain-resistant jacket, gloves and hat.<br>
Quality walking boots with good grip soles.<br>
Binoculars, camera and daypack for carrying them.<br>
Casual clothing and footwear for the lodge and traveling.<br>
Full laundry facilities are available, so excess clothing is not required. </p>
 
 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
 
 
<h2>What about non-hunters?</h2>
 
<p>We can arrange extra activities for the non-hunters in your party where they can soak up the 
flavor of Aotearoa - the land of the long white cloud - on private sightseeing and shopping trips to the most popular tourist areas - Rotorua or Queenstown. </p>
 
 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
 
 
<h2>Do you have group and corporate rates?</h2>
 
<p>With our recent lodge extensions, our facilities are now more suited to handle the ever-increasing demand for corporate and group hunts.</p>

<p>Please be assured, with corporate or group booking, your clients or friends will have world-class hunting and receive specialized personal service.</p>
 
 
<p>&nbsp;</p>
 
 
<h2>How should I make travel arrangements?</h2>
 
<p>From the US, your Travel Agent will book your flight through Los Angeles, CA, to Auckland International Airport, New Zealand. From there, please book your flight to Palmerston North where you will be met. Currently Air New Zealand, United, and Qantas have regular daily flights to New Zealand from Los Angeles. When 
traveling from the East Coast and time allows, we recommend an overnight stay in Auckland to shake off the effects of jet lag. The lodge is a two-and-a-half hour drive north from Palmerston North airport.</p>
