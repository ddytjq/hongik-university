As a result the establishment of the <a href="MG2414.html">frame</a> of reading nucleotides by the <a href="MG243.html">intiation of translation</a> is important for deciphering the genetic code.<br>			<li>The genetic code is redundant, most amino acids being encoded by several codons. The preferred codons in a translated gene usually reflect the relative <a href="MG2415.html">abundances</a> of the corresponding tRNAs.<br>			<li>The redundancy allows some flexibility in nucleotide sequence to accomodate nucleic acid secondary structure features and base composition preferences.<br>			<li>The genetic code is practically universal. There are <a href="#Info">exceptions</a>, however.<br>			<li><a href="MG2421.html">Termination codons</a> are expected on average once every 21 nucleotides. Regions of nucleotide sequences devoid of termination codons in one frame of reading are designated &quot;<a href="MG2431.html">open reading frames</a>&quot;.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Info"></a>Further information</h4>		<ul type="square">			<li>The development of techniques for <a href="../MGW4/MG427.html">nucleotide sequencing</a> of RNA and DNA produced gene sequences that confirmed the conclusions of the above experiments.<br>			<li>Exceptions to the universal code include (<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/htbin-post/Entrez/query?uid=10366854&form=6&db=m&Dopt=b">ref</a>):			<ul>				<li>Human mitochondria: <img src="nav2.gif" width="71" height="98" align="right" usemap="#NavigatorB1D2AD80"><map name=""></map>				<ul>					<li>AUA and AUG code for met					<li>AGA and AGG code for termination					<li>UGA codes for trp				</ul>				<li>Fungal mitochondria				<ul>					<li>UGA codes for trp in <i>Saccharomyces</i>					<li>CUN codes for leu in <i>Neurospora</i><br>					and thr in <i>Saccharomyces</i>				</ul>				<li><i>Platyhelminth and Echinoderm mitochondria: AAA codes for asn</i>				<li>Spiroplasma and mycoplasma mollicutes:				<ul>					<li>UGA codes for trp					<li>CGG codes of termination				</ul>				<li><i>Candida</i> and <i>Saccharomyces</i>: CUG codes for leu				<li><i>Micrococcus</i>: AGA codes for termination			</ul>			<li>The translation of poly(UG) into poly(cys.val) is frame independent. Similarly, Karposi's Sarcoma herpesvirus has a gene, K12, that is translated independently of reading frame. It consists primarily of a mulitply <a href="../MGW1/MG112284.html">tandemly</a> repeated 23 bp sequence. The sequence on one strand is devoid of A residues. <img src="i2411.gif" align="right"><map name=""></map>It thus lacks TAA, TAG and TGA stop codons in all three frames. Initiation at any CUG (inefficient relative to AUG) results in translation of a polypeptide of 23 amino acid residue repeats regardless of the frame of initiation!  <a href="MG2414.html">Frameshifting</a> occurs at the end of each 23 nucleotide repeat as a consequence of the sequence.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a name="bottom"></a><a href="MG24.html">Last</a> | <a href="MG24.html">Overview</a> | <a href="#Facts">Top</a> | <a href="MG2411.html">Next</a><br>			<hr size="2">			This is page 241 of <a href="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</a> by <a href="http://opbs.okstate.edu/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</a>, &copy; 1997-2000<br>			<img height="66" width="102" src="divlogo2.gif" align="right"><br>			<hr size="2">			</p>		</center>		<address>E-mail inquiries to <a href="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu">U.
