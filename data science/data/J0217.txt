<h1>Gordon feasts on rain-shortened Food City 500</h1>

BRISTOL, Tenn. -- Jeff Gordon was awarded his third victory of the season Sunday when
officials cancelled the Food City 500 after 343 laps due to
constant rains at Bristol International Raceway. 
<p>
It may not be the way he dreamed of winning, but Gordon, the
defending event and Winston Cup champion, will take a win even
if it comes at the hands of Mother Nature. 
<p>
&quot;It's not the way I wanted to win. I wanted to see the checkered
flag,&quot; said Gordon, a winner for the second week in a row. &quot;It's
pretty amazing, it's just not the same.&quot; 
<p>
Gordon took the lead from
Rusty Wallace on lap 295 and was still out front when NASCAR
officials finally gave up their attempts to finish the 500-lap race.
<p>
The race ended with Gordon's Hendrick Racing teammate Terry
Labonte in second, followed by Mark Martin, Dale Earnhardt,
Rusty Wallace and current points leader Dale Jarrett. Those were
the only cars on the lead lap at the .533-mile facility. 
<p>
Gordon has had a very strong run, overtaking Rusty Wallace on
lap 293 to reclaim the lead he held for several laps earlier in
the event. 
<p>
After starting the season with a pair of next-to-last-place
finishes, Gordon has come on strong with three wins and a third.
His performance has lifted him into sixth place, 133 points
behind Jarrett. 
<p>
The start of the race was delayed by rain for approximately 45 minutes, and once the race started the rarely-seen red flag emerged three times over the host site of the second short-track event of the 1996 season. The race had been stopped twice due
to rain and once when Darrell Waltrip wrecked one lap after a
restart on lap 325 and spilled highly flammable fuel oil on the
track. 
<p>
During that restart, Dale Earnhardt, Mark Martin and Terry
Labonte slipped past second place Rusty Wallace, dropping him
into fifth.  Winston Cup points leader Dale Jarrett was able to
outrace Gordon to the start-finish line, and rejoined the
leaders on the lead lap when the red flag came out again. 
<p>
Waltrip, Elton Sawyer and Mike Skinner drove the only cars out
of the race. 
<p>
Earnhardt gained 10 points and trails by 37 in the standings.
<p>
Sunday's race covered 182.286 miles and is the shortest Winston
Cup event ever run at Bristol. The previous record for the shortest
race was in the fall of 1983 when Darrell Waltrip won a
rain-shortened event after 419 laps, 223.327 miles.
<p>
The Food City 500, celebrating the 35th year that NASCAR is
running at Bristol, was the sixth of 31 races on the Winston Cup
circuit. The next event is the First Union 400 on April 14 in
North Carolina.
<p>
