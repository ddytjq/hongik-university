  <hr color="#0000FF">
  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin: 0" align="left"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">Grouped
  with potentially difficult opponents - Yugoslavia, Northern Ireland and Turkey
  - England dropped only one point and notched up a series of good
  results.&nbsp; The best of which was an 8-0 hammering of the team who should
  be playing in Asia, Turkey.&nbsp; The team was also in fine form when they
  travelled to Belgrade a month later for a win or bust match.&nbsp; Adams,
  Beardsley, Barnes and Robson all scored in the first half and we won the game
  4-1.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></p>
  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin: 0" align="left"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">The
  finals in Germany were a disaster as far as England were concerned, and we
  ended up losing all three games.&nbsp; By virtue of their impressive
  qualification record, England were made one of two seeds, along side the
  Germans, and were grouped with Ireland, Holland and the USSR.&nbsp; First up
  was the Irish and Houghton put them ahead in the first five minutes and then
  Jack Charlton's side set about hanging on to the lead, it worked and there
  were no more goals in the game.&nbsp; The Dutch also started badly losing 1-0
  to the Russians which set up an important clash with England, the losers of
  this game would be out.&nbsp; Van Basten scored the first of his hat-trick
  before Robson equalised after 53 minutes.&nbsp; England looked in control but
  a leaky defence cost them the game and they lost 3-1.&nbsp;&nbsp; By the time
  we played eventual runners, USSR, England could not qualify and again they
  lost 3-1, Tony Adams scored our goal.</font></p>
