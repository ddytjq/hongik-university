        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Third Straight 
          Win Earns McLean Bob Kelly Memorial</b><br>
          <br>
          A third straight Tyee Chev Olds Stock Car main event victory Saturday 
          by Parksville's <b>Brad McLean</b> allowed him to nudge out Port Alberni's 
          <b>Don Hallgarth</b> by one point to win the Bob Kelly Memorial Trophy 
          on what turned out to be a one groove race track. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Mike Finneron 
          sponsored night of racing also featured the IMCA Modifieds and the Motorcycles, 
          with the Modifieds joining the Stocks to compete for their own Bob Kelly 
          Memorial Trophy. The trophy goes to the driver with the most points 
          earnrd during the night's racing. Mr Kelly was a big part of the Nanaimo 
          auto racing scene before passing away, and since Cassidy Speedway got 
          shut down, Saratoga Speedway has provided a place for the trophy to 
          be competed for. </font><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nanaimo's 
          <b>Jim Joyce</b>, a many-time winner at Cassidy when Mr Kelly was part 
          of racing, won the Crash to Pass Memorial joining McLean and IMCA driver 
          <b>Jason Beaulieu</b> as other 2002 winners. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">For the 3rd straight 
          week McLean used his front row starting position to gain an early lead 
          that he wouldn't give up in winning his 4th main event of the season. 
          Due to an earlier transmisson oil spill around the entire track, the 
          50 lap race became a one groove, follow-the-leader affair. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">On the warm up lap 
          for the stock car heat <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> unknowingly had a transmisson 
          line problem, which spewed fluild all over the track. Even with a great 
          cleanup job by the track crew, the Black Creek oval remained slick all 
          night, leaving the outside groove virtually unuseable. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 9 car main event 
          saw McLean pressured every lap by 16 year old <b>James Dawson</b>, who's 
          #11 car was faster than McLean's, but couldn't try the outside for fear 
          he'd fall back 3 or 4 positions. Dawson had to follow McLean hard every 
          lap, waiting for the slip that didn't come, and settled for a hard earned 
          2nd place finish ahead of Hallgarth and <b>Morley Axelson</b>.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Point leader <b>David 
          Mainse</b> increased his lead over an absent <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> 
          with a 5th place main event finish, despite struggling throughout the 
          race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">For the 2nd straight 
          week McLean won every race he competed in by matching his main event 
          win with wins in the B Dash and heat race, while Nanaimo's Morley Axelson 
          contiued his competitive ways with a win in the A dash. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Oyster River's Jason 
          Beaulieu won his 3rd straight IMCA Modified main event and in doing 
          so collected enough points to win his 2nd straight Bob Kelly Memorial 
          Trophy.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 7 car, 40 lap 
          main event was a very cautious race with only one groove to race on, 
          and it saw very few passes, most being made by Beaulieu who started 
          6th, then picked his spots to move up and grab the lead on the 27th 
          lap from <b>Robbie Haslam</b> on his way to the win ahead of <b>Tim 
          Openshaw</b> and <b>Lawerence O'Connor</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Beaulieu kicked 
          off the night with a fast lap of 15.96, which was followed by trophy 
          dash wins for Haslam in the B, and O'Connor the A dash and the 1st heat. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The second heat 
          was cancelled after only one lap when a three car crash halted action 
          for nearly one hour after Nanaimo's <b>Don Banaskey</b> was shook up 
          in the crash. With the track still slick from the earlier spill, Victoria's 
          <b>Jim Thompson</b> charged into turn one and went sideways, forcing 
          Banaskey to spin to avoid him, which in turn had Haslam going sideways 
          and right into Banaskey's dead car of with a hard thud. The Oyster Fire 
          Department was on site and Banaskey was attended to professionally and 
          quickly as they made sure there were no injuries. After having the roof 
          cut off to remove him, Banaskey climbed out on his own and went to the 
          hospital to be checked over. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This Saturday night 
          the Bomber Cars return from a 3 week break and they will be joined by 
          the fast ground pounding WILROC Sprint cars who visit the speedway for 
          the 2nd time this season.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With 32 Bombers 
          having run so far this season on different nights, it's expected a huge 
          field of cars will be out as the competition within the class grows 
          - only 173 points seperate 1st from 7th. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Racing this and 
          every Saturday night have gates open at 5.30 followed by time trails 
          at 6 and racing at 7pm.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Tickets are still 
          avaiable for the two day Canada Day Weekend featuring a car stunt show,two 
          days of Crash to Pass,Fiqure 8,Island Mini Stocks ,Road Hogs and Dwarf 
          cars highlighted by a huge trademark Speedway Canada Day Fireworks display. 
          Tickets can be purchased form the Tidemark and Sid Williams Theatre's, 
          French Creek Seafood in Parksville, Twin City Auto Parts in Port Alberni 
          and the Smile Centre in Nanaimo. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Guest talk.....New
