 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Chelsea FC</H1><H2 class="entry">Stamford Bridge</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Visiting fans are allocated the North End (nearest the Matthew Harding
end) of the Lower tier of the East Stand. The East Stand is three tier covered
all-seater stand which was built in the mid '70's and is the oldest stand
at Chelsea.
</P><P>The stand serves both food and beer and most clubs get allocated 3000
tickets.
</P><P>Chelsea has it's own TV Channel which is broadcast inside the ground on
matchdays.
</P><P>Away fans are normally permitted to take drums and flags into the ground.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=SW61HS&Title=Stamford+Bridge+(Chelsea+FC)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Ticket Info</H3><P><B>Fans are reminded that tickets for visiting stands should be purchased through their own club, not the club they are visiting.</B></P><p>Chelsea have 2 categories for matches, A &amp; B. 
Category A games are : Arsenal, Leeds, Manchester United, Liverpool, Spurs, West Ham and Fulham. All other games are category B.
</P></p><p>Tickets prices for visiting fans are £28 for category B games and £35 for category A games for 2001/2002 season.  
</P></p><p>Ticket information can be obtained by ringing 09068 121011 (60p per minute) or by clicking 
<a class="entry" href="http:www.chelseafc.co.uk/chelsea/fr_info.htm">here</a>
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>The club has 50+ Spaces for disabled visitors, however only 6 of them are
in the East Stand. For more info on the facilities phone the club on 020
8878 9713 or email simon.watson@virgin.net
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>All the pubs around the ground are home only. This includes those in the
Chelsea Village. As the tube station is only a short walk down the road it
is best to find a pub in another part of London and get the tube to the
ground. 
</P><P>Beer is available in the ground.
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>Chelsea Village has four restaurants, however we believe none
welcome away fans in colours.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>The is a Lloyds TSB with a cash machine near Fulham Broadway tube station.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is SW6 1HS</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Chelsea is approached off the M25. Exit the M25 at junction 15 onto
the M4 and follow this as it runs into the A4. Staying on the A4 go over
the Hammersmith Flyover and travel a further 1.5 miles before turning
right down Warwick Road (A3220) past Earls Court. Follow this down
into Finborough Road and then at the junction with Fulham road turn
right. The ground is on the right.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is an underground car park at the Chelsea Village and limited street
parking. Many visitors park at tube stations around outer London and catch
the tube in.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>Fulham Broadway tube station, served by the District Line, is just a
couple of hundred yards walk from the ground (down Fulham Road).
</P></p><p>The nearest tube is Fulham Broadway
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://www.londontransport.co.uk/tube/mau_home.shtml" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of The Underground</P><p>The nearest station is Stamford Bridge South
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>The Chelsea Village has two hotels (both 4 star) with rooms starting from
&pound;140 (single) and &pound;160 (double) which doesn't even include
breakfast. The Village has its own website at
<a class="entry" href="http://www.chelseavillage.co.uk">
http://www.chelseavillage.co.uk</a>. 
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/london-west.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>The Chelsea Shed was once home to some of the most notorious fans in the
world and whilst Chelsea still has a hooligan element Stamford Bridge is
now a safe place to visit, with friendly fans. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>Contrary to previous reports old favourites such as "One man went to mow" and "Celery" are still 
heard in The Matthew Harding stand, another old song that survives is
</P><P><pre>
Carefree, wherever we may be
We are the famous CFC
And we don't give a f***
Whoever you may be
'Cos we are the famous CFC
</pre>
</P><P>And of course their own blue version of The Red Flag.
</P><P>Some credit the demise of Jason Lee's career partly to Chelsea fans, who
were reputedly the first to sing 'He got a pineapple on his head' to the
one time Forest star. The then Forest manager later blamed Lee's downfall
to 'The whole pineapple campaign'. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>London has huge range of nightlife. Famous nightclubs from The Ministry of
Sound, Home and Heaven to Stringfellows, The Hippodrome and The
Rock Garden. Comedy venues including The Comedy Store and Jongleurs and
live much venues like The Royal Albert Hall, Camden Palace and The Brixton
Academy.
</P><P>London plays host to some of the worlds most famous theatre productions
(and theatres), shows include Cats, Chicago, Les Miserables, The Lion
King, Mamma Mia, Mousetrap, Phantom of the Opera, Starlight Express, Art,
Buddy and The Rocky Horror Show
</P><P>As you would expect London also has a huge choice of restaurants.
</P><P>For a good place to start planning your night out in London try <a class="entry" href="http://www.londonnet.co.uk/ln/guide/index.html">here</a>.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>The choice of things to do in London is endless with the best shops in the
country, many historic buildings and landmarks, lots of museums and
plenty of other tourist attractions to keep everyone happy.
</P><P><I>Shopping</I>
</P><P>There are several shopping areas in London which cater to all tastes. The
main shopping area is down Oxford Street/Regent Street/Bond Street
which has all the usual high street chains and also some large
department stores, one of which is Selfridges.  The famous Carnaby Street
is just off this area towards Soho. 
</P><P>The other famous department store in London, Harrods, is in the
Knightsbridge area. There are also several other designer shops in this
area.
</P><P>The more adventurous may fancy a trip out to Camden Market to look for a
bargain or something different at London's most famous market. The other
market you may wish to visit is Covent Garden which is associated with the
Arts and antiques. Covent Garden also has lots of boutiques, street cafes
and street entertainers.
</P><P><I>Historic Landmarks</I>
</P><P>London has many famous historic landmarks. These include Big Ben &amp;
the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace, Tower of London, Tower
Bridge, Westminster Abbey, St Pauls Cathedral, The Cutty Sark, The Globe
Theatre and Marble Arch. Many of these are open to the public.
</P><P><I>Modern Landmarks</I>
</P><P>Modern London Landmarks include The London Eye (Millennium Wheel), The
Millennium Dome, Canary Wharf and The Telecom Tower.
</P><P><I>Museums and Other Attractions</I>
</P><P>Other attractions include the Waxworks at Madame Tussards and the London
Dungeons. Museums include The Tate Gallery, The Natural History Museum,
The Imperial War Museum and The Victoria &amp; Albert Museum. 
</P><P>There are several parks including Hyde Park and Regents Park. There is a
zoo in Regents Park.
</P><P>There is also the giant arcade Sega World at Piccadilly Circus.
</P><P>For further details of what to do in London click <a class="entry" href="http://www.londonnet.co.uk/ln/guide/index.html">here</a>.
</P><P></P></p><P>You can also use the following to search for events in the area</p><BR><CENTER>
