    <b><font face="Arial, Sans-Serif" size="4" color="#000080" class="ab4">
 Change at the head of the <br>FIFA general secretariat </font></b>
<p>


<img width="105" height="80" border="0" hspace="8" vspace="2" src="http://a1801.g.akamai.net/f/1801/2004/3d/www.fifa.com/images2/fifalogo-b.gif" align="left">
In line with the agreement reached with the FIFA Executive Committee on 31 May 2002, 4 July 2002 marked the end of General Secretary Michel Zen-Ruffinens employment with FIFA. The Executive Committee and Mr Zen-Ruffinen agreed during the meeting in Seoul that he would be responsible for the running of the 2002 FIFA World Cup, but working relations would end by mutual consent after the tournament.
<p>
The administration of world footballs governing body will until further notice be headed by <a href="http://www.fifa2.com/scripts/runisa.dll?M2:gp::67173+mrel/Display+41043+E">Dr. Urs Linsi</a> (Switzerland), who was appointed Acting General Secretary by the FIFA Executive Committee at its meeting in Tokyo on 28 June. Urs Linsi has been Director of the FIFA Finance & Services Division since 1 July 1999 and was promoted to the post of Deputy General Secretary on 1 January 2000.

    <b>2002 FIFA World Cup</b><br>
<b><font face="Arial, Sans-Serif" size="4" color="#000080" class="ab4">
 Korea Republic win <br>Most Entertaining Team Award </font></b><br>
<p>
by <a href="http://www.fifaworldcup.com/en">FIFAworldcup.com</a>
