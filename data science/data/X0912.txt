<H1>Achievements</H1>

<P>
During our 14-year-year history, IMBA has achieved many of our strategic goals and
objectives. Our accomplishments fall in five categories:
</P>

<H3>
<A HREF="#trail_networks">Trail Advocate Networks</A><BR>
<A HREF="#Trailwork">Trailwork</A><BR>
<A HREF="#PublicLands">Public Lands</A><BR>
<A HREF="#Conservation">Conservation</A><BR>
<A HREF="#Communication">Communication</A>
</h3>



<H2><A NAME="trail_networks"></A>Trail Advocate Networks</H2>

<UL>
<P><LI>
  IMBA has built an <B>international network of advocacy clubs</B>. IMBA affiliated groups
are an increasingly effective educational and political force. In the past seven
years, our affiliated club count has grown from less than <B>100 to more than 450</B>.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA clubs and members collectively <B>opened more than a thousand miles of trails</B>
to mountain biking last year. These same people donated more than <B>200,000
volunteer hours</B> to improve public lands.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA is creating a well coordinated and well supported <B>network of <A HREF="../contacts/near_you/state_reps.html">state
representatives</A></B>. IMBA supplies representatives with business cards, pays some
expenses, conducts training, and connects reps through an email listserve and
regular communication with IMBA staff.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA brings mountain bikers, land managers and other types of trail users
together in <B>national and international conferences</B> dedicated to mountain bike
advocacy. Conferences include:
</P>

<P>
<B>International</B><BR>
September 1995 - Germany<BR>
September 1997 - Global Advocacy Summit - Switzerland<BR>
September 1999 - Switzerland<BR>
April 2000 - International Cycling Union's (UCI) 100th anniversary celebration - Paris<BR>
Summer 2000 - Canadian National Trails Symposium - Canada<BR>
March 200- International Mountain Bike Patrol training conference - Italy<BR>
May 2001 -  British Columbia Mountain Bike Access Summit - Canada<BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>United States</B><BR>
January 1996 - National Advocacy Summit - Arizona <BR>
September 1998 -  Pacific Region Summit - Washington<BR>
October 1998 - State Reps Summit - Tennessee <BR>
May 1999 - Second International Trails and Greenways Conference - Pennsylvania<BR>
February 2000 - State Reps Summit -  Texas<BR>
October 2000 - National Mountain Bike Patrol Instructor Training Conference - California<BR>
September 2001 - Kona/IMBA Pacific Northwest Mountain Bike Access Summit - Washington<BR>
March 2002 - Midwest Mountain Bike Summit - Missouri<BR>
April 2002 - IMBA Mountain Bike Advocacy Summit - Utah<BR>
 </P>

<P><LI>
Since 1993, IMBA has recognized and promoted outstanding mountain bike advocacy
projects through our <B>Model Program Awards</B>.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA has built solid <B>partnerships with the bicycling industry</B>. We have boosted
industry support to more than <B><A HREF="../contacts/sponsors.html">100 corporate sponsors</A></B>.
</P>

<P><LI>
Our <A HREF="../nmbp/index.html">National Mountain Bike Patrol</A> now has more
than <B>60 volunteer patrols around world</B>. Their emphasis is on assistance, not
enforcement.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA actively participates in two critical national initiatives: - <B>Bikes Belong</B>,
which helped to gain improved bicycling policies in the national transportation
bill passed in 1998 <B>(TEA-21)</B> and now works to implement that act, and - Americans
for our Heritage and Recreation, working to revive the <B>Land and Water
Conservation Fund</B> to provide almost <B>a billion dollars a year</B> for acquisition of
lands for conservation and recreation.
</P>

<P><LI>
More than 100 IMBA-affiliated groups participate in <B>National Trails Day projects</B>
each year.
</P>

<P><LI>
More than 100 IMBA-affiliated groups annually participate in <B>trailwork projects
with other trail user groups</B>, including hikers, equestrians and motorized users.
</P>

</UL>

<H2><A NAME="Trailwork"></A>Trailwork</H2>

<UL>
<P><LI>
  IMBA clubs each year help to <B>maintain thousands of miles of trail</B>.
</P>

<P><LI>
Our <B>Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crews</B> help activists and land managers throughout the
U.S., Mexico, Canada and Europe.
</P>

<P><LI>
This year, we will conduct more than a <B>dozen IMBA Trailbuilding Schools</B>
coast-to-coast and beyond.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA provides <B>financial support</B> for trail construction, maintenance and advocacy,
in partnership with corporate supporters.
</P>

<P><LI>
Thanks to a <B>partnership with RockShox</B>, we have donated more than <B>1,000 trailwork
tools</B> to our affiliated clubs.
</P>

</UL>

<H2><A NAME="PublicLands"></A>Public Lands</H2>

<UL>
<P><LI>
  IMBA has worked with the <B>US Bureau of Land Management</B> to get hundreds of miles of
routes established, signed, and designated open to bicycling. IMBA signed a
<B>partnership agreement with BLM</B> in conjunction with the agency's 50th Anniversary
in 1998. A revised partnership agreement is being developed this year.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA and the <B>USDA Forest Service</B> signed a national <B>Memorandum of Understanding</B> in
1994 to <B>support and promote trails open to bicycling on national forests</B>. Most
non-Wilderness National Forest trails are open to bicycling. This MOU is being
revised and updated this year.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA is partnering with the <B>National Park Service</B> to <B>open singletracks and jeep
roads in National Parks, Monuments and other Park Service units</B>. In 1998, the
Park Service began work on a new shared-use singletrack on the <B>North Rim of Grand
Canyon National Park</B>. We are working with the NPS to accomodate mountain biking
on more NPS dirt roads and trails.
</P>

</UL>

<H2><A NAME="Conservation"></A>Conservation</H2>

<UL>
<P><LI>
IMBA convinced the <B>Sierra Club to change its guidelines</B> regarding bicycling on
trails and separate its mountain bike policy from its motorized off-road vehicles
policy. <B>The Club now accepts mountain biking as a legitimate use of public lands</B>.
IMBA pledged to help the Club to protect public lands through various actions.
</P>

<P><LI>
In May, 1998, in Park City, Utah, IMBA convened a forum of conservationists,
bicyclists, motorized recreationists, and agency and political figures to discuss
Utah Wilderness. IMBA's board then <B>developed a Utah wilderness policy</B>.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA has been an active partner of <B>Americans for our Heritage and Recreation</B>,
pushing for full <B>funding of the Land and Water Conservation Fund</B> that benefits
open space aquisition and recreation.
</P>

<P><LI>
IMBA <B>supports President Clinton's Roadless Initiative</B>, which calls for a ban on
road construction in pristine sections of National Forests. IMBA's support for
this measure is <B>contingent on continuing access for mountain bikers</B> to
non-Wildnerness Forest Service trails.
</P>


</UL>

<H2><A NAME="Communication"></A>Communication</H2>

<ul>

<P>
<LI>IMBA publishes <B><A HREF="../news/trail_news/index.html">IMBA Trail News</A></B> five times per year. This insightful
newsletter covers a broad spectrum of mountain bike access, trailwork, and
conservation issues.
</P>

<P>
<LI>IMBA has <B>improved the image of mountain bicycling</B> in three ways:
</P>

<P>
<OL><LI> We have <B>popularized the <A HREF="trail_rules.html">IMBA Rules of Trail</A></B> on an international level.
Reproduced millions of times, the IMBA Rules of the Trail are now recognized
around the world as the standard code of conduct for mountain bikers.
</P>

<P>
<LI> We have placed a <B><A HREF="../resources/logos/long_live_ads.html">series of advertisements</A> in national and international
magazines</B> to highlight the importance of responsible riding, volunteer trailwork,
and cooperation with other trail user groups.
</P>

<P>
<LI> We produce and distribute unique educational materials, such as our "<B>Managing
Mountain Bikes" compendium</B>, for land managers and activists. We <B>empower land
managers</B> to find ways to support mountain biking, rather than choosing, when
problems appear, the "easy" route of closure.
</P>
</ol>

<P>
<LI>IMBA has repeatedly <B>enlisted top mountain bike racers</B> as role models to promote
responsible riding, trailwork participation and political activism.
</P>

<P>
<LI>IMBA's web site increasingly <B>serves the mountain biking community</B>, offering
timely and valuable information to help activists, guide and assist land
managers, and improve communication networks in the trails community.
</P>
