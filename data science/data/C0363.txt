                                  <td background width="38" height="100%"> 
                                    <font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> 
                                    <b> </b> </font> </td>
                                  <td background width="398" height="100%">
                                    <h1 style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l14 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .25in left 297.7pt; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" color="#000080" size="3">Friday
                                    6 July 2001</font></h1>
                                    <h1 style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l14 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .25in left 297.7pt; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="4"><b><font color="#000080">Introduction</font></b><o:p>
                                    </font><font face="Arial" size="2">
                                    </o:p>
                                    </font></h1>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">A
                                    meeting took place in London on 5 July 2001,
                                    between the Provisional Liquidators,
                                    representatives of the following brokers and
                                    BIBA. The purpose was to discuss the main
                                    issues raised by brokers which are yet to be
                                    resolved following the appointment of PwC on
                                    17 June. The brokers present were:</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">AON</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Marsh</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Willis</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Jardine
                                    Lloyd Thompson</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Heath
                                    Lambert</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">HSBC</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Those present
                                    agreed that notes of the meeting should be
                                    given the widest possible circulation in
                                    order to assist the broking community and
                                    its clients.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">The following notes represent the
                                    discussions between the Brokers named, Mike
                                    Williams of BIBA and Dan Schwarzmann and
                                    Jeremy Webb of PwC.&nbsp; Note that PL means
                                    the Provisional Liquidators. Further
                                    information will be released as soon as it
                                    becomes available.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2"><b><font color="#000080">Cancellation
                                    of mid-term and long-term policies </font><o:p>
                                    </b>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">PwC were to despatch letters with
                                    cancellation notices / forms on the 6th
                                    July.&nbsp; This has been rescheduled to the
                                    9th July.&nbsp;The effect on
                                    policyholders will be:-
                                    </font></p>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2"><b><font color="#000080">Private
                                        Policyholders <o:p>
                                        </font>
                                        </b>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </font>
                                    <ul>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Where
                                        a policy has a cancellation condition,
                                        the letter will invoke the same.&nbsp;
                                        Where no cancellation condition exists,
                                        the policyholder will be invited to
                                        cancel.&nbsp;&nbsp; This will be
                                        activated by the policyholder signing
                                        and returning the form provided to by
                                        the PL to the Independent.<br>
                                        </font></li>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Processing
                                        will be at Cheadle and notification can
                                        be direct from the client or via the
                                        broker.<br>
                                        </font></li>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Where
                                        there is a cancellation provision and it
                                        is a protected policy (under the PPA),
                                        the refund may be guaranteed by the PPB
                                        for 90% of the pro-rata premium due.&nbsp;
                                        In most cases, there will be no return
                                        if there has been a claim.<br>
                                        </font></li>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Where
                                        there is no cancellation provision, the
                                        PPB will not guarantee the return and it
                                        becomes an unsecured debt.&nbsp; In most
                                        cases, there will be no return if there
                                        has been a claim.<br>
                                        </font></li>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Although
                                        not asked for on the form, PwC felt it
                                        would be helpful if brokers calculated
                                        the pro-rata premium / return premium
                                        due (based on a strict number of days of
                                        cover up to cancellation / 365) and
                                        included this on the form.<br>
                                        </font></li>
                                      <li>
                                        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">Where
                                        premiums have not yet been paid the PL
                                        will only seek the pro-rata time on risk
                                        premium.<o:p>
                                        </o:p>
                                        </font></li>
                                    </ul>
                                    <p style="text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l14 level1 lfo1; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in" align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2"><b><font color="#000080">Corporate policyholders</font><o:p>
                                    </b>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Where
                                    cancellation provisions exist the PwC letter&nbsp;<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    will invoke the same.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l3 level1 lfo3; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">B.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Again where there are no cancellation
                                    provisions the<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; policyholder will be invited
                                    to cancel.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l3 level1 lfo3; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Return premiums will be handled under the
                                    scheme<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; of arrangement.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoBodyText" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l3 level1 lfo3; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">D.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Currently the PPB’s position is that they
                                    will not<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; protect return premiums on
                                    compulsory covers.&nbsp;<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; Their stance is
                                    that they are responsible only for<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; paying
                                    claims / defence costs.&nbsp;</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l3 level1 lfo3; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">E.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    The subject arose as to why a client should<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; voluntarily cancel a compulsory given that
                                    the r.p is<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; not protected.&nbsp; It was felt
                                    best advice still was that<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; clients should
                                    replace cover for these reasons:</font></p>
                                    <p style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l7 level1 lfo5; tab-stops: list .5in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .0"><font face="Arial" size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp; PPB interprets cover strictly in accordance
                                    with<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    the compulsory insurance legislation.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp; Delay<br>
                                    <br>
                                    F.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Although not asked for on the form, PwC felt
                                    it would<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    be helpful if brokers calculated
                                    the pro-rata<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    premium / return premium due
                                    (based on a strict<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    number of days of cover
                                    up to cancellation / 365)<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    and included this
                                    on the form.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l3 level1 lfo3; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">G.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Where premiums have not yet been paid, the<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; pro-rata time on risk premium only will be
                                    due,<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; irrespective of claims in the period up
                                    to<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; cancellation.&nbsp; The premium will be
                                    due for the period<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; from inception / renewal
                                    up to date of cancellation.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial"><b><font color="#000080" size="2" face="Arial">Other issues in
                                    respect of cancellation:</font></b></font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The PL
                                    agreed that return premiums could be made<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    directly to the Client.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">B.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    In this regard, it was noted that if brokers
                                    processed<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; r.p.'s through their normal
                                    systems, they might<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; become liable to their
                                    clients for the full r.p that was<br>
                                    &nbsp; almost
                                    certain to be paid at less than 100% under<br>
                                    &nbsp; the SOA.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in 71.8pt; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">C.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Discussions are ongoing with Premium Finance
                                    and<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; other similar bodies but these are
                                    confidential.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in 71.8pt; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">D.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Position on Premium Payment Warranties will
                                    be<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp; considered on a case by case basis.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">E.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Where commission is involved premiums must
                                    be<br>
                                    &nbsp; paid to the Independent gross.&nbsp; The
                                    PL will pursue<br>
                                    &nbsp; the brokerage on net
                                    payments.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2">F.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brokers should also consider splitting our
                                    premiums<br>
                                    &nbsp; on combined policies between
                                    compulsory and<br>
                                    &nbsp; non-compulsory classes in
                                    case they are treated<br>
                                    &nbsp; differently in the SOA
                                    or by the PPB.</font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; text-indent: -.25in; mso-list: l13 level1 lfo4; tab-stops: list .25in; line-height: 100%; margin-left: .25in"><font face="Arial" size="2"><font color="#000066"><b>Premium
                                    set-off</b></font><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">A.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Under a PL
                                    and subsequent&nbsp; Scheme of<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    Arrangement,
                                    the following&nbsp; set-off provisions may<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    apply:<o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </font></p>
                                    <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: justify; line-height: 100%"><font face="Arial" size="2">&nbsp;<o:p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp; Premiums for the period up until
                                    cancellation may<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    be offset against known and
                                    agreed claims.<o:p>
                                    &nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp; This would operate on a policyholder basis,
                                    so<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    premiums and claims under different
                                    policies and<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    periods could be set-off.<o:p>
                                    &nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Set-off is only possible in respect of the
                                    period<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    from inception / renewal to the date
                                    of the PL.<o:p>
                                    &nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    -&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; In order to encourage payment of premiums
                                    due<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    the PL is willing to provide indemnity
                                    letters and<br>
                                    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
                                    PwC agreed to circulate a
                                    specimen.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
