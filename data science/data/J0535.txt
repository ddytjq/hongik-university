                                    <hr><font  color='000000' ><b>Championship leader edges out Ukawa and Biaggi in thrilling but shortened race at Le Mans</b></font>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="67" height="36"></td>

                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi3d14.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >V</font><font  color='0000cc' >alentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > kept his head in difficult conditions at Le Mans this afternoon, taking his third win of the year in a race shortened by rainfall. The Italian had been involved in a thrilling tussle with team-mate </font><font  color='0000cc' >Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > and </font><font  color='0000cc' >Max Biaggi (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > when drizzle began to fall mid-race, forcing the trio to slow their pace. Both Biaggi and Ukawa led until Rossi regained the lead just before the rain got heavy enough to bring out the red flags, cancelling what might have been an enthralling final few laps.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >	"Safety always has to be the number-one priority in racing, so it was absolutely the correct decision to stop the race"</font><font  color='000000' >, said </font><font  color='0000cc' >Michelin Grand Prix manager Emmanuel Fournier</font><font  color='000000' >. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"But the rain was a shame, because I think there would've been a great battle between these three riders during the last laps."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>

                                          <img align="left" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi1d8.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='000000' >	The race ran seven laps less than the scheduled 28-lap distance, Rossi credited with victory, just 0.217 seconds ahead of Ukawa, Biaggi a further four tenths down.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >"Not finishing the race is always a little strange but it's still a win and 25 important points for the championship"</font><font  color='000000' >, said Rossi who has scored four pole positions and three wins from the first four MotoGP races. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"It was strange because some laps there was rain, some laps sun, and some laps there was more rain than others. That made it very difficult, more difficult than a normal race. Before all that I had pushed very hard but I couldn't make the break, so I decided to ease my pace and conserve my tyres. Then the rain came. Later on I attacked again and I was lucky to be ahead when the race was stopped."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>

                                          <font  color='000000' >	Ukawa, who beat Rossi in a final-lap manoeuvre in last month's South African GP, shadowed his team-mate in the dry early stages, then found himself ahead after Rossi had eased off in the drizzle, Biaggi having taken the lead for just one lap.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >" I wanted more laps but there was a little too much rain for them to allow the race to continue"</font><font  color='000000' >, said Ukawa. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"My machine and my tyres were working very well, I was having a good race, and in these circumstances, second is okay."</font>

                                          <font  color='000000' >	Biaggi was delighted with his third place after a difficult start to his 2002 campaign. New parts for the YZR-M1's computer-controlled engine-braking system transformed the bike here, allowing Biaggi to qualify third, just behind team-mate </font><font  color='0000cc' >Carlos Checa (Marlboro Yamaha Team YZR-M1-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >. In the race Checa was right with the Italian when he crashed after hitting gravel brought on to the track by another machine. <br></font><font  color='0000cc' >"The bike is very rideable now"</font><font  color='000000' >, said Biaggi who completed the first all four-stroke podium since Suzuka. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"My thanks to Yamaha and the team for their work and now we must keep working to further improve the bike."</font>

Both he and </font><font  color='0000cc' >Daijiro Kato (Fortuna Honda Gresini NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > had been running right behind the three front runners during the drizzle, Kato trying a little too hard in the conditions and crashing out at the first chicane. He was unhurt.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >	"Finally I got a good start and could fight with the top group",</font><font  color='000000' > said Abe, the first two-stroke finisher. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"But the difference in acceleration between the two-strokes and the four-strokes is big."</font><font  color='000000' ></font>
