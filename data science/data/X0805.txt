				<td rowspan="2" valign="top" width="196">
				<img src="/images/news/1080.jpg" width="196" height="146"><!--  --><br><img src="/1.gif" width="1" height="5"><br>

				<font class="newsdate"> Thursday, March 22, 2001 11:48 (GMT)</font><br>
				<img src="/1.gif" width="1" height="18"><br>
				<font class="news">So the &#8220;final frontier&#8221; for the Aussies will have to wait for a few more years. Who could have anticipated this after a complete drubbing the visitors inflicted on the Indians in the first Test in Mumbai? However, the reality is that the hosts have had one of the most memorable triumphs in Test history and the Aussies will have to come to terms with it. <br><br>It is not that the Australians played bad cricket to end up on the losing side. They did as much as they could, if not more, but it was a never-seen-before fightback by a handful of gritty Indians that took the series away from them. &#8220; It was a tremendously competitive series,&#8221; said the Australian captain Steve Waugh, &#8220;but the great comebacks by the Indians beat us eventually.&#8221;  &#8220;I am satisfied that my team put in all the effort, that&#8217;s the best they could have done,&#8221; he added.  <br><br>However, the final day was not a cakewalk for the hosts as the match stretched to the wire and the Indians got home only with only two wickets in hand. &#8220;We would have liked to have a few more runs on the board. The match could have gone either way in the final session, but the Indians stuck to it to prevent us from winning,&#8221; Waugh said. &#8220;Looking back, I think it was the three bad collapses in a single session, one in Kolkata and two here, that cost us two matches and the series,&#8221; a disheartened Australian captain remarked. <br><br>On the other hand, Steve Waugh&#8217;s Indian counterpart Sourav Ganguly singled out the match-saving 366-run fifth-wicket partnership between VVS Laxman and Rahul Dravid in Kolkata Test as the turning point of the series. &#8220;After a terrible first Test in Mumbai and first two days in Kolkata, Laxman and Dravid&#8217;s partnership on the third and the fourth day there was the turning point for us in the series,&#8221; said Ganguly. He also lauded Harbhajan Singh for his heroic effort that won the Kolkata and Mumbai Tests. &#8220;Harbhajan bowled like a dream for India. Whenever I asked him to bowl he never failed me and came up successfully,&#8221; Ganguly said.  <br><br>The Punjab off-spinner, who was instrumental in flushing the Australian batsmen throughout, was awarded as the man of the series. He clinched 32 scalps in the series while taking five or more wickets four times and also became the first Indian ever to claim a Test hat-trick. And it seemed like the poetic justice towards the end, as it was Harbhajan who hit the winning runs for India. <br><br>&#8220;I thank my captain, coach and my team-mates for supporting me immensely throughout the series, as without that it would not have been possible for me to do so well,&#8221; said the 20-year-old Sikh. &#8220;I had worked very hard before the series and I was confident of doing well, but I had not imagined doing so well, and that too against one of the best sides in the world,&#8221; he added jubilantly.</font><br>
