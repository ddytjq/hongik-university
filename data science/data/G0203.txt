<p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>This is a brief 
  listing of some books and articles about astronomy and space science education 
  at various levels from graduate school to grade school and at various types 
  of institutions. It is by no means comprehensive, but merely designed to help 
  those approaching the field become acquainted with the rich literature that 
  already exists. (Suggestions are most welcome.) </i></font></p>

<p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>1. 
  <a name="1">General Reports on Astronomy Education</a> </b></font></p>
<p> </p>
<blockquote> 
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Berendzen, R., 
    ed. <b>Education in and History of Modern Astronomy</b>. 1972, Annals of the 
    New York Academy of Sciences, Vol. 198. <br>
    One of the first attempts to systematize and collect information on astronomy 
    education. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Fraknoi, A., 
    et al., eds. <a href="../astro/astropubs/universe.html">The Universe at Your 
    Fingertips: An Astronomy Activity and Resource Notebook.</a> 1995, Astronomical 
    Society of the Pacific. <br>
    An 813-page compilation of hands-on activities, resource lists, and teaching 
    ideas; designed for grades 4-12, but usable at many levels and settings. A 
    product of the Society's Project ASTRO. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Hunt, J., ed. 
    <b>COSMOS: An Educational Challenge: Proceedings of a 1986 GIREP Conference</b>. 
    1986, European Space Agency Publications SP-253. <br>
    Brings together a wide range of mostly European contributions on astronomy 
    education at all levels. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Pasachoff, J. 
    & Percy, J., eds. <b>The Teaching of Astronomy</b>. 1990, Cambridge U. Press. 
    <br>
    Proceedings of an international colloquium on astronomy education in the US 
    and elsewhere. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Pennypacker, 
    C., ed. <b>Hands-on Astronomy for Education</b>. 1992, World Publishing. <br>
    Proceedings of a 1990 conference emphasizing computers and small telescopes, 
    especially in high school settings. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Percy, J., ed. 
    <b>Astronomy Education: Current Developments, Future Coordination</b>. 1996, 
    Astronomical Society of the Pacific Conference Series, Vol. 89. <br>
    The most up-to-date and comprehensive introduction to current issues and projects 
    in astronomy education; with good reviews and resources. </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Sletteback, 
    A. & Jettner, F. <b>"Proceedings of the Conference on Education in Astronomy, 
    August 1969"</b> in Bulletin of the American Astronomical Society, vol. 2, 
    p. 254 (1970). <br>
    Interesting papers showing the concerns and projects of the 1960's, some of 
    which are still very much with us today. </font></p>
</blockquote>

<p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>2. 
  <a name="2">Review Articles</a> </b></font></p>
<p> </p>
<blockquote> 
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Bishop, J. <b>"Astronomy 
    Education in the U.S.: Out from Under a Black Cloud"</b> in The Griffith Observer, 
    Mar. 1980, p. 2. <br>
    Good historical overview of the rise and fall of astronomy in the school curriculum 
    and some educational projects in the 1970's. <i>(See also her "U.S. Astronomy 
    Education: Past, Present and Future" in Science Education, vol. 61, no. 3, 
    p. 295; 1977.) </i></font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Fraknoi, A. 
    <b>"The State of Astronomy Education in the U.S."</b> in Percy, J., ed. Astronomy 
    Education: Current Developments, Future Coordination. 1996, Astronomical Society 
    of the Pacific Conference Series, Vol. 89. <br>
    Sixteen-page overview of astronomy education in many settings. (Accompanied, 
    later in the same book, by a "Catalog of National Astronomy Education Projects".) 
    </font></p>
  <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Sadler, P. <b>"Astronomy's 
    Conceptual Hierarchy"</b> in Percy, J., ed. Astronomy Education: Current Developments, 
    Future Coordination. 1996, Astronomical Society of the Pacific Conference 
    Series, Vol. 89. <br>
    How students learn (and don't learn) astronomical ideas. </font></p>
</blockquote>
