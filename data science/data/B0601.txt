The West Brom was therefore delighted to fly the flag again for Walsall’s stars of the future with a donation of &#163;250 towards the cost of the flight. <br><br>The Walsall youngsters have earned themselves an unbeatable reputation this season after sweeping all before them in the major regional and national competitions for their age group.  It was therefore only natural that they felt able to pit themselves against the best of the continent in the shape of European giants, Real Madrid and Barcelona, as well as the Soccer School of Torrevieja.<br><br>A venture of this magnitude requires funding, which is why the West Brom’s contribution was much appreciated by Bill Jones, Youth Development Officer at Walsall.  As Bill says: “We have forged an excellent relationship with the West Brom and their support for this trip is another example of how they value our attempt to provide an exciting range of experiences for young people.  Indeed, the trip was not simply about football, but also enabled our young players to learn about being together as a team and to mix with young people from other countries and cultures.”  <br><br>Liese Moeller, Assistant Manager at the West Brom’s branch in Walsall applauds the club’s efforts: “We have always been impressed by Walsall’s commitment to young people.  That echoes the West Brom’s own involvement in the community, where we place great emphasis on furthering opportunities for children and young people.
