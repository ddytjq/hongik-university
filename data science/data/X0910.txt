				<p class="indent20"><font class="normaltour"><script language="javascript">writeToursMenu (5)</script>Every one of the 30,000 fans at the Barkatullah Stadium in Jodhpur chewed their nails and so did the millions of fans watching all over the world. What a finish it was to the third one-dayer. Sachin Tendulkar&#8217;s blazing century went in vain as India lost a winning battle by one wicket. Thus it gave Zimbabwe their first ODI win on Indian soil. The visitors thus kept the series alive after India had won the first two one-dayers.<br><br>Riding on a fine 146 from Tendulkar and a late onslaught by tail-ender Zaheer Khan, India amassed 283 for eight in their allotted 50 overs. However, it was unable to restrict the visitors who scraped through in the last over.<br><br>Zimbabwe made an ominous start losing their first three wickets for just 52 runs. Opener Alistair Campbell was in belligerent mood. He was severe against Venkatesh Prasad carting him for three boundaries. But a rising delivery from left-arm seamer Zaheer Khan took him by surprise and he was forced to edge it to wicket-keeper Vijay Dahiya who had a good day behind the wicket.<br><br>Prasad dismissed Stuart Carlisle and Guy Whittal to leave Zimbabwe in dire straits. However, the Flower brothers &#8211; Andy and Grant took the battle to the fore. Andy who is been in a terrific form scoring over 500 runs in the Test series, played in the same vein. The duo picked up singles and twos while hitting an occasional boundary.<br><br>The Flower brothers frustrated the Indian bowlers and the visitors were cruising along when before Prasad broke the 158-run fourth wicket partnership. India suddenly came back into the game when Grant (70), Gavin Rennie (0) and Andy Flower (77) followed in quick succession.<br><br>But it was skipper Heath Streak and tail-ender Mluleki Nkala who did the star turn for Zimbabwe. Nkala especially showed exemplary grit in adding 44 runs for the seventh wicket.<br><br>Earlier, master blaster Sachin Tendulkar blasted his way to his 27th ODI century as India set a stiff target of 284 runs for Zimbabwe.<br><br>The little master&#8217;s form rubbed on Zaheer Khan&#8217;s shoulders as he smote four consecutive sixes (with a wide ball in between) in the last over bowled by Henry Olonga to remain unbeaten on 32 off just 11 balls to catapult the Indian total to 283 for eight in the allotted 50 overs. Ajit Agarkar (13) was the other batsman at crease.<br><br>Each one of the 30,000 odd-crowd at the Barkatullah Stadium in Jodhpur were treated to an array of strokes from Tendulkar as India raced through during the first half of the innings and limped through the middle overs before recovering to put up what could possibly be a match-winning total.<br><br>Tendulkar who had completed a half-century of centuries in the Nagpur Test with a double hundred, hit another classy ton today. He was in blazing form right from the word `go&#8217;. Cutting and pulling he scored most of his runs in boundaries and sixes.<br><br>The visitors played four speedsters on a wicket that had nothing to offer for the pacemen. And with Tendulkar in roaring form, there was no stopping for the 26-year-old world class batsman. Playing on the hard surface was a difficult task but Tendulkar made it look so easy.<br><br>India suffered a set back as it lost skipper Sourav Ganguly in the fourth over of the innings when he was bowled by Bryan Strang. Setting aside this fact, Tendulkar played as only he can. The other man-in-form Rahul Dravid was only happy to play the second fiddle. He was a mere spectator as the former captain launched an assault on the medium pacers. <br><br>Racing to his half-century in just 37 balls (7x4, 2x6), the Mumbai batsman pulverised the Zimbabwean attack. Skipper Heath Streak rotated his bowlers but in vain. India reached the 100-mark in the 15th over. Streak was clearly desperate to break the partnership which was going strong and Grant Flower came to his rescue. Trying to sweep Flower, Dravid top edged for Gavin Rennie to take a superb catch at short fine leg. Tendulkar&#8217;s domination was so much that Dravid&#8217;s contribution in the 114-run second wicket partnership was only 30.<br><br>The visitors came back into the game picking up three quick wickets. India lost both the promising youngsters &#8211; Yuvraj Singh and Hemang Badani in a space of three balls. Yuvraj gave a return catch to Flower while Badani was run out just when Tendulkar was on the verge of his ton. Another new comer Sodhi spooned to Grant Flower at covers. <br><br>However with Tendulkar still around, Zimbabwe&#8217;s problems were not yet over. In the company of Joshi, he completed his century and also helped India cross the 200 mark (40th over) in style smashing two consecutive boundaries off Nkala. Joshi swung his bat to score 25 off just 24 balls and added 57 runs for the sixth wicket before he was stumped out.<br><br>Tendulkar was finally out in the 47th over. Trying to work out Streak down to fine leg, he scooped to Nkala. His lazing innings of 146 came off just 153 balls of which 15 were sent to the ropes and two over them.<br><br>Left-arm spinner Grant Flower was the most successful of the Zimbabwean bowlers picking up three wickets conceding just 43 runs.</font></p>
