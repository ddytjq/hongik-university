<p> The Save Our Seats campaign will set up “donation points” around St James’s Park before and after tonight’s Uefa Cup tie against FC Zurich as part of the bid to raise £30,000 to fund the legal action against the club’s “corporate cleansing” plans.&nbsp;</p>
<p> The campaign is appealing to all fans to back their action, pointing out that although only bondholders have grounds to go to court, victory will benefit every supporter by halting the highly unpopular plans. SOS campaigner Jane Duffy, one of the bondholders bringing the legal case, said: “£30,000 sounds like a lot of money, but if every person attending the game gave us just £1, we could fully fund the action. And it would be a good investment for affected bondholders – indeed, every bondholder – to give £10; if the club get away with their plans, your bond is worthless anyway.”&nbsp;</p>
<p> SOS are also asking bondholders to sign up to attach their names to the legal action. This involves no risk to the individuals concerned, but strengthens the application and reinforces its status as a representative action.</p>
<p>After consultation with the City Council and the police, the SOS stalls, which will be equipped with leaflets and stickers as well as receptacles to receive donations, will be established at the following points from about 6.30pm tonight: Barrack Road, at the entrance to Leazes Park Near the junction of Barrack Road and Strawberry Place Beside the Strawberry Pub ENDS For further details, contact SOS press officer Kevin Miles on 0191 262 6694,
