            <p align="center"><b><a name="Top"></a>CHAUCER HOLDINGS PLC</b><br>
            </p>
            <p align="center"><b>LLOYD'S CAPACITY AUCTIONS </b>&nbsp;<br>
            </p>
            <p align="center"><a href="../main2000.htm" target="_self">Back</a><br>
            </p>
            <p align="left"><br>
              <b>20 October 2000</b></p>
            <p>Following completion of the Lloyd&#146;s capacity auctions, the 
              Directors of Chaucer are pleased to announce that the Group and 
              its associated company have made aggregate purchases of &pound;34.2 
              million of Chaucer syndicate capacity for the 2000 year. </p>
            <p>&pound;32.1 million of capacity has been acquired on behalf of 
              ALIT (No 1) Limited at a cost of &pound;0.8 million and &pound;2.1 
              million on behalf of Chaucer Dedicated Limited (in which Chaucer 
              has an effective 33.3 % economic interest) at a cost of &pound;0.1 
              million. <br>
              <br>
              Prior to the auctions, ALIT (No 1) held &pound;44.4 million of Chaucer 
              capacity for the 2000 year and Chaucer Dedicated Limited held &pound;53.4 
              million. The total holding of Chaucer syndicate capacity by Chaucer 
              and its associates for the 2000 year will therefore be &pound;132.0 
              million (51.7 %). <br>
              <br>
              Prospective holdings of capacity on individual Chaucer syndicates 
              for 2000 are as follows:<br>

            <p class="font">In addition, Chaucer has sold or given notice on its 
              entire &pound;15.6 million holding of capacity on syndicates outside 
              the Chaucer Group. The proceeds of capacity sales were approximately 
              &pound;2.4 million. <br>
              <br>
              All the above figures are subject to adjustment and the December 
              capacity auction for deceased Names. <br>
              <br>
              Malcolm Mackenzie, Chief Executive, stated: <br>
              <br>
              &quot;The acquisition of a further &pound;34.2 million of Chaucer 
              capacity and sale of the balance of the old spread portfolio is 
              a further positive step towards the creation of a Chaucer Integrated 
              Lloyd&#146;s Vehicle and will help simplify the structure and operations 
              of the Group. We are particularly delighted to have moved to a position 
              where we hold over 50% of our own capacity and to have done that 
              at sensible prices.&#148;</p>
