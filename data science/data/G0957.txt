<H2> 
 The scaling relations of early-type galaxies
			       and the tilt of the Fundamental Plane
</H2>
 <p>

 Observatoire de Lyon, F-69651 Saint Genis Laval, France
 <p>

<img src = "/images/rainb_line.gif" alt=" ">
<br>
Abstract
<p>
We have analysed the residuals from the fundamental plane of early-type 
galaxies
as a function of (i) the stellar population parametrized through 
broad-band colours
(U to I) and Mg2 line-strength index, (ii) the rotational support and (iii)
the spatial structure (flattening and shape of the light profile). We find 
significant correlations with the first two parameters and only a marginal
correlation with the shape of the photometric profile.
<p>
The stellar population effect and the contribution of the rotational support
account for 60% and 25%, respectively, of the total tilt of the 
fundamental plane
(with respect to the plane expected from the virial theorem, universal
mass-to-light ratio and homologic spatial/kinematical structures). 
The effect of the spatial structure could also slightly contribute to the tilt.
<p>
As a consequence, the stellar-to-dynamical mass ratio appears constant for
all early-type galaxies within the region probed by this equilibrium relation,
i.e. within about one effective radius.
