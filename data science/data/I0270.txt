  <hr color="#0000FF">
  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin: 0" align="left"><img border="0" src="1994.gif" width="100" height="159" align="left" hspace="20" vspace="20"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>A
  late equaliser from Norway meant that Graham Taylor's World Cup campaign got
  off to a disappointing start, but three straight wins put us in a good
  position before the Holland home game.&nbsp; Two goals in the first twenty
  minutes (Platt &amp; Barnes) and England were flying, but a penalty five
  minutes from time completed the Dutch comeback and the game finished
  2-2.&nbsp; A draw in Poland was followed by the disastrous 2-0 reverse in
  Norway.&nbsp; The return game at home to Poland saw a comfortable 3-0 win
  (Pearce, Gascoigne &amp; Ferdinand) which set up a classic win or bust match
  in Rotterdam against Holland.&nbsp; An even first half ended 0-0 but shortly
  into the second half David Platt was chasing a long through ball when he was the
  victim of a professional foul by Ronald Keoman.&nbsp; The referee awarded a
  penalty at first but changed his mind and gave a free kick outside the
  area!!!!&nbsp; Amazingly, Keoman only got a yellow card and to add insult to
  injury, he himself scored the opening goal from a free kick.&nbsp; The free
  kick had to be taken twice though, the referee not being happy with the first
  attempt for reasons which nobody but himself will ever know.&nbsp; With only
  one game to go, we had to beat San Marino by a cricket score and hope that
  Poland beat the Dutch.&nbsp; We did indeed win 7-1 (after being behind after
  seven seconds) but Holland also won so bye bye USA 94 and bye bye Graham
  Taylor.&nbsp; For the first time since 1938, no British team qualified.</b></font>
