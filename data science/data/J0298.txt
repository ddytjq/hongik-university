<h1>Wallace gas gamble pays off with Miller 400 win</h1>

BROOKLYN, Mich. -- Rusty Wallace finally had a gamble pay off and his luck hold up
as he earned his first superspeedway victory since 1994 Sunday by winning the
NASCAR Miller 400 at Michigan International Speedway.<p> 

Wallace was running fourth when the other race leaders decided
to pit as the race entered its final 10 laps around the two-mile
oval.  Wallace has had mileage gambles hurt him in the past, but
he and crew chief Robin Pemberton bit their lips and won in the
end despite going the final 52 laps -- 104 miles -- without stopping for gas.<p> 

&quot;We were getting killer gas mileage,&quot; said Wallace, who drives a
Ford, which have tended to get inferior mileage to their
Chevrolet competitors. &quot;The car was really good today.&quot;<p> 

	   &quot;We knew it was going to be borderline,&quot; Pemberton said of the fuel situation. &quot;If we stopped (for fuel), there was no
chance (to win).&quot;<p>

	   Sterling Marlin, who had the dominant car in the race, had built
a lead of more than eight seconds -- nearly one-quarter of a lap --
before he stopped for gas on lap 188. That gave the lead to Jeff Gordon, the defending NASCAR Winston
Cup champion. But Gordon stopped on lap 191 to refuel, leaving a crowd of 130,000 wondering if Wallace would win on fumes.<p>
	   &quot;Usually we lose them on fuel mileage,&quot; Wallace said after
going the distance on just three pit stops. &quot;But at Darlington (in
March) we got fourth by conserving fuel, and Robin just kept
reminding me about that. We definitely won this one on fuel
mileage.&quot;<p>

Terry Labonte, who along with crew chief Gary DeHart rolled
the same dice concerning fuel, stayed on the track late and had his gamble pay off by
bringing his Chevrolet home in second place.  When the
Winston Cup points standings are released Monday, Labonte will
trail points leader Dale Earnhardt, who finished ninth Sunday, by 15
points.<p> 

Marlin, Jimmy Spencer and Ernie Irvan rounded out the
top five as the field set an unofficial track record by averaging
166.03 mph with only two caution flags -- for nine laps -- on the
day.  The previous record was set by Davey Allison in 1991 at
160.912 mph.<p> 

&quot;It's a heartbreaker to lose,&quot; Marlin said. &quot;I knew we had to
pit because I ran out (of gas) on that last green-flag stop, so
there was no way I was going to make it all the way.&quot;<p>

Gordon staged a jaw-dropping charge over the course of the
race. He was dropped from the lead to last place -- 32nd -- on the lead
lap when it was discovered he was missing a
lugnut after a caution-period pit stop.  Gordon moved through
the field until he was in second place with 13 laps to go.<p> 

But Gordon was among the leaders who had to pit with about 10
laps to go. Still, he finished an impressive sixth.  Had Gordon
won, it would have been his sixth victory of the year and
his third in a row.<p> 

Wallace's victory was his third of the year and 44th
of his career.  Wallace, widely regarded as the finest
short-track driver currently on the Winston Cup circuit,
recorded wins in 1994 at the larger tracks on the circuit.<p> 

Clearly the dominant car on the day was the Chevrolet of Marlin,
who led for more than 50 laps and claimed an extra five points
for leading the most laps. Marlin had built a lead of more than 10
seconds at one point.<p> 

Spencer, having his best finish of the year in fourth, said he
backed off his accelerator to make sure he finished the race
rather than fight Marlin for third on the last lap.  &quot;The guys
have been building great race cars,&quot; Spencer said.<p> 

Irvan returned to the track that nearly took his life 22 months
ago and performed remarkably well, quickly moving through the
field and joining the frontrunners.  &quot;If I'd remembered the
wreck, it would be tough to come back here and race,&quot; he said.<p>

Ford placed six cars in the top 10; Chevy collected the
other four top spots.  Bobby Hamilton, who started on the pole,
was the top finishing Pontiac in 15th position.<p> 

The Miller 400 was 14th of 31 events scheduled on the Winston Cup circuit. The next event is the Pepsi 400 on July
6 at Daytona (Fla.) International Speedway.<p>
