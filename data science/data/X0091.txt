    <td width="200%" colspan="2"><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>The first Magic
    Million sale in Adelaide was held from February 21 to 23, 2000 with the first two days
    featuring the Select Sale and the final day the Summer Session. For the Select sale the
    sale total was up from previous years with the sale aggregate totalling $9,975,500 with an
    average price of $35,002 and the top price of $230,000 for lot 95. The total lots sold was
    285 with 19 withdrawn and 46 passed in. For Wednesday's Summer session The sale aggregate
    totalled $1,503,750 with an average price of $13,308. The top price was $52,000 for lot
    390. Total lots sold was 1113 with 19 withdrawn and 39 passed in. </small></font><p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>All yearlings sold are eligible for the Magic Millions
    race series including a new $400,000 2yo race scheduled to run on Tokyo City Cup day
    February 2001. </small></font></p>

    Swift Avenger (by Francis Bacon) </small></font></strong></p>
    <p><font face="Arial" color="#004000"><small>This brown filly is a half sister to two
    winners in Pray for Wings (by Amen Brother) and Ganbattee (by Euclase) and is an August
    foal. Her sire will have his first Australian runners next spring but has already sired
    stakewinners such as Silky Feather, It's Personal, Prospector's Flag, etc in the US. She
    was offered by AJ Crabb &amp; Sons Pty Ltd of Smithfield SA and was sold to R Griffiths of
    Victoria for $70,000. </small></font></p>

    is well grown and is a half brother to two winners in Yeatman (Our Poetic Prince) and the
    promising Torlina (by Western Symphony). His sire is well known as the sire of
    stakewinners such as Ballroom Babe, Love to Dance, Wynciti, Citi Success, etc and was
    Champion NZ sire of 2yos in 1994-95 &amp; 1995-96. He is related to the stakewinners Sunny
    Coronation, Far Hills, Miss Sunnytime and Kildaire Pride plus prolific winners Master
    Kildaire and Sir Kildare through his grand dam Sun Festival. He was offered by Toorak
    Thoroughbreds of Macclesfield SA and was purchased by Paul Willetts of NZ for $26,000. </small></font></p>

    sister to stakewinners Exculsive Halo (by Don't Say Halo) and Miss Jugah (by Jugah) and
    winners in Jewel of Oakbank (by Alzao), Exculsive Honour (by Bellwater), Trackman (by

    sire of stakewinners Mahogany, Lady Jakeo, Magic of Money, Tracey's Element, O'Reilly,

    the stakeswinner Elegant Shell (who is the grand dam of this bay colt) and My Evita who
    went on to produce Group 1 winner and successful sire Vitalic. He was sold to Ron Dufficy
    of NSW for $20,000. </small></font></p>
