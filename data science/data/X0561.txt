<H1>On the Trail This Week</H1>

<H2>Week of June July 10-21, 2002</H2>

<a name="l1"></a>
<H3>San Francisco Bay Area, California</H3>

<H3>IMBA Hot Spots Program Focuses on San Francisco Bay Area </H3>

<P>
IMBA clubs and the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew are teaming to promote 12 days of focused advocacy effort in the Bay Area July 10-21, 2002. This coordinated work is part of IMBA's Hot Spots program that works to improve urban trail access and inspire new mountain bike advocates. 
</P>

<P>
IMBA invites the public to attend one or more of the following events during the San Francisco Bay Area IMBA Hot Spot effort. The events are a great opportunity to learn about trailbuilding, trail access and other issues that impact mountain biking in the Bay Area. It's also a chance to meet IMBA staff, the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew and get involved with your local club.
</P>

<H3>Schedule Of Events</H3>

<P>
<B>Friday, July 12 - Joaquin Miller Park, Oakland</B> <BR>
Event: IMBA Trailbuilding School - Part 1, Classroom session. <BR>
Time: 1 p.m. - 5 p.m.<BR>
Details: Join the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew for an afternoon of trailbuilding instruction. First day of two part sequence. Participants are encouraged to attend both days, but it's OK to attend just one part if your schedule won't allow both sessions.<BR>
Location: Sequoia Lodge, 2666 Mountain Blvd, Oakland. <BR>
Registration: Registration required at <A HREF="http://www.btceb.org/school.htm">http://www.btceb.org/school.htm</A> <BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Saturday, July 13 - Joaquin Miller Park, Oakland</B> <BR>
Event: IMBA Trailbuilding School - Part 2, On-the-trail session. <BR>
Time: 9 a.m. - 3 p.m.<BR>
Details: Join the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew for a day of trailbuilding instruction. Second day of two part sequence. Participants are encouraged to attend both days, but it's OK to attend just one part if your schedule won't allow both sessions. Please wear long pants, heavy footwear, work gloves and be prepared for a variety of weather. Water and snacks will be provided, but it's a good idea to bring your own too. <BR>
Location: Sequoia Lodge, 2666 Mountain Blvd, Oakland. <BR>
Registration: Registration required at <A HREF="http://www.btceb.org/school.htm">http://www.btceb.org/school.htm</A> <BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Sunday, July 14 - Camp Tamarancho, Fairfax </B><BR>
Event: Trailwork, ride and party.<BR>
Time: 10 a.m.<BR>
Details: Bring a buddy and help build some cool new trail. Please wear long pants, heavy footwear, work gloves and be prepared for a variety of weather. Bring water and snacks. Following the trailwork we'll ride for about two hours at a social pace. After the ride we'll have a casual party with food and drink. Consider bringing a change of clothes so you don't have to hang out in your riding chamois!<BR>
Location: Camp Tamarancho, 1000 Iron Springs Road, Fairfax. <BR>
More info: <A HREF="http://www.btcmarin.org">http://www.btcmarin.org</A><BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Wednesday, July 17 - Montebello Open Space, Palo Alto</B> <BR>
Event: Mountain Bike Ride<BR>
Time: 5:30 p.m.<BR>
Details: This is an intermediate level group ride hosted by ROMP. The ride is approximately 18-20 miles long with 2,800 vertical feet of climbing.<BR> 
Location: Montebello Open Space Parking Lot. Take Page Mill Road (Palo Alto) west from 280, towards the hills. About seven miles west of 280, you'll find the main Montebello lot on the left.<BR>
More info: <A HREF="mailto:henry_pastorelli@moldev.com">henry_pastorelli@moldev.com</A><BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Thursday, July 18 - REI, Saratoga</B><BR>
Event: Slideshow presented by the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew. <BR>
Time: 7 p.m.<BR>
Details: The Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew builds and rides trails around the world and lives in a car 12 months a year. Come to REI for an entertaining one-hour presentation about the ultimate mountain biking travel adventure. <BR>
Location: REI - Saratoga, 400 El Paseo de Saratoga, Saratoga, (408) 871-8765 <BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Saturday, July 20 - El Corte de Madera (a.k.a. Skeggs), Woodside </B><BR>
Event: Trailwork at El Corte de Madera Open Space Preserve. <BR>
More info: Email <A HREF="mailto:volunteer@openspace.org">volunteer@openspace.org</A> or call 650-691-1200 for full details and location.<BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>Sunday, July 21 - Wilder Ranch, Santa Cruz</B> <BR>
Event: Mountain Bike Ride<BR>
Time: 9 a.m.<BR>
Details: This will be a 2-3 hour ride at a social pace exploring the Wilder Ranch trail system.<BR>
Location: Wilder Ranch main parking lot, 1401 Coast Road, Santa Cruz<BR> 
More info: <A HREF="http://www.mbosc.org">http://www.mbosc.org</A><BR>
</P>

<H3>Ellicottville, NY</H3>

<H3>Join IMBA for the Ellicottville Epic</H3>

<P>
Mountain Bikers are invited to join the Subaru/IMBA Trail Care Crew, IMBA advocacy director Jenn Dice and the Western New York Mountain Bicycling Association for an IMBA Epic Celebration in Ellicottville, New York, July 20-21.
</P>

<P>
Epic celebrations are a weekend-long opportunity to bring mountain bikers together to celebrate our sport and great riding. The first day we focus on trailwork, the second on riding and enjoying the fruits of our labor. Participants camp near the trailhead, share meals, riding stories and ideas. 
</P>

<P>
Epic Celebrations are free to IMBA members and are limited to the first 100 pre-registered riders. Please visit http://www.imba.com/epics and complete the online registration form. Participants are asked to attend both the day-one trailwork and the day-two Epic ride. 
</P>

<P>
The Ellicottville IMBA Epic Celebration will be held at Holimont Ski Area, about one and a half miles from downtown Ellicottville in Western New York. The celebration will begin Friday evening with a group campfire and meeting. Participants are encouraged to arrive on the evening of July 19th to check-in with IMBA staff and settle into their campsite. 
</P>

<P>
Ellicottville IMBA Epic Celebration Schedule: 
</P>

<P>
Friday, July 19<BR>
4-7pm - Participants arrive, check-in, set up camp 6-8pm - Dinner on your own, bring cooking gear and food or visit Ellicottville<BR>
8-10pm - Campfire and group meeting
</P><BR>

<P>
Saturday, July 20<BR>
Breakfast on your own<BR>
9am-1pm - Trailwork<BR>
3-5pm - Optional group ride<BR>
6:30pm - Group Dinner, beer provided by IMBA 8-10pm - Campfire, party and ride briefing 
</P><BR>

<P>
Sunday, July 21<BR>
8am - Epic Group Ride<BR>
</P>

<P>
<B>RIDE DETAILS:</B>
</P>

<P>
Jon Sundquist, IMBA state representative and WNYMBA guru has charted a ride of approximately 31 miles within the McCarty Hill and Rock City State Forests trail system. The ride will have plenty of steep climbs and rocky, rooty descents, broken up by a few sections of old logging roads. 
</P>

<P>
Epic Ride Length: approximately 31 miles Elevation Range: 800 - 1,200 feet
Terrain: Tight, rocky, root strewn singletrack and old logging roads 
</P>

<P>
<B>LOCATION:</B>
The Ellicottville Epic Celebration will start and finish at the Holimont Ski Area, about a mile and a half from downtown Ellicottville. This is where we will camp for the weekend and meet for both the trailwork day and Epic ride. 
</P>

<P>
<B>DIRECTIONS:</B>
From the North: take US 219 to Ellicottville. At the light, continue straight on US 242. Go 1.4 miles from the light. This takes you past the main entrance to Holimont ski area. Turn left at the IMBA Epic signs. 
</P>

<P>
From the South: exit from I-86/NY 17 in Salamanca at the US 219 North exit. 
Travel north ten miles to the traffic light in downtown Ellicottville. At the light, turn left on to US 242 West and travel 1.4 miles. This takes you past the main entrance to Holimont ski area. Turn left at the IMBA Epic signs.
</P>

<P>
<B>BIKE SHOP:</B>
Mud, Sweat and Gears, 22 Monroe St, Ellicottville. 716-699-8300. 
</P>

<P>
<B>WEATHER:</B>
Temperatures average mid-70s to 80s during the day and drop to mid-50s at night. Could be some scattered rain showers, so be prepared. 
</P>

<P>
<B>ARRIVAL:</B>
The celebration will begin on Friday evening with a group campfire and meeting at 8pm. Participants are encouraged to arrive between 4 and 7pm on July 19th to check-in with IMBA staff and settle into their campsite. If you arrive after bed time, just find a place to sleep and we'll meet in the morning. If you can't get there until Saturday, please be ready for trailwork at 9am.
</P>

<P>
CAMPING:
We've made arrangements for everyone to camp at the Holimont ski area. There are bathrooms but no showers, there is a swimming pond though, so bring your suit and towel. No camper hookups.
</P>

<P>
<B>FOOD:</B>
You are responsible for all your own meals, snacks and beverages except dinner on Saturday night. The group dinner on Saturday is a pasta buffet provided by Dina's restaurant. The dinner is approximately $15 a person including tax and tip. Bring cash.
</P>

<P>
IMBA will serve free beer.
</P>

<P>
Plan to bring everthing you'll need for the entire weekend. We hope everyone can park their cars and leave 'em. Bring plenty of food and drinks to stay happy.
</P>

<P>
<B>WHAT TO BRING:</B>
Load up your vehicle with everything you need to be comfortable camping, doing volunteer trailwork, and riding all day. 
</P>

<P>
Camping stuff should include at least: tent, sleeping pad/bag, warm clothing, rain gear, food and cooking gear, flashlight/headlamp, water bottles, camp chair, swimsuit and towel and BUG REPELLANT. 
</P>

<P>
Trailwork gear should include: back pack/hydration pack, work gloves, hat, long sleeve shirt, long pants, sturdy shoes, sun screen, water, snacks and food for lunch. If you have a favorite trailwork tool, feel free to tote it along. Our experience is that running shoes, shorts and other light weight clothing isn't the best for trailwork. Boots, pants and work clothes are best.
</P>

<P>
Riding gear should include: your bike!, helmet, shoes, gloves, rain jacket, basic trailside toolkit, at least two spare inner tubes, patchkit, pump, snacks, plenty of water, and any special tools or parts unique to your bike. Make sure your bike is tuned up before the ride. 
</P>

<P>
The 2002 IMBA Epic Rides program is sponsored by Bike Magazine and Santa Cruz Bicycles. Host club is Western New York Mountain Bicycling Association. 
</P>

<P>
If you would like to attend the IMBA Epic Celebration in Ellicottville, New York, please register on-line at: http://www.imba.com/epics
</P>
