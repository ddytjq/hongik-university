            <b><font face="Arial" size="4" color="#FFFFFF">Speed Channel 
            announces 2002 F1 schedule</font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#FFFF00"><br>
            <br>
            </font><font face="Arial" size="2" color="#FFFFFF">February 25, 2002</font></b>
            </p>

        <td bgcolor="#FFFFEE" valign="top" width="480">
                  <FONT FACE="Arial" size="2">

                  <FONT FACE="Arial">

                  <p>
                  <font size="2">
                  <img border="0" src="../../../images/Logos/SpeedChannel.jpg" align="right" width="225" height="97">SPEED 
                  Channel will again be home to Formula One racing in the United 
                  States, announcing it has reached an agreement to air live 
                  practice, qualifying and race coverage for nearly the entire 
                  17-race Grand Prix schedule in 2002. </font>

                  <p>
                  <font size="2">&quot;Formula One racing is an important part of 
                  SPEED Channel's core programming,&quot; said network President Jim 
                  Liberatore. &quot;Having just moved past the 50-million household 
                  mark, I think it is important that we continue to demonstrate 
                  a commitment to being the premier television destination for 
                  all motor sports fans, and our F1 package certainly supports 
                  that philosophy.&quot;<br>
                  <br>
                  Veteran broadcaster Rick DeBruhl will lead the cast of SPEED 
                  Channel on-air talent, calling play-by-play, with analysis 
                  from former F1 driver David Hobbs and former F1 mechanic Steve 
                  Matchett. Peter Windsor will provide reporting from the 
                  paddock.<br>
                  <br>
                  SPEED Channel's Formula One season kicks off Thursday, Feb. 28 
                  with practice from the Grand Prix of Australia at 9 p.m. (ET). 
                  SPEED Channel will provide live race coverage of 16 of the 17 
                  events, with enhanced coverage of the United States Grand 
                  Prix. Most races will be replayed on same-day tape at 10 p.m. 
                  (ET) -- check local listings.</font></font><FONT FACE="Verdana"><table border="1" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="border-collapse: collapse" width="478" id="AutoNumber1" bordercolor="#CCCCCC">
