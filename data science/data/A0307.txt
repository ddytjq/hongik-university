          <td class="content"> 
		              <p class="contentsubhead">BACS - Direct Debit</p>
            <p>Direct Debits offer businesses and organisations 
              of all sizes a more competitive, cost effective and efficient method 
              for collecting payments. </p>
            <p>A Direct Debit is a written instruction from 
              a customer to their bank or building society authorising you(the 
              Originator) to collect varying payment amounts. </p>
            <p  class="contenttextbold">Preferred By Businesses </p>
            <p>Over 9,000 UK organisations of all sizes choose 
              to use Direct Debits as their preferred method of payment collection. 
              (Source: Direct Debit &quot;An Introduction&quot; June 98) </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Increase business turnover </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Direct Debits have been shown to double 
                successful conversion rates from enquiry to sales when used in 
                promotions. (Source: Direct Debit &quot;An Introduction&quot; 
                June 98) </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Save your business time </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Eliminates time-consuming and laborious 
                reconciliation. You will only be notified of errors and non-payments 
                so that you can carry out reconciliation by exception. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Save your business money </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Administration and processing costs are 
                cheaper than cheques. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Improve Security </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">Eliminates the security risk and costs involved 
                in accepting cash payments. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Improve CashFlow Management </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">You can control the date of receipt of payment 
                into your account and determine the date that funds are cleared. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Flexible Option </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">You have the flexibility to collect variable 
                payment amounts on regular or irregular dates. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Increased Customer Loyalty </p>
            <p>It has been shown that when customers pay by 
              Direct Debit they continue to remain loyal to their supplier and 
              do not search for a replacement. Indeed some Originators have reported 
              a doubling in customer loyalty since offering Direct Debit as a 
              payment option. (Source: Direct Debit &quot;An Introduction&quot; 
              June 98) </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Improved Monitoring of Payments </p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttext">You will receive a report detailing the 
                total number and value of all Direct Debits processed and if applicable 
                any amended or rejected records. </li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Preferred By Customers </p>
            <p>Over 25 million people make more than 1.6 billion 
              Direct Debit payments a year and popularity continues to increase 
              with volumes growing by around 10% year on year. Two-thirds of people 
              in the UK pay at least one bill by Direct Debit. (Source: Direct 
              Debit &quot;An Introduction&quot; June 98) 
            <p>Customers prefer direct debits as they:- </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Enable the customer to spread the cost 
                of large bills.</strong></li>
              <p>Monthly instalments allow the customer to 
                budget for their bills. This can be used as an effective selling 
                proposition for promotions. </p>
              <li><strong>Ensure customers do not pay penalties 
                for delayed payments</strong></li>
              <p>Once a Direct Debit mandate is signed payments 
                are made automatically on the customers behalf. Customers do not 
                have to take postal delays into consideration. </p>
              <li><strong>Saves the customer time &amp; money</strong> 
              </li>
              <p>Eliminates time spent queuing to pay bills.<br/ >
                No postage expenses involved in mailing cheques.<br/ >
                Potential to receive available discounts. </p>
              <li><strong>Assist with CashFlow Management</strong></li>
              <p>Once regular bills are paid by Direct Debit, 
                the customer can then access the remainder of their disposable 
                income. </p>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">How to Get Started </p>
            <ol>
              <li class="contenttext">To become a Direct Debit Originator you 
                require to be sponsored by your Bank. The paper work you will 
                be issuing must be agreed and certain legal documentation needs 
                to be completed. Once these steps have been completed you will 
                be registered as a Direct Debit Originator and can then ask your 
                customers to complete instructions. </li>
              <li class="contenttext">Send a DDI along with the Direct Debit Guarantee 
                details to each customer. Once the customer has returned the completed 
                DDI, check all the details are recorded before forwarding to the 
                customer's bank. </li>
              <li class="contenttext">In the event that the customer's bank reject 
                the DDI , the Instruction will be returned within three working 
                days. </li>
              <li class="contenttext">After three working days, send the customer 
                confirmation of the payment details:- </li>
              <ul>
                <p>Amount<br />
                  Due date<br />
                  Frequency</p>
              </ul>
              <p>This will confirm the details with the customer and allow them 
                to budget for their payment accordingly. </p>
              <li class="contenttext"> Should you need to amend any of the payment 
                details you must notify the customer in advance. The normal minimum 
                period of Advance Notice being 10 working days. </li>
            </ol>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Accessing the Direct Debit System </p>
            <p>There is a choice of methods to access the system either :-</p> 
            <ol>
              <li class="contenttext"> Indirect Access</li>
              <p>You may choose to access the system through 
                the TeleBank&reg; Business BACS module. This is often more suitable 
                for smaller businesses or those collecting a limited number of 
                payments. </p>
              <li class="contenttext"> Direct Access </li>
              <p>The system can be accessed directly from your office by BACSTEL, 
              the telecommunications link. Originators collecting large volumes 
              of Direct Debits find this method faster and more cost effective 
              than indirect access. </p> 
            </ol>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Requirements </p>
            <p>In order to access the system through BACSTEL you will require 
              a PC, appropriate modem and required software. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Availability </p>
            <p>Software can be obtained from your Bank or a proprietary software 
              supplier. Solutions are also available for mid-range and mainframe 
              computer systems. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Security </p>
            <p>All transmissions are secured using passwords unique to each submission. 
              Confirmation of receipt is sent back down the telephone line to 
              notify successful transmission. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Collecting Payments Process </p>
          <ul>
              <li><strong>Information Required</strong></li>
			  </ul>
              <p>A file should be created containing details of customers who have 
              chosen Direct Debit as their preferred method of payment. </p>
              <p>File should include :- </p>
            <p>Customer's details<br />
              Customer's bank account details<br />
              Amount to be collected from customer's account<br />
              Date to be collected<br />
              Details of your account to be credited </p>
            <p>This file is sent via Direct Access or Indirect Access to BACS 
              who make a number of security and validation checks to ensure data 
              is correct. </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Process is completed in 3 days</strong></li> 
			  </ul>
              <p>Day 1 : INPUT - Receipt of Originators payment file at BACS<br />
              Day 2 : PROCESS accepted data<br />
              Day 3 : ENTRY COMPLETED with Beneficiary being credited and Originator 
              debited simultaneously </p>
              <p>Should any of the Direct Debits be returned unpaid by the customer's 
              bank, you will be given details of the reason for non-payment to 
              allow you to take action. Your bank account will be debited with 
              the amount within 2 or 3 working days after it has been credited. 
            </p>
            <p>If appropriate, you may re-present the unpaid Direct Debit provided 
              it is within one month of the date on which it was first presented 
              and the amounts are the same. </p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Amendments and cancellations</strong></li>
			  </ul>
              <p>Your customers may amend or cancel a DDI at any time simply by advising 
              you or normally their bank. All amendments and cancellations must 
              be actioned immediately. Failure to comply may result in you or 
              the bank being liable for any errors. </p>
			  <ul>
            <li><strong>Refunding errors</strong></li>
			</ul>
              <p>In the event of an error being identified, the customer's bank will 
              refund the customer who will in turn reclaim the amount from you. 
            </p>
            <p>If you require any further information please </p>
			<p>
            <a href="/about/201.php" class="link"> e-mail us</a> or <br />
              telephone <strong>+ 44 113 2325203</strong> or <br />
              call into your local Yorkshire branch. </p>
              <p>We look forward to hearing from you. </p>
