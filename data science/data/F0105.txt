<p>1. Editor's Comments<br>
   By: Steven Smith (<a href="Mailto:ssmith@aspalliance.com">
ssmith@aspalliance.com</a>)</p>

<p>Well, I taught my first ASP.NET class to a sold out class of 12 last week, and I was very pleased with the results.  I went with a company called Knowledge Development Centers (<a href="http://www.kdc-cleveland.com/">
http://www.kdc-cleveland.com/</a> ) to host the two-day hands-on course, and they were very professional and handled my needs very well.  The only minor hurdle I encountered was that two of the developer machines wouldn't load VS.NET because their CD-ROM was different from the machine whose ghost image was used.  Apparently Microsoft doesn't want you to replace your CD-ROM, since such a drastic change in your system configuration results in VS.NET locking itself down and requiring re-activation (which didn't work).  Hopefully this "feature" will be gone in the production release.  Nuking the two machines and reinstalling VS.NET from scratch fixed the problem.  You can read more about the class, including some student comments, at <a href="http://aspsmith.com/DesktopDefault.aspx?tabindex=1&tabid=16">
http://aspsmith.com/DesktopDefault.aspx?tabindex=1&tabid=16</a>.  I haven't scheduled another class yet, but I will be announcing my upcoming classes in the next week or two.  I'm sure I'll mention them in this newsletter, but you can sign up to get updates on ASPSmith classes by sending an email to <br> <a href="Mailto:join-aspsmithnews@ls.asplists.com">
join-aspsmithnews@ls.asplists.com</a>.  Thanks.

Steve</p>
