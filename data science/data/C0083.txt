            <p class="font"><b>Introduction</b></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">Although we are reporting 
              to you on a period prior to the World Trade Center tragedy of 11 
              September, I would like to commence by expressing on behalf of the 
              Group our deepest sympathy to all those affected. Many of us have 
              lost friends and colleagues in the insurance and financial services 
              industries and our sincerest condolences go out to their families.  
              </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">It is an event that will have 
              far reaching implications for the insurance industry and will be 
              reflected in our second half results.  More details are included 
              in the Managing Director’s Review.  </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">This is the first accounting 
              period for which we report results solely on an annual accounting 
              basis.  Although this produces a more up-to-date picture than the 
              traditional Lloyd’s three year accounting system, there is inevitably 
              some delay before the significantly improving underwriting conditions 
              show through in the results.  In addition, although we have no material 
              exposure to Directors and Officers insurance, we have experienced 
              some deterioration in our non-marine syndicate’s book of US long 
              tail risks.  In consequence, although we show a half year loss, 
              it owes more to prior year deterioration rather than the current 
              business environment.</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>Results</b></span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">We report an unaudited loss 
              before tax but after minority interests of £6.1 million for the 
              six months ended 30 June 2001 compared to a restated loss before 
              tax of £2.4 million for the six months ended 30 June 2000.  Earnings 
              per share showed a loss of 9.5p (30 June 2000 – loss of 5.3p).  
              The undiluted net tangible asset value per share at 30 June 2001 
              is 52.1p (30 June 2000 – 71.5p).</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">Prior to the occurrence of 
              the US losses, we had planned to increase our interim dividend.  
              We now consider it wise to defer consideration of dividends in respect 
              of the current financial year until the final results are published 
              at the end of April 2002 and shall therefore not be paying an interim 
              dividend.</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>Directorial changes</b></span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">John Parton who has been a 
              non-executive director of the Company since its flotation in 1993 
              reaches the age of 70 next month and has decided to retire at the 
              end of this year.  John has also served on the Board of the Group’s 
              principal operating subsidiary, Chaucer Syndicates Limited, latterly 
              as Chairman.  I would like to express my most sincere thanks for 
              his invaluable contribution and wish him well for his retirement.</span></p>
            <p class="font">I would like to welcome three new non-executive directors 
              to the Group.  Jim Tait, who is executive Chairman of Vesta Insurance 
              Corporation Inc, joined the Board in June of this year, following 
              Vesta’s acquisition of a 9.9% shareholding in the Company.  Chris 
              Forbes, who was formerly Managing Director of Faraday Underwriting 
              Limited, one of the most successful Lloyd’s managing agencies, joined 
              the Board in September.  Following John’s retirement, Chris will 
              chair the Board of Chaucer Syndicates Limited.  Malcolm Cox, formerly 
              active underwriter of syndicate 990 and chairman of the Lloyd’s 
              Underwriters’ Non-Marine Association, will join the Board of Chaucer 
              Syndicates Limited and will take a significant role in our syndicate 
              review process.  We regard ourselves as most fortunate to have secured 
              the services of Jim, Chris and Malcolm who have huge experience 
              of the insurance industry.</p>

            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>World Trade Center </b></span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">I would like to commence this 
              report by concurring with our Chairman’s expression of sympathy 
              for the victims of this appalling event.  </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">We have over the last two 
              weeks carried out an extensive review of how it may affect our Group.  
              Any analysis at this stage is by definition judgemental and will 
              remain so for some time.  Indeed, the ultimate liabilities of the 
              insurance world are likely to take a number of years to determine.  
              Nevertheless, there are some areas of clarity and others where we 
              can make early estimates.</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">Although we have a considerable 
              aviation account, we avoid insuring major US airlines and did not 
              insure either of the two airlines involved.  Our non-marine syndicate 
              participates on the insurance of the two World Trade Center towers.  
              Our motor syndicate is not involved.</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">Our two in-house London Market 
              syndicates underwrite excess of loss reinsurance of insurance companies 
              and other Lloyd’s syndicates that will have exposure to the event.  
              In addition, we will also incur losses on other areas of their account.  
              Our syndicates have comprehensive reinsurance programmes with a 
              first class panel of reinsurers selected for their ability to respond 
              to a catastrophic event such as this.  </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">At present, we estimate that 
              the Group could incur a pre-tax cost of between £8 million and £12 
              million, equivalent to between 7p and 10p per share after tax.  
              We stress that making an early estimate such as this is always difficult 
              and will be subject to change as further information becomes available.  
              </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">The insurance environment 
              from hereon will change radically.  The price and terms of reinsurance 
              cover have risen sharply and its availability will become scarce.  
              Direct rates, already moving considerably upwards before the event, 
              will now increase significantly.  The opportunities for insurance 
              groups like ours will be considerable. </span></p>

            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>Current underwriting markets</b></span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">We have previously reported 
              significantly improving rates in the motor market since 1999.  This 
              has continued into 2001 and we are confident that our motor syndicate 
              is now operating at a profitable level.  The rate of improvement 
              has recently slowed but is still increasing in line with claims 
              inflation.  We intend to increase the 2002 capacity on our motor 
              syndicate by a further 12.5% to take advantage of the additional 
              profitable business, which we believe will be available.</span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">In the marine, aviation and 
              non-marine markets in which we operate, premium rates started to 
              improve in selected classes in late 2000.  This has continued throughout 
              2001 and although some areas have been slower than others, momentum, 
              fuelled by the tragic events in the US, will gather pace and the 
              Group will seek to rise to the new challenges presented by this 
              changed underwriting environment.</span></p>

            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font"><b>Capacity acquisition</b></span></p>
            <p class="font">As a result of the Chaucer Group and deemed associates 
              owning more than 75% of the capacity on syndicates 587 and 1096, 
              we were obliged this year to make offers to the minority holders 
              of capacity rights for the 2002 underwriting year at a price of 
              not less than the highest price paid by Chaucer in the 12 months 
              prior to exceeding the 75% threshold.  The Lloyd’s Auction rules 
              provide that if the Chaucer Group receives acceptances of over 50% 
              of the minority and exceeds 90% ownership, then it will be entitled 
              to apply to Lloyd’s to buy-out any non-accepting capacity holders.</p>
            <p class="font">Accordingly, ALIT (No. 1) Limited, the wholly-owned 
              corporate member of Chaucer Holdings PLC, made announced auction 
              offers in the first Lloyd’s auction this September to acquire all 
              of the prospective participations on syndicates 587 and 1096 for 
              the 2002 year of account other than those to which it and its associates 
              are already entitled.  The offer prices were 0.1p per £1 of capacity 
              in respect of capacity on syndicate 587 and 1.5p per £1 in respect 
              of capacity on syndicate 109<span class="font">6.  </span></p>
            <p class=MsoBodyText><span class="font">In the first Lloyd’s capacity 
              auction for the 2002 underwriting year, we acquired £9.9 million 
              of capacity on syndicate 587 and £6.8 million of capacity on syndicate 
              1096.  As a result, we now have control over 99% of syndicate 1096’s 
              2002 capacity and can apply to buy-out the minority.  We need a 
              further £3.7 million of syndicate 587 capacity to reach the buy-out 
              threshold.  In addition, we have acquired £2.3 million of capacity 
              on syndicate 1084 at 1.1p per £ of capacity.  The total cost to 
              date of our capacity purchases this year is £136,000.</span></p>
