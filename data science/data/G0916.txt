<blockquote>
<p><font size=+2>Activity: Warped Space-Time Model</font></p></center>
<p><a href="#objectives">Instructional Objectives</a><br>
<a href="#time">Time Needed for Activity</a><br>
<a href="#target">Target Grade Level</a><br>
<a href="#background">Background Information</a><br>
<a href="#materials">Materials</a><br>
<a href="#procedures">Procedures</a>


<p><hr><p>
<b><a name="objectives">Instructional Objectives:</a></b>
<blockquote>
<p> Students will -
<ol>
<li> make observations of a teacher demonstration that modes a space-time
continuum warping caused by gravity.
</ol></blockquote>
<p><hr><p>

<b><a name="time">Time Needed for Activity:</a></b>
<blockquote>
<p>A 15-minute demonstration<p></blockquote>
<p><hr><p>

<b><a name="target">Target Grade Level: </a></b>
<blockquote>
<p>High school</p></blockquote>
<p><hr><p>

<b><a name="background">Background Information:</a></b>
<blockquote>
<p>Einstein simplified the treatment of gravity by treating it as if it
simply changed the shape of space.  Within this warped space, all
objects continue to move according to Newton's First Law of Motion and
any apparent change in direction is simply because of changes in the
curvature of space.
</blockquote>
<p><hr><p>

<b><a name="materials">Materials:</a></b>
<ul>
<li>5 x 5 square of black spandex
<li>2 - 5 x 1/4 dowel rods
<li>2 - 1 x 2 boards
<li>Embroidery hoop
<li>Steel marble
<li>Safety pins
<li>String
<li>Miscellaneous masses with hooks</ul>
<p><hr><p>

<b><a name="procedures">Procedures:</a></b>
<blockquote>
<p><b>Frame and Space-Time Continuum</b>

<ol>
<li>Fold over a 3 flap along each edge of the spandex and sew to produce
a sleeve along each side of the fabric.

<p><li>Drill one 1/4" hole halfway through each end of both 1 x 2 
boards.  Place each hole as near to the end of the boards as possible. 
The 1/4" dowels will stretch between the two boards at each end to
produce a square frame.

<p><li>Slip the 1/4" dowels through sleeves on opposite sides of the square
fabric.  Do the same with the 1 x 2 boards with the remaining two sleeves. 
Fit each dowel into the drilled 1/4" holes of the 1 x 2 boards.

<p><li>Set the frame across and between two tables so that only two opposing edges
are resting on the tables.  Cut a circular hole in very center of the 
spandex that is 1 inch smaller in diameter than the embroidery hoop.

<p><li>Pull the edge of the spandex around the cut-out circle into the embroidery hoop. 
Close the hoop to form a frame that pulls the spandex down.  This
represents a black hole.  (If the hole is not deep enough, attach masses
along the edge to pull the fabric down, making the hole deeper.)

<p><li>Use safety pins and string to attach masses in various places
around the bottom side of the fabric.  These represent other medium mass
objects like planets and stars.</ol>

<p><b>Procedure</b>
<ol>
<p><li>Align two tables so the frame can lie between them.  Have students
gather around.

<p><li>Roll the marble across a corner that remains relatively flat and explain
that in the absence of gravity, space is planar and objects in motion
move in straight lines.

<p><li>Explain that objects with mass warp space-time so the course of
traveling objects is altered.  Roll the marble across or/and around a few of
the medium mass objects.

<p><li>Explain that if an object's speed is slow enough and/or the warping
of space by the objects is great enough, objects can get trapped and
crash into the planet or star, or the object can get sucked into the
black hole, never to return.

<p><li>Explain that many objects are neither sucked into the massive
objects to be destroyed nor able to completely escape its
influence.  Instead they may move into any number of elliptical-like
orbits.  Explain that the planets in our system tend to orbit in nearly
circular orbits.  Demonstrate a circular orbit with the marble.  Other
objects, like comets, however, take on long elliptical orbits, when they are near
the orbited object they speed up and when  farther away, they slow.  Again,
demonstrate with the marble.</ol>
