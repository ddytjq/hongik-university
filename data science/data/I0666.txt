<P class=MsoNormal><SPAN style="mso-spacerun: yes"></SPAN></P>
<P class=MsoNormal>Clubs who competed in last season’s UEFA Champions League will receive a total of CHF768m (€521.6m) from the revenue generated by Europe’s&nbsp;leading club competition. </P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>CHF 38 million increase</B><BR>This represents an increase of CHF38m compared with the 2000/01 campaign – with finalists Real Madrid CF and Bayer 04 Leverkusen receiving the largest amounts.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Variety of criteria</B><BR>UEFA said that distribution among the participating clubs would be made in accordance with a variety of criteria. For example, clubs finishing in fourth place in their group in the first group phase, and who were therefore eliminated from the 2001/02 club competitions, are each receiving a bonus of CHF500,000, i.e. a total of CHF4m.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Two equal parts<BR></B>The remaining CHF34m has been divided into two equal parts, with CHF17m paid into the market pool and redistributed with the rest of the pool according to the clubs’ domestic market value.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Total bonus</B><BR>The other CHF17m is being distributed in equal shares to all the participants. Consequently, the 32 clubs not only received the fixed participation bonus of CHF2.5m per club, but also an additional CHF531,250, which means that the total bonus payments per club reached in excess&nbsp;of&nbsp;CHF3m.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Distribution pattern</B><BR>Otherwise, the distribution pattern remained the same as the previous season, with clubs also receiving CHF500,000 for each match played in the first and second group phases (a total of CHF6m for the two phases).</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Victory payments</B><BR>Clubs are also receiving CHF500,000 for each victory in a group match, and CHF250,000 for a draw. The eight quarter-finalists are each being paid a bonus of CHF4m, and each of the four semi-finalists have earned an additional CHF5m.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Lucrative final win</B><BR>UEFA Champions League winners Madrid receive another CHF10m for their triumph in the final, while runners-up Leverkusen will be paid CHF6m. In total, Madrid will receive more than CHF55m for their successful campaign, and Leverkusen’s consolation for finishing as losing finalists in an excellent season for the German club is over CHF49m.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Solidarity principle</B><BR>Under UEFA’s solidarity principle, national associations and clubs from throughout Europe also receive a slice of the lucrative UEFA Champions League cake. The 51 UEFA associations – as at last season, as new members Kazakhstan are not included – each receive a direct payment of CHF300,000.</P>
<P class=MsoNormal><B>Per round played</B><BR>In addition, each club knocked out in the UEFA Champions League and UEFA Cup qualifying rounds, or in the first two UEFA Cup rounds, receives CHF80,000 per round played, up to a maximum CHF400,000. Champion clubs who did not qualify for the UEFA Champions League group stages have each received an additional CHF150,000. </P>
