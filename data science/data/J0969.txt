         <td class="texte10">By the time Michelin decided to withdraw from Formula 1 at the end of the 1984 season, it had won 59 grands prix and 5 titles &#8211; 3 for drivers, 2 for constructors. But the indelible mark Michelin had left was even more significant than its impressive statistical record. Michelin pioneered the use of radial tyres in F1 and, by the time it pulled out after seven and a half years, the whole grid was thus equipped.  If you could choose just one word to describe Michelin's F1 escapades, it would be &#8220;innovative&#8221;.<BR><BR>The Formula 1 of today has changed a great deal.  But while the targets Michelin outlined for the F1 season might appear modest, there is no hiding the fact that the world's leading tyre manufacturer is back at the pinnacle of motor sport. <BR><BR>As group president Edouard Michelin underlines: "This sport has evolved a great deal during the past 17 years. Car technology has moved on and the same is true of tyres. It is going to be a challenge &#8211; but that is just the kind of thing we relish at Michelin."<BR><BR>For its first season back, Michelin has entered partnerships with the BMW WilliamsF1 Team, Jaguar Racing, Mild Seven Benetton Renault Sport, Prost Acer and European Minardi. Toyota will join its list of clients from 2002.<HR></td>
