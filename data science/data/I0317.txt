</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>The Reebok stadium looks amazing from the outside, but sadly inside is painted 
breeze block as far as the eye can see. The number of empty corporate boxes also doesn't 
help improve its image, however its facilities 
are excellent with plenty of bar and food service areas and its own
pre-match TV station (shown in the away end). The bar can get very busy in
the run up to kick off due to most local pubs running a no away colours policy,
forcing away fans to drink in the ground.
</P><P>Away fans are situated in the South Stand which is all seated and covered as 
you would expect for such a new ground. A Done bookmakers kiosk is also
situated inside the away end. Smoking is allowed on the concourse but not in the stand.
Away fans normally get
allocated a maximum of 3000 tickets.
</P><P>The Reebok is situated in the middle of the Middlebrook retail park.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=BL66JW&Title=The+Reebok+Stadium+(Bolton+Wanderers+FC)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Ticket Info</H3><P><B>Fans are reminded that tickets for visiting stands should be purchased through their own club, not the club they are visiting.</B></P><p></P></p><p>Ticket prices in the away end range from £14-£17.
</P></p><p>The ticket office can be contacted on 01204 673601.
Ticket information from the official website can be found by clicking <a class="entry" href="http://www.bwfc.co.uk/match/view/ticket_information">here</a>.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>The club has an indoor viewing point for the disabled and induction loops
for the hard of hearing.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>Whilst there are plenty of pubs on The Middlebrook none allow away club
colours (some don't even allow home colours or fans they suspect as being
away fans). So keep them well covered or enjoy a beer or two in the
ground. 
</P><P>Many away fans leave club shirts, scarves and jackets in the car and head
across to The Horwich Park and then stop by the car park to pick them up
afterwards.
</P><P>Pubs on the main section of The Middlebrook retail park include Old
Orleans, Mamma Amalfi Cafe Bar, Mill House Cafe Bar, Original Brew Bar, 
Bennigans, and there's even a bar in the Hollywood Bowl bowling alley. 
</P><P>New for the 2000/2001 season is Jefferson, yet another cafe bar. Its
located right next door to the Horwich Park Inn. Both the Horwich Park and Jeffersons share
the same car park and have doorman on the entrance to the car park preventing away fans wearing 
colours getting to either pub. 
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry"> Horwich Park Inn
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Bolton-Pub1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Bolton-Pub1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Horwich Park &amp; Jefferson's
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>Horwich Park &amp; Jefferson's
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Horwich Park Inn is located right in front of the main entrance to
the Reebok. On match day the security staff won't even let you enter the
car park (which it also now shares with Jefferson's and The Holiday Inn) if
you are in club colours. This said, neutrally dressed away fans are
welcomed and it proves quite popular with away fans. Away colours have been seen inside the pub
so presumably if you cover them when getting past the security staff on the entrance to the car park
you are okay in the pub showing them!
</P><P>The pub is an Innkeepers Fayre (bearing a striking resemblance to a
Brewers Fayre) and is a typical family eating house, with a beer garden
and kids play area. It can get very busy in the run up to kick off and allow plenty 
of time for getting served.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">Jeffersons
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Bolton-Pub1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Bolton-Pub1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Horwich Park &amp; Jefferson's

<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>Jeffersons is new for the 2000/2001 season. A large American style bar which has a varied menu at reasonable
prices. While not really a football pub Jeffersons does attract quite a few fans before kickoff
though these tend to be mainly home fans.
[/pub_dec]
</P><P></P><P></P><P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=
&Title=Jeffersons
&scale=25000&cat=pub" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P><H5 class="entry"> The Beehive
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Bolton-Beehive-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Bolton-Beehive-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Beehive
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Beehive
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Beehive is located at the end of Burnden Way on Chorley New Road 
(carry straight on past the away car parks about a mile away from the
ground. Its a typical Big Steak pub with everything you'd expect (pool,
sky, video games etc) and even a Wacky Warehouse for the kids.
</P><P>It has its own car park, which whilst not huge may prove cheaper than the
official club car parks.  
</P><P>Very varying reports as to the away-friendliness of this pub have been
received.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>The Middlebrook offers lots of choice ranging from a Bolton
Wanderers themed McDonald's and other big fast food chains (Pizza
Hut, KFC, Burger King etc) to the Abacus oriental restaurant a and
numerous cafe bars. The thrifty may even choose to use ASDA. A notable absence outside the Reebok is the complete
lack of Burger vans.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>ASDA on the Middlebrook has Lloyds, NatWest and Abbey National cash
machines. 
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is BL66JW</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>The ground is situated a few hundred yards from the motorway. (M61, J6). 
Just follow Burnden Way (A6027), its impossible not to see the Reebok
from here.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>The official car park is the only real option and at £5 is not cheap. 
The other car 
parks within the retail complex are under the constant gaze of attendants to 
stop football fans using them.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>'The Reebok' now has its own train station on match days, just a couple of 
hundred yards walk from the ground.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Horwich Parkway
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>A Holiday Inn Express is situated right across from the ground.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/bolton.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Trouble is rare, normally only likely at local derbies
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>The normal away fans response to Boltons 'Lancy Lancy Lancy Lancashire' is 
'W***ky W***ky W***ky Wanderers'. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>Bolton is an OK night out, but Manchester may be preferable.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>Shopping in Bolton is OK and the ground itself is right in the middle of a 
retail park, also Manchester is also a short train ride away.
