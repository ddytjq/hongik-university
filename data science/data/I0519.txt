Pirate Shore forced to retire
10 Jul<br>
			<SPAN Class='gentext' ><P>Bristol Rovers midfielder Jamie Shore has been forced to retire from the game at the age of just 24.</P><P>He had been battling against a succession of knee injuries but has finally decided to call it a day.</P><P>"I think I've been swimming against the current for long enough now, and it's time to go with the flow and call it a day," he told the club's official website.</P><P>"I reported back to training with the rest of the lads this season - I wanted to give it my best shot.</P><P>"We all knew that the outcome would be soon, be it one way or the other, I just didn't expect it to be quite this soon.</P><P>"I knew it was do or die for me, and as it happens, it's just not gone my way.</P><P>"I trained with the rest of the lads and we did a lot of hard running, and because I am a mentally strong person I wanted to stay with the rest of them. But there were times when I had to stop and pull out, not because I was tired, but because of my knee, and mentally I was just thinking to myself what was I doing."</P><P>The midfielder moved to the Pirates four years ago from Norwich City but after making 24 League appearances for the club in his first season, he has failed to return to the first team.</P>
