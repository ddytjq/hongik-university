<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><font size="4">This is the news-page from the Marco Polo Cycling Club.</font></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- We are preparing the participation
in two races in China; the Tour de China and the Qinghai Lake race. The Qinghai
Lake race takes place near Xining at altitudes between 2000 and 4000 meters.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Our New Zealand riders have
participated in the Tour of Serbia. Nathan Dahlberg won a stage. Soon a report
on our site.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Order your own team gear now!
Check:&nbsp; <font color="#00FFFF"><a href="http://www.worldwidecycling.com/gear.htm">www.worldwidecycling.com/gear.htm</a>&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Successes in the
Netherlands! In the G.P. Wieler Revue criterium (June 4) we had two riders on
the podium; Maarten Tjallingii won and Andy Reid (New Zealand) became second.
His brother Robin Reid won the hill-criterium in Wageningen on June 8.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- We had guests over
from Hong Kong. They were in Europe with a delegation of riders and staff to race
and train on the track and on the road with the Marco Polo Cycling Club.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo Cycling Club
participated in two UCI category 1.5 races in Estonia (May 24 and 25) and a
cyclosportive race with thousands of participants (May 26).</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Dutch riders of the club are
successfull! Dennis Hammink won the G.P. Westfahlen in Germany! Maarten
Tjallingii the criterium in Hoogkerk (Netherlands) and Vincent Spierings the
criterium in Krommenie (Netherlands)! Congratulation guys!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo Cycling Club
participated in the Tour de Korea, a UCI category 2.5 race from May 11 untill 17.
Cory Lange from Canada won a stage and rode a day in the leader jersey. Nathan
Dahlberg became 2nd in a stage of his first race since his recovery from the
Malaria he became in the Tour du Faso. There is a report at the
following page: <a href="korea.htm"><font color="#00FF00">click here.</font></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The team in the Tour of Greece
(April 22-28) went strong! Dennis Hammink (Netherlands) became 2nd in a stage and Jelle van
Groezen (Netherlands) was leader in the under 23 classification but became 2nd.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Two Marco Polo riders made the
selection for the New Zealand national team for the Commonwealth Games. Ryan
Russell raced him self into form in the California Tour and mountainbiker Kashi
Leuchs will race on the road.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- We participate in the Paths of King
Nicola, an international stage race starting in Montenegro.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- A strong international team from
the Marco Polo Cycling Club has traveled from everywhere in the world to race in the
California Series in the USA in March. In the Sea Otter Classic we had in our
team of 8 riders, 8 different nationalities. For a report, <a href="californiaseries2002.htm"><font color="#00FFFF">Click
here.&nbsp;</font></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The <a href="racing.htm"><font color="#0000FF">racing
program</font></a> for the Elite selection of the Marco Polo Cycling Club is
updated.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt">-
On Sunday February 10th<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; we had a Marco
Polo Cycling club day at the track. This was</span> in Büttgen (Germany) on a
nice wooden track. We had a nice meeting, trained on the track and exchanged photos and
looked at videos from
many international races and events.<o:p>
</o:p>
</span></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo Cycling Club
trainingcamp was from <span style="mso-bidi-font-size:10.0pt">22nd till
28th of February (for some untill the 3rd of March) in Cannes in the south of France at the
Cote d’Azur.&nbsp;</span> </p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- In December the Marco Polo Cycling
Club excisted one year. In this year we have grown to over 500 members from 45
countries! With the Elite team we participated in 14 stage races all over the
world, we won 4 of them: the Tour du Maroc, Qinghai-Tibet (China), Pahang
cycling Challenge (Malaysia) and the Tour du Faso (Africa). In our Elite team in
Europe we had 8 different nationalities among them 5 national road champions;
Hong Kong, Albania, New Zealand, Uzbekistan and Mongolia. We had incredible
publicity worlwide, but we are still looking for sponsors to support us.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="newspaperscs3.jpg"><img border="2" src="newspaperscs3_small.jpg" alt="newspaperscs3.jpg (29612 bytes)" width="104" height="131" align="left"></a>We
are as happy as the Hong Kong cyclists and their coach with the further
cooperation with the Hong Kong Cycling Association. This years cooperation
starts in January with races in New Zealand and Australia.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">On this photo from a newspaper you
see the Hong Kong cyclists and their coach celebrating after the Tour of South
China Sea.&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><font size="4"><b><u>2001 news:</u></b></font></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- A Dutch selection of the Marco Polo
Cycling Club traveled to Hong Kong to participate in the Tour of South China
Sea. This UCI 2.5 stage race was held from December 25 untill 31.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- On December 5th from 20:00 untill
21.00, Eurosport broadcasted a documentary of the Tour du Faso 2001. Eurosport can
be received in all countries in Europe and many coutries around the world. Check
also the programming of other channels, for example Outdoor Life Network (USA
and Canada) ESPN Star (Asia) and many more will broadcast features about this
race.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="bfteamyellowjersey.jpg"><img border="2" src="bfteamyellowjersey_small.jpg" alt="bfteamyellowjersey.jpg (21473 bytes)" width="104" height="79" align="left"></a>Joost
Legtenberg has won the Tour du Faso 2001! Congratulations Joost!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">After winning the first stage he kept
his leader jersey untill the final stage. Also Bram de Waard and Maarten
Tjallingii won stages, Bram de Waard won the first part of
stage 6. and Maarten Tjallingii won the second stage of the Tour de Faso 2001!
Maarten became 3rd in the General Classification and Nathan Dahlberg 4th. Too
bad for Bram he got a time penalty in the last stage that cost him a top 5
placing.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Follow the adventures of the team in
Burkina Faso, Africa, <a href="burkinafaso.htm"><font color="#FF00FF">click
here</font></a>.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Peep Mikli (Estonia) has won the
Pahang Cycling Challenge, a 5 day stage race in Malaysia. The Marco Polo Cycling
Club had two riders on the podium because Tonton Susanto got 3rd!
Congratulations! For a report <a href="malaysia2001.htm"><font color="#00FF00">click
here</font></a>.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo Cycling Club will
participate in the Tour du Faso in Burkina Faso (Africa). This race will take
place from Oktober 31st untill November 11th. The heat, the bad roads and
primitive conditions will make this race a real challenge. Eurosport will cover
it daily in their newsreports and broadcast a 1 hour documentary afterwards.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Follow the adventures of the team, <a href="burkinafaso.htm"><font color="#FF00FF">click
here</font></a>.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- An international selection of the
Marco Polo Cycling Club was in Malaysia in Oktober. They participated in the
Perlis Open and the Pahang Cycling Challenge.
The following riders were on the team: Nathan Dahlberg, Tim Vincent and Andy Reid
from New Zealand, Peep Mikli and Algis Maasikmets from Estonia and Tonton
Susanto from Indonesia.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="tontonmedal2.jpg"><img border="2" src="tontonmedal2_small.jpg" alt="tontonmedal2.jpg (18374 bytes)" width="96" height="66" align="left"></a>We are proud that Tonton Susanto
became 2nd on G.C. of the Perlis Open (UCI 2.5).
Congratulation!&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Follow the adventours of the team, <a href="malaysia2001.htm"><font color="#00FF00">click

<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- In September the Marco Polo Cycling Club had some great victories: <b>Craig
Thomson won the home race from our friends of the EAWN Foundation</b> that
support the Marco Polo Cycling Club, the criterium in Winterswijk.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b>On the same day Jurgen van Pelt won the 4th stage
of the Tour de Hokkaido</b> and Dennis Hammink was second in the sixth (and
last) stage.&nbsp;Click on the small pictures to see Jurgen win.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="victory1-2-3.jpg"><img border="2" src="victory1-2-3_small.jpg" alt="victory1-2-3.jpg (86088 bytes)" width="238" height="71"></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="hokkaido2001.htm"><font color="#FF00FF">Click
here for a report with photos.</font></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The biggest victory however was for
the team that participated in China in the <b>Qinghai-Tibet stage race, they had the winner of the General Classment. Ulzii
Orshikh Jamsran from Mongolia won this 'epic battle'</b> for Marco Polo, <a href="qinghai-tibet.htm"><font color="#FFFF00">Click
here for a report with photos.</font></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Cyclingnews.com, the major cycling
news site on the internet, offered the Marco Polo Cycling Club a diary on their
site. Special thank's to cyclingnews.com. The first story that is on is about
the Tour de China 2001. Go to <a href="http://www.cyclingnews.com">www.cyclingnews.com</a>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo
Cycling Club participated succesfully in the Tour de China 2001. This years tour
took place in
Inner-Mongolia, July 26th to 29th. One of the favourites for the victory was the
president of the club; Nathan Dahlberg. He was helped by Raymond Raadtgever,
Casper Helling, Andries Bosma and Rob Conijn. Sprinter Dennis Hammink was going
for stage winss and the points jersey.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b>News from China</b>: Dennis
Hammink won two stages, won the points jersey and became 3rd on G.C. Nathan
Dahlberg became 2nd in one stage and got 2nd on G.C. Congratulations Dennis,
Nathan and the rest of the team with this great succes! Now a report with Photos!
<a href="china2001.htm"><font color="#FF0000">click here</font></a>.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Jamsran Ulzii-Orshikh from Mongolia
became vice-worldchampion at the B-World Championships in China. In the road
race he got beaten by Xuezhong Tang from China. The Marco Polo program proved
succesfull in this race, the Hong Kong riders that have been racing together
with Ulzii in Europe: Ngai Ching Wong and Chi Hang Leung became 6th and 13th.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The following riders have joined the Elite selection in Europe; Glen Thomson (National Champion from New
Zealand) and Ervin Lika (National Champion from Albania). These riders together
with Kan Koon Hang (National Champion Hong Kong) and Damir Iratov (National
Champion Uzbekistan) make a total of 4 national Champions in one team!!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Past months the international
Elite-selection of the Marco Polo Cycling Club has been racing in Europe. There were several good placings by, Kam Po Wong (Hong Kong), Robin Reid and
Craig Thomson (New Zealand).</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">In the criterium in Tiel (Netherlands)
Jamsran Ulzii-Orshikh (Mongolia) was 2nd and Dennis Hammink (Netherlands) 3rd!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">In Cras Avernas (Belgium) Remko
Kramer (Netherlands) was 10th in a UCI cat 1.5 race.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Craig Thomson was 3rd in Fulda in
Germany.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Bart Brentjes (currently leading the
UCI world ranking MTB) won the Wieler Revue criterium in 's Heerenberg.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Damir Iratov got third in the strong
pro-criterium in Gelsenkirchen (Germany) this was his second race since he
arrived.....</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Robin Reid got second in Nieuwerkerk,
Liessel,
Hilversum, Stadtlohn (Germany) and Driel (in Driel Dennis Hammink got 3rd). Next
race will be a victory Robin!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Too bad next race was in Dodewaard,
Robin got.........2nd</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- In the pro-criterium in Essen (Germany)
(August 11) Marco Polo riders Dennis Hammink, Remko Kramer and Craig Thomson
became 1st, 3rd and 4th in the field sprint for the fourth place. Because of the
good team-work they beat many Trade Team 1 riders from Telekom and Coast, many
of world's best track riders and a lot of other professionals. The many
spectators gave the Marco Polo riders a special applause.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The next day Glen Thomson got in the
main break in the Versatel Classic in Dortmund (UCI 1.4) he became 9th in this
race that was won by Jan Ulrich. The Marco Polo Cycling Club was the only
amateur team in this event which was visited by almost 100.000 spectators.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The elite selection of the Marco
Polo Cycling Club traveled to Denmark from 15 to 18 June for some races. The
following riders were on the team: From the Spaarselect cyclo-cross team; Bart
Wellens (Belgium), Peter van Santvliet (Belgium), Wim de Vos (Netherlands) and
Damir Iratov (Uzbekistan), Kan Koon Hang (Hong Kong) and Remko Kramer (Netherlands)
completed the team.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Peter van santvliet won the points
jersey, Wim de Vos got 2 times 2nd and one time 3rd in stages and 3rd on G.C.
Damir Iratov got 12th on G.C., this made a 4th placing in the Team G.C.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- June 10, we have
participated in Bochum Sparkassen Giro (Germany)(UCI categorie 1.4). The
following riders were in the selection: Robin Reid and Craig Thomson (New Zealand),
John Kane (Australia), Damir Iratov (Uzbekistan), Koon Hang Kan (Hong Kong),
Christian Quintero Montenegro (Colombia), Dennis Hammink and Remko Kramer
(Netherlands).&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Nathan Dahlberg won the Tour du
Maroc (April 2 to 15). Congratulations Nathan!! He took the leader jersey in the
5th stage and never lost it in the following 8 stages.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">In this UCI ranked 2.5 stage race the
Marco Polo Cycling Club has beaten 6 Trade Teams and 8 national teams.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">For a complete report from this race
klick here: <a href="morocco.htm"><font color="#FF0000">Tour du Maroc</font></a>.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- At the same time Nathan wore the
leader jersey, Kam Po Wong was the leader in the Paths of King Nicola (April 10
to 15), also a UCI ranked 2.5 stage race. Congratulations Kam Po!! Kam Po won
the first stage and wore the leader jersey 3 days. He finished 5th on G.C. but
the final stage with hard climbs was cancelled due to snow.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- We think this is unique, for a
club, to be leader in two international stage races at the same time. There are
just a few Trade Teams in the world that are able to do the same.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Nathan Dahlberg and Robin Reid rode in the Tour de Turkey
(22 to 29 April). Robin got 2nd in a stage.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- Marco Polo Cycling Club has
participated in the Redlands Bicycle Classic (March 13 to 18) and the Sea
Otter Classic (March 22 to 25) in California (USA). These UCI categorie 2.4 races
are the main stage races in the USA. <a href="californiaseries.htm"><font color="#FF0000">For
a report click here</font></a> or surf to races World Wide Cycling. Cory
Lange (Canada) finished 4th in King of the Mountain and 6th in Sprint
Classification of the Redlands Bicycle Classic! And dominique Perras finished
2nd in the Sprint Classification of the Sea Otter Classic!</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">As a preparation the team raced in the Sequoia Classic and the Visalia
Criterium March 10 and 11.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- The Marco Polo Cycling Club has participated in the Tour of Rhodos in
Greece,
February 21 to 25. The following riders were on the team; <span lang="DE" style="mso-ansi-language:DE">Kam-Po
Wong, </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">Siu Lun Ho and King
Nin Lau from Hong Kong,</span> Kashi <span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">Leuchs
and Nathan Dahlberg from New Zealand and</span> Aldo Klomp from the Netherlands.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The riders that came from warm places had a hard time because the first
stages it rained with only 5 degrees Celsius. Kam-Po got sick, King Nin crashed
pretty hard and Nathan had a flat tire on the wrong moment. Aldo and Kashi
finished allright, had no good results but prepared well for the rest of the
season.</p>
