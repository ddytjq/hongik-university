          <td width="520"> 
            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"> 
              <!-- #BeginEditable "title" --><b>Astronomical Pseudo-science: A 
              Skeptic's Resource List (Version 2.3; May 2001)</b><!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>
          </td>
          <td>&nbsp;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" --> 
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font size="3" color="#6666CC"><a name="6"></a>6. 
              Immanuel Velikovsky and Worlds in Collision</font></b></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">&#9;An 
              Austrian psychiatrist and amateur scholar, Velikovsky touted the 
              bizarre thesis that ancient religious writings record evidence of 
              recent catastrophes in the solar system, including the idea that 
              Venus was a comet disgorged by Jupiter in historic times. His writing 
              were once very popular, but now only a small underground of true 
              believers keeps his work alive.</font></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Asimov, 
                I. "Worlds in Confusion" in <i>The Stars in their Courses.</i> 
                1971, Doubleday.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Goldsmith, 
                D., ed. <i>Scientists Confront Velikovsky</i>. 1977, Norton. Proceedings 
                of a symposium at the 1974 meeting of the American Association 
                for the Advancement of Science.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Gould, 
                S. "Velikovsky in Collision" in <i>Natural History</i>, Mar. 1975.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Morrison, 
                D. &amp; Chapman, C. "Catastrophism Gone Wild" in <i>Cosmic Catastrophes.</i> 
                1989, Plenum. Two noted astronomers examine our modern view of 
                how impacts and other catastrophes have shaped the Earth, and, 
                in the process, debunk Velikovsky's ideas.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Oberg, 
                J., et al. "The Velikovsky Affair" in <i>Skeptical Inquirer</i>, 
                Fall 1980. An update and review following Velikovsky's death.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sagan, 
                C. "Venus and Dr. Velikovsky" in <i>Broca's Brain</i>. 1979, Random 
                House. A superb refutation of Velikovsky's ideas.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Stiebing, 
                W. <i>Ancient Astronauts, Cosmic Collisions.</i> 1984, Prometheus. 
                Examines Velikovsky's and Von Daniken's claims; see next section.</font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="pseudobib.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font size="3" color="#6666CC"><a name="7"></a>7. 
              Ancient Astronauts and Erich Von Daniken</font></b></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">&#9;A 
              convicted Swiss embezzler (who wrote part of his best-selling <i>Chariots 
              of the Gods</i> while in jail), Von Daniken claimed that there is 
              a large amount of archaeological evidence that alien visitors helped 
              us build many of the impressive artifacts that ancient civilizations 
              left behind. (The insulting subtext here is that we needed the aliens' 
              help because we humans were too stupid to build the Nazca lines 
              or the pyramids.) In fact, Von Daniken's books involve massive misinterpretations 
              of archaeological data and a great deal of fabrication; the past, 
              as the title of one the best debunking books vividly asserts, is 
              human.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>&nbsp;Books</b></font></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Stiebing, 
                W. <i>Ancient Astronauts, Cosmic Collisions.</i> 1984, Prometheus. 
                A good skeptical introduction.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Story, 
                R. <i>The Space Gods Revealed.</i> 1976, Harper &amp; Row. Debunks 
                Von Daniken's ideas.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Thiering, 
                B. &amp; Castel, E., eds. <i>Some Trust in Chariots.</i> 1975, 
                Popular Library. 16 skeptical articles.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">White, 
                P. <i>The Past is Human.</i> 1974, Taplinger. An archaeological 
                perspective.</font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><font color="#6666CC"><b>Articles</b></font></font></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Lingemann, 
                R. "Erich Von Daniken's Genesis" in the <i>New York Times Book 
                Review,</i> March 30, 1974. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nickell, 
                J. "The Nazca Drawings Revisited" in <i>Skeptical Inquirer</i>, 
                Spring 1983.</font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="pseudobib.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b><font size="3" color="#6666CC"><a name="8"></a>8. 
              Creationism versus Evolution</font></b></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">&#9;Fundamentalist 
              religious thinkers (from a number of religions) have sought to deny 
              the evidence from geology, astronomy, and evolutionary biology about 
              the age and gradual development of the universe, the Earth, and 
              its life-forms. Some have worked hard to get their own brand of 
              &quot;creation science&quot; into the public schools and to undermine 
              the teaching of <i>evolution,</i> one of the most fundamental ideas 
              in modern science. The literature examining this controversy is 
              enormous; the list below is merely a representative sampling.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><font color="#6666CC"><b>Books</b></font></font></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Berra, 
                T. <i>Evolution and the Myth of Creationism: A Basic Guide to 
                the Facts in the Evolution Debate. </i>1990, Stanford U. Press. 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Futuyma, 
                D. <i>Science on Trial: The Case for Evolution.</i> 1983, Pantheon. 
                A leading evolutionary biologist explains the case for evolution 
                that the creationists seek to deny.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Godfrey, 
                L., ed. <i>Scientists Confront Creationism.</i> 1982, Norton. 
                A useful collection of articles.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Kitcher, 
                P. <i>Abusing Science: The Case Against Creationism. </i>1982, 
                MIT Press. A philosopher takes a critical look at the claims against 
                evolution and illuminates the issues involved.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">McGowan, 
                C. <i>In the Beginning: A Scientist Shows Why the Creationists 
                are Wrong.</i> 1984, Prometheus Books. A Canadian zoologist examines 
                and refutes creationist arguments.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Ruse, 
                M., ed. <i>But Is It Science? </i>1996, Prometheus. A collection 
                of articles about the creationism/evolution controversy, by scientists, 
                philosophers, etc.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Strahler, 
                A. Science and Earth History: The Evolution / Creation Controversy. 
                1987, Prometheus Books. A discussion from the geologist&#146;s 
                point of view, with lots of information about dating the Earth&#146;s 
                rocks.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Tuomey, 
                C. <i>God's Own Scientists: Creationists in a Secular World.</i> 
                1994, Rutgers U. Press. An anthropologist examines the culture 
                of creationism as if he were looking at far-away tribe.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Wilson, 
                D., ed. <i>Did the Devil Make Darwin Do It? Modern Perspectives 
                on the Creation-Evolution Controversy.</i> 1983, Iowa State U. 
                Press. Interesting collection of essays, by historians, scientists, 
                and educators, laying out the history of the controversy and the 
                perspectives of the sciences.</font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><font color="#6666CC"><b>Articles</b></font></font></p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Abell, 
                G. &quot;The Ages of the Earth and the Universe&quot; in Godfrey, 
                Laurie, ed. <i>Scientists Confront Creationism. </i>1983, Norton.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Asimov, 
                I. &quot;The Threat of Creationism&quot; in the<i> New York Times 
                Magazine,</i> June 14, 1981, p. 90.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Bobrowski, 
                M. &quot;Teaching Evolutionary Processes to Skeptical Students&quot; 
                in <i>Physics Teacher, </i>Dec. 2000, p. 565. Includes a list 
                of creationist arguments and science teacher responses. Nice summary 
                of issues.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Dutch, 
                S. &quot;A Critique of Creationist Cosmology&quot; in <i>Journal 
                of Geological Education, </i>1982, vol. 30, p. 27.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Rusk, 
                J. &quot;Answers to Creationism&quot; in <i>The Planetarian (Journal 
                of the International Planetarium Society)</i>, Sep. 1988, vol. 
                17, No. 3.</font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Scott, 
                E. "Antievolution and Creationism in the U.S." <i>Annual Reviews 
                of Anthropology,</i> 1997, vol. 26, p. 263. A leading pro-evolution 
                educator summarizes the issues.</font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><font color="#6666CC"><b>Web 
              Sites:</b></font></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">National 
              Center for Science Education [<a href="http://www.ncseweb.org/" target="_blank">www.ncseweb.org/</a>] 
              is the key organization working to oppose the efforts of creationists 
              and to assist educators who want to present the evolutionary perspective. 
              The site is full of excellent information and links.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><i>Science 
              and Creationism</i> is a short booklet from the National Academy 
              of Sciences, with a fine summary of the scientific perspective on 
              evolution: [<a href="http://bob.nap.edu/html/creationism/" target="_blank">bob.nap.edu/html/creationism/</a>]</font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Talk.Origins 
              Archive [<a href="http://www.talkorigins.org" target="_blank">www.talkorigins.org</a>] 
              contains articles, essays, and discussion about all aspects of the 
              creation/evolution controversy.</font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Questions 
              and Answers about Creationism /Evolution: [<a href="http://www2.uic.edu/%7Evuletic/cefec.html" target="_blank">www2.uic.edu/~vuletic/cefec.html</a>] 
              A nicely organized summary of creationist arguments and scientific 
              responses.</font></p>
