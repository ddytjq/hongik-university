            <H1><!-- #BeginEditable "Heading" -->Arithmetic Operators Quiz Answers<!-- #EndEditable --></H1>
            <!-- #BeginEditable "Content" --> 
            <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="4" CELLPADDING="5">
              <TR BGCOLOR="#F8FFF0"> 
                <TD COLSPAN="2"> 
                  <P CLASS="SUBHEADING">The 5 Operators </P>
                </TD>
              </TR>
              <TR VALIGN="TOP" BGCOLOR="#EEFFEE"> 
                <TD WIDTH="100%" COLSPAN="2"> 
                  <TABLE WIDTH="90%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="0" ALIGN="CENTER" BGCOLOR="#CCCCCC">
                    <TR> 
                      <TD> 
                        <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="1" CELLPADDING="3">
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#CCEEFF" VALIGN="TOP" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                            <TD WIDTH="10%" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Expression</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="10%"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">returns...</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="80%" COLSPAN="2"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Reason</P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3+1-8</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>-4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>3+1</TT> is <TT>4</TT>, and <TT>4-8</TT> is <TT>-4</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>9-(4-3)</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>8</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Because of the brackets, <TT>(4-3)</TT> is evaluated first and returns <TT>1</TT>. Then we get <TT>9-1</TT>, which equals 
                                <TT>8</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>6*7</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>42</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Simple maths here! </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>2*1.5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>We are not dealing with division, so <TT>2</TT> lots of <TT>1.5</TT> simply equals <TT>3</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>2*1+5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>7</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Multiplication has higher precedence, so <TT>2*1</TT> is evaluated first and returns <TT>2</TT>. Then we have <TT>2+5</TT> 
                                which equals <TT>7</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>(6+3)*4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>36</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>The bracketed expression has higher precedence than the multiplication operator, so <TT>(6+3)</TT> is evaluated first and 
                                returns <TT>9</TT>. We are left with <TT>9*4</TT> which equals <TT>36</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1/3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>1</TT> divided by <TT>3</TT> is actually <TT>0.333333</TT>..., but this is an <TT>int</TT> divided by an <TT>int</TT> 
                                which always returns an <TT>int</TT> value. Hence <TT>0.333333</TT>... is rounded down to <TT>0</TT>. See the <A HREF="05_4data.htm#float" TARGET="_blank2">Data 
                                Types</A> section if you're unsure about the following division sums. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>6/2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>This is also an <TT>int</TT> divided by an <TT>int</TT>, but since <TT>2</TT> goes into <TT>6</TT> EXACTLY (<TT>3</TT> 
                                times), <TT>3</TT> is the result, as expected. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4/3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>4</TT> divided by <TT>3</TT> is actually <TT>1.333333</TT>..., but since this is an <TT>int</TT> divided by an <TT>int</TT>,<TT>1.333333</TT>... 
                                is rounded down to <TT>1</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>5/2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>2.5</TT> gets rounded down to <TT>2</TT> for the same reason as above. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3.0/2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1.5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>A <TT>float</TT> divided by an <TT>int</TT> returns a <TT>float</TT>, so <TT>1.5</TT> as expected. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>3/2.0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1.5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>An <TT>int</TT> divided by a <TT>float</TT> returns a <TT>float</TT>, so <TT>1.5</TT> as expected. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>5/2.0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>2.5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>An <TT>int</TT> divided by a <TT>float</TT> returns a <TT>float</TT>, so <TT>2.5</TT> as expected. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4*6/2</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>12</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Multiplication and division have the same level of precedence. Left-to-right evaluation is adopted. <TT>6*4</TT> is <TT>24</TT> 
                                and so <TT>24/2</TT> is <TT>12</TT>. </P>
                              <P>If you (incorrectly) did right-to-left evaluation, you'd get <TT>4*3</TT> which still gives <TT>12</TT> - a pure fluke!! 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>20/2*5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>50</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P>Left-to-right again. <TT>20/2</TT> is <TT>10</TT>, <TT>10*5</TT> gives <TT>50</TT>. </P>
                              <P>No fluke this time for right-to-left: <TT>2*5</TT> gives <TT>10</TT>, <TT>20/10</TT> gives <TT>2</TT>, which is incorrect. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4%3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>3</TT> goes into <TT>4</TT> once with <TT>1</TT> as the remainder. If you're stuck, see the <A HREF="07_arith.htm#modulus" TARGET="_blank2">Arithmetic 
                                Operators</A> section. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>17%4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>1</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>4</TT> goes into <TT>17</TT> <TT>4</TT> times with <TT>1</TT> as the remainder (<TT>4*4</TT> is <TT>16</TT>). </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>9%3</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>3</TT> goes into <TT>9</TT> exactly (<TT>3*3</TT> is <TT>9</TT>) so nothing remains. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4%5</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>4</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>5</TT> goes into <TT>4</TT> zero times (since <TT>5</TT> is greater than <TT>4</TT>), so <TT>4</TT> is the remainder. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>7%7/*6*/</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER"><TT>0</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="2" CLASS="justify"> 
                              <P><TT>7</TT> goes into <TT>7</TT> exactly, which leaves no remainder. The <TT>6</TT> has no effect at all! It is commented 
                                out using the opening comment, <TT>/*</TT>, and the closing comment, <TT>*/</TT>. Refer back to <A HREF="02_basic.htm#comments" TARGET="_blank2">The 
                                Basics</A> section for more info on comments. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                        </TABLE>
                      </TD>
                    </TR>

                  <P CLASS="SUBHEADING"><A HREF="#TOP"><IMG SRC="../images/gra_top.gif" WIDTH="22" HEIGHT="16" ALIGN="RIGHT" ALT="Top" BORDER="0"></A>Arithmetic 
                    Assignment and <BR>
                    Increment / Decrement Operators </P>
                </TD>
              </TR>
              <TR VALIGN="TOP" BGCOLOR="#EEFFEE"> 
                <TD WIDTH="100%" COLSPAN="2"> 
                  <TABLE WIDTH="90%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="0" ALIGN="CENTER" BGCOLOR="#CCCCCC">
                    <TR> 
                      <TD> 
                        <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="1" CELLPADDING="3">
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#CCEEFF" VALIGN="TOP" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                            <TD WIDTH="10%" ALIGN="CENTER" NOWRAP> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Statement(s)</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="90%" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Comment(s)</P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <PRE>int a = 5;
int b = 9;
int c = 2;
int d = 7;
int e;
</PRE>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P><TT>int</TT> variables <TT>a</TT>, <TT>b</TT>, <TT>c</TT> and <TT>d</TT> are declared and initialized. </P>
                              <P><TT>e</TT> is also declared as an <TT>int</TT> but remains <SPAN CLASS="RED">UNDEFINED</SPAN> - no value has been assigned 
                                to it yet. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>a += b;</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>a = a+b;</TT> <BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>a</TT> is <TT>5</TT>, <TT>b</TT> is <TT>9</TT>, so this statement assigns <TT>14</TT> to <TT>a</TT>. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>b += 4; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>b = b+4;</TT> <BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>b</TT> is <TT>9</TT>, so this statement assigns <TT>9+4</TT> (which is <TT>13</TT>) to <TT>b</TT>. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>c *= 3; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>c = c*3;</TT> <BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>c</TT> is <TT>2</TT>, so this statement assigns <TT>2*3</TT> (which is <TT>6</TT>) to <TT>c</TT>. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>d -= 4; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>d = d-4;</TT> <BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>d</TT> is <TT>7</TT>, so this statement assigns <TT>7-4</TT> (which is <TT>3</TT>) to <TT>d</TT>. 
                              </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>d *= c-3; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>d = d*(c-3);</TT> - NOT <TT>d = d*c-3;</TT><BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>d</TT> is <TT>3</TT>, <TT>c<TT> </TT></TT>is <TT>6</TT>. Because of the brackets, <TT>(c-3)</TT> 
                                is calculated first before being multiplied by <TT>d</TT>. So <TT>6-3</TT> is <TT>3</TT>, and <TT>3*3</TT> is <TT>9</TT>, 
                                which then gets assigned to <TT>d</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>e = c++;</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P><A HREF="07_arith.htm#increment" TARGET="_blank2">Post incrementing</A> takes place here. The value of <TT>c</TT> gets 
                                assigned to <TT>e</TT>, THEN <TT>c</TT> is increased by <TT>1</TT>. Before this statement is evaluated, <TT>c</TT> is <TT>6</TT>. 
                                So <TT>6</TT> gets assigned to <TT>e</TT>. Then <TT>c</TT> is incremented and holds the value of <TT>7</TT>. </P>
                              <P>Final values: <TT>a</TT>: <TT>14</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;b</TT>: <TT>13</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;c</TT>: <TT>7</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;d</TT>: <TT>9</TT>, 
                                <TT>&nbsp;e</TT>: <TT>6</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                        </TABLE>
                      </TD>
                    </TR>
                  </TABLE>
                  <BR>
                  <TABLE WIDTH="90%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="0" CELLPADDING="0" ALIGN="CENTER" BGCOLOR="#CCCCCC">
                    <TR> 
                      <TD> 
                        <TABLE WIDTH="100%" BORDER="0" CELLSPACING="1" CELLPADDING="3">
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#CCEEFF" VALIGN="TOP" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                            <TD WIDTH="10%" ALIGN="CENTER"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Statement(s)</P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD WIDTH="90%" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P ALIGN="CENTER">Comment(s)</P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <PRE>int f,g;
int h,i;
int j;

f = 2;
g = 6;
</PRE>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P><TT>int</TT> variables <TT>f</TT>, <TT>g</TT>, <TT>h</TT>, <TT>i</TT> and <TT>j</TT> are declared. </P>
                              <P><TT>f</TT> is initialized with <TT>2</TT> and <TT>g</TT> is initialized with <TT>6</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>g %= 2;</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>g = g%2;</TT> <BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>g</TT> is <TT>6</TT>. Since <TT>2</TT> goes into <TT>6</TT> exactly (<TT>3*2</TT> is <TT>6</TT>), 
                                <TT>6%2</TT> returns zero, which gets assigned to <TT>g</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT" NOWRAP> 
                              <P><TT>f -= g+1;</TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>This is the same as saying <TT>f = f-(g+1);</TT> - NOT <TT>f = f-g+1;</TT><BR>
                                Before assignment, <TT>f</TT> is <TT>2</TT>, <TT>g</TT> is <TT>0</TT>. Due to the brackets, <TT>(g+1)</TT> is calculated 
                                first to return <TT>1</TT>. Then <TT>2-1</TT> (which is <TT>1</TT>) gets assigned to <TT>f</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>h = f--; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>Post decrementing takes place here. The value of <TT>f</TT> gets assigned to <TT>h</TT>, THEN <TT>f</TT> is decreased by 
                                <TT>1</TT>. Before this statement is evaluated, <TT>f</TT> is <TT>1</TT>. So <TT>1</TT> gets assigned to <TT>h</TT>. Then 
                                <TT>f</TT> is decremented and now holds the value of <TT>0</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>i = --g; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>Pre decrementing takes place here. <TT>g</TT> is decreased by <TT>1</TT>, then the value of <TT>g</TT> gets assigned to 
                                <TT>i</TT>. Before this statement is evaluated, <TT>g</TT> is <TT>0</TT>. After decrementing, <TT>g</TT> holds the value 
                                of <TT>-1</TT>, which gets assigned to <TT>i</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                          <TR BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" VALIGN="TOP"> 
                            <TD ALIGN="LEFT"> 
                              <P><TT>j = ++h; </TT></P>
                            </TD>
                            <TD BGCOLOR="#F0FFFF" COLSPAN="3"> 
                              <P>Pre incrementing takes place here. <TT>h</TT> is increased by <TT>1</TT>, then the value of <TT>h</TT> gets assigned to 
                                <TT>j</TT>. Before this statement is evaluated, <TT>h</TT> is <TT>1</TT>. After incrementing, <TT>h</TT> holds the value 
                                of <TT>2</TT>, which gets assigned to <TT>j</TT>. </P>
                              <P>Final values: <TT>f</TT>: <TT>0</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;g</TT>: <TT>-1</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;h</TT>: <TT>2</TT>, <TT>&nbsp;i</TT>: <TT>-1</TT>, 
                                <TT>&nbsp;j</TT>: <TT>2</TT>. </P>
                            </TD>
                          </TR>
                        </TABLE>
                      </TD>
                    </TR>
