<div class="Section1">
<h1>Cosmic Microwave Background Polarization Mission</h1>
<p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
<div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
<p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; "><span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">
   &nbsp;</span>(1) What are the Level 1 and Level 2 science requirements 
for  the proposed mission, and how do these motivate the candidate technologies
  which will be investigated?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>
   Explain the connections between the science requirements and the engineering
  requirements on those technologies.</p>
</div>
<p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal">Level 1 science requirements:<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">
   &nbsp; </span><br>
</p>
<blockquote>To detect or set a meaningful upper limit on B-modes of polarization. 
   <br>
  <ul>
    <li>      This requires a sensitive, systematic-free map of the polarization 
 of the Cosmic Microwave Background, with astrophysical foregrounds subtracted. 
Requires all-sky maps in the frequency range approximately  50-500 GHz.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
    <li>Best candidate technology at present is large arrays of  polarization-sensitive
bolometric detectors on a cold 2m telescope.</li>
    <li>To get low systematic levels and to remove foregrounds  sets very
strong requirements on *all* aspects of the mission technology,  not just
the detectors.</li>
  </ul>
  </blockquote>
  <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
  <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
  <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (2) What are the significant technological challenges for these technologies 
 --- i.e., which technical capabilities have not already been demonstrated?</p>
  </div>
  <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
  <ul style="margin-top: 0in; " type="Disc">
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level1 lfo3;tab-stops:list .5in">
   Mission-end      polarization sensitivity of<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
   &nbsp;      </span>&lt; 0.25 uK per map pixel, per frequency and per Stokes 
 parameter,      requiring:</li>
    <ul style="margin-top: 0in; " type="Circle">
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   cold       optics (including the telescope) and excellent control of stray-light 
 so       that the detector sensitivity is limited only by the photon noise 
 of the       CMB itself.</li>
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   Significant       increases in the size of focal plane arrays (The Planck 
 HFI has 4 pixels       at each frequency, CMBPol will need &gt; 2000)</li>
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   A       way in which to couple such a large array optically to the telescope 
 i.e.       a low-distortion field of view of<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
   &nbsp;       </span>at least 5 degrees is required.</li>
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   Corresponding       reductions in the power requirements of associated 
readout  electronics.</li>
    </ul>
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level1 lfo3;tab-stops:list .5in">
   Either      that levels of systematic polarization are well below the
final        sensitivity, or that systematics can be subtracted to acceptable
levels.<br>
         Requires: </li>
    <ul style="margin-top: 0in; " type="Circle">
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   A       good understanding of the instrumental polarization</li>
      <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo3;tab-stops:list 1.0in">
   Some       means by which to modulate the polarization of the sky with 
respect  to       the instrument</li>
    </ul>
    <li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level1 lfo3;tab-stops:list .5in">
   A      robust cooling chain that can cool both the optics and the detectors, 
 and      maintain adequate temperature stability in *all* components</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
  <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
  <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (3) For each technological challenge, what are the metrics by which readiness
for launch is measured --- i.e., aperture, sensitivity, etc. and what is
the technical readiness currently of these technology developments?</p>
  </div>
  <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
  <p class="MsoNormal">Sensitivity and systematics:</p>
  <blockquote>
    <ul>
      <li><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span>will be hard  to measure
on the ground, especially systematics</li>
      <li><span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;</span><span style="mso-spacerun: yes"></span>
The next generation  of bolometric detectors is currently in the design phase.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">
   &nbsp; </span>There are plans to test these technologies on ground-based
 CMB experiments within the next 3-4 years.</li>
    </ul>
    </blockquote>
    <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]--><!--[endif]--></p>
    <p class="MsoNormal">Polarization modulation:</p>
    <blockquote>
      <ul>
        <li><span style="mso-spacerun: yes"></span>Both methods being used
or developed right now -- cold waveplates,  Faraday rotators -- are going
to be difficult to use.</li>
      </ul>
      </blockquote>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
      <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (4) What kinds of demonstrations are required to validate technical readiness?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
&nbsp; </span>Will ground  testing be sufficient, or are there technical
capabilities which can only be demonstrated in space?</p>
      </div>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p>
Ground testing through existing and planned CMB polarization  experiments
*is essential* to demonstrate the technical readiness of new detector and
readout technologies, measurements strategies and control of systematics.</p>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
      <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (5) What is status of each metric --- i.e., compare the current capability
with the required capability?</p>
      </div>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p>
Answered in (3)</p>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
      <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (6) Is the required capability a reasonable extension of the current capability
or does it require a significant advancement or new approach?</p>
      </div>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]--><!--[endif]--></p>
      <ul>
        <li>Prototypes of arrays are in the design phase.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">
   &nbsp; </span>Continued support essential for technology demonstrations</li>
        <li><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p>
Polarization modulation is a crucial issue.</li>
        <li><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p>
Suggested new approach:<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">   &nbsp; </span>Extensive
testing of new technology and methods by university-based  scientific groups
(as outlined in 4) on ground or balloon-based platforms  *before* the AO
will be extremely cost-effective.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]-->&nbsp;<!--[endif]--><o:p></o:p></p>
      <div style="border-top-width: 0.5pt; border-top-style: solid; border-top-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-right-width: 0.5pt; border-right-style: solid; border-right-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-bottom-width: 0.5pt; border-bottom-style: solid; border-bottom-color: rgb(0,0,57); border-left-width: 0.5pt; border-left-style: solid; border-left-color: rgb(0,0,57); padding-top: 1pt; padding-right: 4pt; padding-bottom: 1pt; padding-left: 4pt; ">
      <p class="MsoNormal" style="border-top-width: medium; border-top-style: none; border-top-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-right-width: medium; border-right-style: none; border-right-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-bottom-width: medium; border-bottom-style: none; border-bottom-color: -moz-use-text-color; border-left-width: medium; border-left-style: none; border-left-color: -moz-use-text-color; padding-top: 0in; padding-right: 0in; padding-bottom: 0in; padding-left: 0in; ">
   (7) Is the specific technological challenge being addressed for other
applications?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>Identify technical 
 synergies with other NASA programs if they exist.</p>
      </div>
      <p class="MsoNormal"><!--[if !supportEmptyParas]--><!--[endif]--></p>
      <ul>
        <li>Likely sensors, TES devices, and readout electronics   candidates,
SQUID arrays, under development for Con-X</li>
        <li>Likewise cooler requirements are similar to Con-X</li>
      </ul>
