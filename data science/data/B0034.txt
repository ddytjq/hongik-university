<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><u>The Mini Cash Individual Savings
Account &#150; Tax Free Savings Scheme</u></font></b></p>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><strong><em>Who can open an Individual
Savings Account ?</em></strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">If you are a UK resident aged 18 or over you are able to
  open an Individual Savings Account (ISA) and receive an attractive rate of interest tax
  free. </font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>What can be saved in an
ISA?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">There are three types of account:</font></p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Maxi ISA</font></p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Mini ISA</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; TESSA only
  ISA</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">With a Maxi and Mini ISA, there are three elements:</font></p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2"><strong>Cash</strong> - this means saving your money in
  a straightforward deposit type account offered by building societies and banks.</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2"><strong>Stocks and Shares </strong>- this means saving
  your money in longer-term investments that are linked to the stock market.</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2"><strong>Life Insurance</strong> - this means saving your
  money in life insurance products.</font></p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">A TESSA Only ISA allows you to invest all or part of the
  capital from the proceeds of a TESSA maturity. </font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">The rules of the scheme allow you to subscribe to one
  Maxi ISA or one of each type of Mini ISA in a tax year. In addition, you can subscribe to
  one TESSA Only ISA.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>What ISA schemes does
Saffron Walden Herts &amp; Essex Building Society Offer?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">The Society offers a Mini ISA with a cash component only
  &#150; a Mini Cash ISA. We also offer a TESSA Only ISA for the proceeds from a maturing
  TESSA (see TESSA Only ISA for further details).</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>How much can I invest in
the Society&#146;s Mini Cash ISA?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">You only need £1 to open a Mini Cash ISA and you can
  invest up to £3,000 in each tax year.&nbsp; If you do not subscribe the maximum in any
  tax year, the balance cannot be used in another tax year.</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">You can also invest all or part of the capital from a
  matured TESSA within 6 months of the maturity date. These amounts invested do not count
  towards the ISA subscription limits.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>How and when is interest
paid?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">Your money earns interest from the day after you invest
  in your Mini Cash ISA until the day you withdraw it. The interest is worked out every day
  on the amount you have in the account. (Please see the Society's current Interest Rate
  leaflet).&nbsp; Interest is paid on the 5<sup>th</sup> April each year and we can add this
  money to your account or transfer it to another of your accounts.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>How and when can I
withdraw my money?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">You can gain access to your money penalty free by giving
  us 90 days written notice. Or you can withdraw money straight away, but you will lose 90
  days interest on the amount you take out, even if the account has not been open for such a
  period. All withdrawals under the ISA rules are tax-free. Please note once the
  subscription limit has been reached, no further deposits can be made, regardless of the
  amount of withdrawals.</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">On visiting one of our branches, you can withdraw up to
  £300 in cash and larger amounts by cheque.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>If cash is withdrawn,
can it be paid back in at a later date ?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">Not if it would entail exceeding your annual investment
  maximum of £3,000 in each tax year.&nbsp; For example if you invested £2,700 in the
  current tax year and you withdrew £500, you could not put back the £500 in the same tax
  year - but could pay in up to £300.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><strong><em>Can I transfer my Mini
Cash ISA to another ISA Manager?</em></strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">You are able to transfer your current tax year's
  subscriptions in whole and previous tax years' investments in whole or in part to another
  ISA Manager subject to any conditions imposed by the new ISA Manager.&nbsp; You can make a
  transfer penalty-free by giving us 90 days written notice.&nbsp; Or you can transfer money
  straight away but you will lose 90 days interest on the amount transferred.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>Can I close my Mini Cash
ISA?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">The account can be closed at any time provided you give
  us 90 days notice.&nbsp; If you wish to close the account without notice there will be a
  penalty of 90 days interest.&nbsp; However, if you decide to close our Mini Cash ISA you
  are not allowed to open another ISA (of the same component) in the same tax year.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>What if I decide the
account is not right for me ?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">Tell us within 14 days of opening the new account if it
  does not meet your needs and you can have your money back or switch to another account
  without losing interest. You will also be able to open another ISA in the same tax year if
  you wish.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<u>

<p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"></u><font size="2" color="#FF0000"><em><strong>Does the Mini Cash ISA
meet the CAT standards?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY"><font size="2">The Society's scheme meets the code of voluntary
  standards prescribed by HM Treasury known as CAT standards, with the exception of the
  requirement for 90 days notice to be given for penalty free withdrawals.</font></p>
  <p ALIGN="JUSTIFY">&nbsp;</p>
</blockquote>
