    <td rowspan="2">

      <FONT FACE="Verdana" SIZE="+1" COLOR="#FFCC00" ></FONT></td>
    <td rowspan="14"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00">One 
        of the difficulties in diagnostic parasitology is distinguishing parasitic 
        organisms from other elements in feces and other body fluids. <br>
        The most difficult specimen to differentiate parasites from artifacts 
        in is feces. Fecal specimens consist of many components such as: </font></p>
      <FONT FACE="Verdana" COLOR="#FFCC00"> 
      <ul>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">food residue </font>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">products of digestion 
          </font>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">epithelial cells, 
          leucocytes, erythrocytes</font>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">mucus </font>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">microorganisms 
          such as yeast and bacteria </font>
      </ul>
      </font> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00"> Due 
        to the ratio of fecal debris to parasites, it is not surprising that these 
        artifacts may be incorrectly identified as trophozoites, eggs, cysts and 
        larvae.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00">The 
        purpose of this site is to teach microscopists morphological criteria 
        required to differentiate clinically important parasites from artifact 
        material regularly seen in fecal samples and specimens from other body 
        sites.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00">The 
        goal of this site is not to replace practical training of staff in this 
        specialized area. This site is a supplementary source of reference material 
        that is available to help advanced and beginner microscopists minimize 
        identification errors by being better able to differentiate &quot;facts 
        from artifacts.&quot;</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00">Fecal 
        artifacts illustrated are:</font></p>
      <ul>
        <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#FFCC00">Human 
          cells/cell derived materials</font> 
          <ul>
            <ul>
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Charcot-Leyden 
                crystals</font></li>
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Macrophages</font></li>
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Polymorphonuclear 
                leucocytes</font></li>
              <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Red blood 
                cells<br>
                <br>
                </font></li>
            </ul>
          </ul>
        </li>
        <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Non-human elements 
          </font></li>
        <ul>
          <ul>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Algae</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Fungal conidia</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Mushroom spores</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Plant cells</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Root hairs</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Pollen grains</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Spore</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Vegetable spiral</font></li>
            <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Yeast cell</font></li>
          </ul>
        </ul>
      </ul>
    </td>
