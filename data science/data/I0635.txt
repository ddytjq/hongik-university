    <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 00; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 4" align="left"><font size="7" color="#FF0000"><img border="1" src="1978.gif" width="100" height="159" align="left" hspace="20" vspace="20"></font><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>As
  in Germany four years earlier, we failed to reach the finals.&nbsp; Qualifying
  group 2 was always going to be a two-horse race as both England and Italy beat
  Finland and Luxembourg in their home and away games.&nbsp; After losing 2-0 in
  Rome, the all important game at Wembley was a &quot;must win&quot; for the
  boys in white.&nbsp; Goals from Keegan and Brooking gave us a 2-0 win but that
  was never going to be enough because of our goal difference.&nbsp; Whilst the
  Italians trounced Finland 6-1 in their home game, England could only win 2-1
  and in the end, we failed to top the group by only 3 goals.&nbsp; Once more,
  the Sweaty Socks were Britain's sole entrants in the finals but embarrassed
  themselves by being outclassed by Iran, and only with the help of an own goal
    did they manage to draw 1-1.</b></font></p>
