        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Oyster River's <b>Jason 
          Beaulieu</b> defense of his Canadain IMCA Modified Championship got 
          of to crashing start Saturday night at Victoria's Western Speedway. 
          Beaulieu was involved in a 5 car mishap during the 50 lap main event 
          which knocked him out of his first race of the 2002 season. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Two days later, 
          with some major repairs behind him, Beaulieu returned to Victoria Monday 
          afternoon and showed he was ready to defend his title and go after his 
          3rd straight Canadian championship by collecting fast time and winning 
          the 25 lap main event to come away the early season Western Speedway 
          IMCA points leader. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Saturday night, 
          after setting fast time and placing third in the heat race, Beaulieu 
          found himself 4th in the main event on the 25th lap and quickly moving 
          in on the leaders when leader <b>Brad Purdy</b> from Port Hardy blew 
          his transmission which locked up his car, putting him sideways and collecting 
          2nd and 3rd place drivers <b>Graham Cooke</b> and <b>Lawerence O'Connor</b> 
          leaving Beaulieu nowhere to go, putting him into a spin which left him 
          backwards into the wall. After being narrowly missed by a two other 
          cars, Victoria's <b>Tony Johnson</b> opted to lock up his brakes instead 
          of trying to avoid Beaulieu and in turn ran into Beaulieu's car, sending 
          his car up and over the front end forcing both drivers to watch the 
          remaining laps from the pit area. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Monday afternoon 
          saw Beaulieu go out and set fast time and place third in the heat race, 
          repeating Saturday night's performance up until the main event. This 
          time Beaulieu made sure to get in front quickly, as by the 10th lap 
          he found himself in the lead which he wouldn't give up, winning the 
          race and collecting the needed 40 points. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">To win the Canadian 
          IMCA Championship, the driver's best 12 race results over the whole 
          season are tabulated at end of the season and the driver with the best 
          12 races will be the champion. With 40 points the most a driver can 
          get, Beaulieu will use Monday's win as one of his results while Saturday's 
          DNF will force him to run 13 races this season instead of the planned 
          12. This total could change if any more bad results come his way. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This weekend (May 
          25th) Beaulieu and the rest of the IMCA Modifieds will be at Saratoga 
          Speedway, joining brother Martin and the Tyee Chev Olds Stock cars and 
          the Bomber cars for a full evening of high caliber auto racing.</font></p>

        <p> <font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Wild Thing Rocks 
          Saratoga Speedway</b><br>
          <img src="wildthing.jpg" width="300" height="208" align="left" vspace="5" hspace="5">Over 
          4,000 race fans rocked Saratoga Speedway over two days that featured 
          a highlight-film <b>Monster Truck</b> show Saturday night, along with 
          two days of intense Magic 98.9 Crash to Pass racing and a trophy dash 
          finish to Saturday night's Tyee Chev Olds Stock Car main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Alberta's <b>Marvin 
          Anderson</b> pulled his Monster Truck <b>Wild Thing</b> onto the Black 
          Creek oval and put on the best Monster Truck show since Super Pete back 
          in the 80's, and with his performance he was rewarded with what was 
          without a doubt the biggest pop from a crowd ever heard at the speedway. 
          He not only destroyed every car in his path, he also showed off the 
          truck's power with a full frontstretch wheelie. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><img src="stockwinner.jpg" width="225" height="119" align="right" vspace="5" hspace="5">With 
          a huge crowd of over 2500 on the Tim Horton sponsored weekend the Stock 
          Cars put on a show that had race fans only using the edge of their seats 
          in the 30 lap main event. Parksville's <b>Brad McLean</b> became the 
          5th winner of a main event this season in the first 5 races. With a 
          glorious opportunity to showcase their class, the Stock's main event 
          was all it could be, with each driver pushing their driving abilites 
          to the limit in producing the season's best main event which ended the 
          same way as a trophy dash start - with four cars side by side at the 
          checkered flag. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Starting on the 
          outside pole in the 12 car race, McLean jumped to a first lap lead which 
          would never be anymore than a couple of car lengths as cars battled 
          right behind him in attempts to challenge him for the race lead. First 
          to try was <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> who, along with <b>Don Hallgarth</b> 
          and <b>David Mainse</b>, were right on McLean's bumper waiting for a 
          miscue by the 2001 Bomber points champion who knew that a slip-up would 
          cost him the lead and the race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">After Beaulieu got 
          stuck behind McLean, Hallgarth, then Mainse got around Beaulieu with 
          Hallgarth then getting stuck on the inside with Mainse now taking his 
          turn to challenge McLean. He too would come up short after an exciting 
          5 lap challenge on the outside, with Hallgarth finishing 3rd right on 
          McLean's bumper and Beaulieu 4th on the outside of Hallgarth. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Stock car racing 
          portion of the night's action kicked off with two fast dashes with Courtenay's 
          <b>Mike Meeres</b> grabbing the B dash win, followed by an A dash win 
          for Campbell River's <b>Ray Anderosov</b> - with those two wins it marked 
          the first time in 5 races that <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> didn't capture 
          a trophy dash. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">McLean, in the car 
          that <b>Bruce Nelson</b> won the 1999 season points championship with, 
          has gotten faster each week as was evident in the main event which was 
          his second win of the night after winning the B heat. Hallgarth took 
          the win in the fast A heat.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Following the night's 
          racing Mainse, a three time speedway points champion, saw his point 
          lead increase to 54 points up on Beaulieu whose is 90 points ahead of 
          Hallgarth who is now 136 points ahead of Anderosov for third while Dawson 
          is 33 behind Anderosov in 5th. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><img src="ctopwinner.jpg" width="225" height="144" align="left" vspace="5" hspace="5">Going 
          into the two day weekend Nanaimo's <b>Jimmy Joyce</b> was 1 point behind 
          fellow Nanaimo driver <b>Mark Haslam</b> in the Crash to Pass points 
          race and after a big win in Saturday's main event and a 3rd place finish 
          in Sunday's main, along with two A heat wins, he nows finds himself 
          as the new point leader .</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The two days of 
          racing was a tame affair compared to most races as track offcials attemped 
          to curtail some of the hard hitting that was destroying some cars. They 
          decided to enforce the hit to pass rule where drivers were to only hit 
          once while passing, elimnating some of the unnecessary crashing which 
          made for a bigger field of cars for each race.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The double race 
          weekend for the Magic 98.9 sponsored class opened Saturday night with 
          two non-contact dash wins going to Nanaimo's <b>Dave Starrie</b> and 
          Campbell River's <b>David Crowhurst,</b> while heat race wins went to 
          Starrie and Joyce. Sunday, before 1500 race fans on a rain dampened 
          track, Victoria's <b>Mike Noyce</b> took home the main event win while 
          other race wins went to <b>Jeff May</b>, Noyce and Joyce in the trophy 
          dashes, while Starrie captured another weekend win in the B heat. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><img src="bomberwinner.jpg" width="225" height="127" align="right" vspace="5" hspace="5">Sunday 
          night's showers forced track officals track to cancel the Bomber points 
          racing portion of the program, allowing 15 Bombers to race on the wet 
          track for fun. By the time the main event came around the track was 
          dry enough for the drivers to race at full speed, making for a competitive 
          25 lap race won by Campbell River's <b>Darren Larsen</b>. The wet track 
          and the two Larsen brothers have gone hand in hand this season. Just 
          like on opening day when the Bombers also ran on a wet track, both drivers 
          came away with a couple of wins on Sunday night when Darren also won 
          the B heat while <b>Darrell Larsen</b> won the C dash and B heat before 
          slamming hard into the wall while leading the main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Black Creek's <b>Brian 
          Sullivan</b> made his first appearance of the season in his #12 Ford 
          a winning one in the A dash, while Quadra's <b>Little Richard Olsen</b> 
          took the B dash honors. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The two days also 
          featured the Dwarf and Figure 8 cars and both held their own in entertaining 
          the big crowds, with fast action in the small Dwarf cars and some crashing 
          and bashing in the Figure 8 class.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><img src="dwarfwinner.jpg" width="225" height="157" align="left" vspace="5" hspace="5">The 
          7 car Dwarf field ran six races over the two days in their scaled down 
          race cars, and their speed and appearance made them enjoyable to watch 
          as their races lacked no sense of holding back even when the track was 
          slick on Sunday night. Dwarf car race winners included <b>Grant Able</b> 
          the trophy dash and main event Saturday night with the heat race win 
          going to <b>John Walker</b>. Sunday night, Nanaimo's <b>Barry Anderson</b> 
          took all three race wins.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><img src="mf8winner.jpg" width="225" height="148" align="right" vspace="5" hspace="5">A 
          six car Figure 8 field was on hand making for a weekend of bumper car 
          type action with cars bumping and spinning each other to the enjoyment 
          of the big crowds. Four different drivers won the six races with every 
          car making it through the weekend still running. Saturday's race winners 
          included <b>Dustin Purden</b> in the trophy dash and Duncan's <b>Rick 
          James</b> took the heat and main event, while Sunday night's winners 
          included <b>Chris Stoddard</b> in the trophy dash, <b>Tyler Clough</b> 
          the heat race and James his 2nd main event win. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Before Saturday 
          night's Fiqure 8 racing, crash-to-pass racer <b>Jeff May</b> presented 
          his mom <b>Lyn May</b> with a late Mothers Day present - her own Figure 
          8 car that she knew nothing about! She had said she wouldn't mind trying 
          Figure 8 racing, then went out and raced all weekend ending with a third 
          place finish in Sunday's main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This Saturday night 
          the IMCA Modifides featuring Oyster River's <b>Jason Beaulieu</b> the 
          defending two time Canadain IMCA champion and they will be joined by 
          the Stock and Bomber cars with all the action set for 7pm. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Guest talk.........Jason 
          Beaulieu's defense of his Canadain IMCA points championship got of to 
          a crashing start as he was elimated from the main event after being 
          in the middle of a five car mishap Saturday night in Victoria.Earlier 
          Beaulieu posted fast time and 3rd in the heat and was on his way to 
          a 40 point night before the main event wreck.....Looks like <b>Marvin 
          Anderson</b> and his <b>Monster Trucks</b> will be back in 2003 and 
          possibly with his newsest truck a 1961 Firetruck that he is turning 
          into a 26 seat ride truck........Guest driver of the week goes to Monster 
          truck driver Anderson who not only entertained the speedway's big crowds, 
          but gave my son <b>Ryan</b> the brithday present of a lifetime with 
          a ride with myself in the cab of the ride truck Sunday morning. Marvin 
          you're many kid's real Superman!!! </font></p>
