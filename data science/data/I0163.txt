<td class="FansCustom3Body"><DIV style="TEXT-ALIGN: center">EXTRACTS FROM REGULATIONS<BR><DIV style="TEXT-ALIGN: center">SECTION 2: MEMBERSHIP</DIV></DIV><BR><STRONG>3&nbsp;THE LEAGUE</STRONG><BR><STRONG><BR>3.1</STRONG>&nbsp;Membership of The League shall constitute an agreement between The League and each Club to be bound by and comply with:<BR>3.1.1&nbsp;&nbsp;these Regulations and the Articles of Association;<BR>3.1.2&nbsp;&nbsp;the Football Association Rules;<BR>3.1.3&nbsp;&nbsp;the Laws of the Game;<BR>3.1.4&nbsp;&nbsp;the Rules and Regulations of any body of which The League is a member;<BR>3.1.5&nbsp;the terms of The Tripartite Agreement insofar as such terms apply to the members of The League for that Season;<BR>3.1.6&nbsp;&nbsp;the terms of any agreement entered into by The League; and<BR>3.1.7&nbsp;the terms of any Cup or other competitions conducted or controlled by The League.<BR>each of the aforesaid as altered, revoked or added to from time to time.<BR><BR><STRONG>3.2</STRONG>&nbsp;Further, for the avoidance of doubt, every Club, by becoming and remaining a member of The League, agrees to compete in the Championship, The Football League Cup, The Football Association Challenge Cup and all other competitions conducted or controlled by The League and in which it is eligible to compete.<BR><BR><STRONG>3.3</STRONG>&nbsp;Such agreement to have effect from the date of the Club's admission to The League and terminate upon such Club ceasing to be a member (but without prejudice to any rights or claims which may have arisen or arise in respect of acts or omissions prior to such date).<BR><BR><STRONG>4&nbsp;RESIGNATION FROM MEMBERSHIP<BR></STRONG><BR><STRONG>4.1</STRONG>&nbsp;A Club may only resign from membership of The League at the end of a Season.&nbsp; Any Club wishing to resign from membership of The League otherwise than by reason of promotion to The Premier League or relegation to The Football Conference shall give provisional notice of its intention to do so by 1 January in the year concerned and shall confirm its resignation by 1 April in that year.<BR><BR><STRONG>4.2</STRONG>&nbsp;Any Club which is in breach of the provisions of this Regulation shall on demand indemnify The League against all losses, damages, liabilities, costs or expenses suffered or incurred by The League which result directly or indirectly from such breach including, but without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing, any loss of income or profits from any sponsorship or other commercial agreement or arrangement.<BR>&nbsp;<BR><DIV style="TEXT-ALIGN: center">SECTION 3: THE LEAGUE<BR></DIV><STRONG>5&nbsp;DIVISIONAL STRUCTURE OF THE LEAGUE CHAMPIONSHIP</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>5.1</STRONG>&nbsp;Not more than 72 Clubs shall compete in the League Championship.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The Clubs from time to time in membership of The League shall be grouped into three divisions, to be known as the First, Second and Third Divisions respectively.&nbsp; The First and Second Divisions shall each consist of 24 Clubs.&nbsp; The Third Division shall comprise the remainder of the Clubs then in membership of The League.&nbsp; Subject to the provisions of the Articles of Association and these Regulations, The League may from time to time and upon such terms and conditions as it may think fit admit any Association Football club as a member or expel or accept the retirement of any member from The League provided always that no member may be expelled without the sanction of a special resolution passed at an Annual or Extraordinary General Meeting of The League.&nbsp; Any casual vacancy occurring in the membership of The League may be filled by the Board.<BR><BR><STRONG>5.2</STRONG>&nbsp;The numbers of Clubs in each Division can only be changed from 24 with the agreement of any other Division affected by such changes.<BR><BR><STRONG>6&nbsp;COMPETITIONS ORGANISED BY THE LEAGUE</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>6.1</STRONG>&nbsp;The League shall conduct and in every respect control each Season Football Competitions, being both League and Cup Competitions, to be called The League Championship, The Football League Cup, and such other Competitions as the Board may from time to time determine and at all times safeguard the interest of the Clubs.&nbsp; <BR>For the avoidance of doubt, such competitions may include small sided competitions, or such other competitions, being variants of association football, as the Board may from time to time determine.<BR><BR><STRONG>6.2</STRONG>&nbsp;All Clubs and the members from time to time of The Premier League shall take part in The Football League Cup.&nbsp; All Clubs that are eligible shall take part in such other Competitions as the Board may from time to time determine each Season.&nbsp; Whenever requested by the Board all Clubs shall take part in any small sided games or other Competitions organised by The League and shall observe the Rules of the relevant competition.<BR><BR><STRONG>6.3</STRONG>&nbsp;The control of all competitions that the Board may decide to promote shall be vested in the Executive who shall in all ways act as the Management Committee of the Competition and determine the Rules of the Competition.<BR><BR><STRONG>6.4</STRONG>&nbsp;The Board shall have power to arrange Inter&#64979;League or other Match or Matches and to authorise or arrange or control additional Competitions and/or Matches in which Clubs take part as they in their discretion may deem expedient always subject to The Football Association Regulations for the Sanction and Control of Leagues and Competitions with the exception of Football Association and County Football Association Competitions.&nbsp; For any of the purposes aforesaid they may select such of the grounds and Registered Players of the Clubs upon such terms and conditions as they may think fit.&nbsp; Any Club or Registered Player failing to comply with the directions of the Board under this Regulation shall be guilty of misconduct.<BR><BR><STRONG>7&nbsp;APPLICATION FOR AND RELEGATION FROM MEMBERSHIP</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>7.1</STRONG>&nbsp;Any Association Football club seeking to be admitted to membership of The League pursuant to the provisions of Regulation 11.1.3 (automatic election for Champion Club of The Football Conference) shall apply via The Football Conference to the Secretary as soon as it secures the Championship of The Football Conference.<BR><BR><STRONG>7.2</STRONG>&nbsp;At the end of each Season the Club occupying the bottom place of the Third Division of the League Championship shall, in accordance with the Articles of Association and these Regulations, cease to be a member of The League and join The Football Conference, save as otherwise provided in Regulations 10 and 11.<BR><BR><STRONG>7.3</STRONG>&nbsp;At the end of each Season Clubs occupying the bottom three places in The Premier League shall, in accordance with The Premier League's Articles of Association, Rules and Regulations, be admitted to membership of The League in accordance with the provisions of the Articles of Association and these Regulations.&nbsp; The two Clubs which finish in the highest positions in the First Division of The League Championship, together with the Club winning the First Division Play&#64979;Off of The League Championship in accordance with Regulation 10, in accordance with the provisions of the Articles of Association and these Regulations cease to be members of The League and join The Premier League.<BR><BR><STRONG>8&nbsp;DIVISIONAL CRITERIA</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>8.1</STRONG>&nbsp;The Clubs have adopted the membership criteria and the timescales for both existing Clubs and those who become members of The League in the future, as set out in Appendix&nbsp; I (Criteria), subject to any dispensations granted by the Board.<BR><BR><STRONG>8.2</STRONG>&nbsp;A Club which does not satisfy the appropriate criteria set out in Part 1 (capacity and seating) by the due date may be expelled from membership of The League.&nbsp; The Board will convene a general meeting within 60 days of the due date and will propose an ordinary resolution for the expulsion of that Club.&nbsp; If the resolution is passed the Club shall cease to be a member of the League on date specified in the resolution.&nbsp; In either passing or not passing the resolution the Clubs may impose conditions on such Club for continued membership of The League.<BR><BR><STRONG>8.3</STRONG>&nbsp;A Club which does not satisfy the appropriate criteria set out in Part 2 (floodlighting) by the due date will be relegated to a lower Division with floodlighting criteria which the Club satisfies.&nbsp; A Club which does not satisfy the Third Division Criteria may be relegated to The Football Conference.&nbsp; The Board will convene a general meeting within 60 days of the due date and will propose an ordinary resolution for the relegation of such Club to The Football Conference.&nbsp; In either passing or not passing the resolution the Clubs may impose conditions on such Club for continued membership of The League. <BR><BR><STRONG>8.4</STRONG>&nbsp;A Club which does not satisfy the appropriate criteria set out in Part 3 by the due date, will be subject to fixed fines in accordance with Regulation 71.3.<BR><BR><STRONG>9&nbsp;METHOD OF DETERMINING LEAGUE POSITIONS</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>9.1</STRONG>&nbsp;At the end of each Season the Club in each Division scoring the highest number of points shall be declared the Champion Club of that Division.&nbsp;&nbsp; Three points shall be awarded for each League Match won.&nbsp; One point will be awarded to each Club for a drawn match.&nbsp; If two or more Clubs have scored the same number of points their position in the Division shall be determined on goal difference, that is to say, the difference between the total number of goals scored by and against a Club in League Matches in that Season, and the higher or highest placed Club shall be the Club with the larger or largest goal difference.&nbsp; If any two or more Clubs have scored the same number of points and have the same goal difference the higher or highest placed Club shall be the Club having scored the most goals in League Matches in that Season.&nbsp; If two or more Clubs have the same number of points, goal difference and goals scored the highest placed Club shall be determined by the respective League records against each other, taking into account in order of precedence, points gained, goal difference and goals scored.&nbsp; If the above procedures do not separate the Clubs, then the Clubs concerned shall play off a deciding League Match or Matches under arrangements approved by the Board on a neutral ground.&nbsp; The net gate money after deducting the usual match expenses shall be equally divided between the two competing Clubs.&nbsp; Any Club staging such a play off match shall be entitled to charge as an expense against the receipts of the match 10% of the gross gate receipts after deduction of Value Added Tax.&nbsp; In the event of such a match being arranged by the Board to be played on the ground of a Club which is not a member of The League, then a similar charge as an expense against the receipts of the match shall be paid to The League who shall otherwise be responsible for the financial arrangements made with the Club staging the match.&nbsp; These provisions shall determine the ranking of Clubs in their respective Divisions.<BR><BR><STRONG>10&nbsp;PROMOTION, RELEGATION AND PLAY-OFFS</STRONG><BR><BR><STRONG>10.1</STRONG>&nbsp;The following provisions shall have effect in relation to the admission of new members of The League and promotion, relegation and retirement:&#64979;<BR>10.1.1&nbsp;Promotion and relegation between The Premier League and Division One <BR>10.1.1.1&nbsp;At the end of each Season, The League shall accept into membership the three Clubs finishing in the lowest three places in The Premier League, in accordance with the Articles of Association, rules and regulations of The Premier League.<BR>10.1.1.2&nbsp;At the end of each Season, the two Clubs finishing in the highest positions in the First Division of The League shall be promoted to The Premier League in accordance with the Articles of Association, rules and regulations of The Premier League.&nbsp; The four next highest Clubs in the First Division shall take part in a Play-Off Competition.&nbsp; The winner of the Play-Off Competition shall also be promoted to The Premier League upon the same terms.<BR>10.1.1.3&nbsp;The number of Clubs to be promoted and relegated between The Premier League and the First Division shall be determined by agreement between The Premier League and the First Division.<BR>10.1.2&nbsp;Promotion and relegation between Divisions One, Two and Three <BR>10.1.2.1&nbsp;At the end of each Season, the two Clubs finishing in
