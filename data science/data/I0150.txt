  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin: 0" align="left"><font face="BellGothic Black"><img border="0" src="1990.gif" width="100" height="159" align="left" hspace="20" vspace="20"></font><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>A&nbsp;quiet
  passage to the finals for England was much needed after a rough Euro 88 in
  Germany, where we had lost all three games.&nbsp; Qualification was earned without conceding
  a goal and scoring ten.&nbsp; Despite this, the width of the crossbar saved us in
  the final 0-0 draw away in Poland.&nbsp; We would be joined in Italy by
  Scotland.&nbsp; Our first game was against Ireland who were managed by English
  World Cup winner, Jackie Charlton and out of his squad of 22 players, Niall
  Quinn was the only one who was actually born over there, hence the nickname, England Reserves.&nbsp; The game ended up 1-1 after Lineker opened
  the scoring but to be fair, the match was dreadful.&nbsp; A change of system
  and tactics saw Bobby Robson's side outplay Holland in the next game, but
  despite two disallowed goals, we had to settle for a 0-0 draw.&nbsp; Our 1-0
  win over Egypt (Mark Wright) and the fact that every other game was a draw
  meant that we finished top of our group, whereas we progressed without losing, the Irish progressed without winning.&nbsp; Scotland didn't reach
  round two (isn't that stating the obvious??) because of defeat at the hands of
  footballing giants Costa
 Rica!!! In the second round England faced
  Belgium in a very tight contest which was heading for penalties until 30
  seconds before the end of extra time, David Platt volleyed home Gazza's free
  kick, 1-0.&nbsp; In the quarter finals we faced surprise outfit Cameroon, once
  more David Platt put us 1-0 up but the Africans fought back to lead 2-1 until
  eight minutes from time when Lineker scored from the spot. In the extra 30
  minutes Lineker again from the penalty spot scored to give us a 3-2 win and a
  semi final place against Germany in Turin.&nbsp; It was the quarter final where we
  said Goodbye (or words to that effect) to Ireland who went home without winning
  a single match - except on penalties.&nbsp; In the Semi Final, we were much
  the better side although a late Lineker goal only gave us a 1-1 draw.&nbsp; In
  extra time, Waddle hit the post and the outstanding Gazza was booked meaning
  he'd miss the final anyway, but no more goals were scored.&nbsp; Lady luck was
  not on our side and the Germans went through and went on to win the cup.</b></font>
