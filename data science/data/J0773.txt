<h1>Road Atlanta</h1>
<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>
Road Atlanta is a 12 turn road course located near Gainesville,
Georgia, 45 miles north of downtown Atlanta, just off of I-85.
</p>
<p>
A number of changes have been made to Road Atlanta's layout for the 1998
season; see their web site at
<a href="http://www.roadatlanta.com/">http://www.roadatlanta.com/</a>
for more details.
</p>

<h2><a name="NewsFlash">News Flash -- Road Atlanta Sold</a></h2>
<p>
Road Atlanta Announces Sale To Panoz Automotive November 4, 1996, 1:11 PM EST
</p>
<p>
BRASELTON, Ga., Nov. 4 /PRNewswire/ -- Road Atlanta Ltd., located in
Braselton, Georgia, announced today that it has signed a letter of
intent which provides for the sale of its 2.52 mile road racing circuit
and associated business to Panoz Automotive Development Company, also
located in Braselton.
</p>
<p>
Panoz Automotive developed and manufactures the Panoz Roadster,
described recently by Sports Car International magazine as "the most
exciting production machine on the U.S. market." The current model Panoz
Roadster is the first American-produced aluminum-intensive vehicle.
</p>
<p>
Panoz Automotive recently introduced the Esperante coupe, an
all-aluminum high-performance luxury sports car, and its Esperante GTR 1
Le Mans race cars, which are being developed in collaboration with Jack
Roush and Adrian Reynard to compete next year at Le Mans and other GTR 1
venues. Panoz Automotive will manufacture the cars at its newly expanded
Braselton facilities.
</p>
<p>
Panoz Automotive has entered into a distribution agreement with Roush
Corporation for distribution of its cars exclusively through select Ford
dealers throughout the United States. The new Panoz Automotive
manufacturing facilities are designed to produce 300 Roadsters and 700
Esperante cars per year.
</p>
<p>
Jim Kanely, president and chief executive of Road Atlanta, said, "We
are very proud of the progress we have made in returning Road Atlanta
from bankruptcy four years ago to its current position as one of the top
road racing circuits in the world. The opportunity to participate as
part of Panoz Automotive as they develop exciting high-technology sports
cars is extremely exciting to us. We have been looking since early 1996
for the right business partner with vision, an interest in the business
and the financial resources to allow us to continue our long-term
development program for Road Atlanta. The sale to Panoz Automotive
provides us with that opportunity."
</p>
<p>
Daniel Panoz, president, and Donald Panoz, chairman and C.E.O. of Panoz
Automotive, said in a joint statement: "The acquisition of Road Atlanta
is a natural fit for our company in that it will help showcase our
sports cars and our racing activities. Our cars are built for high
performance, reliability and value and they appeal to consumers who
follow motor sports." SOURCE Panoz Automotive Development Company
</p>

<h2><a name="GettingThere">Getting to Road Atlanta</a></h2>
<p>
To get to the track from Atlanta, take I-85 north to exit 49
("Hwy 53 - Braselton/Hoschton".) Turn left after taking the exit
and look for Road Atlanta on the left after about 5.5 miles.
