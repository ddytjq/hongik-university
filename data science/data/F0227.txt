<p ALIGN="CENTER"></font><font size="6"><strong>Steps to improve the Quality of your
Software</strong></font></p>
</font>

<p ALIGN="CENTER">&nbsp;</p>
<b>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000">Plan your Project well</font> </big>

<ol>
  </b></u><font size="4">
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">Plan your project thoroughly</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Write up a project definition document&nbsp; which includes
    Project Specifications , Your&nbsp; Goals and Proposals to meet them</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Draw all your forms on paper first</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">For All complicated functions :- Draw <b>flow charts</b> <b>Class
    Diagrams</b> (Booch , Rumbaugh) or write out <b>algorithms </b>on paper</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">The more time you spend on designing the less time you will spend
    on debugging</font></font><font SIZE="2"></li>
</ol>

<p></font><font size="4"><font color="#FF0000"><u><strong>Coding the project</strong></u></font>
</font><font size="5">

<ol>
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">When coding procedures (functions or Subs)
    keep them the size of the screen height so that it is easy to view the whole procedure. </font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Split large functions into smaller procedures</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Each procedure should <b>only do one task </font></li>
  </b>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Spend time to simplify your procedures (KISS Keep It Simple
    Stupid)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Include a testing stratergy in&nbsp; your plan</font></li>
  <u><b><p></font><font SIZE="2">&nbsp;</p>
  </b></u>
</ol>

<p align="left"></font><font color="#FF0000" size="4"><u><b>Programmers Comments</b></u></font><font size="5"></p>

<ol>
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">Use as much inline comments in you code to
    explain what you are doing (Remember someone will have to fix your code or make changes to
    your code your comments are all he has to help him)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Try to achieve a 10 to 20 % ratio of comments in source code</font></li>
  <b>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Use Program / Module / Class Templates</b> to document you code
    with the following information</font></li>
</ol>

<ul>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Module /Class Name</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Version number (module/Class)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Version date</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Programmer&#146;s Name</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Description of the module/class </font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Description of procedures/methods and variables/properties</font></font></li>
</ul>

<p><u><b>&nbsp;</p>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000">Naming variables and procedures carefully</font> </big>

<ol>
  </b></u><font size="3">
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Use <strong>meaningful </strong>variable and procedure <strong>names
    </strong>so as to explain what they store or do</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Don&#146;t re-use your variables in modules or procedures It is
    better to declare more variables as this can lead to bugs</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">The <b>Hungarian Naming convention</b> is most useful in
    describing what your variables or functions return </font></li>
</ol>

<blockquote>
  <p><font color="#0000FF">This can also help with elimination of bugs caused such as
  passing strings to numeric variables of procedures</font></p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#0000FF">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
By prefixing variables procedures with a few letters to describe what they return </font></p>
<b>

<p><font color="#0000FF">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
<u>Prefix</u> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Type returned</u>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <u>Example</u></font> 

<ul>
  </b>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">n
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    Integer
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    nMyvar</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">s
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    String
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    sMystring</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">v
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    Variant
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    vMyvariant</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">b
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    Boolean
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    bMyBool</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">dte
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Date
    &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
    dteMyVar</font></li>
  </font>
</ul>

<p><b><u>&nbsp;</p>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000">Version control</font></u></b> </big>

<ol>
  <font size="4">
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">Use Version control (Oops I smashed my
    program again now I cant make it work)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">There are many tool available that keep track of the versions of
    your software allowing you to cut back to a previous working version quickly. There are
    good products on the market.</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">A simple way of implementing version control is to open a folder
    with date plus 001 as name and if you make another major change that day you simply make
    another folder with name as date plus 002</font></font><font SIZE="2"></li>
  </font>
</ol>

<p><font SIZE="2">&nbsp;</p>
</font>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000"><strong><u>Control Project with a Project Planning Tool</u></strong></font>
</big>

<ol>
  <font size="3">
  <li><font color="#0000FF">In a Commercial environment you need to set milestones to
    determine when you will deliver your project</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Use Optimistic/Pessimistic/Most likely times to determine
    component delivery times (see below)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Use a Gantt Chart or a&nbsp; Pert Chart (Microsoft Project) to
    determine when you will deliver you can deliver you give each component a name and a time
    to write .</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Use your project management tool to keep track as to whether you
    are ahead or behind schedule</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">By using such a tool you can revise the component delivery times
    and so advise you customer when you will be able to deliver</font></li>
  <p><font color="#0000FF">Optimistic Pessimistic Most Likely delivery times </font></p>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">To determine how long you will take to complete a component use
    this little rule</font></li>
  <li></font><font size="3" color="#0000FF">Estimate the fastest time you can deliver (<strong>optimistic</strong>)</font><font color="#0000FF" size="4"><strong> OT</strong></font><font size="3"></li>
  <li></font><font size="3" color="#0000FF">Estimate the longest time you could deliver (<strong>Pessimistic</strong>)</font><font color="#0000FF" size="4"><strong>PT</strong></font><font size="3"></li>
  <li></font><font size="3" color="#0000FF">Estimate the <strong>most likely time</strong> to
    deliver</font><font color="#0000FF" size="4"><strong> MT</strong></font><font size="3"></li>
  <li></font><font size="3" color="#0000FF">Delivery Time=</font><font color="#0000FF" size="4"> (3*MT+OT+PT)/5</font><font SIZE="2"><b><u></li>
  </u></b></font>
</ol>
<u><b>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000">Testing Software</font> </big>

<ol>
  </b></u><font size="4">
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">Plan you testing strategy</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Try to test with <strong>&#147;Bad Data&#148;</strong> its easy to
    make something work with &quot;good data&quot;</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Performance testing to determine whether changes yielded
    improvement</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Test all values that can be passed to functions and all values
    that can be returned</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">You need to verify if the code checks user input and data from
    database</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Determine that no files are left open and all handles are released
    </font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Beta testing with selected target users trained to use the system </font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Three levels of testing</font></li>
</ol>

<ul>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">You may test all functions individually </font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">However you may also want to test how functions interact together</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Testing the whole system </font></font><font SIZE="2"></li>
</ul>

<p></font><u><b>&nbsp;</p>

<p><big><font color="#FF0000">Quality Control</font> </big>

<ol>
  </b></u><font size="4">
  <li></font><font size="3"><font color="#0000FF">Inspecting Software Documentation flowcharts</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Is the designs flow-charted efficient can improvements be made</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Check that are sufficient comments which are relevant in the code</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Checking the User Documentation, Training materials</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Running trial runs Acceptance testing of software</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Does the software meet the design specifications</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Does the work presented comply with Company&#146;s Quality
    Standards ( ISO9001)</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Make sure variables in procedures are not used for different
    purposes </font></font></li>
</ol>
<b><u>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
</u></b>

<p><big><b><u><font color="#FF0000">Project File</font></u></b> </big>

<ol>
  <font size="3">
  <li><font color="#0000FF">If you are writing an application for commercial purposes open a
    project file and put all flow diagrams Class diagrams and print outs of algorithms as well
    as a hard copy of you&#146;re the latest version of your software in it (Now you will
    appreciate the need for inline comments.</font></li>
  <li></font><font color="#0000FF" size="3">Include the Project definition document &nbsp;
    which includes the&nbsp; Project Specifications</font><font size="3"></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Print out and file your delivery project plan</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Keep minutes of all meetings with customer as the customer will
    accuse you of not delivering what he thinks he told you</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Get customer to agree with your concepts before coding the
    application</font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF">Get customer to sign off your programs and file these this
    prevents disputes</font></font></li>
  <li><font color="#0000FF" size="3">File all comments from User evaluations and your
    proprosals to meet their expectations</font></li>
