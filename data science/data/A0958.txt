<tr><td bgcolor=FFFF66><CENTER><IMG SRC="/images/mortgages/exp_head.gif" WIDTH=289 HEIGHT=98 HSPACE=0 VSPACE=0 ALT="Mortgages Explained"></CENTER><P><BR>      <FONT SIZE=2 FACE="arial,helvetica"> <B>Click <A HREF="/mortgages/mortgagetable.asp">here</A>       for a comparison of all current mortgage offers and their latest interest       rates.</B>      <P> The following glossary should help you to understand some of the more         unfamiliar terms you may come across on the site.      <P>       </FONT>       <UL>        <FONT SIZE=2 FACE="arial,helvetica">         <LI><B>ADVANCE</B><BR>          The mortgage loan.           <P>         <LI><B>APR</B><BR>          Annual Percentage Rate - a percentage calculation of the total charge           for the loan, including fees and interest.           <P>         <LI><B>CAPITAL</B><BR>          The amount that you owe, excluding interest and other charges.           <P>         <LI><B>COMPLETION</B><BR>          Either:<BR>          (1) The start of the loan.<BR>          (2) When ownership of the property is legally transferred to you.           <P>         <LI><B>CONTRACT</B><BR>          The written document between the buyer and the seller agreeing transfer           of ownership of the property.           <P>         <LI><B>CONVEYANCING</B><BR>          The legal work involved in the purchase and sale of land or the transfer           of a mortgage.           <P>         <LI><B>DEPOSIT</B><BR>          A sum paid to the seller by the buyer, on exchange of contracts, to           guarantee that the sale will be completed. The buyer may forfeit the           deposit if they do not complete.           <P>         <LI><B>DORMANT LOAN</B><BR>          The generic term for a loan where the repayment due only represents           the interest charged for the loan. This type of loan is usually repaid           upon maturity of an investment linked product. This type of loan is           also commonly referred to as an "interest only" loan.           <P>         <LI><B>ENDOWMENT MORTGAGE</B><BR>          An interest-only mortgage supported by an endowment policy.           <P>         <LI><B>ENDOWMENT POLICY</B><BR>          A combined investment and life assurance policy. This is usually taken           out with an interest-only mortgage and used as a means of repaying the           debt at the end of the mortgage term.           <P>         <LI><B>EQUITY</B><BR>          The difference between the value of your property and the amount that           you owe on your mortgage(s).           <P>         <LI><B>EXCHANGE OF CONTRACTS</B><BR>          The point when both buyer and seller are legally bound to complete the           transaction. At this point, responsibility for insuring the property           passes to the buyer.           <P>         <LI><B>FREEHOLD</B><BR>          You own the property, and the land on which it stands, if you have a           freehold for it.           <P>         <LI><B>GAZUMPING</B><BR>          The process by which a seller, who has already accepted an offer on           a property, accepts a higher offer from someone else, before contracts           have been exchanged. (Illegal in Scotland).           <P>         <LI><B>GROUND RENT</B><BR>          The annual charge payable to the freeholders of the property by the           leaseholders.           <P>         <LI><B>GUARANTOR</B><BR>          Any person who promises to pay the borrower's debt should he/she default           on payment.           <P>         <LI><B>HOME-BUYER'S REPORT</B><BR>          A report on the property prepared by a surveyor and paid for by the           buyer. Less extensive than a full structural survey.           <P>         <LI><B>INTEREST ONLY MORTGAGE</B><BR>          A mortgage where interest only is paid to the lender and capital is           repaid at the end of the term or upon the sale of the property being           mortgaged.           <P>         <LI><B>LEASEHOLD</B><BR>          The right to possession without ownership of a property for a specified           period of time, under the terms of a lease. Ultimate ownership remains           with the freeholder.           <P>         <LI><B>LIFE ASSURANCE</B><BR>          An insurance policy that pays a lump sum on death to the policyholder's           estate.           <P>         <LI><B>LOAN TO VALUE</B><BR>          The percentage of the loan against the value of the property.           <P>         <LI><B>LOCAL AUTHORITY SEARCH</B><BR>          Enquiries to the local authority regarding sewage/drainage arrangements,           plans for new road building in the area of the property and legality           of any building work previously carried out. <FONT SIZE=2 FACE="arial,helvetica">           </font>        </font>      </ul>      <UL>        <FONT SIZE=2 FACE="arial,helvetica">        <LI><B>MORTGAGE</B><BR>          A loan secured against a property.           <P>         <LI><B>MORTGAGEE</B><BR>          The lender.           <P>         <LI><B>MORTGAGOR</B><BR>          The borrower.           <P>         <LI><B>MORTGAGE TERM</B><BR>          The time over which the mortgage is to be repaid.           <P>         <LI><B>PAYMENT PROTECTION</B><BR>          Insurance which pays your mortgage repayments in the event that you           lose your income through sickness, injury or unemployment. Northern           Rock's payment protection insurance is called Payment Protection.           <P>         <LI><B>PENSION PLAN</B><BR>          An investment plan that provides an income on retirement and an optional           tax-free lump sum. This lump sum is sometimes used to repay the capital           of an interest-only/dormant mortgage.           <P>         <LI><B>PRODUCT EARLY REPAYMENT CHARGE</B><BR>          A charge payable on some loans if the loan is repaid in full or partial           lump sum repayments are made within a specified period.           <P>         <LI><B>REDEMPTION</B><BR>          When a mortgage is paid off.           <P>         <LI><B>REMORTGAGE</B><BR>          Taking out a new mortgage with another lender, on the same property,           to repay your existing lender.           <P>         <LI><B>REPAYMENT MORTGAGE</B><BR>          Where capital and interest is paid monthly until the loan is repaid.           <P>         <LI><B>RISK CHARGE</B><BR>          A one-off payment to the lender for an insurance policy when borrowing           above a certain percentage of the property value. The policy covers           the lender's risk of selling a repossessed property at a loss should           you fall into arrears. This can be added to the loan at the start of           the mortgage.           <P>         <LI><B>SEALING FEE</B><BR>          A charge paid to the lender to cover administrative costs when redemption           takes place.           <P>         <LI><B>STAMP DUTY</B><BR>          Government tax on the purchase price of a property:<BR>          1% between &#163;60,000 and &#163;250,000<BR>    3% between &#163;250,001 and &#163;500,000<BR>    4% on &#163;500,001 and above     <P>         <LI><B>STRUCTURAL SURVEY</B><BR>          A full inspection of a property by a surveyor on behalf of and paid           for by the buyer. Much more extensive than a Home-Buyer's Report.           <P>         <LI><B>SUBJECT TO CONTRACT</B><BR>          The phrase used before exchange of contracts, which allows either party           to withdraw from a proposed transaction without penalty.           <P>         <LI><B>SURVEYOR/VALUER</B><BR>          The person who carries out surveys and valuations of properties. Must           be a member of either the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors or           the Incorporated Society of Valuers and Auctioneers.           <P>         <LI><B>TITLE DEEDS/DOCUMENTS</B><BR>          The legal documents that provide proof of ownership of a property.           <P>         <LI><B>TRANSFER DEED</B><BR>          The form providing details of the transfer of ownership of a property.           These must be sent to the Land Registry for registration.           <P>         <LI><B>UNIT TRUST</B><BR>          A type of investment, which is sometimes used instead of an endowment           policy to repay the capital of an Interest-only/dormant mortgage at           the end of the mortgage term.           <P>         <LI><B>VALUATION</B><BR>          An inspection of the property by a valuer acting on behalf of the lender           to ensure its suitability as security against the mortgage.       </ul><P><CENTER>Copyright Northern Rock plc 2000</CENTER><P ALIGN=right><A HREF="#top">back to top</A></FONT></TD></TR></TABLE></BODY></HTML>
