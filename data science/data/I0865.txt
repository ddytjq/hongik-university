src="../images/stadium/04a_arw.gif"></td>									<td width="443" align="left" valign="top"><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>KEY ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES<br>										</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										The existing Wembley Stadium is an international icon and Wembley National Stadium Ltd and the World Stadium Team have gone to great lengths to replicate or interpret features of the original in the new Stadium.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>Main fa&ccedil;ade and Arch</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										The key view of the stadium for most visiting fans is the walk up Olympic Way towards the main fa&ccedil;ade and towers. We have been at pains to replicate this experience in the new Stadium.<br>										<br>										The famous twin towers have been replaced with a spectacular 133 metre high arch. This will tower over the 52 metre high stadium and provide a dramatic landmark across the city. The Wembley operations team are particularly excited about the opportunities for spectacular lighting effects that the arch provides. Early hopes of using two huge masts proved impractical for engineering reasons.<br>										<br>										The front fa&ccedil;ade still follows the model of the original with a Banqueting Hall sitting between two 'bookends'. Circulation spaces run off the side of this. In the new stadium this circulation will be by escalator, rather than staircase.<br>										<br>										The change from four masts to the single arch allows a larger concourse to be created around the stadium.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>The Sliding Roof and the Pitch</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										The sliding roof remains an integral part of the design for the new Stadium. Wembley's pitch is famously one of the finest in the world. Maintaining this quality while designing a new stadium that is considerably taller than the existing building, and placing fans closer to the pitch has been a key challenge for the design team. Options such as a palletised pitch (moving the pitch in and out of the Stadium between events) or regularly relaying the pitch were rejected as inappropriate for Wembley. Instead computer models have been made of air movement and sunlight on the existing pitch and a unique moving roof designed for the new Stadium. This will be left open between events but can be moved to line up with the touch-line within 15 minutes, ensuring every spectator is sheltered during an event.<br>										<br>										In bright sunlight the roof can be withdrawn to allow clear TV pictures. At 3pm on Cup Final day, for instance, only the two southern corner flags will be in shadow.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>The Bowl</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										Two key features of the current design are carried over. The acoustics of the new ground will take the current ground as a benchmark. Recordings taken during the 1999 FA Cup Final and models of the Stadium created by using three blasts of white noise during England v Poland earlier this year will form the basis of sophisticated computer models that will allow the design team to tune the acoustics of the new stadium.<br>										<br>										A key feature of the current stadium is that almost all spectators sit in a single bowl. This is a central feature of the new design with almost all supporters or concert goers able to share the event with 90,000 other fans.<br>										<br>										An athletics track will only be built when it is needed. This ensures that spectators for all events will sit as close to the action as possible.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>Royal Box</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										One of the most recognised features of Wembley is the presentation of trophies from the Royal Box rather than on the pitch. The new Royal Box is in the same position, in the middle of the north stand, as in the current ground. Teams will walk up to the box to collect trophies.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>The External Concourse</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										To accommodate an external concourse all around the Stadium, the new building will move 30 metres north, towards Wembley Park Station. This concourse will be able to provide food and drink to 40,000 spectators at any one time, minimising disturbance to local residents while encouraging fans to arrive early.<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>Orientation</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										The orientation of the Stadium remains east west, with the main fa&ccedil;ade pointing north down Olympic Way.<br>										<br>										<br>										</font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447"><b>WEMBLEY NEW ENGLISH NATIONAL STADIUM</b></font><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"><br>										<br>										PLANNING APPLICATION OVERVIEW<br>										</font><a href="R4929-009a.doc"><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447">Click here to download Overview Document</font></a><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"> of the Planning Application for the new stadium.<br>										<br>										TRANSPORT OVERVIEW REPORT<br>										</font><a href="transport.doc"><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#3e2447">Click here to download Overview Report</font></a><font size="2" face="Verdana" color="#231428"> for the new stadium.<br>										<br>										PLANNING BRIEF<br>										</font>
