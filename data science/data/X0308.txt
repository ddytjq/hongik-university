<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>MINNEAPOLIS&nbsp;-- Call Darian Townes a defensive enforcer. Call him the next Patrick Ewing. Or Alonzo Mourning. Or Dikembe Mutombo. Or just call him the best shot-blocker in prep basketball's Class of 2003.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Just know that he doesn't want to hear it.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I'm tired of playing defense," Townes said with a sigh this past weekend here at the Howard Pulley program's Sabes Foundation Invitational. "I tried to get more of a scoring rep this year."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The 6-foot-9 center from Alexandria, Va., averaged 13.8 points per game as a junior for Archbishop Carroll High School in Washington, D.C.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he did add some skills to his offensive repertoire.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's got nice touch," said Boo Williams, his AAU coach, "he can shoot a 15-footer, and he has a little jump hook. But he's a shot-blocking guy."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>True enough. Townes is what he is and does what he does. He alters shots. He swats shots. He erases shots.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"That's his skill," TheInsidersHoops.com national recruiting analyst Dave Telep said. "You're either a shot-blocker or you aren't. It's natural instincts, shot-blocking, and Darian has them."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>A lot of them.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I think," Townes said when asked about his tricks of the trade. "I look at the person. I take my time. Then I contest the shot. Most people throw up silly shots."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Easy to see why.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Townes broke Mourning's Virginia state record by blocking 16 shots against Chantilly in a game during his sophomore season at T.C. Williams High in Alexandria. He finished the year with a startling total of 183.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>As a junior, he showed all-around talent in one particularly strong game at the Slam Dunk to the Beach in Lewes, Del., hitting Memphis East with a 17-point, 11-rebound, 10-block triple-double in a double-overtime loss.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He has great timing," said Williams, who's been around the grassroots block enough to know what works and what doesn't. "Outside of Alonzo, he's the best shot-blocker we've ever had.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Alonzo went after more shots&nbsp;-- he was a complete animal in high school&nbsp;-- but Darian's coming along."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he's coming along in 'Zo's footsteps&nbsp;-- from Virginia to Williams' Hampton Roads-based club and on to Georgetown.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Townes sometimes played with Kansas in his March Madness video games growing up. But his choice was mostly D.C.'s Big East outpost. So it was no surprise when he committed to coach Craig Esherick and the Hoyas during his sophomore year.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Georgetown's been my favorite team ever since I was young," he said. "Alonzo, Dikembe, Patrick Ewing, those were my idols."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And now they're comparisons waiting to happen.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"That's a mouthful," Williams said. "I hate to make comparisons of that nature. Darian's goal should be to achieve the most he can achieve&nbsp;-- to reach his full potential."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Plus, Townes is a different sort of shot-blocker, starting with the obvious fact that he's only 6-9. </FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's not as big as some of the past shot-blockers in Georgetown history," Telep explained. "But he's bouncy, he's got an active body, and he moves well from block to block. He's just not 7-1. But Darian will be the latest in a long line there at Georgetown."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Even if he says he wants to bring a little more Allen Iverson and a little less Dikembe Mutombo.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>MIKE THE MOUTH (SOMETIMES)</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Mike Jefferson likes to talk. Mike Jefferson <I>loves</I> to talk.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoBodyText2 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"All day long," said L.J. Goolsby, his AAU coach with the Kansas City Nets. "You can't get him to shut up."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Especially not when it comes to his playful rat-a-tat-tat trash talk.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's basically like a little comedian out there," said Eric Bossi, a Missouri-based recruiting analyst. "He really enjoys being on the court. He has fun out there talking trash&nbsp;-- but not thug trash talk."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But as for talking to his teammates instead of his opponents, well, that's been more of an issue for the 5-9 point guard from Kansas City's Westport High School.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"We had a long talk with Mike about being more vocal," Goolsby said this past weekend during the Howard Pulley program's Sabes Foundation Invitational. "That's what a point guard has to be, and that's what he needs to do to play like he's capable of playing."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Which is how he played here at the University of Minnesota.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He doesn't usually have to be The Man for the Nets. (Keith Wooden, the skilled forward from Lawrence, typically takes care of that role.) Just like he doesn't usually have to be The Man for his school team. (A sophomore by the name of Brandon "Brother of Jaron and Kareem" Rush has that title.)</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But with Wooden at home during this weekend's action&nbsp;-- he was busy with a prom back in Kansas&nbsp;-- Jefferson had to step up a little bit more. And he did. Especially with his shooting.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"We've been getting easier shots because of better ball movement," Goolsby said. "So Mike isn't forcing shots. He's knocking them down."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"They left me open," Jefferson said matter-of-factly. "I'm confident enough to shoot the ball."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And then yap about it. As long as they continue to go in.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"What happens is that he has a tendency to play to the crowd," Bossi said. "He's a confident shooter, but he sometimes fades back and leaves the release short.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"When he follows, though, he's dangerous. He's just got to stay under control and not get overly excited."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Because that's when he's best.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Jefferson averaged 18.9 points and six assists per game as the yin to Rush's yang. Westport went 24-6 and made it to the state semifinals this year.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>His strengths? Just ask M-Jeff.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Going to the basket," he said. "Going baseline. Getting my teammates involved. Playing up and down."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Some of the Midwest's best mid-major programs think so, too. Southern Illinois leads an interested group that also includes St. Louis, Indiana State, Tulsa and Wichita State.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** All-everything Akron kid <B>LeBron James</B> wasn't supposed to play a whole heck of a lot of AAU ball this summer. But the 6-7 junior joined coach <B>Chris Grier</B> and his Michigan Hurricanes and helped lead them to the championship at the adidas Hoop Challenge at Cleveland State University. James and 'Canes stalwart <B>Olu Famutimi</B> each scored 19 in the finals against <B>Mike Duncan</B>'s Cleveland Basketball Club. "Let me tell you something," Grier said Wednesday night while driving to the Pistons-Celtics game at the Palace. "LeBron's play had Olu's play go to a whole 'nother level. Olu was doing things I've never seen him do in his whole life. It was like&nbsp;-- and I'm not exaggerating&nbsp;-- it was like the <B>Scottie Pippen</B> and <B>Michael Jordan</B> of high school basketball. They were playing. Everyone else was just watching." And thus continueth The Hype.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Speaking of The Chosen One, <B>Chris Lawlor</B> unveiled his All-American teams in Wednesday's USA Today, and James was the paper's national player of the year. Also on the first team: <B>Raymond Felton</B>, the UNC-bound point guard from Latta, S.C.; Orlando post terror <B>Amare Stoudemire</B>, fresh off his declaration for the NBA draft; <B>Carmelo Anthony</B>, the magnificent Oak Hill swingman headed to Syracuse; and Los Angeles wing <B>Hassan Adams</B>, a somewhat surprising first-team selection. Brooklyn's <B>Sebastian Telfair</B> made the third team&nbsp;-- the only sophomore among the 15 picks&nbsp;-- and Beaumont, Texas, junior center <B>Kendrick Perkins</B> made the second team.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Bobby Jacobs</B> has the paper work finished for all but three of the 17 teams expected to make up the first annual War on the Shore prep tournament in Milford, Del., this coming November. Massachusetts powers Winchendon and Worcester lead a field that'll showcase roughly 100 Division I prospects. "The gym will be rocking," Jacobs said earlier this week. "People will be packed in like sardines." Jacobs, of course, is also the mastermind behind the post-Christmas Slam Dunk to the Beach.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Add another name to the </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=717&amp;section_id=734"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3>unusually strong sophomore class down in Texas</FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>. <B>La Marcus Aldridge</B>, a 6-11 sophomore post from Seagoville High School in Dallas, has spent the spring forging a big-time rep. "He's pretty damn good," said <B>Mitch Malone</B>, his AAU coach with the Texas Bluechips. "People are telling me he's probably the best big man I've ever had." Which is a strong statement, considering Malone's teams in the past have featured guys like New Jersey Nets star <B>Kenyon Martin</B>, Tennessee center <B>Brandon Crump</B>, Arizona State big man <B>Ike Diogu</B> and Georgia Tech-bound McDonald's All-American <B>Chris Bosh</B>. "A lot of coaches don't know if this kid's going to make it to college," Malone added. "A lot of them are saying they're not even going to recruit him because they don't know if he'll be there."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Georgia Elite AAU coach <B>Linzy Davis</B> will be in Durham, N.C., this coming Saturday to watch one of his prize pupils&nbsp;-- Memphis-bound post player <B>Almamy Thiero</B>&nbsp;-- graduate from Mount Zion Christian Academy. (Thiero got a qualifying ACT score earlier this week.) Then he'll fly back to Atlanta and head over to Morrow, Ga., where he and Kentucky HoopStars kingpin <B>Eddie Ford</B> are joint-running a small event at Clayton State.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** The quote of this past weekend in the Twin Cities came from veteran Hampton, Va., AAU coach <B>Boo Williams</B>. He was chatting about a former player of his named <B>Alonzo Mourning</B>. "I see some of these high school kids entering the draft," he said, "and Alonzo would've hurt them." Maybe. Maybe not. But it is interesting to think about how many kids&nbsp;-- and which kids&nbsp;-- never would have played a single year of college ball had they been born 10 or 15 years later.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Jason Wingate</B>&nbsp;-- the unsung point guard for Rice High School's New York city and state champions&nbsp;-- committed to Manhattan earlier this week. The 6-foot Wingate was a second-team All-Catholic pick in NYC. The little guy did little things for the Raiders&nbsp;-- but important things. "That's what made him so important," Rice coach <B>Maurice Hicks</B> told the New York Post. "He knew what to do for us to win."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Tommy Amaker</B>'s getting after it on the recruiting trail. The Michigan coach picked up his second in-state verbal commitment in the last week&nbsp;-- and this time it's from a current sophomore. <B>Ronald Coleman</B>, a 6-5 wing from Romulus, Mich., joined Detroit junior shooting guard <B>Dion Harris</B> and pledged his allegiance to the Wolverines. "I knew I liked Michigan," he told <B>Dave Telep</B> of TheInsidersHoops.com. "I was just waiting for them to tell me that I was going to get a scholarship." Coleman averaged 17 points, 10 rebounds and three assists this past winter.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Darryl Watkins</B> was apparently the star of last weekend's New Jersey AAU championships&nbsp;-- the 6-11 Paterson Catholic junior had 15 points, five rebounds and three blocks for the Tim Thomas Playaz in the finals&nbsp;-- but St. Patrick point guard <B>Mike Nardi</B>'s return was noteworthy as well. The 6-2 point guard was sidelined with a series of foot injuries for almost his entire junior year. "Nardi looked pretty good," NJHoops.com guru <B>Jay Gomes</B> said. "He's just not 100 percent yet. But he shot the ball really well. He's shooting it a lot better than he used to." There was a lot of time to hone his form, obviously, when he couldn't run fives.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Big Apple basketball presence <B>Gary DeCesare</B> started a forum on </FONT><A href="http://members.aol.com/_ht_a/nycnjhoops/index.htm"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3>New York City's High Hoops website</FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3> that has taken off in the last week. DeCesare, the veteran coach at Bronx schoolboy power St. Raymond's, posted his thoughts on "the state of basketball" and asked for responses. The replies have been terrific. Replies have come from high school coaches (Lincoln's <B>Tiny Morton</B>, Bishop Loughlin's <B>Ted Gustus</B>), AAU coaches (<B>Gary Charles</B> of the Long Island Panthers, <B>Tom Jennings</B> of the Westchester Hawks), journalists (<B>Peter Abraham</B> of the Westchester Journal News) and scouts (<B>Nathan Blue</B>). This kind of open-ended banter, incidentally, is what's good&nbsp;-- or at least what can be good&nbsp;-- about the Internet.</FONT></P>
