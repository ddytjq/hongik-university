            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><font color="#000000" face="Arial" size="5"><b>Tongue
            in cheek, we take out the crystal ball again</b></font>
            </p>
            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center">&nbsp;
            </p>
            <p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 6" align="center"><b><font size="2" color="#000000">
            &nbsp;</font><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial" size="2">by Mark Cipolloni</font><font color="#0066FF"><br>
            </font><font color="#000000" face="Arial" size="2">January 6, 2002</font></b></p>
        </td>
          </tr>
  </center>
          <tr>
        <td bgcolor="#000000" valign="middle" align="center" width="480" height="21">
            <p align="left"><font color="#FFFFFF" size="3" face="Arial"><b>Go to

            to discuss this article</b></font>
        </td>

          <tr>
          <TD align=right bgcolor=#FFFFEE vAlign=top width=640>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">It's that time of year again
      when we dust off the old crystal ball and peer into the future of auto racing.&nbsp;
      Since CART's imminent doom has been uttered more times than you can count,
      we look ahead to the year 2008.&nbsp; Will CART still be alive, or just a
      figment of our imagination?</font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">As we polish the dust off the
      old ball.....wait, there it is, I can see it, can you?&nbsp; It's becoming
      clearer now.</font></p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Chris
          Pook is celebrating his 6th anniversary with CART and is voted the
          sports most powerful personality for the first time.&nbsp; CART's
          board of director's no longer questions what he is doing, they just
          give a slight bow when he walks past.&nbsp; Jon Vannini files an SEC
          form 13G declaring he's satisfied with CART's management and is taking
          a back seat now.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          Prost team is celebrating their 5th anniversary in CART.&nbsp; Not
          able to raise enough money to stay in F1, they decided to race in CART
          instead.&nbsp; The team gets $20 million annually in backing from Air France and
          employs two French drivers.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          subsequently adds a race in Paris around the Eiffel Tower.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Is
          that Jacques
          Villeneuve?&nbsp; Yes, it is, he's celebrating his 6th anniversary with CART.&nbsp; Having
          finally gotten fed up with being a back marker in F1 driving inferior
          equipment, he returned to CART with a Forsythe/Pollack/Gorne 2-car
          Molson Beer team.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          signed a 50-year contract with FedEx, renamed their cars FedEx cars and
          every FedEx truck had a picture of a FedEx car on the side and back.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          FedEx and CART Super Bowl TV ad was voted best and everyone was
          talking about the FedEx Car ad the next day around the office water cooler
          instead of who won the game.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Team
          Andretti drivers Marco
          Andretti and Mini Al Unser are favorites for the 2008
          title</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          Maserati engine is rebadged a Ferrari by Fiat.&nbsp; The CART
          race at Monza sells out again and Imola continues to lobby for a race.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Both
          BMW and Mercedes are fighting to sponsor the German 500, which now has a race day crowd of 175,000.&nbsp; Marlboro sponsors the Nazareth
          IRL race that plays before 27,000 fans.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          celebrates after one of its teams win the Indy 500 for the 8th straight
          time.&nbsp; A.J. Foyt throws another temper tantrum, slaps Arie Luyendyk
          in the back of the head again, but is rushed to
          the hospital when he pops a vein in his neck. Tony George promptly
          re-implements the 25/8 rule but an Indianapolis judge rules it illegal
          under the right-to-work laws.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          and IRL then merge under the IndyCar banner, with the IRL Division
          being all ovals, and the CART division road courses and overseas
          events.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          CART division buys the ALMS series and this 1-2 combo packs them in on
          every road course they go to.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          Mexico City race surpasses 500,000 on race day for the first time.&nbsp;
          Marlboro sponsors the Phoenix IRL race that plays before 29,000 fans,
          the highest in 5 years.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">China
          passes Japan for the #2 largest GNP and CART's sponsors are clamoring
          to do business there.&nbsp; CART
          and ALMS draw 450,000 for the 5th annual Shanghai GP and Bernie
          Ecclestone tries to shoot himself in the head, but misses when someone
          yells Monopoly.&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally takes a clue from NASCAR and starts a <a href="http://www.xmradio.com/">XM satellite
          radio</a> station while NASCAR celebrates its 6th anniversary on XM.&nbsp;
          Available anywhere in the USA via satellite, XM radios are now
          standard equipment in all cars and fans can now listen to CART news
          whenever they are on the road.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Gil
          deFerran and Helio Castroneves are ecstatic when CART announces they
          are returning to Brazil.&nbsp; Both drivers left Team Penske after
          2002 because they got tired driving around in circles in the IRL
          division.&nbsp; Meanwhile Team Penske's new drivers, Buck George and
          Billy France turn right for the first time in their careers when the
          world's first oval track winners circle is constructed in the middle
          of the main grandstands outside the track.&nbsp; They marvel at the
          opportunity.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          worldwide cumulative TV audience surpasses 4 billion for the first
          time in 2008 and annual TV revenues surpass $100 million for the first
          time.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Tony
          Stewart wins the Daytona 500 in a Target Chip Ganassi
          entry, and finishes 2nd to Marco Andretti for the CART title.&nbsp;
          Chip Ganassi again threatens to move to the IRL division.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally calls itself a 'World Championship Series' after 6 more years
          of procrastination</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          CART sponsored Winston Cup car wins the Daytona 500.&nbsp; Mike Helton
          fires Gary Nelson because the car passes post-race inspection.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Toyota
          finally wins the CART championship over arch-rival Honda who had
          returned to CART in 2003 after saying they couldn't have an engine
          ready in time.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          most popular present at Christmas is the toy CART FedEx car that plays
          the CART theme song Top Gun.&nbsp; It's electric motor screams to
          100,000 RPM and can outrun an IRL car from 0 to 100 mph.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Carl
          Haas finally wins the Indy 500 but swallows his cigar and needs an
          emergency operation to remove the blockage.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          bank account hits $1 billion and they're still arguing over whether to
          invest the money in the series or to sit on it in case there's another
          economic recession.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally adopts standing starts on its road courses after drivers in
          the last row at Long Beach complain because the pole sitter lapped
          them before they took the green flag.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The Beijing,
          China mayor cries foul because the CART race in Shanghai got higher TV ratings
          than the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing when a Chinese driver sat on the pole
          and won the race.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          F1 Concorde Agreement expires and the engine manufacturers tell F1
          team owners, CART and the IRL they want a common engine formula for
          all three series.&nbsp; Everyone agrees and Bernie Ecclestone tries to
          shoot himself again, but blows off three toes instead.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          Speed Channel surpasses ESPN as the #1 sports channel.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally buys Skip Barber Racing, opens a go-kart facility in every
          major city, and starts the CART amateur go-kart series, which becomes a
          popular High School sport.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Fosters
          Beer outbids Honda for the title sponsorship of the Surfers Paradise
          race and an Australian driver finally gets a full-time ride in CART.&nbsp;
          The race attendance surpasses 400,000.&nbsp; A plane crashes into the
          grandstands at Michigan Speedway during the Marlboro 400 but misses
          all 15,000 fans.&nbsp; The IRL calls it a miracle and gives each fan a
          pack of Marlboro's to calm their nerves.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          European office manager Pat Leahy announces that all four of CART's
          European races (England, Germany, Italy, France) are once again a
          sellout.&nbsp; The European Union declares the 4-week CART
          extravaganza <i>European Speedweeks</i>.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          decides to race on Motegi's road course and attendance doubles.&nbsp;
          CART decides to do the same at the Eurospeedway in Germany and 6-time
          World Champion Michael Schumacher decides CART isn't so dangerous
          anymore and jumps ship.&nbsp; The German 500 becomes the biggest
          sporting event in Germany.&nbsp; Marlboro sponsors the Colorado
          Springs IRL race that plays before 24,000 fans.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          Florida Speedweeks kickoff race, the Kool GP of Miami, is such a hit
          the race has to move to another downtown location because the 150,000
          fans will no longer fit in Bayfront Park.&nbsp; Race attendance grows
          to 200,000 immediately.&nbsp; Marlboro sponsors the Homestead IRL race
          that plays before 31,000 fans but remains loyal to Team Penske.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Road
          America finally builds pit row garages and suites and puts up some big
          screen TV's so the fans can see all the action around the beautiful
          4-mile circuit.&nbsp; Attendance doubles.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Mid-Ohio
          widens its track by 20 feet after CART declares it too narrow for good
          racing.&nbsp; An on-track pass for the lead is made for the first time
          in 10 years.&nbsp; Barbara Trueman admits she should have done it
          years ago.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          NY City Grand Prix becomes the darling of the financial world when the
          city agrees the race should be run around the Wall Street district and
          the rebuilt World Trade Centers site.&nbsp; CART and its teams
          entertain the CEO's of all the major corporations during a weekend
          long Big Apple Extravaganza.&nbsp; All the hotels in NY City are sold
          out and outgoing NY City Mayor Michael Bloomberg calls the race the
          highlight of his tenure.&nbsp; He retires from politics and
          uses some of his $ billions to start a CART team.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          hires Steven Speilberg to direct its new movie <i>Racing Gladiators</i>
          and an all-star cast.&nbsp; The movie is #1 at the box office for ten
          straight weeks.&nbsp; CART and FedEx run ads in all the movie theaters
          that are so funny adults and children can't stop talking about them.
          Every movie attendee gets a free tee-shirt with a FedEx Car on it and
          a 50% ticket discount to a CART race near them. All CART races are
          complete sellouts. FedEx is so happy with its relationship with CART
          it asks to extend its contract from 50 years to 100 years.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">24
          countries submit applications to host a CART race.&nbsp; They are told
          nothing will become available for 10 years.&nbsp; South Korea buys the
          Milwaukee race date from Carl Haas for $50 million and Carl finally
          lights a cigar to celebrate.&nbsp; Only he forgot and bit the lighted
          end and is rushed to the hospital with 3rd degree burns over 50% of
          his tongue.&nbsp; He never smokes again.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          virtual reality PC game is so realistic it requires 1-terrabyte of RAM
          and a Pentium 10 processor to run.&nbsp; The game is so popular EA
          Sports can't produce them fast enough and a black market develops for
          illegal copies.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">John
          Deere becomes the official sponsor of the IRL division and offers a
          higher revving version of the IRL race engine in it's top-line of farm
          tractors.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          starts a Legends series for all its retired FedEx car stars using old
          FedEx cars and left over 2.65L turbo engines instead of Cobra
          replicas.&nbsp; The series becomes so popular ABC wins the bid to
          televise all 15 races.&nbsp; The Geritol Metamucil Viagra Legends
          series becomes the #1 TV show in Florida.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          opens a museum and Omni Vision complex in Indianapolis and names it
          after Mario Andretti (<i>Mario Andretti CART FedEx Entertainment
          Center</i>), America's greatest driver.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">NASCAR
          replaces the carburetors on its 1950's push rod engines with fuel injection
          and Bill France Sr. rolls over in his grave.&nbsp; NASCAR fans mourn
          the loss.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">10-time
          Winston Cup champion Jeff Gordon retires from NASCAR and starts a CART
          FedEx team.&nbsp; Bill France Sr. rolls over in his grave again.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally removes all fuel restrictions from its series when it dawns on
          them the 1979 oil embargo is over.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART,
          FedEx and Kelloggs rollout a new high-energy breakfast cereal and
          every box contains a toy FedEx car.&nbsp; Any kid who collects all 30
          cars gets an all expense paid trip for four to a race of their choice.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">Pepsi
          becomes the official soft drink of the FedEx series, Coca Cola the
          official drink of the ALMS series, and Ex-Lax the official medicine of
          the Geritol Metamucil Viagra Legends series.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART's
          2-seater FedEx ride-along car program gets past the insurance issues
          and becomes a big hit with the fans.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          finally requires all road course promoters to provide cranes to remove
          wrecked cars quickly.&nbsp; Full-course cautions become almost nonexistent
          and we once again get back to racing instead of playing follow the
          pace car.&nbsp; For the first time in 8 years, the pace car doesn't
          lead the most laps.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">CART
          removes 100 pounds from the minimum weight of a Champ Car so when they
          race in Montreal their lap times are only 4 seconds a lap slower than
          the F1 cars instead of 8.&nbsp; F1 fans still laugh and look down
          their noses at CART. Reynard and Lola just unbolt the 100 pounds of
          ballast strapped to their cars. The EPA congratulates CART for getting
          rid of all that lead.&nbsp; Bernie Ecclestone and Niki Lauda remind
          everyone again that CART is 'lazy' racing.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          CART race at Monza outdraws the F1 race for the first time and Bernie
          Ecclestone tries to shoot himself in the head once more.&nbsp; He
          misses again and shoots off his last two toes when someone asks him
          how come FedEx cars actually have drivers whereas F1 cars are driven
          with joysticks from the pits.&nbsp; Flavio Briatore smashes his
          joystick to the ground when his Windows Operating System locks up for
          the 5th time and both cars crash out of the race.&nbsp; Ferrari's Ross
          Braun beats Williams' Patrick Head for the race win but fails
          post-race inspection when his joystick is found to be 1/8 inch too
          long.</font></li>
        <li>
          <p align="left" style="margin-top: 6"><font face="Arial" size="2">The
          Toyota F1 car wins the Japanese GP at Suzuki with a daring last lap
          pass by their 12-year old video game ringer Tora Toyoda, the great great
          grandson of Hiroshi Toyoda.&nbsp; F1 fans are all up in arms because
