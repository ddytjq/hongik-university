         <td width="5" height="5"><img src="/img/phototheque/00.gif" width="5" height="5"></td>
         <td class="texte10">There is no better way to summarise Michelin's participation in motorcycle grand prix racing than to underline its continued presence since 1974 and to point out the huge number of innovations that have helped it reap an incredible number of wins and titles in all categories.<BR><BR>The story began in 1973, when the PZ tyre first appeared in the Michelin range. It wasn't long before it began to make its mark and Kent Andersson used Michelin tyres to win the 125cc world title in 1974.<BR><BR>Subsequently, Michelin began to develop tyres specifically for competition and its grand prix domination commenced. In 1976 Barry Sheene gave Michelin its first 500cc world title and a first grand slam followed one year later with success in the 50, 125, 250, 350 and 500cc classes.<BR><BR>In the early Eighties the radial tyre sparked another racing revolution: riders profited from the latest tyres to suit their preference for an aggressive, sliding style. Initially they allowed the rear wheel to drift... then they started to do it with both. It was an era marked by Wayne Rainey, Kevin Schwantz, Mick Doohan and their adversaries.<BR><BR>Today Michelin supplies tyres to the majority of the 500cc field, but its objectives remain unchanged: it wants to provide maximum grip while giving riders the top-quality feedback they need to get the most from the incredible power of current-generation machines. It is not by chance that current 500cc motorcycle grand prix are as fast, close and exciting as ever.<HR></td>
