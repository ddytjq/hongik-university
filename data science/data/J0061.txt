</FONT><HR>I've been reading about alot of dusty tracks this year and alot of people complaining.  There are a few points I'd like to make.  When you go pointing the finger dont always go to the track crew first!  Why dont you make a little make believe track in your back yard....right out in the middle of a field.  Let the track bake in the 90 degree summer sun for a week and then throw some water on it.  See how fast it dries up.  My point is, sometimes no matter how much water you put on the track it will still just suck it up and get dusty.  A perfect example is Raceway 7 last friday.  I sat down in turn 4 (arguably the worst turn dust wise) and when I went to go sit down I natually tried to wipe the dust off with my hand.  What I got was balls of dust that rolled up into balls of dirt.  I have never seen dust so heavily saturated with water that it could by rolled up into balls.  No matter how much water they put on the track (it was alot) it was still going to be dusty.  Another thing is I think when preparing a track the people doing it take driver safety into consideration over the fans liking it or not.  I have seen Sharon probably the dustiest I've ever seen it this year...but I'll be damned if I see a big hole in it.  It has pretty much been smooth for most of the year.  Sure they could dump the water on the track to keep the dust down and have the track roll up on itself and cause a red flag every other lap.  So next time you get a little dust on you, just remember you're at a DIRT track and if you can't handle the dust do us all a favor and stop whinning and leave, and remember they do try as hard as they can with the somewhat limited resources they have!</FONT><P align=right><FONT SIZE="1" color="#800080" face="Verdana, Arial">IP: 65.25.20.196</font></P></td></tr><tr bgcolor="#f7f7f7">
