<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Japan's largest stage-race, the Tour
de Hokkiado will this year take place from September 14 to september 20.&nbsp; This race will take the riders in 7
stages from Aibetsu to Saporro and covers a total distance of 810 km.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Here you can find reports of the team
World Wide Cycling send to this event. We show the results and
interviews with the riders. There will be pictures and stories from the riders about their
travel and their adventures.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><a href="travels.htm"><font color="#0000FF">If you are interested to travel to
Japan yourself to participate in an event, Click here to see how.&nbsp;</font></a></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b>September 12th:</b></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">A part of the Team of RTV de Zwaluwen
has arrived in the city of Saporro, on Hokkaido one of the most nothern Islands
of Japan. Luckily the bad wheater that hit Japan was on another part. It is dry
and the temperature is nice, perfect conditions for the race. The rest of the
team will arrive tomorrow from Beijing, China.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b>September 14:</b></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Everybody has arrived well. We are
now in Ishikawa where the openingceremoniy will be. It was raining today, so the
riders only did a ride of an hour to losen the legs. The weatherforecast for
tomorrow sounds better.I</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The field of this years Tour de
Hokkaido is very strong. The Canadians have come with a very strong team. For
example Eric Wohlberg and, Czeslaw Lukaszewicz the Canadian Champion are here to
prepare for the Olympics in Sydney.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The Irish national team is also one
of the favourites. Then there are some Kazachs (and we know them from China!) in
a Turkish team.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">The top three from last year will be
there again; the winner of 1999, Ken Hashikawa; number 2, Yoshiuki Abe; and
number 3 Kazuyuki Manabe.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Teamleader Gudo Kramer has set the
following goals for his team:</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- 1 rider with the first three in the
general classification</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- 1 stagewin</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- first three in the team
classification</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- 3 riders with the first 15 in the
general classification</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">- 1 rider with the first three in the
points classification</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0"><b>September 15:</b></p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Today it was very hard for the
cyclist in the Tour de Hokkaido. It was raining all day and the temperatures
were just above 10 degrees Celsius. On the Jomon-Pass (altitude 800 meter) there
was a stormy wind.&nbsp;</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Only Nathan Dahlberg and Pete Swenson
could keep up with the first group. The others of the team lost time. At this
moment we don't have the results yet. Winner was Mark Scanlon from Ireland. The
juniors world championships from Valkenburg 1998. For a long time he was leading
by himself, he got caught back but still had enough power to win the
sprint!&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">From the one extreme into the other.
After the cold and rain yesterday. Today the peloton was tortured bij extreme
heat. The mountains were harder then we thought. On the stage profiles this
stage seemed to be not to hard. This brings promisses for the real hard stages
that are coming. The peloton went into little pieces today. Only Pete Swenson
could stay in the first group.</p>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0">Dennis Hammink had to go to the
doping control today. He was dried out by the long stage in the heat. To be able
to urinate he drunk a lot of cold water. His stomag could not handle it and he
threw up. He also got dizzy and the doctor decided to give him an infusion. Team
leader Gudo Kramer, who had almost no food today faded away. Then the doctor had
two patients&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
