  <p align="left" style="word-spacing: 0; line-height: 100%; text-indent: 0; margin: 0"><font face="Times New Roman" size="4">The
  1972 qualification process saw England qualify in emphatic style, topping
  their group and conceding just three goals in the process.&nbsp; The only
  points we dropped in the whole campaign were at home to Switzerland in the
  penultimate match.&nbsp; Confidence was high as Lee, Peters, Hurst, Chivers
  and Alan Clarke were all scoring regularly and the team hadn't lost since
  being dumped out of the World Cup in Mexico two years earlier.&nbsp; But, the
  quarter final draw pitched us against the West Germans again and once more
  they put us out before the semi final stage.&nbsp; We went down 3-1 at Wembley
  in the first leg with Franny Lee scoring our goal.&nbsp; Two weeks later in
  Berlin, England managed a creditable 0-0 draw but it wasn't enough to progress
  to the final stage in Belgium.&nbsp; The Germans went on to beat the hosts and
  then USSR in the final to win the trophy.</font></p>
