<FONT color="#0000ff" face="Verdana" size="2"><STRONG>Home Security Advice</STRONG></FONT>
<tr>
<TD class="text8" vAlign="top" width="440"><br><font face="Verdana" size="1"><b>The feeling that someone else has been in your home can live with you for a long time. The advice in this leaflet will help stop you being the next burglary victim.</b></font><br><br>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><B>Doors</B></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><BR>Outside doors should have deadlocks that at least conform to British Standard 3621. These locks can only be opened by a key so a burglar cannot just use a plastic card to push back the tongue of the lock or break a glass panel and reach in to open it.<br><br>Doors that you usually lock from the inside - for example the back door - should also be fitted with bolts. But locks and bolts are only as strong as the door and the frame to which they are fitted. So check the woodwork and replace it if weak or rotten.<br><br>Double doors should have bolts (preferably security bolts with removable keys) at the top and bottom of both doors as well as a lock. On patio doors, additional security locks should be fitted to stop the slicing frame being lifted off the tracks. The sliding leaf of patio doors should be fitted on the inside.</FONT></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><B>Windows</B></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><BR>Most burglaries are through windows. Key operated locks should be fitted to all accessible windows - those on the ground floor and those near drainpipes and flat roofs. These locks are inexpensive to buy and easy to fit.</FONT></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><B>Keys</B></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><BR>Never leave keys in a lock - always take them with you. Never leave keys in a "secret" hiding place - thieves know all the hiding places. Leave a spare key with a trusted neighbour.</FONT></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><B>Callers</B></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><BR>Check the identity of all callers. Before opening your door fully, ask to see their identity card if they claim to be officials. Don't be fooled by a uniform. Telephone their office if in any doubt. A door viewer or door chain will help you to see who is at the door without opening it fully.</FONT></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
<TABLE align="center" border="0" cellPadding="0" cellSpacing="0" width="400">
<TR>
<TD colSpan="3"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><B>Going out</B></FONT></TD></TR>
<TR>
<TD bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="400"><FONT face="Verdana" size="1"><BR>A burglar can be in and out of your home in two minutes. So always shut and lock all windows - however short a time you are going to be away. Don't forget garages and sheds - they contain valuable items and tools useful to a burglar. Chain and padlock ladders or keep them in locked sheds or garages.</FONT></TD>
<TD align="right" bgColor="white" class="tablet" width="19"></TD></TR></TABLE></P>
<P>
