        Platinni.&nbsp; Twenty seven seconds into the game and
        Brian Robson converted a Paul Mariner cross to give
        England the lead.&nbsp; Robson powerfully headed home the
        second and Mariner himself scored the third in a 3-1
        win.&nbsp; We next beat the Czechs 2-0 and finally Kuwait
        1-0 to finish the first stage without dropping a
        point.&nbsp; The second round was another group system
        and we had to play the Germans and hosts Spain for a
        place in the semi-finals.&nbsp; The England supporters
        who made the trip to Bilbao for the first three games
        were welcomed with open arms, but once they moved out of
        Basque country and into the Spanish
        Capital, Madrid, they found a very different
        atmosphere.&nbsp; The police, who were bitter about us
        beating Argentina to reclaim the Falklands, were caught
        on camera beating the English with sticks whilst they
        were drinking in a bar.&nbsp; On the pitch, a 0-0 draw
        with Germany meant that we needed to beat Spain 2-0 to
        progress, but even with the arrival of Keegan and
        Brooking, they only managed another 0-0 draw and went
        home undefeated.&nbsp;&nbsp; The Ulstermen also reached
        the second round after beating Spain in Valencia, but
        were well beaten by France 4-1.&nbsp;</b></font> </p>
