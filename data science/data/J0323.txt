        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Joyce Wins Bob 
          Kelly Memorial</b></font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">It was only fitting 
          that one of the winningest drivers at Nanaimo's Cassidy Speedway would 
          win a trophy named after a man who put so much effort into racing that 
          they named a trophy in his honor. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Nanaimo's <b>Jim 
          Joyce</b> a former point champion at the now closed track, and the current 
          Saratoga Speedway Magic 98.9 Crash to Pass points leader, used a win 
          in the 15 lap main event to collect the most points for the night's 
          racing to win the <b>Bob Kelly Memorial Trophy</b> for the Crash to 
          Pass divison. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Little Caesars 
          Pizza sponsored night of racing for the crashers kicked off with two 
          trophy dashes which saw defending point champion<b> Mark Stephens</b> 
          return after missing 3 races and win the B dash, followed by an A dash 
          win for Nanaimo's <b>Mark Haslam.</b> The first contact race of the 
          night saw Haslam collect his second straight win in the heat race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">For the second week 
          in a row Parksville's <b>Brad McLean</b> got an early jump on the field, 
          and never looked back, to win his second straight Tyee Stock Car main 
          event. Starting on the outside pole position, McLean took the race lead 
          on the first lap and never saw another car's rear bumper over the next 
          25 laps, leaving all the close bumper to bumper racing behind in winning 
          his 3rd main event of the season. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With McLean having 
          first place in his pocket the battle behind him was intense as Courtenay's 
          <b>Mike Meeres</b> held a determined <b>David Mainse</b> and <b>Ray 
          Anderosov</b> off to finish second with Mainse grabbing third ahead 
          of Anderosov. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">With his third place 
          finish Mainse improved his point lead over <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> to 
          27 after Beaulieu finished 6th in a damaged car. McLean's main event 
          win gave him three victories on the night after collecting wins in the 
          B Dash and 12 lap heat race, while <b>Don Hallgarth</b> took home the 
          win in the fast A Dash.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">A special eight 
          lap race followed the trophy dashes and it included five drivers who 
          hadn't won a main event. Winner Ralph von Kampen and fifth place Mike 
          Schott each recieved a $150 racing tire from Tyee Chev Olds, who also 
          presented main event winner McLean with a tire.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The heat race saw 
          a three car crash on the 10th lap invovling <b>Dave Hargrave</b> ,<b>James 
          Dawson</b> and Beaulieu who both crashed hard into the backside tires 
          while trying to avoid a spun out Hargrave.The mishap ended the night's 
          racing for Hargrave, while Dawson continued. Beaulieu had some major 
          repairs done to make the main event, repairs that will mean replacing 
          the entire front clip which, as of Saturday night, Beaulieu wasn't sure 
          that he would do. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Saturday's racing 
          also included a three race program for the Figure 8 Mini Stocks and 
          it marked the return to racing for the <b>Bohn</b> family as dad <b>Glen</b> 
          won the heat race, and younger son <b>Nathan</b> won the trophy dash 
          and older son <b>Jarrett</b> won the main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This Saturday night 
          the IMCA Modifieds return to compete in their version of the Bob Kelly 
          Memorial going to the driver with the most points on the night. They 
          will be joined by the Stock Cars, who will be racing for the 9th week 
          in a row, but who get a two week break after Saturday night. Motorcycles 
          are also on the program.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Guest talk....The 
          next three weeks will go a long way to decide <b>Jason Beaulieu's</b> 
          chances of repeating as Canadain IMCA champion as after this race at 
          Saratoga he travels to Victoria the following Saturday then in two weeks 
          he finds himself in Vernon BC. I'll have a in depth report on all his 
          results.....Big,Big tip of the hat goes to Tyee Chev Olds for their 
          sponsorship of the Stock cars and their tire giveaway Saturday night.Thanks 
          Bill....Guest driver of the week goes to Joyce for his hard driveing 
          in the main and the sportsman like way he did it. </font> </p>
