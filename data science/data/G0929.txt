            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"> 
              <!-- #BeginEditable "title" --><b>Environmental Issues and Astronomy: 
              An Introductory Resource Guide</b><!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>
          </td>
          <td>&nbsp;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" --> 
            <p><a href="environmentprint.html"><img src="../../images_main/print.gif" width="20" height="26" border="0" alt="printer-friendly version"></a> 
              <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="environmentprint.html">printer-friendly 
              version</a> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"><img src="../../images_main/6666cc.gif" width="500" height="2"></font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1">by 
              Andrew Fraknoi<br>
              (Foothill College & the Astronomical Society of the Pacific)<br>
              [Version 1.1; Jan. 2000]</font> </p>
            <p> <font size="1" face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">&copy; 
              2000 by Andrew Fraknoi. The right to reproduce for any non-profit 
              educational purpose is hereby granted. For any other use, contact 
              the author at: <a href="mailto:fraknoiandrew@fhda.edu">fraknoiandrew@fhda.edu</a>.</font> 
            </p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="toc"></a>Table 
              of Contents</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">1. 
              <a href="#1">Light Pollution</a><br>
              2. <a href="#2">Observatory Sites and the Environment</a><br>
              3. <a href="environment02.html#3">Radio Interference</a><br>
              4. <a href="environment02.html#4">Preventing the Contamination of 
              Earth or Other Worlds</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Like 
              every other human endeavor, the exploration of the universe takes 
              place in the context of our terrestrial environment. As human activity 
              changes that environment, issues are raised for both astronomers 
              and those who enjoy the fruits of their research. In this resource 
              list, we examine four areas where the environment and the needs 
              of astronomers meet: the problems caused by the uncontrolled growth 
              of city lights; the issue of reconciling the need of new observatory 
              sites with the safeguarding of endangered species; the difficulty 
              of protecting the frequencies (channels) needed for radio astronomy 
              from the encroachment of cellular phones and other forms of radio 
              communication on Earth; and the thorny problem of how we protect 
              other planets from Earth micro-organisms and the Earth from possible 
              extra-terrestrial microbes. Additions to this list are most welcome. 
              </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><b><a name="1">1. 
              Light Pollution</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Few 
              places on Earth remain truly dark as human civilization and its 
              electric lights spread across the globe. For astronomers, trying 
              to collect the faint light of distant objects, this "light pollution" 
              has become a serious problem. Some lights are worse than others 
              in terms of the number of colors (wavelengths) they block from celestial 
              objects. The sources below describe the attempts to balance the 
              needs of cities with the requirements of observatories, and the 
              work astronomers are doing to educate political leaders and the 
              public about this issue. </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Readings 
              </b> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Crawford, 
              D. & Hunter, T. "The Battle Against Light Pollution" in <i>Sky & 
              Telescope,</i> July 1990, p. 23. A fine review of the current status 
              of problems and solution. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Crawford, 
              D. & Robinson, W. <i>Light Pollution: Problems and Solutions.</i> 
              1990, Astronomical Society of the Pacific. A set of 20 slides and 
              a 24-page information booklet to help teach and inform about the 
              issues. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Davidson, 
              K. "Hype in Space: Billboards and Other Threats to the Dark" in 
              Mercury, May/June 1993, p. 80. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Hendry, 
              A. "Light Pollution: A Status Report" in <i>Sky & Telescope,</i> 
              June 1984, p. 504. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Hunter, 
              T. & Goff, B. "Shielding the Night Sky" in <i>Astronomy,</i> Sep. 
              1988, p. 47. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Hunter, 
              T. & Crawford, D. "A Timetable for Better Lighting" in <i>Sky & 
              Telescope,</i> Mar. 1995, p. 96. Progress report on quest for better 
              public lighting in the Northeast. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Kosai, 
              H., et al. "Night Sky Brightness Over Japan" in <i>Sky & Telescope,</i> 
              Nov. 1992, p. 564. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Levy, 
              D. "Ten Dark-Sky Years" in <i>Sky & Telescope,</i> Sep. 1998, p. 
              32. On the work of the International Dark-Sky Association. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Mood, 
              J. & S. "Palomar and the Politics of Light Pollution" in <i>Astronomy,</i> 
              Nov. 1985, p. 6. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> O'Meara, 
              S. "Dark Nights are Safe Nights" in <i>Sky & Telescope,</i> Sep. 
              1998, p. 84. On ways you can help safeguard the night in your community.<br>
              </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sperling, 
              N. "Light Pollution: A Challenge for Astronomers" in <i>Mercury,</i> 
              Sep/Oct. 1986, p. 144. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Sullivan, 
              W. "Our Endangered Night Skies" in <i>Sky & Telescope,</i> May 1984, 
              p. 412. On how the pollution in light and radio waves can be detected 
              from space. </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Taubes, 
              G. "Twinkle, Twinkle, Great Big Bauble" in <i>Discover,</i> Nov. 
              1987, p. 60. On the ring of light the French had proposed putting 
              into space. </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Web 
              Sites </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <ul>
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.darksky.org/%7Eida/index.html" target="_blank">International 
                Dark-Sky Association (IDA)</a><br>
                - this non-profit organization works to inform the public about 
                light pollution and to minimize its effects; their web site is 
                a treasure-trove of information, advocacy, and news. </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.aas.org/%7Elight/pollution.html" target="_blank">American 
                Astronomical Society Committee on Light Pollution, Radio Interference 
                and Space Debris</a> <br>
                - has a more technical bibliography than ours, resolutions, web 
                links, etc. </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.dark-skies.freeserve.co.uk/" target="_blank">British 
                Astronomical Association Campaign for Dark Skies</a><br>
                - an organization in Great Britain that works with IDA; its site 
                has information sheets, newsletters, examples, links, etc. </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.skypub.com/resources/lightpollution/lightpollution.html" target="_blank">Sky 
                & Telescope Light Pollution Notes</a><br>
                - has the text of relevant articles from the magazine (for amateur 
                astronomers) and a set of notes updating issues around the world 
                by contributing editor Fred Schaaf.</font> 
            </ul>
            <p> </p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font> </p>
            <p><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="3" color="#6666CC"><a name="2">2. 
              Observatory Sites and the Environment</a> </font></b></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">While 
              many new observatories must now file an environmental impact statement, 
              the most publicized clash between astronomers and environmentalist 
              was over a site in Arizona called Mount Graham, where there was 
              some concerned over an endangered species of squirrel. </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Readings 
              </b> </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Waldrop, 
              M. "The Long, Sad Saga of Mount Graham" in <i>Science,</i> 22 June 
              1990, vol. 248, p. 1479. The story of how a new site for telescopes 
              in Arizona pits astronomers and environmentalists against each other. 
              </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Travis, 
              J. "Scopes and Squirrels Return to Court" in <i>Science,</i> 2 Sep. 
              1994, vol. 265, p. 1356. A sequel to the above. (Further updates 
              can be found on the web sites below.) </font></p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Muro, 
              M. "Array Plans Blocked by Indian Ritual Site" in <i>Science,</i> 
              10 Sep. 1999, vol. 285, p. 1650. How proximity to a native American 
              ritual sweat hut is endangering plans for an array of gamma-ray 
              telescopes. (Update in <i>Science,</i> 24 Dec. 1999, vol. 286, p. 
              2433.) </font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>Web 
              Sites </b></font></p>
            <ul>
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://medusa.as.arizona.edu/graham/graham.html" target="_blank">Mt. 
                Graham International Observatory Site</a><br>
                - this official web site for the U. of Arizona, summarizes cultural 
                and legal issues concerning the observatory site in the Coronado 
                national forest, including the squirrels issue and concerns by 
                some members of Native American tribes about disturbing sacred 
                burial grounds </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://clavius.as.arizona.edu/vo/ecology.html" target="_blank">Vatican 
                Observatory Statement on Mount Graham and Ecology</a><br>
                - a position paper by one of the observatories using Mt. Graham. 
                </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.seds.org/nodes/NODEv5n1-4.html" target="_blank">Mt. 
                Graham: Non-controversy of the Decade</a><br>
                - an opinion piece strongly favoring the observatory, by a space 
                enthusiast </font> 
              <li> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><a href="http://www.phoenixnew%20times.com/1997/071196/vs-1.html" target="_blank">"Star 
                Gate"</a><br>
                - this article from an alternative news weekly in Phoenix tells 
                the Mt. Graham story from the point of view of the environmental 
                activists, a radically different perspective from the astronomers. 
                </font> 
            </ul>
