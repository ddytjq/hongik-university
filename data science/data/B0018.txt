            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><br>
            <b><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Mortgage
            Process</span></font></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">With
            many of the processing functions relating to the origination and
            servicing of mortgage loans outsourced, Kensington&#146;s management
            team is able to focus on the more critical areas of the business
            including:<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">approving
                loan applications;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">maintaining
                reconciliation and other financial controls over the systems
                operated by HML;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">reviewing
                the arrears management and collections process;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">developing
                relationships with the providers of short-term loan facilities;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">developing
                securitisation structures for off-balance sheet funding;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoBodyText"><font face="Verdana" size="2">controlling
                the marketing and advertising of Kensington&#146;s products and
                promoting the brand<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;</span>name;</font></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">developing
                new products and the ongoing review of existing products; and</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">establishing
                relationships with Packagers and providing on-site support for
                their Kensington related activities.<o:p>
                </o:p>
                </font></span></li>
            </ul>
              <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">The
              Kensington mortgage process is split into six constituent parts:<o:p>
              </o:p>
              </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Distribution
                Management;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Product
                Development;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Risk
                Management and Underwriting;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Funding
                and Securitisation;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Customer
                Administration; and</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Knowledge
                Management.<o:p>
                </o:p>
                </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b>Loan
            categories<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </b>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">There
            are currently four broad loan categories, which are sub-divided into
            a number of products. The four loan categories are:<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">&#147;Status&#148;
                where borrowers must prove their income in support of their loan
                application;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">&#147;Self-certified&#148;
                where borrowers are not required to fully prove their income in
                support of their loan application;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">&#147;Buy
                to Let&#148; where the property is let by the borrower as an
                investment; and</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">&#147;Right
                to Buy&#148; where borrowers are purchasing properties from a local
                authority under the Housing Act 1985.<o:p>
                </o:p>
                </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2">For
            details of our current products, please <a href="../ProductWizard/ProductOverview.htm">click
            here</a></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b>Rates
            and charges<o:p></o:p>
            </b>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Interest
            rates are set quarterly and are a fixed percentage over either the
            London Inter Bank Offered Rate (&quot;LIBOR&quot;)
            or the Kensington Variable Rate, &quot;KVR&quot;. Kensington finances its mortgage
            portfolio through short-term loan facilities which carry interest
            rates fixed by reference to &quot;LIBOR&quot; and a margin.
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">In
            addition, Kensington offers fixed and discount rates for a short
            initial time period on certain loans.
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">All
            borrowers are required to pay early redemption charges if the loan
            is repaid in the first three years. The level of charge depends on
            the product variant chosen by the borrower, but is set prior to
            completion of the loan. All applicants are also charged a fee on
            completion of the loan.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b>Ancillary
            Products<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </b>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            requires its borrowers to have buildings insurance over the security
            property. Kensington offers to arrange for its borrowers certain
            insurance cover, most importantly buildings and contents insurance
            and mortgage payment protection cover. These insurance products are
            underwritten by large recognised insurance underwriters.<o:p> Kensington's
            borrowers are, however, under no obligation to purchase any such
            products from Kensington.</o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Risk
            Management and Underwriting<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><i>Underwriting
            process<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </i>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            uses a carefully controlled underwriting process to assess loan
            applications and to ensure that its lending criteria are applied
            rigorously. All underwriters are issued with comprehensive lending
            policy manuals and operational procedures manuals to reinforce the
            underwriting methodology. Packagers receive manuals providing
            guidance on lending criteria, procedures and types of borrower and
            property acceptable to Kensington. This information is in turn
            circulated to other intermediaries. This makes the loan approval
            process more efficient because applications submitted to Packagers,
            and in turn from them to Kensington&#146;s underwriters, are more
            likely to conform to the lending criteria.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington's
            underwriters review each application for a mortgage loan (whether
            or not submitted via a Packager) and ensure that it conforms to
            Kensington&#146;s lending criteria. Only when an application has been
            reviewed and approved by a Kensington underwriter is an offer of a
            mortgage loan issued. <o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">To
            assist the underwriters and to ensure consistency of data capture,
            Kensington has developed bespoke underwriting software. This
            software reinforces the underwriting procedures by checking the data
            against Kensington&#146;s lending criteria for the product being
            approved.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Packagers<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Packagers
            are only appointed after an extensive checking and approval process,
            including visits by senior sales and underwriting managers. When
            appointed, all Packagers must be registered with the &quot;MCCB&quot; and hold a
            current &quot;CCA&quot; License. The Management Credit Committee gives the final
            approval for new Packagers and reviews relationships with and the
            performance of existing Packagers.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Funding
            and Securitisation<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            finances the origination of mortgages on balance sheet using
            short-term loan facilities typically for a term of 364 days with a
            variable interest rate calculated by reference to LIBOR plus a
            margin. It then securitises the loan portfolios, removing them from
            the balance sheet, and uses part of the proceeds to repay the
            short-term loan facilities.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Short-term
            loan facilities<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            originates mortgages through the Origination Subsidiaries, each
            financed by a separate short-term loan facility.</font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Each
            facility is fully secured by a fixed and floating charge over all of
            the assets of the Origination Subsidiary
            including the mortgage loans.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">The
            short-term loan facilities are repaid following each securitisation
            by an amount related to the principal balances of the mortgage loans
            sold.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <o:p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><b>Securitisation<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </b>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Background<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            uses the financing technique of securitisation to gain access to
            long-term funds to support its origination process. When sufficient
            numbers of mortgage loans have been originated by the Origination
            Subsidiaries, they are assigned to an &quot;SPV&quot; Company, which issues debt
            securities secured by the underlying mortgage assets. The SPV
            Company uses the proceeds from the sale of the debt securities to
            pay Kensington for the mortgage loans. Kensington uses part of these
            proceeds to repay the short-term loan facilities.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Market
            Positioning<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            was the first specialist non-conforming mortgage lender in the UK to
            carry out securitisations:&nbsp;
            </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"> with securities denominated in Euros and
            US dollars;
            </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">including Detachable A Coupons and Mortgage Early
            Redemption Certificates; and&nbsp;
            </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2"> including prefunding.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">RMS
            1 and RMS 2 securities were upgraded in August 2000 by Standard
            &amp; Poor&#146;s and Fitch with securities originally rated A upgraded
            to AA, and those originally rated BB being
            upgraded to BBB.<o:p>
            RMS 1 closed down in march 2001. RMS 3 and RMS 4 were upgraded in
            March 2001</o:p>
            by Fitch Rating with Class M notes rated A being upgraded to AA and
            Class B notes rated BB being upgraded to BBB.
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">The
            most recent transaction, RMS 9, had three AAA rated Note tranches
            (Sterling slow pay, US Dollar fast pay and US Dollar slow pay) which
            were sold to 23 investors (5 Sterling slow pay, 8 US Dollar fast
            pay, 10 US Dollar slow pay). The investors comprised banks, building
            societies and funds from the UK, Europe, the USA and Australasia.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Structure<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">The
            RMS deals are structured with the senior rated bonds supported by
            subordinated bonds, liquidity facilities and reserve funds. The
            senior bonds are rated AAA and typically account for 87-88 per cent.
            of the transaction. The subordinated bonds are rated A and either
            BBB or BB. The size of the reserve fund (typically 1-2 per cent. of
            the transaction) and the liquidity facility depends on the exact
            structure chosen for the transaction.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Investments<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            purchases certain of the securities issued by SPV Companies, funding
            its acquisition by a secured term loan facility from, typically, one
            of the lenders providing short-term credit facilities to the
            Origination Subsidiaries. These securities are:<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1;
tab-stops:list .75in;mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana">(i)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            &nbsp;&nbsp; </span>Detachable A Coupons: DACs are AAA rated securities which
            represent an interest only entitlement on the senior class (AAA) of
            notes issued in respect of a securitisation by the SPV Company. They
            carry no right to any principal payment and the payment entitlement
            is contingent upon the relevant senior notes, to which the relevant
            DACs were originally attached, remaining outstanding. The senior
            class of notes in respect of each securitisation is redeemed out of
            principal receipts from borrowers, prior to </font></span><font face="Verdana"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">payment
            of the subordinated notes. High prepayment rates in respect of
            mortgage loans will cause the senior notes to be redeemed quicker
            and will therefore reduce the value of the DACs.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1;
tab-stops:list .75in;mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana">(ii)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            </span></font></span><font face="Verdana"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Mortgage
            Early Redemption Certificates: MERCs are AAA rated securities which
            represent a pass through entitlement to receive redemption charges
            paid to an SPV Company by a borrower upon early redemption of the
            relevant loan. A high rate of early repayment of loans will result
            in an increase in the value of the MERC. Consequently, the MERCs
            provide a partial hedge to the DACs in respect of increasing and
            reducing prepayment rates.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1;
tab-stops:list .75in;mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana">(iii)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></font></span><font face="Verdana"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">C
            Notes: to fund various expenses of, and reserves required in, a
            securitisation, Kensington will purchase C Notes issued by the SPV
            Company. These securities will suffer any losses incurred as a
            result of there being insufficient excess cash over the life of the
            securitisation to pay the C Note interest due and to repay the C
            Note principal. The reserve fund released on the maturity of the
            securitisation can also be used to repay Kensington (as holder of
            the C Notes). In earlier
            transactions these Notes were referred to as start-up loans.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in;mso-list:l0 level1 lfo1;
tab-stops:list .75in;mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana">(iv)<span style="font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            </span></font></span><font face="Verdana"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Residual
            Certificates: RCs carry an entitlement to all residual revenue (less
            a small retained profit after the C Notes have been repaid) received
            by the SPV Companies. Earlier transactions provided for the payment
            of deferred consideration represented by the residual revenue
            generated by the SPV Companies. Kensington does not attribute any
            balance sheet value to either the Residual Certificates or the
            deferred consideration. Any income is accounted for on receipt.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">These
            SPV Investments are valued by Kensington at original cost. The cost
            of the DACs and MERCs are amortised monthly over their estimated
            life. The net balance sheet value of the C Notes is reviewed monthly
            for possible impairment. </span>
            </font>
            </o:p>
            </p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Prefunding<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">In
            the more recent securitisations, the relevant SPV Companies
            have raised, by way of funding in the capital markets, principal in
            excess of the sum used to acquire the initial pool of loans. The
            excess is placed on deposit with a suitably rated bank at a
            guaranteed minimum interest rate and is used over a period
            (typically not exceeding three months) to enable the SPV Company to
            acquire further loans from the relevant loan originators. This
            process is known as &#147;prefunding&#148; and enables Kensington to
            reduce the use of short-term loan facilities.
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Recourse
            to Kensington following Securitisation<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Once
            a portfolio of loans has been sold by the Origination Subsidiary to
            the SPV Company, Kensington retains liability in relation to the
            loans sold in respect of:<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">representations
                and warranties provided by the Origination Subsidiary in favour
                of the SPV Company, in relation to, amongst other things,
                compliance with lending and eligibility criteria and
                non-default; and</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">where
                the value of Unverified Loans exceeds the assumptions made for
                the purpose of that securitisation,
            the Origination Subsidiary&#146;s obligation to repurchase loans with a
            face value equal to such excess.<o:p>
                &nbsp;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <o:p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><i><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Arrears
            and Loss Mitigation<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></i></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Through
            the process of securitisation, Kensington sells portfolios of
            mortgage loans to SPV Companies for cash which the SPV Companies
            raise by issuing debt securities in the capital markets. By selling
            the mortgages, Kensington transfers the principal risk of default
            and associated losses on those mortgage loans to the SPV Companies.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Under
            the securitisation structure, Norland Capital Limited assumes the role of
            special servicer responsible for providing instructions to HML and
            exercising certain discretions relating to the portfolio on behalf
            of the SPV Company, for which Norland Capital Limited receives a fee.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">As
            described above, Kensington also purchases SPV Investments,
            including C Notes. The total return on C Notes over their lives can
            be impaired if the performance of the portfolio, in particular as
            regards arrears and losses, turns out to be significantly worse than
            expected. Kensington is thus further incentivised to ensure the
            optimum performance of the portfolio in providing services to the
            SPV Company.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">The
            arrears and loss performance of the securitised portfolios is
            important because this information will be available to potential
            investors in future securitisations who will take into account the
            performance of existing securitised portfolios when deciding whether
            to invest in a new issue. Good historic portfolio performance will
            assist Kensington in executing future securitisations.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Customer
            Administration<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington
            contracts with Homeloan Management Limited for the provision of
            administration, processing and issuing of mortgage loan offers, and,
            following completion of the loan, servicing of Kensington&#146;s loan
            portfolio and the mortgage portfolios of the SPV Companies,
            including the collection of interest, fees and other payments due
            from borrowers. HML is a subsidiary of Skipton Building Society and
            services loan portfolios.</span></font> <font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">HML provides full time employees dedicated to
            servicing and administration for Kensington. In addition, Kensington
            employs its own arrears managers, underwriting managers and underwriters
            who are located in HML&#146;s offices underwriting loans and
            monitoring, supervising and managing the administration processes.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Traditional
            lenders might not make any contact with a borrower until the
            borrower is 60 to 90 days in default on an interest payment.
            Kensington&#146;s policy is pro-active, and it has put in place a
            number of processes, which HML implements to Kensington&#146;s service
            standards, designed to reduce losses:<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Borrowers
                are required to make payment by direct debit;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Telephone
                numbers are obtained for each borrower prior to the completion
                of their loan and a reminder call is made to them before
                payment is due for the first month. For certain borrowers
                reminder calls are made for the first six payments;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Post-completion
                counselling is offered to certain borrowers;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">A
                telephone call is normally made to all borrowers within 48 hours
                of HML being notified the first time a payment is missed;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Debit
                card payments are accepted from borrowers in arrears;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Arrears
                counsellors are instructed to visit the majority of borrowers
                where one or two payments are overdue and no arrangement to make
                payment has been agreed;</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">During
                the litigation process, HML continues to try to reach a payment
                solution with the borrower; and</font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li>
                <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Pre-eviction
                counselling is currently provided to the majority of defaulting
                borrowers prior to repossession.<o:p>
                </o:p>
                </font></span></li>
            </ul>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Knowledge
            Management<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            has developed information systems that capture and track information
            on Kensington&#146;s borrowers and their loan history, referred to as
            the Data Warehouse. The
            information is available to Kensington&#146;s management, and
            Kensington also offers limited access to the Data
            Warehouse to third parties (for example, allowing selected Packagers
            to track the progress of their application on-line and bondholders
            to see the performance of the mortgage portfolios underlying
            securitisations on a dedicated website).<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            utilises its Data Warehouse to help to plan and manage its business.
            The Data Warehouse provides information to assist in origination
            (through better product design, distribution targeting, pricing,
            market positioning and informed lending), funding (improved
            portfolio management and transaction structuring) and customer
            administration (management of arrears, loss mitigation and
            Kensington&#146;s ancillary products and services, including building
            and contents insurance, mortgage payment protection, and further
            advances).</font></span>
