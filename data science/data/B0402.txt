<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>Robert Hollinshead, chief 
  executive, commented:</B> </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">"These results show another 
  outstanding year for the Newcastle Building Society. We are committed to remaining 
  a mutual society and our performance over the last year provides a clear indication 
  that our strategy is working. Our mortgage and investment products have consistently 
  compared favourably to those offered by the banks, resulting in our reporting 
  above average performance in both the savings and mortgage markets. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">"As a mutual, we aim to 
  achieve a level of profit which is sufficient to sustain the strong capital 
  base of the Society. The increase reported in these figures is exactly in line 
  with this policy of profit sufficiency and it is particularly encouraging to 
  have achieved that key objective, while at the same time giving more back to 
  our members in the form of a lower net interest margin." </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>Financial Performance</B> 
  </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Profit after tax has risen 
  from &pound;10.30m to &pound;11.31m, an increase of 9.8%. This has been achieved 
  despite the Society reducing the net interest margin, that is the difference 
  between what savers are paid and what borrowers are charged, from 1.39% to 1.28%. 
  </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Group management expenses 
  have grown by only 3.5% despite a significant investment in new technology. 
  This has lead to a reduction in the management expense ratio from 1.10% to 1.02%. 
  </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The charge for bad and doubtful 
  debts has fallen from &pound;2.3m to &pound;0.6m, reflecting the excellent quality 
  of the mortgage portfolio. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Total assets have increased 
  from &pound;2.15bn to &pound;2.41bn, an increase of 12.2%. The growth in both 
  mortgage and savings balances is significantly ahead of what was achieved in 
  1999. </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>Wider services through 
  technology</B> </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The joint venture company 
  between the Newcastle (75 per cent holding) and Melton Mowbray Building Society 
  (25 per cent holding), was the first of its kind involving UK building societies 
  when it was launched in 1997. The company, Newton Facilities Management Limited 
  (NFML), continues to provide managed information technology-based services which 
  are helping to spread the costs of service provision for smaller societies and 
  other financial institutions. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Most recently, NFML secured 
  a contract to provide a full range of IT support services to Stafford Railway 
  Building Society. Under the agreement, NFML will provide all of the software, 
  hardware and associated services, required by the Stafford Railway. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In 1999, the success of 
  the Newcastle's call centre led to the Society securing a contract to handle 
  the administration of direct mortgage sales on behalf of Kensington Mortgage 
  Company. That success was repeated in 2000, when the Newcastle was appointed 
  by Mortgages PLC to provide a mortgage packaging and call centre service. </font>
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>More choice through technology</B> 
  </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In June, the Newcastle became 
  one of the first societies to offer an on-line savings account via the Internet. 
  It took just nine months to turn the concept of NewcastleNet savings into reality 
  and while security difficulties have dogged some of the other financial institutions 
  which have since launched similar accounts, the Newcastle's has operated without 
  problems and within published service levels, while generating large volumes 
  of business for the Society. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In 2001, the Newcastle will 
  be launching a range of online products and services which will keep it at the 
  technological forefront of the financial services industry. As an example, the 
  Society will set up a virtual shopping mall from which customers can purchase 
  products at prices lower than those available on the High Street. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">In recognition that not 
  everyone has access to the required technology, Internet terminals will be placed 
  in branches during the course of 2001. This will enable branch customers to 
  access a wider range of the Society's products and services, in an environment 
  which is familiar to them. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>Increased efficiency 
  through technology</B> </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">During the year, a significant 
  investment was made in workflow and image technology. The new system, which 
  incorporates the very latest technology in this field, went live in December 
  and will improve efficiency, leading to even higher standards of customer service 
  and greater flexibility in working patterns for staff. </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The roll out of the Society's 
  customer management system was completed in 2000. This provides a comprehensive 
  picture of the relationship the Society has with its customers and ensures that 
  products and services are better matched to individual customer requirements. 
  </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><B>Outlook</B> </font>
<P> 
<P><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Newcastle Building Society 
  is well placed to meet the challenges of a rapidly changing and increasingly 
  competitive financial services industry. Technology is transforming the way 
  business is transacted and the expectations that customers have of financial 
  institutions. The Society is at the forefront of new developments and will continue 
  to utilise technology wherever it is appropriate and beneficial to do so, while 
  recognising the value of the branch network to a great many of its members. 
  </font>
