<p>A selection of the Marco Polo Cycling Club has traveled to China to
participated in the Tour de China 2001. This years tour took place in
Inner-Mongolia, July 26th to 29th. One of the favourites for the victory was the
president of the club; Nathan Dahlberg. He was helped by Raymond Raadtgever,
Casper Helling, Andries Bosma and Rob Conijn. Sprinter Dennis Hammink was going
for stage wins and the points jersey.</p>

you interested in international cycling become a member of the Marco Polo

<p class="MsoNormal">Report (by Team Manager Anno Pedersen):</p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">The team
had four days in Beijing for getting used to the time difference and changes of
food and climate. According to the Marco Polo jetlag and survival strategy, long
training rides where done from day one (90; 120 and 170 km), meals where ordered
where the locals eat (they know how to stay healthy). With training and
touristing the days where full and everybody slept well.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">The
traffic in Beijing is chaotic, but slow, great for some rodeo riding, motor
pacing and high speed manoeuvrings. The Chinese act like they see a bunch of
crazy bike riders every day! Of course we had our own team crit on the Tianmen
square again. After the four days in Beijing, we took the night train to Hohhot
City, Inner Mongolia and in the train we slept very well too, in the ‘hard
sleepers’, no extra luxury for us. On the train we met our strongest
competitors for the race, the Kazakhstan national team with Kravchenko and
Lavreneko, the numbers one and two from the GC in 2000.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-GB" lang="EN-GB">July
25th</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">No
touristing anymore from now, the race starting tomorrow and we are here to win.
Talks with riders and a team meeting, team managers meeting and other
preparations fill my day. I am here not only as team director, but also as
massager and mechanic. The team budget cannot bear costs of airplane tickets so
one fool has to suffer… Is this what they call investing in a great project? A
lot of work to be done, but Nathan and Dennis are also excellent mechanics and
know what’s going on. The riders will get the rest they need, not me though…</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">The
profile of the stages is not really mountainous, every second will be important,
is our judgement, this will turn out to be true. Teams from Japan (national),
Australia, Hong Kong (national), Taipei, Mongolia (national) and China have also
turned up and fill the 62 rider field.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-GB" lang="EN-GB">July
26th</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-ansi-language:EN-GB">In the
first stage Nathan gets the chance to gain time, in a four riders group. We
decide that this is a great opportunity and start blocking in the pack.
Lavrenenko is in the leading group, but not Kravchenko. Lavrenenko wins the
stage and gains four bonus seconds on Dahlberg who finishes second.</span></p>
