            <p class="introtitle">Tell me about Stakeholder&nbsp;Pensions</p>
            <p class="body">Below is our guide to Stakeholder Pensions. It covers 
              everything you need to set up and run a Stakeholder Pension scheme 
              for your employees.</p>
            <p><span class="body">To go to a particular area of interest, click 
              the relevant link below. If you would like to print out the whole 
              page, click the "print" button to the right.</span></p>
            <p></p>
            <p class="body">For details of how you and your staff can benefit 
              from a Stakeholder Pension, <a href="benefits.htm">click&nbsp;here</a>.</p>
            <p><span class="body"><a href="#reason-for-introduction">Reason for introduction</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#flexibility">Flexibility</a><br>
			  </span><span class="body"><a href="#contributions">Contributions</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#charges">Charges</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#regulation">Regulation</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#eligibility">Eligibility</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#selecting-a-stakeholder-pension-scheme">Selecting a Stakeholder Pension scheme</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#contributing-to-the-scheme">Contributing to the scheme</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#payroll-deduction">Payroll deduction</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#forwarding-employee-contributions-taken-from-payroll">Forwarding employee contributions taken from payroll</a><br>
              </span><span class="body"><a href="#scheme-registration">Scheme registration</a></span></p>
            <p class="body"><span class="bodytitle"><a name="reason-for-introduction">Reason for introduction</a></span> 
              <br>
              The Government announced its intention to reform pensions from April 
              2001. A full copy of the Government's document (published on 25th 
              May 2000) can be viewed by visiting the Department of Social Security 
              website, <a href="javascript:leavingHSBC('../leaving-hsbc.htm','http://www.dss.gov.uk')">www.dss.gov.uk</a> 
              . This document outlines the key proposals to change the face of 
              pensions. </p>
            <p class="body">A new simple, flexible and low cost pension will be 
              introduced known as a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension')">Stakeholder 
              Pension</a></span>. The low charges and product simplicity should 
              make retirement planning more attractive and easier for many people. 
              Individuals will normally be able to take the pension benefits built 
              up from a Stakeholder Pension in addition to <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#state-pension')">State 
              pension</a></span> and other pension benefits. </p>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="flexibility">Flexibility</a></span><br>
            <ul>
              <li> <span class="body">Stakeholder Pensions are low cost and flexible 
                retirement savings plans. </span></li>
              <li><span class="body"> Contributions from as little as £20 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                can be made, whether this is a regular or single payment. </span></li>
              <li><span class="body"> Your employees will be able to stop and 
                start contributions whenever it suits them without charge. </span></li>
              <li><span class="body"> Your employees will be able to transfer 
                their Stakeholder Pensions between Stakeholder Pension scheme 
                providers. </span></li>
              <li><span class="body"> Contributions of up to £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                per person each year can be paid without any earnings. </span></li>
              <li><span class="body"> Contributions over £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                will also be allowed. The maximum levels will depend on age and 
                earnings, as with current personal pension plans. Please see the 
                table below for details.</span></li>
            </ul>
            <p><span class="bodytitle"><a name="contributions">Contributions</a></span> <br>
              <span class="body">The Inland Revenue sets contributions levels 
              based on age and earnings. These levels are shown in the table below. 
              These are the total contributions that can be made by the employer 
              and the employee. </span></p>
            <table width="440" border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0" height="119">
              <tr bgcolor="#CCCCCC"> 
                <td colspan="2" class="bodybold">Inland Revenue limits on contributions 
                  into a personal pension per tax year.</td>
              </tr>
              <tr> 
                <td bgcolor="#CCCCCC" class="bodybold" height="183" width="110" valign="top"> 
                  <p>Age 35* and under<br>
                    <br>
                    Age 36* to 45 <br>
                    <br>
                    Age 46* to 50<br>
                    <br>
                    Age 51* to 55 <br>
                    <br>
                    Age 56* to 60 <br>
                    <br>
                    Age 61* to 74 </p>
                </td>
                <td bgcolor="#F3F3F3" height="183" width="194" class="body" valign="top">17.5% 
                  of <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#net-relevant-earnings')">net 
                  relevant earnings</a></span> <br>
                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  20% of net relevant earnings <br>
                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  25% of net relevant earnings <br>
                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  30% of net relevant earnings<br>
                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  35% of net relevant earnings <br>
                  up to £91,800** <br>
                  40% of net relevant earnings <br>
                  up to £91,800**</td>
              </tr>
            </table>
            <p><span class="body">*Age at beginning of the tax year. <br>
              **The earnings cap for 2000/2001 tax year. This may change in the 
              future. </span></p>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="charges">Charges</a></span> <br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> The only charge that can be taken from a Stakeholder Pension 
                is an <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#annual-management-charge')">annual management charge</a></span>. 
                This is limited to a maximum of 1% of the pension fund each year. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="regulation">Regulation</a></span> <br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> The <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">Occupational Pensions Regulatory 
                Authority (OPRA)</a></span> will regulate the operation of all schemes 
                and maintain a register of them. Employers responsible for offering 
                a Stakeholder Pension will also need to ensure the Stakeholder 
                Pension they have designated for their employees is continually 
                registered with <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">OPRA</a></span>. </li>
              <li> The <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#fsa')">Financial Services Authority (FSA)</a></span> 
                will regulate the marketing of schemes. <br>
              </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="eligibility">Eligibility</a></span> <br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> To take out a Stakeholder Pension, your employees must be <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#uk-resident')">resident 
                and ordinarily resident in the UK</a></span>. </li>
              <li> They must be under the age of 75. There is no minimum age. 
              </li>
              <li> Stakeholder Pensions may be held at the same time as other 
                <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension')">Stakeholder Pensions</a>, <a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#personal-pension-plan')">Personal Pension 
                Plans</a>, <a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#sipp')">Self-Invested Personal Pensions</a></span> and <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#retirement-annuity-contract')">Retirement 
                Annuity Contracts</a></span> as long as total contributions do not 
                exceed the relevant limits. </li>
              <li> Employees who are members of <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#occupational-pension-scheme')">occupational 
                pension schemes</a></span> can contribute up to £3,600 <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#gross')">gross</a></span> 
                per year into a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension')">Stakeholder 
                Pension</a></span> provided they earn less than £30,000 per year. 
                <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#controlling-director')">Controlling 
                directors</a></span> cannot contribute to a <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension')">Stakeholder 
                Pension</a></span>.<br>
              </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="selecting-a-stakeholder-pension-scheme">Selecting a Stakeholder Pension scheme</a></span> 
            <br>
            <ul>
              <li><span class="body">If you are setting up a Stakeholder Pension 
                scheme it will be your responsibility to choose one which all 
                eligible employees will be able to join. You will however, be 
                required to consult with your employees (or their representatives) 
                on the choice of <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension-provider')">Stakeholder 
                Pension provider</a></span>, although the final decision will 
                ultimately be yours. </span></li>
            </ul>
            <ul>
              <li><span class="body">The process of selecting a scheme should 
                be neutral, with you making enough information available about 
                the Stakeholder Pension for employees to make an informed decision. 
                Do not provide them with any advice on retirement planning. Please 
                note that even after you have selected your Stakeholder Pension 
                your employees will not have to join the scheme you are offering. 
                </span></li>
            </ul>
           <span class="bodytitle"><a name="contributing-to-the-scheme">Contributing to the scheme</a></span><br>
            <ul>
              <li><span class="body">Once you have chosen your scheme you will 
                need to decide whether you want to make a contribution on your 
                employees' behalf, and if so how much the contribution will be. 
                This is a major decision for your business but there are benefits 
                in doing do. <a href="benefits.htm">Click here</a> to find out 
                about these benefits.</span><br>
              </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="payroll-deduction">Payroll deduction</a></span><br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> You must have a payroll deduction facility in operation if 
                the employee wishes to make contributions direct from their wages. 
                This will involve collecting the employee's contributions from 
                their net basic pay and paying the money into a bank account nominated 
                by yourself for the Stakeholder Pension provider to collect by 
                Direct Debit. It is, however, up to each individual employee how 
                they make their own contributions. They can decide to make these 
                by Direct Debit. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> Where employee contributions are deducted from payroll, you 
                are obliged to make changes to contributions (increase, decrease 
                or stop) requested by the employee, at least every six months. 
                <br>
                <br>
                Note: if an employee requests a change to their contributions, 
                you can decline to make that change for up to six months after 
                the previous change. If you decline a change you must inform the 
                employee in writing that you are not complying with their request 
                and inform them when the change will be made. You can, however, 
                accept employee changes to their contributions as frequently as 
                you like. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> Employees electing to make contributions to their Stakeholder 
                Pension from payroll will need to provide you with a form of authority. 
                HSBC can provide a copy of this form for you to pass to your employees. 
                The payroll deduction authority will provide you with sufficient 
                information from each of your employees to set them up on the 
                internet. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> If you choose HSBC to administer your Stakeholder Pension, 
                we will provide you with an online schedule of the payments that 
                are due to be made by you and your employees, ten days before 
                your business account is due to be debited. You will then be required 
                to authorise online that this payment can be taken. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> For example, if you are paying monthly you will need to authorise 
                the amount we take each month prior to the date the payment is 
                due for collection. You will need to ensure that the correct amounts 
                are taken from your employees and the exact amount of money is 
                available to be debited from your nominated business account. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> The payment of contributions should be authorised so that payment 
                can be taken by HSBC no later than the 19th of the month following 
                payroll deduction. </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="forwarding-employee-contributions-taken-from-payroll">Forwarding employee contributions taken from payroll</a></span><br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> Where you deduct employees' contributions from their salary 
                and/or have agreed to make contributions on behalf on an employee, 
                these will need to be sent to the <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension-provider')">Stakeholder 
                Pension provider</a></span> within the timescale set out by <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">OPRA</a></span>. 
                This is 19 days after the end of the month in which the contribution 
                was collected from an employee or the month in which you agreed 
                to make a contribution on behalf of an employee. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> Failure to do so may result in legal proceedings being brought 
                against the business by <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">OPRA</a></span>. For 
                example, if a payroll deduction is made on 28 March, you will 
                need to have sent the payment onto the Stakeholder Pension provider 
                by the 19 April. </li>
            </ul>
            <span class="bodytitle"><a name="scheme-registration">Scheme registration</a></span><br>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> You will also be responsible for checking that the <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#stakeholder-pension-provider')">Stakeholder 
                Pension scheme provider</a></span> you have selected is registered 
                with OPRA. Once the scheme has been selected you will also be 
                responsible for checking on a regular basis that the scheme is 
                still registered as a Stakeholder Pension scheme. </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> The HSBC Life (UK) Limited Stakeholder Pension Scheme is registered 
                with <span class="bodybold"><a href="javascript:popUp('../jargon-buster.htm#opra')">OPRA</a></span>. 
              </li>
            </ul>
            <ul class="body">
              <li> OPRA will maintain a register confirming which product providers 
                are authorised to conduct Stakeholder Pension business. This can 
                be viewed on their website <a href="../../offsite.asp?url=http://www.opra.gov.uk" target="_blank">www.opra.gov.uk</a>. 
              </li>
            </ul>
