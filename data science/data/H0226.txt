</font> <b><font size="+2" face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Plant 
Elements</font></b> 
<table width="700" border="0">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="3"> 
      <p><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Plant elements 
        seen in feces may resemble eggs and cysts of parasites.</font></b></p>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr valign="middle"> 
    <td colspan="3"> 
      <p>&nbsp;</p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Phase Contrast 
        x 400</b></font></p>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr> 
    <td width="28%" align="left"><img src="image/16plantcell.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="16 - Plant element - phase contrast x 400"></td>
    <td valign="middle" width="45%"> 
      <p align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">These 
        objects may be mistaken for thin shelled hookworm eggs or decorticated 
        Ascaris eggs. </font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The irregular 
        contour of the outer membrane and the general appearance should suggest 
        the plant origin of this microscopic finding. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td valign="middle" width="27%" align="right"><img src="image/87plantelements.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="87 - Plant element - phase contrast x 400"></td>

<table width="700">
  <tr> 
    <td> 
      <p><img src="image/15plantmat.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="15 - Plant material - phase contrast x400"></p>
    </td>
    <td> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Pollen grains<a name="pollen"></a> 
        may closely resemble Taenia eggs. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Careful study of the 
        inner mass reveals its lack of typical features of an oncosphere normally 
        seen in Taenia eggs (embryonic hooklets, etc.). </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Absence of the radial 
        striation is also a good clue. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td width="34%" align="right"><img src="image/13plantmat.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="13 - Plant material - phase contrast x 400"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<hr width="700" align="left">
<table width="700">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="2" width="225"> 
      <p align="left"><img src="image/14plantelement.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="14 - Plant element - phase contrast x 400"></p>
    </td>
    <td width="485"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">This object could 
        be mistaken for a thin shelled hookworm egg. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The irregular contour 
        of the outer membrane and the lack of the bluntly rounded ends suggest 
        its plant origin. </font></p>
    </td>
  </tr></table>
<hr width="700" align="left">
<table width="700">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="2"> 
      <p align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">This 
        artifact could be confused with an unembryonated egg of Diphyllobothrium 
        latum. </font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><br>
        Lack of an operculum and the uneveness of the cell wall indicate that 
        this object is of plant origin. </font></p>
      <p align="left"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">A small 
        knob-like structure is somewhat evident in this picture which could lead 
        to further confusion. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td width="30%"><img src="image/88plantelement_lbl.jpg" width="200" height="200" alt="188 - Plant element - D. latum artifact - phase contrast x 400"></td>

    <td> 
      <p><b><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Trichrome Stain 
        x 1000</font></b></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">These pollen grains 
        stand out due to their golden colour. They do not pick up the stain. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The striated outer 
        wall may lead to confusion with Taenia eggs, but the small size, uneven 
        thickness of the wall and lack of internal contents do not suggest an 
        egg. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td><img src="image/52pollen.jpg" width="300" height="225" alt="52 - Pollen grains - trichrome stain x 1000"></td>

<table width="700">
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td colspan="2"><b><font size="+2" face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><a name="Plant_hairs">Root 
      hairs</a> &amp; other plant elements</font></b></td>
  </tr>
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td width=510> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">These are fuzz from 
        peaches seen frequently in stools. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">They can be confused 
        with Strongyloides or other nematode larvae by the inexperienced eye. 
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The refractile core 
        should not be mistaken for the well differentiated esophagus or intestine 
        of a nematode larva. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td width=227 align="right"><img src="image/4planthair.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="4 - Plant hair  - phase contrast x 100"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table width="700">
  <tr> 
    <td width=312 align="left"> 
      <div align="left"><img src="image/5planthair.jpg" width="300" height="225" align="left" alt="Plant hairs - phase contrast x 100"> 
      </div>
    
    <td width=425> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Although one end of 
        the hair may be tapering and smoothly rounded as a nematode larvae, the 
        other end usually is blunt and irregular a feature not seen in a larval 
        parasite. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Root hairs are usually 
        straight, but when flexed (on the left), can be interpreted as larvae. 
        </font></p>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>

      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Note the lack of the

        object is not like the typical flagellum of Giardia. This object appears 
        to be a spore that is germinating. </font></p>
