        <td vAlign="top" width="500" height="1">The WHO Human Genetics Programme (HGN) aims to
        develop genetic approaches to control the most common hereditary diseases and those having
        a genetic predisposition. In addition, it seeks to provide a response for WHO's direction
        and support to the many questions posed by current genetic challenges. HGN continues to
        strengthen community approaches to the control of genetic diseases and to develop a
        network of international collaborating programmes. In concert with various nongovernmental
        and international organizations, the WHO Human Genetics Programme covers the following
        medical genetic areas: </td>

          <li>Monogenic diseases (thalassaemias and sickle cell disorder, cystic fibrosis,
            haemochromatosis, haemophilia);</li>
          <li>Congenital disorders (monitoring of congenital malformations);</li>
          <li>Common diseases with a genetic predisposition (cardiovascular diseases, familial
            hypercholesterolaemia, cancer, diabetis mellitus, mental disorders);</li>
          <li>Community genetic service delivery;</li>
          <li>New genetic knowledge which includes monitoring of human genome research and the
            ethical, legal and social aspects of its application to practice;</li>
          <li>Education and training aimed at communities and health professionals (guidelines,
            education materials).</li>
