<H1>Bank's Brewery West Midlands (Regional) League</H1>
<H2>Founded: 1889</H2>
<P>The West Midlands (Regional) League was formed as the Birmingham &amp;
District League on 31 May 1889 which makes it one of the oldest leagues in
existence. It was renamed the West Midlands (Regional) League in 1962 to reflect
the wider area from which it drew member clubs.</P>
<H3>Founder Members</H3>
<P>Aston Victoria, Great Bridge Unity, Hednesford Town, Ironbridge,
Kidderminster Harriers, Kidderminster Olympic, Langley Green Victoria, Oldbury
Town, Smethwick Carriage Works, Unity Gas Department, Wellington St. George's,
Willenhall Pickwick.</P>
<H2>Area Covered</H2>
<P>The league covers the West Midlands and includes clubs from South
Staffordshire, NorthWorcestershire, Herefordshire and Shropshire.</P>
<H2>Structure</H2>
<P>3 divisions, 50 clubs<BR><A HREF="#wmrp">Premier Division</A> (21 clubs)<BR><A
HREF="#wmr1n">Division One (North)</A> (15 clubs)<BR><A HREF="#wmr1n">Division
One (South)</A> (14 clubs)</P>

<H3>Relegation: To <A HREF="#wmr1n">Division One (North)</A> and

Town, Ettingshall Holy Trinity, Gornal Athletic, Kington Town, Ludlow Town, Lye

Tividale, Walsall Wood, Westfields, Wolverhampton Casuals, Wolverhampton United.</P>
<H4>Joining</H4>
<P>Promoted from Division One (North): Bandon, Star (previously Lawdon Mardon
Star).<BR>Promoted from Division One (South): Smethwick Rangers, Tipton Town.<BR>Relegated
from Southern League Midland Division: Dudley Town (inactive during season

<H4>Leaving</H4>
