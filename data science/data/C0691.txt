            <p class="font"><b>World Trade Center Losses</b></p>
            <p class="font">On 20 September 2001, the Board of Chaucer Holdings 
              PLC (&quot;Chaucer&quot;) estimated, in respect of the events of 
              11 September, that the Group could incur a pre-tax loss of between 
              &pound;8 million and &pound;12 million, equivalent to between 7p 
              and 10p per share after tax. It was stressed that making an early 
              estimate such as this was extremely difficult and would be subject 
              to change as further information became available. Current indications 
              are that Chaucer's position has improved from the first estimates. 
              However, the Board of Chaucer considers it prudent to leave its 
              previous estimates unchanged for the time being.</p>

            <p class="font">The results and forecasts are net of standard personal 
              expenses at managing agent's level (i.e. after annual subscriptions, 
              levies and managing agents' fees and commissions but before members' 
              agents' charges). The forecasts are made subject to the usual caveats 
              that apply to Lloyd's syndicate forecasts and are based on information 
              prepared for the 31 December 2001 Syndicate Returns. All three Chaucer 
              syndicates are closing their 1999 year of account in accordance 
              with Lloyd's normal timetable.</p>
            <p class="font"><b>2001 Year of Account</b></p>
            <p class="font">The Directors of Chaucer are encouraged by the conditions 
              experienced in the motor market in 2001 and expect syndicate 587 
              to return to profitability for that year of account. Clearly, both 
              syndicates 1084 and 1096 will be affected by the events of 11 September 
              and, in the case of syndicate 1084, other insurance catastrophes 
              in 2001. However the year was showing improvements in rates and 
              conditions prior to 11 September and these have gained considerable 
              momentum since that date.</p>
            <p> </p>
            <p class="font"><b>Current trading and outlook</b></p>
            <p class="font">Rates on many marine and non-marine classes have continued 
              to improve into 2002 and Chaucer remains encouraged by the trading 
              prospects that now exist in those markets. The motor account is 
              well positioned to benefit from the progress made over the past 
              two years.</p>
