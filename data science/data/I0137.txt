SRC="/images/Tranmere-Ground7-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Cow Shed
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>The Cow Shed
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Visiting fans are allocated the covered 'Cowshed Stand', which
is a fairly steep stand and has 2500 seats and no obscured view, but has
a severe lack of toilets (especially for the ladies). The entrance is on
Borough Road opposite the Prenton Park Pub. This was originally the home
end but the Tranmere fans campaigned successfully to move to the more
imposing Asda Kop End. 
</P><P>There is a food servery under the stand serving hot-dogs, burgers and
hot drinks (pies are a notable exception). There is also beer available
in the Cowshed Stand (new for 2000 season) although the bar closes 10
minutes before  kick off and doesn't reopen until half time. The food area
also has at least one TV, but only showing SKY sports, not Tranmere
specific coverage.
</P><P>If you are wanting to take flags, drums and banners in its best to check
with the club beforehand as while these have been seen at the ground the
Tranmere fans have been having some problems getting there own in. At
least one drummer who asked for permission this season has been denied it.
</P><P>The ground also has no score-board or clock (at least not that was
visible from The Cowshed).
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=CH429PY&Title=Prenton+Park+(Tranmere+Rovers+F+C)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>There are 38 disabled spaces in front of the Main Stand, for which booking
is essential. The club has a limited number of spaces with commentary for
the visually impaired.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>There are three pubs close to the ground which let away fans in, 'The
Prenton Park' and 'The Mersey Clipper' are within sight of the ground
and the 'Sportsman' which is only a five minute walk away.  
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Prenton Park
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Tranmere-Prenton-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Tranmere-Prenton-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Prenton Park
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Prenton Park
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p></P><P>The Prenton Park pub, situated on the corner of Borough Road and Prenton
Road West, is extremely popular with away fans and is very close to the
away turnstiles. It is a large, cavernous pub which has pool tables, Sky
TV and a small car park. It is regularly visited by the local police on
matchdays and is the pre-match choice for many of the club stewards.
</P><P>Service can be very slow, especially in the run up to kick off and it is
sometimes closed straight after the game. 
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Mersey Clipper
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Tranmere-Clipper-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Tranmere-Clipper-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Mersey Clipper
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Mersey Clipper
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Mersey Clipper, behind the Main Stand, is popular with visiting
supporters as well as home fans. There is a car park which might have
room if you get there early enough and food is served (though they stop
serving food well before kick-off) and whilst a little pricey the
quantity was good and offered a wide range of choice.
</P><P>There is usually a police presence on matchdays and expect plastic
glasses (and no real ale).
</P></p><H5 class="entry">Sportsman
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Tranmere-Sportsman-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Tranmere-Sportsman-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Sportsman
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Sportsman
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Sportsman is a five minute walk away from the ground on Prenton Road 
East. The pub attracts a good mix of home and away fans and is usually
quieter and more relaxed than the two pubs next to the ground. Food is
available on both Saturdays and for mid-week fixtures, and it is also
very reasonably priced.
  
There is also a pool table and large screen TV. There is limited parking
at the rear of the pub.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>There is a chippie called the Eastern Delight on Borough Road close to the
ground which does Chinese and English food and is recommended by the home
fans. There is also a chippie, the Wirral Takeaway a little further down
Borough Road. Sayers the Bakers, which serve a good selection of pies
pasties and sausage rolls is in between the two takeaways. 
</P><P>The is also a small burger van next to the Prenton Park pub.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>There are 2 cash machines, Lloyds TSB and HSBC, located about 10
minutes away on Woodchurch Road. To get there go down Prenton Road and
turn right onto Storeton Road. At the junction with Woodchurch
Road (A552) turn left and the cash machines are a short way down.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is CH42 9PY</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>The nearest bookies which is called 'Dave Pluck' is in the shops on
Borough Road near to the ground.
There is also one opposite the Half Way House pub on Storeton Road.
</P></p><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>If approaching from the north head through Liverpool and go through the
Queensway Mersey Tunnel (signed towards Birkenhead). Upon exiting the
tunnel bear right onto the A552 towards Wallasey. Follow the A552 for 1.5
miles and then turn left onto the B5151 (Storeton Road) and then left onto
Prenton Road West and the ground should be on your right.
</P><P>If approaching from the south exit the M6 at junction 20 onto the M56
West. Exit the M56 at junction 15 onto the M53. Exit the M53 at Junction 4
and exit the roundabout onto the B5151 (Mount Road) towards Bebington.
After 2.5 miles this becomes Storeton Road and then turn right onto
Prenton Road West and the ground will be on your right.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is very limited parking available at the ground and the immediate
area around the ground has residents schemes in operation. For street
parking try around Woodchurch Lane (the turning before Prenton Road West
if traveling from the M53) or alternatively head up Prenton Road East and
turn right at the junction (Sportsman pub on the right), then take
the second exit off the roundabout into Bebington Road. There is usually
parking available in the side streets off Bebington Road.
</P><P>If you arrive early enough there is also some parking available on Prenton
Road East itself.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>The nearest stations to Prenton Park are Birkenhead Central and Rock
Ferry. Connections to these stations are from the Wirral Line Low Level 
station which you can transfer to from Liverpool Lime Street.
</P><P>From Birkenhead Central cross Argyle Street and walk along Borough Road.
This should take about 25 mins.
</P><P>One alternative if you don't fancy walking is to get off at the new
Conway Park station and walk down the road to the new bus station where
there are buses to Prenton Park.
</P><P>From Rock Ferry turn right onto Bedford Road and continue straight on
as this runs into Bedford Avenue and then Bedford Drive. At the road
junction just after Prenton High School go across the junction into
Mount Road. Turn right at the traffic lights part way down Mount Road onto
Borough Road. The ground is a short way down here on your left. This
should take about 15 mins.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Birkenhead Central
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/birkenhe.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Tranmere fans are friendly and trouble is very rare. We found a great
atmosphere in the pubs before the game and displaying colours didn't seem
to be a problem anywhere. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>One report we had said "The police tend to keep a low profile unless
needed", however a recent visit revealed that there were more around
than at many other 1st division grounds, but they did tend to be in the
background. 
</P><P>On one visit, the stewards did an excellent job of controlling, informing
and even entertaining several hundred of fans forced to queue half way round
the ground due to technical problems with the turnstiles. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>In recent years Tranmere have been noted for singing 'Ten men, we only
need ten men'.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>For ideas of other things to do in the area check out the <a class="entry" href="http://www.merseyworld.com/wirral_tourist">Wirral mini guide</a>.
