<pre><b>PGA Tour: Greater Milwaukee Open</b> <br>Par: 71	Yards: 6,759		Venue: Brown Deer Park Golf Course <br>Dates: July 11-14		Location: Milwaukee, Wis. <br>Purse: $3.1 million		Defending Champion: Shigeki Maruyama <br>TV: 3:30-6 p.m. ET Thu.-Fri. on TGC, 2-4 p.m. ET Sat.-Sun. on ABC <br><b>Links to complete Greater Milwaukee Open coverage at bottom</b></pre><p> <P>
<I>By PGA.com news services</I><p> <P>
Shigeki Maruyama’s victory in the 2001 Greater Milwaukee Open didn’t come without difficulty. Despite shooting a final-round 66, Maruyama was caught by Charles Howell III, who fired a final-round 64 highlighted by birdies on the final four holes. But Maruyama made a routine birdie on the first playoff hole to win his second PGA Tour event and the first by a Japanese player on the United States mainland. <P>
Maruyama returns to Brown Deer Park Golf Course this week with a victory already under his belt this year, a May win at the Verizon Bryon Nelson Classic. He recently finished tied for fourth at The Memorial Tournament and tied for 16th at the U.S. Open. <P>
<b>ON COURSE: </b>The 35th Greater Milwaukee Open returns to Brown Deer Park for the ninth straight year. George Hansen created the course in 1929, and Andy North and Roger Packard later refurbished it and put it into its current 6,759-yard, par-71 configuration. Before the event moved to Brown Deer Park in 1994, it spent 21 years at Tuckaway Country Club in Franklin, Wis.  <P>
<b>ON TV: </b>The Golf Channel and Fox Sports Net both will carry the opening rounds from 3:30-6 p.m. ET on Thursday and Friday. The weekend coverage is on ABC from 2-4 p.m. ET on Saturday and Sunday. <P>
<b>NATIVE SONS: </b>While a player from Wisconsin has never won the Greater Milwaukee Open, each of the state’s touring pros has come close in recent years. Appleton native J.P. Hayes finished tied for third in each of the last two tournaments. Madison native Jerry Kelly, who won last week at the Advil Western Open, finished second in 1996 and third in 1999. Fox Point native Skip Kendall tied for sixth in 1999 and tied for ninth in 2000. Edgerton native Steve Stricker finished tied for third in 1996 and tied for second in 1998. All four rank among the top-25 career money winners at the event, Stricker (7), Kelly (8), Hayes (10), and Kendall (23).  <P>
<b>RECORD BOOK: </b>Loren Roberts set the event's scoring record of 24-under 260 in 2000, and tied the 54-hole mark of 190-under 194 that Jesper Parnevik had set in 1996. Robert Gamez set the 36-hole record of 127 in 1991, and the 18-hole record of 61 is shared by Ken Green (1988), Robert Gamez (1991) and Loren Roberts (2000). <P>
<b>ROBERTS AT BROWN DEER: </b>Loren Roberts has played in each GMO since the tournament moved to Brown Deer Park in 1994. He has made the cut in each of those years, with two victories (1996 and 2000) and two runner-up finishes (1994 and 1997). In 32 rounds, he has shot in the 60s 24 times and has been under par 26 times. <P>
<b>MILWAUKEE MOOLAH: </b>The purse this week is $3.1 million, with the winner pocketing $558,000. Second place is worth $334,800, with third paying $210,800. <P>
<b>MARUYAMA’S DEFENSE: </b>Shigeki Maruyama is currently 10th on the PGA Tour money list. In winning the Verizon Byron Nelson Classic in May, he became the first Asian with multiple PGA Tour victories. Isao Aoki of Japan won the Hawaiian Open in 1983, T.C. Chen of Taiwan won the Los Angeles Open in 1987 and K.J. Choi of South Korea won in New Orleans in May. Maruyama looks to become the sixth multiple winner of the GMO, joining Dave Stockton (1968 and 1973), Dave Eichelberger (1971 and 1977), Calvin Peete (1979 and 1982) and Loren Roberts (1996 and 2000). <P>
<b>WHERE DO WE GO FROM HERE: </b>From Milwaukee, the tour heads off in two directions. Some of the players will head to Muirfield, Scotland, for the British Open, while others will play in the B.C. Open in New York State. David Duval is the defending British Open champ, while Jeff Sluman is the reigning B.C. Open champ. <P>
