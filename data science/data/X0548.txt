                                                                 SRC="./bostonhockey_1570_5868098.gif" VSPACE=0 HSPACE=0 ALIGN="TOP" BORDER=0><BR></font><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">CCM SKATES - PLAYERS<BR><BR><BR><BR><IMG
                                                                 ID="Picture17" HEIGHT=115 WIDTH=150 SRC="./bostonhockey_1570_9697322.gif" VSPACE=0 HSPACE=0 ALIGN="TOP" BORDER=0><BR>CCM GOAL SKATES <BR><BR><BR>Notice the difference

                                                <TD WIDTH="55%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">Buying skates comes down to personal preference, but following some simple guidelines can ensure that your 
                                                    skates last long and comfortable. <BR><B>1.</B>
                                                     Generally, skates will fit 1 to 1/2 sizes smaller than your shoes size. CCM tends to fit a full two sizes smaller. For women, this size is 2 to 2 1/2 sizes smaller. CCM skates also tend to be wider, and bauers tend to be narrow. If you have wide feet, you might find that CCMs are much more comfortable, whereas if you have narrow feet, you might find them too loose. In the end however, a proper fit is determined by trying on a number of different types and sizes and feeling which ones are most comfortable.
                                                    <BR><B>2.</B>
                                                     When trying the skate on (and or putting it on at all), you should kick your heel into the very back of the boot, and your toes should only bearly be able to feel the toe cap. If they curl, or are crushed in any way, the boot does not fit properly.
                                                    <BR><B>3.</B>
                                                     As you lace up the boot, be sure to kick your heel back so that it stays firmly locked against he back of the boot. Laces should be snug, but not too tight. Common practice is to have looser lacing along the front of the footbed, tigher lacing along the part of your foot where the ankle meets the foot, and then slightly looser up near the top. 
                                                    <BR><B>4.</B>
                                                     When the skates are laced, get up and walk around. The heels should remain firmly against the back of the skate. If it slips or there is movement, then the skate is too large. Your foot should be comfortable in the footbed, and your toes should be able to lay flat in the boot. It is always a good idea to take the skates off completely and trying them again before making any final decisions.
                                                    <BR><B>5.</B>
                                                     When fitting children, remember not to fit them too large, even though some room for growth should be given. It is wise to only have about a half an inch between the heel and the boot when their foot is pushed up as far as it can go into an unlaced boot. Also, remember to buy them as close to the season they need them for as possible, so that they give you more wear before you must buy another pair.

                                                <TD VALIGN=TOP WIDTH="55%" BGCOLOR="#FFFF99"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">To ensure a proper fitting glove, bend your forearm, and have someone measure the distance 
                                                    between your fingertips and the crease in your elbow. Choose a size glove that matches that distance in inches. Also, choose a glove with a pliable palm, and a dense thumb. Buying gloves with the 
                                                    proper fit is crucial, because if they are too small, they can jam your fingers and if they are too large, they can inhibit your play. Often, the leather gloves will last longer, provide better 
                                                    protection and will be more comfortable, even though they are drastically more expensive.</FONT></TD>

                                                <TD WIDTH="55%" BGCOLOR="#FFFFCC"><P><FONT SIZE="-2" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica">To ensure a proper fitting pad, have someone measure the length between your shoulder pad and the cuff of 
                                                    your glove. This measurement will be the size elbow pad that is appropriate. When the elbow pad is fastened properly, there will be no space between the top of the glove and the bottom of the 
                                                    elbow pad. There will also be no gap between the shoulder cups of the shoulder pads and the elbow pads. If you use a short cuffed glove, remember to buy a longer elbow pad to ensure that you are 
                                                    properly protected</FONT><FONT SIZE="-1" FACE="Verdana,Tahoma,Arial,Helvetica"><B>.</B></FONT></TD>

Because foam does not settle with time, no allowances need to be made for settling in the initial fitting.

                                                    about the head. This is to prevent them from slipping over the eyes during play. Most helmets are adjustable, but remember to fit them anyway, because different brands still fit slightly 
                                                    differently. An ill-fitting helmet can leave you with a head or neckache. Some leagues may require a face shield or a wire cage to be attached to the helmet. All helmets come with little screws 
                                                    on the side which can be unscrewed and used to attach a face cage. <BR><BR><B>For Goaltenders:</B>
