<a name="business1"></a><span class="bodytextbold">Who can open a business account?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">The business account is available to clubs, associations and societies resident in the UK.
</span>

<a name="business2"></a><span class="bodytextbold">What types of business account are available?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">You can choose a Direct Access Savings Account or a 10 Day Notice Account.  Both offer competitive tiered interest rates.  The 10 Day Notice Account offers higher interest but you must give 10 clear days’ notice before making any withdrawals.  Both accounts work alongside an existing bank or building society business account (known as the designated external account).
</span>

<a name="business3"></a><span class="bodytextbold">What is a Savings Plan?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">
A Savings Plan can hold up to 99 different accounts for the same business and all the accounts are completely separate.  This means you can set up different named accounts for different things.  A Savings Plan can include Direct Access Savings Accounts as well as 10 Day Notice Accounts.
</span>
<BR><BR>

<a name="business4"></a><span class="bodytextbold">Are there any charges for this account?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">
Association accounts do not have any charges attached to them for normal transactions.  However, with a 10 Day Notice Account, you will be charged a penalty equal to 10 days gross interest on the amount withdrawn if you do not give us 10 clear days’ notice of withdrawal. There is no limit to the number of withdrawals that you can request.  In addition, interim statements can be requested at any time free of charge.<br>
<i>Charges will be made for Clearing House Automated Payment System (CHAPS) transfers, any cheques that are not honoured and  failed direct debits.</i>
</span>

<a name="business5"></a><span class="bodytextbold">How secure is the business account?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext"> Your Association account is easy to access and yet incredibly secure.  There is a minimum of two and a maximum of three authorised persons on all charity accounts.  These are the only people able to make transactions on the account or make alterations to the way in which it is run.  Each authorised person has their own personal identification number (PIN) and password which allows for business to be transacted security between your designated external account and your Standard Life Bank charity account
<br><br>
There is also the option to register for our online banking facility, once your Savings Plan is opened.  If you transact online, you have the reassurance that we use the latest security technology - you will be asked for your internet User ID and password when you log on. And, just in case you forget to log off, the system will automatically log off for you if you haven't used your mouse or keypad after a few minutes. 
</span>

<a name="business6"></a><span class="bodytextbold">How do I make deposits and withdrawals? </span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">When you open a business account you will be asked to provide details of your existing bank or building society business account.  This account will be used for all transactions to and from your Standard Life Bank account and must be in the name of your business. This is referred to as your designated external account. 
<br><br>
It's simple to deposit or withdraw money: </span>
<ul>
<li><span class="bodytext"><b>Online Banking</b> 
<br><br>
Once you have opened your Business Savings Plan, you may register for Online Banking. You register via this website or by simply calling us on 0845 609 0257. When you have registered for online banking we will send you your UserId and internet password by post. 
<br><br>
You can use your UserId and internet password to carry out transactions on your accounts during our online banking hours (Monday to Friday 7am-11pm, on Saturday 8am-8pm and Sunday 12pm-8pm). 
<br><br>
There are certain amendments to the business account which can only be made over the telephone, such as a change of authorised person, so it is important that you retain details of your PIN and password even if you  generally transact online.</span></li>
<br><br>
<li><span class="bodytext"><b>Telephone banking</b>
<br><br>
Just ring us Monday to Friday 8am-8pm or Saturday 8am-5pm. You will be asked for a random number from your PIN and a character from your password. Money can be transferred easily from your designated external account into your Standard Life Bank account. If you wish, you can make regular payments into your business account by Direct Debit. </span></li>
</ul><span class="bodytext">
All deposits and withdrawals are made to and from your designated external account.  Initial deposits can be made by CHAPS, cheque or Bank Giro Credit.  
<br><br>
When you're withdrawing money from your business account, it will be paid into your designated external business account. The process will normally take 3 working days but you will receive interest right up until the money is in your designated external account.   Withdrawals made from a 10 Day Notice Account will be subject to 10 days’ notice.  If you are unable to give us 10 days’ notice of withdrawal, you will be charged a penalty equal to 10 days’ gross interest on the amount withdrawn which will be deducted from the remaining balance on the account.
</span>

<a name="business7"></a><span class="bodytextbold">What is pooling?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">Within your Plan you can have up to 99 accounts. We pay interest on each individual account at the rate which applies to the total balance of all the pooled accounts held in the Plan. As our interest rate is calculated in tiers, this means your club could earn even more. 
<br><br>
You can set up separate named accounts for different things within a Savings Plan – for example, running costs, emergency funds, and advertising budget.  All the accounts remain completely separate but this facility makes the administration of separate funds easy while potentially giving you access to higher returns.
<br><br>
You must pool accounts with the same notice period – you can’t pool 10 Day Notice Accounts with Direct Access Savings Accounts.
</span>
<BR><BR>

<a name="business8"></a><span class="bodytextbold">How and when is interest paid?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">Interest is paid to each individual account at the rate which applies to the total Savings Plan balance. Up to 99 accounts can be held within each Savings Plan. Interest, which is calculated daily, can be paid either monthly or annually. It can be: </span>
<ul>
<li><span class="bodytext">added to the individual account </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">transferred to another account in the same Savings Plan </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">paid to the designated external account </span></li>
</ul>
<span class="bodytext">
Please note, interest is paid GROSS to Clubs Associations & Societies.
</span>

<a name="business9"></a><span class="bodytextbold">When will I receive statements? 
</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">We will normally send you statements on an annual basis but you can request them monthly, quarterly or half yearly.   
<br><br>
If you use our Online Banking service you can browse your account during our Internet banking hours Monday to Friday 7am - 11pm, Saturday 8am - 8pm and Sunday 12 noon - 8pm. 
</span>

<a name="business10"></a><span class="bodytextbold">How do I open a Business account?</span>
<br>
<span class="bodytext">Opening a association account is remarkably quick and simple. All you have to do is ring us on 08457 555659 Monday to Friday 8am - 10pm or on Saturday 8am - 5pm. We will take details and issue a Welcome Pack directly to your business address, for the attention of the first authorised person. A PIN/password letter will be issued to each authorised person at their home address. You then complete the necessary documents in the Welcome Pack and return them to us along with the information requested. We will issue an opening balance statement direct to the business address upon receipt of your initial deposit. 
<br><br>
When calling to open the Business Plan we require the following details  </span>
<ul>
<li><span class="bodytext">Full  name and postal address for the club, society or association </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">Details of authorised persons - full name, home address, business telephone number </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">Designated bank/building society name and address, account name, number and sort code </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">Initial deposit/regular deposit amount (if regular, frequency and date) </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">Where and how often interest should be paid </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">Frequency of statements required </span></li>
</ul><span class="bodytext">
If you are calling on behalf of a client </span></li>
<ul>
<li><span class="bodytext">Your name and company name </span></li>
<li><span class="bodytext">your agency code (if applicable)</span></li>
</ul>
