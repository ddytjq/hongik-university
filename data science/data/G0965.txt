          <td width="520"> 
            <h2><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" color="#6666CC"> 
              <!-- #BeginEditable "title" --><b>Astronomy Education: A Selective 
              Bibliography</b><!-- #EndEditable --></font></h2>
          </td>
          <td>&nbsp;</td>
        </tr>
        <tr> 
          <td valign="top" width="520"><!-- #BeginEditable "content" -->
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>9. 
              <a name="9">Astronomy Education Through the Media</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Byrd, D. <b>"Astronomy on the Air"</b> in Mercury, Jan/Feb. 1980, 
                p. 7. <br>
                On the StarDate radio program. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Goldman, S. <b>"Prime Time Astronomers: The Astronomers TV Series"</b> 
                in Sky & Telescope, May 1991, p. 472. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Goldsmith, D. <b>"Two Years in Hollywood: An Astronomer in Television 
                Land"</b> in Mercury, Mar/Apr. 1991, p. 34. <br>
                Experiences in the world of public television. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                O'Meara, S. <b>"Jack Horkheimer: "Star Hustler"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                May 1989, p. 544. <br>
                On planetarium director who does a series of short astronomy shows 
                on public television. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Reis, R. & Fraknoi, A. <b>"A Syndicated Newspaper Column on Astronomy"</b> 
                in Mercury, Jan/Feb. 1977, p. 1. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Robinson, L. <b>"How Sky & Telescope Came to Be"</b> in Sky & 
                Telescope, Nov. 1991, p.472. <br>
                History of the main magazine that tied scientist, amateurs, and 
                teachers together. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sagan, C. <b>The Demon Haunted World</b>. 1995, Random House. 
                <br>
                Provocative, heart-felt essays on the prevalence of pseudo-science 
                and the role of the media in encouraging it. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p> </p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="educ_bib.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>10. 
              <a name="10">Astronomy Education With Computers</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bothun, G. <b>"Teaching Via Electrons"</b> in Cause/Effect, Winter 
                1996, p. 37. <br>
                On web-based, networked courses at the U. of Oregon. A web-version 
                of this article is at: <a href="http://www.zebu.oregon.edu/special/cause.html" target="_blank">zebu.oregon.edu/special/cause.html</a> 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bruning, D. <b>"Computers and Astronomy"</b> in Astronomy, May 
                1994, p. 58. <br>
                A guide to astronomy software and how it can be used. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bruning, D. <b>"Blasting Along the Infobahn"</b> in Astronomy, 
                June 1995, p. 74. <br>
                On astronomy on the World Wide Web. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bruning, D. <b>"Students in Cyberspace"</b> in Astronomy, Oct. 
                1995, p. 48. <br>
                On educational sites on the World Wide Web. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Dahlman, L. <b>"Image Processing in Astronomy Education"</b> in 
                Mercury, Ma/Apr. 1994, p. 24. <br>
                On the big project at the Univ. of Arizona started by astronomers, 
                but now being used in many areas of education. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Goldman, S. <b>"Astronomy on the Internet"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Aug. 1995, p. 20. (Regular updates are found in most issues.) 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Marschall, L. <b>"Virtual Professional Astronomy"</b> in Sky & 
                Telescope, Aug. 1995, p. 92. <br>
                On Project CLEA, which provides college-level computerized lab 
                exercises. (See also: White, J. "Have Observatory, Will Travel" 
                in Mercury, May/June 1996, p. 16.) </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Mosley, J. <b>"Software Roundup for 1995"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Aug. 1995. <br>
                A review of the best software packages for general astronomy. 
                (Mosley reviews new software in most issues of Sky & Telescope 
                magazine.) </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Pompea, S. & Blurton, C. <b>"A Walk Through the Astronomy Village"</b> 
                in Mercury, Jan/Feb. 1995, p. 32. <br>
                A multimedia package from NASA. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sneider, D. & Berndt, H. <b>"A Galaxy of Astronomy & Space Software"</b> 
                in Technology & Learning, May/June 1994, p. 20. <br>
                Reviews of software packages from K-12 teaching perspective. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Wallin, J. <b>"Astronomy Education on the Web"</b> in Sky & Telescope, 
                Nov. 1997, p. 68. (See also the response by S. Goldman on p. 71.) 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Zombeck, M. <b>"From Spreadsheets to Scratch Pads"</b> in Sky 
                & Telescope, Oct. 1997, p. 60. <br>
                Software to simplify doing mathematical modeling in astronomy. 
                </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p> </p>
            <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="1"><a href="educ_bib.html#toc">table 
              of contents</a></font></p>
            <p> <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2" color="#6666CC"><b>11. 
              <a name="11">Teaching Resources for Astronomy</a> </b></font></p>
            <p> </p>
            <blockquote> 
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Ball, N., et al., eds. <b>Project SPICA: A Teacher Resource to 
                Enhance Astronomy Education</b>. 1994, Kendall-Hunt. <br>
                A collection of 43 astronomy activities at the 6th-12th grade 
                level from a project at the Center for Astrophysics. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Bruning, D. <b>"Charting a Path Through the Night Sky"</b> in 
                Astronomy, Oct. 1993, p. 74. <br>
                Comparative review of star atlases and guides. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Coyle, H, et al. <b>Project STAR: The Universe in Your Hands</b>. 
                1993, Kendall Hunt. <br>
                A high school astronomy supplement, full on sophisticated hands-on 
                projects. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A., ed. <a href="../astro/astropubs/universe.html">The 
                Universe at Your Fingertips</a>. 1996, Astronomical Society of 
                the Pacific. (see <a href="educ_bib.html#1">section 1</a> above) 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A. <b>The Cosmos in the Classroom</b>. 1995, Saunders 
                College Publishing. <br>
                Annotated lists of books, articles, audio-visual materials, and 
                software, organized by topic, for college instructors. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A. <b>"Scientific Responses to Pseudoscience Related 
                to Astronomy"</b> in Mercury, Sep/Oct. 1990, p. 144. <br>
                Resources for debunking astrology, UFO's, ancient astronauts, 
                etc. (Updated version available from the author by mail or see 
                the web site: <a href="pseudobib.html">Astronomical Pseudo-science: 
                A Skeptic's Resource List</a>.) </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Fraknoi, A. & Freitag, R. <b>"Women in Astronomy: A Bibliography"</b> 
                in Mercury, Jan/Feb. 1992, p. 46. <br>
                Part of a special issue on women in astronomy. (For an update, 
                see the web site: <a href="womenast_bib.html">Women in Astronomy: 
                An Introductory Bibliography</a>.) </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Gibson, B. <b>The Astronomer's Sourcebook</b>. 1992, Woodbine 
                House, 5615 Fishers Lance, Rockville, MD 20852. <br>
                A guide to astronomical equipment, publications, planetaria, organizations, 
                events, etc. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Pasachoff, J. <b>"Textbooks and Electronic Media: Instructional 
                Materials for Astronomy"</b> in Percy, J., ed. Astronomy Education: 
                Current Developments, Future Coordination. 1996, Astronomical 
                Society of the Pacific Conference Series, Vol. 89. <br>
                Includes a list of texts, software, and educational web sites. 
                </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                SETI Institute: <b>Life in the Universe Project. 1995 - 1996</b>, 
                Teacher Idea Press (800-237-6124). <br>
                A series of six curriculum and activities books, for grades 5-6 
                and 7-8, based on the Drake Equation and exposing students to 
                the scientific ideas behind the search for life elsewhere. </font></p>
              <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> 
                Sneider, C., et al. <b>Great Explorations in Math and Science 
                (GEMS)</b>. 1986 Å 1993, Lawrence Hall of Science, U. of California, 
                Berkeley 94720. <br>
                This series of wonderful elementary activity books includes several 
                astronomy titles, including Earth, Moon, and Stars and The Moons 
                of Jupiter. </font></p>
            </blockquote>
            <p> </p>
