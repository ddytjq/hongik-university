<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">The dart board is hung so that the
center of the bullseye is</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>1.74m
(5'8&quot;)</FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">from
the floor. The throwing line, which is also referred to as the oche (pronounced
&quot;ockey&quot;), the ockey (mostly in America), or the toeline, is generally
located</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>2.37m
(7'9.25&quot;)</FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">from
the face of the dartboard measured horizontally. This is the recognized
world standard asset by the World Darts Federation and is played as such
in most areas. However,</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000">there
are variations in distance and some people play from 2.29m (7'6&quot;),
2.44m (8') and as far as 2.74m (9'). </FONT></I></B></P></CENTER>

<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">When hanging the board care must
be taken to ensure that the floor does not slope away from the wall. If
it does, the height of the bullseye will not be located the correct height
from the floor in relationship to where the player is standing. If this
is the case, the location of the dart board on the wall will have to be
adjusted either up or down whichever the case may be.</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF">
</FONT></P></CENTER>

<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">When measuring the distance from
the dart board to the toe line along the floor from the wall, care must
be taken to include the distance from the wall to the front surface of
the dartboard in the measurement to ensure proper placement. This procedure
can be facilitated by tying a weight such as a large nut to a length of
string and using it as a plumb bob by placing the other end of the string
on the board's surface and letting the weight dangle near the floor. Place
a mark on the floor with a piece of chalk and use this as the starting
point for measuring the distance to the toe line. </FONT></I></B></P></CENTER>

<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">An easier method is to measure diagonally
from the center of the bullseye to the floor</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF">
</FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>2.931m (115-3/8&quot;)</FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#008080">.
This will reduce the margin of error as long as the bullseye has been placed
at the correct height and the floor and the wall make a right angle. </FONT></I></B></P></CENTER>

<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>The National Darts
Federation Of Canada (NDFC)</FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF">
</FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">specifies that the minimum length of
the toe line must be</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>610mm
(24&quot;) long.</FONT></FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF"> </FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">Furthermore,
a raised oche is specified and must be a</FONT></I></B><FONT COLOR="#5757FF">
</FONT><B><I><FONT COLOR="#FF0000"><FONT SIZE=+1>minimum of 38mm (1.5&quot;)
high</FONT></FONT><FONT COLOR="#008080">. Although a raised oche is preferred,
in most applications, the toe line is a length of tape place along the
floor parallel to the wall the dart board is mounted on.</FONT></I></B></P></CENTER>

<CENTER><P><B><I><FONT COLOR="#008080">The Official throwing distance,
for most countries, is </FONT><FONT COLOR="#FF0000">2.37 meters</FONT><FONT COLOR="#008080">,
as measured along the floor, from the plane of the face of the dartboard.
In feet, this is </FONT><FONT COLOR="#FF0000">7 feet, 9-1/4</FONT><FONT COLOR="#008080">
inches. The height of the board, to the center of the bull, is </FONT><FONT COLOR="#FF0000">173
centimeters, or 5 feet, 8 inches</FONT><FONT COLOR="#008080">. </FONT></I></B></P></CENTER>
