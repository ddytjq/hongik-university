            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><br>
            <b><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Background
            </span></font></b><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font size="2"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana">In
            November 1994, Kensington was founded to provide
            mortgage loans to the large number of people who were unable to
            satisfy the lending criteria of traditional lenders. In order to grow
            quickly and efficiently, Kensington outsourced many of its business
            functions to third party service providers enabling it to obtain
            economies of scale, access leading edge mortgage administration
            capability and deliver a variable cost infrastructure and the
            operational qualities of a developed organisation. At the same time,
            Kensington recruited personnel with significant mortgage industry
            experience to manage, monitor and supervise those service providers
            enabling Kensington to retain ultimate control of its operations.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </font></span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style="mso-ansi-language: EN-US"><font face="Verdana" size="2">Kensington
            commenced trading on 1 September 1995 and made its first loan in
            December 1995. It<o:p>
            securitised
            its first loan portfolio (RMS1) of £67 million in December 1996.&nbsp;
            </o:p>
            </font></span></p>
            <o:p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Business<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></b></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington,
            trading under the name &#147;Kensington Mortgage Company&#148;, is a
            market leader in the non-conforming residential mortgage sector in
            the United Kingdom. It makes loans, secured on residential property,
            to persons who generally do not conform to the underwriting criteria
            of the traditional suppliers of residential mortgage loans.
            Kensington&#146;s borrowers include the recently self-employed,
            independent or short-term contractors, older borrowers, temporary
            employees, people who may have experienced previous credit problems
            and borrowers who require larger loans, provided that they meet
            Kensington&#146;s underwriting criteria. Kensington charges its
            borrowers interest rates on their loans which are higher than
            average bank and building society rates available to conforming
            borrowers.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington
            originates loans via its extensive relationships with Packagers. It
            has also tested channels to access the public direct. <o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington
            seeks to minimise its fixed overheads by outsourcing the day-to-day
            administration of its loan portfolio to Homeloan Management Limited,
            &quot;HML&quot;, a subsidiary of Skipton Building Society. HML receives approved loan
            packages from Kensington&#146;s underwriters, processes and issues the
            mortgage offer and, following completion of the loan, undertakes the
            collection of interest, fees and other payments due from the
            borrower. However, Kensington employs its own underwriters to assess
            loan applications and managers to oversee the administration
            process.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington
            initially finances new loans to its borrowers using a number of
            short-term loan facilities from institutional lenders each expiring
            at different times. After assembling a portfolio of new loans it
            refinances them using the process of securitisation in which
            portfolios of new loans are sold to Special Purpose Vehicle
            Companies, &quot;SPV&quot;, which issue rated bonds in the capital markets secured on
            the purchased loans. From the proceeds of the bonds the SPV
            Companies pay Kensington for the loans and these proceeds are in
            turn used by Kensington to repay the short-term facilities.
            Kensington retains an interest in the performance of the securitised
            loan portfolios through its acquisition of various investments in
            the SPV Companies.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Under
            the direction of Kensington, HML continues to administer the various
            securitised loan portfolios alongside Kensington&#146;s as yet
            unsecuritised loans. The SPV Companies pay fees to Kensington for
            its supervision of HML.<o:p>
            </o:p>
            </span></font></p>
            <p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><font face="Verdana" size="2"><span style="color: black; mso-ansi-language: EN-US">Kensington
            is a member of the Council of Mortgage Lenders, &quot;CML&quot;,&nbsp; and complies with its
            voluntary code of practice. It is also registered with the Mortgage
            Code Compliance Board, &quot;MCCB&quot;, (the body responsible for monitoring
            compliance with the CML Code) and is a member of the Intermediary
            Mortgage Lenders&#146; Association. Its Origination Subsidiaries are
            licensed under the Consumer Credit Act 1974,&quot;CCA&quot;, and the appropriate
            companies in the Group (including Origination Subsidiaries) have licenses
            under the Data Protection Act 1998. Where appropriate, the
            Origination Subsidiaries have Approved Lender status from the
            Department of the Environment Transport and the Regions, &quot;DETR&quot;,
            to enable them to make loans to
            borrowers exercising a &#147;Right to Buy&#148; their home under the
            provisions of the Housing Act 1985. Kensington aims to adopt best
            lending practices as well as to comply with the applicable laws and
            regulations affecting its business.</span></font><span style="font-size:10.5pt;color:black;mso-ansi-language:EN-US"><o:p>
