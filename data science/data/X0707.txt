<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>A state championship is nice. Two straight is even better. But three in a row? That's a pattern. It's a trend. It's a sign.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>For Jason Fraser&nbsp;-- the 6-9 Villanova-bound post player from Amityville, N.Y., on Long Island&nbsp;-- it's a personal mantra.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He just won't let those guys lose," TheInsidersHoops.com recruiting expert Dave Telep said. "It's as simple as that. That's his deal."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Fraser scored 19 points, grabbed a tournament-record 25 rebounds and blocked five shots in guiding Amityville to a 67-57 weekend win over Peekskill in the New York State public schools Class B championship game.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's played pretty much like that all year&nbsp;-- every game," Amityville coach Jack Agostino said earlier this week. "He's been so consistent. To do what he did in such a big game, such a big setting, is tremendous."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Fraser finished the weekend&nbsp;-- the semis and the finals&nbsp;-- with 48 points and 43 boards. Amityville's Warriors became the first Class B team in state history to win three straight. Their star collected his second tourney MVP award.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Words can't explain all this," Fraser told Newsday. "You just have to suck it all in and enjoy the moment."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He's had plenty of experience with such moments.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The 'Nova-bound is certainly no stranger to winning. He won AAU tournaments with the Tim Thomas Playaz. He won them with the Long Island Panthers. And he does nothing but win state crowns with his high school squad.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Next up&nbsp;-- before heading down I-95 to start his career with coach Jay Wright's Wildcats&nbsp;-- are all-star appearances at the EA SPORTS Roundball Classic next week in Chicago and the McDonald's All-American Game April 4 at Madison Square Garden.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>How's he handling all the attention?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's getting better at it," Agostino said earlier this week. "He's even more humble and more mature and more focused. He's mature well beyond his age.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He cares about people and his teammates. People don't really understand that until they actually meet him."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Big East better be ready to make acquaintances with Fraser. Because the kid doesn't do anything but win.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>It's officially a pattern at this point.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>SHERIDAN, PLAYAZ SET TO PLAY&nbsp;-- AND WIN</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Finally.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>After a series of postseason slip-ups over the last couple of years, big-time junior post man Will Sheridan and the Sanford School, in Hockessin, Del., won the state championship.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Coach Stan Waterman's team got 21 points, 14 rebounds and four blocks from the big guy en route to a 63-44 title-clinching victory over Hodgson Vo-Tech this past weekend. Sheridan, 6-8 and 240 pounds, averaged 17.9 points and eight boards in four playoff games.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Now his focus shifts to the spring and summer season&nbsp;-- where he'll run the circuit with Jimmy Salmon's Paterson, N.J.-based Tim Thomas Playaz&nbsp;-- and his recruitment.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I can't really say who my leader is," Sheridan told Jeff Goodman of PrepStars.com. "I'm not really sure. I haven't weighed my options yet."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He'll have plenty. On his early list: Maryland, Duke, Virginia, Wake Forest, Michigan, Tennessee, Kentucky, Florida State, Seton Hall and Rutgers.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And the Playaz&nbsp;-- Sheridan or anybody else on the roster&nbsp;-- shouldn't have any problem whatsoever with exposure in the next four to six months.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Salmon's loaded group looks to be one of the best conglomerates heading into the spring and summer season.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Among those also expected to be on the 17-and-under list: Darryl Watkins, a powerful 6-10 center from Paterson Catholic; Marquis Webb, a shooting guard, also from PC; Jamar Nutter, a shifty guard from Seton Hall Prep in West Orange; Demetris Nichols, a swingman from St. Andrew's in Barrington, R.I.; Sean Banks, a versatile wing from Bergen Catholic in Oradell, N.J.; and Artie Bowers, a sturdy swinger from St. Benedict's in Newark.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The only potential weakness? Play at the point. No more Elijah Ingram. (He's off to St. John's.) No more Dwayne Lee. (He's off to St. Joe's.) And the 6-2 Nutter isn't really a one.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"We don't have a prototypical point guard," Salmon said. "We have to be a different type of team. But we are big and we are long."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And they are talented.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Starting with Banks.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's playing really well right now," Salmon said. "I don't know what the class looks like as a whole&nbsp;-- I don't know if he's top five in the country&nbsp;-- but he is really good. Sean is more than capable of getting to the basket when you need that."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>WHO NEEDS AAU? NOT AL-KALEEM</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Folks wonder why shooting percentages are down these days. Jack O'Brien doesn't.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The AAU thing has gotten out of hand," the Charlestown, Mass., prep coach said. "The kids don't work on their game. They just go around the country and play."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But not his superstar. Florida-bound shooting guard Rashid Al-Kaleem didn't play much AAU during the spring and summer. Instead he stayed home in Boston and put himself through an individual workout regimen&nbsp;-- over and over and over.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>That paid off on Saturday. Al-Kaleem scored 17 of his 20 points in the first half to lead Charlestown High to an 88-68 win over Groton-Dunstable at the Worcester Centrum. The victory gave the Townies' their third straight Massachusetts Division 2 state championship.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"This one is the most important," the 6-4 Al-Kaleem told the Boston Herald. "The five seniors have been sticking by each other for four years. To win it our last year is the best thing."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And on a bum ankle to boot. The 6-4 superstar&nbsp;-- one of five senior starters for Charlestown&nbsp;-- played the game with a nagging left ankle sprain.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He was just amazing," O'Brien told the paper. "That ankle is probably 60 to 70 percent. He was doing it off adrenaline. He wanted to go. It's our last game. You couldn't ask any more from him."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>It's been that way all year long.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>The Townies won their bracket at the Slam Dunk to the Beach in Lewes, Del., beating the Sanford School from Hockessin, Del., and Ohio's Dublin Coffman. They also claimed their second consecutive Boston City League title.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Al-Kaleem averaged 25.6 points, 12.4 rebounds and seven assists from his two-guard slot&nbsp;-- which, of course, is why schools like UConn, Boston College and Ohio State coveted his services.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But Billy Donovan's Florida program was his final choice.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The staff, obviously, was a big reason," O'Brien said. "Billy is a great, great person, and the staff really cares about the kids. There's a family atmosphere. And the way they play is a great fit."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But Al-Kaleem's shooting ability would've been a welcome commodity anywhere.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He's a shooter, first and foremost, above anything else," Telep said. "The reason why Florida was so attracted&nbsp;-- and a lot of other high-major programs&nbsp;-- is because he can do some things off the catch.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He can put it on the floor and make a jump shot. That's the difference. He's skilled enough to create a little bit of separation to get his own shot."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And he didn't learn that on the summer circuit.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Two of Illinois' more prominent high school coaches are set to direct the two teams in the EA SPORTS Roundball Classic next Wednesday at Chicago's United Center. <B>Gordon Kerkman</B> has won 524 games in 26 years at West Aurora High. <B>Bob Williams</B> has compiled 212 victories in just 11 years at Schaumburg. The stars set to be on hand in the Windy City include <B>Raymond Felton</B> of Latta, S.C., <B>DeAngelo Collins</B> of Inglewood, Calif., <B>Lenny Cooke</B> of Old Tappan, N.J., <B>Jason Fraser</B> of Amityville, N.Y., and <B>Daniel Horton</B> of Cedar Hill, Texas.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Let the hype continue: <B>Sebastian Telfair</B> did something this past weekend that his older cousin couldn't do until his senior year&nbsp;-- win a New York City Public School Athletic League championship. The sophomore point guard prodigy scored 16 points in Brooklyn Lincoln's 65-51 victory over borough rival Robeson Saturday at Madison Square Garden. The older cousin, of course, is <B>Stephon Marbury</B>. "Sebastian did exactly what a point guard is supposed to do," Lincoln coach <B>Tiny Morton</B> told the New York Daily News. "And, God, he's got two more years to go. It's unbelievable."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Lexington Catholic boss <B>Danny Haney</B> won his first Kentucky state championship with an 83-53 blowout against Paducah Tilghman Saturday at Rupp Arena&nbsp;-- and the 44-year-old coach could be looking at a couple more. <B>William Graham</B>, <B>Demetrius Green</B> and <B>Harrison Morton</B>&nbsp;-- all sophomores!&nbsp;-- combined to score 45 of the Knights' first 53 points in the title game. "Watching them play and just being around them makes me feel young," Haney told <B>Mike Fields</B> of the Lexington Herald-Leader. "It's been that way all season. They're just so much fun to be around."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Sports Illustrated cover boy <B>LeBron James</B> played in front of a state-record crowd of 20,562 last week at Gund Arena as his Akron St. Vincent-St. Mary team moved forward in the playoffs with a 78-49 victory over Warrensville Heights. The junior wunderkind had 21 points, 16 rebounds, nine assists and four blocks in the regional final. Next up for James' Irish are the Division II state semifinals Thursday night.</FONT></P>

<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Springfield Lanphier couldn't get it done against Chicago Westinghouse in Illinois' AA state championship game this past weekend. <B>Andre Iguodala</B>&nbsp;-- formerly an Arkansas signee&nbsp;-- scored 54 points in his final two games as a schoolboy. Big-time junior <B>Richard McBride </B>had 22 for 32-2 Lanphier in the final.</FONT></P></DIV>
