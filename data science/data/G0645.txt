<H3>Just for fun...<IMG SRC="cobe_icon.gif" HSPACE=20 VSPACE=4  ALIGN=LEFT></H3>

<P>The COBE&nbsp;satellite was launched on a Delta rocket from the Vandenberg
Air Force Base at Pt. Arguello, California on November 18, 1989. What?
You missed the launch?&nbsp;Don't be sorry ... you can always watch this</P>

<UL>
<LI><A HREF="./tarp/cobe_launch.mpg">movie of the COBE&nbsp;launch</A>
(mpeg)</LI>
</UL>

<P>How would the Earth appear if you were aboard the COBE&nbsp;satellite
right now? Thanks to the <A HREF="http://www.fourmilab.ch/">Fourmilab</A>
in Switzerland, you can </P>

<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.fourmilab.ch/cgi-bin/uncgi/Earth?xbird=COBE">see
the Earth &quot;from&quot; COBE</A> (while keeping your feet on the ground).</LI>
</UL>

<P>Note that the <I>terminator</I> (the imaginary line separating the Sunlit
side of Earth from the dark, or night, side) is in view (zoom out to see
this more clearly). This is because, by design, COBE&nbsp;was placed in
a <A HREF="COBE_03_orbit.pdf">&quot;Sun-synchronous&quot; orbit, as illustrated
here</A>. By pointing the COBE&nbsp;instruments directly away from the
Earth and using a protective shade called the &quot;Sun-Earth shield,&quot;
neither Sunlight nor light reflected from or emitted by the Earth enters
the instruments, giving them the best possible view (from Earth orbit)
of the Universe.</P>

<H3>Perspectives on cosmology<IMG SRC="COBE_01_bang.gif" HSPACE=20 HEIGHT=216 WIDTH=144 ALIGN=RIGHT></H3>

<P>The following documents are listed in order of increasing technical
content, ranging from a popular account of cosmology to a full-fledged
tutorial. The essay by Dr. Kashlinsky is moderately detailed yet concise,
and it includes links to more detailed information.</P>

<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://rst.gsfc.nasa.gov/AppA/A1.html">&quot;Origin and
Development of the Universe,&quot;</A> by Nicholas M. Short - a popular
view of modern cosmology (this is a section of <I><A HREF="http://rst.gsfc.nasa.gov/">The
End-to-end Remote Sensing Tutorial</A></I>)</LI>

<LI><A HREF="http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov/html/web_site.html">&quot;Introduction
to Cosmology&quot;</A> - sponsored by <A HREF="http://www.nasa.gov">NASA's</A>
Microwave Anisotropy Probe (<A HREF="http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov">MAP</A>)
mission</LI>

<LI><A HREF="cosmo_exact.html">&quot;Making the Science Exact: Recent Developments
in Cosmology,&quot;</A> - an essay by cosmologist Alexander Kashlinsky,
featuring the COBE&nbsp;discoveries</LI>

<LI><A HREF="http://www.astro.ucla.edu/~wright/cosmolog.htm">Cosmology
tutorial</A> by <A HREF="swg.html">COBE&nbsp;Science Team</A> member Ned
Wright of UCLA</LI>

<P><B>also see ...</B></P>
</UL>

<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/staff/blynds/tmp.html">&quot;About
Temperature,&quot;</A>&nbsp;a tutorial by Beverly Lynds, which describes
<A HREF="http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/staff/blynds/tmp.html#TR">blackbody
(thermal)&nbsp;emission</A> and <A HREF="http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/staff/blynds/tmp.html#Uni">features
the COBE&nbsp;FIRAS&nbsp;spectrum</A> of the cosmic microwave background</LI>

<LI><A HREF="http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov/html/glossary.html">Glossary of terms</A>
- sponsored by NASA's Microwave Anisotropy Probe (<A HREF="http://map.gsfc.nasa.gov">MAP</A>)
mission</LI>
</UL>
