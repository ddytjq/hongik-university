          <td valign="top" width="100%" colspan="3"> 
            <p><span class="engfanz">Sven Goran Eriksson put the mistakes down 
              to inexperience and insisted his youngsters would benefit from a 
              match which brought only his second defeat in 12 games in charge.<br>
              <br>
              &quot;Joe Cole played very well. He worked very hard and made the 
              goal for Robbie Fowler,&quot; said Eriksson. &quot;But he is young 
              and when you are young you make a mistake now and then, especially 
              if the opponents are good at counter-attacks.<br>
              <br>
              &quot;It was a valuable lesson for Cole. I'm not worried or angry 
              about it. If you are playing Italy for the first time in your life 
              maybe in two months or two years, you learn something from it.&quot;<br>
              <br>
              Eriksson insisted England, who were missing Steven Gerrard, Paul 
              Scholes, Rio Ferdinand and Ashley Cole, were &quot;unlucky&quot; 
              to lose a game in which he used 10 substitutions and which essentially
