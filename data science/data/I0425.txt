now it is the turn of uefa.com readers</B></P>
<P><table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="104" align="right">  <tr>    <td width="1"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="104" colspan="2"><A HREF='javascript:fnOpenWindow("/multimedia/PopUpPhoto600x400.htmx?strUrl=/MultimediaFiles/Photo/magazine/Magazine/26794_600X400.jpg","Photo","no","no","no","no","no","no","no",599,470,"","")'><IMG width="104" height="102" src="/MultimediaFiles/Photo/magazine/Magazine/26794_104X102.jpg" border="0"></A></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="1" rowspan="2" bgcolor="#000000"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><table cellpadding="2" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="103">      <tr>        <td width="103" class="PictureCaption">England's Rio Ferdinand<br>(©empics)</td>      </tr>    </table></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr></table>Last week, our poll revealed that uefa.com readers had named Turkey coach Senol Günes as their coach of the tournament, and when it came to voting for players, there is no doubt that Turkey's triumphant procession to the semi-finals had left a strong impression on uefa.com readers. All but one of our readers' XI play their football in Europe, and no less than four of the players play for Turkey. Here's how the squad line up.</P><B>
<P>Goalkeeper:</P>
<P><table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="104" align="right">  <tr>    <td width="1"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="104" colspan="2"><IMG width="104" height="102" src="/MultimediaFiles/Photo/magazine/Magazine/27421_104X102.jpg" border="0"></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="1" rowspan="2" bgcolor="#000000"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><table cellpadding="2" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="103">      <tr>        <td width="103" class="PictureCaption">Michael Ballack of Germany <br>(©empics)</td>      </tr>    </table></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr></table>Rüstu Reçber, Turkey</B><BR>Oliver Kahn may have been the Golden Ball winner, but there is no doubt in uefa.com readers' minds who the goalkeeping star of the World Cup was. Rüstu Reçber was in sensational form for Turkey, commanding his area and demonstrating superb agility in making a string of excellent saves. 11,632 readers voted for Rüstu making him by far the most popular player in our poll, and earning him 69.96 per cent of the votes in the goalkeeping section.</P><B>
<P>Defenders:</P>
<P><table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="104" align="right">  <tr>    <td width="1"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="104" colspan="2"><A HREF='javascript:fnOpenWindow("/multimedia/PopUpPhoto600x400.htmx?strUrl=/MultimediaFiles/Photo/magazine/Magazine/27513_600X400.jpg","Photo","no","no","no","no","no","no","no",599,470,"","")'><IMG width="104" height="102" src="/MultimediaFiles/Photo/magazine/Magazine/27513_104X102.jpg" border="0"></A></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="1" rowspan="2" bgcolor="#000000"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="1" height="1"></td>    <td width="103"><table cellpadding="2" cellspacing="0" border="0" width="103">      <tr>        <td width="103" class="PictureCaption">Brazil's Rivaldo and Ronaldo with trophy<br>(©empics)</td>      </tr>    </table></td>  </tr>  <tr>    <td width="103"><img src="/images/all/black_spacer.gif" border="0" width="103" height="1"></td>  </tr></table>Alpay Özalan, Turkey</B><BR>Another Turkish winner came from the defenders section of our poll, with Aston Villa FC's Alpay Özalan receiving 6,891 votes from readers - over 18 per cent of the overall defensive votes. Rightly so, of course, for with his strength, determination and exceptional tackling, Alpay graced the World Cup finals, forming the core of an obstinate Turkish defensive unit. In the two games against Brazil, Alpay was on exceptional form and there is little doubt that the biggest clubs in Europe will have been taking note of his glorious performances in the Far East.</P>
<P><B>Roberto Carlos, Brazil</B><BR>All in all, it has not been a bad year for Roberto Carlos. First the UEFA Champions League title with Real Madrid CF, then the FIFA World Cup with Brazil and now a place in the uefa.com readers' team of the tournament. Brazil's resilience in defence surprised many in Korea/Japan, but the ability of Roberto Carlos to make positive contributions going forward - and of course his terrific free-kick against China - demonstrated his extraordinary versatility.</P>
<P><B>Cafu, Brazil</B><BR>Captain of Brazil in the absence of Emerson da Rosa, Cafu demonstrated the leadership skills to match his exceptional talents in defence. While his team-mate Roberto Carlos earned several hundred more votes from uefa.com readers, Cafu still earned over 12.5 per cent of the defensive vote for his displays in his third consecutive World Cup finals. The AS Roma defender has stated his intention to match Pelé's record of playing in four finals tournaments as he eyes a place in the 2006 finals in Germany. Clearly, uefa.com readers would love to see him achieve that feat.</P>
<P><B>Rio Ferdinand, England</B><BR>The most expensive defender of all time when he made his move from West Ham United FC to Leeds United AFC, there had been doubts over Rio Ferdinand's concentration levels until he showed what he was really capable of in the Far East. Memories of the great England captain Bobby Moore were revived by his supremely cultured defensive play and unhurried style. Still barely into his 20s, over 11 per cent of uefa.com readers were in no doubt that Ferdinand's best is yet to come.<BR><BR><B>Midfield:</P>
<P>Ümit Davala, Turkey<BR></B>Another triumph for Turkey in the midfield poll, nearly a quarter of the readers who voted were delighted by Ümit Davala's displays at the World Cup finals. He may have had a disappointing spell in Italy after being loaned back to Galatasaray SK from Internazionale FC, but he showed power, pace and no shortage of skill in Korea/Japan to underline his credentials as a great player in a great Turkey side. A winning goal against Japan and the assist for Ilhan Mansiz's 'golden goal' against Senegal were his most telling contributions, and if there were a poll for haircut of the tournament, Ümit would be near the top of the heap too.</P>
<P><B>Michael Ballack, Germany</B><BR>It was collective endeavour as much as individual skill that earned Germany a place in the World Cup final, but the tireless creativity of Michael Ballack was a badge of everything that was good about German football in this tournament. His winning goal against the United States was impressive enough, but Ballack showed his selfless qualities to best effect when scoring the winner against the Korean Republic in the semi-finals moments after a yellow card had ensured that he would be suspended for the final itself. After a heartbreaking season with Bayer 04 Leverkusen, almost 19 per cent of our readers clearly wish Ballack well as he heads for FC Bayern München.</P>
<P><B>Yoo Sang-chul, Korean Republic</B><BR>The only player in our uefa.com readers XI to ply his trade outside Europe, Yoo Sang-chul was regarded by many as the best midfield holding player of the tournament. Almost nine per cent of our readers were certainly convinced that Yoo's relentless endeavour and unquenchable spirit had been the crowning jewel of the Koreans' team-orientated performance at the finals. The 30-year-old Kashiwa Reysol player also made the FIFA World Cup All-Star Team and may yet have a chance to show his skills at club level in Europe.</P><B>
<P>Attackers:</P>
<P>Hasan Sas, Turkey</B><BR>Another winner for Turkey, Hasan Sas polled nearly 21 per cent of the striking vote to cap a tournament which saw him come of age on the highest stage. Just two goals in the tournament is possibly more of a reflection of his exceptional support play than any deficiencies as a target man, and there is little doubt that the first of those goals, to put Turkey into the lead against Brazil in their Group C game, was beautifully worked and superbly finished. A star for Galatasaray as they took the Turkish title, Hasan showed his ability as a holding player and the spirit of Günes's Turkey. </P>
<P><B>Ronaldo, Brazil<BR></B>Well, having taken the tournament's 'golden boot' award, one cannot really begrudge Ronaldo a place in the uefa.com readers' XI. Not least because he proved himself to be a player of devastating skill and artistry with an exceptional finishing touch. The 'three Rs' attacking formation of Rivaldo, Ronaldo and Ronaldinho was certainly Brazil's most potent weapon, and for Ronaldo himself, overcoming long-term injury and the pain of defeat in the final of France 98 marked a huge personal achievement for the Internazionale FC striker. A great story and an even more terrific player. </P>
<P><B>Rivaldo, Brazil</B><BR>The battle for a place alongside Hasan and Rivaldo in our readers poll was unsurprisingly fierce, with Italy's Christian Vieri and Senegal's El Hadji Diouf both within 80 votes of snatching Rivaldo's place. However, despite the sour memories of his play-acting against Turkey, there was no doubting the FC Barcelona player's immense reserves of skill and goalscoring ability. With just one year to run on his contract at the Camp Nou, Barcelona can expect fierce competition for Rivaldo's signature next summer.</P>
