        color="#000000">

    It seems there is a fundamental disconnect between the two projects in their respective frameworks for user interface construction. I'm actually glad to see the SWT effort moving ahead with momentum because there are definitely some cases where a performant UI is a "make or break" factor in the application space. I'm not saying Swing is bad, but I think there is a place in the world for both approaches.<BR><BR>At JavaOne I had a good discussion with Roman Stanek, the founder of NetBeans. He had interesting insights about the differences that exist just because Eclipse was started some years after NetBeans. He seemed to suggest that sometimes projects need to start over to enter the next generation, that incremental progress from the long-established base will sometimes be less effective than  a fresh beginning.<BR><BR>I wonder if Sun sees it that way?<BR><BR>Rick<BR>
