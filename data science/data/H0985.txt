<p><FONT FACE="Verdana" SIZE="+2" COLOR="#FFCC00">Dientamoeba fragilis</font></p>
<table width="90%" border="0">
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td width="90%" valign="middle"> 
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>D. 
        fragilis</b><br>
        <font size="-1">Phase contrast x 400</font></font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">D. fragilis is a flagellate, 
        formerly considered an amoeba. Usual range in size is 9-12 <font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"></font><font face="Symbol">&#109;</font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">m</font>. 
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">It has no evident 
        flagella and exists in the trophozoite stage only (shown on the right). 
        </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">It contains one or 
        two nuclei which can be seen in both stained or unstained preparations.</font></p>
      </td>
    <td width=20%><img src="image/10dfragilis.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="10 - D. fragilis"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table width="90%" border="0">
  <tr> 
    <td width=20%><img src="image/50dfragilis.jpg" width="225" height="225" align="right" alt="50 - D. fragilis" lowsrc="image/50dfragilis_low.jpg"> 
    <td width=80%> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">One nucleus is seen 
        in 40% of the organism and the karyosome usually is fragmented into 4-8 
        segments, as shown on the left.</font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Uninucleate trophozoites 
        are more difficult to identified than the binucleate form (shown below) 
        because they can be easily confused with other small nonpathogenic Entamoeba 
        like <a href="enana.htm">Endolimax nana</a>.</font></p>
      </td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table width="90%" border="0">
  <tr> 
    <td width=80%> 
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>D. 
        fragilis</b><br>
        <font size="-1">Trichrome Stain x 1000</font></font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">There are no peripheral 
        chromatin on the nuclear membrane, and a horizontal line connecting both 
        nuclei is usually quite evident. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The cytoplasm may 
        be finely to heavily granular and may be vacuolated.</font></p>
    </td>
    <td width=20%><IMG SRC="image/80dfragilis.jpg" WIDTH="200" HEIGHT="200" BORDER="0" ALT="80 - D. fragilis"> 
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>

    <td>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">One of the difficulties 
        in diagnosing Dientamoeba infection is the fact that it often stain pale 
        so that it blends in well with the background fecal material. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Unless the reader 
        continually uses the fine adjustment these organism can easily be missed. 
        </font></p>
