<h1 align="center"><u>Dark Nebulae</u></h1>
<p>Sometimes known as absorption nebulae these are dark concentrations of
gas and dust which often have stars forming inside them.  Dark nebulae
can be seen if they obscure part of a bright nebula (eg. the Horsehead
nebula) or if they block out background stars (eg. the Coalsack).</p>
<center><img src="nhorsehd.jpg" width="500" height="170" alt="Digitized Sky Survey image" /></center>
<center>The Horsehead Nebula</center>
<p>Here is a map showing many of the dark nebula regions within 2000 light
years plotted onto the galactic plane.  The map shows the positions of 742 dark 
nebulae listed by Dutra and Bica.  A few bright nebulae are also marked.  These 
dark nebulae are the important regions of star formation in our local part of the 
Orion Arm.  For example, the famous Pleiades star cluster consists of hot young 
stars that formed among the dark clouds of Taurus.</p>
