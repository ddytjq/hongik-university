            <p class="contentsubhead">BACS - Direct Credit</p>
            <p>Whether you are a small business or large organisation you can benefit from using the Direct Credit System
              as it enables you to transfer funds electronically directly into a specified bank or building society account.</p>
            <p>A company making as few as 50 cheque payments a week for any of the following reasons may realise substantial
              annual savings:- </p>
            <p><strong>Paying Salaries and wages to employees and reimbursing expenses</strong><br>
              This has become the standard "best practice" in payroll administration</p>
            <p><span class="contenttextbold">Paying essential costs involved in running your business</span><br>
              Direct Credit is an efficient method for paying essential bills such as rates, rent, electricity etc.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Paying regular suppliers</p>
            <p><strong>Preferred by Business</strong><br>
              The benefits of using Direct Credit in particular, for paying salaries and wages are summarised as follows:-</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Saving Your Business Time and Money</strong><br>
                Eliminates the cost of cheque stationary and where relevant, the overheads of automated cheque production.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><strong>Savings in Administration Time</strong><br>
                Removes time-consuming laborious administration associated with preparing cheques, collating payment with
                payslips and removes the employee downtime involved in their distribution and collection.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><strong>Improved Cash Flow Management</strong><br>
                The certainty of payment date facilitates optimum cash flow management and removes the need to fund the payroll
                before the employee's accounts are credited. Each payment file generates just a single debit to the employer's
                account on a specified date.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><strong>Greater Security</strong><br>
                Eliminates the security risks and costs involved in delivering and holding cash on the premises which in turn
                may also reduce insurance costs.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><strong>Greater Reliability</strong><br>
                There is no risk of encoding faults and clearing delays to which paper credits are vulnerable.</li>
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Preferred by Employees</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Immediate Access to Pay Cheque</strong><br>
                Unlike cheques, Direct Credit gives the employee cleared funds guaranteed to be available on pay day. Removes
                the risk of payment being delayed in the post.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li><strong>Greater Security</strong><br>
                Eliminates the security risks that the employee may face from robbery or the loss of wage packets.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li><strong>Greater Reliability</strong><br>
                Money is automatically credited to their account on known dates even when they are sick, on holiday or on
                shift work outside normal hours.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li><strong>Improved Cash Management</strong><br>
                Employees may choose to pay essential bills by Direct Debit and may have the opportunity to receive discounts
                for early payment. Once all bills are paid they are able to budget on the remaining funds available.</li>
              </ul>
              <p class="contenttextbold">Payment Process</p>
              <ul>
			  <li><strong>Information Required</strong><br>
                In order to make payments by Direct Credits you will need the following details of Employees Bank/Building
                Society account to be credited :-
                <p></p>
                1) Sort code <br>
                2) Account Number</li>
              <p></p>
              <li><strong>Process is completed in 3 days</strong>
                <p></p>
                Day 1 : INPUT in accordance with the stipulated timetable<br>
                Day 2 : PROCESS accepted data<br>
                Day 3 : ENTRY COMPLETED with Beneficiary being credited and Originator debited simultaneously</li>
              <p></p>
              <li><strong>You remain in Control</strong></li>
            </ul>
            <p>You have the option of submitting Direct Credit payment instructions up to 30 days prior to payment date.</p>
            <p>You have the capability of altering the payment information after the instructions have been submitted provided
              your Bank is notified prior to a specified cut-off time.</p>
            <p><strong>Accessing the Direct Credit System</strong><br>
              There is a choice of methods to access the system either :-</p>
            <p><strong>1) Indirect Access</strong><br>
              You may choose to access the system through the TeleBank&#174; Business BACS module (information can be found
              from our home page). This is often more suitable for smaller businesses or those collecting a limited number
              of payments.</p>
            <p><strong>2) Direct Access</strong><br>
              The system can be accessed directly from your office by BACSTEL, the telecommunications link. Originators collecting
              large volumes of Direct Credits find this method faster and more cost effective than indirect access.</p>
            <p><strong>Requirements</strong><br>
              In order to access the system through BACSTEL you will require a PC, appropriate modem and required software.</p>
            <p><strong>Availability</strong><br>
              Software can be obtained from your Bank or a proprietary software supplier.<br>
              Solutions are also available for mid-range and mainframe computer systems.</p>
            <p><strong>Security</strong><br>
              All transmissions are secured using passwords unique to each submission.<br>
              Confirmation of receipt is sent back down the telephone line to notify successful transmission.</p>
            <p>If you require any further information please</p>

              <li>or telephone <strong>+ 44 028 9089 3412 </strong> or <b>+44 028 9089 3414</b></li>
              <li>call into your nearest Northern branch.</li>
            </ul>
