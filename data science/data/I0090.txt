  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 4" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>England's
  passage to Mexico in 1986 was a fairly comfortable one.&nbsp; A good start to
  the first two games (where we scored 13 goals, conceding 0) before a Mark
  Hately strike gave us a vital 1-0 win in Northern Ireland.&nbsp; Two away
  draws meant that our qualification to the finals was confirmed by avoiding
  defeat in the last three Wembley fixtures.&nbsp; The Ulstermen qualified by
  finishing as runners up in our group whilst Scotland qualified ahead of Wales
  only on goal difference. Once the tournament began, our campaign got off to a disastrous
  start with a 1-0 defeat to Portugal.&nbsp; Even more misery was to follow, a
  0-0 draw with eventual group winners Morocco was made worse with an
  injury to skipper Brian Robson and a sending off for Ray Wilkins.&nbsp; The
  final group game against Poland was a must win, but cometh the hour, cometh
  Gary Lineker, his first half hat-trick gave us a 3-0 win.&nbsp; That set up a
  second<img border="0" src="lineke86.gif" align="right" hspace="15" vspace="15" width="295" height="200">
 round match with Paraguay, Lineker scored two
  more in another 3-0 victory, that meant we'd play Argentina in the quarters, our
  first meeting since the Falklands Conflict four years earlier.&nbsp; Unfortunately
  England were beaten by Maradona, not by his footballing skills but by his
  cheating.&nbsp; First we had to suffer his dives from tackles from which Terry
  Fenwick was booked.&nbsp; This booking
  made sure that Fenwick could not kick him up his big fat arse on the run up to
  his second goal.&nbsp; Maradona's first goal was a clear handball which only
  recently he has admitted.&nbsp; Gary Lineker pulled a goal back (which earned
  him the golden boot award) and he almost equalised, only thwarted by an amazing
  goal line clearance.&nbsp; Who said cheats never prosper?&nbsp;&nbsp;</b></font>
