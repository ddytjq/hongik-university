There are 5 principal kinds: H1, H2A,H2B, H3 and H4.						<li>Olins and Olins observed 10 nm beads on strings in chromatin spread on electron microscope grids.						<li>Hewish and Burgoyne treated chromatin with <a href="#Info">nucleases</a>, extracted DNA, and observed its size distribution. The pattern found is shown at left (left lane short digestion times; right lane, long digestion times).						<li>The nuclease-treated chromatin (before DNA extraction) was fractionated by size. One fraction contained the 146 bp DNA. It also contained two each of H2a, H2b, H3, and H4 histones per DNA fragment.					</ul>					<center>						<p><img src="i112262.gif" width="200" height="26"></center>				</td>			</tr>		</table>		</h4>		<p>&nbsp;</p>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a></p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Interp"></a>Interpretations</h4>		<ul type="circle">			<li>DNA exists in cells as a complex with proteins.			<li>At the first level of structure, eight histone molecules are clustered on 146 bp of DNA. These clusters, called <strong>nucleosomes</strong>, are separated from one another by 20 to 100 bp (depending on cell type, organism and physiological status) of spacer DNA.			<li>Extensive characterization of nucleosomes by neutron diffraction, crystallization, X-ray diffraction, protein crosslinking, immuno electron microscopy and other techniques revealed further properties.			<ul>				<li>The DNA is wrapped left-handed around a 3.2 nm radius core of histones;				<li>There are 1.8 turns of DNA per nucleosome.				<li>DNase nicking of nucleosomes shows an average periodicity of 10.7 bp in center; 10.0 near ends.				<li>The DNA is kinked; kinks are found at +/- 1 and +/- 4.				<li>Alpha helical regions of histones have basic residues that contact the major groove of the DNA.			</ul>			<li>The folding of DNA in nucleosomes results in a compaction factor of about 7 5. Higher levels of chromatin structure involve histone H1 and non-histone chromosomal proteins.		</ul>		<center>			<p><a href="#Facts">Facts</a> | <a href="#Interp">Interpretations</a> | <a href="#Info">Further Info.</a> | <a href="#bottom">Other Pages</a><br>			</p>		</center>		<h4><a name="Info"></a>Further information</h4>		<ul type="square">			<li>The continous length and the large size of chromosomal DNA's are inconsistent with the observed size of morphological chromosomes. Resolution of the inconsistency requires packaging of the DNA in more compact structures. Biochemical <a href="http://www.ultranet.com/~jkimball/BiologyPages/N/Nucleus.html">studies</a> have identified a DNA-protein complex, <a href="http://www.average.org/~pruss/nucleosome.html">the nucleosome core particle (NCP)</a>, representing the first level of packaging. They also identify how the NCP's are distributed along the DNA. The NCP's provide a link between biochemical (submicroscopic) structure and <a href="http://www.csc.fi/jpr/emt/movies/chr.mpg">structures</a> observed by <a href="http://www.csc.fi/jpr/emt/movies/chromosome.html">electron microscopy</a>. Biochemical and <a href="http://cellbio.utmb.edu/cellbio/nucleus2.htm#nucleoprotein">ultrastructural information</a> have been combined in models of <a href="http://outcast.gene.com/ae/AB/GG/nucleosome.html">10 nm diameter nucleosomal fibers</a> and <a href="http://www.cchem.berkeley.edu/~jehgrp/index.html">30 nm supernucleosomal fibers</a>. Nucleosome structure and dynamics is an area of active <a href="http://www.mol.biol.ethz.ch/richmond/index.html">current research</a> in <a href="http://www.average.org/~pruss/Nucleosomes/othersites.html">many laboratories</a>. <img src="nav4.gif" width="69" height="71" align="right" usemap="#nav4B1F889C9"><map name=""></map>		</ul>		<menu>			<li>DNA double-helix when constrained by being a covalently closed circle or by being immobilized by proteins can have superhelical twists. Nucleosomal and supernucleosomal DNA, on removal of proteins is superhelical.			<li>The original Hewish-Burgoyne experiment (<a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/htbin-post/Entrez/query?uid=4711166&form=6&db=m&Dopt=b">ref</a>) used an endogenous nuclease that was activatable in the presence of calcium ions. It is this nuclease that is responsible for DNA breakdown during apoptotic cell death.		</menu>		<center>			<p><a name="bottom"></a><a href="MG11225.html">Last</a> | <a href="MG1129.html">Vocabulary</a> | <a href="MG1122.html">Overview</a> | <a href="#Facts">Top</a> | <a href="MG11227.html">Next</a><br>			<br>			</p>		</center>		<p>		<hr>		</p>		<p>This is page 11226 of <a href="../MG01.html">Molecular Genetics</a> by <a href="http://opbs.okstate.edu/~melcher/UM.html">Ulrich Melcher</a>, &copy; 1997, 1998, 1999<br>		<hr>		</p>		<address>E-mail inquiries to <a href="mailto:umelcher@biochem.okstate.edu">U.
