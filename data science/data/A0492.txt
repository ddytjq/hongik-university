          <td class="content"> 
            <p class="contentsubhead">BACS - Direct Debit</p>
            <p>Direct Debits offer businesses and organisations of all sizes a more competitive, cost effective and efficient 
              method for collecting payments.</p>
            <p>A Direct Debit is a written instruction from a customer to their bank or building society authorising you (the 
              Originator) to collect varying payment amounts. </p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Preferred By Businesses</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Benefits</p>
            <ul>
              <li class="contenttextbold">Increase business turnover<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li class="contenttextbold">Save your business time<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><span class="contenttextbold">Save your business money</span><br>
                Administration and processing costs are cheaper than cheques.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><span class="contenttextbold">Improve Security</span><br>
                Eliminates the security risk and costs involved in accepting cash payments.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><span class="contenttextbold">Improve CashFlow Management</span><br>
                You can control the date of receipt of payment into your account and determine the date that funds are cleared.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><span class="contenttextbold">Flexible Option</span><br>
                You have the flexibility to collect variable payment amounts on regular or irregular dates.<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li class="contenttextbold">Increased Customer Loyalty<br>
                <br>
              </li>
              <li><span class="contenttextbold">Improved Monitoring of Payments</span><br>
                You will receive a report detailing the total number and value of all Direct Debits processed and if applicable 
                any amended or rejected records.</li>
            </ul>
            <p><span class="contenttextbold">Preferred By Customers</span><br>
              Customers prefer direct debits as they:</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Enable the customer to spread the cost of large bills</strong><br>
                Monthly instalments allow the customer to budget for their bills. This can be used as an effective selling 
                proposition for promotions.<br>
                <br>
              <li><strong>Ensure customers do not pay penalties for delayed payments</strong><br>
                Once a Direct Debit mandate is signed payments are made automatically on the customers behalf. Customers do 
                not have to take postal delays into consideration.<br>
                <br>
              <li><strong>Saves the customer time & money </strong><br>
                Eliminates time spent queuing to pay bills.<br>
                No postage expenses involved in mailing cheques.<br>
                Potential to receive available discounts.<br>
                <br>
              <li><strong>Assist with CashFlow Management</strong><br>
                Once regular bills are paid by Direct Debit, the customer can then assess the remainder of their disposable 
                income.
            </ul>
            <p class="contenttextbold">How to Get Started</p>
            <ol>
              <li>To become a Direct Debit Originator you require to be sponsored by your Bank. The paper work you will be 
                issuing must be agreed and certain legal documentation needs to be completed. Once these steps have been completed 
                you will be registered as a Direct Debit Originator and can then ask your customers to complete instructions.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li>Send a DDI along with the Direct Debit Guarantee details to each customer. Once the customer has returned 
                the completed DDI, check all the details are recorded before forwarding to the customer's bank.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li>In the event that the customer's bank reject the DDI , the Instruction will be returned within three working 
                days.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li>After three working days, send the customer confirmation of the payment details:- 
                <ul>
                  Amount<br>
                  Due date<br>
                  Frequency<br>
                </ul>
                <p></p>
                This will confirm the details with the customer and allow them to budget for their payment accordingly.</li>
              <p></p>
              <li>Should you need to amend any of the payment details you must notify the customer in advance. The normal 
                minimum period of Advance Notice being 10 working days.</li>
            </ol>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Accessing the Direct Debit System</p>
            <p>There is a choice of methods to access the system either :-</p>
            <p><strong>1) Indirect Access</strong><br>
              You may choose to access the system through the TeleBank® Business BACS module. This is often more suitable 
              for smaller businesses or those collecting a limited number of payments.</p>
            <p><strong>2) Direct Access </strong><br>
              The system can be accessed directly from your office by BACSTEL, the telecommunications link. Originators collecting 
              large volumes of Direct Debits find this method faster and more cost effective than indirect access.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Requirements</p>
            <p>In order to access the system through BACSTEL you will require a PC, appropriate modem and required software.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Availability</p>
            <p>Software can be obtained from your Bank or a proprietary software supplier. Solutions are also available for 
              mid-range and mainframe computer systems.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Security</p>
            <p>All transmissions are secured using passwords unique to each submission.<br>
              Confirmation of receipt is sent back down the telephone line to notify successful transmission.</p>
            <p class="contenttextbold">Collecting Payments Process</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Information Required</strong></li>
            </ul>
            <p>A file should be created containing details of customers who have chosen Direct Debit as their preferred method 
              of payment.</p>
            <p>File should include :-</p>
            <p>Customer's details<br>
              Customer's bank account details<br>
              Amount to be collected from customer's account<br>
              Date to be collected<br>
              Reference number<br>
              Details of your account to be credited</p>
            <p>The format of the file is dependant on the chosen access method and help, in this respect, will be given by 
              your bank.</p>
            <p>This file is sent via Direct Access or Indirect Access to BACS who make a number of security and validation 
              checks to ensure data is correct.</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Process is completed in 3 days</strong></li>
            </ul>
            <p>Day 1 : INPUT - Receipt of Originators payment file at BACS<br>
              Day 2 : PROCESS accepted data<br>
              Day 3 : ENTRY COMPLETED with Beneficiary being credited and Originator debited simultaneously</p>
            <p>Should any of the Direct Debits be returned unpaid by the customer's bank, you will be given details of the 
              reason for non-payment to allow you to take action. Your bank account will be debited with the amount within 
              2 or 3 working days after it has been credited.</p>
            <p> If appropriate, you may re-present the unpaid Direct Debit provided it is within one month of the date on 
              which it was first presented and the amounts are the same.</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Amendments and cancellations</strong></li>
            </ul>
            <p>Your customers may amend or cancel a DDI at any time simply by advising you or normally their bank. All amendments 
              and cancellations must be actioned immediately. Failure to comply may result in you or the bank being liable 
              for any errors.</p>
            <ul>
              <li><strong>Refunding errors</strong></li>
            </ul>
            <p>In the event of an error being identified, the customer's bank will refund the customer who will in turn reclaim 
              the amount from you.</p>
            <p>If you require any further information please</p>
            <ul>
              <li><a href="/about/nbon_web_abo_php_004.php" class="link" target="_top">e-mail</a> us</li>
              <li>or telephone <strong>+ 44 028 9089 3412</strong> or <span class="contenttextbold">+44 028 9089 3414</span></li>
              <li>call into your nearest Northern branch.</li>
            </ul>
            <p>We look forward to hearing from you.</p>
