  <p>Poet. Playwright. Pirates fan. Two Finger Carney is all of those things,
  and more. In the latest edition of&nbsp;<a href="http://baseball1.com/carney/">Notes
  from the Shadows of Cooperstown</a>, he offers his own unique thoughts on the
  game and his observations from outside the lines.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
  <p>Carney&#8217;s latest book, <a
  href="http://pages.prodigy.net/scottfiesthumel/ecptriple.htm">Tripleheader
  Tales, Memories, and Notes from the Shadows of Cooperstown</a>, is now
  available.</p>

  <p align=center style='text-align:center'><b><span style='font-size:18.0pt'>The
  Myth of Free Agency<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
  <p style='margin-left:.25in'>White Sox owner Bill Veeck once remarked,
  &quot;It isn't the high price of stars that is expensive, it's the high price
  of mediocrity.&quot; The reality is that the high salaries paid for
  superstars is often so high that it makes it difficult for teams to compete. <a
  href="http://baseball1.com/lahman/index.php?storyid=44">Sean Lahman discusses
  that theory, and points out that it has been fifteen years since the team
  with the highest paid player has made the playoffs.</a> </p>
