	  <A HREF="http://www.dec.state.ny.us/website/dfwmr/marine/swflaws.htm" TARGET="_self"><SMALL>NYS-DEC
	  MARINE REGULATIONS 2002</SMALL></A>
	</H1>
	<P ALIGN=Center>
	<A HREF="http://www.dec.state.ny.us/website/dfwmr/marine/swfrecrd.htm" TARGET="_self"><B>Record
	Fish in New York</B></A>
	<P>
	<FONT COLOR="#ff0000"><B><FONT SIZE="4">Stripers:</FONT></B></FONT>
	<UL>
	  <LI CLASS="mvd-P">
	    <B><FONT COLOR="LIME">Stripers</FONT></B> will be found in the surf and the
	    bays as they migrate from their winter homes and head <B>EAST.</B>
	  <LI CLASS="mvd-P">
	    Now is the time for bait in the surf. A double hook rig on a long leader
	    in fishfinder mode will do the trick. Starting out with 3/0 Mustad hooks
	    with skimmers as bait will get you into school sized
	    <B><FONT COLOR="LIME">Stripers</FONT></B>. Later on in the month, when the
	    larger fish arrive, switch over to 5/0 Mustad hooks again with skimmers as
	    bait.
	  <LI CLASS="mvd-P">
	    <P ALIGN=Center>
	    <B><FONT COLOR="RED">HINT </FONT></B>Put a 3 inch red cigar float on your
	    leader line. Then attach this rig to your main line in fishfinder mde. Adjust
	    the float to where it keeps both hook and bait about 5 inches above the sand.
	    This enables the migrating <B><FONT COLOR="LIME">Stripers</FONT></B> to see
	    your baits and allows your baits to get the scent trail further out into
	    where the stripers are passing by.<BR>
	    <FONT COLOR="#ffff00"><A HREF="http://www.ancient-mariner.org" TARGET="_self"><IMG
