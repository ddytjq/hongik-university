<p><FONT FACE="Verdana" SIZE="+2" COLOR="#FFCC00">Entamoeba histolytica</font></p>
<table>
  <tr valign="top"> 
    <td width=80% valign="middle"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The trophozoites may 
        range in size from 10-60 <font face="Symbol">&#109;</font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">m</font>. 
        <br>
        <br>
        The single nucleus may not be visible in unstained preparations. <br>
        </font> </p>
      </td>
    <td width=20%><img src="image/92ehisto.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="92-E. histolytica trophozoites - phase contrast x400" lowsrc="image/low/92ehisto.jpg"></td>
  </tr></table> 
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td align="left" width="206"><img src="image/2-25ehisto-troph.jpg" width="225" height="225" align="left" alt="2-25 E. histolytica trophozoite - phase contrast x 400" lowsrc="image/low/2-25ehisto-troph.jpg"> 
    <td width="546"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The nucleus 
      has a small compact karyosome that is usually centrally located but maybe 
      eccentric.<br>
      <br>
      The peripheral chromatin typically is finely granular and is distributed 
      evenly over the inner surface of the nuclear membrane.</font></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td width=80%> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Mature cysts are spherical 
        and contain four nuclei, the usual size ranges from 12-15 <font face="Symbol">&#109;</font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">m</font>. 
        Except for their smaller size, the structures of the nuclei in the cysts 
        are similar to those in the trophozoites. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Nuclei are visible 
        in wet preparations and the glycogen present in the mature cyst is usually 
        diffuse.</font></p>
    </td>
    <td width=225 height="225"><IMG SRC="image/95ehisto.jpg" WIDTH="225" HEIGHT="225" BORDER="0" ALT="95-Entamoeba histolytica cyst - phase contrast x 400" lowsrc="image/low/95ehisto.jpg"></td>
  </tr>
</table>
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td width="20%"><IMG SRC="image/34ehisto.jpg" WIDTH="225" HEIGHT="225" BORDER="0" ALT="Entamoeba histolytica cyst - phase contrast x 400" lowsrc="image/low/34ehisto.jpg"></td>
    <td width="614"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">In young 
      cysts the glycogen is a discrete mass normally pushing the nucleus to one 
      side and contains a spread out karyosome. Immature cysts like these can 
      be easily confused with E. coli cysts which maybe the same size. </font> 
  </tr>
</table>
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td width="614" height="224"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Immature 
      cysts may contain one or two nuclei as seen in these three cysts. </font> 
    <td width="225" height="224"><IMG SRC="image/96ehisto.jpg" WIDTH="225" HEIGHT="225" BORDER="0" ALT="Entamoeba histolytica cyst - phase contrast x 400" lowsrc="image/low/96ehisto.jpg">
  </tr>
</table>
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td width="20%"><img src="image/2-10ehisto-cyst1.jpg" width="225" height="225" border="0" alt="Entamoeba histolytica cyst - trichrome x 1000" lowsrc="image/low/2-10ehisto-cyst1.jpg"></td>
    <td width="614"> 
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Entamoeba 
        histolytica cyst </b><br>
        Trichrome stain x1000 </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">A binucleate cyst 
        containing a chromatoidal body is seen in this field. The small eccentric 
        karyosome is not very evident.</font></p>
      
  </tr>
</table>
<table>
  <tr> 
    <td width="225"><img src="image/2-01ehisto-troph.jpg" width="225" height="225" border="0" alt="Entamoeba histolytica troph - trichrome x 1000"></td>
    <td width="614">
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>Entamoeba 
        histolytica trophozoite </b><br>
        Trichrome stain x1000 </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Note the characteristic 
        nuclear structure of this amoeba. ie. a discrete, small karyosome and 
        evenly distributed peripheral chromatin. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Small vacuoles are 
        present in the cytoplasm.</font></p>
    
  </tr>
