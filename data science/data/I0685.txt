			<div align="left">
				<font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b>Rugby League at Wembley</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">Rugby League first came to Wembley in 1929 when the League felt that there were no grounds in the north of England capable of hosting the increasingly popular Rugby League Challenge Cup Final. The first final was won by Wigan who beat Dewsbury 13-2. Since then the Rugby League Challenge Cup has joined the FA Cup as one of Wembley's two annual carnival days.<br>
				<br>
				Because Rugby League is an almost entirely Northern affair, the atmosphere on the day of the finals is not unlike that of a remarkably cheerful invasion of the capital city.<br>
				<br>
				As Alex Murphey, variously of St. Helens, Leigh, Warrington, Wigan, Lancashire and Great Britain has put it: &quot;I don't think anybody outside of the Rugby League world can know just how much value we place on the final day at Wembley. Besides being the most important domestic match of each season, it's a lads' day out, the works trip, a day by the seaside, and Christmas - all rolled into one.&quot;<br>
				</font></div>
			<center>
				<p><a href="cupstats.asp"><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular"><img height="78" width="97" src="../images/main/history/rugby02.jpg" border="0"><br>
				<br>
				Click for full list of Wembley Challenge Cup Finals</font></a></p>
			</center>
			<div align="left">
				<p><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#870000"><b><br>
				Rugby Union at Wembley</b></font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="black"><br>
				<br>
				</font><font face="Arial,Helvetica,Geneva,Swiss,SunSans-Regular" color="#5e5533">Rugby Union, traditionally kept very separate from rugby league, has only recently been played at Wembley. With the re-building of Cardiff Arms Park, Wembley became the home ground for the Welsh team during 1998 and 1999. A unique place in Welsh affections will have been secured with a dramatic injury-time victory over England in April 1999.<br>
				<br>
				England had also used Wembley as a temporary home in 1992 when 39,000 people watched them beat Canada as Twickenham was being redeveloped. A wartime England Team played Scotland at Wembley in 1942.<br>
