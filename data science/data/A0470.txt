                <p align="left"><font face="Arial" size="2">
                <strong>How the new European currency may affect you</strong></font></p>
                <p><font face="Arial" size="2">The euro has now been fully introduced in the EMU* first wave countries, also known as
                the euro zone.</font></p>
                <p><font face="Arial" size="2">The euro zone countries are: Austria, Belgium, Finland, France, Germany, Greece,
                Ireland, Italy, Luxembourg, The Netherlands, Portugal and Spain and their old currencies
                can now no longer be used.</font></p>
                <p><font face="Arial" size="2">This page tells you how the euro might affect your and aims to answer any questions you
                may have. It is particularly useful if you travel to the euro zone or make regular
                transactions to and from those countries. You may find that you benefit from the
                introduction of the euro into the euro zone, because you'll only have to deal with one
                currency rather than several.</font></p>
                <p><font face="Arial"><small>* European Monetary Union</small></font></p>
                <p><font face="Arial" size="2">The Royal Bank of Scotland offers a number of euro products and services, including:</font><ul>
                  <li><font face="Arial" size="2">a full range of euro notes and travellers cheques, available to order from branches at
                    competitive rates of exchange</font></li>
                  <li><font face="Arial" size="2">a selection of euro accounts</font></li>
                  <li><font face="Arial" size="2">a comprehensive range of services for making and receiving payments to and from euro
                    zone countries</font></li>
                </ul>

                will have to be in euro. Equally, if you receive payment from the euro zone, it will also
                have to be in euro.</font></p>

                eight coins**: 1,2,5,10,20,50 cents and &#128;1 and &#128;2. One euro is made up of 100
                cents. Each coin has one common side and one country-specific side.</font></p>
