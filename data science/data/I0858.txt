 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Aston Villa</H1><H2 class="entry">Villa Park</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Villa01-North1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Villa01-North1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="North Stand" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>North Stand</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Villa01-Main-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Villa01-Main-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Holte End Entrance" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Holte End Entrance</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Villa01-Holt-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Villa01-Holt-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Holte End
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Holte End
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Visiting fans are allocated seats in the Lower part of the covered North Stand, which is no smoking, as is all of Villa Park
and (acording to the club) has 
no obscured view seats. There are two big video
walls inside the ground which cater for all the fans. There is also
a Ladbrokes kiosk. Large flags, banners and drums are not normally allowed
into the ground.
</P><P>For such a prestigeous ground the facilities for away fans are pretty poor, its very cramped with far few toilets (expect a huge
queue) and no beer on sale, despite the fact <B>the club themselves</B> told us otherwise.
</P><P>The stand itself is a converted terrace which means not only are the seats very close together, but you are also seated far far lower 
than is sensible, also if your behind the goal (especially around row "O") the away goal is obscured by the crossbar.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=B66HE&Title=Villa+Park+(Aston+Villa)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Ticket Info</H3><P><B>Fans are reminded that tickets for visiting stands should be purchased through their own club, not the club they are visiting.</B></P><p></P></p><p>Away tickets are normally &pound;20 for adults and &pound;10 for juniors and OAPs.
</P></p><p>Ticket info can be found <a class="entry" href="http://www.avfc.co.uk/match/view/ticket_information" tagert="_new">here</a>. The ticket office
phone number is 0121 327 5353. The official web site claims to offer on-line ticket sales, but it simply redirects to ticket master, who only
offer tickets for a selection of games.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>Visiting disabled supporters share the facilities of the recently redeveloped Trinity Road
stand with home disabled fans. The facilities include lowered windows at the refreshments kiosk
</P><P>It is also possible to pre book headphones with match commentry. Contact the club on 0121 327 5353.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>The Cap &amp; Gown is definately the Away pub of choice but is far from ideal.
The Harriers on the corner of The Broadway and Davey Road reportedly lets away fans
in and also serves food. Most of the other pubs around the ground are
'Members only'.
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Cap &amp; Gown
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><p></P><P>The Cap &amp; Gown is located very close to the away end on where Witton Lane, Witton Road, Ashton Lane and Brookvale Road all join. It is
a large pub with in two parts allowing both home and and away only sections. One downside is that Away fans are charged an 
entry fee (&pound;1) where as home
fans aren't and second is that there is no real ale on sale (but at least real glasses are used).
</P><P>As well as the main bar area away fans also have acess to a small games room, but as the pool table is covered this is little more 
than an overflow for the main room.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>There are quite a few good takeaways around the ground. The Witton 
Fish Bar &amp; Kebab House on the corner of Witton Road and Bevington Road
(2 minutes from the ground) is recommended. According to Mac from
the <a class="entry" href="http://www.heroesandvillains.net">Heroes and Villains</a>
website Masala Fish in Naan Bread with spicy scallops are a must!! 
</P><P>There are also a couple of Indian/Bangladeshi takeaways and a lot of burger vans (which seemed slightly more
expensive than most other Premiership clubs).
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>The nearest cash machine is in the One Stop Shopping Centre in Perry Bar 
to the north-west of the ground.
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is B6 6HE</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Villa Park is approached via the M6 for the majority of visitors. If
coming from the North exit the M6 at Junction 7 and follow the A34
towards Birmingham. After just over 3 miles turn left off this road
and follow the A4040 towards Witton. At the first main roundabout the
ground will be in front of you on the left.
</P><P>If approaching from the East via the M5 join the M6 at junction 8 and
travel south. Then exit the M6 at junction 7 and proceed as above.
</P><P>If traveling from the south exit the M6 at junction 6 and follow signs for
Birmingham NE. At the roundabout junction with the A38 take the A38 exit
towards Aston. After 1/2 mile turn right into Aston Hall Road which runs
towards the ground.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>There is no parking available at the ground unless a pass has been
arranged and passes are only given out for very good reasons. There are
15 associated car parks near to the ground, one of the best being at
Aston Villa Leisure Centre Car Park which holds 600 cars.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>The nearest station to Villa Park is Witton station which has trains from
Birmingham New St and Walsall. The ground is a very short walk down
station road.
</P><P>Aston station is also only 10 minutes walk away from the ground. Upon
exiting the station turn left towards Ashton Hall Road. Turn left onto
Ashton Hall Road and follow this under the motorway where it runs into
Witton Lane. Head up Witton Lane and the ground will be in front of
you on the right.
</P><P>If you arrive in Birmingham New St it is advisable that you catch a train
to either of the above stations as the ground is a 20 minute taxi ride
away from Birmingham New Street.
</P></p><p>The nearest station is Witton Lane
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/birmingh.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Fans</H3><p>Andi Wilson, an Everton fan sent us the following "There is never very much trouble around the ground 
BUT there has been quite a bit of trouble at new street station. But mainly the villa fans
are a very nice (but unpredictable) bunch .This ground is the best I have ever been to."
</P><P>Whilst we found the fans to be friendly enough, we can't agree to his comments about the ground !
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Police And Stewards</H3><p><P>Andi Wilson also sent us the following regarding the police ".The police can be very heavy handed outside
the ground but are more relaxed in the ground.".  
</P><P>The stewards seemed very laid back on our last visit, standing in you seats and in the aisle seemed to go ignored
and depsite constant tannoy annoucements that the ground was no-smoking the stewards seemed happy to turn a blind eye.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>Birmingham City Centre should prove enough to keep non-football fans happy for
a while. For details of what's on in Birmingham check out
<a class="entry" href="http://www.Birmingham.gov.uk">
http://www.birmingham.gov.uk</a>.
</P></p><P>You can also use the following to search for events in the area</p><BR><CENTER>
