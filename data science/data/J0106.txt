        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Rob Scott Makes 
          It Two In A Row </b><br>
          The smell in the air and the unmistakable roar ringing in their ears 
          race fans were treated to the <b>Wilroc Sprint Car</b> racing Saturday 
          night at <b>Saratoga Speedway</b> along with the Tyee Chev Olds <b>Stock 
          Cars</b> and the <b>Road Racing Motorcycles</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Vancouver Island 
          based Sprints showed up at the Black Creek oval with 10 highly competitive 
          cars for their second race of the 2002 season and they kicked off the 
          Mike Finneron sponsored night of racing with a "Hurricane" in time trails 
          as Victoria's <b>Jeff "Hurricane" Montgomery</b> set a new lap record 
          with of 13.663 nipping his old mark of 16.863. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Sidney's <b>Rob 
          Scott</b>, who was coming off a opening race main event win the previous 
          weekend in Victoria, rode his momentom into Saratoga as he came away 
          with two big wins, including the 25 lap main event in his 33 year old 
          1970 Tipke Roadster. Scott, a point champion in 10 different classes 
          including a Canadain IMCA championship, took over the race lead from 
          <b>Dave Conway</b> on the 7th lap, then built up a comfortable lead 
          which he would need at the end in holding off a charging Montgomery 
          to grab the win ahead of Montgomery and <b>Darren Yates</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Victoria's <b>Dan 
          Fisher</b> kicked off a fast night of high-winged racing with a win 
          in the B dash followed by wins for Nanaimo's <b>Britt Gillette</b> in 
          the 53-second A dash, while the heat races had Scott take the 1st heat 
          and <b>Randy Vincent</b> the 2nd of 12 lap races.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The 1st heat saw 
          Montgomery tag the back of Gillette's car and end up on the tires, causing 
          serious damage which, with the help of <b>Dave Hargrave's</b> welder 
          and a highly capable pit crew, he was able to return for the main event. 
          </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">For the 4th time 
          this season a different driver captured the Stock car main event as 
          Courtenay's <b>Mike Meers</b> led every lap to win the 30 lap race ahead 
          of <b>Brad McLean</b>, <b>Dave Hargrave</b>, who later failed post race 
          tech, <b>David Mainse</b> and <b>James Dawson</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Meers, the 97 Bomber 
          co-point champion, who with his dad <b>Jim</b> moved up to the Sportsman 
          class in 2001, purchased a car from Victoria in the off season and rejoined 
          the Stock car class for this year, while dad Jim is continuing in the 
          Sportsman class. Saturday night saw Meers have problems in qualifing 
          which left him on the outside pole for the main event which he smartly 
          took advantage of to move into a first lap lead which he would never 
          give up, leaving the racing behind him which was a 30 lap struggle for 
          every car in closely contested race. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Mainse who went 
          into the night 7 points ahead of <b>Martin Beaulieu</b> for the points 
          lead came away 26 points up after Saturday's racing and also came close 
          took bettering Beaulieu's track record in time trails with a quick 16.53 
          - just off the lap record of 16.44. Last year saw Mainse struggle early 
          in the season with a number of non finishes that put him behind in the 
          points race early with little chance of challenging Beaulieu for the 
          championship. However, this season has seen the 2 time speedway point 
          champion put together four strong performances to sit 1st overall in 
          the points race.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"> Parksville's <b>Brad 
          McLean</b> kicked off the night's racing with his first of two wins 
          in the C dash to go along with his B heat win, while other winners included 
          the 4th trophy dash win in four race nights for <b>Ralph von Kampen</b> 
          in the B dash, Port Alberni's <b>Don Hallgarth</b> the A dash and Comox's 
          <b>Rob Ryan</b> won a very competitive A heat. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Joining the Sprints 
          and the Stock cars were the Motorcycles with Nanaimo's <b>Dave Rogers</b> 
          winning the heat and main event while Nanaimo's <b>Leon Drzewiecki</b> 
          won the trophy dash.</font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Dwarf cars had 
          four cars on hand to run three exhibition style races to promote their 
          class and race winners were <b>Ron Jepsen</b> in the dash and heat, 
          while <b>Barry Anderson</b> won the main event. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">This weekend the 
          speedway hosts two power filled days of <b>Monster Truck</b> and <b>Crash 
          to Pass</b> action with Saturday night's show including the <b>Stock 
          cars</b>, <b>Dwarf Cars</b> and <b>Figure 8</b> cars. Sunday night the 
          <b>Bomber Cars</b> join the <b>Dwarf</b> and <b>Figure 8</b> cars. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Heading into the 
          double header weekend Nanaimo's <b>Mark Haslam</b> has a mere 1 point 
          lead over fellow Hub City racer <b>Jimmy Joyce</b> and only 18 up on 
          Campbell River's <b>David Crowhurst</b> while Victoria's <b>Brian Baker</b> 
          is only 37 back and Campbell River's <b>Jeff May</b> is only 50 behind 
          Haslam. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">The Bomber class 
          has only 95 points separating the top 7 cars led by <b>Mike Dionne</b> 
          followed by <b>BJ Bradly</b>, <b>Darren Larsen</b>, <b>Chris Beaulieu</b>, 
          <b>Rick Martin</b>, <b>Cody Gorosh</b> and <b>Jason Waters</b>. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Racing action this 
          weekend goes <b>rain or shine</b> and both nights have gates open at 
          5 followed by time trails at 6 and all the car crushing, bashing and 
          racing gets under way at 7pm with monster trucks <b>Way Too Cool</b> 
          and <b>Wild Thing</b> on hand. </font></p>
        <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Guest talk .......With 
          no demo racing scheduled for Victoria it is expected a influx of their 
          cars will be on hand..........<b>Rob Leighton</b> missed Saturdays racing 
          after hitting wall hard on May 4th......The WILROC Sprint cars return 
          June 22nd.........<b>Jason Beaulieu</b> gets his season under way this 
          weekend with races Saturday night and Monday afternoon in Victoria as 
          he sets out to defend his Canadain IMCA championship...........Guest 
          driver of the week goes to Sprint car racer Scott as no matter what 
          this guys brings to Saratoga he drives it well! </font></p>
