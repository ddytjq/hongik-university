      <h2 align="center"><big>Software Review</big></h2>
      <div align="center"><center><table border="0" cellpadding="4" width="95%">
          <tr>
            <td><h3 align="center"><font size="1">Planning Practice Just Got Easier!</font><font
        size="2"><br>
              </font><font size="1">by Steve Garrett</font></h3>
              <font size="2"><i><h3></i></font><small><img
        src="../../../../images/Reviews/version2_1.gif" alt="version2_1.gif (4076 bytes)"
        align="right" border="1" WIDTH="226" HEIGHT="48">Practice Planner Pro for Basketball- new
        Version 2.0</small><br>
              <small><small>by SportsNet USA</small></small><br>
              <small>Address: P.O. Box 221323<br>
        Santa Clarita, CA 91322-1323</small><br>
              <small><small>Telephone: 805-288-2255</small><br>
        Web site: <a href="http://www.PracticePlannerPro.com">http://www.PracticePlannerPro.com</a></small></h3>
              <p><small><br>
              <font color="#004000"><a href="../../../../hoopstore/mall_index_backup11-29-99.htm"><img
        src="../../../../hoopstore/Images/bin_button.gif" alt="bin_button.gif (2965 bytes)"
        align="middle" border="0" hspace="5" vspace="5" WIDTH="96" HEIGHT="30"></a></font></small></p>
              <p><small><small>Note: Practice Planner Pro has now been improved with the release of
        Version 2.0.&nbsp; Not only do you get the time saving practice planning capabilities
        listed here in the original review but the following new features as well:</small></small></p>
              <ul>
                <li><small><small>You can now store up to 500 drills in catagories and sub-catagories that
            you create.</small></small></li>
                <li><small><small>You can pre-assign intended numbers of minutes and points of emphasis for
            each drill similar to Windows95 &quot;Auto-Text&quot; feature.</small></small></li>
                <li><small><small>Name your plans and store them in folders you create.</small></small></li>
                <li><small><small>Create players practice groupings from between 2 and 11 players for up to
            70 players.&nbsp; This is an ideal tool for organizing camp drills and competitions.</small></small></li>
                <li><small><small>Free upgrade to v2.0 for previous purchasers of v1.0 by contacting
            SportsNetUSA</small></small></li>
              </ul>
              <p><small><small>Practice Planner Pro for Basketball- Version 2.0 is designed for coaches
        at all levels who want to maximize their abilities to create organized and effective
        practice plans. The program brings to a coach&#146;s fingertips the essential information
        in the practice planning process. It also makes the process of planning practice faster
        and easier. It is simple to use with all the features easily accessed with just a point
        and click of the mouse.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>Practice Planner Pro for Basketball allows a coach to view information
        such as a comprehensive list of drills, post-practice and game evaluations, and scouting
        reports while filling out the plan. This provides a coach vital information in determining
        what specific drills need to be included in the plan, taking into account past
        performances and future opponents. Post-practice and game evaluations, scouting reports,
        and the comprehensive drill list are accessed and viewed with just a click of the mouse.</small></small><font
        size="2"></p>
              <h3></font><font color="#FF8040" size="1">Features</font></h3>
              <p><small><small>New Practice Plan </small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>This feature is designed just like the practice plan that coaches have
        filled out by hand for years. The program automatically fills in the date and practice
        plan number. The coach can easily enter announcements, starting time, and defensive and
        offensive emphasis-of-the-day information. The offensive- and defensive-emphasis feature
        is optional and can be turned off so it does not appear on the plan.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><img src="../../../../images/Graphics/ProPracPlan.jpg"
        alt="ProPracPlan.jpg (18966 bytes)" align="left" border="1" hspace="10" vspace="10"
        WIDTH="170" HEIGHT="170"><small>As the activities and drills are being entered in the
        plan, practice times are automatically calculated based on the number of minutes entered
        for each. For example, if the starting time of practice is 2:00 and the first drill
        entered is for 10 minutes, a running log of practice time is automatically calculated as
        2:00-2:10. This is a real time saver.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>Drills or activities can be entered in two different ways. All drills can
        be entered before a new plan is created by opening the drills feature of the program and
        listing the names of drills and activities. The complete list will then appear in a
        drop-down menu while the plan is being filled out. This allows a coach to scroll through
        the entire list of drills helping him to select the right one. Simply highlighting the
        desired drill and clicking with the mouse will automatically enter the name of the drill,
        another real time saver.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>The other way of entering a drill is to simply type in the name of each
        drill for every time segment. After you enter the name of each new drill, a prompt will
        come up asking if the drill should be saved to the permanent drill list. By answering yes,
        the drill can then be categorized as defensive, offensive, or other. It will then appear
        in the drop-down menu list for use in future plans. As new plans are created, the
        comprehensive drill list accumulates, adding all new drills and activities. This is not
        only a tremendous time saver but also a feature that helps a coach be more effective in
        choosing the drill or activity that will be of most benefit to the team.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>Let&#146;s say a new plan has just been created and the coach has
        forgotten to include a specific drill to help prepare the team for the next opponent. The
        insert and delete feature allows for adding or removing a drill anywhere within the plan.
        This gives the user the ability to edit a plan once it is completed.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>Professional-looking plans can be printed out with your team name at the
        top. Multiple copies can be created from the printer to be given to the coaching staff and
        team members. If you know you are going to have to plan a practice but you do not have
        access to a computer, you can create blank practice plans that you can fill out by hand
        and then later enter in the computer.</small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Old Plans </small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>All plans are stored by date and practice number. Viewing an old plan has
        never been so easy. You don&#146;t have to worry about where you are going to store old
        plans. You just simply point and click to open any old plan.</small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Drills</small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>The drill feature allows for storing all drills in defensive, offensive,
        and other categories. They can be entered by simply typing the name of the drill in the
        dialogue box within the desired category and pressing either ENTER or the ADD button. You
        can have all of your drills entered in no time. </small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Evaluations</small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>Since evaluating both practice and game performances provides important
        information in determining what will be covered in future practice sessions, this feature
        is a valuable one. The dialogue box allows you to set up evaluations according to your own
        preference. You can simply list the points that need to be worked on after a particular
        practice or game, or you can be as detailed as you want to be. These evaluations are saved
        in the same manner as old practice plans. The beauty of this feature is that you can
        easily view the evaluations while you are deciding what drills to include in that
        day&#146;s practice. If you evaluated the past Friday night's game and made a note that
        your team shot free throws poorly, you can include extra time for free throws in your
        practice schedule. These evaluations can also be printed out.</small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Scouting Reports</small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>Preparing for an upcoming opponent also provides criteria for practice
        planning. If you are facing a team that runs a 2-2-1 full-court press the entire game, the
        practice floor is where the preparation takes place. The scouting-report feature is set up
        similar to the evaluations feature. You can create as detailed a scouting report as you
        want with the ability to view it while you create your plan. These scouting reports can be
        easily printed out.</small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Practice Teams</small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>This is an outstanding feature to help coaches really be organized. You
        can also save valuable practice time. You can create 2-, 3-, 4-, and 5- player teams for
        your various drills. When you are running your 3-on- 3 Block-Out Drill in practice, you no
        longer have to stop practice and say, &quot;OK, let&#146;s see, Jimmy and David, go with
        Mark against Steve, Greg, and Joe, etc.&quot; This feature allows you to print out all of
        your practice teams on a single sheet. You can post it on the wall and require that all
        players check it before practice or during breaks to know what specific teams they are on.
        This saves practice time and it also lets your players know that you are highly organized
        and prepared.</small></small></p>
              <p><small><small>You are required to enter all the names of your players in the
        &quot;Players&quot; feature. This list then appears as you begin to create teams. You
        simply highlight the particular player&#146;s name and click the plus (+) button to add
        the player to a team. Click the minus (-) button and the player is removed from the team
        and placed back into the overall list of player names. A tab button is used to access the
        2-, 3-, 4-, and 5- player team features.</small></small></p>
              <h3><font color="#FF8040"><small><small>Summary</small></small></font></h3>
              <p><small><small>Practice Planner Pro for Basketball- Version 2.0 can definitely help any
        coach at any level be more organized and effective in creating daily practice plans. You
        can download a free 30-day full version demo of the program or receive a copy on disk by
        mail by visiting HoopTech.com. By using the various features of the program, you can
        maximize your ability to create practice plans that meet the needs of your team over the
        course of the entire season. For coaches who are not accustomed to such a high level of
        organization in this particular phase of coaching, Practice Planner Pro for Basketball
        makes the process considerably easier. When you think about it, other that the talent
        level you have to work with, there is probably no other single more important factor to
        your team's success than the effective planning of practices and their execution on the
        practice floor. </small></small></p>
