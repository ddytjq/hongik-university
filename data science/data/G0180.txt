<center><strong><font color=navy size=4>Comments on Table 8, 7A, and 4A</font></strong></center>
<p>
<IMG SRC=pixelshim.gif width=36 height=7 ALIGN=left>There's not that much to say about Table 8, except that I did it so you (and I) could have a better idea of how the 2QZ and SDSS quasars at high red shift were affecting the previous number counts.
<p>
<IMG SRC=pixelshim.gif width=36 height=7 ALIGN=left>I used a different, less sophisticated way to calculate the relative distance intervals on previous editions of these essays.   I assumed a zero density universe with no cosmological constant.   The way the quasars were behaving on those tables with this different grouping provided a vague indication of an antipode in the upper three range of red shifts.   Obviously, the addition of the 2QZ and SDSS quasars gives a clearer hint of a passing through a minimum in number counts in the mid three range of red shifts, which is not particularly well defined in the above table of the Veron 9th quasars.
<p>
<IMG SRC=pixelshim.gif width=36 height=7 ALIGN=left>When you compare Table 8 to Table 4 (realizing the biggest difference is the inclusion of the quasars in the 2QZ and SDSS catalogues in Table 7), it almost seems as though the fog is lifting.
