          <font face="Times New Roman,Times,serif" color="black" size="3">
          <P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Wes Grandstaff wants to see Texan gunner Bracey Wright shoot. And he wants to see Floridian counterpart Rashad Anderson shoot. He wants to see them shoot together.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<?xml:namespace prefix = o ns = "urn:schemas-microsoft-com:office:office" /><o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Everybody says Rashad is the best shooter in the country," the Team Texas AAU coach said this week, "but I'd like to see him and Bracey play a game of H-O-R-S-E. Although it might take a while."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>It might take an eternity.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Both heralded high schoolers are among the elite perimeter marksmen in the prep class of 2002&nbsp;-- Roanoke, Va., Duke signee J.J. Redick is probably the only one who's better&nbsp;-- but Anderson just missed the cut for the McDonald's All-American Game. Wright made it. The Indiana signee is scheduled to let fly for the West team April 4 at Madison Square Garden.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Yet folks around the nation seem to have kind of forgotten about Wright. The 6-foot-3 wing guard from The Colony, Texas, was an acknowledged top-10-caliber overall prospect heading into last year's spring and summer circuit. He still is.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But with the national media limelight shining on other stories in the Lone Star State&nbsp;-- Dallas Lincoln's Chris Bosh's emergence as one of the top big men in America, his team's 40-0 run to the mythical national championship, junior stud Kendrick Perkins over in Beaumont, even Ndi Ebi's continued progress in Houston&nbsp;-- Wright slipped under the grassroots radar a bit.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>One word: How?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>All he did was average 24.7 points, 6.3 rebounds, 2.7 assists and two blocks a game for the 29-3 squad at The Colony High.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>He was also voted Most Outstanding Player in late December at the Beach Ball Classic in Myrtle after he dropped 44 points on Philly power Simon Gratz&nbsp;-- a single-game event record previously held by former Lower Merion, Pa., superstar Kobe Bryant.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And his six-plus boards an outing&nbsp;-- for all the talk about his shooting and scoring ability&nbsp;-- were tops on the team.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It's not like he had a bad year," Colony coach Tommy Thomas said. "He was tremendous. He was our go-to-guy."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>On a roster with Division I guards Deron Williams and Bamfield Harmon as well. Williams is a 6-3 point headed for Illinois. Harmon is a 5-10 warrior&nbsp;-- Bam!&nbsp;-- who's going to Rhode Island.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>But Wright was The Colony's pulse.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"He steps up in big games," Grandstaff said. "He's not really emotional. You never know if he's missed his last shot or made his last shot. I've seen him hit game-winners and just walk off the floor. He's a leader."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And a battler. He had to be for the under-sized Cougars.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Wright guarded Bosh when The Colony went up against Lincoln&nbsp;-- "I just fronted him the whole game," he explains&nbsp;-- and even matched up with Florence, S.C., center Major Wingate at the Beach Ball.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"This year we were small," Thomas said. "It speaks to his versatility and his willingness to do what it's going to take to make the team successful."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Don't expect that post work to continue next year in Bloomington. The Hoosiers, of course, have big guys who can take care of the banging on the blocks. Wright is set to do what he does best&nbsp;-- shooting, slashing, scoring.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I think he's going to be big-time," Grandstaff said. "They've always had some shooters, but they've never really had athletic shooters. Bracey can go to the hole and dunk on you."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Not that Grandstaff is anything close to an impartial observer. He watched Wright develop for four springs and summers with his Team Texas program.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And Wright teamed with Dallas point guard Bryan Hopkins to lead the Nike-sponsored Texans to tourney victories last July at the Peach Jam in North Augusta, S.C., and the Super Showcase a week later in Orlando.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"What he and Hop did for my program was just unbelievable," Grandstaff said. "They put us on the map&nbsp;-- as well as themselves."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Wright, for one, sits No. 10 in the latest Prep Stars Recruiter's Handbook rankings. But that hardly even matters anymore. The Colony kid's high school career is over. He went for over 2,100 points in four years and helped the Cougars to an aggregate record of 113-15.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>And now he's set his sights on Mike Davis' program at IU.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"The No. 1 thing he's going to have to concentrate on is his body," Thomas said. "He's a tremendous athlete, with great elevation, but he needs to get a little stronger. But he can defend, he's got such long arms, and he's got the skills to do just about whatever he cares to do."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Which is win.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"When I think of Indiana, I think of winning," Wright said. "With the coaching change, with the new style Coach Davis has brought, that's something I'd like to be a part of."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Can he contribute right away?</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I feel I can," he said. "I love the big-time games. I love being on the main stage. That's what I was born to do."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>That, of course, and H-O-R-S-E.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H1 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">BIG-TIME (SEAN) BANKS<SPAN style="FONT-WEIGHT: normal"><o:p></o:p></SPAN></FONT></FONT></H1>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Bergen Catholic won New Jersey's Parochial A state championship for the first time since 1994 by beating St. Augustine of Richland&nbsp;71-69 Saturday at the Dunn Center in Elizabeth.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Thank you, Sean Banks. The 6-7 swingman tallied 25 points on his way to MVP honors.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"It was all about composure and patience," he told the Bergen Record. "We didn't want to leave here empty-handed."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Let this be a warning: Banks figures to be one of the top rising seniors in America this spring and summer. He's slated to be one of the more sought-after prospects at the adidas ABCD Camp in nearby Hackensack. And he'll travel the AAU circuit with coach Jimmy Salmon's loaded Tim Thomas Playaz.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Sean's stepping up, and that's what big players do," Crusaders coach Joe Dionisio told the Record. "He's defending and rebound. He's just elevated his game."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>All the way to averages of 17 points, 6.7 rebounds and three blocks a night for the 24-6 team from Oradell.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>High-major coaching staffs have noticed. Syracuse, Maryland, UConn, Villanova and Rutgers are among those programs wooing him.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<H2 style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>AROUND AND ABOUT</FONT></H2>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Bo Ryan</B> and <B>Tom Crean</B> both have their teams in the NCAA Tournament draw&nbsp;-- Wisconsin and Marquette, respectively&nbsp;-- and now they're trying to lock down the state's talent base. The top target: Appleton West center <B>Brian Butch</B>. The 6-11 prospect, averaging 23 points a game this year, already has scholarship offers from Arizona, Tennessee and Purdue, according to the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel. But he's considering staying put and playing for one of the two in-state biggies.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"Brian had kind of written off Wisconsin until Bo Ryan was hired," West coach <B>Greg Hartjes</B> told the Journal Sentinel. Not anymore. "When I was an assistant, I relished the opportunity someday to have the team fighting for first place or winning a conference championship and then going into homes for recruiting. I would think my assistants right now are like, 'OK, boy, open those doors up. Let's get in.'"</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Amare Stoudemire</B> wants to play golf. Seriously. "It's something I've been wanting to do for a long time," the 6-10 manchild is quoted as saying in a press release distributed earlier this week by <B>Kelvin Lindsey</B> of <B>Marc Little</B> Public Relations, the firm that's been working with Stoudemire since last summer's Nike Camp. The update also informs all interested parties that the kid's going to his senior prom and working out with a personal trainer.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>Oh, and about the decision he's going to have to make, whether or not to hit college for a year or just go straight to the NBA? Quoth Florida's 6A Player of the Year: "That decision is coming. I have a tutor who is helping me with the entrance exams so I can qualify for college if that is the option I choose." Here's hoping another press release shows up when he does.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Manhattan Rice is set to face Bronx All Hallows Wednesday night at Fordham for New York City's Catholic "A" championship. Said this </FONT><A href="http://www.basketballamerica.com/show_feature.html?article_id=447&amp;section_id=563"><FONT face="Times New Roman" color=#800080 size=3>right here back in January</FONT></A><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>, but it needs to be said again: The winner of this conference&nbsp;-- the best high school league in the land&nbsp;-- ought to be in the nation's top 5. Period. Rice is sixth in this week's USA Today poll and in the Student Sports rankings. Coach <B>Maurice Hicks</B>' 26-2 Raiders are heavily favored in their fray with 18-10 All Hallows.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>"I've been waking up every day at 5, just waiting for the game to come," 7-2 Rice giant <B>Shagari Alleyne</B> told the New York Post. "We had to get back here and win to bring back the tradition. I came to some championship games and always wanted to be a part of it. Now it's mandatory that we win."</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** The 4A championship game in Texas received the most media attention. And rightfully so: <B>Chris Bosh</B> of Dallas Lincoln and <B>Kendrick Perkins</B> of Beaumont Ozen&nbsp;-- the top two big men in Texas and two of the best in the whole country&nbsp;-- went at each other. But the ending in the 5A game was just too cool: Junior point guard <B>Chris Ross</B> threw in a half-court buzzer-beater to help San Antonio Jay beat Dallas Kimball 54-53. It was his first basket of the game&nbsp;... and it was his birthday.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** Jersey City's St. Anthony high school juggernaut moved to 27-1 by winning its 23rd Parochial B state championship this past weekend. That leaves <B>Bob Hurley</B>'s program one title shy of tying Cheyenne (Wyo.) Central for the national record. Now the second-ranked Friars are set to shoot for their second straight Tournament of Champions trophy.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; TEXT-ALIGN: justify"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** <B>Wes Grandstaff</B> says his Team Texas squad will run this spring and summer with backcourt stars <B>Dez Willingham</B> from Desoto and <B>Brett McDade</B> from Denton Ryan. Grandstaff's kids are scheduled to play at Boo Williams in Hampton, Va., in April, Howard Pulley's Sabes Foundation Invitational in Minneapolis in May, the Rumble in the Bronx in June and the Peach Jam and Super Showcase in Orlando in July.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoBodyText style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT size=3><FONT face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></P>
<P class=MsoBodyText style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face="Times New Roman" size=3>*** North Carolina signee <B>Sean May</B>'s high school career is over. His Bloomington North team lost to Martinsville, Ind., Saturday night in a regional semifinal. He had 17 points and 10 boards in the defeat. Next stop: Chapel Hill. Well, first the McDonald's game and the Jordan Brand Cap Classic in April&nbsp;... then Chapel Hill.</FONT></P>
