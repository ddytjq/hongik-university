</FONT><HR>Most people around the area cut vinyl on a cutter/plotter, which small cheap ones can be had for less than 2K, and the bigger "PRO" 30" machines (like I use) are up around 5K. But you get what you pay for.<P>The printing machines start at around 5K-6K and go up to 50K. The real cheap ones around 5k, the decals fade fast and don't last long. The high end stuff like the Gerber Edge cost's around 30K, and the Summa Chrome is 50K, even those will fade without lamination to protect against the sun's UV.<P>I saw a Hooters decal Flinner made on his Edge for J Johnson, and by the end of the year the decal was almost all white from the fading.<P>There are a few guys (very few) who do it, because the cost to get a good machine is sky high, and without lamination they don't hold up very long. Technology is getting better, but soo are the prices to get it.<P>That's the main reason most of us don't get into it.....too much cash to spend for somthing that is not going to last as long as regular vinyl. But there are people who print on vinyl, but they only can tell you "up" to two years against fading, and regular vinyl is garranteed for 5, 7 and even 9 years against fading.<P>Just giving you the pro's and con's of what you are asking for.<P>Good luck and I hope you find what you need. <P></FONT><P align=right><FONT SIZE="1" color="#800080" face="Verdana, Arial">IP: 24.154.165.53</font></P></td></tr><tr bgcolor="#f7f7f7">
