  <p style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; word-spacing: 0; margin: 0" align="left" HSPACE="40" VSPACE="40"><font face="CG Times" size="4"><b>This
  was Glen Hoddle's one and only world cup.&nbsp; In qualification we were
  nearly perfect but lost our first ever home qualifier in the World Cup to
  Italy, even though we were the better team.&nbsp; When the final group game
  arrived, we went to Rome needing only a draw to win the group and in front of
  8,000 beltless and coinless English fans, a 0-0 draw was achieved&nbsp; whilst
  runners-up Italy
  qualified by beating Russia in a two legged play-off.&nbsp; Hoddle then made a
  huge decision and left Gascoigne out of the squad, even though he played in
  five games throughout qualification.&nbsp; In our first game in Marseille, England
  marched past Tunisia 2-0 with goals from Shearer and a cracker from Scholes
  but the headlines were taken from the beach where English supporters were
  attacked, arrested and then blamed for a huge disturbance which was started by
  French-Africans.&nbsp; <img border="0" src="lens.gif" align="right" hspace="10" vspace="10" width="300" height="161">We
  then went to Toulouse to play Romania and were 1-0 down before Hoddle finally
  sent Michael Owen onto the pitch.&nbsp; A few minutes later the Liverpool player had
  equalised but England
  eventually lost 2-1 with a soft goal in the dying seconds.&nbsp; That meant we had
  to beat Colombia in Lens in Northern France to progress.&nbsp; A huge England
  following crossed the channel and took over the towns of Lens, Lille, Arras
  and Calais.&nbsp; Anderton opened the scoring and then Beckham curled a
  free-kick into the net for his first goal for England to make the final score
  2-0 and set up a meeting with Argentina in St Ettiene.&nbsp; The game was
  electric from start to finish and included two penalties, a Beckham sending
  off, a brilliant solo goal from Owen, three other goals and one disallowed
  golden goal from Sol Campbell, but once more it was the dreaded penalty shoot
  out that eliminated England.&nbsp;&nbsp;</b></font>
