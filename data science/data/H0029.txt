<A NAME="gluteal"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Gluteal Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="tensor fascia lata"><FONT SIZE=+1>Tensor fascia lata</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: 
	<OL><LI>anterior aspect of iliac crest
	<LI>anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS)</OL>
<LI>Insertion: anterior aspect of IT band, below greater trochanter
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>hip flexion
	<LI>medially rotate & abduct a flexed thigh
	<LI>tenses IT tract to support femur on the tibia during standing</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>superior gluteal artery
	<LI>lateral femoral circumflex artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: superior gluteal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="gluteus maximus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Gluteus maximus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: 
	<OL><LI>outer rim of ilium (medial aspect)
	<LI>dorsal surface of sacrum and coccyx
	<LI>sacrotuberous ligament</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>IT band (primary insertion)
	<LI>gluteal tuberosity of femur</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>powerful  extensor of hip
	<LI>laterally rotates thigh
	<LI>upper fibers aid in abduction of thigh
	<LI>fibers of IT band stabilize a fully extended knee</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>inferior gluteal artery (primary)
	<LI>superior gluteal artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: inferior gluteal nerve, L5,S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="gluteus medius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Gluteus medius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: 
	<OL><LI>outer aspect of ilium (between iliac crest and anterior and posterior gluteal lines)
	<LI>upper fascia (AKA gluteal aponeurosis)</OL>
<LI>Insertion: superior aspect of greater trochanter
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>anterior and lateral fibers abduct and medially rotate the thigh
	<LI>posterior fibers may laterally rotate thigh
	<LI>stabilizes the pelvis and prevents free limb from sagging during gait</OL>
<LI>Blood: superior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: superior gluteal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="gluteus minimus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Gluteus minimus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: outer aspect of ilium (between anterior and inferior gluteal lines)
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>greater trochanter (anterior to medius)
	<LI>articular capsule of hip joint</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>abduct and medially rotate the thigh
	<LI>stabilizes the pelvis and prevents free limb from sagging during gait</OL>
<LI>Blood: superior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: superior gluteal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="piriformis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Piriformis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: pelvic surface of sacrum (anterior portion)
<LI>Insertion: medial surface of greater trochanter (through greater sciatic foramen)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>lateral rotation of extended thigh
	<LI>abducts a flexed thigh</OL>
<LI>Blood: 
	<OL><LI>superior gluteal artery
	<LI>inferior gluteal artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: nerve to piriformis, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="superior gemellus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Superior gemellus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: ischial spine
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of greater trochanter via upper tendon of obturator internus
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>laterally rotates femur
	<LI>abducts thigh when flexed</OL>
<LI>Blood: inferior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: nerve to obturator internus, L5,S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="obturator internus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Obturator internus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>internal aspect margins of obturator foramen
	<LI>obturator membrane</OL>
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of greater trochanter (through lesser sciatic foramen)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>laterally rotates femur
	<LI>abducts thigh when flexed</OL>
<LI>Blood: inferior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: nerve to obturator internus, L5,S1,2</UL>

					
<BR>
<P><A NAME="inferior gemellus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Inferior gemellus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: ischial tuberosity
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of greater trochanter via lower tendon of obturator internus
<LI>Action: laterally rotates femur
<LI>Blood: inferior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: nerve to quadratus femoris, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="quadratus femoris"><FONT SIZE=+1>Quadratus femoris</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: lateral aspect of ischial tuberosity
<LI>Insertion: quadrate line (along posterior aspect of femur and intertrochanteric crest)	
<LI>Action: laterally rotates femur
<LI>Blood: inferior gluteal artery
<LI>Nerve: nerve to quadratus femoris, L4,5,S1</UL>


<P><BR>
<A NAME="postthigh"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Posterior Thigh Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="semitendinosus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Semitendinosus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: ischial tuberosity
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>medial aspect of tibial shaft
	<LI>contributes to the pez anserine</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends hip
	<LI>flexes knee
	<LI>medially rotates tibia</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>perforating branches of profunda femoris
	<LI>inferior gluteal artery (to upper)</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve of sciatic bundle, L5,S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="semimembranosus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Semimembranosus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: ischial tuberosity
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>posterior medial aspect of medial tibial condyle
	<LI>fibers join to form most of oblique popliteal ligament (& medial meniscus)</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexes knee
	<LI>extends hip
	<LI>medially rotates tibia
	<LI>pulls medial meniscus posterior during flexion</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>perforating branches of profunda femoris
	<LI>inferior gluteal artery (to upper)</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve of sciatic bundle, L5,S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="biceps femoris"><FONT SIZE=+1>Biceps femoris</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>long head: ischial tuberosity
	<LI>short head: lateral lip of linea aspera and the lateral intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>head of fibula
	<LI>maybe to the lateral tibial condyle</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexor at the knee (mainly short head)
	<LI>laterally rotates thigh if flexed at the knee
	<LI>extends hip (long head)</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>perforating branches of profunda femoris
	<LI>inferior gluteal artery (to upper)</OL>
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>long head - tibial nerve, L5,S1,2
	<LI>short head - common peroneal nerve, L5,S1</OL></UL>


<A HREF="#adductor magnus">Adductor magnus,</A> posterior fibers are sometimes considered part of this group. Its information is listed below with the other thigh adductors.

<A NAME="addthigh"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Adductor Thigh Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>

<BR>
<P>Note:  The muscles in this group may also receive blood from obturator artery.


<BR>
<P><A NAME="adductor longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Adductor longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: anterior surface of pubis, just inferior to the pubic tubercle
<LI>Insertion: medial lip of linea aspera on middle half of femur
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adducts thigh
	<LI>flexes thigh
	<LI>may laterally rotate thigh at the hip</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of femoral artery
<LI>Nerve: obturator nerve, L2,3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="adductor brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Adductor brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: body & inferior ramus of pubis
<LI>Insertion: superior portion of linea aspera
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adducts thigh (major)
	<LI>aids in flexion of thigh
	<LI>may laterally rotate thigh at the hip</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of femoral artery
<LI>Nerve: obturator nerve, L2,3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="adductor magnus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Adductor magnus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>anterior fibers: inferior pubic ramus
	<LI>oblique fibers: ischial ramus
	<LI>posterior fibers: ischial tuberosity</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>proximal 1/3 of linea aspera
	<LI>adductor tubercle</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adducts the thigh
	<LI>posterior fibers also extend and laterally rotate thigh</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>muscular branches of profunda femoris</OL>
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>anterior fibers: obturator nerve, L2,3,4
	<LI>posterior fibers: tibial nerve of sciatic bundle, L4,5</OL></UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="gracilis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Gracilis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: body of pubis & inferior pubic ramus
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>medial surface of proximal tibia, inferior to tibial condyle
	<LI>contributes to the pez anserine</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adducts thigh
	<LI>flexes knee
	<LI>medially rotates tibia</OL>
<LI>Blood: obturator artery
<LI>Nerve: obturator nerve, L2,3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="obturator externus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Obturator externus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial surface of obturator foramen
	<LI>external surface of obturator membrane</OL>
<LI>Insertion: trochanteric fossa of femur
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>laterally rotates thigh
	<LI>assists in flexion of hip joint</OL>
<LI>Blood: obturator artery
<LI>Nerve: obturator nerve, L2,3,4</UL>



<P><BR>
<A NAME="antthigh"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Anterior Thigh Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="sartorius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Sartorius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: anterior superior iliac spine (ASIS)
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>upper medial surface of body of tibia
	<LI>contributes to pez anserine</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexes hip and knee
	<LI>laterally rotates thigh if flexed at the hip</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>muscular branches of profunda femoris artery
	<LI>saphenous branch of descending genicular artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, L2,3</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="rectus femoris"><FONT SIZE=+1>Rectus femoris</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>anterior head: anterior inferior iliac spine (AIIS)
	<LI>posterior head: ilium just above the acetabulum</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>common quadriceps tendon into patella
	<LI>tibial tuberosity via patellar ligament</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends knee
	<LI>flexes hip</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, [L2],3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="vastus lateralis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Vastus lateralis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>greater trochanter
	<LI>lateral lip of linea aspera
	<LI>lateral intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>common quadriceps tendon into patella
	<LI>tibial tuberosity via patellar ligament</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends knee
	<LI>can abnormally displace patella</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, [L2],3,4</UL>



<BR>
<P><A NAME="vastus intermedius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Vastus intermedius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: anterior lateral aspect of the femoral shaft
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>common quadriceps tendon into patella
	<LI>tibial tuberosity via patellar ligament</OL>
<LI>Action: extends knee
<LI>Blood: lateral femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, [L2],3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="vastus medialis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Vastus medialis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>intertrochanteric line of femur
	<LI>medial aspect of linea aspera</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>common quadriceps tendon into patella
	<LI>tibial tuberosity via patellar ligament</OL>
<LI>Action: extends knee
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>muscular branches of profunda femoris artery
	<LI>saphenous branch of descending genicular artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, [L2],3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="articularis genus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Articularis genus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>distal portion of anterior femoral surface, close to the knee
	<LI>off the deep fibers of the vastus intermedius</OL>
<LI>Insertion: synovial membrane of the knee joint
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>pulls the synovial membrane of the knee superior with knee extension
	<LI>prevents impingement of the synovial membrane between patella and the femur</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: branches of femoral nerve, L3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="psoas major"><FONT SIZE=+1>Psoas major</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>transverse processes of L1-L5
	<LI>vertebral bodies of T12-L4 and the intervening intervertebral discs</OL>
<LI>Insertion: iliopsoas tendon to the lesser trochanter of the femur
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>hip flexion
	<LI>lateral rotation</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of medial femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: ventral rami, L1,2,3</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="illiacus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Illiacus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: inner surface of upper iliac fossa
<LI>Insertion: iliopsoas tendon to the lesser trochanter of the femur
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>powerful hip flexion
	<LI>lateral rotation</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of medial femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve: femoral nerve, L3,4</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="pectineus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Pectineus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>pectineal line of the pubis
	<LI>superior pubic ramus</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<UL><LI>the pectineal line of the femur
	<LI>(just below the lesser trochanter on the posterior aspect of the femur)</UL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexes hip
	<LI>adducts thigh
	<LI>medially rotates thigh</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of medial femoral circumflex artery
<LI>Nerve:
	<UL><LI>femoral nerve, L3,4 OR
	<LI>obturator nerve, L2,3,4 OR
	<LI>femoral L3,4 and accessory obturator L3,4</OL></UL>

<A NAME="postleg"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Posterior Leg Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="gastrocnemius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Gastrocnemius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial head: just above medial condyle of femur
	<LI>lateral head: just above lateral condyle of femur</OL>
<LI>Insertion: calcaneus via lateral portion of calcaneal tendon
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>plantarflex the ankle
	<LI>knee flexion (when not weight bearing)
	<LI>stabilizes ankle & knee when standing</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>sural branches of popliteal artery
	<LI>muscular branches of peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="soleus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Soleus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>upper fibula
	<LI>soleal line of tibia</OL>
<LI>Insertion: calcaneus via medial portion of calcaneal tendon
<LI>Action: plantarflex the foot
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>sural branches of popliteal artery
	<LI>muscular branches of peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, S1,2</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="plantaris"><FONT SIZE=+1>Plantaris</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: above the lateral head of gastrocnemius on femur
<LI>Insertion: calcaneus, medial to calcaneal tendon, or blending with the calcaneal tendon
<LI>Action: like a weak gastrocnemius
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>sural branches of popliteal artery
	<LI>muscular branches of peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, S1,2</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="popliteus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Popliteus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lateral femoral condyle
	<LI>arcuate popliteal ligament
	<LI>lateral meniscus
	<LI>knee joint capsule</OL>
<LI>Insertion: posterior tibial surface above the soleal line
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>insertion fixed: laterally rotates femur on tibia & unlocks knee
	<LI>origin fixed: medially rotates tibia on femur & unlocks knee</OL>
<LI>Blood: sural branches of popliteal artery
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, L5,S1</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="flexor digitorum longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Flexor digitorum longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>posterior surface of tibia
	<LI>crural fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion: plantar surface of bases of the 2-5th distal phalanges
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>primarily flexes 2nd - 5th toes
	<LI>weak plantarflexor
	<LI>weak inversion & adduction of foot</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, L5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="tibialis posterior"><FONT SIZE=+1>Tibialis posterior</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>posterior, proximal tibia
	<LI>interosseous membrane
	<LI>medial surface of fibula</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>navicular tuberosity (principle)
	<LI>all 3 cuneiforms (plantar surface)
	<LI>bases of 2nd-4th metatarsals
	<LI>cuboid
	<LI>sustentaculum tali of calcaneus</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>stabilizes ankle
	<LI>inversion & adduction of foot
	<LI>prevents hyperpronation while in gait
	<LI>weak plantarflexion of ankle</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, L5,S1</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="flexor hallucis longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Flexor hallucis longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>posterior, inferior 2/3 of fibula
	<LI>interosseous membrane
	<LI>crural fascia & posterior intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: plantar surface of distal phalanx of hallux
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexes big toe (hallux)
	<LI>weak plantarflexion of the foot
	<LI>weak inversion & adduction of foot</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>peroneal artery
	<LI>posterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: tibial nerve, L5,S1,2</UL>

<A NAME="antlatleg"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Anterolateral Leg Musculature</H2>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="peroneus longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Peroneus longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>head of the fibula
	<LI>proximal 2/3 of lateral fibula
	<LI>adjacent intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>plantar surface of cuboid
	<LI>base of 1st & (2nd) metatarsal
	<LI>plantar surface of medial cuneiform</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>eversion & abduction of the foot
	<LI>weak plantarflexion of the foot at the transverse tarsal joint</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the peroneal artery
<LI>Nerve: superficial peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>



<BR>
<P><A NAME="peroneus brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Peroneus brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>distal 2/3 of lateral fibula
	<LI>posterior and anterior intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: tuberosity on lateral aspect of base of 5th metatarsal
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>eversion & abduction of the foot
	<LI>weak plantarflexion of foot</OL>
<LI>Blood: muscular branches of the peroneal artery
<LI>Nerve: superficial peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>

<BR>
<P><A NAME="tibialis anterior"><FONT SIZE=+1>Tibialis anterior</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lateral tibial condyle
	<LI>proximal 2/3 of anteriolateral surface of tibia
	<LI>interosseous membrane
	<LI>anterior intermuscular septum & crural fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>medial & plantar surface of base of 1st metatarsal
	<LI>medial & plantar surface of the cuneiform</OL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>strongest dorsiflexor
	<LI>inverts & adducts the foot</OL>
<LI>Blood: anterior tibial artery
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="extensor hallucis longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Extensor hallucis longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial aspect of the fibula
	<LI>interosseous membrane
	<LI>crural fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion: dorsal surface of base of proximal and distal phalanx of hallux
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends distal phalanx of big toe
	<LI>weak dorsiflexor
	<LI>weak inversion & adduction</OL>
<LI>Blood: anterior tibial artery
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="extensor digitorum longus"><FONT SIZE=+1>Extensor digitorum longus</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lateral condyle of the tibia
	<LI>upper anterior surface of fibula
	<LI>interosseous membrane
	<LI>crural fascia</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<UL><LI>dorsal surface of the bases of the middle & distal phalanxes of the 2nd-5th rays
	<LI>(via 4 tendons and giving a fibrous expansion)</UL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends the lateral 4 toes
	<LI>weak dorsiflexor & everts foot</OL>
<LI>Blood: anterior tibial artery
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="peroneus tertius"><FONT SIZE=+1>Peroneus tertius</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>distal 1/3 of anterior fibula
	<LI>distal & lateral aspect of extensor digitorum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: dorsal surface of base of 5th metatarsal
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>extends the 5th toe
	<LI>weak dorsiflexor & everts foot</OL>
<LI>Blood: anterior tibial artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5,S1</UL>

<A NAME="foot"><H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Foot Musculature</H2>
<p><p align=center><A HREF="#page_top"><IMG SRC="images/colorbar_up.gif" WIDTH=504 HEIGHT=13 BORDER=0 ALT=
"------------------------====================------------------------"></A>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="abductor hallucis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Abductor hallucis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial process of calcaneal tuberosity
	<LI>flexor retinaculum
	<LI>plantar aponeurosis
	<LI>medial intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of base of proximal phalanx of hallux
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flexes the big toe (primary action)
	<LI>may assist in abduction of big toe</OL>
<LI>Blood: medial plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: medial plantar nerve, L5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="flexor digitorum brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Flexor digitorum brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial process of calcaneal tuberosity
	<LI>plantar aponeurosis</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<UL><LI>both sides of the bases of the middle phalanx of rays 2-5
	<LI>(each of the 4 tendons splits forming tunnel for FDL)</UL>
<LI>Action: flexes toes 2-5
<LI>Blood: medial plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: medial plantar nerve, L5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="abductor digiti minimi"><FONT SIZE=+1>Abductor digiti minimi</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>lateral & medial processes of the calcaneal tuberosity
	<LI>plantar aponeurosis
	<LI>lateral intermuscular septum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: lateral aspect of base of proximal phalanx of 5th ray
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>abducts 5th toe
	<LI>aids in flexing</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="abductor ossis metatarsi quinti"><FONT SIZE=+1>Abductor ossis metatarsi quinti</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: from fibers of abductor digiti minimi
<LI>Insertion: into the 5th metatarsal
<LI>Action: abducts the 5th ray
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="quadratus plantae"><FONT SIZE=+1>Quadratus plantae</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial head: medial calcaneus
	<LI>lateral head: lateral calcaneus & long plantar ligament</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<UL><LI>lateral margin of tendon of flexor digitorum longus (FDL)
	<LI>may send slips into the distal tendons</UL>
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>assists FDL in flexing the distal phalanxes of 2nd-5th toes
	<LI>corrects FDL from pulling toes medially</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="lumbricals"><FONT SIZE=+1>Lumbricals</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin: from tendons of FDL:
	<OL><LI>1st: medial aspect of tendon to 2nd ray
	<LI>2nd-4th: two heads between the tendons in which they lie</OL>
<LI>Insertion: extensor tendons of EDL on dorsal foot
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>flex proximal phalanges at MTP
	<LI>extend middle & distal phalanges at IP</OL>
<LI>Blood:
	<OL><LI>1st: medial plantar artery
	<LI>2nd-4th: lateral plantar artery</OL>
<LI>Nerve:
	<OL><LI>1st: medial plantar nerve, L5,S1
	<LI>2nd-4th: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</OL></UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="flexor hallucis brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Flexor hallucis brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>medial aspect of the cuboid
	<LI>lateral cuneiform</OL>
<LI>Insertion:
	<OL><LI>medial aspect of base of proximal phalanx of hallux
	<LI>lateral aspect of base of proximal phalanx of hallux</OL>
<LI>Action: flexes hallux at MTP
<LI>Blood: medial plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: medial plantar nerve, L5,S1</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="adductor hallucis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Adductor hallucis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>oblique head: base of 2nd-4th metatarsals & long plantar ligament
	<LI>transverse head: deep transverse metatarsal ligament & plantar ligaments at MTP joints</OL>
<LI>Insertion: lateral aspect of base of proximal phalanx of hallux
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adduction of hallux at MTP
	<LI>flexes hallux at MTP</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="flexor digiti minimi brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Flexor digiti minimi brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>base of 5th metatarsal
	<LI>digital sheath of peroneus longus</OL>
<LI>Insertion: lateral aspect of base of proximal phalanx of 5th ray
<LI>Action: flexes the 5th toe at MTP
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar artery
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve, S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="plantar interossei"><FONT SIZE=+1>Plantar interossei</FONT> (3 muscles)
<UL><LI>Origin: medial aspect of 3rd-5th metatarsals (each muscle has a single head)
<LI>Insertion: medial aspect of base of proximal phalanx of the same ray (of 3rd-5th rays)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>adduct toes 3-5
	<LI>flex toes 3-5 at MTP</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar arch
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve (deep branch), S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="dorsal interossei"><FONT SIZE=+1>Dorsal interossei</FONT> (4 muscles)
<UL><LI>Origin: from both metatarsals between which they lie
<LI>Insertion: base of proximal phalanx closest to the axis of the foot (2nd ray)
<LI>Action:
	<OL><LI>abduct toes 2-4
	<LI>flexes toes 2-4 at MTP</OL>
<LI>Blood: lateral plantar arch
<LI>Nerve: lateral plantar nerve (deep branch), S1,2</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="extensor hallucis brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Extensor hallucis brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>upper anterolateral calcaneus
	<LI>inferior extensor retinaculum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: base of proximal phalanx of hallux
<LI>Action: extends hallux
<LI>Blood: dorsalis pedis artery
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5</UL>


<BR>
<P><A NAME="extensor digitorum brevis"><FONT SIZE=+1>Extensor digitorum brevis</FONT>
<UL><LI>Origin:
	<OL><LI>upper anterolateral calcaneus
	<LI>inferior extensor retinaculum</OL>
<LI>Insertion: middle & distal phalanges of 2nd-4th rays (via EDL)
<LI>Action: extends 2nd-4th rays
<LI>Blood: dorsalis pedis artery
<LI>Nerve: deep peroneal nerve, L4,5</UL>
