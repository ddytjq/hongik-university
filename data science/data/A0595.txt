		    <p class="contentsubhead">Yorkshire Bank Announces Annual Results</p>
		  <p class="contenttextbold">2 November 2000</p>
		  <p>Yorkshire Bank today released its annual results and announced pre-tax profits of £217.6 million, an increase of 31.7%. </p>
		  <p>Total lending is up 20.4% to £5,991.7 million compared with the same period last year, with the strong growth in both personal and business loans. </p>
		  <p>"These results reflect the Bank's ability to retain existing customers and attract new ones by offering clearly differentiated products and services which capitalise on the group's global expertise", said Stuart Grimshaw, Chief Executive of Yorkshire Bank. </p>
		  <p>In the highly competitive segment of personal finance, the Bank achieved an impressive 30.3% growth rate in mortgage business. This was due in part to the introduction of the flexible repayment mortgage to the UK following its development by the Group in New Zealand. </p>
		  <p>A 12-week campaign on personal loan sales achieved record volumes of new business with new loans up 66% on the previous year. </p>
		  <p>"Yorkshire Bank has taken a positive stance in favour of its customers, launching the new card-based product which provides low-cost access to the banking system and carefully assessing its role in the community where the Bank is the sole provider of services", said Mr Grimshaw. </p>
		  <p>Yorkshire Bank has expanded the number of ways the customer can access Bank services. During the year, the Bank became one of the first in the world to offer customers access to a new generation of ATMs with an increased range of features including the potential for Internet access. The Bank intends to introduce full Internet banking in the coming year. </p>
		  <p>"Delivering tailored products and services through an appropriate variety of channels is a key strategy for us", said Mr Grimshaw. "Rising customer satisfaction levels show we are on the correct course". </p>
		  <p>The introduction of innovative retail products such as the NSPCC Charity Bond and the Guaranteed Equity Bond have been well received by the market. The charity bond raised £20,000 for the NSPCC. </p>
		  <p>The launch of Yorkshire Bank's Tailored Business Loans has been particularly successful, extending the benefits of interest rate and foreign exchange risk management to a wider range of businesses. </p>
		  <p>Major new Executive and Business Banking Centres were opened in Leeds, Manchester and York. York also hosts Yorkshire Bank's first Agribusiness Banking Centre, further evidence of the Bank's long-term commitment to the industry. </p>
		  <p>The Group's Wholesale Financial Services division in the UK acted as one of the lead managers in support of the Vodafone acquisition of Mannesmann, the largest hostile take-over in history. </p>
		  <p>Yorkshire Bank Insurance Services returned another strong performance, with gross income up by 14% to £15.4 million. </p>
		    <p class="contenttextbold">Yorkshire Bank PLC</p>
