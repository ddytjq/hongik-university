                      <h1>Mortgage Rates and Charges</h1>
<h3>Consumer Credit Information</h3>
<p>The Consumer Credit Act requires the Annual Percentage Rate to be based on borrowers
gross repayments.  As repayments are required monthly, this means that the mortgage
rates are normally lower than the corresponding APR.  The table below shows a typical
example of the APR and total amount payable based on daily interest and the assumptions
that follow.</p>
<h3>Assumptions</h3>
<ol>
<li><p>The mortgage rates shown will not be varied. In practice building society interest
rates may vary.</p>
<li><p>The initial charges are for estimated proposal fees and solicitors mortgage
costs based on a loan of £50,000 over a 25 year term and a purchase price of £70,000.</p>
<li><p>The timing of the mortgage advance is the beginning of the month.  Repayments
are made monthly on the date due under the mortgage deed.</p>
<li><p>The mortgage is paid at the end of the term.</p>
<li><p>The total amount payable under the interest only mortgage excludes a monthly
premium for an appropriate repayment vehicle.</p>
<li><p>The examples do not allow for any insurance premiums.</p>
</ol>

<h3>Mortgage Arrears</h3>
<p>The Cheshire is very keen to help any of its borrowers who suffer financial difficulties
and we work with our customers to resolve problems wherever possible. If you ever
find yourself in financial difficulties, you should let us know as soon as possible.
Due to the nature of this work, however, there are instances where the Society may
have to levy fees and pass on appropriate legal costs.</p>
<p>Depending on the status of your account, the following charges may be added to
your mortgage.</p>
