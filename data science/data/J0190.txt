<h1>Victory caps Irvan's return from near-fatal crash</h1>

LOUDON, N.H. -- Ernie Irvan completed his miraculous comeback from a nearly fatal crash less than two years ago by claiming victory in the NASCAR Jiffy Lube 300 at New Hampshire International Raceway on Sunday.
<p> 
&quot;Until you win, you never know if you're going to do it
again,&quot; said Irvan, who won the Jiffy Lube 300 on Sunday for his
first victory since crashing at Brooklyn, Mich., on Aug. 20, 1994.
&quot;Maybe this will shut them up.&quot;
<p>
Irvan took the lead on the 276th lap and beat teammate Dale Jarrett to the finish by 5.5 seconds for his
13th career victory and his first since May of 1994, when he won
three times.
<p>
Irvan was leading the Winston Cup points race that year when he
crashed at Michigan.  He was given little chance
for survival, but he left the hospital several weeks later
and, after sitting out 14 months, returned for the final three
races last year.
<p>
&quot;I did like (crew chief) Larry McReynolds wanted -- I did a
Polish victory lap in honor of Alan Kulwicki and Davey Allison,&quot;
said Irvan, who promply turned his Ford Thunderbird around after
crossing the finish and took a victory lap in the wrong
direction at the conclusion of the race.  &quot;I didn't even think
about winning until the last lap.&quot; 
<p>
Despite nagging health problems caused by the 1994 crash, Irvan appeared back in the driver's seat this year with eight
finishes in the top 10, including a second, before Sunday. He finished fifth the previous two weeks.
<p>
Irvan started sixth and led several times for short stretches,
including when Jeff Gordon lost power on the 237th lap. Gordon, the
defending champion and running third in the overall point
standings, suffered two ignitions failures, and wound up 34th.
<p>
&quot;I never had any doubt in my mind that I could do it again from
the time I started testing,&quot; Irvan said. &quot;I had a lot of injuries,
but I got hooked up with a lot of good doctors.
<p>
&quot;We've struggled all year -- some of it was my fault, some
theirs -- but this (winning) is what it's all about in Winston
Cup racing.&quot;
<p>
Irvan last pitted on lap 244, and took on only two tires and
some fuel.
<p>
&quot;I didn't want to lose a lap,&quot; McReynolds
said about his decision to change only two tires. &quot;Seven, eight,
nine, 10 seconds; that's all. Track position was as important as
I've seen in any race.&quot;
<p>
Jarrett, the driver who replaced Irvan last year and after a
strong season was added to the Yates Racing effort, was
tremendously happy with his teammate's victory. 
<p>
&quot;I'm not sure I'd have been more happy if I'd won,&quot; said
Jarrett, the winner of this year's Daytona 500 and is fourth in Winston Cup points.  Jarrett recovered from a spinout
early in the race to claw his way to the front of the field. 
<p>
Fourth-place Jeff Burton, who drives a Ford for Roush Racing,
also expressed his pleasure at Irvan's victory. 
<p>
&quot;It's pretty special to see the 28 car win with all he had to go
through,&quot; said Burton, the 1994 NASCAR Rookie of the Year.  &quot;I'd
rather win, but if it's not one of the other Roush cars or my
brother (Ward Burton), I'm happy to see him.&quot; 
<p>
Terry Labonte finished sixth to overtake Dale Earnhardt
for the points lead. Labonte, who started the day five points
behind Earnhardt, led until he had to stop for gas on
the 275th lap. <p>

Earnhardt, with four of his seven titles in the 1990s, broke on
the last lap, finished 12th and fell 18 points behind Labonte, who
is seeking his first title since 1984. Gordon dropped 116 points
behind.<p>

Ricky Rudd finished third, Jeff Burton fourth and Robert
Pressley fifth on the hot, sunny day before 82,000 fans, the
largest crowd to jam the speedway since it ran its first Winston
Cup race in 1993.<p>

Pole-sitter Ricky Craven ended up 26th after having problems throughout the race.<p>

Gordon had appeared on his way to victory after he took the
lead after a restart on the 195th lap. He opened a good spread
over Irvan, but he lost power out of Turn 3 and was forced to
coast around the track.<p>

There were eight caution flags for 49 laps. Thirty-three of the
40 cars were running at the end in the rugged race that featured a
lot of bumping early.<p>
