<h1>Gordon gasses up, grabs TranSouth 400</h1>
<p>DARLINGTON, S.C. -- Jeff Gordon is back on track. <p>
The defending Winston Cup champion had just enough to gas -- covering the final 73 laps without a pit stop -- to win Sunday's TranSouth Financial 400 at Darlington International Raceway.<p>
Gordon, who finished 42nd and 40th in the season's first two races, has now earned finishes of first, third and first in the past three races. <p>
Gordon's better fuel mileage was the key to overcoming his chief rival at the end of the race, Dale Jarrett. <p>
&quot;We had an awesome car all day,&quot; said Gordon, who collected $97,310 and averaged 124.793 miles per hour in victory. &quot;Dale gave us a real fight. I didn't know if I could beat him.&quot; <p>
With Gordon held up in heavy traffic, Jarrett took the lead 15 laps from the end of the 293-lap race. <p>
           &quot;When he first got by me, my crew said not to worry because he had to stop,&quot; Gordon explained. &quot;I kept asking them if they were sure because he sure wasn't driving like a guy who knows he has to stop.&quot; <p>
Gordon admitted his tank was, &quot;Pretty close (to empty).&quot; <p>
As the &quot;Track too Tough to Tame&quot; swallowed up many Winston Cup competitors throughout the day, Gordon was the only car who stayed among the leaders all day. <p>
&quot;This was a real tough place for us when we first started racing,&quot; said Gordon, who has 11 career victories, including last September's Southern 500 on the 1.366-mile Darlington oval. &quot;Winning two in a row at this place is pretty unbelievable.&quot; <p>
Jarrett, the current points leader, battled Gordon hard, as the two drivers traded the lead lap after lap before Jarrett ran out of gas with only two laps left. He finished two laps down in 15th position, his worst finish of the season.<p>
Gordon led six times for 189 laps. But Jarrett, who started the season with a victory in the Daytona 500 and has led the points since, began closing on the leader in the final 50 laps, eventually taking the lead. <p>
But on lap 285, Jarrett was pinned behind a slower driver, Dave Marcis. He found himself squeezed into the wall, bouncing off the concrete as he slipped past Marcis. <p>
&quot;All of sudden, boom, opportunity just goes into your mind,&quot; Gordon said. &quot;You see something like that and you think, 'There it is. Now take advantage.' <p>
Gordon did, passing Jarrett on lap 287 and leading the rest of the way. <p>
Bobby Labonte, a three-time winner last season, had his strongest race of the season, finishing second. Labonte, who finished 1.40 seconds behind Gordon, outdueled Ricky Craven for second with four laps left.<p>
Dale Earnhardt was not a factor all day. Earnhardt started 27th, and never cracked the top 10, ultimately finishing a lap down in 14th position.<p>
Sterling Marlin, last year's winner, had a run at the lead, but the disadvantage of pitting on the back stretch at dropped him from second to 13th during a yellow flag pit stop on Lap 203. <p>
Robert Pressley had a strong car, but while leading his ninth lap he blew his engine and crashed hard into the turn three wall. His Chevrolet Monte Carlo caught fire before it finally stopped near the front pit area, outside of turn four. Pressley was not injured. <p>
In all, the caution flag came out 11 times for 56 laps.<p>
Chevrolets held four of the top five spots, as the model captured its fourth race in five events. Dale Jarrett's win at the Daytona 500 was the only win for Ford Thunderbirds.<p><hr>
