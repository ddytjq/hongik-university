	        		<B>YOUR QUESTIONS ANSWERED</B><BR>
					<br><br><b>WHAT RATE OF INTEREST WILL I GET?</B><br>
The rate of interest for the Children’s Savings Bond is guaranteed to be 1% higher than the Bank of England base rate and will track this rate whether it goes up or down. This means that when the Bank of England base rate changes, the interest rate on your Children’s Savings Bond will also change within 30 days.<BR><BR>

Interest rates effective 1 March 2002.<BR><BR> 

The present Bank of England base rate is 4% and assuming that this rate remains the same throughout the term of your Children's Savings Bond, the interest rate will be the rates shown in the box above.<BR><BR>
*	Interest will be paid or credited after deduction of income tax at the lower rate of 20% or, subject to the required certification, gross.<BR><BR>

**	AER stands for Annual Equivalent Rate and illustrates what the interest rate would be if interest was paid and added each year.  The calculation is made on the assumption that the Bank of England base rate will not change throughout the term of your Children’s Savings Bond.<BR><BR>
<UL>
<LI>	Please note: the Bank of England base rate can fluctuate in the future.</LI><BR><BR>

<LI>	Rates correct at time of product being launched - 1 March 2002.</LI>
</UL>
Interest is payable annually and will be credited to your account on 1 May each year.  The Society reserves the right to change the date on which interest is credited to your account.<BR><BR>
If the balance of your Children’s Savings Bond falls below £5, the interest rate payable for the period that the balance remains below £5 will be the Society’s variable base savings rate applicable at the time.

<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>WHO CAN OPEN AN ACCOUNT?</B><BR>
					Anyone under the age of 18 can open a Children’s Savings Bond and operate the account for the full three year term.<br><br>

For accounts operated through Investments Direct, any child under 16 wishing to open a Children’s Savings Bond will need a parent, guardian or representative to open and operate their account for them as trustee(s).  A Trustee must remain in place until the child reaches the age of 16, after which, if required, the account may be transferred into the child’s sole name.<br><br>

Trustees operating the account on behalf of a child are required to choose the order of names of the Trustees on the account.  The Society only recognises the first named Trustee on the account as being eligible to vote.
<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>HOW MUCH CAN I INVEST?</B><BR>
					You can open a Children’s Savings Bond with as little as £5 or as much as £150.<br><br>

You must add a minimum of £5 or up to a maximum of £150 to your account each calendar month after your Children’s Savings Bond has been opened but you do not need to add the same amount each month.<br><br>

The Society may close your Children’s Savings Bond if the balance falls below £5.<br><br>

Only one Children’s Savings Bond can be opened by the trustee for each child and the account can only have one named beneficiary.<br><br>
<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>WHAT ACCESS TO MY MONEY DO I HAVE?</B><BR>
					You will be able to make one withdrawal per annum without notice or penalty.  However, you must maintain at least £5 in the account.<br><br>

You can of course close the account at any time without notice or penalty.<br><br>

A minimum of 11 monthly payments must be made each year e.g. if you miss a payment in June, you will not be able to miss any more payments until the following year in June.  If two or more payments are missed in any one anniversary year, the interest rate will be the variable rate paid on our First Post Account, or such other account which is available at that time from our current range, which the Society considers is similar, until the next payment is received. 
<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>WHAT HAPPENS AFTER THREE YEARS?</B><BR>
					Your Children’s Savings Bond will mature on the third anniversary from the date of opening, at which time we would aim to contact you.<br><br>

Once your account has matured the interest rate you will receive will be a variable interest rate which will be the same as the rate paid on our First Post Account, or such other account that the Society considers is similar and is available at that time.

<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>DO I HAVE TO PAY TAX?</B><BR>
					If you are under 16 and eligible to have your interest paid gross, a parent or guardian will need to complete Inland Revenue Form R85 to avoid tax being taken off your interest.  Alternatively, if you are over 16 and eligible, and operating your own account, you will need to complete the Inland Revenue Form R85 to avoid tax being taken off your interest.
<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>HOW DO I OPEN A CHILDREN'S SAVINGS BOND?</B><BR>
					The initial payment in to your Children’s Savings Bond can only be made by cheque or transfer from an existing Society investment account.  The subsequent regular monthly payments must be made by standing order from a bank or building society account.  You must allow at least four working days for the payment to reach the Society where payments are made from a bank or building society account - please take this into account when asking  your bank to send payments as you must ensure that a payment is received in your Children’s Savings Bond in each calendar month.<br><br>

We will accept any additional payments to your account by cheque but these, when added to your regular monthly payment, must not exceed the £150 per month limit.
<BR><BR>
      			
	        		<B>PROOF OF IDENTITY</B><BR>
					Proof of identity and verification of address are required for all applicants and those named on the Account as beneficiaries or trustees.  Two original documents, not more than three months old are required for each investor, photocopies are not acceptable.  The documents will be returned to you and should be either:<br><br>
<ul>
<li>	Utility bill (e.g. Gas/Electricity)</li>
<LI>	Bank/Building Society Statement</LI>
<LI>	Current Council Tax/Notice of Tax Coding</li>
</ul>
and <B>MUST</b> show the name and address of the investor as it appears on the application form.  Further verification checks will be carried out by the Society.  If you already have an account with the Society bearing your current address, identification is not required.<br><br>

Unless proof of identity is provided, and all appropriate sections of the application form have been completed, including date of birth, we will have to return your investment to you.<br><br>

<B>If the account is to be held in the sole name of a child (age 16 to 18):</b>  One form of identification (such as birth certificate/medical card) and one proof of address (such as a bank or building society savings account statement or a  parent’s original utility bill, not more than three months old) are required.<br><br>

Further details of acceptable identification will be provided by telephoning Investments Direct on 0845 458 4522* between 9.00 a.m. - 5.00 p.m., Monday to Friday.
