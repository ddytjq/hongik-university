                                    19th<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt"><span style="mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">
                                    </span></b>June 2001</font></span>
                                    <p>
                                    <font face="Arial" size="2"><b><font size="4" color="#000080">Independent
                                    Insurance liquidation -<br>
                                    Broker questions and answers<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </font></b></font>
                                    <font face="Arial" size="2"><span lang="EN-GB" style="mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><br>
                                    <br>
                                    </b>
                                    </span>
                                    <font color="#000080" face="Arial"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><b>Q1:<span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Policyholders’ Protection Board
                                    (PPB) – what is the position?<span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><o:p>
                                    </o:p>
                                    </span></b></span></font><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span></span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Existing
                                    Independent policies remain in effect unless
                                    and until cancelled.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Independent is
                                    currently in provisional liquidation and the
                                    provisions of the Policyholders’
                                    Protection Act will not come into effect
                                    unless the company goes into full
                                    liquidation and claims cannot be met from
                                    other sources.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    Statutory covers such as Employers’
                                    Liability insurance may remain in force but
                                    it is likely that it will take some time
                                    before full settlement is achieved.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    <b style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><font color="#000080">Q2:
                                    Will the ABI cover any claims and if so, to
                                    what extent?</font></b> </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    No.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>The
                                    ABI is simply a trade association and has no
                                    regulatory or financial powers or
                                    responsibilities in this case.&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><font color="#000080">Q3:
                                    What is the situation regarding long term
                                    policies – eg: three years?</font></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><br>
                                    A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    As with annual policies it is unlikely that
                                    any claims arising before 18 June 2001 will
                                    be settled in full, or that valid cover will
                                    continue after this date.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Cover should therefore be replaced
                                    immediately with a new insurer.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span>The
                                    position on premiums remains unclear: in
                                    effect most premiums on longer-term policies
                                    are paid in annual instalments and are
                                    therefore still due for payment to the
                                    insurer.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>It is possible that the liquidators
                                    may issue notice of cancellation on
                                    Independent policies, including long-term
                                    contracts. In such circumstances the
                                    insured’s responsibility to pay the
                                    remaining premium instalments may cease.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><font color="#000080">Q4:
                                    What happens if a client’s house burns
                                    down tonight? Will they be covered?</font></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    If cover has not been placed with a new
                                    insurer, the client will be treated as an
                                    unsecured creditor in the event of a claim
                                    against Independent.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>It appears unlikely that any such
                                    claim will be settled in full by the
                                    insurer.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Furthermore, there will be
                                    significant delays.<span style="mso-spacerun:
yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></span><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><font color="#000080"><b>Q5:
                                    Can brokers leave Employers’ Liability in
                                    place and trust that it will be dealt with
                                    by the PPB?</b></font></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    No.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp; </span>See
                                    answer to Q1 above.&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span><span lang="EN-GB"><font face="Arial" color="#000080"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><br>
                                    <b>Q6: Where insurance has been placed
                                    elsewhere, will brokers have to advise
                                    Independent?</b></span></font></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    In order to avoid the possibility of ‘dual
                                    insurance’, Independent should be notified
                                    of cancellation when new cover is confirmed
                                    by a new insurer.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>The new insurer should be advised
                                    that the cover is being arranged to replace
                                    an Independent policy.&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span><font color="#000080"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><br>
                                    <span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">Q7.
                                    Personal lines – should brokers advise
                                    clients that there is no cover under
                                    policies or wait a couple of days to see if
                                    someone will take over personal lines as
                                    rumoured?</b></span></span></font><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><br>
                                    </b></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    Brokers should recommend immediate
                                    replacement of cover.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span><font color="#000080"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal" lang="EN-GB"><br>
                                    <b style="mso-bidi-font-weight: normal; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-weight: normal">Q8:&nbsp;
                                    With regard to direct debits, what are the
                                    implications if clients stop paying?&nbsp;&nbsp;</span></b></span></font><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><br>
                                    </b></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    Clients remain legally bound to pay
                                    instalments under credit arrangements with
                                    Independent, or through any bona fide
                                    premium financing agreement.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Any suspension or cancellation of
                                    such payments may result in an approach from
                                    the liquidators seeking settlement in full
                                    for the full premium.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><font color="#000080">Q9.
                                    What is the definitive definition of a
                                    private policyholder and a commercial
                                    policyholder in terms of the
                                    Policyholders’ Protection Act?</font></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    The PPA refers to: ‘Corporate bodies’
                                    and to ‘an individual or a partnership or
                                    other unincorporated body of persons all of
                                    whom are individuals.’<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    </span></span><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><font color="#000080">Q10:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes">&nbsp;
                                    </span>Is the Independent helpline still
                                    open?</font></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; color: blue"><br>
                                    </span><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">A:</span></b><span lang="EN-GB" style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman">
                                    Yes, as of 19 June 2001 the liquidators have
                                    announced that the helpline 0161 741 1010
                                    has been re-opened and is being manned by 50
                                    staff from Independent.<br>
                                    <br>
                                    <br>
                                    <b style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><span style="font-size: 11.0pt; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman"><font color="#000080"><span>Q11.&nbsp;
                                    My client’s Terrorism cover is placed with
