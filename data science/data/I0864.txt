          <td valign="top" width="100%" colspan="3" bgcolor="#F0F0F0"><b class="newsheading1">MINI 
            REPORT</b></td>
        </tr>
        <tr>
          <td valign="top" width="100%" colspan="3" class="engfanz"> 
            <p>England are through to the quarter-finals of the World Cup after 
              beating Denmark 3-0 in Niigata. An incredible first half of football, 
              Denmark having 61% of the possession but England leading 3-0. England 
              got of to the perfect start with Rio Ferdinand getting his head 
              to a David Beckham header that was fluffed into the net by Danish 
              keeper Sorensen. It got even better on 22 minutes Michael Owen turning 
              on to a Nicky Butt flick to tuck the ball into the corner of the 
              net. Denmark had most of the first half play after that and Sand 
              missed a great chance to pull one back. Then just before half-time 
              England broke and Beckham found Emile Heskey outside the box and 
              his well struck shot skidded under Sorensen. Owen was replaced by 
              Robbie Fowler at half-time with a worrying groing strain. The second 
              half was a non event with Denmark having possession but creating 
              little and the English defence looking very solid again.</p>
