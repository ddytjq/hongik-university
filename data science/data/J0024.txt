<h1>Quick pit vaults Earnhardt to Purolator win</h1>

ATLANTA -- Speed on the track and in the pits vaulted Dale Earnhardt past Terry Labonte, giving him a win in Sunday's NASCAR Purolator 500 at Atlanta Motor Speedway. 
<p>
With the victory, the 70th of his career, Earnhardt passes stock car racing legend Cale Yarborough for the most career wins at the speedway with eight. 
<p>
Labonte, Jeff Gordon, Ernie Irvan and Jeremy Mayfield rounded out the top five finishers. Ken Schrader, a Hendrick Motorsports teammate of Labonte and Gordon, was the final car on the lead lap in sixth position. 
<p>
The victory puts Earnhardt back on the path for what would be his record-shattering eighth career Winston Cup championship. Earnhardt, the first two-time winner of the 1996 season, is currently tied with Richard Petty, the all-time winningest NASCAR driver, with seven career championships.
<p> 
Earnhardt began the day in the 18th position but within six laps had emerged in the top five. He fought his way through the other top contenders and sat on Labonte's rear bumper until seizing an opportunity for the lead on the 49th lap. 
<p>
But just five laps later, Earnhardt was limping into the pits after his Chevrolet Monte Carlo ran out of gas.  The early incident surprisingly didn't cost Earnhardt, as the rest of the field also was forced to pit. 
<p>
With only three caution flags all day, pit crew efficiency played a tremendous role in Earnhardt's victory. His final pit stop was three seconds faster than Labonte, giving Earnhardt the lead and what turned out to be an ample margin over his top challenger. 
<p>
&quot;That last stop was a great stop,&quot; said Earnhardt, rebounding from a 31st-place finish last week. &quot;We stomped 'em in the pits. This is a good day for us.&quot; 
<p>
Labonte was trying to become the first driver to sweep a weekend's events in Atlanta after winning the Busch Grand National series Busch Light 300 on Saturday.
<p> 
&quot;We had a great car today,&quot; said Labonte, whose Chevrolet Monte Carlo was among the three cars impounded for wind tunnel testing by NASCAR. Labonte is the only driver to lead at least one lap in all Winston Cup events this season. 
<p>
Gordon said that his crew chief Ray Evernham made modifications to the car Sunday which dramatically improved performance on his Chevrolet. 
<p>
&quot;The three car (Earnhardt) was consistent, and my teammate Terry was tough today,&quot; said Gordon, the defending event and Winston Cup champion. 
<p>
Irvan had his best race since returning from a life-threatening wreck at Michigan in August 1994. Irvan started in the 37th position and moved up to lead the race before finishing fourth.
<p> 
Mayfield's fifth-place finish was the career best of his 52 Winston Cup races. His only previous top 10 finish was an eighth at Pocono last year. 
<p>
&quot;Everything went good for us today,&quot; said Yarborough, the owner of Mayfield's team. &quot;I think it's a sign of things to come.&quot; 
<p>
Only three caution flags interrupted Sunday's race, none caused by wrecks. 
<p>
Dale Jarrett will retain his lead in the Winston Cup points battle, holding an unofficial margin of 50 points over Earnhardt. Jarrett's 11th place finish was his only finish outside of the top two this season. 
<p>
Ricky Rudd, Bill Elliott and Ricky Craven will round out the top five in points. Jeff Gordon will move into 16th and Jeff Burton, who failed to qualify for the race, will drop from second to 14th. 
<p>
This was Chevy's 20th victory in 49 events at Atlanta Motor Speedway since 1972, by far the best of any manufacturer. 
<p>
The race was a 500-mile event of 328 laps around the 1.522-mile oval at the Hampton, Georgia facility. 
<p>
The next NASCAR Winston Cup event is the TranSouth Financial 400 at Darlington, South Carolina on March 24.
