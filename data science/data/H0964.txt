<strong><font size="+3">Systematic Identification of Optimal HIV-1 CTL Epitopes</font></strong></DIV>
<hr size=2>
<br>Christian Brander<strong><sup>1</sup></strong> and Bruce D. Walker<strong><sup>2</sup></strong>
<br>
<br><strong><sup>1</sup></strong>AIDS Research Center, Massachusetts General Hospital, 149 13th Street, Room 5234,
<br><strong><sup>2</sup></strong>Charlestown,   MA  02129, USA, and Harvard Medical School Boston,  MA  USA
<p>
<strong> Updated October 1997</strong>
<p>
Characterization of  the cellular immune response against  HIV has led to
the  identification of numerous HLA class I restricted  CTL epitopes and
many laboratories have contributed to the description of CTL epitopes in
most of the HIV-1 proteins. Since the last update of this CTL epitope list
in 1996, a number of new epitopes have been published and are included in
the current update. References  have also been updated for epitopes previously listed as personal communications which have since been published.
<p>
For this update, we  maintained the same  rigid criteria for inclusion in
the list of optimal epitopes as in previous years. First, the peptide has
to be involved in the natural immune response against HIV. Thus, only
epitopes that have been shown to induce a specific CTL response upon HIV
infection <i> in vivo</i> are included. Second, the peptides have to be truncated to the size of the optimal epitope, which is the shortest epitope that is recognized at  the lowest peptide concentration; and third, the HLA class I
restricting molecule has to be identified. We therefore excluded epitopes
from the list of optimal epitopes if only the binding to a certain class I
molecule has been shown but not the immunogenicity <i> in vivo</i>,  or if  no
peptide titration assay  with truncated peptides has been reported. However, those epitopes are included in the extended CTL epitope list in Part-I of this
database.  Nine newly listed optimal epitopes are included this year, bringing the total number to 110. These epitopes add to the previously identified pattern of CTL epitopes being widely distributed over the expressed HIV proteins.
<P>
In order to keep this list updated it would be very helpful for us to
receive information about newly identified epitopes as they become
available. For comments and/or contributions to this CTL epitope list (C.B. or
B.W.) or the main database (B.K.) please call, write, or mail us at:
