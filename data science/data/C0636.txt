                            <IMG SRC="../images/about/red_arrow_w.gif" WIDTH="9" HEIGHT="9" BORDER="0" ALT="" HSPACE="3"><B CLASS="subhead">
                            
                                Friends Provident announces new life and pensions business for the first quarter 2002 and the proposed acquisition by Friends Ivory and Sime of Royal  SunAlliance Investments
                              
                            </B>
                          <P ALIGN="right">30 Apr, 2002</P>
                        </SPAN>
                    
                        <SPAN CLASS="bodyText">
                            
                                <p><b>New Business<br></b></p><ul><li><p>Total new life and pensions business premiums (regular plus single premiums) increased by 26.7% to £425.5 million for the first quarter 2002 (£335.9m in first quarter 2001). On an Annual Premium Equivalent (APE*) basis new life and pensions business for the first quarter 2002 increased by 10.2% to £83.5 million (£75.8 m in first quarter 2001).</p></li></ul><ul><li><p>New life APE, including income protection, increased by 7.7% to £29.4 million (£27.3m in first quarter 2001) buoyed by a significant increase of 54.2% in single premium business to £164.7 million (£106.8m in first quarter 2001).</p></li></ul><ul><li><p>New pensions APE increased by 11.5% to £54.1 million (£48.5m in first quarter 2001) including an increase in new group pensions business of 11.9% to £31.9 million (£28.5m in first quarter 2001).<br><br><i>* The Annual Premium Equivalent (APE) basis is new regular premiums plus 10% of single premiums</i></p></li></ul><p><b>Proposed Acquisition<br><br></b> The Board of Friends Ivory &amp; Sime plc (FIS), Friends Providents 67% owned asset management subsidiary, announced today that it has entered into a conditional agreement to acquire Royal &amp; SunAlliance Investments (R&amp;SA Investments) for £240 million. As part of the transaction, FIS and Royal &amp; SunAlliance Insurance Group (R&amp;SA Group) have also agreed terms under which FIS will become investment manager, under an exclusive 10 year contract, of the UK life insurance and general insurance funds of R&amp;SA Group. The consideration will be funded from FISs internal cash resources and a loan from Friends Provident of £190 million on normal commercial terms.</p><p>The transaction is in line with Friends Providents stated objective to grow the groups asset management business both organically and by acquisition. R&amp;SA Investments had approximately £36 billion of funds under management at 31 March 2002 of which 85% represented life insurance and general insurance funds. As a result, funds under management will roughly double to £70 billion (as at 31 December 2001) positioning FIS as one of the top 10 UK active asset managers. The net assets of R&amp;SA Investments as at 31 December 2001 were £31.7 million. Profits before tax, and one-off items of £3.7 million, were £14.0 million for the year ended 31 December 2001. The financial effects on Friends Provident plc will be positive but not material. However, the transaction is anticipated to better position the Group to develop its presence in the retail investment market.</p><p>Shareholders are referred to the announcement issued by the Board of FIS earlier today containing further details of the transaction and its effect on FIS.</p><p>Friends Providents Group Chief Executive, Keith Satchell, said:</p><blockquote><p>"With a 27% increase in total new business premiums (up 10% on an Annual Premium Equivalent basis) for the first quarter 2002 over the same period in 2001 we have made a good start to the year against our expectation that industry sales will grow more slowly this year. We made strong progress in the life single premium market but growth overall was affected by a slow down in the number of new group pension schemes sold in the first quarter. However, I believe that we are well placed to build our group pensions sales over the year as we take advantage of the increasing trend in favour of defined contribution pension schemes. I am also confident that the recent improvements to our protection products and the promotional campaign on our Investment Portfolio Bond during the second quarter will contribute to Friends Provident making good progress over the year.</p><p>The announcement made today by Friends Ivory &amp; Sime plc of the proposed acquisition of R&amp;SA Investments advances the Groups strategic commitment to build its asset management operation. The enlarged asset management business will have double the funds under management and so will become one of the top 10 UK active asset managers."</p></blockquote><p align="center"><b>- Ends -</b></p><p>For further information contact:</p><p>Friends Provident Brian Wilkinson 01306 653405</p><p>Di Skidmore 01306 654483</p><p>Jim Murdoch 01722 311447</p><p>Bell Pottinger John Coles 0207 861 3868</p>
