              <p><img border="0" src="../images/howbegan.gif" width="180" height="28"><BR><font face="Verdana,arial" size="2">Canoes
              and Kayaks have been used for transportation and hunting for many
              hundreds of years. Canoes where originally built from materials
              such as wood that was covered in bark and whalebone, and then animal skins. It really depended on what region of the
              world you where from to what material your canoe was built from.
</p>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/wiecom.gif" width="260" height="28"><BR>It
              entered the world as a competitive sport in 1886 when John
              MacGregor invented the sport and also founded the Canoe Club at
              Richmond, Surrey, in England in the year of 1886.<BR>In 1924 the sports governing body (The International
              Canoe Federation) was set
              up which is still going today in its location in Budapest, Hungary.
</p>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/wietog.gif" width="310" height="28"><BR> It
              first appeared in the Olympic Games in 1924 in Paris as a
              demonstration sport ( which is not really an official part)&nbsp;
              but since then it has been a very popular event.<BR>It appeared as an official sport in 1936 in the Olympics in Berlin
              where it gained medal status and has done ever since.</p>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/areas.gif" width="380" height="28"><BR>The
              areas of Slalom and wild water were developed in the 1930's. Soon
              after that, Slalom and Wild water&nbsp; entered the Olympics as
              medal winning sports.<BR>Rodeo is when you have to perform as many
              tricks as possible in a given amount of time.<BR>Slalom is when you have to go down a stretch of water, going through gates as you do the Slalom. If you miss a gate you will lose points.<BR>Sea Kayaking is when you kayak in the sea instead of water inland.<BR>Sprint is when you sprint in a canoe, down a straight bit
              of water for distances of 500m or 1000m in terms or individuals races.</p>
