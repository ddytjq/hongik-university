    <b><u><font SIZE="2"><p ALIGN="CENTER"></font><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">-EXPLANATORY

    <p><font face="Arial">The new tax free ISA was available from 6<sup>th</sup> April 1999.
    There are no new PEPS and TESSA's from 6<sup>th</sup> April 1999. ISA's give anyone over
    16 years a new opportunity to protect their savings from tax.</font></b></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">The Holmesdale is offering both a &quot;Mini Cash ISA&quot; and a
    &quot;Tessa ISA&quot;. The Society is not offering a Maxi ISA, which includes Insurance
    and Stocks and Shares. </font></font><font face="Arial" color="#000000"><small>The Mini
    Cash ISA is available to existing members or Surrey residents only. The Tessa ISA is
    available to existing Holmesdale Tessa Account Holders.</small></font><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><a href="isainfo.htm#How much can I save?">How much can I save?</a><br>

    <b><p></font><a name="How much can I save?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">How
    much can I save?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">There is an annual allowance on the Mini Cash ISA of £3,000 up to
    the year ended 5th April 2006.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">You may only subscribe to one mini cash ISA a year, but you can
    subscribe up to £3,000 in a mini stocks and shares ISA and £1,000 in a mini insurance
    ISA with another provider. By subscribing to this ISA, you may not subscribe to a maxi ISA
    in the same year. (A maxi ISA includes a stocks and shares component and may also include
    cash and insurance components. There is a maximum overall subscription limit of £7,000 in
    a maxi ISA in the years ending 5th April 2000/2006). However, you can also subscribe up to
    the amount deposited in a matured TESSA in a mini cash ISA or a TESSA-only ISA without it
    counting towards the annual ISA subscription limits. You have up to 6 months from the date
    the TESSA matures to do so.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">With the TESSA ISA you will be able to reinvest the whole capital
    sum (but not the interest) on top of your annual ISA allowance. The interest could
    however, be used for a Mini Cash ISA if your limit has not been exceeded. A Tessa maturity
    certificate must be available at the time the account is opened. Subscriptions to a
    TESSA-Only ISA do not count towards the ISA annual subscription limits. You may have a
    TESSA-Only ISA with the Society even if you already, or might wish to subscribe to a Mini
    or Maxi ISA with another provider.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the

    <p><font face="Arial">You can only add to a Tessa ISA up to the amount of capital in your
    matured Tessa. However, once a Mini Cash ISA is opened you can add the capital of a
    matured Tessa.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the

    <b><p></font><a name="What about tax?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">What
    about tax?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Everything you earn in an ISA is completely free of UK Income Tax
    and Capital Gains Tax. Also, you do not lose any tax exemption if you make withdrawals of
    capital, as you do with a Tessa.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <b><p></font><a name="Can I have a joint account?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">Can I have a joint account?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Only accounts in your sole name are allowed.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <b><p></font><a name="Do you have to buy from the same provider each year?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">Do you have to buy from the same provider each year?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">You do not have to.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <p></font><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2"><a name="Do you have to keep you ISA for a set period of time?"><strong>Do you have to keep
    your ISA for a set period of time?</strong></a></font><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">No, they are not term investments and there is no minimum or maximum
    qualifying period.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <b><p></font><a name="Can I get my money out easily if I need to?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">Can I get my money out easily if I need to?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Yes, you will be able to take money from an ISA at any time without
    losing any tax relief. However, the ISA does need 60 days written notice of withdrawal or
    60 days loss of interest on the amount withdrawn.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <b><p></font><a name="If I take money out, can I put it back in again?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">If I take money out, can I put it back in again?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Not in the same tax year, because the allowance applies to the sum
    total of your deposits. For example, you invest £2900 into a Mini Cash ISA when your
    allowance is £3000 you then take out £300. You could not put back that £300, but you
    would be able to put in a further £100 to bring the total deposited in one tax year up to
    £3000.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">If the account falls below £500 it will be closed.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the
    top of the page.</font></strong></p>
    <b><p></font><a name="How long can you go on saving in ISA's?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">How long can you go on saving in ISA's?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">The Government has guaranteed that they will run for at least 10
    years.</font></p>
    <p><strong><font face="Arial">Click <a href="isainfo.htm#Top">here</a> to go back to the

    <b><p></font><a name="What are CAT Standards?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">What
    are CAT Standards?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">These are voluntary standards on fair CHARGES, easy ACCESS and
    decent TERMS.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">These are:-</font></p>
    <b><p><font face="Arial"><font color="#0000FF">Charges</b>:</font> No Charges</font></p>
    <b><p><font face="Arial"><font color="#0000FF">Access:</font></b> Minimum transaction size
    no greater than £10. Withdrawals with 7 working days or less.</font></p>
    <b><p><font face="Arial"><font color="#0000FF">Terms:</font></b> Interest rate no lower
    than 2% below base rate.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Rate increase within one month of base rate increase.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">No special conditions e.g. limit on number of withdrawals.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">As our minimum investment will be £500, we shall be prevented from
    offering CAT standards, but we will be offering competitive products.</font></p>

    <b><p></font><a name="Who can subscribe to an ISA?"><font face="Arial" color="#0000FF" SIZE="2">Who can subscribe to an ISA?</font></a></b><font SIZE="2"></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Anyone who is 16 years or over.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">You must not have subscribed to another ISA of the same components
    type in the same tax year.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Anyone who is resident or ordinarily resident in the UK.</font></p>
    <p><font face="Arial">Withdrawals at sixty days written notice with full interest or on
    demand with 60 days loss of interest on the amount withdrawn.</font></p>
