              <p><img border="0" src="../images/howbegan.gif" width="180" height="28"><BR><font face="Verdana,arial" size="2">Surfing
              began many hundreds of years ago. Captain James Cook came across
              the people of the Hawaiian Islands surfing in 1778. The boards
              then were made out of materials such as long hardwood slabs
              weighing 68kg or more.<BR>So only the really fit could do it at the time.
</p>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/cametoe.gif" width="210" height="28"><BR>Captain
              James Cook brought it back to Europe. But it did not become a
              popular sport until in the 1930's when the development of lighter
              surfboards became available with materials such as
              plywood and balsa being used with fins for steering, instead of having solid
              wooden boards with no fins on for steering.<BR>It became even more popular in the 1960's when an even lighter surfboard hit the market.
              It was made out of such new materials as Styrofoam and fiberglass.
</p>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/eventsport.gif" width="400" height="28"><BR> The
              following events notable in the organization of surfing are:-</p>
              <ol>
                <li>The founding of the Outrigger Canoe Club in Waikiki, Hawaii
                  in 1907.</li>
                <li>The Introduction of surfing at Redondo Beach, California in
                  1908.</li>
                <li>The American Surfing association was founded in 1976.</li>
              </ol>
              <p><img border="0" src="../images/sbtoday.gif" width="200" height="28"><BR>The surfboard of today measures 2.7 to 3.0 meters in
              length, 56 to 58 cm in width and 7.6 to 10.2 cm in thickness. Weighing no
              more than 11.3kg, this particular board is a great hit with the teenagers of today.</p>
