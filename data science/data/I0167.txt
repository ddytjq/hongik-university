 Engine Ver 0.31</H4><H1 class="entry">Arsenal FC</H1><H2 class="entry">Highbury</H2><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">The Ground</H3><table class="entry" cellspacing=0><TR><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Ground2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Ground2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Highbury" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Highbury</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Ground5-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Ground5-Thumb.jpg" ALT="Away Turnstiles" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>Away Turnstiles</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Ground7-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Ground7-Thumb.jpg" ALT="South Stand" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>South Stand</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD class="entry"><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Ground9-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Ground9-Thumb.jpg" ALT="East Stand 
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry"><B>East Stand 
</B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER" class="entry">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD><TD></TD></TR></TABLE><p><P>Highbury is a very impressive, but very old and compact ground. The front rows of seats are extremely close 
to the pitch. Away fans are located in the single tiered South Stand (Clock End) overlooked by the only
corporate boxes in the ground. Only the rear of the stand is covered allowing
people in the first dozen rows to get soaked when it rains. 
</P><P>The facilities at the ground
are somewhat limited for a Premiership ground with only one kiosk serving beer and two more serving food in the 
whole of the South Stand. Pies are only available at the beer kiosk (not the food kiosks surprisingly)
which means that getting a pie at half time is impossible. It is also worth noting that on our last
visit the bar wasn't open at half time. There is also a lack of toilet facilities for the number 
of fans that the South Stand can accommodate. The ground plays host to a branch of Ladbrokes and
also has two JumboTron screens, allowing away fans to view the pre-match entertainment.
</P><P>Whilst flags and banners are normally allowed into the ground there is normally nowhere to display them.
</P></P><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://uk.multimap.com/p/browse.cgi?pc=N51BU&Title=Highbury+(Arsenal+FC)&scale=25000&cat=loc" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of the surrounding area</P></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Ticket Info</H3><P><B>Fans are reminded that tickets for visiting stands should be purchased through their own club, not the club they are visiting.</B></P><p></P></p><p>Adult ticket prices for visiting fans are £24
</P></p><p>The ticket office can be contacted on 020 74133366 or <a class="entry" href="mailto:boxoffice@arsenal.co.uk">email</a>. For ticket information 
from the official website click <a class="entry" href="http://www.arsenal.com/sportsappcontroller.jsp?command=forwardOnly&nextPage=boxoffice" target="_new">here</a>
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width="100%"><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Disabled</H3><p>There are 10 spaces for visitng wheelchair fans in the corner of the South Stand. There are also 10 spaces available for away fans for the visually impaired.
There away area has one toilet for the disabled. 
</P><P>For more information contact the club on 020 7704 4044.
</P></p></td></tr></table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Pubs</H3><p><P>Most pubs close to the ground are home only, so drinking away from the ground 
is recommended, with the exception of the Drayton Park which is next to the away coach parking and
gets full with visiting fans.
</P></p><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><H5 class="entry">The Drayton Park
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Pub2-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Pub2-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Drayton Park
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Drayton Park
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Drayton Park is situated on Drayton Park Road,
approximately 5 minutes walk from Highbury and next to the away coach parking. Due to its proximatity to the away coach parking
it gets full with away fans and is now the designated away pub. Surprisingly for a busy away pub close to 
the ground plastic glasses are not used and both the pub and police seem to be happy with fans standing outside.
There are two Burger Vans situated right outside the pub and also an Off Licence next door which tends to attract away fans outside as well.
There is sky TV but unfortunately no real ale.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Marquess Tavern
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><p>One of the very few away friendly pubs around the ground. Its located
about 15 minutes walk away on Canonbury Street. Malcolm Smythe sent us the following about it
"....Peter and Norma Footman (mine host at the Marquess) pride themselves
on the food that is served through lunchtime to 9pm(ish) ...."
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Worlds End
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR></TR></TABLE><p>Located on Stroud Green Rd its quite a walk to the ground, but at least away
fans are welcome. Expect to be out numbered by the locals.
</P></p><H5 class="entry">The Highbury Barn
</H5><P ALIGN=LEFT></P><TABLE><TR><TD><P><TABLE><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><A HREF="/cgi-bin/ag.pl?club=disppic&pfname=/images/Arsenal-Pub1-Full.jpg" TARGET="_new"><IMG SRC="/images/Arsenal-Pub1-Thumb.jpg" ALT="The Highbury Barn
" WIDTH="100" HEIGHT="75" BORDER="0" ALIGN="TEXTTOP"></A></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER"><B>The Highbury Barn
<B></TD></TR><TR><TD ALIGN="CENTER">Click for bigger picture</TD></TR></TABLE></P></TD></TR></TABLE><p>The Highbury Barn is a very large pub (as the name suggests) popular with Arsenal fans. While away
fans are welcomed expect to be asked to cover up your colours and be well out numbered.
</P><P>Malcolm Smythe offered the following directions (to replace our incorrect
ones). "... Highbury
Barn couldn't be any closer to Arsenal's ground. Cross Highbury
Park where the pub is situated, down the hill to Aubert Park on the left and
within two minutes the glory of Highbury Stadium (and the Marble Halls) hove
into view!..."
</P><P>Another Arsenal fan previously sent us the following
" ... Very much a home fans pub but away fans are welcomed. Heavy police
presence, but the atmosphere is friendly enough...."
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Food</H3><p><P>There is quite a choice of burger vans around the ground. A short walk away on Holloway Road there
are numerous cafes and takeaways to cater for all tastes.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Cash Machines</H3><p><P>The nearest cash machines are at Barclays and, Lloyds TSB on Seven Sisters
Road, about half a mile from the ground. 
</P></p><p>Click <a class="entry" href="http://link.infonow.net/bin/findNow?CLIENT_ID=LINK_ATM" target="_new">here</A> to go to <a class="entry" HREF="http://www.link.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">LINKs</a> locate a cash machine page. The local postcode is N51BU</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Bookmakers</H3><p>A branch of Ladbrokes can be found in the ground.
</P></p><p>Online betting is always available through <a class="entry" 
href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">Ladbrokes</a> by
clicking <a class="entry" href="https://www.ladbrokes.co.uk/bet?AFF_ID=121"  TARGET="_NEW">here</a>.</P></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Directions</H3><p>Head towards Central London on the A1, at the Archway it becomes Holloway Rd. 
A mile further on you pass Holloway Rd tube station, then take the third
left into Drayton Rd. After just less than a mile turn right into Aubert
Park and then take the third left into Avenell Road.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Parking</H3><p>Your best bet is to park on the street (which can be limited) or park away 
from the ground get the tube in. 
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Train</H3><p>Both Drayton Park and Finsbury Park stations are within walking distance of the
ground. Arsenal also has its own tube station.
</P></p><p>The nearest tube is Arsenal
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://www.londontransport.co.uk/tube/mau_home.shtml" target="_new">here</a> to see a map of The Underground</P><p>The nearest station is Finsbury Park
. Click <a class="entry" href="http://212.87.65.227/bin/newquery.exe/en" target="_new">here</a> to check train times</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Accommodation</H3><p>There is little in the way of accommodation near to the ground but there
is plenty of choice in the centre of London.
</P></p><p>Click 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk/london-north.html"  TARGET="_NEW">
here</a> to see the local accomodation available from 
<a class="entry" href="http://www.smoothhound.co.uk"  TARGET="_NEW">  Smoothhound</a>.</p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Chants</H3><p>No matter what the standard of the football, "Boring, Boring Arsenal" is
still 
common with away fans. Of course "One Nil to the Arsenal" is synonymous
with the club.
On our last visit to Highbury the home fans were unbelievably quiet in the ground despite winning. The only 
time that they made any noise worth noting was when singing Veira to the tune of Volari when he was warming up.
</P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">After The Game</H3><p>London has huge range of nightlife. Famous nightclubs from The Ministry of
Sound, Home and Heaven to Stringfellows, The Hippodrome and The
Rock Garden. Comedy venues including The Comedy Store and Jongleurs and
live much venues like The Royal Albert Hall, Camden Palace and The Brixton
Academy.
</P><P>London plays host to some of the worlds most famous theatre productions
(and theatres), shows include Cats, Chicago, Les Miserables, The Lion
King, Mamma Mia, Mousetrap, Phantom of the Opera, Starlight Express, Art,
Buddy and The Rocky Horror Show
</P><P>As you would expect London also has a huge choice of restaurants.
</P><P>For a good place to start planning your night out in London try <a class="entry" href="http://www.londonnet.co.uk/ln/guide/index.html">here</a>.
</P><P></P></p></td></tr><table><BR><table class="entry" cellspacing=0 width=100%><tr><td><H3 class="entry">Local Attractions</H3><p>The choice of things to do in London is endless with the best shops in the
country, many historic buildings and landmarks, lots of museums and
plenty of other tourist attractions to keep everyone happy.
</P><P><I>Shopping</I>
</P><P>There are several shopping areas in London which cater to all tastes. The
main shopping area is down Oxford Street/Regent Street/Bond Street
which has all the usual high street chains and also some large
department stores, one of which is Selfridges.  The famous Carnaby Street
is just off this area towards Soho. 
</P><P>The other famous department store in London, Harrods, is in the
Knightsbridge area. There are also several other designer shops in this
area.
</P><P>The more adventurous may fancy a trip out to Camden Market to look for a
bargain or something different at London's most famous market. The other
market you may wish to visit is Covent Garden which is associated with the
Arts and antiques. Covent Garden also has lots of boutiques, street cafes
and street entertainers.
</P><P><I>Historic Landmarks</I>
</P><P>London has many famous historic landmarks. These include Big Ben &amp;
the Houses of Parliament, Buckingham Palace, Tower of London, Tower
Bridge, Westminster Abbey, St Pauls Cathedral, The Cutty Sark, The Globe
Theatre and Marble Arch. Many of these are open to the public.
</P><P><I>Modern Landmarks</I>
</P><P>Modern London Landmarks include The London Eye (Millennium Wheel), The
Millennium Dome, Canary Wharf and The Telecom Tower.
</P><P><I>Museums and Other Attractions</I>
</P><P>Other attractions include the Waxworks at Madame Tussards and the London
Dungeons. Museums include The Tate Gallery, The Natural History Museum,
The Imperial War Museum and The Victoria &amp; Albert Museum. 
</P><P>There are several parks including Hyde Park and Regents Park. There is a
zoo in Regents Park.
</P><P>There is also the giant arcade Sega World at Piccadilly Circus.
</P><P>For further details of what to do in London click <a class="entry" href="http://www.londonnet.co.uk/ln/guide/index.html">here</a>.
</P></p><P>You can also use the following to search for events in the area</p><BR><CENTER>
