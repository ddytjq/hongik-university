        <UL>
          <LI><a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/publicat/twistedladder/"><b>The 
            New Genetics™: Medicine and the Human Genome</b></a> is a multimedia 
            CD-ROM for those interested in the impact of genetics and genomics 
            on healthcare and society. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.bscs.org/cp_hs.html"><b>BSCS Downloadable Curriculum 
            Modules</b></a> for high school teachers and students 
            <p> 
            <ul>
              <li> <b>Genes, Environment, and Human Behavior</b> (2000)<br>
                This module includes five student activities and extensive background 
                information on the methods and assumptions of behavioral genetics. 
                Students are introduced to the complex interactions of genetic, 
                developmental, and environmental factors and their influence on 
                human behavior. An important goal is to develop student appreciation 
                of the potential impact of emerging knowledge on public policy. 
                Available free in <a href="http://www.bscs.org/pdf/projects/HGN4/HGN-IV.pdf">PDF</a> 
                or for $5 from BSCS [719/531-5550, Fax: -9104, <a href="mailto:info@bscs.org">info@bscs.org</a>]. 
                <p> 
              <li> <b>Human Genetic Variation</b> (1999)<br>
                Students will discover how greater understanding of human genetic 
                variation is leading to more specific and effective medical treatments. 
                Five inquiry-based activities involve directly in building their 
                knowledge of genetic variation and in analyzing ethical, legal, 
                and social issues related to genomic data. Available free from 
                the <a href="http://science-education.nih.gov/customers.nsf/highschool.htm">NIH Office of 
                Science Education</a> . 
                <p> 
              <li><b>The Puzzle of Inheritance: Genetics and the Methods of Science</b> 
                (1997)<br>
                The six activities in this module focus on nontraditional concepts 
                of inheritance to demonstrate the nature and methods of science. 
                The module provides teachers with extensive background information, 
                associated ethical considerations, and a wide range of genetic 
                topics. Available free in <a href="http://www.bscs.org/pdf/projects/HGN3/HGN-III.pdf">PDF</a> 
                or for $5 from BSCS [719/531-5550, Fax: -9104, <a href="mailto:info@bscs.org">info@bscs.org</a>]. 
                <p> 
              <li><b>Mapping and Sequencing the Human Genome: Science, Ethics, 
                and Public Policy</b> (1997) <br>
                This curriculum supplement describes the history, organization, 
                and funding of the HGP and is designed for approximately one week 
                of classroom instruction. Teacher background materials include 
                sections about the science and technology used in the HGP as well 
                as the project's ethical and public policy dimensions. The supplement 
                includes four classroom activities that give students experience 
                working with a database of DNA sequences, analyzing the genetic 
                contribution to human traits, assessing who should have access 
                to genetic data, and using knowledge about our genetics to establish 
                public policy. Available free in <a href="http://www.bscs.org/cp_hs_mod_mhg.html">PDF</a> 
                .[719/531-5550, Fax: -9104, <a href="mailto:info@bscs.org">info@bscs.org</a>]. 
                <p> 
              <li><b>The Human Genome Project: Biology, Computers, and Privacy</b> 
                (1996)<br>
                In the seven activities that constitute this module, students 
                learn the structures, uses, limitations, and implications of genomic 
                databases; both print materials and software are included. Teachers 
                are provided with background information on the construction and 
                use of genetic databases and the possible misuse of generalized 
                and personal data in areas such as healthcare, employment, and 
                insurance. Available for free in <a href="http://www.bscs.org/pdf/projects/HGN2/HGN-II.pdf">PDF</a> 
                or for $5 from BSCS [719/531-5550, Fax: -9104, <a href="mailto:info@bscs.org">info@bscs.org</a>]. 
                <br>
                <br>
            </ul>

            and applications in healthcare, agriculture, the environment, and 
            industry, written for 7th to 10th grade students.

              Destiny; and The Obesity Gene. A teacher's guide is also downloadable. 
              [Classroom sets of this and other issues can be ordered. Contact 
              800/796-5806, Fax: 814/238-4081 or order from the <a href="http://www.bio.org/library/yourworld/ywow.htm">Website</a>.] 
            <p>

            <P><a href="http://www.nslc.wustl.edu/science_outreach/curriculum/genetics/index.html"><b>Modern 
              Genetics for All Students</b></a>--developed by Washington University 
              Science Outreach, this is a free, downloadable curriculum for teachers 
              as part of an enhancement project aimed at increasing literacy in 
              human genetics and molecular biology among high school students 
              at all learning levels. 
            <P> 
          <LI> <a href="http://www.nhgri.nih.gov/educationkit"><b>The Human Genome 
            Project: Exploring our Molecular Selves</b></a> --This website includes 
            video and other offerings for high school students and the interested 
            public on the Human Genome Project and the future of medicine. 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://vector.cshl.org"><b>DNA Learning Center</b></a> 
            (DNALC)--from Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory. This website has numerous 
            educational tools including DNA From the Beginning (this series of 
            pages a person from basic concepts of inheritance through up-to-the-minute 
            methods of DNA analysis); Bioinformatics tools for teachers and students; 
            a biology animation library; restriction maps and complete nucleotide 
            sequences for a set of plasmids specifically designed by the DNALC 
            for educational use; an eugenics image archive; and genetics origins 
            (a study of human evolution). 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://avery.rutgers.edu/WSSP/Begin/index.html"><b>Waksman 
            Student Scholars Program</b></a><br>
            This program includes online tutorials in the biosciences, an online 
            bioinformatics challenge for students, and numerous other resources 
            for high school teachers and students. 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.holidaylectures.org/askascientist/"><b>Ask a 
            Scientist</b></a><br>
            Read answers to some of the common biology questions on topics like 
            eye color.<br>
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://genetics-education-partnership.mbt.washington.edu/"><b>Genetics 
            Education Partnership</b></a> -- The Genetics Education Partnership 
            (GEP) is a learning community of kindergarten through 12th grade teachers, 
            scientists, and genetics professionals from throughout Washington 
            state who are committed to genetics teaching. Their website includes 
            an online genetics education guide for K-12, genetics instructional 
            materials, classroom activities, and suggestions for cross-disciplinary 
            activities. 
            <P> 
          <LI><b><a href="http://chroma.mbt.washington.edu/outreach/hands_on_science.html"><i>"I 
            Don't Know, Let's Do the Experiment"</i>: Microscopes, Cells, DNA 
            and You</a></b> --a hands-on, inquiry science resource for learning 
            about and doing science, and teaching optics, cells, and DNA. Suitable 
            for upper elementary and middle school teachers. 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://science-education.nih.gov/newsnapshots/TOC_Chips/toc_chips.html"><b>DNA 
            Chips: A Genetics Lab in the Palm of Your Hand</b></a> --a special 
            issue of Snapshots of Science and Medicine: Modern Biology for High 
            School Classrooms. The entire issue is printable and includes a teacher's 
            guide. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://GSLC.genetics.utah.edu"><b>Genetic Science Learning 
            Center</b></a> --This site includes tutorials on Basic Genetics, Genetic 
            Disorders, and Genetics in Society plus special units on Neurofibromatosis, 
            Genetic Testing of Newborn Infants, and the Genetics of Deafness. 
            It also provides listings of online and hands-on activities, group 
            activities, integrated genetics activities, and other resources for 
            students of all ages. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.hhmi.org/GeneticTrail/"><b> Blazing a Genetic 
            Trail</b></a><b> </b>--a research overview on mutant genes and hereditary 
            diseases. Includes special sections on A Brief Key to Basic Genetics 
            and How to Conquer a Genetic Disease. Site presented by Howard Hughes 
            Medical Institute. Suitable for advanced middle school and high school 
            students.<br>
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.ornl.gov/hgmis/resource/lincoln.html"><b>Abraham 
            Lincoln's DNA and Other Adventures in Genetics</b></a> --a book which 
            details twenty-four true, wide-ranging tales of crime, history, human 
            behavior, illness and ethics to illustrate the principles of human 
            genetics. The book uses stories to explain how we can use DNA to understand 
            the past, addresses the DNA revolution in the courts, considers whether 
            genes make us the way we are, explores the possibilities of genetic 
            engineering of plants and animals, discusses the genetic revolution 
            in medicine, and wonders about emerging genetic technologies and their 
            impact on individual choice. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.biology.arizona.edu/"><b>Biology Project</b></a> 
            --an online interactive resource for learning biology. Presented by 
            the University of Arizona, the Biology Project provides tutorials, 
            problem sets, and activities in a number of areas of genetic research 
            including forensics, inheritance, karyotyping, gene expression, and 
            Mendelian genetics. Many of the units are also available in Spanish 
            and/or Portuguese. Suitable for high school and college students. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://genscope.concord.org/"><b>GenScope</b></a>--free, 
            downloadable software that allows students to manipulate the DNA of 
            an imaginary species of dragons and observe the effects at all levels. 
            Suitable for high school students. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.dnafiles.org/home.html"><b>DNA Files</b></a>--web 
            site that accompanies two series of radio programs broadcast on NPR. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.rdg.ac.uk/EIBE/ENGLISH/INTRO.HTM"><b>European 
            Initiative for Biotechnology Education</b></a> (EIBE)--teaching materials 
            for 16 -19 year olds including a variety of experimental protocols, 
            practical activities, role-plays, information and debates. Research 
            areas include microbes, DNA profiling, transgenic plants, etc. Units 
            are downloadable in PDF and are published in several languages. 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.ncbe.reading.ac.uk/NCBE/PROTOCOLS/PRACBK/main.html"><b>Practical 
            Biotechnology</b></a>--guide to a range of investigations encompassing 
            microbiology, plant tissue culture and enzyme technology for older 
            elementary, middle, and high school students. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.accessexcellence.com/"><b>Access Excellence</b></a> 
            --a national educational program that provides high school biology 
            and life science teachers access to their colleagues, scientists, 
            and critical sources of new scientific information via the World Wide 
            Web. The site includes teaching aids, news, graphics, career information, 
            activities, and other resources. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.esp.org/foundations/genetics/classical/"><b>Classic 
            Papers in Genetics</b></a>--a free service from Electronic Scholarly 
            Publishing, downloadable PDFs of original manuscripts of some of the 
            most important papers in the history of genetics research. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.phys.ksu.edu/gene/"><b>Genetics Education NEtwork 
            (GENE) Project </b></a>--site provides teacher-scientist collaborations 
            to adapt modern research organisms and techniques to the classroom 
            through workshops, laboratory procedures, video tape, and computer 
            simulation. Suitable for high school and college students. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.pbs.org/gene/"><b>A Question of Genes: Inherited 
            Risks</b></a>--this website accompanies the PBS documentary by the 
            same name. It contains numerous &quot;what if&quot; scenarios for 
            classroom discussion. It also contains a teacher's guide and information 
            for ordering a copy of the documentary. 
            <p> 
          <LI><b><a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/israel/">Lost Tribes of 
            Israel</a></b>--this website accompanies the PBS documentary by the 
            same name. The program follows anthropologist Tudor Parfitt's journey 
            to find both the lost city of the Lemba and the truth behind their 
            claim to an ancient Jewish heritage. The site contains exercises to 
            analyze Y-chromosome data to construct a family tree. It also contains 
            a teacher's guide and information for ordering a copy of the documentary. 
            <P> 
          <LI> <b><a href="http://www.npr.org/programs/morning/features/2001/feb/010212.cellsounds.html">Cell 
            Sounds</a></b> --go inside a cell with sounds and pictures from NPR's 
            Morning Edition. 
            <P> 
          <LI> <a href="http://www.nature.com/genomics/human/slide-show/"><b>Nature 
            Poster Slide Show</b></a>--a graphical presentation of the methods, 
            meanings, and implications of the complete genome sequence. Suitable 
            for high school and college students. 
            <P> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.microbeworld.org/mlc/pages/activities.asp"><b>Microbe 
            World</b></a> --17 hands-on activities that correlate with and build 
            on the themes and ideas presented in the PBS series, Intimate Strangers: 
            Unseen Life on Earth. These activities provide a fun way for kids 
            (and adults, too) to explore the wonders of the MicrobeWorld. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://gnn.tigr.org/main.shtml"><b>TIGR Genome News Network 
            Education Resources</b></a> --online news magazine, covering genomics, 
            proteomics, and related news in biomedical, microbial, and agricultural 
            research. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://genetics-education-partnership.mbt.washington.edu/"><b>Genetics 
            Education Partnership</b></a>--a learning community of kindergarten

            from throughout Washington state who are committed to genetics teaching. 
            Site includes a downloadable Genetics Education Guide, genetics activities, 
            and a list of reviewed genetics teaching text with accompanying ordering 
            information. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.netspace.org/MendelWeb/"><b>MendelWeb</b></a>--an 
            educational resource for teachers and students interested in the origins 
            of classical genetics, introductory data analysis, elementary plant 
            science, and the history and literature of science. Suitable for high 
            school and college students. 
            <p> 
          <LI><a href="http://www.kumc.edu/gec/"><b>Genetics Education Center</b></a> 
            --collections of links from the University of Kansas.

            school students.</LI>
