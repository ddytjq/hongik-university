          <TD WIDTH="305" VALIGN="top"> 
            <p><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica,Geneva, sans-serif" SIZE="2"> <BR>
              <!-- main body text - HEADLINE!--> <FONT COLOR="#000066" SIZE="3"><B> 
              Legal Expenses Insurance as<br>
              </B></FONT></FONT><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica,Geneva, sans-serif" SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#000066" SIZE="3"><B>an 
              Employee Benefit</B></FONT></FONT></p>
            <p><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica,Geneva, sans-serif" SIZE="2"><FONT COLOR="#000066" SIZE="3"><B><font size="2">Frequently 
              Asked Questions</font></B></FONT> </FONT><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica,Geneva, sans-serif" SIZE="2"> 
              </FONT></p>
            <FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica,Geneva, sans-serif" SIZE="2"> 
            <p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>Who 
              pays the premium; the company or the employee?</b></font></p>
            </FONT> 
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">We 
              can cater for either: It's up to you! Some companies like to purchase 
              cover for all their employees and some prefer to give their employees 
              the option to purchase cover themselves.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>Why 
              would I want my employees to have employment cover?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Strange 
              as it may seem, many employers would rather arrange for actions 
              brought by their staff to be handled by an independent, objective 
              insurer, as opposed to a trade union where subjective considerations 
              might come into play. This is also good for the employee as the 
              case will be assessed on its own merits and will not be clouded 
              by any consideration of the workforce as a whole. </font><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">We 
              do not allow employees to make frivolous claims against their employers.<br>
              <br>
              <b>Does the company have to get involved in complicated administration 
              procedures?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">No. 
              All we will require is a statement at agreed intervals showing the 
              names of employees covered, or a contact to be telephoned to check 
              the employee's details if a claim is received.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>My 
              company already has a legal expenses policy with you. Will this 
              be a problem?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">All 
              our Legal Expenses claims are treated in the strictest confidence 
              and the separate handling of commercial and personal claims should 
              ensure that there is no conflict of interest.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>My 
              company has both its Public and Employer's Liability policies with 
              Royal &amp; SunAlliance. What happens if an employee claims for 
              an injury that occurred at work?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">All 
              Legal Expenses claims are dealt with by FirstAssist, which is required 
              by law to operate independently from its parent company, Royal &amp; 
              Sun Alliance. If an employee is injured at work (assuming they have 
              personal injury cover on their policy), they can still make a claim 
              and it will be treated as any other claim of this type, irrespective 
              of who is insuring the other party.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>I 
              have a limited amount per employee in the budget to spend on legal 
              expenses insurance. Can you provide me with cover to fit my budget?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Yes. 
              We can create a bespoke policy to meet your company's needs both 
              in terms of cover and premium.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>Would 
              legal expenses insurance really benefit the employees? After all, 
              can't they claim Legal Aid?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Not 
              if they earn more than &pound;84 a week, have assets in excess of 
              &pound;1000, or have more than &pound;3000 equity in their home. 
              The simple fact is, following the curbing of the Legal Aid budget, 
              very few full-time employees can hope to receive state assistance 
              for most types of legal action.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>If 
              the employee pays for the policy, how would the premium be collected?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Most 
              employers find it convenient to collect the premium from the employee 
              via the payroll system and then pass the total premium to us once 
              a month. However, we do try to be flexible and can use other methods 
              to suit the employer. </font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><b>Can 
              pensioners participate in the scheme?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Yes, 
              if you want them to. Provided the premium can be collected, retired 
              staff members can continue to enjoy the benefit of legal protection.</font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura"><br>
              <b>Can part-time workers be covered?</b></font></p>
            <p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif, Futurist, Futura">Yes. 
              Again it is down to the employer whether the cover is available 
              to all employees or just certain categories of staff.</font> </p>
