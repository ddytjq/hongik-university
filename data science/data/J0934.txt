<h2>Introduction</h2>
<p>
Nelson Ledges is a very fast 2 mile road course
located in eastern Ohio.
The track has been gradually deterioriating for many years,
but a major track improvement program is going forward.
As of this writing (17 March 1997) major problem areas in the
pavement have been torn out down to the dirt and are being rebuilt
from the ground up;
new drainage has been provided for a spring that was causing
significant problems with the track surface.
A number of gravel traps are being added,
and plans are to make hot showers available and
possibly even revive the flush toilets
(some of the problems of the composting toilets,
including the updrafts, are definitely being dealt with.)
</p>
<p>
It is planned to completely repave between 1997 and 1998,
and to relocate/replace the bridge over the pit straight.
The current track owner is talking with several groups
of prospective investors,
one of which could potentially take ownership of the track over
a period of years if a deal is worked out.
</p>
<p>
The track has established a web page with info on their
1997 Schedule at
<a href="http://www.fastone.com/Nelson/Nelson97.html">http://www.fastone.com/Nelson/Nelson97.html</a>.
This address is different from the one available earlier in the year
that disappeared without warning.
</p>

<h2><a name="GettingThere">Getting to Nelson Ledges</a></h2>
<p>
The track is on Route 305 near US-422, about 20 miles northwest of Warren, OH.
From Exit 14 on I-80 (the Ohio Turnpike), take Route 5 southwest (toward
Revenna) about two miles, then turn right (north) onto Route 534 for about 10
miles, then turn left (northwest) onto US-422 for about half a mile, and
finally turn left (west) onto Route 305.
The track is about two miles ahead on the left.
Registration is in the barn on the left side of the access road.
</p>
<h2><a name="RacersGuide">A Racers' Guide to Nelson Ledges</a></h2>
<p>
Nelson Ledges Road course is two miles long, with 13 flag stations and little
elevation change.
The track may be rented for testing; contact the track manager.
Leaded racing gas, air, water, and food are available in the paddock.
Camping is permitted for a small fee (no hookups).
</p>
