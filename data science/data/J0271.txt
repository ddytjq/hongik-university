<h1>Dale &amp; Dale II: Earnhardt wins this time</h1>

<hr>
ROCKINGHAM, N.C. -- A week after dueling for the Daytona 500, Dale and Dale were at it again Sunday in the Goodwrench Service 400.
<p>
Jarrett, who extended Earnhardt's record to 0-for-18 in the
Daytona 500, was unable to hold off the seven-time Winston
Cup champion at North Carolina Motor Speedway. Earnhardt took the checkered flag under caution in the 393-lap Goodwrench 400. 
<p>
&quot;You've got to really pace yourself at this race track because
of the tire wear, but I was really going for it there at the end,&quot;
said Earnhardt, 44. &quot;Jarrett was getting stronger
every pit stop.&quot;
<p>
Earnhardt and Jarrett renewed their rivalry from last week, when
Jarrett denied Earnhardt his first win in the Daytona 500. The
two will be tied at the top of the Winston Cup points standings
when they are released Monday.
<p>
Earnhardt, running second to Jarrett, passed him late in the
race Sunday and stayed in front the
rest of the way. Earnhardt took the checkered flag under caution
for the 69th victory of his Winston Cup career.
<p>
&quot;I thought I had a shot there, but I got raced hard by a lapped
car (Derrike Cope) and burned my tires up,&quot; Jarrett said.
<p>
The caution, caused when Bobby Hillin Jr. hit the wall, came out
with just two laps remaining at the 1.017-mile oval.
<p>
Earnhardt and Jarrett swapped leads late in the race, but it was
Earnhardt who made the biggest move. He drove his Chevrolet Monte
Carlo under Jarrett's Ford Thunderbird 15 laps from the end for his
third Rockingham victory.
<p>
&quot;We're really happy with the car, the way it ran,&quot; said
Earnhardt, who notched his third win at Rockingham. &quot;We were a
little short at Daytona last week, but we made it up here.&quot; 
<p>
Ricky Craven checked in with a career-best third place behind
Jarrett, followed by Ricky Rudd in fourth and Steve Grissom in
fifth. 
<p>
Earnhardt found himself in several battles for the lead during
the race.  He took advantage of Terry Labonte's misfortune when
overheating forced Labonte out of the race after 235 laps.
Labonte's Chevrolet led a race-high 198 laps before exiting. 
<p>
&quot;This is the second week in a row we've had a really good car,&quot;
Labonte said. &quot;It's a real shame. We'll have to get 'em next
week.&quot; 
<p>
It was also the second consecutive week that debris caused Labonte
troubles.  He led the most laps at Daytona before a plastic bag
stuck in his air intake forced him off the track and deep in the
field.  Sunday's mechanical difficulties were caused, in part, by
paper that blocked the front grill of his Monte Carlo. 
<p>
Jimmy Spencer, whose Ford Thunderbird was among the fastest cars
on the track, battled back to pass Earnhardt for the lead from
11th place after being assessed a penalty for a pit road
violation. Spencer was dominant, leading a total of 53 laps,
before exiting with engine problems. 
<p>
&quot;This was clearly the best (career) run,&quot; Spencer said after
being knocked out of the race. &quot;I'd like to see (Richard)
Petty's car win this race for sentimental reasons.&quot; 
<p>
It looked as if that might happen, as Bobby Hamilton in the
Petty-owned Pontiac Grand Prix asserted himself and looked like
he would run away with the race. 
<p>
Hamilton and Earnhardt rounded Turn 4 with Hamilton holding a
slight edge when Earnhardt tapped Hamilton's back bumper. The
nudge was just enough to send Hamilton into the wall, leading to
structural problems that would end his chance to put Petty in
victory lane for the first time since 1984. 
<p>
Loy Allen Jr. suffered a broken right shoulder after an
accident in Turn 2 on Lap 173.  Allen was airlifted to
Carolinas Medical Center in Charlotte, where he ws undergoing more tests.
<p>
Allen's wrecked Ford Thunderbird came to rest in the entrance to
the back pit area, eventually resulting in a red flag which
stopped the race on Lap 179. 
<p>
The race was tough for defending Winston Cup and event champion
Jeff Gordon, who developed engine trouble on Lap 125 and left
the race. Gordon finished 40th after a 42nd-place finish last
week at Daytona. 
<p>
&quot;We're being humbled right now, realizing how good things went
for us last year,&quot; Gordon said. 
<p>
It turned out to be a terrible day for the three-car Hendrick
Motorsports team that began the race with high hopes.
<p>
Jeff Gordon, the defending race winner and 1995 Winston Cup
champion, had his second consecutive disappointing race. After
finishing 42nd in the 43-car Daytona 500, Gordon started Sunday's
race on the outside of the front row and was running fifth when
smoke burst from his Chevy on the 126th of 393 laps.
<p>
It was listed as an unspecified engine problem. The car was
pushed behind the wall for repairs. Gordon, 
Labonte's teammate, was there for 24 laps before getting back on
track only briefly. He wound up 40th.
<p>
Ken Schrader, the other Hendrick driver, who finished third at
Daytona, had two burnt wheel bearings that sent him back to the
garage area twice for extensive repairs.
<p>
But their biggest disappointment was Labonte, who led 198
of the first 234 laps before being sidelined by an engine problem
while leading Earnhardt.
<p>
