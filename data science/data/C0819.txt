Government introduces replacement for SERPS

<P>
<TABLE CELLSPACING="3" CELLPADDING="0" BORDER="0">

<TR VALIGN=TOP>
<TD><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1"><B>The State Second Pension</B></FONT></TD>
<TD ALIGN=RIGHT><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1">18/3/2002</FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD COLSPAN=2><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1">Goodbye SERPS, Hello S2P<BR>&nbsp;</FONT></TD></TR>
<TR><TD COLSPAN=2><FONT FACE="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" SIZE="-1"><p>

By 6th April 2002 the State Earnings Related Pension Scheme (SERPS) will be no more, replaced by the State Second Pension (S2P).  Any hopes that life will become less complicated are misplaced - S2P is very similar to SERPS in that the pension will be calculated on re-valued earnings between the lower earnings limit and the upper earnings limit but with the refinement that within those limits the earnings are split into three bands and the pension is calculated at different rates in each of the three bands. </p>
<p>

The pension will be based on averaged revalued annual earnings in each of the following bands as follows: </p>
<p>

<ul>
  <li>40% of Band 1 Earnings (namely earnings between the LEL and a low earnings threshold of £10,800)&nbsp;</li>
  <li>10% of Band 2 Earnings (namely earnings between the low earnings threshold and an upper earnings threshold of £24,600)&nbsp;</li>
  <li>20% of Band 3 Earnings between the upper earnings threshold and the UEL&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p>

The SERPS accrual rate is 20%.  S2P changes this so that the accrual is double the SERPS rate in Band 1, half in Band 2 and the same in Band 3.  For those who retire before April 2009, there is an addition to ensure their pension is not less than they would have received under SERPS. </p>
<p>

The benefits under S2P are accordingly skewed more towards those with low to medium earnings – those earning less than the upper earnings threshold are likely to be better off than under SERPS.  The intention is that those with higher earnings will be encouraged to contract-out. </p>
<p> 

Under S2P anyone earning above the LEL but less than the low earnings threshold will be deemed to earn at least the low earnings threshold, so will accrue pension at the Band 1 rate although just paying contributions on their actual earnings.  Also, some carers and some people suffering long-term incapacity will be treated as if they had earnings at the low earnings threshold. </p>
<p>

In five or six years’ time, Phase 2 of S2P will be introduced. Those under a certain age at that time (probably 45) will become entitled to a flat rate pension with everyone treated as if their earnings were equal to the lower earnings threshold; actual earnings will no longer be relevant for pensions but will still govern the amount of NI contributions.  People over age 45 at that time will stay in Phase 1 for the rest of their working lives. </p>
<p><b>


Contracting-out and S2P</b> </p>
<p>

Contracting-out will still be permitted and the introduction of S2P will probably make little difference to contracted-out schemes. </p>
<p>

For occupational pension schemes, the rebates will be calculated on the same basis as at the moment so that earners will be contracted-out from the
<u> SERPS</u> level of benefit and the rebates will follow the current SERPS rebates – flat rate for salary related schemes and (mainly) age-related for money purchase schemes.  The Government decided to continue the current form of rebates because it was persuaded that it would create undue complexity if they were to require schemes to provide benefits for their members at least comparable to each of the three new bands.  To ensure that those who earn up to the upper earnings threshold will not lose out, the State will provide an additional pension compensating them for the difference between the SERPS pension and S2P. </p>
<p> 

The rebates for personal pension schemes and stakeholder schemes will be more complicated.  They will still be paid on an age-related basis.  However, the actual percentage will differ for each Band.  The rebate for Band 1 Earnings is within the range of 8.4% to 21% depending on age, for Band 2 Earnings the rebate is in the range of 2.10% to 5.25% and for the Band 3 Earnings is in the range of 4.2% to 10.5%. </p>
<p><i>

The above is based on a note prepared by Lawrence Graham, solicitors, and is intended only as a summary of developments not as a definitive statement of the law.</i></p><BR>&nbsp;</FONT></TD></TR>
