<div class="mainheader">SEU Cosmic Journeys Roadmap</div>

<p>NASA's Structure and Evolution of the Universe (SEU) Theme is updating its <a href="http://universe.gsfc.nasa.gov/roadmap/cosmicj.html" title="2001 Roadmap">Roadmap</a> in support of the <a href="http://spacescience.nasa.gov/">NASA Office of Space Science (OSS)</a> strategic planning process.  The Roadmap and Strategic Plan will cover the years 2003-2028.  A <a href="/roadmap/team.html">Roadmap Team</a> has been formed with the following <a href="/roadmap/charter.html">Charter</a>. The team will report back to the <a href="http://spacescience.nasa.gov/adv/">Structure and Evolution of the Universe Subcommittee (SEUS)</a>. The roadmapping process will be completed by the fall of 2002.</p>

<p>The current members of the Roadmap team are: Sterl Phinney (chair), Paul Hertz (ex-officio, NASA/HQ), Sean Carroll, Sarah Church, Roy Gould (E/PO), Craig Hogan, Rocky Kolb (ex-officio), Dan Lester, Steven Kahn, Robert March, Michael Shull, Simon Swordy, and Nicholas White. Contact information is <a href="/roadmap/team.html">here</a>.</p>

<div class="subheader">Scope of NASA's Structure and Evolution of the Universe Theme.</div>

<p>The science objectives of the SEU Theme are:</p>
<ul>
<li>To understand the structure of the universe, from its earliest beginnings to its ultimate fate.
<li>To explore the cycles of matter and energy in the evolving universe.
<li>To explore the ultimate limits of gravity and energy in the universe.
<li>To elucidate the fundamental laws of physics.
</ul>

<div class="subheader">Scope of the 2003 Roadmap</div>

<p>The 2003 Roadmap will cover the years 2003-2028.
The 2003 Roadmap will consider all space missions (with the exception of those which should be realized as Explorer missions -see <a href="#format">criteria</a>) and related scientific and techological activities relevant to the SEU science objectives. This includes observations of the universe using particles, gravitational waves, or electromagnetic radiation of any wavelength and tests of the fundamental laws of physics relevant to astrophysics and cosmology.  The 2000 SEU Roadmap, covering the years 2003-2023, may be found <a href="http://universe.gsfc.nasa.gov/roadmap/cosmicj.html">here</a>.

<p>The SEU science objectives may be focused across a broad range of astrophysics and are intended to be complimentary to the science objectives of <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/index.html">NASA's Astronomical Search for Origins Theme</a> (including the major Origins missions: <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/ngst.html">NGST</a>, <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/sim.html">SIM</a>, <a href="http://origins.jpl.nasa.gov/missions/tpf.html">TPF</a>).  Previous SEU Roadmaps have identified high priority mission sets which support the breadth of SEU science.  NASA realizes that all high priority science objectives cannot be realized first.  The <a href="/roadmap/charter.html">charter of the 2003 SEU Roadmap Team</a> directs the Roadmap Team to identify and articulate the highest priority science objective and the mission set needed to address that objective.  It is anticipated that the Roadmap Team will identify additional high-priority science objectives and missions that do not support the highest priority science objective; these objectives and missions will be identified in an appendix to the Roadmap.  Implementing these other objectives and missions will be addressed in future Roadmaps. <em>All high priority science objectives cannot be implemented first.</em></p>
