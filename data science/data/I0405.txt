Canaries set to be a PLC
9 Jul<br>
			<SPAN Class='gentext' ><P>Norwich City is set to become a public limited company after an extraordinary general meeting of shareholders at Carrow Road agreed for some new shares to be issued.</P><P>Canaries chairman Roger Munby has issued a passionate plea to the supporters to assist the Nationwide Division One club in the new venture that could raise over £10million in just a few months.</P><P>"This is the time for all our supporters to once again get behind the club in the most obvious way by purchasing a share of its ownership," Munby told the club's official website.</P><P>"The board have taken a considerable amount of time deliberating the public offer option and have not taken the decision lightly. However, we believe that it is absolutely necessary for the club to take this route so that, if the offer is successful, we avoid other more drastic and less popular ways of raising income or reducing costs.</P><P>"Last season Norwich City earned around £2.7million in television income. Following Carlton and Granada's actions the club will now receive substantially less income over the next two seasons than the £5million we had budgeted to receive.</P><P>"This threatens our survival in the way we are currently organised. It threatens our ability to move forward from our point to take-off. It certainly threatens the continuity we must now aim to achieve.</P><P>"Therefore our first point of appeal is to our fans through the public offer of shares to aim to raise funds to at least make up the loss that television has produced. If this works we can continue to do our best to turn our point of take-off into real achievement."</P>
