<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b><font size="3">TWO OPTIONS</font></b><br>
  There are two versions of our Home Equity Release Mortgage: the Standard Home 
  Equity Release Mortgage which gives a cash lump sum and Cash Plus which gives 
  you a combination of an optional cash lump sum and a guaranteed fixed monthly 
  cash release</font><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">.</font></p>
<p><a name="option1"></a></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b>OPTION 1: THE STANDARD 
  HOME EQUITY RELEASE MORTGAGE: A CASH LUMP SUM </b><br>
  The standard way of using the Home Equity Release Mortgage is to take a lump 
  sum. This is an ideal way to get cash for one-off expenses such as home repairs, 
  an extension or a new car. </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">It is possible to borrow 
  up to 50% of the value of your property but the precise amount depends upon 
  your age and your property's valuation. The percentage of your property's value 
  which is available to you as a loan, increases with your age. In the case of 
  joint borrowers we use the age of the youngest borrower to work out your maximum 
  loan. The table shows how this works. </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The minimum property value 
  we will consider is &#163;40,000. </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The minimum amount we will 
  lend is &#163;5,000. </font></p>

<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2"><b>Example</b> <br>
  Mr and Mrs Jones are aged 74 and 71 respectively. They have a house worth &#163;200,000. 
  </font></p>
<p><font face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif" size="2">Because Mrs Jones is the 
  younger, they fall in the '67-71' bracket entitling them to 25% of the value 
  of their house, i.e. &#163;50,000. </font></p>
<p><a name="option2"></a></p>
<p><font size="3" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><b><font size="2">OPTION 
  2: THE CASH PLUS HOME EQUITY RELEASE MORTGAGE: CASH NOW PLUS A GUARANTEED FIXED 
  MONTHLY INCOME FOR THE REST OF YOUR LIFE </font></b></font><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"><br>
  The Cash Plus version of the Home Equity Release Mortgage gives you a monthly 
  cash release for the rest of your life (or until you require long term care 
  or move into sheltered accommodation) plus the option of taking an immediate 
  lump sum (minimum &#163;1,000). </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">This version of the Home 
  Equity Release Mortgage is ideal if your priority is to provide some secure 
  and guaranteed cash for your future. It's entirely up to you whether you take 
  advantage of the cash lump sum as well. </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">With Cash Plus, you can 
  choose the level of monthly cash release you require and then count on receiving 
  that amount - guaranteed - every single month. Naturally, the more you choose 
  to receive monthly, the less will be the cash you receive as a lump sum up front. 
  </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">The table below gives a 
  guide to your Cash Plus options. As with the Standard Home Equity Release Mortgage, 
  the older you are, the more money you can release from your property. But in 
  each case, it is up to you how you balance your monthly cash release with your 
  cash lump sum. </font></p>

  column tell you what percentage of your home's value you can release as a cash 
  lump sum. For someone 60 years old, the maximum would be 14%, while for those 
  aged 89 or more the maximum would be 50%. </font></p>

  of the table show what percentage of your home's value you could receive as 
  a monthly cash release according to your age. </font></p>
<p><font size="2" face="Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">By cross-referencing both 
  the figures in the middle and those on the left hand side, you can find the 
  different combinations of lump sum and monthly cash release that are possible 
  for your circumstances. </font></p>
