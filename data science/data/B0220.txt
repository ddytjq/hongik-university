							<TD colspan="2"><b>At the Principality we continue to be committed to working with the community in which we operate, and we always strive to ensure that groups and projects that we support provide a direct and tangible benefit to communities in Wales. </b><p>

For many years, Principality staff members have been involved in various community-based projects, such as the Prince's Trust Volunteers Scheme, where employed & unemployed young people work on projects in the community, and gain new skills into the bargain. Another such activity is the 'Race for life', where representatives from the Society have taken part annually in a series of 5km walks and runs, exclusively for women, which raises money for charity. <p>

Other recent projects include the funding of the National Botanic Gardens' Centre for Lifelong Learning (called 'Principality House'), and the sponsorship of National Science Week at Techniquest in Cardiff Bay. The provision of an award in the Primary School category (this year won by Darran Park Primary from Ferndale in the Rhondda) in the annual Welsh Heritage Schools Initiative is a further illustration of the Principality's fervent commitment to community-based causes. </td>

									Principality Building Society, Wales' largest independent financial services organisation, today announced a £100,000 sponsorship package for the Noah's Ark Appeal, as part of the fundraising effort to achieve the £5 million needed to build the first phase of a new Children's Hospital for Wales. The money provided by the Principality will sponsor four individual bedrooms at the new hospital, based at Heath, Cardiff. These rooms will be named after the Society in perpetuity. <p>

Presenting a cheque to Charlotte Church, Vice Patron of the Noah's Ark Appeal, Principality Chairman, Peter Davies, said, "We are absolutely delighted and indeed very proud to be making this contribution to the new Children's Hospital for Wales. I can think of no better 'all Wales' community project for us to be involved in at this time. The need for the Hospital has been very clearly articulated, and we feel the need to play our part in investing now in the health of future generations of Welsh children."<p>

In response, Lyn Jones, Chairman of the Trustees of the Noah's Ark Appeal said, "This is wonderful news for us. On behalf of the Trustees and everybody involved in the Noah's Ark Appeal, it is a very pleasant duty for me to thank Principality Building Society for their wholehearted support and for their generous contribution to the Appeal."<p>    

He continued, " We are now more than halfway to our initial target and with current fundraising in place and major events planned for next year, the Trustees are confident that the project will be in a position to go to tender early in 2002 - and even more so as a result of the Principality's major contribution today."<p> 

In addition to their sponsorship, the Principality will support the Noah's Ark Appeal with fundraising activities in each of the Society's 47 branches across Wales and the Marches. Chief Executive, John Mitchell particularly welcomed this aspect of the Society's support, stating, "It is very important to us that the people of Wales feel that this is their own project. The additional funding raised throughout our branch network will keep people in touch and give them an opportunity to contribute to the new Children's Hospital."<p>

Mr Mitchell continued, "As a mutual society, Principality Building Society is committed to developing and maintaining close links with the communities in which it operates. Sponsorship of the Children's Hospital for Wales, combined with our existing range of community initiatives, further demonstrates our commitment to achieve this goal."<BR>

<b>Calling young artists of the future….</b><p>
Under 11s have chance to design the front cover of a starry panto programme for Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs in Project Panto supported by Principality Building Society. <p>
In a move supported by The Principality, budding young artists are to have the chance to design the cover of the programme for this year’s star-studded pantomime at the New Theatre, Cardiff in an open competition for under 11s from Cardiff, Newport and the Valleys. The Project Panto competition supported by Principality Building Society is now open with entry details available in local Principality branches or from the New Theatre’s Education department on 029 2087 8572.<p>

On behalf of The Principality, supporters of this year’s Project Panto, Michael McGuire, General Manager, Sales & Marketing, said:
" We at the Principality are particularly delighted that our sponsorship of 'Project Panto' has enabled the New Theatre to support the arts at school level. We believe the Society's success derives from the strength of our roots in communities throughout Wales, in much the same way as the cover competition's success will result from healthy competition in many local schools. On behalf of Principality Building Society, I wish all entrants the best of luck in the cover competition."<p>

Building on the success of previous years, hundreds of entries from schools and individuals are expected over the coming weeks. There will be nine prizes, ranging from the first prize including a family ticket for the pantomime starring Linda Lusardi, Sam Kane and Sooty and a back-stage visit to meet the stars to goodie bags provided by The Principality to the six runners up. A second and third prize will include tickets for the panto, which is already set to become the theatre’s most successful ever.<p>

Entries for the competition should be at least A4 portrait size, on the theme of Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs and must feature the panto title - the entrant’s name, age, address and phone number should be clearly marked on the reverse.<p>
<b>The closing date for entries is 12 November. All entries should be sent to Panto Programme Cover Competition, New Theatre, Park Place, Cardiff, CF10 3LN. Senior staff of the New Theatre and Principality Building Society will judge the entries in November.</b><p>

Catch the New Theatre’s most popular panto ever, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs from 15 December to 26 January. Tickets start at just £7 with discounts for groups, people with disabilities, students, children and over 60s. For more information, call New Theatre Box Office on 029 2087 8889 (open 10am to 6pm Monday to Saturday).<p>

									Principality Building Society today reaffirmed their status as Official Financial Services Sponsor of the WPGA Championship of Europe. In a move to showcase their support of the successful Wales’ Ryder Cup Bid, the Principality have signed an agreement which will help to support the third richest tournament on the Evian Ladies European Tour. 

<p> The new tournament sponsored by the Society was held from July 26-29 at Royal Porthcawl GC. It featured a selection of international players who compete on both sides of the Atlantic, including the majority of Europe's victorious Solheim Cup 2000 team. With the WPGA Championship falling the week before the Weetabix British Open, it is likely to attract stars such as Laura Davies, Sophie Gustafson and Catrin Nilsmark to one of the finest links in South Wales.  

John Mitchell, the Chief Executive of the Principality was delighted with the partnership,

“This is an exciting venture for Principality Building Society.  Our aim is to support both the Ladies European Tour and the game of golf in Wales. We also recognise that this is a stepping stone towards achieving the ambition of bringing the Ryder Cup to Wales in 2010, which we are very proud to be a part of”. 

<p> Wales' plans to host the Ryder Cup in 2010 have thrust professional golf to the forefront of the 2001 Welsh sporting calendar.  The WPGA Championship is one of the major tournaments in Wales’ ‘Summer of Golf’ programme, designed to attract some of the finest players in the world to Welsh courses during 2001. 

<p> As Tony Lewis, Chairman of Wales Ryder Cup bid pointed out,

“We have cut no corners in our efforts to bring the 2010 Ryder Cup to Wales for the very first time. We are also determined to showcase our strengths by supporting the Ladies European Tour as well as the men's European and Seniors Tours.  Therefore, we are delighted to have the support of Principality Building Society for this event as we strive to encourage further Welsh businesses to get behind the bid”.

<p> To celebrate this exciting new partnership, the Principality offered 2 of its lady customers an exclusive opportunity to compete in the Pro-Am tournament at the WPGA Championships on Wednesday 25th July. Application forms for this unique prize draw were available at any branch of the Principality.

<p> The Wales Ryder Cup bid committee is a partnership led by the National Assembly for Wales with participation from the Welsh Development Agency, the Wales Tourist Board, Sports Council for Wales, the Welsh Golfing Union, the Welsh Ladies’ Golf Union, Newport County Borough Council and The Celtic Manor Resort, as well as Principality Building Society.     
<p> In the Assembly, the bid enjoyed the support of all four political parties.   MPs, local authorities throughout Wales and a wide range of celebrities have all supported the bid.
<p> Strong backing for the bid came from all the leading Welsh players including Ian Woosnam, Phil Price, David Park, Becky Brewerton, Becky Morgan and Eleanor Pilgrim. Brian Huggett, a veteran of seven Ryder Cups, is also a member of the bid committee.<p>

									Principality Building Society are sponsoring a major event in the 2001 Gower Festival – a performance by Swansea Bach choir, conducted by John Hugh Thomas. The popular choir will be singing numerous renditions of works with a naval flavour to commemorate the 25th anniversary of the Festival. The Swansea Bach Choir evening is on Saturday July 21st, at All Saints Church, Oystermouth.<p> 

Peter Davies, Chairman of the Principality said, “As a true Building Society, the Principality is committed to building and maintaining close links with the community in which it operates. The sponsorship of the Gower Festival is testament to this.”<p>

Principality Building Society, as the largest independent financial institution based in

							<TD colspan="2"><b>Sowing the Seeds of Future Learning</b>

Principality Building Society has sponsored the development of a Lifelong Learning Centre at The National Botanic Garden of Wales, which opened in May 2000.<p>

When The National Botanic Garden of Wales opened to the public on 24th May 2000, it was the first national botanic garden to be built in the United Kingdom for over two hundred years. Located in the Middleton Hall Estate close to Carmarthen in West Wales, it places education at the centre of all of its activities. Visitors learn about the important role plants play in our natural habitats, and how we need to live in better harmony with our natural environment.<p>

<i>Principality House</i><br> The Lifelong Learning Centre is named Principality House, and is located in one of only two remaining regency buildings on site. The centre has now been fully restored, and stands in an excellent position close to the Great Glasshouse. Inside, there are four teaching areas, suited to differing group sizes and activities; the largest of these can accommodate one hundred people. In addition to their formal teaching use, the rooms are available for use as meeting rooms and for community activities.<p> 

<i>Education at The Garden</i><br>
 Visitors to Principality House fall into four main groups <br>
1. Further or higher education students following accredited courses, offered in partnership with existing providers. eg. Garden Studies, Botany, Rural Studies.<br>
2. Continuing professional development, particularly in the fields of conservation, environmental education and teaching.<br> 
3. Adult and community education following non-accredited courses in a  wide variety of topics 
eg. garden design, botanical illustration, photography, flower arranging, practical horticultural techniques.<br>
4. Schools groups as part of the main education programme, or for special events eg. GNVQ workshop days.<p>

Courses are organised for weekdays, evenings, weekends and as summer-schools, to meet the various needs of visitors. The National Botanic Garden of Wales is committed to providing bilingual education and training wherever possible.<p>

<i>An excellent resource</i><br>
 Visitors will also have access to all the features of this unique site including
the Great Glasshouse, which houses thousands of plants from one of the world’s most endangered habitats. The 568 acre estate contains historic parklands and naturalised habitats and farms together with habitat management areas, including a Water Discovery Centre with dipping pools and laboratory area.
The 'Physicians of Myddfai' exhibition shows how plants are used in healing around the world.<p>

<i>Looking to the future</i><br>
 As a flagship Millennium project in Wales, the Garden has attracted millions of visitors since its opening in May 2000. It is a long-term venture with investment planned for the future by building a garden for the twenty-first century and beyond. A primary aim is to help people understand the vital role of plants in all of our lives, and the importance of conserving natural habitats. <p>

Principality Building Society is pleased to share in the Lifelong Learning Centre's vision of the future by investing in the education of visitors from Wales and beyond, and providing a first class education facility. The Principality Lifelong Learning Centre has played an integral part in the exciting programme of events since the Garden's inception in Summer 2000.<p>

The National Botanic Garden of Wales is a £43.3million project which has attracted a £21.7million grant from the Millennium Commission.<p>

									Darran Park Primary School, from Rhondda Cynon Taff, have recently been the recipients of a prestigious award, donated by Principality Building Society. The Society donated £500 to the school as part of their annual support for the Welsh Heritage Schools Initiative. <p> 

The topic of the project was 'The Eisteddfod and its local tradition', and the idea stemmed from the School's own annual Eisteddfod. It was seen as a good way to learn about the Culture of Wales. The school used various resources, such as first-hand evidence and the internet to research their project. They invited various people connected with the Eisteddfod tradition to the School, such as a local bard (who is also a school governor) who talked about the Gorsedd traditions, as well as a past pupil, who taught the pupils about the tradition of the crown.<p> 

The pupils at Darran Park undertook various activities connected with Eisteddfod traditions as part of the project, such as composing a song, making crowns and even making a bardic chair which the help of a local craftsman ! The chair will now be used annually at the school Eisteddfod.<p> 

The money provided by the Principality will contribute towards much-needed school equipment and the research project itself will be used to help future students at Darren Park Primary. The award was presented in a ceremony at the National Museum of Wales in Cardiff, by Michael McGuire, General Manager, to several of the pupils involved in the project. (see attached photograph).<p> 

Mr McGuire said, "I am delighted that our award has helped the Welsh Heritage Schools Initiative fulfill its objective of raising the profile of Wales' Schools.  I am particularly pleased that our sponsorship has helped preserve an important part of Welsh history for years to come."<p> 

Lady Trotman - Dickenson, Chairperson of the Welsh Heritage Schools Initiative, said of the Society's involvement in the scheme, 
" The Initiative are most grateful to the Principality for the very generous support to our scheme over the years."<p>
