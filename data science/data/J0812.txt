                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                    <hr><font  color='000000' ><b>Rossi wins his second GP of 2002 while MotoGP newcomer Kato impresses in second place</b></font>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67"><img src="/img/00.gif" width="67" height="36"></td>
                                </tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">

                                  </td>

                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/rosi2d21.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='0000cc' >Valentino Rossi (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > scored his second victory of the new four-stroke MotoGP era at Jerez this afternoon, extending his World Championship lead in front of a wildly enthusiastic 130,000-strong crowd. But the Italian was chased hard by class rookie </font><font  color='0000cc' >Daijiro Kato (Fortuna Honda Gresini NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >, the first 500 two-stroke rider to seriously challenge the so far dominant four-strokes. Kato finished just 1.190 seconds behind Rossi after getting the better of a frantic tussle with South African GP winner </font><font  color='0000cc' >Tohru Ukawa (Repsol Honda Team RC211V-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' >. Michelin riders dominated today's race, the fastest-ever Spanish GP, taking the first six positions, with Rossi a stunning 25 seconds faster than his winning pace in last year's 500 GP here. The dramatic increase in race pace can be attributed to the speed of Rossi's RCV plus the extra grip provided by Michelin's new S4 rear slick.</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>
                                <tr>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          
                                          <img align="left" alt="Race start" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/startd26.jpg" vspace=10 >
                                          <font  color='0000cc' >"That was a pretty good race for us,"</font><font  color='000000' > said Michelin Grand Prix manager Emmanuel Fournier. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"All our tyres seemed to work well with normal levels of wear, even though we'd not raced on this surface before. The first three guys all ran the same spec tyres  a medium-compound S4 16.5in rear and a medium version of our latest front. I think Valentino's strategy was good today  he changed tactics after getting beaten at Welkom! Kato was very strong in the late stages, riding so hard to stay with Rossi. He's obviously still learning to ride the 500 and I think this race showed that HRC are also still developing their 500."</font><font  color='000000' ><br>	Rossi had to work hard for his 41st Grand Prix success. After getting the holeshot from pole position he slipped to third and then to eighth after </font><font  color='0000cc' >Kenny Roberts (Telefonica Movistar Suzuki GSV-R-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > got sideways into turn one and barged him off the racing line. Cool as ever, Rossi steadily worked his way back towards the front, slotting into second behind Ukawa on lap six. He stayed there for 11 laps while Kato worked his way forward, demoting </font><font  color='0000cc' >Loris Capirossi (West Honda Pons NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > and </font><font  color='0000cc' >Alex Barros (West Honda Pons NSR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > to fourth and fifth. Rossi finally took the lead with 11 laps to go when the three 500 riders were snapping at his heels.<br></font><font  color='0000cc' >"	I felt Kenny hit me, I guess he must've made a mistake braking for turn one,"</font><font  color='000000' > said Rossi. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I was just lucky I didn't crash and after that it was like starting all over again. The bike was a little hard to ride with a full fuel tank but I managed to get into second and follow Ukawa's good rhythm. By the end I was sliding around a bit but I still think we could've gone three or four tenths quicker."</font>
                                        
                                        <br><br>
                                  </td>
                                  <td class="texte10" width="67">&nbsp;</td></tr>

                                  <td class="texte10" width="469">
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                          <img align="right" alt="Daijiro Katoh (Fortuna Honda Gresini)" border=0  hspace=10 src="/upload/article/kato2d3.jpg" vspace=10>
                                          
                                          <font  color='000000' >Kato, who won last year's Spanish 250 GP, was delighted with his first MotoGP podium finish. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"I'm very proud,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the Japanese after only his third race in biking's premier class. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"Now I will devote all my energies to closing the gap on Valentino."</font><font  color='000000' ><br>	Ukawa led 15 of the first 16 laps but had to be happy with third place which moved him into second in the World Championship. The Japanese had an entertaining duel for second with Kato and then for third with fourth-place finisher Capirossi. Both battles swung this way and that, the lighter two-stroke NSRs faster into the corners and the more powerful four-stroke RCV quicker on the way out. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"That was a spectacular race!"</font><font  color='000000' > grinned Capirossi whose team-mate Barros was just happy to be fifth and back on the pace. The Brazilian led lap one and ended the race just four seconds down on Rossi.<br>	 </font><font  color='0000cc' >Norick Abe (Antena 3 Yamaha-d'Antin YZR500-Michelin)</font><font  color='000000' > led the best of the rest in sixth, ahead of Nobuatsu Aoki (Proton KR) and Roberts, who achieved his first finish of the year after his team switched to Michelin tyres. "</font><font  color='0000cc' >The tyres were consistent,"</font><font  color='000000' > said the American, whose Suzuki four-stroke only started its track-testing programme in January. </font><font  color='0000cc' >"Now we know what we need to do to get better."</font>
