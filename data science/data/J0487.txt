<a NAME="Tracks"><hr SIZE="5"></a>
<font SIZE="+4"><b>Race Tracks:</b><br><font SIZE="+0"><br>

<a HREF="evergreen"><font SIZE="+2">Evergreen Raceway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Northeastern PA
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1/3 mile flat asphalt oval. SB Modifieds, Pro Stocks, Thrillers, 4-Cylinders, KLASCAR Legend Series, and Micro Stocks.</ul>

<font SIZE="+2">Flemington Motor Speedway
<font SIZE="+1"> - New Jersey
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>5/8 mile asphalt oval. SB Modifieds, Street Stocks, Thrillers, and 4-Cylinders.</ul>

<a HREF="tioga"><font SIZE="+2">Tioga Speedway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Binghamton, New York
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1/2 mile asphalt oval. NASCAR Modifieds, Late Models, Sportman Modifieds, and Strictly Stocks.</ul>

<a HREF="http://subjungle.com/DFWMotorsport/cowtown"><font SIZE="+2">Cowtown Speedway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - whereabouts unknown!
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1/4 mile high-banked dirt oval track</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.tulsaweb.com/hallett"><font SIZE="+2">Hallett Motor Racing Circuit</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Tulsa, Oklahoma
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>a 1.8 mile, 10 turn Road Racing course hosting: Auto Racing, Motorcycles and even high speed Go
Karts. Participating organizations include: Competition Motorsports Association (COMMA), Sports Car Club of America (SCCA),
American Indy Car Series, Central Motorcycle Riders Association, IKF &amp; KART Go Karting, as well as local Porsche, Shelby, Alfa
Romeo, Corvette and BMW clubs.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.martinsvillespeedway.com"><font SIZE="+2">Martinsville Speedway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Martinsville, Virginia
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>.526-mile speedway hosting four NASCAR race weekends 
each year including two NASCAR Winston Cup races, two NASCAR Late Model Stock races, 
a NASCAR Craftsman Truck Series race and a NASCAR Slim Jim All Pro Series.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.racemiami.com"><font SIZE="+2">Metro-Dade Homestead Motorsports Complex</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Miami, Florida
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>The Homestead Motorsports Complex is the only racing facility of its kind. With its ability to 
support a wide variety of motorports events, NASCAR, IndyCar, SCCA TransAm, and even the 
SuperBikes of the AMA will call the Homestead Motorsports Complex home in 1996.</ul>
 
<a HREF="http://www.mosport.com"><font SIZE="+2">Mosport Park</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Ontario, Canada
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>Road course and oval track motor racing featuring the 
IMSA Endurance Championship series, racing twice around the face of Zodiac's 
Ultimate Sports Watch!</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.searspoint.com"><font SIZE="+2">Sears Point Raceway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Sonoma, California
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>North America's most complete and versatile motorsports complex.
The twisting, challenging 2.52 mile road course and championship quarter-mile drag
strip showcase the best in motorsports entertainment. It hosts several nationally 
televised events including NASCAR Winston Cup, NHRA Drag Racing, and the SCCA
Trans-Am Tour.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/Charlotte.html"><font SIZE="+2">Charlotte Motor Speedway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - North Carolina
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1.5 mile oval with a 2.25 mile infield road course.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/Daytona.html"><font SIZE="+2">Daytona International Speedway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Daytona, Florida
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>2.5 mile superspeedway with a 3.81 mile road course in the
infield. There are 6 turns in the infield section and the majority of the speedway 
is used. A high speed chicane is located in the back straight approaching NASCAR 3 to 
keep the speeds down. The speeds will still be higher than most SCCA racers have 
normally seen.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/NCMS.html"><font SIZE="+2">North Carolina Motor Speedway (aka Rockingham)</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Rockingham, North Carolina
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1.5 road course built in a NASCAR oval.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/NHIS.html"><font SIZE="+2">New Hampshire International Speedway Road Course</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Loudon, New Hampshire
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>1 mile oval with a 1.6 mile road course. NHIS carries a full slate of SCCA regionals, an SCCA 
National, two NASCAR Busch Grand National North races, a NASCAR Winston Cup race, an 
Indycar race, and various and sundry motorcycle races. The Pro races are all run on 
the oval; the primary user of the road course is the SCCA.</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/Pocono.html"><font SIZE="+2">Pocono International Raceway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Northeastern Pennsylvania
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>Pocono hosts two NASCAR Winston Cup races, an SCCA
National and a number of SCCA Regionals. Pocono has hosted Indycar races in the 
past, but not recently. There is a possibility that an Indycar race might be sanctioned 
by the new IRL organization, but no prosect of an Indycar sanctioned by the current CART
Indycar organization.</ul>

<a HREF="Indianapolis" Raceway Park><font SIZE="+2">Indianapolis Raceway Park</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Indianapolis, Indiana
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>IRP is owned by the NHRA, and contains a dragstrip, a 5/8 mile paved oval, and the road
course. Spectator and televised events are run each year at all three tracks in the facility. Perhaps the most famous IRP event is the
evening of USAC oval track racing offered the night before the Indianapolis 500 (which is held at an obscure facility in nearby
Speedway, Indiana.)</ul>

<a HREF="http://www.emi.com/~rwelty/tracks/Phoenix.html"><font SIZE="+2">Phoenix International Raceway</a>
<font SIZE="+1"> - Phoenix, Arizona
<font SIZE="+0"><ul>awaiting current information to be posted...</ul>
