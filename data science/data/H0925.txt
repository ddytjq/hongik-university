    <td valign="middle" colspan="2" width="606"> 
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Polymorphonuclear 
        Leucocytes <br>
        (white blood cells) <br>
        </font></p>
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Trichrome 
        stain<br>
        light microscopy x 1000</font></p>
      <p align="center"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Could 
        be confused with E. histolytica amoebic cyst due to similarity in size 
        </font></p>
      <p align="center">&nbsp;</p>
    </td>
  </tr></table>
<hr width="700" align="left">
<table width="700">
  <tr> 
    <td colspan="2"> 
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Ratio of nuclear material 
        to cytoplasm is 1:1<br>
        Entamoeba cyst is 1:2 to 1:3 </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">There are 2-4 nuclear 
        segments in the leucocytes. The narrow chromatin bands joining them may 
        not be visible. </font></p>
      <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Frequently there is 
        no central mass to be mistaken for a karyosome. </font></p>
    </td>
    <td width="225" align="right"><img src="image/41polys.jpg" width="225" height="225" alt="41 - WBC - Trichrome stain x 1000"></td>
  </tr></table>
