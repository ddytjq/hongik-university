<h1>Heartland Park</h1>

<h2>Introduction</h2>
Heartland Park is a facility which includes a
multiple-configuration Road Course
and a 3/8 mile clay oval which was added for the 2001
racing season after a brief experiement with a temporary
dirt oval.
The facility is located just
outside of Topeka, Kansas.  The track is used by the
<a href="/Organizations/SCCA/">SCCA</a>,
<a href="/Organizations/IMSA/">PSR</a>, AMA, IKF, NASCAR,
<a href="/Organizations/ASA/">ASA</a>,
and
<a href="/Organizations/ARCA/">ARCA</a>.
There are 5 road course
configurations that are regularly used, including the 2.5 mile
"Grand Prix" circuit and the 1.8 mile "Full NASCAR
circuit", used by ARCA, ASA, SCCA, and NASCAR.
</p>
<p>
The oval has 8 degrees of banking in 90 foot radius
turns, with 6 degrees of banking in the straightaways.
</p>
<p>
Heartland Park has their own web site at
<a href="http://www.hpt.com/">http://www.hpt.com/</a>.
</p>
<p>
Most of the information in this page was supplied by Kris B. Mandt
(kbmandt@aol.com) of the Des Moines Valley Region of the SCCA. 
</p>

<h2><a name="GettingThere">Getting to Heartland Park</a></h2>
<h3>From Topeka</h3>
Take US highway 75 south to the junction of 75 and Gary Ormsby Drive.
<p>
<h3>From Kansas City</h3>
Take I-70 to the South Topeka exit (exit 177), and go south on US 75.
<p>
<h3>From Wichita</h3>
Take the Kansas Turnpike to the South Topeka exit (exit 177), and go
south on US 75.
<p>
<h3>from I-470</h3>
Take the Topeka Blvd/US 75 exit, and go south to the track on US 75.
